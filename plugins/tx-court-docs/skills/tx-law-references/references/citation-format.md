# Texas citation format — Texas Rules of Form ("Greenbook")

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Style guidance only. When in doubt, follow the current
> edition of the **Texas Rules of Form** (the "Greenbook," published by the
> Texas Law Review Association), *The Bluebook*, and any local-rule citation
> requirement of the filing court.

Texas legal citation follows the **Texas Rules of Form** ("Greenbook") layered
on *The Bluebook*. Texas **abolished its official Texas Reports**, so reported
Texas decisions are cited to the **South Western Reporter** (`S.W.`, `S.W.2d`,
`S.W.3d`). The two Texas signatures are the **court / district designation**
and the **petition-history (writ-history) parenthetical**.

## Cases — Supreme Court of Texas

Cite to the South Western Reporter with a **`Tex.`** court designation and the
year:

```
In re Columbia Med. Ctr. of Las Colinas, 290 S.W.3d 204 (Tex. 2009)
```

- **Case name** — italicized (or underlined in typed documents).
- **Court designation** — **`Tex.`** for the Supreme Court of Texas (the civil
  court of last resort). Criminal opinions of the **Court of Criminal Appeals**
  use **`Tex. Crim. App.`**
- **No petition history** is appended to a Supreme Court of Texas opinion.

## Cases — Courts of Appeals (district + petition history)

Court of Appeals opinions add the **court of appeals district** (the city
seat) **and** a **petition-history parenthetical**:

```
Doe v. Roe, 123 S.W.3d 456 (Tex. App.—Dallas 2003, pet. denied)
```

- **District** — the intermediate court is identified by its seat after the
  em dash: `Tex. App.—Houston [14th Dist.]`, `Tex. App.—Austin`,
  `Tex. App.—San Antonio`, `Tex. App.—Tyler`, etc. (Houston has two districts,
  designated `[1st Dist.]` and `[14th Dist.]`.)
- **Petition-history parenthetical** (a Texas signature — the disposition of
  any petition for review to the Supreme Court of Texas):
  - **`no pet.`** — no petition for review was filed.
  - **`pet. denied`** — the Supreme Court denied the petition for review (the
    court of appeals judgment stands; "denied" carries a different
    precedential nuance than "refused").
  - **`pet. ref'd`** — the petition was refused (the Supreme Court treats the
    court of appeals opinion as **correct** — historically the strongest
    writ-history designation).
  - Other dispositions: `pet. dism'd`, `pet. dism'd w.o.j.`, `pet. granted`,
    and the **older writ-history** forms for pre-1997 cases (`writ ref'd`,
    `writ ref'd n.r.e.`, `no writ`). Confirm the correct era's terminology.
- **Pinpoint** — `123 S.W.3d 456, 459 (Tex. App.—Dallas 2003, pet. denied)`.

> **Verify the petition history.** The writ/petition-history parenthetical is
> easy to get wrong and signals precedential weight — confirm it against the
> reporter or CourtListener before relying on the cite. Worked examples here
> are **form only**; verify any specific cite before placing it in a filing.

## Statutes

Cite the named Texas code and section with the section symbol:

```
Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.004
Tex. Fam. Code § 154.125
Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17.50
Tex. Fin. Code § 392.101
Tex. Prop. Code § 41.001
Tex. Gov't Code § 662.003
```

- Use `§` for a single section and `§§` for a range or multiple sections.
- Subsections and subdivisions in parentheses: `Tex. Bus. & Com. Code
  § 17.46(b)`.
- Common code abbreviations: **CPRC** = Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code; the DTPA
  lives in **Tex. Bus. & Com. Code**; the TDCA lives in **Tex. Fin. Code**.

## Rules

Cite the relevant Texas rule set by its standard abbreviation and rule number:

```
Tex. R. Civ. P. 166a            (Texas Rules of Civil Procedure)
Tex. R. Civ. P. 166a(i)         (no-evidence summary judgment)
Tex. R. Civ. P. 91a             (baseless-cause-of-action dismissal)
Tex. R. Civ. P. 500–510         (Justice Court — Part V)
Tex. R. Evid. 803(6)            (Texas Rules of Evidence)
Tex. R. Evid. 902(10)
Tex. R. App. P. 26.1            (Texas Rules of Appellate Procedure)
```

- In-text shorthand "**TRCP**" and "**Tex. R. Evid.**" is common in briefing;
  use the full Greenbook form in formal citations.

## Constitutional and other authority

- **Texas Constitution**: `Tex. Const. art. I, § 19`.
- **Court of Criminal Appeals** opinions: `Tex. Crim. App.` (the criminal
  court of last resort) — distinct from `Tex.` (Supreme Court of Texas, civil).

## Practical notes

- **Always verify a citation** against `online-sources.md` /
  `legal-data-apis.md` (CourtListener Texas courts, the txcourts.gov opinions
  pages) before relying on it — including the **petition-history
  parenthetical**, which reflects precedential weight.
- When a court of appeals opinion's petition history is not yet final, note the
  pendency and update the parenthetical when the Supreme Court of Texas acts.
