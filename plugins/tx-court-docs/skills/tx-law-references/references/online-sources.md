# Texas law — canonical online sources (human-facing)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** URL catalog only. Sites and paths change; confirm you
> are on the official source and that the content is current before relying on
> it.

## Statutes — Texas Statutes

- **Texas Statutes (Texas Legislature)**:
  `https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/`
  - Browse by code. Codes load-bearing for this plugin: **Civil Practice &
    Remedies Code** (limitations Ch. 16; proportionate responsibility Ch. 33;
    UDJA Ch. 37; attorney fees Ch. 38; sanctions Ch. 9/10; judgments &
    enforcement Ch. 31/34; unsworn declaration § 132.001); **Finance Code**
    (Texas Debt Collection Act Ch. 392; consumer loans Ch. 342); **Business &
    Commerce Code** (DTPA Ch. 17; Texas UCC); **Property Code** (eviction
    Ch. 24; homestead Ch. 41; personal-property exemptions Ch. 42; residential
    tenancies Ch. 92); **Government Code** (legal holidays § 662.003; court
    organization Ch. 24/25/26; justice courts Ch. 27); **Family Code** (divorce
    Ch. 6; property Ch. 3/7; conservatorship Ch. 153; support Ch. 154;
    maintenance Ch. 8; UCCJEA Ch. 152; UIFSA Ch. 159; protective orders
    Title 4).
  - Section pages have stable, parseable HTML; the docs are also published as
    per-code PDFs. The legislature's bill history is at
    `https://capitol.texas.gov/`.

## Court rules — TRCP / Tex. R. Evid. / Tex. R. App. P.

- **Texas Judicial Branch (txcourts.gov) — Rules & Forms**:
  `https://www.txcourts.gov/rules-forms/rules-standards/`
  - The **Texas Rules of Civil Procedure**, **Texas Rules of Evidence**, and
    **Texas Rules of Appellate Procedure** are published by the **Supreme Court
    of Texas** and posted as PDFs on the Judicial Branch site, along with
    **pending and recently adopted rule amendments** (check the amendments /
    miscellaneous-docket pages before relying on a rule's current text).
  - The **Texas Rules of Judicial Administration** (incl. Rule 10 technology /
    e-filing standards) are on the same site.

## Self-service, forms, and e-filing

- **TexasLawHelp.org** (`https://texaslawhelp.org/`) — the Texas Legal
  Services Center / State Bar self-help portal: statewide fillable forms
  (divorce, SAPCR, debt-claim answer, eviction, **Statement of Inability to
  Afford Payment of Court Costs**), guided interviews, and self-represented
  litigant instructions.
- **eFileTexas** (`https://efiletexas.gov/`) — the statewide electronic-filing
  portal (Tyler Technologies **Odyssey File & Serve**), under **TRCP 21(f)**.
  E-filing is **mandatory for attorneys** in civil cases in all courts;
  **self-represented filers may e-file** and are encouraged to. Justice courts
  also file through eFileTexas.
- **County and district clerks** — each county publishes local filing
  instructions, local rules, and fee schedules. In a Texas county: the
  **District Clerk** handles district-court (and felony) filings; the **County
  Clerk** handles County Courts at Law, probate, and misdemeanors; each
  **Justice of the Peace** precinct has its own clerk. Search "<county>
  district clerk Texas" / "<county> JP precinct."

## Appellate opinions

- **Texas Judicial Branch — Supreme Court & Courts of Appeals opinions**:
  the Supreme Court of Texas opinions and orders, and the 14/15 intermediate
  Courts of Appeals opinions, are published through txcourts.gov
  (`https://www.txcourts.gov/`) and the individual court pages. The **Court of
  Criminal Appeals** (criminal court of last resort) publishes separately.

## Regulators

- **Office of Consumer Credit Commissioner (OCCC)**:
  `https://occc.texas.gov/` — regulates consumer credit in Texas (consumer
  loans under Finance Code Ch. 342, debt management, and related licensees);
  publishes licensee lookups, complaint intake, and regulatory guidance.
- **Texas Secretary of State**: `https://www.sos.state.tx.us/` — among other
  things, the filing office for the **third-party debt collector / credit
  bureau surety bond** (Tex. Fin. Code § 392.101). Use the SOS resources to
  check whether a third-party collector has filed the required bond.
- **Texas Office of the Attorney General**: `https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/`
  — Consumer Protection Division; also the **Title IV-D** child-support agency
  that publishes the current child-support guideline figures and the
  net-resources cap.

## Free secondary mirrors (verify against the official source)

- **CourtListener** (Free Law Project) — Supreme Court of Texas, Court of
  Criminal Appeals, and Texas Courts of Appeals opinions:
  `https://www.courtlistener.com/` (see `legal-data-apis.md` for the court
  identifiers and API).
- **Justia / Google Scholar** — convenient full-text search of Texas opinions;
  treat as a finding aid, then confirm the citation, **petition history**, and
  current status against the official opinion.
