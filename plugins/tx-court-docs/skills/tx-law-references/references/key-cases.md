## Default judgment — new trial standard (TRCP 320 / 329b)

- **The *Craddock* test.** A defaulting defendant is entitled to a new trial
  when (1) the failure to answer or appear was **not intentional or the result
  of conscious indifference**, but due to **accident or mistake**; (2) the
  motion sets up a **meritorious defense**; and (3) granting it will **not
  cause delay or otherwise injure** the plaintiff. This is the central
  framework for setting aside a no-answer default. **Texas authority:**
  *Craddock v. Sunshine Bus Lines, Inc.*, 134 Tex. 388, 133 S.W.2d 124 (1939).
  *(Well-established; **confirm** the citation and that the current
  formulation/refinements still control before relying.)*

## Summary judgment standards (TRCP 166a)

- **Traditional 166a(c).** The movant must show **no genuine issue of material
  fact** and entitlement to judgment as a matter of law; evidence is viewed in
  the light most favorable to the non-movant, indulging every reasonable
  inference in its favor. *(Doctrine described. **TODO:** pull and verify the
  controlling Supreme Court of Texas opinion stating the traditional standard

- **No-evidence 166a(i).** After adequate time for discovery, the motion
  asserts there is **no evidence** of one or more essential elements of a claim
  or defense on which the non-movant bears the burden of proof; the non-movant
  defeats the motion by producing **more than a scintilla** of probative
  evidence raising a genuine fact issue. *(Doctrine described. **TODO:** verify

## Pleading-stage dismissal — TRCP 91a

- **Rule 91a "no basis in law or fact."** A cause of action has **no basis in
  law** if the allegations, taken as true, together with reasonable
  inferences, do not entitle the claimant to the relief sought; **no basis in
  fact** if no reasonable person could believe the facts pleaded. *(Doctrine

## Attorney fees — CPRC Chapter 38

- **Recoverability and segregation.** A prevailing party on a contract claim
  may recover reasonable attorney fees under CPRC Ch. 38, but a claimant
  generally must **segregate** recoverable fees from fees incurred solely on
  claims for which fees are not recoverable, unless the legal services are so
  intertwined that segregation is not required. *(Doctrine described. **TODO:**

## Family law — best interest of the child

- **The *Holley* factors.** In a SAPCR / conservatorship determination, the
  **best interest of the child** is decided by weighing a non-exhaustive set of
  factors — including the child's desires, the child's present and future
  emotional and physical needs, any present or future danger, the parental
  abilities of those seeking custody, available programs, the plans for the
  child, the stability of the proposed home, acts or omissions of a parent that
  may indicate the existing relationship is improper, and any excuse for those
  acts or omissions. **Texas authority:** *Holley v. Adams*, 544 S.W.2d 367
  (Tex. 1976). *(Well-established; **confirm** the citation and that the factor
  list as applied is current — courts also apply the statutory best-interest
  factors — before relying.)*

## Family law — community-property characterization

- **Community-property presumption.** Property possessed during or on
  dissolution of marriage is presumed community property (Tex. Fam. Code
  § 3.003); the spouse claiming separate property must rebut the presumption by
  **clear and convincing evidence** (the standard Texas courts apply), often
  through **tracing**. *(Doctrine described; § 3.003 is statutory. **TODO:**

## Consumer protection — DTPA (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Ch. 17)

- **DTPA standing and the producing-cause standard.** To recover under the
  DTPA a plaintiff must be a **"consumer"** (one who sought or acquired goods
  or services by purchase or lease) and must show a laundry-list violation,
  breach of warranty, or unconscionable action that was a **producing cause**
  of damages; **knowing or intentional** conduct supports **additional
  (treble-range) damages** under § 17.50. *(Doctrine described; the elements
  are statutory. **TODO:** pull and verify a controlling Supreme Court of

## Consumer / collection — sworn account & business-records foundation

- **Sworn account (TRCP 185).** A properly verified account is **prima facie**
  proof of the claim; the defendant must file a **sworn denial** (TRCP 93(10) /
  185) to put the account in issue, after which the plaintiff must prove the
  account by competent evidence. *(Doctrine described; the procedure is
  rule-based. **TODO:** verify a controlling Texas opinion on the effect of a
  defective verification / the sworn-denial requirement before citing.)*
- **Business-records / assigned-debt foundation.** In an assigned-debt
  ("debt-buyer") suit, the plaintiff must lay a **Rule 803(6) / 902(10)**
  foundation for the **original creditor's** records; an affidavit from the
  assignee's employee who lacks knowledge of the original creditor's
  record-keeping is the classic foundation/hearsay vulnerability. *(Doctrine
