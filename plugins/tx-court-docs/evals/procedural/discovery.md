# tx-discovery — TRCP 190 control plan + caps + motion to compel

## Prompt

I'm in a Texas district court suit and I want to send the other side
written discovery and figure out what discovery level my case is on.
How many interrogatories can I send, when are responses due, and what
if they don't answer?

## Expected triggers

- `tx-discovery`

## Acceptance criteria

### Control plan / level selection

- [ ] Explains the **TRCP 190 discovery control plan** with **Level 1**
      (expedited / tied to TRCP 169 expedited actions), **Level 2** (the
      default plan), and **Level 3** (court-ordered tailored plan), and
      that Level 1 applies to claims at or below the expedited-action
      ceiling (reading the current ceiling from the corpus)
- [ ] Notes the discovery period day-counts live in TRCP 190 and routes
      them to the corpus rather than reciting them from memory

### Interrogatory cap + response window

- [ ] States the **25-interrogatory cap** (TRCP 190.3 / 197),
      confirming the current cap and what counts toward it against the
      corpus
- [ ] States written-discovery responses are generally due in **30
      days** (with the 33+3 service nuances pointed to the corpus), and
      that requests for admission are **deemed admitted** if not
      answered within the deadline (TRCP 198)

### Enforcement

- [ ] Identifies a **motion to compel / sanctions under TRCP 215** as
      the remedy for non-response, and that produced documents are
      self-authenticated under **TRCP 193.7** unless objected to

## Common failure modes

- Omits the Level 1/2/3 framework or treats discovery as unlimited
- Recites the interrogatory cap or discovery-period days from memory
- Misses the 30-day window or the RFA deemed-admitted rule
