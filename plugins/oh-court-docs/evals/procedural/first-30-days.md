# oh-first-30-days — Answer + Civ. R. 12(B) triage

## Prompt

I just got served with a credit-card collection complaint
in Ohio Common Pleas. Plaintiff is LVNV Funding. The
complaint says I owe $4,800 on a Citibank card. What
should my answer cover, and should I consider a Civ. R.
12(B)(6) motion?

## Expected triggers

- `oh-first-30-days`
- `oh-consumer-debt`
- `oh-draft-motion`

## Acceptance criteria

### Answer

- [ ] Notes 28-day deadline under Civ. R. 12(A)(1)
- [ ] Walks paragraph-by-paragraph denial / admission
      conventions
- [ ] Lists relevant affirmative defenses: **SOL** (R.C.
      2305.06 — 6-yr written contract), **lack of
      standing** (chain of title), **failure to state a
      claim**, **payment**, **accord and satisfaction**,
      **statute of frauds** where applicable
- [ ] Notes compulsory **Ohio CSPA counterclaim** under
      R.C. 1345.09 (treble + mandatory fees)

### 12(B)(6) triage

- [ ] Cites **Civ. R. 12(B)(6)**
- [ ] Cites *Mitchell v. Lawson Milk Co.* or *State ex
      rel. Hanson v. Guernsey Cty. Bd. of Commrs.* as the
      Ohio pleading-standard cite
- [ ] Notes Ohio remains a **notice-pleading** state
      (Twombly/Iqbal NOT adopted)

## Common failure modes

- Wrong answer deadline
- Missing CSPA counterclaim
- Applying Twombly/Iqbal as if Ohio law (it's not)
- Failing to flag the chain-of-title defense
