# Civic Center Courthouse — San Francisco Superior Court

## Overview

The Civic Center Courthouse is the primary courthouse for San
Francisco Superior Court and the venue for all general civil
(unlimited) matters in the City and County of San Francisco.

- **Address**: 400 McAllister Street, San Francisco, CA 94102
- **General information**: (415) 551-3888 (Civil Division)
- **Court website**: **https://www.sfcourts.org**
- **Hours**: Monday-Friday, 8:30 a.m.-4:00 p.m. (clerk's
  filing counter; verify current hours at sfcourts.org)

## Location and surroundings

The Civic Center Courthouse is located in the heart of San
Francisco's Civic Center district, across Fulton Street from
the San Francisco Main Library and one block east of City Hall.

- **BART/MUNI**: Civic Center/UN Plaza BART station is directly
  adjacent (Market and Hyde streets). The station serves the
  BART and multiple MUNI Metro lines. Most attorneys and
  litigants take BART/MUNI.
- **Parking**: On-street metered parking on McAllister and
  nearby streets; the Civic Center Garage (355 McAllister St.)
  is a short walk. Expect limited and expensive parking —
  BART is strongly recommended.
- **Security**: Standard security screening at the main
  entrance (McAllister St. side). Arrive 20-30 minutes early
  to clear security.
- **Accessibility**: Fully ADA-accessible; elevator access
  to all floors.

## Courthouse layout — civil departments

The Civic Center Courthouse is a multi-story building. Civil
departments are on multiple floors:

| Department | Location | Function |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Dept. 302 | 3rd floor | Law and motion (unlimited general civil) |
| Dept. 304 | 3rd floor | General civil (assigned cases) |
| Dept. 305 | 3rd floor | General civil (assigned cases) |
| Dept. 306 | 3rd floor | Complex civil |
| Dept. 610 | 6th floor | Complex civil / class actions |
| Various (2nd floor) | 2nd floor | Probate, family law |

**Note**: Department assignments and locations change. Always
verify the current department assignment on the SFSC case portal
at **https://efiling.sfcourts.org** before appearing.

## Clerk's office

- **Civil Division**: Ground floor, Room 103
- **Filing**: Walk-in filing at Room 103 counter; photo ID
  may be required for self-represented litigants
- **Fee payment**: Cash, check, money order, or credit card
  at the filing counter; ePayment for eFiled documents
- **Copies and certification**: Request at the clerk's counter;
  certified copies typically processed same-day or next-day
- **Self-help center**: Room 103 also houses materials for
  self-represented litigants; free Judicial Council forms
  available

## Hall of Justice (criminal / limited civil / UD)

A second SFSC courthouse handles criminal matters and certain
civil actions:

- **Address**: 850 Bryant Street, San Francisco, CA 94103
- **Use**: Criminal arraignments and trials; unlawful detainer
  (small claims and limited civil); traffic; civil harassment
  restraining orders
- **Not for general unlimited civil**: Do NOT file unlimited
  civil cases at the Hall of Justice; they belong at 400
  McAllister

## eFiling — File & ServeXpress

San Francisco Superior Court requires eFiling for represented
parties through **File & ServeXpress (FServeXpress)**:

- **Portal**: **https://www.fileandservexpress.com/**
- **Help center**: SFSC eFiling guide at sfcourts.org/civil-
  division/efiling/
- **Account**: Register a firm account; add cases using the
  SFSC case number (e.g., "CGC-24-123456")
- **Service**: Parties who register in a case receive electronic
  service automatically for subsequent filings; ensure all
  registered parties are on the service list
- **Accepted format**: PDF only; maximum file size 10 MB per
  document (split large filings)
- **Document codes**: Use the drop-down matching the CRC 2.112
  document type (e.g., "Motion," "Declaration," "Proposed Order")

**For self-represented litigants**: eFiling through File &
ServeXpress is voluntary; you may paper-file at Room 103,
Civic Center Courthouse. Bring:
1. Original + 1 copy of each document
2. Filing fee (check, money order, or exact cash) or a completed
   FW-001 fee waiver application
3. A Proof of Service showing you have served all other parties

## Courtroom 302 — physical layout

Dept. 302 (law and motion) is a standard California superior
court courtroom:

- Bench (judge's podium) at front, elevated
- Clerk's station to the judge's right (from the bench)
- Two counsel tables facing the bench
- Moving party traditionally at the table closer to the jury
  box (left side as you face the bench); but check local practice
- Galley seating for approximately 30-50 observers
- Public address system for recording

**Hearing behavior at tentative-ruling hearings**:
- If you contested the tentative: be prepared to deliver oral
  argument; keep it focused on the specific issues you raised
  when contesting
- Do not re-argue the papers from scratch; the judge has read
  them
- 10-15 minutes of argument per side is typical unless the
  judge allows more
- Address the judge as "Your Honor"

## After-hours emergency filings

For TROs and other emergency matters when the clerk's office
is closed:

- The SFSC duty judge can be reached through the main court
  line: (415) 551-3888; follow the emergency instructions
- After-hours eFiling through File & ServeXpress is accepted
  but not stamped until the next business day
