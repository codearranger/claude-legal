# Department 302 — Law and Motion (SFSC)

## Overview

Department 302 is the San Francisco Superior Court's general
civil **law and motion department**. All law and motion matters
for unlimited civil cases not assigned to a dedicated complex-
civil or trial department come through Dept. 302.

- **Location**: Civic Center Courthouse, 400 McAllister St.,
  Room 302 (3rd floor)
- **Clerk**: (415) 551-4012
- **Calendar**: Monday-Friday, 9:00 a.m., court days only
- **Judge**: Assignment changes; check sfcourts.org for the
  current Dept. 302 assignment

## Types of matters heard

Dept. 302 hears:
- Demurrers (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.10 et seq.)
- Motions to strike (Code Civ. Proc., § 435 et seq.)
- Motions to compel discovery responses and further responses
  (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.300, 2031.310, 2033.290)
- Motions for protective orders (Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.060)
- Motions for summary judgment and summary adjudication
  (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c)
- Motions for attorney fees and costs
- Motions to consolidate and coordinate
- All other law-and-motion matters in unlimited civil cases
  unless the case is assigned to a complex-civil or trial dept.

## Reservation system

Before filing a law-and-motion paper, the moving party must
reserve a hearing date through the SFSC reservation system.

**Step 1 — Access the SFSC reservation portal**

**https://www.sfcourts.org/civil-division/reservations/**

Or navigate from sfcourts.org → Civil Division → Reservations.

**Step 2 — Select Dept. 302 and motion type**

Select "Department 302 — Law and Motion" from the department
list. Select the motion type from the dropdown menu:
- Demurrer
- Motion to Strike
- Discovery Motion (compel, protective order, etc.)
- Summary Judgment / Adjudication
- Other Civil Motion

**Step 3 — Select a date and time**

Available dates appear in a calendar. Most hearings are at
**9:00 a.m.** Dates that are greyed out are fully booked or
are non-court days.

**Step 4 — Confirm and record the Reservation ID**

The system issues a **Reservation ID** (also called a reservation
number). Record this ID — it must appear on the face of every
document in the motion set.

Place the Reservation ID in the caption:

```
Date:  March 15, 2025
Time:  9:00 a.m.
Dept.: 302
Judge: Hon. [Current Dept. 302 Judge]
Reservation ID: [XXXXXXXX]
```

**Step 5 — File within the reservation window**

Most SFSC reservations must be followed by filing within 5-7
court days. If you miss the window, the reservation expires
and you must re-reserve.

**Step 6 — eFile through File & ServeXpress**

After reserving, file all motion documents through File &
ServeXpress (https://www.fileandservexpress.com/), selecting
Department 302 in the portal.

## Notice requirements

SFSC Dept. 302 follows the statewide Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b)
notice requirements:

| Service method | Minimum notice before hearing |
|---------------|-------------------------------|
| Electronic service (File & ServeXpress eService) | 16 court days |
| Personal service | 16 court days |
| Overnight courier | 18 court days (16 + 2) |
| First-class mail | 16 court days + 5 calendar days |

**MSJ/MSA**: 75-day notice (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(a));
opposition due 14 days before; reply due 5 days before.

## Tentative-ruling regime (CRC 3.1308; SFSC Local Rule 8.3)

### How it works

1. **Tentative posted**: The Dept. 302 judge posts a tentative
   ruling by **4:00 p.m.** on the court day before the hearing.

2. **Finding the tentative**:
   - Web: **https://www.sfcourts.org/civil-division/tentative-rulings/**
     Search by case number or department (select "302")
   - Phone: Call Dept. 302 at (415) 551-4012 (call after 4:00 p.m.
     on the day before the hearing)

3. **Contest the tentative**:
   - Any party that wants oral argument must:
     (a) Notify **Dept. 302** by calling (415) 551-4012 by
         **4:00 p.m.** the court day before the hearing
     (b) Notify **all other parties** by the same deadline
   - "Contesting" means requesting oral argument; you do not
     need to explain your reason on the call

4. **If no party contests**: The tentative automatically becomes
   the **court's final order** without a hearing. The parties
   do not need to appear.

5. **If any party contests**: The hearing proceeds at 9:00 a.m.
   as scheduled. The judge will hear argument and may adopt,
   modify, or reject the tentative.

### Practical workflow — the day before a hearing

```
By 4:00 p.m. —
  Check sfcourts.org/civil-division/tentative-rulings/

If tentative is UNFAVORABLE to you:
  1. Call Dept. 302: (415) 551-4012
     "My name is [NAME]. I am [counsel for / self-represented]
      [Defendant / Plaintiff] in [case name], Case No. [CGC-24-
      123456]. I am calling to contest the tentative ruling on
      the [motion type] set for tomorrow at 9:00 a.m. and request
      oral argument."
  2. Immediately call or email opposing party/counsel to notify
     them you have contested.
  3. Appear at 9:00 a.m. tomorrow.

If tentative is FAVORABLE to you:
  1. Check again at 8:30 a.m. the morning of the hearing to
     confirm the other side did not contest.
  2. If uncontested: you need not appear. The tentative is
     entered as the order.
  3. If the other side contested (you'll see it on the portal
     or learn from a call): appear at 9:00 a.m.
```

### If you miss the contest deadline

If you fail to call by 4:00 p.m. to contest, the court may
decline to hear oral argument. Some judges will still allow
argument if you appear promptly at 9:00 a.m. and explain the
oversight — but this is discretionary. Do not rely on it.

### After an uncontested favorable tentative

When the tentative becomes the final order, you will typically
need to:
1. Submit a **proposed order** consistent with the tentative (if
   you had not already filed one, or if the tentative modified
   your proposed order)
2. eFile the proposed order through File & ServeXpress with
   document code "Proposed Order for Signature"
3. Serve the signed order on all parties

## Opposition and reply timing

Per Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b):
- **Opposition** due 9 court days before the hearing
- **Reply** due 5 court days before the hearing

**If no opposition is filed by 9 court days before**: the moving
party may note this in the reply or at the hearing. The court
will typically grant an uncontested motion if the moving papers
establish a prima facie basis for relief.

## Complex matters and ADR

For cases assigned to Dept. 302 that also have active ADR
proceedings (SFSC ADR is actively used — see SFSC Local Rules,
Division IV):
- If parties have a pending ADR session, the court may continue
  Dept. 302 hearings to allow ADR to conclude
- Bring the ADR status to the court's attention in the motion
  papers or at the CMC

## Courtesy copies for Dept. 302

SFSC Local Rules (check current version) may require paper
courtesy copies for specific motion types. Common practice:

- For **MSJ motions**: physical courtesy copies are typically
  expected (large exhibit packages are hard to review on screen)
- For **routine discovery motions**: eFiled papers often
  sufficient; check Dept. 302's current standing order at
  sfcourts.org
- Courtesy copies should be: three-hole-punched; tabbed for
  exhibits; labeled with party and case number on a cover page;
  delivered to Dept. 302 the court day before the hearing

## Direct-calendar cases and Dept. 302

Once a case is assigned to a **trial department** (typically
after the CMC), law-and-motion matters may shift to the trial
judge's calendar rather than Dept. 302. Always confirm:
- Whether the case is still in the law-and-motion phase
  (Dept. 302) or trial phase (trial department)
- Use the SFSC case portal to check the case assignment

For cases in the direct-calendar phase (post-CMC, assigned to
a specific trial judge), contact that judge's clerk directly
to schedule law-and-motion hearings — do not use the Dept. 302
reservation system.
