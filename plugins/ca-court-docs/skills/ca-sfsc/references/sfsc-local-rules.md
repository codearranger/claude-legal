# SFSC Local Rules — Civil Practice Summary

The San Francisco Superior Court Local Rules (the "SFSC Local
Rules") supplement the California Rules of Court for matters
filed in San Francisco. The full text is available at:

**https://www.sfcourts.org/civil-division/local-rules/**

Always pull the current version — SFSC updates local rules
periodically. The rules below reflect the general structure;
section numbers may differ slightly in the current version.

## Part 1 — General provisions and case management (Rules 1-3)

| Rule | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 1.1 | Scope; applies to all civil matters in the superior court |
| 2.1 | First paper requirements; designation of counsel of record |
| 2.4 | Related-case designation (form CM-015); mandatory if another pending case involves common issues of law or fact |
| 3.1 | Case management conference (CMC) — scheduled ~180 days after filing |
| 3.2 | CMC statement (CM-110) due 15 calendar days before CMC |
| 3.3 | Trial date setting; continuances require good cause by noticed motion or ex parte (CRC rule 3.1332) |
| 3.4 | Case management orders (CMO) issued at CMC; CMO controls trial date, discovery cutoff, motion cutoff, FSC date |
| 3.5 | Final Status Conference (FSC) — typically 2 weeks before trial; all motions in limine, trial briefs, and jury instructions due |
| 3.6 | Trial assignment; SFSC may transfer case to a different trial department |

**CMC practice in SFSC**:
- CMC statements (form CM-110) must describe ADR status,
  discovery status, estimated trial length, and related cases
- SFSC judges actively promote early ADR at the CMC —
  expect the judge to ask about ADR and order it
- A joint CMC statement filed by both parties is efficient;
  it requires prior agreement with opposing counsel/party

## Part 4 — ADR Program (Rules 4.1-4.12)

SFSC has one of the most active ADR programs among California
superior courts. ADR is strongly encouraged (and often ordered)
at the first CMC.

| Rule | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 4.1 | ADR program overview; participation options |
| 4.2 | Judicial arbitration — mandatory for cases ≤ $50,000 (Code Civ. Proc., § 1141.11); non-binding unless parties stipulate otherwise |
| 4.3 | Voluntary mediation — private mediator or SFSC panel |
| 4.4 | Early Neutral Evaluation (ENE) — neutral evaluator gives a non-binding assessment; particularly effective in contract and commercial disputes |
| 4.5 | Parties must file an ADR Stipulation (form ADR-101) at or before the CMC confirming the ADR method selected |
| 4.7 | ADR completion deadline — typically 8-10 months before trial; specified in the CMO |
| 4.10 | SFSC mediator panel roster; parties may select an SFSC panel mediator or agree on a private mediator |
| 4.11 | If ADR is not completed by the CMO deadline, the court may impose sanctions or advance the trial date |
| 4.12 | Confidentiality of ADR proceedings (Evid. Code, §§ 1115-1128) |

**ADR coordinator**: SFSC ADR Office, (415) 551-3876
**Panel roster**: https://www.sfcourts.org/adr/

### Choosing an ADR method in SFSC

- **Mediation**: Best for cases involving ongoing relationships,
  factual disputes, or where parties are far apart on valuation.
  The SFSC panel includes highly experienced retired judges and
  senior attorneys; cost is typically $200-$400/hour split
  between the parties (less for income-qualified parties).
- **ENE**: Best for cases where a neutral expert assessment of
  the merits would sharpen settlement positions. The evaluator
  is typically a substantive expert in the relevant area of law.
- **Judicial arbitration**: Mandatory for ≤ $50,000 cases;
  binding if both parties so stipulate; non-binding otherwise
  (either party may request a trial de novo within 60 days of
  the arbitration award per Code Civ. Proc., § 1141.20).

## Part 5 — Discovery (Rules 5.1-5.6)

| Rule | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 5.1 | Discovery conference — available before or after discovery disputes arise; either party may request a conference with the court |
| 5.3 | Meet-and-confer for discovery disputes: must be **telephonic or in-person** (email alone is not sufficient under SFSC Local Rule 5.3 unless the parties have agreed in writing to email M&C) |
| 5.4 | Discovery motions — must be accompanied by a Separate Statement (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345) |
| 5.5 | Deadline to file discovery motions: 45 days after service of responses (subject to Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.300(c), 2031.310(c)) |
| 5.6 | Expert discovery — stipulate to exchange dates under Code Civ. Proc., § 2034.230; demand for expert exchange due 70 days before trial (Code Civ. Proc., § 2034.210) |

**SFSC discovery M&C note**: SFSC Local Rule 5.3 requires that
meet-and-confer for discovery disputes be conducted by phone
or in person. A letter or email alone does NOT satisfy the
M&C requirement in SFSC (contrast with courts where a letter
is accepted). If the opposing party refuses to confer by phone,
document that refusal in your declaration.

Standard M&C sequence:
1. Send a detailed letter identifying each deficient response
   and requesting supplemental responses within 15 days
2. If no adequate response, call opposing counsel/party for a
   live M&C conference (document the call in writing afterward)
3. If still unresolved, file the discovery motion with a
   declaration describing both the letter and the phone call

## Part 7 — Law and motion (Rules 7.1-7.9)

| Rule | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 7.1 | Notice requirements — follow Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b): 16 court days for most motions |
| 7.2 | Reservation required via SFSC Civil Reservation System before filing (see `references/dept-302-law-and-motion.md`) |
| 7.3 | Separate Statement required for discovery motions (CRC rule 3.1345) |
| 7.4 | Memorandum page limit: 15 pages for opening brief; 10 pages for reply (CRC rule 3.1113) |
| 7.5 | Demurrers — meet and confer per Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41; declaration of M&C efforts required |
| 7.6 | MSJ — 75-day notice (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(a)); Separate Statement of Undisputed Facts required |
| 7.7 | Motions in limine — schedule through trial department; reserve at FSC |
| 7.9 | Unlawful detainer — expedited schedule at the Hall of Justice (850 Bryant St.); 5 court days to answer |

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 9.1 | MSJ and MSA papers must comply with CRC rule 3.1350-3.1354; Separate Statement of Undisputed Material Facts required; each material fact must cite supporting evidence |

## Part 10 — Complex civil (Rule 10.1)

Cases designated complex under CRC rule 3.400 are assigned to
the SFSC Complex Civil Department (currently Dept. 302 for some
complex matters or a dedicated complex department — check the
case portal for the current assignment).

Complex-designation request: file Judicial Council form CM-010
simultaneously with the complaint.

Typical SFSC complex designations:
- Class actions under CCP § 382
- Construction defect with 10+ plaintiffs
- Securities fraud
- Coordinated proceedings (Code Civ. Proc., § 404)
- Environmental / toxic tort

## Part 11 — Unlawful detainer (Rule 11.1)

UD cases are filed and heard at the **Hall of Justice**, 850
Bryant Street, not at the Civic Center Courthouse. Key rules:
- Defendant has 5 court days to answer (Code Civ. Proc., § 1167)
- Trial within 20 days of request for trial setting
  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1170.5)
- eFiling through File & ServeXpress; separate UD case code

## Filing fees (SFSC)

Same Government Code schedule as all California courts
(Gov. Code, §§ 70600-70679):

- First paper (unlimited civil): $435
- Motion: $60
- MSJ: $500
- Fee waiver: form FW-001 (file simultaneously with complaint
  or first paper); FW-001 is free to file

Check current fees at **https://www.sfcourts.org/fees/**. SFSC
may impose a Complex Litigation Surcharge of $20 for cases
designated complex.

## Electronic service rules (SFSC / CRC)

Parties who eFile through File & ServeXpress consent to
electronic service for all subsequent documents in the case
(Code Civ. Proc., § 1010.6; CRC rule 2.251). For parties
not registered for eService (uncommon in counseled cases;
may occur with pro se opposing parties), serve by mail (+5
calendar days notice) or personal service.

## Judicial Council forms (statewide)

SFSC uses the same Judicial Council forms as all California
courts. Key civil forms:
- **CM-010**: Civil Case Cover Sheet (complex designation)
- **CM-110**: Case Management Statement
- **POS-030**: Proof of Service (Civil)
- **FW-001**: Application for Waiver of Court Fees and Costs
- **ADR-101**: Stipulation to ADR and Delay of Initial CMC
- **CIV-050**: Demurrer (checklist)

Forms available at: **https://www.courts.ca.gov/forms.htm**
