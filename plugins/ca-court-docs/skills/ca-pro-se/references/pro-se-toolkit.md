# Pro Se Toolkit — California

## Judicial Council forms

### Overview

California Superior Courts use **Judicial Council of California**
standardized forms for most civil filings. Under California Rules of
Court, rule 1.35, forms designated as "Mandatory" must be used exactly
as formatted. Forms designated as "Optional" may be substituted with
a pleading in your own format that contains all required information.

All current forms are available at:
https://www.courts.ca.gov/forms.htm

Forms are searchable by form number or keyword. Download the current
version before filing — forms are updated periodically and courts may
reject an outdated version.

### Frequently used civil forms

**Case initiation:**
- **SUM-100** — Summons (mandatory; accompanies complaint)
- **CIV-110** — Request for Dismissal

**Service:**
- **POS-030** — Proof of Service by First-Class Mail (mandatory
  form for mail service attestation)
- **POS-040** — Proof of Personal Service (mandatory for initial
  service of summons)
- **POS-010** — Proof of Service of Summons (for the sheriff's
  return or process server's proof)

**Declarations and attachments:**
- **MC-025** — Attachment (use when a form doesn't have enough
  space; check "Additional pages attached" box on the main form
  and attach MC-025 with the continuation)
- **MC-031** — Declaration (a general-purpose blank declaration
  form; fill in the case information at top)

**Default proceedings:**
- **CIV-100** — Request to Enter Default (mandatory)
- **CIV-140** — Application and Declaration for Default Judgment
  by Court (for small-claims-type amounts without hearing)
- **JUD-100** — Proposed Judgment (attach to default judgment
  request)

**Post-judgment collection:**
- **EJ-001** — Abstract of Judgment — Civil and Small Claims
- **WG-001 through WG-035** series — Earnings Withholding
  (Wage Garnishment) Order
- **EJ-130** — Writ of Execution
- **EJ-170** — Notice of Opposition to Claim of Exemption

## In Forma Pauperis — Fee Waiver

### What it covers

Under California Government Code sections 68630–68641, courts must
waive initial filing fees for qualifying litigants. The waiver
covers:

- **Court filing fees** (complaint, answer, cross-complaint,
  appeal)
- **Reporter's fees** for hearings and trials
- **Jury fees** (jury deposit)
- **Sheriff's fees** for service of summons (Code Civ. Proc.,
  § 68637(e))
- **Other fees and costs** specified in Government Code section
  68637

The waiver does **not** cover the opposing party's attorney fees
if you lose, or expert witness fees. It is purely a court-fee
waiver.

### Income and asset eligibility

Automatic qualification (Govt. Code, § 68632):

- Receiving: Medi-Cal, SSI/SSP, CalFresh (SNAP), CalWORKs
  (TANF), County Indigent Health Care, In-Home Supportive Services
  (IHSS), or Supplemental Security Income
- Income at or below **125% of the federal poverty level**

Means-test qualification (Govt. Code, § 68633):

- Monthly income minus monthly essential expenses leaves less
  than $1,500 per month (or similar threshold — check current
  Judicial Council guidelines); or
- Annual income under 150% of federal poverty level

The clerk makes the initial determination. If denied, you may
request a hearing before a judge.

### The forms

**FW-001 — Request to Waive Court Fees** (mandatory form)

This is the application. File it at the clerk's window along
with or before your first filing.

- Answer all questions completely — incomplete applications are
  returned
- Attach supporting documentation (pay stubs, bank statements,
  proof of benefits) if the clerk requests it
- The court must rule on the application within 5 court days
  (Govt. Code, § 68634(a))

**FW-003 — Order on Court Fee Waiver** (the court's ruling)

The clerk or judge signs FW-003 granting or denying the waiver.
Keep a copy — some clerks require you to present it at each
subsequent filing to confirm the waiver is still in effect.

**FW-010 — Request to Waive Additional Court Fees** (for ongoing
fees after the initial waiver — e.g., jury fees when the case
approaches trial)

### After the waiver is granted

- The waiver applies to all **court** fees in the same case
- If your financial situation changes materially, the court may
  reconsider (Govt. Code, § 68636)
- If you recover money or property in the case, the court may
  order repayment of the waived fees from the recovery
  (Govt. Code, § 68637(b))
- The waiver is **case-specific** — re-apply if you file a new
  case

### If the waiver is denied

File a Request for Hearing on Order re: Court Fees (FW-006)
within 10 days. You get a hearing before a judge, not just a
clerk review.

## Self-Help Centers

Every California Superior Court is required to maintain a Self-Help
Center under Government Code section 68651. Staff at Self-Help
Centers:

- Provide **procedural** assistance with forms
- Answer **procedural** questions ("which form do I file?", "what is
  the filing deadline?")
- Do **not** provide legal advice about your case's merits
- May review your completed forms for completeness

**These centers are free and open to the public regardless of
income.** You do not need to qualify for a fee waiver to use them.

| Compel discovery | Motion to Compel Further Responses | Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.300, 2031.310, 2033.290 |
| Set aside a default | Motion to Set Aside / Vacate Default | Code Civ. Proc., § 473 |
| Win before trial | Motion for Summary Judgment | Code Civ. Proc., § 437c |
| Add a defense / claim late | Motion for Leave to Amend | Code Civ. Proc., § 473(a) |
| Obtain a protective order | Motion for Protective Order | Code Civ. Proc., § 2031.060 |
