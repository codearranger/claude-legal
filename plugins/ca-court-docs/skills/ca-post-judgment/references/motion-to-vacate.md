# California Motion to Vacate Default or Judgment
## Code Civ. Proc., § 473 — Checklist and Template

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify against current rules and case
> law before filing.

---

## When to use this reference

Use this reference when a default, default judgment, or order of
dismissal has been entered against the defendant and the defendant
seeks relief under Code Civ. Proc., § 473.

---

## Two branches of § 473(b)

### Branch 1: Mandatory relief (attorney-fault affidavit)

**Statutory text** (abbreviated): "[T]he court shall, whenever an
application for relief is made no more than six months after entry
of judgment, is in proper form, and is accompanied by an attorney's
sworn affidavit attesting to his or her mistake, inadvertence,
surprise, or neglect, vacate any (1) resulting default entered by
the clerk against his or her client, and (2) resulting default
judgment or dismissal entered against his or her client, unless
the court finds that the default or dismissal was not in fact
caused by the attorney's mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or
neglect." (Code Civ. Proc., § 473(b).)

**Elements to establish:**

1. The attorney's sworn affidavit of fault — must specifically
   identify the nature of the mistake.
2. Application filed within 6 months of entry of the order/judgment.
3. Proposed responsive pleading attached (or proposed answer).
4. Copy of the default judgment or order attached.

**Key cases:**
- *Zamora v. Clayborn Contracting Group, Inc.* (2002) 28 Cal.4th
  249, 257 — "The mandatory provision of section 473(b) requires
  the court to grant relief when a motion is timely made and
  supported by a sufficient attorney affidavit of fault."
- *Hu v. Fang* (2002) 104 Cal.App.4th 61 — attorney must actually
  swear that the default or dismissal resulted from the attorney's
  own mistake; self-serving statements insufficient.
- *SJP Limited Partnership v. City of Los Angeles* (2006) 136
  Cal.App.4th 511 — attorney affidavit that does not specifically
  admit fault is insufficient.

**Fees:** the court shall "direct the attorney to pay reasonable
compensatory legal fees and costs to opposing counsel or parties"
(Code Civ. Proc., § 473(b)).

---

### Branch 2: Discretionary relief (mistake/excusable neglect)

**Standard**: The court may, in its discretion, relieve a party
from a judgment, dismissal, order, or other proceeding taken
against the party through **mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or
excusable neglect**.

**Elements to establish:**
1. Specific ground — which of mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or
   excusable neglect?
2. Filed within 6 months of entry.
3. Diligence — the party moved promptly upon learning of the
   default.
4. **Meritorious defense** — if vacated, the defendant has a
   defense with potential merit.
5. Proposed answer or responsive pleading attached (Code Civ.
   Proc., § 473(b)).

**Key cases:**
- *Rappleyea v. Campbell* (1994) 8 Cal.4th 975, 981–982 —
  "Notwithstanding the strong policy in favor of trial on the
  merits, the court has discretion to deny relief where the
  moving party fails to demonstrate a sufficiently meritorious
  defense or the lack of diligence." (Paraphrased from context.)
- *Huh v. Wang* (2007) 158 Cal.App.4th 1406 — pro se defendant's
  misunderstanding of procedural rules may support excusable neglect
  if reasonable.
- *Fasuyi v. Permatex, Inc.* (2008) 167 Cal.App.4th 681 —
  defendant must show both excusable neglect and a meritorious
  defense; one without the other is insufficient.

---

### Void judgment — § 473(d) (no time limit)

A void judgment may be set aside at any time. A judgment is void
if the court lacked personal jurisdiction (e.g., defective
service) or subject-matter jurisdiction. File as a motion under
§ 473(d); no 6-month cap.

---

## 6-month deadline computation

The 6-month period runs from entry of the judgment, dismissal, or
order — not from when the debtor learned of it (for discretionary
relief). To check the date:

1. Pull the case docket on the relevant county superior court
   online portal.
2. Identify the date the default was entered by the clerk (Code
   Civ. Proc., § 585(a)) or the default judgment was entered
   (§ 585(b)).
3. Count 6 calendar months forward. If the 6-month anniversary
   falls on a weekend or court holiday, the deadline extends to
   the next court day (Code Civ. Proc., § 12).

**Warning**: courts treat the 6-month cap as jurisdictional for
discretionary relief. File before the deadline, not on it.

## Checklist — mandatory relief (attorney fault)

- [ ] Identify the exact date of default or dismissal entry.
- [ ] Confirm filing is within 6 months.
- [ ] Draft attorney's sworn affidavit of fault (attorney must
      sign, not the client).
- [ ] Attach proposed responsive pleading (answer, demurrer, etc.).
- [ ] Attach copy of the order/judgment to be vacated.
- [ ] File motion + notice + declaration + proposed order.
- [ ] Pay filing fee (or apply for fee waiver — Forms FW-001/003).
- [ ] Serve on all parties.

---

## Checklist — discretionary relief (client)

- [ ] Identify the exact date of default or dismissal entry.
- [ ] Confirm filing is within 6 months.
- [ ] Draft detailed client declaration explaining:
      - The specific circumstances of the mistake/neglect.
      - When the client first learned of the default.
      - What the client did promptly upon learning.
      - The substantive defense(s) the client has on the merits.
- [ ] Attach proposed answer (showing meritorious defense).
- [ ] Draft memorandum of points and authorities citing § 473(b)
      and key cases.
- [ ] Attach copy of the complaint and the default/judgment.
- [ ] File motion + notice + declaration + memorandum + proposed
      order + proof of service.
- [ ] Serve on all parties under Code Civ. Proc., § 1011 et seq.

## Motion structure

```
SUPERIOR COURT OF CALIFORNIA
COUNTY OF [COUNTY]

[PLAINTIFF],
             Plaintiff,             Case No. [NUMBER]

    v.                              DEFENDANT'S NOTICE OF MOTION
                                    AND MOTION TO VACATE DEFAULT
[DEFENDANT],                        JUDGMENT;
             Defendant.             MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND
                                    AUTHORITIES; DECLARATION OF
                                    [DEFENDANT]

Hearing Date: [DATE]
Time:         [TIME]
Dept.:        [DEPT.]
```

**Body structure:**
1. Notice of motion (1 paragraph)
2. Motion (relief requested)
3. Memorandum of Points and Authorities
   - I. Introduction (1–2 paragraphs)
   - II. Statement of Facts (numbered, each with record cite)
   - III. Legal Standard (§ 473(b) text)
   - IV. Argument (ground + diligence + meritorious defense)
   - V. Conclusion
4. Declaration of Defendant
5. Exhibit A: Proposed Answer
6. Exhibit B: Copy of default judgment
7. [Exhibit C: Attorney affidavit of fault, if mandatory relief]
8. Proposed Order
9. Proof of Service

---

## After the hearing

If the motion is granted:
- The default or judgment is vacated.
- The defendant's proposed answer is deemed filed as of the
  hearing date.
- Discovery begins anew from the answer date.
- The case proceeds on the merits.

If the motion is denied:
- File a notice of appeal within 60 days of service of written
  notice of entry of order (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 8.104).
- Consult an attorney regarding the merits of the appeal.
