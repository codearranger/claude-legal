# Satisfaction of Judgment — California
## Code Civ. Proc., §§ 724.010–724.260 and Form EJ-100

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify against current rules and case
> law before filing.

---

## Overview

When a money judgment is paid in full (or partially satisfied),
California law requires the judgment creditor to file an
**Acknowledgment of Satisfaction of Judgment** (Form EJ-100).
The debtor can compel this by making a written demand; if the
creditor fails to file within 15 days, the debtor may recover
statutory damages.

## When the judgment is paid in full

### Creditor's obligation (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.050)

Upon payment in full of the judgment (including principal,
interest, and costs), the judgment creditor must:

1. **Immediately execute** an acknowledgment of satisfaction
   of judgment (Form EJ-100).
2. **File** it with the court that entered the judgment.
3. **Record** it in any county where an abstract of judgment
   was recorded (which placed a lien on real property).

Without a formal demand, there is no hard statutory deadline for
the creditor to voluntarily file. In practice, many creditors
file immediately upon receipt of payment because they want to
remove their obligation.

---

## The 15-day demand procedure (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.050)

If the creditor has not spontaneously filed, the debtor (or
debtor's attorney) may send a written demand:

### Demand letter requirements

The demand letter must:
1. Be in writing.
2. Identify the judgment (court, case number, date of judgment,
   amount originally entered).
3. State that the judgment has been paid in full (or describe
   the partial payment).
4. Request that the creditor execute and file Form EJ-100.
5. Cite Code Civ. Proc., § 724.050.

**Delivery**: personal delivery or mail to the creditor and/or
the creditor's attorney of record.

### Demand letter template

```
[Date]

To: [Judgment Creditor Name and Address]
    [Creditor's Attorney, if any]

Re: Demand for Acknowledgment of Satisfaction of Judgment
    [Court Name], Case No. [Number]
    Judgment entered [Date], in favor of [Creditor] against
    [Debtor], for $[Amount]

Dear [Creditor / Counsel]:

Pursuant to Code of Civil Procedure section 724.050, I hereby
demand that you execute and file an Acknowledgment of
Satisfaction of Judgment (Judicial Council Form EJ-100)
within 15 days of your receipt of this demand.

The above-captioned judgment was paid in full on [Date of
Payment]. Proof of payment is attached as Exhibit A.

If you fail to file the Acknowledgment within 15 days of
receipt of this demand, I will seek the remedy prescribed by
Code of Civil Procedure section 724.080, including statutory
damages of $100 plus any actual damages I sustain as a result
of your failure.

Please send a copy of the filed Acknowledgment to me at:
[Debtor's name, address, phone, email]

Respectfully,
[Debtor's Name / Attorney]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

---

### 15-day deadline

After receipt of the demand (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.050(d)):
- The creditor has **15 days** to execute, file, and deliver a
  copy of Form EJ-100 to the judgment debtor.
- If the creditor has not filed within 15 days, the debtor's
  remedies activate.

---

## Penalty for failure (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.080)

If the creditor fails to file the Acknowledgment within 15 days
of a valid written demand:

- **$100 statutory damages** (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.080(a)).
- **Actual damages** sustained by the debtor as a result of the
  failure (e.g., credit damage from the unsatisfied judgment,
  costs of a motion to compel satisfaction).

The debtor recovers these amounts by:
1. Filing a motion for an order requiring the creditor to file
   the acknowledgment (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.260).
2. Simultaneously requesting the $100 statutory penalty and
   actual damages.

---

## Motion for order of satisfaction (Code Civ. Proc., § 724.260)

If the creditor refuses or fails to file within the 15-day
window, the debtor may file:

**Motion for Order Declaring Judgment Satisfied**

Structure:
1. Notice of motion
2. Memorandum of points and authorities
   - Statement of judgment (case number, date, amount)
   - Evidence of full payment (receipts, bank records, payoff
     letter from creditor)
   - Evidence of written demand and failure to respond
   - § 724.080 statutory penalty request
3. Declaration of debtor (sworn, with payment evidence)
4. Exhibit A: Copy of judgment
5. Exhibit B: Proof of payment (cancelled check, wire
   confirmation, payoff letter)
6. Exhibit C: Demand letter (with proof of delivery)
7. Exhibit D: Creditor's non-response (or inadequate response)
8. Proposed Order
9. Proof of Service

## Form EJ-100 (Acknowledgment of Satisfaction of Judgment)

Available at: courts.ca.gov/forms (search "EJ-100").

The form covers:
- **Full satisfaction** — judgment paid in full.
- **Partial satisfaction** — specify the amount paid; the
  remaining balance owed.
- **Matured installment** — one or more installments are paid.

The creditor (or creditor's authorized agent) must sign the
form. If the creditor is a corporation, an authorized officer
must sign.

The signed Form EJ-100 is:
1. Filed with the court clerk.
2. If an abstract of judgment was recorded, also recorded with
   the county recorder in each county where the abstract was
   recorded (to release the judgment lien).

---

## Recording the release (judgment lien)

If the judgment creditor recorded an **abstract of judgment**
(Form EJ-001) in the county recorder's office (which created a
lien on the debtor's real property in that county), the debtor
must also ensure the **acknowledgment of satisfaction is
recorded** in the same county to release the lien.

If the creditor refuses to record the release, the debtor can
record a certified copy of the court's order of satisfaction
(Code Civ. Proc., § 724.260(e)).
