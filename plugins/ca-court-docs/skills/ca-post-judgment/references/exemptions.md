# California Exemption Catalog
## Code Civ. Proc., §§ 703.140 and 704.010–704.995

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Dollar amounts are periodically adjusted
> by statute. Verify current amounts against leginfo.legislature.ca.gov
> before relying on this reference.

---

## Overview

California has **one exemption system** that applies in California
state court enforcement proceedings: the "California system" under
Code Civ. Proc., §§ 704.010–704.995. The "bankruptcy exemptions"
listed in Code Civ. Proc., § 703.140 are available **only** to
debtors who file for federal bankruptcy — they cannot be used to
resist state court judgment enforcement.

Under California law, debtors may not elect between the California
system and the federal bankruptcy exemptions. (California has
opted out of the federal exemptions under 11 U.S.C. § 522(b)(2).)

## Real property

### Homestead — Cal. Civ. Code § 704.730 (as amended by AB 1885, eff. Jan. 1, 2021)

The homestead exemption protects equity in the debtor's principal
residence:

| Category | Amount |
|---|---|
| Base exemption (all counties) | $300,000 |
| Upper limit (high-cost counties) | $600,000 |

The actual exemption amount equals the **median sale price of
a single-family home in the county where the homestead is
located** in the prior calendar year, with:
- A floor of $300,000, and
- A ceiling of $600,000.

The California Department of Housing and Community Development
publishes annual county median home prices. In most Bay Area and
Southern California counties, the exemption is at or near
$600,000.

**Applies to:** principal residence only — single-family home,
condo, co-op, mobile home, boat (if principal residence).

**Does not protect:** vacation homes, investment properties.

**Automatic homestead** (Code Civ. Proc., § 704.720): declared
automatically; no separate declaration required since 2021. But
filing a homestead declaration in the county recorder still
provides additional protections in some circumstances.

## Personal property

### Motor vehicle — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.010

| Category | Exemption amount |
|---|---|
| Motor vehicle equity | $3,625 |
| Motor vehicle (modified for disability) | $5,525 |

One motor vehicle per debtor. Equity = fair market value minus
amount owed on any lien. The exempt amount applies to the
debtor's equity, not the vehicle's full value.

### Household items — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.020

Ordinary and reasonably necessary household furnishings,
appliances, clothing, and food needed for debtor and their
family: **$9,525 aggregate value** (as of recent revision).

### Health aids — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.050

Prescribed health aids (wheelchair, prosthetics, hearing aids,
CPAP, etc.) for the debtor or a dependent: **unlimited**.

### Tools of trade — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.060

Tools, implements, instruments, materials, uniforms, books,
equipment, and vehicles (commercial only) **reasonably
necessary to and actually used by the debtor in the exercise of
the trade, business, or profession by which the debtor earns a
livelihood**:

| Category | Exemption |
|---|---|
| Tools, instruments, equipment, books | $8,725 |
| One commercial vehicle used for trade | $4,850 |
| Farm tools and crops | $8,725 |

### Personal items — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.040

Jewelry, heirlooms, and works of art: **$9,525 aggregate**.

### Bank account (wages) — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.080

Wages paid directly into a deposit account within the preceding
**30 days**: protected up to **$1,826** minimum. This is a
rolling protection that covers wages deposited in the 30-day
period prior to the levy.

## Retirement and financial accounts

### Pension plans and IRAs — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.115

| Category | Exemption |
|---|---|
| ERISA-qualified plans (401(k), 403(b), pension, profit-sharing) | Unlimited |
| IRAs (traditional and Roth) | Up to $1,512,350 (adjusts with ERISA cap) |
| Public employee retirement (CalPERS, CalSTRS) | Unlimited (separate statutes) |
| Keoghs (self-employed) | Unlimited if ERISA-equivalent |

### Social Security / federal benefits — federal law

Social Security, SSI, federal Railroad Retirement, Veterans
benefits, SSDI: **fully exempt** under federal law (42 U.S.C.
§ 407; 42 U.S.C. § 1383(d); 38 U.S.C. § 5301). California
courts recognize the federal preemption.

## Income-based exemptions

### Workers' compensation — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.160

Benefits, claims, awards, and compensation under workers'
compensation law: **unlimited**.

### Unemployment compensation — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.120

Unemployment insurance and disability insurance benefits under
the Unemployment Insurance Code: **unlimited**.

### Disability and health insurance — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.130

Disability and health insurance benefits for the debtor and
their dependents: **unlimited**.

### Public assistance — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.170

Aid from county, state, or federal government (CalWORKs, SNAP/
CalFresh, Medi-Cal, SSI, TANF, GA/GR): **unlimited**.

---

## Insurance and other protected property

### Life insurance proceeds — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.100

Matured proceeds of life insurance if the insured was a member
of the debtor's household (upon death): **unlimited** if
reasonably necessary for support.

### Loan value of life insurance — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.100(b)

Loan value of unmatured life insurance: **$13,975**.

### Annuities (periodic payment) — Code Civ. Proc., § 704.100(c)

Periodic payments from annuities or pension plans needed for
support: **unlimited if reasonably necessary**.

## Claiming an exemption in enforcement proceedings

1. File **Form EJ-160** (Claim of Exemption — Property) or
   **Form WG-006** (Claim of Exemption — Earnings) within
   **10 days** of service of notice of levy or earnings
   withholding order.
2. Attach financial statement if required (Forms EJ-165 or
   WG-007).
3. File with the **levying officer** (sheriff or marshal),
   not the court directly.
4. Serve a copy on the judgment creditor or creditor's attorney
   at the same time.
5. Attend the hearing if the creditor opposes.

---

## Adjustments

California exemption amounts are adjusted periodically by
statute (roughly every 3 years). **Always verify the current
amount** at leginfo.legislature.ca.gov before relying on a
specific dollar figure.
