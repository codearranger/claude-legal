# California Garnishment Response
## Earnings Withholding Order and Bank Levy — Procedure and Forms

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify against current rules and case law
> before filing.

---

## Two main forms of post-judgment levy

### 1. Wage garnishment (Earnings Withholding Order)

**Source**: Code Civ. Proc., §§ 706.010–706.154.

**How it works:**

1. Judgment creditor obtains a **writ of execution** (Form EJ-130)
   from the court clerk.
2. Creditor prepares an **Earnings Withholding Order** (Form
   EJ-150) and delivers it to the levying officer (sheriff or
   marshal) with a fee.
3. Sheriff/marshal serves Form EJ-150 on the employer.
4. Employer must begin withholding from the debtor's next paycheck
   after receiving the order (generally within 10 days).
5. The employer withholds and pays to the levying officer; the
   levying officer remits to the judgment creditor.

**Withholding limits** (Code Civ. Proc., § 706.050; 15 U.S.C.
§ 1673):

The amount withheld per pay period is the LESSER of:
- **25% of disposable earnings**, OR
- The amount by which disposable earnings exceed **30 times the
  federal minimum wage** per week (currently $7.25/hr × 30 =
  $217.50/week; for biweekly pay: $435.00; for monthly: $942.50).

"Disposable earnings" = gross pay minus legally required
deductions (federal and state income tax withholding, FICA, SDI,
Medicare — but NOT voluntary deductions such as 401(k)
contributions or health insurance premiums).

**Effect on employer**: Employer must comply. Failure to withhold
after receiving the order exposes the employer to an order to
pay the amount that should have been withheld (Code Civ. Proc.,
§ 706.154).

### 2. Bank levy (Levy on Financial Institution)

**Source**: Code Civ. Proc., §§ 700.140–700.160, 699.510 et seq.

**How it works:**

1. Creditor obtains writ of execution (Form EJ-130).
2. Creditor delivers the writ to the levying officer with
   instructions.
3. Levying officer serves the financial institution.
4. Bank immediately freezes funds in the debtor's accounts up
   to the judgment balance.
5. After the 10-day exemption-claim period, frozen funds are
   delivered to the levying officer (absent a timely exemption
   claim).

**Federal benefit protection (31 C.F.R. § 212)**: Banks are
required to automatically protect 2 months of direct-deposited
federal benefits (Social Security, SSI, VA benefits, federal
Railroad Retirement, SSDI) from the levy — no claim of exemption
required for that protected amount. Funds beyond the 2-month
amount may still be claimed exempt if they retain their federal
benefit character.

## How to respond to a wage garnishment

### Step 1: Identify the order (Form EJ-150)

Upon receiving the earnings withholding order, the employer is
served; the employee (debtor) receives a copy. The debtor must:
- **Calendar the 10-day deadline** from service of the order to
  file a Claim of Exemption.
- Identify what is being garnished.

### Step 2: Determine applicable exemptions

See `references/exemptions.md` for the full list. For wage
garnishment, the most common exemptions:

- **Earnings exemption** (Code Civ. Proc., § 706.051): earnings
  needed to support the debtor and their family may be fully
  exempt if the debtor can show financial hardship.
- **Public benefits (unemployment, CalWORKs, SSI)**: fully exempt
  — wages that are really benefit payments cannot be garnished.
- **Judgment is satisfied or void**: if the debtor already paid
  or the judgment is void, no withholding is authorized.

### Step 3: File Form WG-006 (Claim of Exemption — Earnings)

File within **10 days** of service of the order on the employer:

- **Form WG-006** (Claim of Exemption): identifies the basis for
  the exemption.
- **Form WG-007** (Financial Statement): documents income,
  expenses, dependents, and why the earnings are needed for
  support.

File with the **levying officer** (not the court directly);
the levying officer forwards to the court.

### Step 4: Serve the creditor

Serve a copy of Forms WG-006 and WG-007 on the judgment creditor
or the creditor's attorney at the same time they are filed.

### Step 5: Court hearing

The creditor has **10 days** from service of the claim of
exemption to file a **Notice of Opposition** (Form WG-009). If
no opposition is filed, the earnings withholding order is
modified or terminated per the claim.

If opposition is filed, the court sets a hearing (typically
within 20 days). At the hearing, the debtor presents their
financial showing; the creditor opposes.

## How to respond to a bank levy

### Step 1: Identify the levy notice

The levying officer serves the financial institution and sends
the debtor a notice. The debtor has **10 days** from service of
the notice to file a Claim of Exemption.

### Step 2: File Form EJ-160 (Claim of Exemption)

- **Form EJ-160** (Claim of Exemption for Property Levy): check
  the applicable exemption category.
- **Form EJ-165** (Financial Statement, if required): documents
  financial need.

File with the **levying officer** (Code Civ. Proc., § 703.510).

### Step 3: Serve the creditor

Same-day service on judgment creditor/counsel (Code Civ. Proc.,
§ 703.540(a)).

### Step 4: Hearing

Same procedure as earnings — creditor has 10 days to oppose;
court hearing if opposed (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 703.570–703.580).

## Forms at a glance

| Form | Name | Use |
|---|---|---|
| EJ-130 | Writ of Execution | Obtained by creditor; authorizes levy |
| EJ-150 | Earnings Withholding Order | Served on employer |
| WG-006 | Claim of Exemption (Earnings) | Filed by debtor within 10 days |
| WG-007 | Financial Statement (Earnings) | Accompanies WG-006 |
| WG-009 | Notice of Opposition to Claim of Exemption | Filed by creditor within 10 days |
| EJ-160 | Claim of Exemption (Property) | Filed by debtor for bank levy |
| EJ-165 | Financial Statement (Property) | Accompanies EJ-160 |

All Judicial Council forms: courts.ca.gov/forms
