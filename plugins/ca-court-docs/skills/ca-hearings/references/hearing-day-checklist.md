# Hearing-Day Checklist — California Superior Court

## Day before the hearing

- [ ] Check for tentative ruling on the court's website by
      3:00-4:00 p.m. (posting times vary by court and department)
- [ ] Determine whether to contest the tentative:
      - Adverse tentative → **contest by 4:30 p.m.**
      - Favorable tentative → watch whether other side contests
- [ ] If contesting:
      - Notify the court (LACourtConnect / department phone)
      - Send email to opposing counsel ("I contest the tentative
        and intend to appear and argue")
      - Save confirmation of both notifications
- [ ] Review your motion, opposition/reply, all exhibits, and
      the tentative ruling
- [ ] Outline 2-3 main argument points
- [ ] Outline 2-3 likely judge questions and your answers
- [ ] Prepare your opening statement (case name, your name,
      motion title, relief requested — 15-20 seconds)
- [ ] Prepare your closing statement (the specific order and
      sanctions amount you want — 20 seconds)
- [ ] Write argument notes on a single index card or half-sheet
- [ ] Confirm hearing date, time, courtroom/department
- [ ] Confirm mode (in-person vs. remote) — check standing order
- [ ] If remote: confirm platform (LACourtConnect for LASC; Zoom
      or other for other courts)
- [ ] Write down the backup phone dial-in number for remote hearings
- [ ] If in-person: confirm courthouse address and which entrance
      to use; print driving or transit directions

---

## Morning of the hearing

### If remote

- [ ] Log in to the platform (LACourtConnect / Zoom / Teams)
      15 minutes before hearing time
- [ ] Test audio and video
- [ ] Confirm microphone mutes and unmutes correctly
- [ ] Check camera position (eye level; face lit from front)
- [ ] Have documents open and tabbed (exhibits accessible quickly)
- [ ] Backup dial-in number visible and ready
- [ ] Phone silenced (but available for backup dial-in)
- [ ] Door closed; background quiet; no distractions
- [ ] Dressed professionally from shoulders up

### If in-person

- [ ] Leave home/office early — budget 30 minutes extra for
      courthouse security (especially LASC and SFSC)
- [ ] Arrive at the courthouse 20-30 minutes before hearing time
- [ ] Check the courtroom number (confirm department in advance;
      it may have changed)
- [ ] Go through security (laptop screened; phone through the
      detector)
- [ ] Sign the courtroom appearance sheet if present at the door
- [ ] Sit in the gallery; do not approach counsel table until
      your case is called
- [ ] Phone on silent or off
- [ ] Documents organized and tabbed

---

## At the hearing

### When called

- [ ] Stand immediately when the case is called
- [ ] Approach the designated counsel table (ask clerk if unclear)
- [ ] State appearance for the record: "[Name], In Pro Per, for
      the defendant [or plaintiff]"

### During argument

- [ ] Address the tentative (if adverse, explain why it should
      not be adopted)
- [ ] Make your 2-3 main points — concisely
- [ ] Stop immediately when the judge starts speaking
- [ ] Answer judge questions directly: answer first, then explain
- [ ] Cite record by exhibit letter and declaration paragraph
- [ ] Request rebuttal if needed: "Your Honor, may I briefly
      respond?"
- [ ] Close with the specific relief requested

---

## After the hearing

- [ ] Note the ruling (exact language, with date and department)
- [ ] If unclear, ask for clarification before leaving
- [ ] Thank the judge briefly
- [ ] If prevailing: prepare proposed Order After Hearing within
      the time set by the court (typically 15 days)
      → see ca-submit-order
- [ ] If losing: note deadline for any motion for reconsideration
      (Code Civ. Proc., § 1008 — within 10 days after service of
      written notice of the order; or before entry of judgment)
- [ ] Calendar any compliance deadlines from the ruling
- [ ] Send follow-up email to opposing counsel confirming ruling
      if needed for the record

---

## Checklist notes by court

### LASC-specific

- [ ] LACourtConnect account created and tested (appearances.lacourt.org)
- [ ] Hearing confirmed in LACourtConnect — look for hearing in
      your case list
- [ ] Backup phone number for the LASC department saved

### SFSC-specific

- [ ] Zoom link obtained from case docket or clerk email
- [ ] Backup Zoom dial-in number saved
- [ ] CRS (Court Reservation System) reservation confirmed

### General

- [ ] Working copies delivered (if required by local rule for
      your court and motion type) — typically due by noon 2 court
      days before the hearing (check local rules)
- [ ] Proof of service of all moving papers filed with the court
