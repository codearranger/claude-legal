# Remote Appearances — California Superior Court

## Legal authority

California Rules of Court, rule 3.672 (effective January 1, 2023)
establishes the statewide framework for remote appearances in civil
law-and-motion proceedings. Key provisions:

- Any party or attorney may appear **remotely** for civil law-and-
  motion, case management, and most other civil hearings
- Courts may **not** require in-person attendance for law-and-motion
  absent good cause or a local rule adopted under CRC 3.672(e)
- Courts must offer a remote-appearance option for any hearing at
  which a remote option is available
- Judges retain discretion to require in-person appearance for
  specific hearings (e.g., evidentiary hearings, trials, complex
  summary judgment)

## Platform guide by court

California does not have one statewide platform. Each court (and
often each department) sets its own platform.

### Los Angeles Superior Court (LASC) — LACourtConnect

**Platform:** LACourtConnect (appearances.lacourt.org)
**Required** for LASC civil remote appearances — Zoom or other
platforms are **not accepted** at LASC.

**Setup steps:**

1. Go to https://appearances.lacourt.org/
2. Create an account (one-time setup; free)
3. Provide name, email, phone number, and role (party or attorney)
4. Confirm your email address
5. Log in and search for your case by case number or party name
6. Find the hearing you want to appear for
7. Select "Appear Remotely" and confirm your remote appearance
8. The system will send you a link and audio call-in number

**Day of the hearing:**

- Log in 15 minutes before the hearing time
- Select your case and join the hearing room
- Keep your microphone muted until the judge calls your case
- When called, unmute and state: "[Name], In Pro Per, appearing
  remotely for the defendant in Case No. [No.]"

**Backup (always have this ready):**

- The dial-in phone number for your department is listed in the
  LACourtConnect hearing details. Write it down. If your video
  drops, call in immediately.

**LASC department-specific notes:**

- Some LASC departments default to in-person for complex motions
  (SJ, anti-SLAPP) even if they generally offer LACourtConnect
  for routine motions. Check the standing order.
- LASC posts standing orders for each judge at
  lacourt.org/division/civil (click on your department number).

### San Francisco Superior Court (SFSC) — Zoom

**Platform:** Zoom (court-issued links)
**Link location:** Posted in the case's online docket or sent by
the clerk when the hearing is confirmed.

**Setup steps:**

1. Install Zoom at https://zoom.us/ (free account sufficient)
2. The Zoom link and Meeting ID/Passcode are typically in the
   court's online calendar or sent to parties by email
3. Log in 10 minutes early
4. Use the "waiting room" — the clerk will admit you when the
   judge is ready

**Backup:** Every SFSC Zoom hearing has a dial-in phone number
listed in the meeting invitation. Save it.

---

### San Diego Superior Court — Microsoft Teams / Zoom

San Diego has moved many civil departments to **Microsoft Teams**
for remote appearances, while others use Zoom. Platform varies by
department.

**Find the link:** Check the case docket on the San Diego court's
website (sdfamily.org for family; civil at sdcourt.ca.gov) or
call the department clerk.

**Microsoft Teams setup:**

1. Download the Teams app from microsoft.com/teams (free)
2. You do not need a Microsoft account — join as a guest using
   your name and case number
3. The meeting link will have a "Join as a guest" option

## Technical setup for any remote hearing

### Equipment

- **Use a laptop or desktop computer** rather than a phone for
  video. Camera quality and audio clarity matter more than you
  think — a judge's perception of a litigant who appears as a
  blurry, audio-clipping image is unfavorable.
- **Headphones with a built-in microphone** are strongly
  recommended. They eliminate echo and background noise that
  laptop speakers and microphones create.
- **Ethernet connection** if available — Wi-Fi can drop during
  the hearing. A dropped connection in the middle of argument is
  catastrophic.

### Environment

- **Quiet room.** No background TV, children, pets, or loud
  neighbors. Find the quietest spot available and close the door.
- **Plain background.** A blank wall, bookshelf, or a blurred
  / conservative virtual background. Avoid a background that
  looks unprofessional or cluttered.
- **Lighting.** Face a window or light source — don't sit with
  a window behind you (this creates a silhouette). The judge
  should be able to see your face clearly.

### Camera position

- Position the camera at **eye level** (prop up a laptop on books
  if necessary). A camera angled up from below or down from above
  looks amateurish.
- Look at the **camera lens** when speaking, not at the screen.
  On video, looking at the screen while talking looks like you
  are looking down or away. Eye contact with the camera is the
  equivalent of eye contact with the judge.

---

## Conduct on video

- **Mute when not speaking.** Unmute only when it is your turn
  to speak or when the judge asks you a question.
- **Do not read from notes on screen while looking down.** Keep
  your argument notes either printed next to the camera or on a
  secondary monitor at camera height so your eyes stay near the
  camera level.
- **Do not talk over others.** The audio delay on video can cause
  overlapping speech. Pause slightly longer than you normally
  would before starting to speak.
- **Dress professionally from the camera up.** The judge can see
  you from the shoulders up. Wear a collared shirt or equivalent.
  Business casual minimum — same standard as in-person.
- **Have your documents open.** If the judge asks about a specific
  exhibit, be ready to describe it verbally ("Exhibit A, page 2,
  first paragraph"). Do not screen-share unless the judge
  specifically asks — sharing your screen in a motion hearing is
  generally not expected or welcomed.

## Technical failure protocol

If your audio or video drops during the hearing:

1. **Stay on the line / attempt to reconnect immediately.** Do
   not close the browser or app — attempt to rejoin the same
   meeting link.
2. **Dial in by phone** using the backup number you wrote down
   before the hearing. Identify yourself: "Your Honor, this is
   [Name], In Pro Per for the defendant — I lost video and am
   rejoining by phone."
3. **Text or email the department clerk** if you cannot rejoin
   within 2 minutes. Many departments have a clerk email on the
   court's website.
4. If you cannot reconnect in time: Call the department
   immediately after the hearing and explain. Ask whether the
   matter can be continued. Document the technical failure with
   screenshots or a note of the time and error.

## Common remote-appearance mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Using Zoom for an LASC hearing | LASC only accepts LACourtConnect; appearance will not be registered |
| Not creating a LACourtConnect account before the hearing | Cannot join in time; missed appearance |
| Using phone for video when laptop is available | Audio quality worse; camera angle often bad |
| No backup dial-in number written down | No recovery option when video drops |
| Leaving microphone unmuted while others speak | Background noise disrupts hearing; clerk or judge will mute you |
| Sitting with window behind you | Face appears as silhouette; judge cannot see you clearly |
| Reading from notes while looking down | Appears distracted; poor visual connection |
| No headphones — laptop speakers active | Echo; feedback; audio problems for everyone |
