# Example Filings — California Consumer-Debt Defense

This directory contains **synthetic example filings** for a
California debt-buyer-defense fact pattern. All parties,
case numbers, dates, and amounts are **entirely fictitious**.
These examples are for educational and drafting-reference
purposes only.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** These are drafting models.
> California court requirements change. Verify all format
> rules, local rules, and deadlines before filing any document.

## Fictional fact pattern

All examples use the following fictional scenario:

**Case**: LVNV Funding, LLC v. Jane Doe
**Court**: Los Angeles Superior Court — Limited Civil Division
**Case No.**: 25LCLC01234
**Plaintiff**: LVNV Funding, LLC (a Delaware LLC; a debt buyer)
**Original Creditor**: Bank of America, N.A.
**Alleged debt**: Credit-card account, last 4 digits 5678
**Alleged amount**: $6,847.22
**Date complaint filed**: January 15, 2025
**Charge-off date**: Approximately December 2019
**Date of last payment**: Allegedly November 15, 2020

Note: If the last payment was November 15, 2020, the
4-year SOL under CCP § 337 would expire approximately
November 15, 2024 — six weeks before plaintiff filed suit
on January 15, 2025. This makes the action potentially
time-barred.

**Defendant**: Jane Doe (pro se filer)

## Key California format notes

- **Caption**: Use "SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF
  CALIFORNIA, COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES" (or relevant county)
- **Limited civil vs. unlimited civil**: Cases at or below
  $25,000 (as of Jan 1, 2024) are "limited civil" — CCP
  § 85. Cases over $25,000 are "unlimited civil."
  Limited civil has abbreviated procedures.
- **Separate statement**: Most discovery motions in California
  require a separate statement per Cal. Rules of Court,
  rule 3.1345 — a side-by-side table of each disputed
  request, response, and ground for compel
- **Proof of Service**: California uses "Proof of Service"
  not "Certificate of Service." Judicial Council form POS-030
  (by mail) or POS-040 (electronic service) are the standard
  forms. A narrative proof of service is also acceptable.
- **Penalty of perjury**: California declarations use
  CCP § 2015.5 language: "I declare under penalty of perjury
  under the laws of the State of California that the
  foregoing is true and correct"
- **Signature block**: Pro se defendant signs their own name;
  no bar number; add "In Pro Per" or "Pro Se"

## How to use these examples

1. Read the example that matches the filing you need to draft
2. Adapt the parties, dates, case number, and facts
3. Check current local rules for the specific court
4. Run `ca-statewide-format` to verify format compliance
5. Run `ca-quality-check` before filing
6. Verify all statutory citations against current California
   law
