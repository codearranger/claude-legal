# Example — Proof of Service (California)

Synthetic example. Fictional parties. Not legal advice.
See README.md for the fictional fact pattern.

California uses **"Proof of Service"** (not "Certificate
of Service"). The standard forms are:

- **POS-030**: Proof of Service by Mail
- **POS-040**: Proof of Electronic Service

A narrative Proof of Service is also acceptable. The
examples below show both a narrative format and references
to the Judicial Council forms.

---

## Example 1: Proof of Service by Mail (Narrative)

```
       SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA
              COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

LVNV FUNDING, LLC v. JANE DOE     │  Case No. 25LCLC01234


                 PROOF OF SERVICE BY MAIL
                  (CCP §§ 1013, 1013a)

I, [Name of server — if not a party, state relationship],
declare:

1.   I am over the age of 18 years and not a party to the
     above-captioned action.

     [If defendant is the server: I am the Defendant in
     this action, and I am representing myself in pro per.
     Under CCP § 1013(b), a party representing themselves
     may serve documents by mail.]

2.   My business/residence address is [Address],
     [City, State, ZIP].

3.   On [Date of mailing], I served the following
     document(s) on the parties listed below by placing
     true copies in sealed envelopes with postage fully
     prepaid, addressed as shown below, and depositing
     the envelopes in the United States mail at [City,
     State]:

     DOCUMENT(S) SERVED:

     - Defendant's First Set of Requests for Production
       of Documents and Things (25 pages)
     - Proof of Service

     PARTIES SERVED:

     Plaintiff's Counsel of Record:
     [Attorney Name]
     [Firm Name]
     [Address]
     [City, State, ZIP]

4.   I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws
     of the State of California that the foregoing is
     true and correct.

Executed on [Date], at [City], California.


____________________________________
[Name of Declarant]
[Address]
[Phone]
```

## Example 2: Proof of Electronic Service (Narrative)

```
                PROOF OF ELECTRONIC SERVICE
                    (CCP § 1010.6)

I, [Name], declare:

1.   I am over the age of 18 years and [not a party to /
     the Defendant in] the above-captioned action.

2.   My electronic service address is [email address].

3.   On [Date], I served the following document(s) on
     the parties listed below by transmitting a true copy
     via electronic transmission to the electronic service
     addresses shown, pursuant to CCP § 1010.6 and
     Cal. Rules of Court, rule 2.251:

     DOCUMENT(S) SERVED:

     - Defendant's Notice of Motion and Motion to Compel
       Further Responses to Requests for Production;
       Memorandum of Points and Authorities (22 pages)
     - Declaration of Jane Doe in Support (8 pages)
     - Separate Statement (12 pages)
     - [Proposed] Order Granting Motion to Compel (3 pages)
     - This Proof of Electronic Service

     PARTIES SERVED:

     Plaintiff's Counsel of Record:
     [Attorney Name]
     [Firm Name]
     Email: [attorney email address]

4.   The transmission was completed and was reported as
     complete without error.

5.   I declare under penalty of perjury under the laws
     of the State of California that the foregoing is
     true and correct.

Executed on [Date], at [City], California.


____________________________________
[Name of Declarant]
[Address]
[Email]
```

## Example 3: Using Judicial Council Form POS-030

The Judicial Council's form POS-030 is the standard
Proof of Service by First-Class Mail. It is available at:

https://www.courts.ca.gov/documents/pos030.pdf

**When to use POS-030**:
- Service by U.S. mail of any court document in a
  California civil action

**Required fields on POS-030**:
- Your name and address (as server)
- Case name, number, and court
- Names and addresses of parties served
- Exact description of the documents served
- Date of mailing
- Your signature under penalty of perjury

**Note**: If the server is a party, they must comply with
CCP § 1013(b) — some courts have local rules or judicial
preferences about whether self-service by a party is
acceptable for all documents. Check local rules.

## Example 4: Form POS-040 (Electronic Service)

The Judicial Council's form POS-040 is for proof of
electronic service under CCP § 1010.6.

Available at: https://www.courts.ca.gov/documents/pos040.pdf

**Requirements for electronic service** (CCP § 1010.6):
- The recipient must have agreed to electronic service,
  OR the court must have ordered electronic service
- A self-represented party (like defendant in this
  example) may consent to electronic service by filing
  a consent-to-electronic-service form
- Once consent is given, service by email is valid

## California service rules — quick reference

| Method | Rule | Deadline added |
|--------|------|----------------|
| Mail (in-state) | CCP § 1013(a) | +5 calendar days |
| Mail (out-of-state) | CCP § 1013(a) | +10 calendar days |
| Electronic service | CCP § 1010.6(a)(3) | +2 court days |
| Personal service | CCP § 1011 | None |
| Overnight delivery | CCP § 1013(c) | +2 calendar days |

**Example**: If plaintiff's response to RFPs is due 30
days after service, and you served by mail on March 10,
the 30-day period begins March 15 (+ 5 days for mail),
and the response is due April 14.

## Key features illustrated

1. **"Proof of Service"** (not "Certificate of Service") —
   California terminology

2. **"Not a party" declaration** — required for CCP § 1013
   service; if a party is the server, they must cite the
   specific authority allowing self-service

3. **"Under penalty of perjury under the laws of the
   State of California"** — state-law perjury language
   per CCP § 2015.5

4. **"Executed on [Date], at [City], California"** —
   execution date and location required

5. **Specific document list** — list every document served,
   with page count if available

6. **Service date** — the date of actual mailing or
   electronic transmission
