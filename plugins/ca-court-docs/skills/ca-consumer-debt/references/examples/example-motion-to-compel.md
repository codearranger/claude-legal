(1) Compelling Plaintiff to provide further responses to

    Requests Nos. 1, 3, 4, and 13;

(2) Overruling Plaintiff's objections to those Requests;
    and

(3) Awarding Defendant sanctions in the form of reasonable
    expenses and attorney's fees incurred in making this
    Motion, under CCP §§ 2023.010, 2031.310(h).

This Motion is made on the grounds that: (a) Plaintiff's
objections are without merit; (b) Plaintiff has failed to
provide full and complete responses to discovery going to
the heart of Plaintiff's claims and Defendant's defenses;
and (c) Plaintiff failed to respond in good faith to
Defendant's meet-and-confer efforts.

This Motion is based on this Notice, the accompanying
Memorandum of Points and Authorities, the Declaration of
Jane Doe filed herewith, the Separate Statement filed
herewith, and all records and files in this action.


           MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND AUTHORITIES

I.   INTRODUCTION

     Plaintiff LVNV Funding, LLC has brought this action
     seeking $6,847.22 from Defendant Jane Doe on an
     alleged credit-card account originally issued by
     Bank of America, N.A. Defendant denies owing this
     debt, and asserts that this action is time-barred
     under CCP § 337, that Plaintiff lacks standing for
     failure of chain of title, and that Plaintiff lacks
     a valid DFPI license.

     Defendant served targeted Requests for Production
     seeking the documents that go to the heart of these
     defenses: the cardholder agreement (establishing
     the contract terms), the bills of sale and assignment
     schedules (establishing chain of title), and the DFPI
     license (establishing capacity to sue). Plaintiff
     refuses to produce any of these documents.

     This Court should order full production and impose
     sanctions.

II.  STATEMENT OF FACTS

     1.  Plaintiff filed the Complaint on January 15, 2025,
         alleging that Defendant owes $6,847.22 on an
         account "acquired from Bank of America, N.A."
         (Compl. ¶¶ 3–4.)

     2.  Defendant filed her Answer on February 12, 2025,
         denying the alleged debt and asserting, among
         other defenses: (a) the 4-year statute of
         limitations under CCP § 337; (b) FDBPA pleading
         deficiency; (c) unlicensed debt collection under
         Cal. Fin. Code, § 100002; and (d) defective chain
         of title. (Doe Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. 1.)

     3.  On March 10, 2025, Defendant served First
         Requests for Production Nos. 1–21 targeting
         the cardholder agreement, chain of title
         documents, DFPI license, and collection conduct.
         (Doe Decl. ¶ 3; Ex. 2.)

     4.  On April 9, 2025, Plaintiff served Responses,
         objecting to RFP Nos. 1, 3, 4, and 13 on grounds
         of "not in possession," "trade secret," and
         "relevance." (Doe Decl. ¶ 4; Ex. 3.)

     5.  On April 15, 2025, Defendant sent a meet-and-
         confer letter identifying each deficient response,
         proposing resolutions (including a protective
         order for trade-secret claims), and requesting
         supplementation by May 5, 2025. (Doe Decl. ¶ 5;
         Ex. 4.)

     6.  On April 23, 2025, counsel spoke by phone for
         approximately 25 minutes. Plaintiff's counsel
         stated that Plaintiff would not produce the
         cardholder agreement, would not produce the bills
         of sale without a protective order (but did not
         propose terms), and would not produce DFPI license
         documentation. (Doe Decl. ¶ 6; Ex. 5.)

     7.  Defendant sent a follow-up email on April 23,
         2025, memorializing the call. (Doe Decl. ¶ 6;
         Ex. 5.)

     8.  As of the date of this Motion, Plaintiff has not
         supplemented its Responses as to RFPs 1, 3, 4,
         and 13. The parties are at impasse. (Doe Decl. ¶ 7.)

III. THE DISCOVERY REQUESTS ARE PROPER AND WITHIN SCOPE

     A.  The cardholder agreement (RFP 1) and chain-of-
         title documents (RFPs 3 and 4) are directly
         relevant to Plaintiff's claim and Defendant's
         defenses.

         CCP § 2017.010 defines the scope of discovery as
         any matter that is relevant to a claim or defense
         and proportional to the needs of the case. The
         cardholder agreement and chain-of-title documents
         go to the heart of Plaintiff's claim:

         - The cardholder agreement establishes whether the
           alleged contract existed and its terms
         - The bills of sale and assignment schedules
           establish whether Plaintiff owns the account
           (an essential element of Plaintiff's case)
         - Without the cardholder agreement, Plaintiff
           cannot authenticate the original-creditor records
           under Cal. Evid. Code § 1271

     B.  Plaintiff's "not in possession" objection
         contradicts Plaintiff's Complaint.

         Plaintiff alleges in Paragraph 4 of the Complaint
         that it "acquired the Account from Bank of America,
         N.A." The Bill of Sale and Assignment Schedule
         are the documents evidencing that acquisition.
         A party cannot simultaneously allege ownership
         and disclaim control over the documents that
         establish it.

         "Possession, custody, or control" includes the
         legal right to obtain documents. If Plaintiff's
         bulk sale agreement gives Plaintiff a contractual
         right to access the original account file,
         Plaintiff has "control." See, e.g., Lipton v.
         Superior Court (1996) 48 Cal.App.4th 1599, 1611
         (control includes the right to obtain documents
         on demand).

     C.  Plaintiff's "trade secret" objection as to the
         Bills of Sale is unfounded.

         Plaintiff has not identified what specific
         information in the Bills of Sale constitutes a
         trade secret. The names of sellers and buyers,
         transfer dates, and similar information in a
         Bill of Sale are not trade secrets. If any
         information were genuinely sensitive, a protective
         order under CCP § 2031.060(c) would address it.
         Defendant has offered to negotiate such a
         protective order. Plaintiff has refused without
         explanation.

     D.  DFPI license status (RFP 13) is plainly relevant.

         Defendant's Third Affirmative Defense is that
         Plaintiff lacks a valid DFPI license under Cal.
         Fin. Code, § 100002. Plaintiff's licensing status
         is the central fact in this defense. It is not
         privileged, confidential, or burdensome to produce
         — it is a public-record matter.

IV.  SANCTIONS ARE WARRANTED

     CCP § 2031.310(h) provides that the court shall
     impose monetary sanctions on any party who
     unsuccessfully makes or opposes a motion to compel,
     unless the court finds the losing party's position
     was substantially justified.

     Plaintiff's position on each request is not
     substantially justified:

     - Claiming "not in possession" of the document
       that proves Plaintiff's ownership allegation
       is frivolous
     - Claiming "trade secret" without identifying any
       specific information is a boilerplate objection
     - Claiming "relevance" for a DFPI license when
       Defendant's answer expressly raises non-licensure
       as an affirmative defense is unreasonable

     Defendant requests sanctions in the amount of
     $[N] (approximately [N] hours at $[rate/hour]),
     representing the reasonable value of the time
     spent preparing and filing this Motion.

V.   CONCLUSION

     For the foregoing reasons, Defendant respectfully
     requests that the Court:

     (1) Order Plaintiff to provide full and complete
         further responses to RFP Nos. 1, 3, 4, and 13
         within 14 days of the Order;
     (2) Overrule Plaintiff's objections; and
     (3) Award Defendant sanctions in the amount of
         $[N] under CCP § 2031.310(h).

## Key features illustrated

1. **California limited civil caption** with "LIMITED CIVIL
   DIVISION" header
2. **Notice of Motion** — required separately from
   Memorandum; states date, time, department
3. **Three forms of relief requested** — production +
   overrule objections + sanctions; CCP § 2031.310(h)
4. **Statement of Facts** tied to declarations and
   exhibits (Doe Decl. ¶N; Ex. N)
5. **45-day deadline** — motion filed May 24, 2025 (45 days
   from April 9 responses); this example is within deadline
6. **Trade secret counter**: offered protective order;
   plaintiff declined — that strengthens the motion
7. **Sanctions under § 2031.310(h)**: mandatory unless
   court finds "substantial justification"
8. **Separate statement required**: Cal. Rules of Court,
   rule 3.1345 mandates a separate statement for all
   discovery motions in California; filed as separate document

## Separate statement format

The separate statement (a required separate filing in
California discovery motions) contains, for each disputed
request:

```
REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION NO. 1:
[Full text of the request]

PLAINTIFF'S RESPONSE:
[Full text of the response]

REASON FURTHER RESPONSE SHOULD BE COMPELLED:
[Specific argument for why the response is deficient]
```

See Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345 for exact format
requirements.
