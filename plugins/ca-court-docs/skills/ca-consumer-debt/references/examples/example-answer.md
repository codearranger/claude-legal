       SUPERIOR COURT OF THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA
              COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES
            LIMITED CIVIL DIVISION

LVNV FUNDING, LLC,                 │  Case No. 25LCLC01234
a Delaware limited liability       │
company,                           │  DEFENDANT JANE DOE'S
                                   │  ANSWER, AFFIRMATIVE
          Plaintiff,               │  DEFENSES, AND
                                   │  COUNTERCLAIMS
     vs.                           │
                                   │  [Amount demanded:
JANE DOE, an individual,           │   $6,847.22]
                                   │
          Defendant.               │

                        ANSWER

Defendant Jane Doe, appearing in pro per, answers
Plaintiff's Complaint as follows:

                 GENERAL DENIAL

Pursuant to CCP § 431.30(d), Defendant generally denies
each and every allegation of the Complaint.

            SPECIFIC ADMISSIONS AND DENIALS

1.   PARAGRAPH 1 OF COMPLAINT (Jurisdiction and venue):
     ADMITTED that this Court has jurisdiction and that
     venue may be proper in Los Angeles County. DENIED that
     Plaintiff is "duly authorized to collect debts in
     California," for lack of sufficient information.

2.   PARAGRAPH 2 OF COMPLAINT (Defendant's identity):
     ADMITTED that Jane Doe is an individual residing in
     Los Angeles County.

3.   PARAGRAPH 3 OF COMPLAINT (allegation of account):
     DENIED. Defendant disputes the existence, ownership,
     and amount of the alleged account.

4.   PARAGRAPH 4 OF COMPLAINT (allegation that Plaintiff
     acquired the account by assignment from Bank of
     America): DENIED for lack of information sufficient
     to admit or deny. Defendant has never received
     documentation establishing this alleged assignment.

5.   PARAGRAPH 5 OF COMPLAINT (alleged balance of
     $6,847.22): DENIED. Defendant disputes this amount.

6.   PARAGRAPHS 6–10 OF COMPLAINT (allegations of
     breach and indebtedness): DENIED.

                  AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

FIRST AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Statute of Limitations — CCP § 337)

7.   Plaintiff's cause of action is barred by the statute
     of limitations. The alleged account was last paid
     more than four (4) years before Plaintiff filed this
     action on January 15, 2025. Under CCP § 337, the
     limitations period on a written contract is four years.
     The alleged date of last payment (November 15, 2020)
     preceded the filing of this action by more than four
     years. This action is time-barred.

SECOND AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(FDBPA Pleading Deficiency — Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.58)

8.   Plaintiff is a "debt buyer" within the meaning of Cal.
     Civ. Code, § 1788.50. Plaintiff's Complaint fails to
     include all information required by Cal. Civ. Code,
     § 1788.58, including: (a) the date of charge-off;
     (b) the balance owed at charge-off; (c) the complete
     chain of ownership from the original creditor to
     Plaintiff. Plaintiff's noncompliance with § 1788.58
     is a complete defense to this action under § 1788.62.

THIRD AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Unlicensed Debt Collector — Cal. Fin. Code, § 100002)

9.   Plaintiff is a "debt buyer" and/or "debt collector"
     within the meaning of Cal. Fin. Code, § 100001.
     Plaintiff was required to obtain a license from the
     California Department of Financial Protection and
     Innovation (DFPI) before engaging in debt-collection
     activity in California, including filing this action.
     On information and belief, Plaintiff did not hold a
     valid active DFPI license as of the date of filing.
     Plaintiff's unlicensed activity violates Cal. Fin.
     Code, § 100002 and constitutes an unlawful business
     practice under Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code, § 17200.

FOURTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Lack of Standing — Defective Chain of Title)

10.  Plaintiff alleges it "acquired" the alleged account
     through an assignment from Bank of America. Plaintiff
     cannot prove the chain of title from Bank of America
     to itself. Plaintiff lacks the foundational documents
     — the cardholder agreement, bill of sale, and
     assignment schedule — necessary to establish ownership
     of the specific alleged account and the right to sue.
     Plaintiff therefore lacks standing to maintain this
     action.

FIFTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Failure to Provide FDBPA Pre-Suit Notice —
Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.60)

11.  Plaintiff failed to provide Defendant with the written
     pre-suit notice required by Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.60
     before filing this action. Failure to provide pre-suit
     notice is an affirmative defense to the collection
     action.

SIXTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Failure to Provide Validation Notice —
15 U.S.C. § 1692g; 12 CFR § 1006.34)

12.  Plaintiff failed to provide a validation notice
     complying with 15 U.S.C. § 1692g(a) and 12 CFR
     § 1006.34 within 5 days of its initial communication
     with Defendant regarding the alleged account.

SEVENTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Unauthorized Amount — 15 U.S.C. § 1692f(1);
Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.14(a))

13.  The amount claimed by Plaintiff includes post-
     charge-off interest and fees not authorized by the
     original cardholder agreement, in violation of 15
     U.S.C. § 1692f(1) and Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.14(a).

EIGHTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(No Revival of Statute of Limitations — CCP § 360)

14.  No written acknowledgment or unambiguous partial
     payment by Defendant has restarted the statute of
     limitations under CCP § 360. Plaintiff cannot establish
     revival of the limitations period.

NINTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(No Account Stated)

15.  No "account stated" was ever created between Defendant
     and any party because Defendant disputes the existence
     and amount of the alleged account and has not
     acquiesced to any statement of balance.

TENTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Payment)

16.  To the extent any amount was owed, it has been paid
     in full by prior payments on the account.

ELEVENTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Unclean Hands)

17.  Plaintiff's claims are barred by the doctrine of
     unclean hands because Plaintiff has filed this action
     without possessing legally sufficient documentation
     of ownership, and has made material misrepresentations
     in the Complaint.

TWELFTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Waiver and Estoppel)

18.  Plaintiff's claims are barred by waiver and/or
     estoppel based on Plaintiff's failure to provide
     notice and documentation required by California and
     federal law prior to filing suit.

                     COUNTERCLAIMS

19.  Defendant/Counter-Claimant Jane Doe, in pro per,
     asserts the following counterclaims against
     Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant LVNV Funding, LLC.

FIRST CAUSE OF ACTION — Fair Debt Collection Practices
Act and Regulation F
(15 U.S.C. §§ 1692 et seq.; 12 CFR pt 1006)

20.  Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant LVNV Funding, LLC is a
     "debt collector" within the meaning of 15 U.S.C.
     § 1692a(6) in that it regularly collects or attempts
     to collect consumer debts.

21.  The alleged debt, if it exists, is a "consumer debt"
     within the meaning of 15 U.S.C. § 1692a(5) — an
     obligation arising from a personal credit-card account
     used for personal, family, or household purposes.

22.  Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant violated the FDCPA and
     Regulation F by:

     (a)  Bringing this action on a debt that, on
          Plaintiff's own records, became time-barred under
          CCP § 337 before January 15, 2025, in violation
          of 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(5), § 1692f, and 12 CFR
          § 1006.26(b);

     (b)  Falsely representing the character, amount, and
          legal status of the alleged debt (implying it is
          legally enforceable when it is time-barred), in
          violation of 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(2);

     (c)  Attempting to collect amounts (post-charge-off
          interest and fees) not authorized by the original
          cardholder agreement, in violation of 15 U.S.C.
          § 1692f(1);

     (d)  Failing to provide a validation notice complying
          with 15 U.S.C. § 1692g(a) and 12 CFR § 1006.34
          before or within 5 days of initial communication;

     (e)  Using deceptive means to collect a debt by filing
          this action without possessing authenticated
          chain-of-title documents, in violation of 15 U.S.C.
          § 1692e(10).

23.  As a direct and proximate result, Counter-Claimant
     suffered actual damages including time spent
     responding to this action, lost wages from court
     appearances, costs incurred, and emotional distress.
     Counter-Claimant is entitled to $1,000 in statutory
     damages under 15 U.S.C. § 1692k(a)(2)(A).

SECOND CAUSE OF ACTION — California Rosenthal Fair Debt
Collection Practices Act
(Cal. Civ. Code, §§ 1788–1788.33)

24.  Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant is a "debt collector"
     within the meaning of Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.2(c).

25.  The alleged debt is a "consumer debt" within the
     meaning of Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.2(f).

26.  Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant violated the Rosenthal
     Act by:

     (a)  Falsely representing the character, extent, and
          amount of the alleged debt, in violation of Cal.
          Civ. Code, § 1788.13(b) and § 1788.17
          (incorporating 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(2));

     (b)  Bringing a legal proceeding to enforce a time-
          barred debt, falsely representing that legal
          action was legitimately available, in violation
          of Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.13(f) and § 1788.17
          (incorporating 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(5));

     (c)  Attempting to collect amounts not authorized by
          the agreement creating the debt, in violation of
          Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.14(a) and § 1788.17
          (incorporating 15 U.S.C. § 1692f(1));

     (d)  Failing to provide a validation notice under
          Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.17 (incorporating 15
          U.S.C. § 1692g(a)).

27.  Counter-Claimant is entitled to actual damages, plus
     statutory damages of $100 to $1,000 per violation
     under Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.30(a)(2), and reasonable
     attorney's fees and costs under § 1788.30(c).

THIRD CAUSE OF ACTION — Unlawful Business Practice
(Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code, § 17200)

28.  Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant engaged in "unlawful"
     business practices within the meaning of Bus. & Prof.
     Code, § 17200, including:

     (a)  Operating in California without a valid DFPI
          license as required by Cal. Fin. Code, § 100002;

     (b)  Failing to comply with the pre-suit notice
          requirements of Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.60;

     (c)  Filing a complaint that fails to comply with
          Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.58;

     (d)  Violating the FDCPA and Rosenthal Act as alleged
          in the First and Second Causes of Action above.

29.  As a result of these unlawful business practices,
     Counter-Claimant has suffered injury in fact and lost
     money and property as alleged herein.

30.  Counter-Claimant is entitled to injunctive relief
     ordering Plaintiff to cease its unlawful collection
     activity and to restitution of any amounts collected.

FOURTH CAUSE OF ACTION — Fair Debt Buying Practices Act
(Cal. Civ. Code, §§ 1788.50–1788.64)

31.  Plaintiff is a "debt buyer" within the meaning of
     Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.50(a).

32.  Plaintiff failed to comply with the requirements of
     Cal. Civ. Code, §§ 1788.52, 1788.58, and 1788.60,
     as alleged in the Affirmative Defenses above.

33.  Plaintiff's noncompliance entitles Counter-Claimant
     to costs and attorney's fees under Cal. Civ. Code,
     § 1788.62.

                    PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Defendant/Counter-Claimant Jane Doe prays for:

A.   Judgment against Plaintiff on Plaintiff's Complaint,
     dismissing it with prejudice;

B.   On the First Cause of Action (FDCPA / Reg F):
     (i)  Actual damages to be proven at trial;
     (ii) Statutory damages of $1,000 under 15 U.S.C.
          § 1692k(a)(2)(A);
     (iii) Reasonable attorney's fees and costs under
          15 U.S.C. § 1692k(a)(3);

C.   On the Second Cause of Action (Rosenthal Act):
     (i)  Actual damages;
     (ii) Statutory damages of $100 to $1,000 per violation
          under Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.30(a)(2);
     (iii) Reasonable attorney's fees and costs under
          Cal. Civ. Code, § 1788.30(c);

D.   On the Third Cause of Action (UCL):
     (i)  Injunctive relief prohibiting Plaintiff from
          continuing its unlicensed and unlawful collection
          activity;
     (ii) Restitution of any amounts collected by Plaintiff
          through unlawful means;
     (iii) Attorney's fees under CCP § 1021.5;

E.   On the Fourth Cause of Action (FDBPA):
     Costs and attorney's fees under Cal. Civ. Code,
     § 1788.62;

F.   A declaration that the alleged debt is unenforceable,
     time-barred, and is not owed by Defendant;

G.   Costs of suit under CCP § 1032;

H.   Such other and further relief as the Court deems just.

## Key features illustrated

1. **General denial** (CCP § 431.30(d)) — California
   limited civil courts permit a general denial for
   complaints not verified; this simplifies the answer
2. **12 affirmative defenses** in order of strength:
   SOL first (often dispositive)
3. **4 counterclaims**: FDCPA/Reg F + Rosenthal + UCL +
   FDBPA — cover the same conduct with different remedies
4. **Prayer** itemizes each ground with specific damages
   and fee statutes
5. **Signature block**: "In Pro Per" (California term for
   pro se); no bar number
