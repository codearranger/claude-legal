## Key features illustrated

1. **"[PROPOSED] ORDER"** in the title — California courts
   expect the proposed order to be flagged as proposed

2. **Hearing recitals** — who appeared; what was considered
   (moving papers, opposition, declaration, oral argument)

3. **"Good cause appearing"** — standard California
   transitional phrase before ordering relief

4. **Numbered paragraphs** — one order per paragraph,
   keyed to each request

5. **14-day production deadline** from date of order (not
   from hearing) — allows time if order is signed after
   the hearing

6. **Redaction protection for non-requested accounts**
   in RFP 4 — balanced approach

7. **Two sanctions options** — drafting counsel fills in
   the correct amount; the court may award partial or no
   sanctions; both options are included

8. **Failure-to-comply consequence** — alerts plaintiff
   that further non-compliance may result in terminating
   sanctions under CCP § 2023.030

9. **Signature line for the judge** — leave blank; judge
   signs
