# Example — Meet-and-Confer Letter (CCP § 2031.310(b)(2))

Synthetic example. Fictional parties. Not legal advice.
See README.md for the fictional fact pattern.

```
[Date: e.g., April 15, 2025]

Via Electronic Mail and U.S. Mail

[Plaintiff's Counsel]
[Firm Name]
[Address]
[Email]

Re:  Meet and Confer — Defendant's First Set of
     Requests for Production of Documents and Things
     Case No. 25LCLC01234
     LVNV Funding, LLC v. Jane Doe

Counsel:

I am writing to meet and confer pursuant to CCP
§ 2031.310(b)(2) regarding Plaintiff's Responses to
Defendant's First Set of Requests for Production of
Documents and Things, served March 10, 2025. Plaintiff
served Responses on April 9, 2025 (the 30-day deadline
under CCP § 2031.260).

Please note that my deadline to file a motion to compel
further responses is 45 days from April 9, 2025, which
is May 24, 2025 (CCP § 2031.310(c)). I am writing
promptly to preserve adequate time for informal
resolution.

I am writing in good faith to identify each response
that I believe is deficient and to explain my position.
I invite a phone call to discuss these issues further.
Please let me know your availability for a call this week.

          DEFICIENT RESPONSES

1.  REQUEST NO. 1 — CARDHOLDER AGREEMENT

    Plaintiff's response: "Plaintiff objects to this
    request on the grounds that it is not in Plaintiff's
    possession, custody, or control."

    My position: Plaintiff's Complaint, at paragraph 4,
    alleges that Plaintiff "acquired the Account from Bank
    of America, N.A. through a valid assignment." A party
    that claims ownership of a contractual obligation
    cannot simultaneously disclaim possession of the
    contract itself.

    Moreover, "possession, custody, or control" under
    California law includes the legal right to obtain
    documents. Plaintiff's bulk sale agreement with its
    seller should include a contractual right to access
    the original account documentation. If so, Plaintiff
    has control of the cardholder agreement.

    Under CCP § 2031.230, if Plaintiff genuinely lacks
    possession, it must state so specifically and describe
    what it knows about where the cardholder agreement
    can be found and the efforts it has made to obtain it.

    I request that Plaintiff supplement by: (a) producing
    the cardholder agreement, or (b) providing a detailed
    sworn statement explaining why it cannot obtain the
    cardholder agreement and identifying the person at
    Bank of America who would have it.

    Deadline for supplementation: May 5, 2025.

2.  REQUEST NO. 3 — BILLS OF SALE

    Plaintiff's response: "Plaintiff objects on grounds
    of trade secret and confidential business information."

    My position: A "trade secret" objection requires
    specificity — what specific information is claimed
    to be a trade secret, and how would its disclosure
    harm Plaintiff? A bare assertion of trade secret is
    insufficient.

    To address Plaintiff's confidentiality concerns,
    I am willing to enter into a stipulated protective
    order under CCP § 2031.060(c) that limits disclosure
    of the Bills of Sale to this litigation and requires
    destruction after the case concludes.

    Please identify the specific confidential information
    at issue and propose the protective-order language
    you would accept.

3.  REQUEST NO. 4 — ASSIGNMENT SCHEDULE

    Plaintiff's response: Plaintiff produced a spreadsheet
    with all rows except one (which may or may not be
    mine) redacted.

    My position: The redaction is overbroad. The
    document's structural elements — column headers,
    date information, page numbers, the signature block
    showing who signed the schedule and when — are not
    confidential and must remain visible for authenticity
    evaluation under Cal. Evid. Code § 1271. A document
    that has been edited to show only a single row cannot
    be authenticated as a business record made in the
    regular course of business.

    I request production of the schedule with:
    (a) Other accounts' identifying information (name,
        account number, balance) redacted row-by-row; but
    (b) All structural elements visible: headers, column
        labels, dates, footer, page numbers, and signature
        block.

    A protective order is available for any row-level
    information that is genuinely confidential.

4.  REQUEST NO. 13 — DFPI LICENSE

    Plaintiff's response: "Plaintiff objects on grounds
    of relevance."

    My position: This request is directly relevant to
    my Third Affirmative Defense (unlicensed collector —
    Cal. Fin. Code, § 100002). Plaintiff's licensing
    status as of the date of filing is a threshold legal
    issue.

    A DFPI license is not privileged, confidential, or
    burdensome to produce. Plaintiff either has a license
    or it does not. If it has one, production is simple.
    If it does not, the consequence follows from California
    law.

    I request that Plaintiff supplement within 14 days by
    producing its DFPI license certificate, or by stating
    affirmatively (under oath) that Plaintiff did not hold
    a valid DFPI license as of January 15, 2025.

          INVITATION TO RESOLVE

I am available for a phone call on:

    - Tuesday, April 22, 2025, between 10 AM and 2 PM
    - Wednesday, April 23, 2025, between 9 AM and noon
    - Thursday, April 24, 2025, between 1 PM and 5 PM

Please propose a time. I will send a follow-up email
confirming the substance of our call.

If I do not receive a response by April 22, 2025, or if
we cannot resolve these disputes by May 5, 2025, I will
file a motion to compel further responses under CCP
§ 2031.310 before the May 24, 2025 deadline.

Respectfully,

____________________________________
Jane Doe
Defendant, In Pro Per
123 Main Street
Los Angeles, CA 90001
```

## Key features illustrated

1. **Reference to CCP § 2031.310(b)(2)** — identifies the
   statutory basis for the M&C requirement at the outset

2. **45-day deadline explicitly stated** — demonstrates
   awareness of the motion-to-compel deadline and urgency

3. **Request-by-request analysis** — each disputed request
   listed separately with the specific objection and the
   defendant's counter-argument

4. **Legal basis for each position** — cites the specific
   statutory or evidentiary basis for why the response is
   deficient

5. **Specific proposed resolution** for each dispute —
   California courts expect parties to propose alternatives
   (protective orders, limited production, etc.)

6. **Concrete deadlines for supplementation** — "by May 5,
   2025" — avoids open-ended stalling

7. **Availability for phone call** with specific dates
   and times

8. **Consequence if unresolved** — "I will file a motion
   to compel" — clear statement of next steps without
   being inflammatory

9. **Tone**: firm but professional; not threatening;
   inviting resolution
