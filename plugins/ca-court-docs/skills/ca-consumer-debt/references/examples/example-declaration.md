## Key features illustrated

1. **CCP § 2015.5 perjury clause** at the close: "I
   declare under penalty of perjury under the laws of
   the State of California that the foregoing is true
   and correct." California requires state-law perjury
   language (not Oregon's format, not federal's).

2. **"Executed on [Date], at Los Angeles, California"** —
   California requires the execution date and location
   (CCP § 2015.5(a)).

3. **Numbered paragraphs** — one fact per paragraph for
   clarity.

4. **Personal knowledge** — every fact is within the
   declarant's personal observation; no hearsay.

5. **No argument** — pure facts. Argument lives in the
   opposition memorandum.

6. **Exhibit authentication** — each exhibit is referenced
   as "A true and correct copy is attached as Exhibit N."

7. **DFPI screenshot** — important to capture the date
   of the search to establish the license status at filing.

8. **SOL computation** — the declaration establishes the
   key SOL dates directly, supporting the defense.

9. **No bar number** — pro per defendant signs their own
   name without a bar number.

## What this declaration does NOT do

- Make legal arguments (those are in the opposition
  memorandum)
- Characterize plaintiff's conduct ("Plaintiff is acting
  in bad faith")
- State legal conclusions ("Plaintiff violated the FDCPA")
- Speculate about plaintiff's motives

The declaration sticks to facts the declarant personally
observed or has documentary evidence for.
