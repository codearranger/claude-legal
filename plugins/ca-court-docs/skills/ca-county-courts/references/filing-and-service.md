# California Filing and Service — County-by-County Variations

California has no single statewide e-filing system. Each
Superior Court adopts its own e-filing arrangement, governed by
Cal. Rules of Court 2.250-2.261. This file inventories the
per-county systems and the procedural variations they impose.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the current e-filing system,
> filing fees, and service-of-process rules against the court's
> website before filing.

## The statewide e-filing rules (CRC 2.250-2.261)

The Judicial Council adopted CRC 2.250-2.261 to standardize
e-filing baselines:

- **CRC 2.250** — definitions
- **CRC 2.251** — mandatory e-filing in mandatory courts; per-
  court rules govern voluntary courts
- **CRC 2.253** — court's discretion to require e-filing
- **CRC 2.254** — service of e-filed documents
- **CRC 2.255** — confidential / sealed e-filings
- **CRC 2.256** — technical requirements (PDF, text-
  searchable, bookmarked, formatted per CRC 2.100-2.119)
- **CRC 2.257** — Electronic signatures
- **CRC 2.259** — Effective filing date / time

Each county can adopt its own e-filing requirements above this
floor. The vendor differs by county.

## E-filing systems by county (selected)

### Odyssey eFileCA (Tyler Technologies-based)

Most populous CA superior courts use Odyssey eFileCA. Vendors
build on top of this platform:

- Los Angeles County (LASC) — efilingsupport.lacourt.org
- San Diego County (SDSC) — sdcourt.ca.gov
- Santa Clara County (SCSC) — scscourt.org
- Alameda County — alameda.courts.ca.gov
- Riverside County — riverside.courts.ca.gov
- San Bernardino County — sb-court.org
- Contra Costa County — cc-courts.org

**Vendors**: Filers register with one of multiple Tyler-
approved EFSPs (Electronic Filing Service Providers):
- One Legal (onelegal.com)
- ImageSoft FileTime (filetime.com)
- Green Filing
- File & ServeXpress
- FileFile

Each vendor presents the Odyssey eFileCA backend differently;
fees per filing vary by vendor.

## Filing fees (Govt. Code §§ 70611-70617)

Filing fees are set statewide:

| Filing | Fee | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Unlimited civil first appearance | $435 | Gov. Code, § 70611 |
| Limited civil first appearance (≤ $25K) | $370 | Gov. Code, § 70613 |
| Limited civil first appearance (≤ $10K) | $370 (varies by amount) | Gov. Code, § 70613 |
| Small claims (≤ $1,500) | $30 | Gov. Code, § 70613 |
| Small claims ($1,501-$5,000) | $50 | Gov. Code, § 70613 |
| Small claims ($5,001-$10,000) | $75 | Gov. Code, § 70613 |
| Motion (subsequent appearance / non-paper) | $60 | Gov. Code, § 70617 |
| Ex parte application | $60 | Gov. Code, § 70617 |
| Demurrer (post-answer) | $60 | Gov. Code, § 70617 |
| Stipulation for entry of judgment | $25 | Various |
| Petition for writ | $435 | Gov. Code, § 70615 |
| Abstract of judgment | $25 | Various |

Fees adjusted from time to time; verify against current
schedule at courts.ca.gov.

## Fee waivers (in forma pauperis)

- **Form FW-001** — Request to Waive Court Fees (Judicial
  Council form, mandatory)
- **Form FW-003** — Order on Court Fee Waiver
- **Authority**: Gov. Code, §§ 68630-68641
- **Eligibility**:
  - (a) Receipt of specific public benefits (e.g., MediCal,
    SSI, CalWORKs, GA/GR, food assistance, IHSS, CAPI, Tribal
    TANF)
  - (b) Household income at or below 125% of federal poverty
    guidelines
  - (c) Cannot pay fees + basic necessities
- **Coverage**: filing fees, jury fees, court reporter fees
  (for trials > 1 hour), motion fees

## Service of process — initial summons

Initial service of summons is governed by CCP §§ 412.10-417.40.
This is **NOT** the same as service of subsequent papers, which
is governed by CCP §§ 1010-1020.

### Methods of initial service

1. **Personal service** (CCP § 415.10) — preferred; hand-
   delivery to defendant or authorized agent.
2. **Substituted service** (CCP § 415.20) — leave with adult
   household member or office mailbox + mail follow-up; service
   complete 10 days after mailing.
3. **Service by mail with Notice and Acknowledgment of Receipt
   (NORF)** (CCP § 415.30) — mailed; complete on date of
   acknowledgment; if no acknowledgment, must be served by
   other method.
4. **Service on entity** (CCP §§ 415.40, 416.10-416.95) —
   serve agent for service of process; corporations through
   Cal. SoS-listed agent.
5. **Service by publication** (CCP § 415.50) — last resort;
   requires court order; 4 consecutive weeks in newspaper of
   general circulation.
6. **Service outside California** (CCP § 415.40) — direct mail
   to defendant + Form POS-015 acknowledgment, or international
   methods.

### Proof of Service forms

- **POS-010** — Proof of Service of Summons (initial service)
- **POS-015** — Notice and Acknowledgment of Receipt (CCP
  § 415.30)
- **POS-030** — Proof of Service by First-Class Mail — Civil
- **POS-040** — Proof of Service — Civil (general)
- **POS-050** — Proof of Electronic Service

## Service of subsequent papers — CCP §§ 1010-1020

After initial service, motions and other papers are served per
CCP §§ 1010-1020:

| Method | Time added | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Personal service | None | CCP § 1011 |
| Mail (in-state) | +5 calendar days | CCP § 1013(a) |
| Mail (out-of-state) | +10 calendar days | CCP § 1013(a) |
| Mail (outside U.S.) | +20 calendar days | CCP § 1013(a) |
| Fax | +2 court days | CCP § 1013(c) |
| Express mail / overnight | +2 court days | CCP § 1013(d) |
| Electronic (e-service) | +2 court days | CCP § 1010.6(a)(3)(B) |

### Electronic service consent

- **Represented parties**: E-service is the default for parties
  represented by counsel in mandatory e-filing courts; opt-out
  available only by court order.
- **Pro per parties**: E-service requires the pro per's express
  written consent (CCP § 1010.6(c)(2)).

## Working copies / chambers copies

Many CA Superior Court judges require a paper "working copy" of
filed motion packets delivered to chambers, separately from
e-filing. Standing orders vary by department:

- **LASC**: many departments require working copies — check
  the department's standing order at lacourt.org
- **SFSC**: typically not required separately from e-filed
  packet
- **OCSC**: department-specific
- **Other counties**: department-specific

Working copies typically must be received by chambers a few
court days before the hearing.
