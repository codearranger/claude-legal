# Downtown Los Angeles — Stanley Mosk and Spring Street Courthouses

## Stanley Mosk Courthouse (Central Courthouse)

The Stanley Mosk Courthouse is the flagship civil courthouse
for Los Angeles Superior Court and the primary venue for
unlimited civil litigation in Central Los Angeles.

- **Address**: 111 N. Hill Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012
- **Phone**: LASC Civil Division — (213) 830-0800 (general
  information line; verify current number at lacourt.org)
- **Hours**: Monday-Friday, 8:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m. (clerk's
  filing counter may close earlier for same-day filing —
  confirm at lacourt.org)
- **Floors**: 17 floors; civil departments primarily on upper
  floors (Dept. 1 through Dept. 75 and beyond, non-sequential)
- **Clerk's counter (filing)**: Ground floor, Room 102 —
  walk-in filing, fee payment, and case information

### Key civil departments — Stanley Mosk

Because LASC has approximately 500 judicial officers and
departments shift regularly, the following should be treated
as a **sample** of the typical department structure. Always
confirm the specific department on the LASC case portal at
**https://www.lacourt.org** before filing or appearing.

| Department type | Typical location | Notes |
|----------------|-----------------|-------|
| General unlimited civil | Floors 4-17 | Dept. 1-75 range; random assignment |
| Complex civil (CCP 437c) | Central Civil West (600 S. Commonwealth) | Separate building |
| Writs and receivers | Various | Check case portal |
| Law and motion calendar | Per dept. | LASC has no centralized law-and-motion dept. like SFSC's Dept. 302 |

### Directions and parking

- **Metro**: Civic Center/Grand Park station (Red/Purple Line),
  immediately adjacent. Most civil litigants and attorneys use
  Metro to avoid parking costs.
- **Parking**: LA County parking structures on nearby streets
  (Temple, Hill, First). Rates are expensive; check
  spothero.com or parkwhiz.com before driving.
- **Security**: Standard federal-style security screening at
  main entrance. Arrive at least 30 minutes before a hearing
  to clear security.
- **Accessibility**: Fully ADA-accessible; elevator access
  to all floors.

### Clerk's operations

- **E-filing confirmation**: LASC Odyssey eFileCA electronic
  filings are visible in the case portal within hours of
  acceptance; paper filings at Room 102 are same-day if
  received by 4:00 p.m.
- **Fee payment**: Credit card, check, or money order at the
  clerk's counter; online payment through Odyssey for eFiled
  documents.
- **Self-help center**: LASC Self-Help Legal Access Center is
  at 111 N. Hill St. (ground floor) — free legal assistance
  for self-represented litigants; hours vary.
- **Certified copies**: request at the clerk's counter with
  case number and document ID; same-day or next-day processing.

## Spring Street Courthouse

The Spring Street Courthouse is a second major civil venue in
downtown Los Angeles, handling additional unlimited civil
departments and writs.

- **Address**: 312 N. Spring Street, Los Angeles, CA 90012
- **Phone**: (213) 974-5411 (verify at lacourt.org)
- **Hours**: Monday-Friday, 8:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m.
- **Metro**: Civic Center/Grand Park (Red/Purple Line) or
  Union Station (short walk)
- **Use**: Overflow unlimited civil departments; some complex
  civil departments; writ proceedings

### Relation to Stanley Mosk

Spring Street and Stanley Mosk are approximately two blocks
apart. A case assigned to Spring Street in the caption will
have a department number that identifies it (LASC department
numbers are unique across the building — there is only one
Dept. 32, whether it sits in Stanley Mosk or Spring Street).

The case portal confirms the courthouse for each department.

## Central Civil West Courthouse (Complex Civil Program)

For cases designated complex under Cal. Rules of Court,
rule 3.400:

- **Address**: 600 S. Commonwealth Avenue, Los Angeles 90005
- **Phone**: (213) 351-8895
- **Hours**: Monday-Friday, 8:30 a.m.-4:30 p.m.
- **Use**: LASC Complex Civil Program; class actions,
  coordinated proceedings, environmental, securities, mass
  torts, construction defect

Complex cases are transferred to CCW at or after the initial
case management conference when the court determines the
case meets the complexity threshold.

## LASC district courthouses (civil)

Depending on the location of the cause of action or the
defendant's residence (Code Civ. Proc., § 395), civil cases
may be filed in LASC district courthouses outside downtown:

| Pasadena Courthouse | 300 E. Walnut St., Pasadena 91101 | Northeast | GDCV |
| Long Beach Courthouse | 275 Magnolia Ave., Long Beach 90802 | South | LBCV |
| Compton Courthouse | 200 W. Compton Blvd., Compton 90220 | Southwest (limited) | CMCV |
| Torrance Courthouse | 825 Maple Ave., Torrance 90503 | Southwest | Various |
| Chatsworth Courthouse | 9425 Penfield Ave., Chatsworth 91311 | Northwest West | Various |
| Glendale Courthouse | 600 E. Broadway, Glendale 91206 | North | GDCV |

**Venue selection**: The moving party typically files where the
cause of action arose or where the defendant resides/has its
principal place of business (Code Civ. Proc., § 395(a)).
For contractual consumer-debt cases, the plaintiff has the
burden of proper venue; if filed in the wrong location, the
defendant may move to transfer under Code Civ. Proc., § 395.1.

## After-hours emergency filings

For true emergencies requiring after-hours filing (e.g., a
TRO on a day when the clerk's office is closed):

- LASC has an after-hours filing procedure; contact the LASC
  duty judge through the court's main line: (213) 830-0800
- After-hours eFiling through Odyssey is available 24/7 but
  is not "accepted" until the next business day

## LASC Self-Help Center

111 N. Hill Street, Room 118 (Ground Floor)
Los Angeles, CA 90012
Phone: (213) 830-0844

Services: Legal forms, document preparation assistance,
referrals to legal aid, fee-waiver assistance. Open Monday
through Friday; hours vary — check lacourt.org/selflegalhelp/.

Self-represented litigants should visit the Self-Help Center
before filing complex motions or when uncertain about
procedures. The Center's staff cannot provide legal advice
but can help with forms and procedures.
