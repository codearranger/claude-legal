# LASC Local Rules — Key Civil Practice Sections

The Los Angeles Superior Court Local Rules (the "LASC Local Rules"
or "L.R.") supplement the California Rules of Court. The full
text is available at:

**https://www.lacourt.org/rules/ui/index.aspx**

Always pull the current version before citing — LASC typically
updates local rules annually. The rules below reflect the general
structure; section numbers may shift.

## Filing and e-filing rules (L.R. 2.x)

| Rule | Subject |
|------|---------|
| 2.2 | Electronic filing mandatory for represented parties in unlimited civil; voluntary for self-represented parties |
| 2.3 | Electronic service authorized under Code Civ. Proc., § 1010.6; parties who eFile consent to eService through the portal |
| 2.5 | Documents not accepted for eFiling (sealed documents, lodged documents, certain exhibits) |
| 2.7 | Technical failure — if the eFiling system fails, the deadline is tolled; party must notify the court and file at the clerk's counter within 24 hours |
| 2.10 | Self-represented litigants may paper-file at the clerk's counter even in cases with mandatory eFiling for represented parties |

**Odyssey eFileCA portal**: https://efiling.lacourt.org
- Document codes: match the CRC 2.112 document type
- Each attachment filed as a separate PDF
- System generates a "Filing ID" that replaces a case stamp for
  e-filed documents; the Filing ID is the eFiling confirmation

## Case management and CMC (L.R. 3.28, 3.5)

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 3.28 | Case management conferences scheduled ~120 days after complaint filing; CMC statement (CM-110) due 15 calendar days before CMC |
| 3.5 | Trial date setting; continuances require a showing of good cause; request must be made by noticed motion or ex parte application (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1332) |
| 3.20 | Trial readiness — parties must be ready to start trial on the trial date; LASC takes trial date compliance seriously; last-minute continuances disfavored |

**Form CM-110** (Judicial Council form): available at
**https://www.courts.ca.gov/forms.htm**. The CM-110 asks:
- Status of service on all parties
- Whether any party is in default
- ADR preference and status
- Estimated trial length
- Whether case is related to other cases
- Status of discovery and dispositive motions

Both sides must file the CM-110; filing a joint CM-110 requires
opposing party agreement but is efficient.

## Tentative rulings (L.R. 3.31)

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 3.31 | Tentative rulings posted for civil law-and-motion matters by 2:00 p.m. the court day before the hearing; contest by calling the department by 4:00 p.m.; if unchallenged, tentative becomes the final order |

**Tentative ruling portal**:
**https://www.lacourt.org/tentativerulingweb/ui/**

Search by case number, department, or both. If the tentative is
not posted by 3:00 p.m. the day before, call the department
clerk to confirm whether a tentative will issue.

**Contesting the tentative — procedure**:
1. Call the department clerk (number listed on the department
   page at lacourt.org)
2. State: case name, case number, party you represent, and that
   you wish to contest the tentative
3. Notify opposing party/counsel immediately after notifying
   the court (same 4:00 p.m. deadline)
4. Appear at the scheduled hearing time; be prepared to argue

**If the tentative favors you**:
- You are not required to appear if no other party contests
- Check the tentative portal again the morning of the hearing
  to confirm no last-minute contest has been filed
- If no party contests by 4:00 p.m. the day before, the
  tentative is automatically entered as the order — call the
  court clerk the next day to confirm entry

## Discovery motions (L.R. 3.57)

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 3.57 | Motions to compel further discovery responses must be accompanied by a Separate Statement under Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345; failure to file Separate Statement results in denial of the motion |
| 3.57 | Meet-and-confer declaration required under Code Civ. Proc., § 2016.040; must describe all meet-and-confer efforts in detail |

**Separate Statement format** (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345):
- Column 1: full text of the discovery request
- Column 2: full text of the response
- Column 3: why a further response should be ordered (including
  any applicable objection analysis)
- File as a separate document; typically titled "SEPARATE
  STATEMENT OF DISCOVERY IN DISPUTE IN SUPPORT OF [MOTION TITLE]"

**Discovery deadline to file motion**: 45 days after service of
responses (Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.300(c), 2031.310(c),
2033.290(c)); parties may extend by written agreement.

## Demurrers (L.R. 3.26)

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 3.26 | Demurrers to complaints: meet-and-confer required before filing (Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41); declaration of meet-and-confer efforts must accompany demurrer |
| 3.26 | Demurrer hearing reserved through CRS; same notice period as other motions (16 court days) |

**Code Civ. Proc., § 430.41** requires the demurring party to
meet and confer with the pleading party at least 5 days before
the demurrer is filed (or earlier if the response to the
complaint is due in fewer than 15 days). The meet-and-confer
may be in person, by telephone, or by letter.

## Motions for summary judgment (L.R. 3.20-3.25)

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 3.20 | MSJ motions must include a Separate Statement of Undisputed Material Facts (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(b)(1)) |
| 3.21 | Notice period: 75 days (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(a)) |
| 3.22 | Opposition due 14 days before hearing; reply due 5 days before hearing (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(b)(2)) |
| 3.23 | Expert declarations attached to MSJ must comply with Evid. Code, § 720 (qualification) and § 801 (opinion basis) |
| 3.25 | If MSJ papers exceed 20 pages, a table of contents and table of authorities is required |

**Courtesy copies for MSJ**: LASC's standing orders for many
departments require physical courtesy copies for MSJ papers,
typically delivered the court day before the hearing. Check the
specific department's standing order.

## Motions in limine (L.R. 3.10)

Per L.R. 3.10 and the applicable trial department's standing
order:

- Motions in limine filed and served: typically per the trial
  department's order (often 5-10 court days before trial)
- Use the Final Status Conference (FSC) for oral argument on
  motions in limine; the trial judge sets the schedule

**Note**: LASC complex-civil and standard civil departments
have different MIL schedules — always check the trial
department's standing order.

## Complex civil litigation (L.R. 8.90-8.100)

Cases meeting the complexity threshold (Cal. Rules of Court,
rule 3.400) are transferred to the **Complex Civil Program** at
Central Civil West Courthouse (600 S. Commonwealth Ave.).

Common complex-civil designations:
- Class actions (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.764)
- Coordinated proceedings (Code Civ. Proc., § 404 et seq.)
- Construction defect with 10+ plaintiffs
- Securities fraud
- Environmental/toxic tort
- Mass tort
- Insurance coverage disputes involving multiple insurers/policies

Complex cases have their own Judicial Council form (CM-010)
for the initial "Complex Civil Case Designation" designation
request.

## ADR

LASC local rules encourage early ADR. The CMC order typically
directs parties to complete ADR before the Final Status
Conference. ADR options:
- Voluntary settlement conference with a court-appointed
  settlement officer (free)
- Private mediator agreed upon by parties
- Judicial arbitration (mandatory for cases ≤ $50,000 under
  Code Civ. Proc., § 1141.11)
- Neutral evaluation

LASC ADR Program: **https://www.lacourt.org/division/adr/**

## Unlawful detainer (L.R. 7.9)

Unlawful detainer (UD) cases follow an accelerated schedule:
- Defendant has 5 court days to answer (Code Civ. Proc., § 1167)
- Trial within 20 days of request for trial setting (Code Civ.
  Proc., § 1170.5)
- UD cases filed at district courthouses; for the Central
  district, at Stanley Mosk

For consumer-debt cases that are technically UD actions (rare),
confirm proper venue and the accelerated UD schedule applies.

## Fees reference

Current filing fees are posted at:
**https://www.lacourt.org/division/civil/fees.aspx**

Key fees (subject to annual adjustment):
- First paper, unlimited civil: $435
- First paper, limited civil (over $10,000): $225
- Answer: same as complaint
- Motion (unlimited civil): $60
- Summary judgment: $500
- Fee waiver application: form FW-001 (no fee to file)

## Department standing orders

Every LASC judge may issue department-specific standing orders
that supplement the Local Rules. These are critical — they can
require specific courtesy-copy formats, set different briefing
schedules, or impose unique motion-practice rules.

Find standing orders at:
**https://www.lacourt.org/division/civil/civil.aspx**

Click the department number. If no standing order is posted,
follow the Local Rules and Cal. Rules of Court as written.
