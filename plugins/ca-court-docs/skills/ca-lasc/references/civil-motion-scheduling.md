# LASC Civil Motion Scheduling

## Overview

Unlike San Francisco's centralized Dept. 302 law-and-motion
calendar, **LASC does not have a single centralized law-and-
motion department for general unlimited civil cases**. Each case
is assigned to a specific department, and law-and-motion
hearings are heard by the **assigned judge** in that department.

The key tools for scheduling are:

1. **LASC Court Reservation System (CRS)**: online self-service
   portal for most civil motion types
2. **Department-direct scheduling**: a minority of departments
   use their own clerk's calendar rather than the CRS
3. **Ex parte applications**: direct contact with the department
   clerk, bypassing the CRS

## LASC Court Reservation System (CRS)

The CRS is the primary scheduling portal for civil law-and-motion
hearings in unlimited civil cases.

### Access

**https://www.lacourt.org/courtreservation/ui/index.aspx**

Account registration is required. Attorneys use their State Bar
number; self-represented litigants register with name and case
information.

### Step-by-step reservation

**Step 1 — Verify the assigned department**

Check the LASC case portal at **https://www.lacourt.org** by
searching the case number or party name. Confirm the exact
department number and courthouse location.

**Step 2 — Select department and motion type**

In the CRS:
1. Select the courthouse (e.g., Stanley Mosk)
2. Select the department (e.g., Dept. 36)
3. Select the motion type from the dropdown:
   - Demurrer
   - Motion to Strike
   - Motion to Compel (discovery)
   - Motion for Summary Judgment
   - Motion to Continue Trial
   - Other Civil Motion
   - etc.

**Step 3 — Choose a date**

Available hearing dates appear in a calendar. Dates are:
- Monday-Friday, excluding court holidays
- Hearing times are typically 8:30 a.m. or 10:00 a.m.
  (varies by department)
- Availability reflects the department's calendar capacity —
  peak times (Jan., Sept.) may have limited slots

**Step 4 — Complete the reservation**

Provide the case number, case name, and filing party. The CRS
issues a **Reservation ID** (sometimes called the "booking
number" or "CRS number").

**Step 5 — Include the Reservation ID on all motion papers**

In the caption (right column, after the hearing info):

```
Date:  March 15, 2025
Time:  10:00 a.m.
Dept.: 36
Judge: Hon. John Smith
Reservation No.: CRS-XXXXXXXX
```

If the Reservation ID is missing from the motion papers, the
clerk may reject the filing or the department may take the
motion off calendar.

**Step 6 — File within the reservation window**

The CRS reservation is typically held for a limited period
(often 5-7 court days). If you do not file within that window,
the reservation may expire and the date will be released. File
through Odyssey eFileCA promptly after reserving.

### Departments that do NOT use the CRS

Not all LASC departments use the CRS. Some departments,
particularly those handling complex civil matters or certain
specialized calendars, use **direct-to-clerk scheduling** —
contact the department clerk by phone to request a hearing date.

Check the LASC department pages:
**https://www.lacourt.org/division/civil/civil.aspx**

Each department page often lists the preferred scheduling
method and the clerk's phone number.

## Notice requirements (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b))

| Service method | Notice period before hearing |
|---------------|------------------------------|
| Electronic service (eService via Odyssey) | 16 court days |
| Personal service | 16 court days |
| Overnight courier | 16 court days + 2 court days = 18 court days |
| First-class mail | 16 court days + 5 calendar days |

**Court days** = all calendar days except Saturdays, Sundays,
and the California judicial holidays listed in Code Civ. Proc.,
§ 135:
- New Year's Day (Jan. 1)
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day (3rd Monday in January)
- Presidents' Day (3rd Monday in February)
- César Chávez Day (March 31)
- Memorial Day (last Monday in May)
- Juneteenth (June 19)
- Independence Day (July 4)
- Labor Day (1st Monday in September)
- Columbus Day / Indigenous Peoples Day (2nd Monday in October)
- Veterans Day (November 11)
- Thanksgiving Day (4th Thursday in November)
- Christmas Day (December 25)

When a holiday falls on Saturday, the preceding Friday is the
court holiday; when it falls on Sunday, the following Monday.

**Counting court days**: begin counting on the day after service
(not including the service date). Count forward, skipping all
non-court days.

**Example**: If service by eService occurs on Monday, February 3,
2025, the first court day is February 4. Counting 16 court days
forward: Feb. 4 = 1, Feb. 5 = 2, ... (skip weekends and
Presidents' Day Feb. 17) ... the 16th court day is Feb. 26.
The earliest hearing date is February 26, 2025.

## Discovery motion timing

For motions to compel further responses to interrogatories,
requests for production, or requests for admission:

**Deadline to file**: 45 days after service of the responses
(Code Civ. Proc., §§ 2030.300(c), 2031.310(c), 2033.290(c)).
If the parties agree to extend, the extension must be in writing.

**Meet and confer**: required before filing any discovery motion
(Code Civ. Proc., § 2016.040). The declaration of the moving
party must describe the meet-and-confer efforts. Best practice:
send a detailed letter identifying each deficient response and
requesting supplemental responses within 15 days; if no adequate
response, file the motion.

**Separate Statement**: Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345, requires
a Separate Statement for motions to compel further discovery
responses. This is a separate document listing each request in
dispute, the response given, and why a further response should
be ordered. Failure to file the Separate Statement is grounds
for summary denial.

## Summary judgment timing

Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(a): the moving party must give at least
**75 days' notice** of the hearing. If served by mail, add 5
calendar days. If service is by overnight courier, add 2 court
days.

Opposition is due **14 days before the hearing**; reply is due
**5 days before the hearing** (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(b)(2)).

Because of the long notice period, reserve the MSJ hearing date
as early as possible — CRS slots for MSJ hearings may book out
2-3 months in advance.

## Opposition and reply timing (general rule)

Per Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b):
- **Opposition** filed and served: 9 court days before the hearing
- **Reply** filed and served: 5 court days before the hearing

If the opposing party failed to serve opposition 9 court days
before the hearing, the moving party may argue the matter on
the tentative without opposition (though the court will still
review the papers).

## Ex parte applications

For emergency relief (TRO, OSC, preliminary injunction on an
accelerated schedule):

1. **Notice**: notify all parties by **10:00 a.m.** on the court
   day before the ex parte appearance (Cal. Rules of Court,
   rule 3.1203)
2. **Filing**: submit the ex parte application at the department
   clerk's window at or before 8:30 a.m. (or as early as the
   department opens)
3. **The court's discretion**: the judge may grant the TRO on
   the papers without a hearing, grant a hearing, or deny; the
   applicant is typically notified by the department clerk the
   same day
4. **Declaration re notice**: explain who was notified and by
   what method — or why notice was not given (Cal. Rules of
   Court, rule 3.1204(b))

Common LASC ex parte relief scenarios:
- Temporary restraining order (Code Civ. Proc., § 527)
- Appointment of a receiver (Code Civ. Proc., § 564)
- Continuance of a trial date (Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1332)
- Emergency protective order (domestic violence / civil harassment)

## Motion practice tips for LASC

1. **Check department standing orders**: Many LASC judges post
   department-specific rules on the LASC website. These can
   include: page-limit exceptions, specific briefing schedules,
   required number of courtesy copies, and preferences for
   proposed orders. Find them at:
   **https://www.lacourt.org/division/civil/civil.aspx** → click
   the department number

2. **Courtesy copies**: Some LASC departments require or
   strongly prefer paper courtesy copies delivered to the
   department the day before the hearing. The standing order
   will specify. Courtesy copies should be:
   - Three-hole punched (not two-hole; this is a clerk
     preference at LASC vs. the filed copies which use two-hole)
   - Tabbed for exhibits
   - Delivered in a binder or manila folder

3. **Tentative ruling check**: Check the LASC tentative-ruling
   website (**https://www.lacourt.org/tentativerulingweb/ui/**)
   by 2:00 p.m. the day before the hearing. If unfavorable,
   call the department by 4:00 p.m. to contest.

4. **Virtual hearings**: Since COVID-19, many LASC departments
   continue to offer (or require) telephonic or video appearances
   for law-and-motion. Check the department's standing order for
   the current remote-hearing policy. For phone appearances, LASC
   uses a CourtCall or direct-dial system — confirm with the
   department clerk.

5. **Case management conference (CMC)**: The CMC is typically
   scheduled approximately 120 days after the complaint is filed.
   File the CM-110 Case Management Statement 15 calendar days
   before the CMC. The CMC order sets all deadlines.
