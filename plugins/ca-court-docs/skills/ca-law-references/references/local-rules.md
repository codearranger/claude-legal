# Local Rules — California Superior Courts

Each California Superior Court adopts its own local rules under
**Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.20**, which limits local rules to
matters not covered by the CRC or preempted by state law.
When a local rule conflicts with the CRC, the CRC controls.

This reference summarizes the most-used local rule provisions
for the high-volume courts. Pull the current local rules PDF
from each court's website before citing — local rules are
updated frequently.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify all local rule citations against
> the current version from the court's website.

## Los Angeles Superior Court (LASC)

The largest trial court in the United States; approximately
31 courthouses across Los Angeles County.

**Website**: https://www.lacourt.org/
**Local rules**: https://www.lacourt.org/division/civil/CI0078.aspx

### Division 1 — General rules

- **LASC rule 1.20** — All filings must include the department
  number on the caption; papers filed without department
  number may be rejected
- **LASC rule 2.8** — Courtesy copies required for motions in
  certain departments; check the department's courtroom
  information page
- **LASC rule 3.24** — Electronic filings: LASC accepts
  electronic filings through the court's eFiling portal;
  mandatory for some case types in some departments

### Division 3 — Civil proceedings

- **LASC rule 3.3** — Case management: the court assigns cases
  at filing; complex cases assigned to a Complex Civil
  Litigation department
- **LASC rule 3.4** — Discovery cut-off: all discovery (except
  expert) must be completed at least 30 days before trial;
  expert discovery at least 15 days before trial
- **LASC rule 3.15** — Ex parte applications: filed in the
  assigned department; must include a declaration of notice
  or excuse from notice; ex parte hearing typically at
  8:30 a.m. or 1:30 p.m.

### Department-specific practices

LASC has numerous departments, each with its own individual
courtroom rules posted online. Always consult the assigned
department's courtroom-specific information before setting
a hearing.

- **Stanley Mosk Courthouse** (111 N. Hill St., Los Angeles):
  general unlimited civil; Departments 1-87+
- **Spring Street Courthouse**: complex litigation panels
- **Beverly Hills, Long Beach, Pomona, Pasadena, Van Nuys,
  Torrance, Compton** — district courthouses with their own
  local departments

### Online scheduling

LASC has a web-based reservation system for motion hearings
in many departments:
https://www.lacourt.org/tentativeRulingNet/

Tentative rulings for many LASC civil departments are posted
online by 12:00 noon the court day before the hearing. A
party wishing to contest a tentative must notify the court
and opposing counsel by 4:00 p.m. the day before.

## San Francisco Superior Court (SFSC)

**Website**: https://www.sfsuperiorcourt.org/
**Local rules**: https://www.sfsuperiorcourt.org/divisions/civil/local-rules

### Civil rules

- **SFSC rule 9.3** — Complex Litigation Department: cases
  designated complex under CRC 3.400 are assigned to the
  Complex Litigation Department (typically Department 302)
- **SFSC rule 10.4** — Law and motion: hearings in Departments
  501-502; motions must be reserved through the court's online
  reservation system
- **SFSC rule 10.6** — Tentative rulings: posted by 3:00 p.m.
  the court day before the hearing; parties who do not request
  oral argument by 4:00 p.m. waive the right to be heard
- **SFSC rule 11** — Ex parte applications: filed in Department
  501; heard Monday-Friday at 9:00 a.m.
- **SFSC rule 12** — Mandatory settlement conferences:
  required in unlimited civil cases; typically set 30-60 days
  before trial

### Civil case management (SFSC)

SFSC uses a "Fast Track" system under which cases are assigned
to one of three tracks (expedited, standard, complex) and
assigned to a judge from filing. The assigned judge manages the
case from CMC to trial.

## Orange County Superior Court (OCSC)

**Website**: https://www.occourts.org/
**Local rules**: https://www.occourts.org/general-public/local-rules/

### Civil rules

- **OCSC rule 354** — Law and motion reservations required;
  use the court's online scheduling system
- **OCSC rule 358** — Tentative rulings: posted by 1:30 p.m.
  the court day before the hearing; parties must notify the
  court by 4:00 p.m. if they want to argue; otherwise
  tentative becomes final
- **OCSC rule 360** — Complex litigation: designated complex
  cases go to the Probate Court Complex Civil Program
  (Department C16 or C17)
- **OCSC rule 380** — Discovery motions require meet-and-confer
  declaration and an informal discovery conference (IDC) before
  a hearing will be scheduled in most departments

### Courthouses

- **Central Justice Center** (700 Civic Center Drive W., Santa
  Ana): general civil and complex
- **Lamoreaux Justice Center** (North Orange County): north-
  county unlimited civil
- **Harbor Justice Center** (south county)

## General rules applicable statewide (CRC + local interaction)

### CRC 3.20 — Limits on local rules

Local rules may not:
- (1) be inconsistent with any other statute or rule of court
- (2) require the filing of papers that would otherwise not
  require filing
- (3) impose requirements beyond those required by the CRC
  without good cause

This limits the extent to which a court can impose unique or
burdensome local procedures. A local rule that conflicts with
the CRC is invalid.

### CRC 3.400-3.403 — Complex litigation

Cases may be designated complex if they involve multiple
parties, difficult legal or factual issues, and substantial
post-judgment judicial supervision. Complex cases are assigned
to a single judge who manages the case through trial. Complex
designation must be requested by a party or ordered sua sponte.

Key complex indicators: multiple parties with separate
representation, cross-claims or coordination issues, novel
legal questions, anticipated discovery involving more than
20 depositions, or potential class action designation.

### CRC 2.540 — Electronic filing and service

Electronic filing is now mandatory in most California Superior
Courts for many case types. Each court has an approved
electronic filing service provider. Local rules supplement
CRC 2.540 with court-specific portal information.

## Ex parte applications — statewide rules

Under CRC 3.1200-3.1207, an ex parte application requires:
- Notice to all parties (unless notice is excused for good
  cause or is unnecessary because of the nature of the relief)
- Notice by 10:00 a.m. the court day before the hearing
  (CRC 3.1203)
- Declaration explaining why notice was given, or why it
  could not be or should not be required
- Grounds: irreparable harm, immediate danger, or other good
  cause

Each court implements ex parte practice through local rules;
consult the local rules and the assigned department's
courtroom information.

## ADR local rules

CRC 3.890-3.898 establish the framework for court-ordered
alternative dispute resolution (ADR). Most California Superior
Courts have local ADR rules:

- Mandatory mediation programs for unlimited civil cases (LASC,
  SFSC, OCSC, and others)
- Judicial Arbitration for cases under the applicable
  amount-in-controversy ceiling (varies by court)
- Neutral evaluation programs

ADR proceedings are confidential under Cal. Evid. Code
§§ 1115-1128 (mediation confidentiality).

## Inquiring about a local rule

When the user asks about a specific local rule, the workflow is:

1. Identify the court (LASC? SFSC? OCSC? Other?)
2. Fetch the current local rules from the court's website
3. Locate the relevant rule
4. Quote the verbatim text and note the effective date
5. Flag any recent amendments

Never paraphrase from memory in a filing or court-facing
communication — pull the current text from the court's website.
