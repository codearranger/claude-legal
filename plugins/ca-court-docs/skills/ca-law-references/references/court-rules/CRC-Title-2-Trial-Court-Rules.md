## Overview

CRC Title 2 governs the mechanics of filing documents in California trial courts. Division 1 (General Provisions), Division 2 (Format of Papers), Division 3 (Filing and Service), and Division 4 (Electronic Filing and Service) are the divisions most relevant to civil practice. Every filed document must comply with Division 2 format rules; noncomplying documents may be rejected at filing or stricken on motion.

---

## Division 1 — General Provisions

### Rule 1.20 — Redaction of Personal Information

**Summary.** Parties must redact or omit specified personal identifiers from documents filed in court to protect privacy. The rule applies to paper and electronic filings alike.

**Operative text (key subdivisions):**

```
(a) Definitions.
    As used in this rule:
    (1) "Personal identifier" means a Social Security number, driver's
    license number, financial account number, date of birth (except year),
    and home address.

(b) Redaction required.
    Except as provided in subdivision (c), a party must not include, or
    must redact where inclusion is otherwise required, the following
    personal identifiers from documents filed in the court:
    (1) A party's full Social Security number;
    (2) A party's full financial account number;
    (3) A minor's full date of birth;
    (4) A minor's full name.

(c) Exceptions.
    The following documents are excluded from the redaction requirement:
    (1) Documents filed under seal pursuant to a court order;
    (2) Documents submitted to the court for an in camera inspection;
    (3) Documents protected by existing law.
```

**Cross-reference:** CRC 1.201 (further privacy protections); CCP § 367.3 (sealed records).

## Division 2 — Format of Papers

### Rule 2.100 — Application of Division 2

**Summary.** Division 2 (Rules 2.100–2.119) applies to every document submitted for filing in a trial court, including exhibits attached to declarations, unless a specific rule exempts the document.

```
(a) Scope.
    The rules in this division apply to all documents filed in the
    trial courts of California except as provided in (b).

(b) Exceptions.
    The following documents are exempt from the requirements of this
    division:
    (1) Preprinted Judicial Council forms;
    (2) Documents produced by a court's case management system;
    (3) Documents not intended to be filed, such as process served on
    a party.
```

---

### Rule 2.101 — Page Size, Margins, and Lines

**Summary.** Filed documents must be on letter-size paper (8.5 × 11 inches), with specific margin requirements on all sides, and with numbered lines on each page.

```
(a) Paper.
    All documents must be filed on 8½-by-11-inch paper.

(b) Margins.
    Documents must have the following margins:
    (1) Left margin: at least one inch;
    (2) Right margin: at least one-half inch;
    (3) Top margin: at least one inch on the first page; at least
    three-quarters inch on subsequent pages;
    (4) Bottom margin: at least three-quarters inch.

(c) Line numbers.
    Each page must have a line number at the left margin. Line numbers
    must appear at evenly spaced intervals, be aligned with the text,
    and run consecutively on each page. The number of lines per page
    must be between 26 and 28.

(d) Headers and footers.
    (1) The footer on each page must contain the page number, centered
    or right-aligned.
    (2) A document title may appear in a header.
```

---

### Rule 2.102 — Font and Type Size

**Summary.** Documents must use a legible font; typewritten documents must use a 12-point or larger font; handwritten documents must be legible.

```
(a) Typewritten or printed text.
    Text must be in a font no smaller than 12 points.

(b) Proportional fonts.
    The font must be proportional (e.g., Times New Roman, Arial,
    or similar). Monospaced fonts (e.g., Courier) may be used if
    no smaller than 12-point.

(c) Handwritten documents.
    Handwritten documents must be legible and written in dark ink.
```

---

### Rule 2.103 — Spacing

**Summary.** Text must be at least one-and-a-half-spaced (with double-spacing as the alternative) except for indented quotations, footnotes, and certain other material.

```
(a) Spacing.
    Text must be at least 1.5-spaced. The following material may be
    single-spaced:
    (1) Quoted material that is indented from both margins;
    (2) Footnotes;
    (3) Headings and subheadings;
    (4) Captions;
    (5) Lists or tables.

(b) Double-spacing.
    Double-spacing satisfies the requirement of subdivision (a).
```

---

### Rule 2.104 — Two-Hole Punching

**Summary.** All paper filings must be two-hole punched at the top center of each page, with holes approximately two and three-quarter inches apart.

```
Paper documents must have two holes, each approximately three-eighths
inch in diameter, at the top center of each page, centered approximately
one-half inch from the top and approximately two and three-quarters
inches apart.
```

---

### Rule 2.105 — Binding

**Summary.** Multiple pages of a document must be secured with a binder clip or similar device, not stapled, for filing, unless the court directs otherwise.

---

### Rule 2.106 — Tabs and Exhibits

**Summary.** When a document has exhibits, each exhibit must be separated by a tab and identified by exhibit letter or number in the caption of the exhibit.

```
(a) Tabs.
    When a document is submitted with exhibits, each exhibit must be
    separated by a tab labeled with the exhibit number or letter that
    corresponds to the reference in the main document.

(b) Index.
    A document with five or more exhibits should include an index of
    exhibits.
```

---

### Rule 2.107 — Cover or First Page

**Summary.** Every document must have a cover page (or a first page) containing the information required by Rule 2.111 (caption) unless the document is a Judicial Council form.

---

### Rule 2.108 — Electronic Documents

**Summary.** Electronic documents submitted for filing are subject to the same format requirements as paper documents, adapted for electronic media (font, margins, etc.); page-size requirements apply to the rendered document.

---

### Rule 2.110 — Definitions

**Summary.** Provides definitions for terms used throughout Division 2, including "document," "filed," "lodged," and "submitted."

---

### Rule 2.111 — Caption

**Summary.** Every filed document must have a caption on its first page that includes the court name, parties' names, case number, document title, and hearing information (date, time, department). The caption is one of the most-cited format rules in California civil practice.

```
(a) Contents.
    The caption of every document must include all of the following:
    (1) The name of the court;
    (2) The title of the action or proceeding;
    (3) The number of the case;
    (4) The title of the document (see rule 2.112);
    (5) In a party's initial filing in an action or proceeding, the
    party's address for service and telephone number;
    (6) The date, time, and department in which the matter is scheduled
    to be heard, if applicable.

(b) Party designations.
    The title of the action must include:
    (1) The names of all parties unless the action has been given a
    short title by the court;
    (2) The designation of each party (e.g., "Plaintiff," "Defendant,"
    "Petitioner," "Respondent").

(c) Attorney information.
    Below the case information, and before the caption begins, the
    first page must contain:
    (1) The name, address, telephone number, fax number, e-mail address,
    and State Bar number of the attorney(s) of record, or, for a
    self-represented party, the party's name, address, and telephone number.
    (2) The name of the party the attorney represents.
```

**Practice note.** The first 4.5 inches of the first page (left side, lines 1–10 approximately) are reserved for attorney/party contact information. The court name and title begin at approximately line 8 at the center or right portion.

---

### Rule 2.112 — Document Title

**Summary.** Every document must have a title that identifies the document type. Combined motions must separately list each component.

```
(a) Title required.
    Every filed document must have a title.

(b) Combined documents.
    When a document combines two or more documents (e.g., a notice of
    motion and memorandum of points and authorities), the title must
    identify each separate document incorporated.

(c) Clarity.
    The title must describe the nature of the document accurately.
    For example:
    - "NOTICE OF MOTION AND MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER RESPONSES
      TO FORM INTERROGATORIES, SET ONE; MEMORANDUM OF POINTS AND
      AUTHORITIES; DECLARATION OF [NAME]"
```

---

### Rule 2.113 — Signature

**Summary.** Every document filed by an attorney or self-represented party must be signed. An electronic signature "/s/ [Name]" satisfies the requirement for electronically filed documents.

```
(a) Requirement.
    Every pleading, petition, written notice of motion, or other paper
    filed or served must be signed by the attorney of record or, if the
    party is self-represented, by the party.

(b) Electronic signature.
    An electronic signature ("/s/ [attorney name]") satisfies the
    signature requirement for electronically filed documents.

(c) Effect.
    A signature constitutes a certificate that the attorney has read
    the document; that to the best of the attorney's knowledge,
    information, and belief, there is good ground to support it; and
    that it is not interposed for delay.
```

---

### Rule 2.116 — Pagination

**Summary.** Pages must be consecutively numbered beginning with page 1 at the first page of text (after any cover page). The pagination must appear at the bottom of each page.

---

### Rule 2.117 — Provisions Applicable to Parties Represented by Counsel

**Summary.** When a party is represented by counsel, documents must identify the attorney's State Bar number, firm name, address, phone, fax, and email on the cover page.

---

### Rule 2.119 — Noncomplying Documents

**Summary.** A court clerk may decline to file a document that does not comply with this division, or the court may strike a document on motion or on its own motion. The court must allow a party a reasonable opportunity to correct a format deficiency before striking.

```
(a) Rejection at filing.
    A court clerk may refuse to file a document that does not comply
    with the rules in this division.

(b) Striking.
    The court may strike a document that does not comply with this
    division on its own motion or on the motion of a party. Before
    striking, the court must give the party that filed the noncomplying
    document notice and an opportunity to correct the deficiency.

(c) Waiver.
    A failure to object to noncompliance waives the objection, except
    that the court may strike on its own motion at any time before
    judgment.
```

## Division 3 — Filing and Service

### Rule 2.200 — Filing by Fax

**Summary.** Courts may allow fax filing under local rules; the filed document must be legible and the original retained by the filing party for a specified period.

---

### Rule 2.210 — Lodging Documents

**Summary.** A document is "lodged" when it is submitted to the court for the judge's consideration but not formally filed (e.g., proposed orders, confidential declarations). A lodged document becomes part of the record only if ordered filed.

---

### Rule 2.251 — Electronic Service

**Summary.** Service by electronic means (email or electronic service provider) is authorized where the served party has consented. Consent may be express or inferred from participation in a mandatory e-filing system.

```
(a) Authorization.
    A document may be served electronically if:
    (1) The party to be served has agreed to electronic service or
    is required to accept electronic service under these rules or
    a court order; and
    (2) Service is accomplished in the manner specified in this rule.

(b) Consent.
    A party or attorney who registers for electronic filing or service
    through a court's electronic filing service provider consents to
    electronic service through that provider.

(c) When effective.
    Electronic service is complete at the time of transmission, but is
    not effective if the party to be served learns that the transmission
    did not reach the party or the party's electronic service address.

(d) Extension of time.
    When a document is served electronically, any period calculated
    from service is extended by two court days. (See also CCP § 1010.6.)
```

---

### Rule 2.252 — Electronic Filing — General Provisions

**Summary.** Electronic filing ("e-filing") is the submission of documents to the court through an approved electronic filing service provider (EFSP) or directly through the court's portal. E-filed documents have the same legal effect as paper-filed documents.

---

### Rule 2.253 — Electronic Filing — Mandatory E-Filing

**Summary.** The Judicial Council may designate courts or case types for mandatory electronic filing. In courts where e-filing is mandatory, paper filing is permitted only on a showing of good cause (e.g., technological inability).

```
(a) Mandatory e-filing.
    The Judicial Council may adopt rules or courts may establish
    local rules requiring electronic filing in specified case types.

(b) Exemption.
    A party that is unable to file electronically may request a
    court order exempting that party from the mandatory e-filing
    requirement. The request must show good cause, such as lack of
    internet access or technology.

(c) Self-represented parties.
    Self-represented parties are generally exempt from mandatory
    e-filing requirements unless the court provides e-filing without
    cost and with adequate technical assistance.
```

---

### Rule 2.254 — Electronic Filing — Technical Requirements

**Summary.** Documents submitted for e-filing must be in PDF format (PDF/A preferred), must be text-searchable where practicable, and must not exceed the court's file-size limits. Scanned images that are not text-searchable are disfavored but accepted.

---

### Rule 2.255 — Electronic Filing — Confirmation and Date of Filing

**Summary.** The date of filing for an e-filed document is the date the court's electronic filing system accepts the document, not the date it is reviewed by the clerk. A confirmed e-filing transaction creates a filing timestamp that controls deadlines.

```
(a) Date of filing.
    A document that is electronically filed with the court is deemed
    filed on the date the court's electronic filing system receives
    the document, if the document is subsequently accepted for filing.

(b) Confirmation.
    The electronic filing service provider must provide the filer with
    a confirmation notice that includes the date and time of filing.

(c) Rejection.
    If the clerk rejects a document after initial acceptance, the
    filing date is the date of resubmission (after correcting the
    deficiency), not the original transmission date.
```

---

### Rule 2.256 — Electronic Filing — Format of Electronic Documents

**Summary.** E-filed documents must use the same format as paper documents (same margins, font, page size); the document must be formatted for 8.5 × 11 inches. Bookmarks are encouraged for lengthy documents.

---

### Rule 2.257 — Electronic Signature Requirements

**Summary.** An electronic document may be signed by (a) applying a digital signature or (b) including "/s/ [name]" in the signature block. The attorney remains responsible for the content of e-filed documents.

```
(a) Types of electronic signatures.
    A signature on an electronic document may be:
    (1) A digital signature that complies with the court's or EFSP's
    requirements; or
    (2) The typed name of the signer preceded by "/s/" (e.g., "/s/ Jane Doe").

(b) Multiple signatures.
    When a document requires the signatures of more than one party
    (e.g., a stipulation), each party must provide an electronic
    signature.

(c) Responsibility.
    The attorney of record is responsible for the accuracy of any
    electronic signature on a document filed under that attorney's
    login credentials.
```

---

### Rule 2.258 — Electronic Filing — Service Through the EFSP

**Summary.** A court's electronic filing service provider may serve conformed copies on parties who have consented to electronic service; service through the EFSP satisfies the service requirements of CCP § 1010.6.

---

### Rule 2.259 — Electronic Filing — Access to Electronic Records

**Summary.** Electronic court records are subject to the same access and privacy rules as paper records; sealed records remain sealed regardless of format.

---

### Rule 2.260 — Electronic Filing — Signatures on Original Documents

**Summary.** When an electronic document requires an original wet signature (e.g., a declaration under penalty of perjury), the signing party must sign a paper copy that the filing attorney retains; the electronic file contains the typed "/s/ [Name]" signature.

---

### Rule 2.261 — Electronic Filing — Retention of Original Documents

**Summary.** The attorney or self-represented party who e-files a document with an original wet signature must retain the signed original for the period specified by court rules (generally the duration of the case plus one year or until the time for appeal has expired).

```
(a) Retention requirement.
    An attorney or party that electronically files a document that
    was originally signed on paper must retain the original document
    for one year after the time for appeal expires in the action or
    proceeding, or until final resolution, whichever is later.

(b) Production on demand.
    The original document must be produced for inspection by the court
    or a party upon request.
```

## Division 4 — Exhibits

### Rule 2.300 — Exhibits Generally

**Summary.** Exhibits attached to court filings must be identified by a numbered or lettered tab and must include a cover page or page reference identifying the source. An exhibit that was created in a foreign language must be accompanied by a certified English translation.

---

### Rule 2.306 — Exhibits — Authentication

**Summary.** Exhibits submitted with motions or declarations must be properly authenticated. The submitting party's declaration must state the basis for authentication (e.g., personal knowledge, certified copy).

## Checklist — Format Compliance (CRC 2.100–2.119)

- [ ] 8.5 × 11-inch paper
- [ ] Left margin ≥ 1 inch; right margin ≥ 0.5 inch; top margin ≥ 1 inch (first page); bottom margin ≥ 0.75 inch
- [ ] Font ≥ 12 point, proportional
- [ ] Spacing ≥ 1.5 (body text)
- [ ] Line numbers, 26–28 lines per page
- [ ] Two-hole punch, centered at top
- [ ] Caption per Rule 2.111: court name, parties, case number, document title, hearing date/time/dept
- [ ] Document title per Rule 2.112
- [ ] Attorney bar number, address, phone, fax, email on page 1
- [ ] Consecutive page numbering starting at 1
- [ ] Personal identifiers redacted per Rule 1.20
- [ ] Exhibits tabbed and indexed (if 5+)
