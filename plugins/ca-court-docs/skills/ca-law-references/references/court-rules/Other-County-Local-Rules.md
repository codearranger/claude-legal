## Overview

California has 58 superior courts, one per county. This file surveys the eight next-most-populous counties after Los Angeles, San Francisco, and Orange: San Diego, Santa Clara, Alameda, Riverside, San Bernardino, Sacramento, Contra Costa, and Fresno. For each court, this file provides: courthouse addresses, key local rule features, URLs, and notable distinctions from the CRC baseline.

---

## 1. San Diego Superior Court (SDSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.sdcourt.ca.gov/pls/portal/docs/PAGE/SDCOURT/GENERALINFORMATION/FORMS/RULESANDFORMS/SD001.PDF
- **e-Filing portal:** https://efiling.sdcourt.ca.gov/
- **Main civil courthouse:** Hall of Justice, 330 West Broadway, San Diego, CA 92101

### Notable Features

**Tentative rulings.** Most civil departments post tentative rulings by 2:30 p.m. the court day before the hearing. The deadline to request oral argument is typically by 4:00 p.m. the day before. Check each department's web page for variations.

**Complex litigation.** Complex cases are assigned to the Complex Civil Litigation Division at the Hall of Justice. The Complex Division has a dedicated judge and frequently issues standing orders covering the full case lifecycle.

**IDC requirement.** San Diego requires a "Mandatory Discovery Conference" (equivalent to an IDC) in unlimited civil departments before certain discovery motions. The conference is before the assigned judge or a neutral evaluator. Check the department's standing order.

**North County Division.** San Diego has a major branch courthouse at Vista (325 S. Melrose Drive). Cases filed in North County follow the same local rules but may have department-specific procedures.

**CRC 2.111 caption format.** SDSC strictly enforces the caption rules (CRC 2.111); documents filed without proper captions are routinely rejected by the clerk's office.

**Pro se clinic.** SDSC operates a robust Self-Help Center at the Hall of Justice; self-represented litigants can obtain assistance with forms and procedures.

## 2. Santa Clara Superior Court (SCSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.scscourt.org/court_divisions/civil/civil_rules.shtml
- **e-Filing portal:** https://efiling.scscourt.org/
- **Main civil courthouse:** Downtown Courthouse, 191 N. First Street, San Jose, CA 95113

### Notable Features

**Tentative rulings.** SCSC posts tentative rulings on its website by 2:00 p.m. the court day before the hearing. To request oral argument, contact the department by 4:00 p.m. that same day. SCSC's tentative ruling system is among the most user-friendly in the state; rulings are searchable by case number.

**Mandatory e-filing.** Mandatory e-filing in unlimited civil cases for represented parties. The court uses One Legal and File & Serve as authorized EFSPs.

**"Direct Calendar" system.** SCSC uses a "Direct Calendar" (DC) system: each case is assigned to a single judge at filing, and all law and motion goes to the assigned judge (not a separate law and motion department). This eliminates the need to reserve a hearing date in a separate law and motion department.

**Discovery motions.** The assigned judge hears all discovery motions. An IDC is not universally required but many judges in the complex and unlimited civil departments strongly encourage it. Check the assigned judge's standing order.

**Complex case.** The Complex Civil Litigation program is typically assigned to Departments 1 or 2 of the Downtown Courthouse.

**Working copies.** Working copies (courtesy copies) are required in most departments for motions. Deliver one working copy to the department with a "Working Copy" cover page.

## 3. Alameda County Superior Court (ACSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.alameda.courts.ca.gov/courts/forms/local-rules
- **e-Filing:** https://efiling.alameda.courts.ca.gov/
- **Main civil courthouse:** Rene C. Davidson Courthouse, 1225 Fallon St., Oakland, CA 94612; Hayward Hall of Justice (Hayward)

### Notable Features

**Two main courthouses.** The Rene C. Davidson Courthouse in Oakland handles most unlimited civil cases. The Hayward Hall of Justice handles cases filed in the southern Alameda area.

**Department 22 — Law and Motion.** ACSC handles all law and motion in unlimited civil cases through Department 22 (Oakland). A hearing date must be reserved before filing the motion.

**Tentative rulings.** Department 22 posts tentative rulings on the Alameda courts website. The deadline to request oral argument is typically 4:00 p.m. the court day before.

**Complex litigation.** Complex cases assigned to the Complex Civil Litigation Department (check current department designation on the court's website).

**Meet-and-confer.** ACSC strictly enforces the CRC and CCP meet-and-confer requirements. Declarations of meet and confer must specifically describe the substance of discussions, not merely state that a letter was sent.

## 4. Riverside Superior Court (RIVC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.riverside.courts.ca.gov/localrules/index.html
- **e-Filing:** https://efiling.riverside.courts.ca.gov/
- **Main civil courthouse:** Hall of Justice, 4100 Main Street, Riverside, CA 92501; Murrieta-Southwest Justice Center

### Notable Features

**Two main branches.** The Riverside Hall of Justice handles most unlimited civil cases in the western portion of the county. The Southwest Justice Center in Murrieta handles cases from the Temecula/Murrieta area.

**Tentative rulings.** Riverside Superior Court posts tentative rulings on its website. Verify the specific deadline for each department (varies); most are by 3:00 p.m. the day before the hearing.

**IDC not universally required.** As of 2026, not all Riverside civil departments require a pre-filing IDC for discovery motions. Check the assigned department's standing order.

**Complex litigation.** A dedicated complex litigation program assigns complex cases to specialized departments (Riverside Hall of Justice). For Inland Empire practice, Riverside is the court of primary importance.

**Temecula area cases.** Cases filed at the Southwest Justice Center may face longer motion calendars due to volume. Budget extra lead time when scheduling hearings.

## 5. San Bernardino Superior Court (SBSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.sb-court.org/divisions/civil-division/local-rules
- **e-Filing:** Via authorized EFSPs
- **Main civil courthouse:** Arrowhead Regional Courthouse (previously Rancho Cucamonga Justice Center), 8303 N. Haven Ave., Rancho Cucamonga, CA 91730; San Bernardino Justice Center, 247 W. Third St., San Bernardino

### Notable Features

**Decentralized geography.** San Bernardino is California's largest county by area. Civil cases may be filed at San Bernardino Justice Center, Rancho Cucamonga, Victorville (High Desert Justice Center), or other branches depending on where the cause of action arose or where defendant resides.

**Tentative rulings.** Unlimited civil departments post tentative rulings on the court's website. Contact the department for the specific posting time (varies by department).

**Complex litigation.** Complex cases are assigned to specialized departments in San Bernardino or Rancho Cucamonga.

**Pro per division.** SBSC has an active self-help center serving Inland Empire self-represented litigants.

## 6. Sacramento Superior Court (SACSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.saccourt.ca.gov/general-info/local-rules.aspx
- **e-Filing:** https://efiling.saccourt.ca.gov/
- **Main civil courthouse:** Gordon D. Schaber Courthouse, 720 9th Street, Sacramento, CA 95814

### Notable Features

**Central law and motion.** Unlimited civil law and motion matters are heard in designated departments. Hearing dates are reserved through the clerk's office or online system.

**Tentative rulings.** Sacramento posts tentative rulings on its website by 2:00 p.m. the court day before the hearing (earlier than the CRC default). To request oral argument, notify the department by 4:00 p.m. the day before.

**Government entity defendants.** Sacramento is the seat of California state government; many cases involving state agencies, the Governor's Office, or state boards are filed in Sacramento. Government Claims Act (Gov. Code §§ 900–915.4) compliance is a threshold requirement before suing a state entity.

**Local rule on demurrers.** SACSC has specific local rules requiring the demurring party to schedule the hearing date before filing the demurrer and to serve the notice of hearing with the demurrer papers (not as a separate filing).

**Complex litigation.** The complex case program is housed in designated departments.

## 7. Contra Costa County Superior Court (CCSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.cc-courts.org/courts/local-rules
- **e-Filing:** Via authorized EFSPs
- **Main civil courthouse:** A.F. Bray Courthouse, 725 Court St., Martinez, CA 94553; Wakefield Taylor Courthouse (Richmond)

### Notable Features

**Two main locations.** The Martinez courthouse (county seat) handles most unlimited civil cases. The Richmond courthouse handles cases from the western county area.

**Tentative rulings.** Department-specific; check the assigned department's page. Tentative rulings are generally posted the afternoon before the hearing.

**Master calendar in Martinez.** Law and motion matters in unlimited civil cases are handled through a master calendar in designated departments. A hearing date reservation is required.

**ADR emphasis.** Contra Costa strongly encourages ADR; a full-service ADR program is available. The court will typically order parties to ADR at the first CMC if the case appears amenable to mediation.

## 8. Fresno Superior Court (FSC)

- **Local Rules URL:** https://www.fresno.courts.ca.gov/local-rules
- **e-Filing:** https://efiling.fresno.courts.ca.gov/
- **Main civil courthouse:** B.F. Sisk Federal Building Annex / Fresno Superior Court, 1130 O Street, Fresno, CA 93724

### Notable Features

**Central Valley practice.** Fresno is the primary court for the San Joaquin Valley region. Agricultural disputes, water rights, and commercial cases involving Central Valley industries are common.

**Tentative rulings.** Fresno posts tentative rulings by 4:00 p.m. the court day before the hearing. Request oral argument by contacting the department by 4:00 p.m. that day.

**Discovery.** Standard CRC and CCP discovery rules apply. Meet-and-confer is required; the court enforces the requirement strictly and expects counsel to exchange substantive correspondence, not form letters.

**Complex litigation.** Fresno has a complex litigation program for cases that qualify under CRC 3.400.

## Universal Practice Points for All California Courts

1. **Before filing any motion**, check: (a) the court's local rules, (b) the assigned department's standing order posted on the court's website, and (c) any "department information" page.

2. **Tentative ruling times vary.** Do not assume the CRC default (3:00 p.m.) applies. LASC posts at 8:30 a.m.; OCSC at 1:30 p.m.; SCSC at 2:00 p.m.; FSC at 4:00 p.m.

3. **IDC requirements vary.** OCSC has a near-universal IDC requirement; LASC and SFSC impose it in some departments; other courts rarely require it formally.

4. **e-Filing is mandatory** for represented parties in unlimited civil cases in all major California counties. Paper filing requires a showing of good cause or applies only to self-represented parties.

5. **Department standing orders** frequently override the general local rules. Always read the assigned judge's standing order before filing.
