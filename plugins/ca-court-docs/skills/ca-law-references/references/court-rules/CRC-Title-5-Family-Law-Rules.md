## Overview

CRC Title 5 governs family law proceedings (dissolution, legal separation, nullity, parentage, domestic violence restraining orders, support, and property division) and juvenile court proceedings (dependency and delinquency). The `ca-court-docs` plugin focuses on civil practice; family law is covered here for completeness and for cross-over issues (e.g., enforcement of judgments, qualified domestic relations orders, third-party liability). Practitioners regularly handling family law matters should consult specialized resources in addition to this summary.

---

## Division 1 — General Provisions

### Rule 5.2 — Application of Title 5

**Summary.** Title 5 applies to all proceedings under the Family Code and the Welfare and Institutions Code heard in the family law and juvenile departments of the superior court. Title 3 civil rules apply to the extent they are not inconsistent with Title 5.

### Rule 5.4 — Judicial Council Forms

**Summary.** Family law proceedings require extensive use of mandatory Judicial Council forms (FL- series and CLETS forms). The forms are mandatory where they exist; judicial discretion to accept equivalent non-Judicial-Council pleadings is limited.

## Division 2 — Counsel of Record

### Rule 5.70 — Designation of Counsel

**Summary.** Attorneys in family law proceedings must file a substitution of attorney or notice of limited scope representation when entering or withdrawing from a case. Unbundled (limited scope) representation is expressly permitted in family law under Cal. Rules of Court, rules 5.70–5.83.

## Division 5 — Pleadings and Petitions

### Rule 5.118 — Petition for Dissolution, Legal Separation, or Nullity

**Summary.** A petition for dissolution of marriage (FL-100), legal separation (FL-100), or nullity (FL-100) commences a family law case. The petitioner must also file the summons (FL-110), which is a standard summons with Automatic Temporary Restraining Orders (ATROs) printed on the back.

**Key ATROs.** The FL-110 summons imposes ATROs on both parties from the date of service (respondent) or filing (petitioner), prohibiting: (1) removing minor children from the state, (2) transferring, encumbering, or disposing of property, (3) canceling or modifying insurance. Violation of ATROs can result in contempt or sanctions.

### Rule 5.122 — Proof of Service

**Summary.** The respondent must be personally served with the petition and summons (FL-115 form for proof of service). Service by publication is available if personal service cannot be accomplished after diligent search.

## Division 7 — Discovery and Income and Expense Issues

### Rule 5.260 — Disclosure Requirements

**Summary.** Family Code §§ 2100–2113 require both parties to serve preliminary declarations of disclosure (FL-140, FL-142, FL-150) early in the case. The preliminary declaration of disclosure is mandatory; failure to serve it is grounds for sanctions and may affect property division. Final declarations of disclosure are also required before entry of judgment unless waived.

```
(a) Preliminary declaration of disclosure — deadline.
    The petitioner must serve the preliminary declaration of
    disclosure no later than 60 days after filing the petition.
    The respondent must serve it within 60 days after filing the
    response.

(b) Contents.
    The preliminary declaration includes:
    (1) FL-140 (Declaration of Disclosure);
    (2) FL-142 (Schedule of Assets and Debts);
    (3) FL-150 (Income and Expense Declaration);
    (4) Copies of last 2 years' tax returns.

(c) Final declaration.
    A final declaration of disclosure must be served before entry
    of judgment, unless waived in writing by both parties.
```

### Rule 5.280 — Request for Order

**Summary.** In family law, a "motion" is called a "Request for Order" (RFO, FL-300). The noticing party files FL-300 and sets a hearing. The responding party files a Responsive Declaration (FL-320). The 16-court-day notice rule of CRC 3.1300 applies to RFOs, subject to family law local rules.

## Division 9 — Support

### Rule 5.400 — Guideline Child Support

**Summary.** California uses a mandatory statewide uniform guideline for child support (Family Code § 4055). The guideline formula is complex; judicial council provides a computer program (DissoMaster or XSpouse) to calculate guideline support. Deviation from guideline requires findings. Local courts may have specific RFO procedures for support modification.

---

## Division 10 — Domestic Violence

### Rule 5.490 — Domestic Violence Restraining Order (DVRO) — Emergency

**Summary.** An Emergency Protective Order (EPO) may be issued by law enforcement; a Temporary Restraining Order (TRO, DV-110) is issued by the court ex parte on an application (DV-100) upon a showing of reasonable grounds. A hearing on the permanent DVRO (DV-700) must be set within 25 days of the TRO.
