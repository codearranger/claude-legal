## Overview

CRC Title 7 governs practice in the probate departments of California superior courts, including decedents' estates, conservatorships, guardianships, and trust proceedings. The `ca-court-docs` plugin focuses on civil practice; probate is covered here for completeness and for cross-over issues that civil practitioners encounter (e.g., creditor claims against estates, survival actions, wrongful death, and enforcement of judgments against decedents' estates). Probate specialists should consult the full Probate Code and specialized practice guides.

---

## Division 1 — General Provisions

### Rule 7.1 — Application of Title 7

**Summary.** Title 7 applies to all probate proceedings under the Probate Code. Title 3 civil rules apply to probate proceedings to the extent not inconsistent with Title 7 or the Probate Code.

### Rule 7.3 — Definitions

**Summary.** Key defined terms in probate practice: "decedent," "personal representative" (executor or administrator), "interested person," "petition," "notice of petition," and "order." In probate, a "petition" is the equivalent of a complaint; parties are "petitioners," "objectors," or "interested persons."

## Division 3 — Decedents' Estates

### Rule 7.151 — Petition to Administer Estate

**Summary.** A probate proceeding is commenced by filing a Petition to Administer Estate (DE-111). The petition is set for hearing, with notice published in a newspaper of general circulation and mailed to interested persons. The court appoints the personal representative at the initial hearing.

### Rule 7.175 — Inventory and Appraisal

**Summary.** Within four months of appointment, the personal representative must file an Inventory and Appraisal (DE-160) prepared by a probate referee (appointed by the court). The inventory lists all estate assets and their appraised values; it forms the basis for the estate tax return and creditor claim cap.

### Rule 7.250 — Creditor Claims

**Summary.** Creditors (including judgment creditors) must file a creditor claim against the estate within the later of: four months after appointment of the personal representative, or 60 days after notice of administration is mailed to the creditor. Creditor claims are governed by Probate Code §§ 9000–9399.

**Civil practice note.** If a defendant dies after a civil judgment is entered, the judgment creditor must file a creditor claim in the decedent's estate to enforce the judgment against estate assets. The Enforcement of Judgments Law (CCP §§ 680–724) operates in parallel with creditor claim procedures; counsel should file the claim promptly.

### Rule 7.550 — Court Supervision of Sales

**Summary.** Sales of estate real property and certain personal property require court confirmation (Probate Code §§ 10300–10312). A noticed petition for confirmation is filed; the hearing is set; overbidders may appear. This is distinct from the independent administration of estates (IAEA, Probate Code §§ 10400–10592), under which the personal representative may act without court supervision in many matters.

## Division 4 — Guardianships and Conservatorships

### Rule 7.1000 — Petition for Appointment

**Summary.** A guardianship (for a minor) or conservatorship (for an adult) is commenced by petition (GC-310 for guardianship of person; GC-210 for conservatorship of person). The court appoints an investigator who interviews the proposed ward or conservatee and files a report. A hearing is required.

### Rule 7.1012 — Annual Accountings

**Summary.** A conservator must file an accounting annually with the court (Form GC-400(A)). The accounting must show all receipts and disbursements and the inventory value of the estate. Failure to account timely can result in removal.

---

## Division 5 — Trust Proceedings

### Rule 7.1003 — Trust Petitions

**Summary.** Trust proceedings under Probate Code §§ 17200–17210 are commenced by petition filed in the probate department. A trust petition may seek: instructions on the trustee's duties, approval of accounts, appointment or removal of trustee, construction of trust terms, or termination of the trust.
