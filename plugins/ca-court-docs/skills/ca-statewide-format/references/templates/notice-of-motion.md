# Notice of Motion Template (California)

California's analog to Oregon's "Notice of Hearing" is the
**Notice of Motion**. Under Code of Civil Procedure section 1010,
every motion must be accompanied by a notice to the opposing
party stating the time, date, place, and nature of the hearing.

## Who files it and when

The **moving party** files the Notice of Motion with (or as
part of) the motion papers, after reserving a hearing date
through the court's reservation system (e.g., LASC CRS, SFSC
Reservation System). The Notice of Motion is typically combined
with the Motion itself in a single document titled:

`NOTICE OF MOTION AND MOTION TO [RELIEF SOUGHT]`

Some courts and practitioners file the Notice of Motion as a
separate document from the Motion and Memorandum. Either format
is acceptable.

## Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b) notice requirements

The moving party must serve the Notice of Motion at least
**16 court days** before the hearing. Add additional days if
service is not by electronic transmission or personal service:

- **+5 calendar days** for service by first-class mail
  (Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b); CCP § 1013)
- **+2 court days** for overnight courier
- **+0** for electronic service or personal delivery

Court days = all days except Saturdays, Sundays, and California
judicial holidays (Code Civ. Proc., § 135).

**Exception**: For motions for summary judgment/adjudication,
the notice period is **75 days** (Code Civ. Proc., § 437c(a)).
Opposition is due 14 days before the hearing; reply 5 days before.

## What changes per court

| Court | Reservation system | Notice posting time | Contest window |
|-------|------------------|--------------------|--------------  |
| LASC (Stanley Mosk) | LASC CRS online | 2:00 p.m. day before | Call dept. by 4:00 p.m. |
| SFSC (Dept. 302) | SFSC Reservation System | 4:00 p.m. day before | Call Dept. 302 by 4:00 p.m. |
| Orange County | OC eCourt reservation | 1:30 p.m. day before | Call dept. by 4:00 p.m. |
| San Diego | Dept.-specific | Varies by dept. | Call dept. by time specified |
| Alameda (Dept. 1) | Call or online | 1:30 p.m. day before | Call Dept. 1 by 4:00 p.m. |
| Sacramento | Dept.-specific | Varies | Varies |

Always check the specific department's standing orders for local
variations from the statewide CRC 3.1308 tentative-ruling rule.

## Pro se filer notes

- A self-represented litigant must give the same notice and
  follow the same CRS/reservation process as an attorney
- Include your name, address, phone, and email in the caption
  block and at the bottom of the signature
- Use "Defendant, self-represented" (not "pro se") in the
  signature block for California state courts
- Confirm service on the opposing party: for eFiled documents,
  check that the opposing party's email is registered in the
  eFiling portal for eService; if not, serve by mail (+5 days)
  or personal delivery

## What NOT to include in the Notice of Motion

- Factual declarations (those go in the Declaration)
- Exhibits (those go in the Declaration or as attachments to
  the Separate Statement)
- Argument (that goes in the Memorandum)

The Notice of Motion and the introductory paragraphs of the
combined notice/motion identify the relief sought, the basis
in statute or rule, and the documents on which the motion
relies. The argument itself is in the Memorandum.
