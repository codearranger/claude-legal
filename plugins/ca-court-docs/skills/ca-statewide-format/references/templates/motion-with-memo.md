# Motion with Memorandum Template (California)

A complete scaffold for a California civil motion with an
accompanying Memorandum of Points and Authorities. Compliant
with CRC 2.100-2.119, CRC 2.111, CRC 2.112, and the notice
requirements of Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b).

## Document set for a noticed motion

California motion practice typically involves these separate
documents, each filed as an individual PDF:

1. **Notice of Motion and Motion** (this template)
2. **Memorandum of Points and Authorities** (combined or
   separate)
3. **Supporting Declaration(s)** (see `declaration.md`)
4. **Separate Statement** (required for discovery motions —
   Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1345)
5. **Proposed Order** (see `proposed-order.md`)
6. **Proof of Service** (POS-030 or declaration of service)

Many practitioners combine the Notice of Motion, Motion, and
Memorandum into a single document — this is acceptable and
common.

## Memorandum page limit

Cal. Rules of Court, rule 3.1113(d): memoranda in support of
or in opposition to a motion may not exceed **15 pages** without
a court order. Rule 3.1113(e): a reply memorandum may not exceed
**10 pages** without a court order.

The page limits do not include exhibits, declarations, or
proposed orders — only the memorandum text itself.

## Separate Statement requirement (discovery motions)

For any motion to compel further discovery responses, Cal. Rules
of Court, rule 3.1345 requires a **Separate Statement of
Discovery in Dispute** filed as a separate document. The
Separate Statement lists each discovery request in dispute with:
1. The full text of the request
2. The full text of the response
3. The reason a further response should be ordered

Failure to file the Separate Statement is grounds for denial of
the motion. See `ca-discovery` for the Separate Statement format.

## Opposition and reply timing

Under Code Civ. Proc., § 1005(b):
- **Opposition** due 9 court days before hearing
- **Reply** due 5 court days before hearing

For motions served by mail (+5 calendar days to notice period):
- **Opposition** due 9 court days before hearing (no change —
  the opposition timing runs from the hearing date, not the
  service date)
- **Reply** due 5 court days before hearing
