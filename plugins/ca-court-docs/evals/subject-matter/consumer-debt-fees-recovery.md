# ca-consumer-debt — Fee recovery framework

## Prompt

If I win my consumer-debt case in California, can I get attorney's
fees? I'm pro per — does that affect anything?

## Expected triggers

- `ca-consumer-debt`

## Acceptance criteria

### Fee-shifting authorities

- [ ] **Rosenthal Act** § 1788.30(c) — one-way fee shifting for
      prevailing consumer (broadly available)
- [ ] **FDCPA** § 1692k(a)(3) — federal one-way fees for
      prevailing consumer
- [ ] **Civil Code § 1717** — reciprocal fees on contract claims
      with attorney's-fee clause
- [ ] **CCP § 1021.5** — private-attorney-general fees
- [ ] **CCP § 998** — settlement-offer fee shifting
- [ ] **UCL** (Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200) — generally NO fees
      unless via § 1021.5 PAG theory

### Pro per implications

- [ ] **Pro per cannot recover attorney's fees** for own time
      (general rule; *Trope v. Katz* (1995) 11 Cal.4th 274)
- [ ] BUT can recover statutory damages + actual damages +
      costs
- [ ] CCP § 1032 costs recoverable regardless of pro per
      status

### Statutory damages

- [ ] Rosenthal: $100-$1,000 per violation (§ 1788.30(b))
- [ ] FDCPA: up to $1,000 (15 U.S.C. § 1692k(a)(2))

### Cost recovery (CCP § 1032 / § 1033.5)

- [ ] Prevailing party costs as a matter of right
- [ ] Allowable: filing fees, service, deposition costs, jury
      fees, witness fees
- [ ] Not allowable: photocopies (other than exhibits),
      postage, basic phone

### Memorandum of Costs

- [ ] Form MC-010
- [ ] 15 days from notice of entry of judgment (CRC 3.1700(a))
- [ ] Motion to tax costs: 15 days from service (CRC 3.1700(b))

## Common failure modes

- Telling pro per they can claim attorney's fees for their
  own time
- Missing the *Trope v. Katz* pro-per attorney's-fees bar
- Reporting UCL has fees automatically (it doesn't, absent
  § 1021.5)
- Importing OR's ORS 20.080 demand-letter fees regime
- Importing WA's RCW 4.84 fee framework
- Missing CCP § 998 settlement-offer leverage
- Missing the Memorandum of Costs procedure / deadline
