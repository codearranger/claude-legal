# Integration — End-to-end motion to compel packet

## Prompt

In my LASC debt-buyer case (case # 25STCV01234, dept. 26),
plaintiff served verified responses to my RFPs on May 1, 2026.
The responses are boilerplate objections to every RFP about chain
of title. I want to file a motion to compel further responses.
Walk me through what to do and produce the full packet.

## Acceptance criteria

### Timeline analysis

- [ ] Verified responses served May 1, 2026
- [ ] **45-day jurisdictional deadline** (CCP § 2031.310(c))
      → June 15, 2026
- [ ] Mail extension (5 cal days) — apply if responses were
      served by mail
- [ ] Discovery cutoff (30 days before trial) — check trial
      date
- [ ] Motion-hearing cutoff (15 days before trial)

### Pre-motion requirements

- [ ] Meet-and-confer (CCP § 2031.310(b)(2)) — letter or call
      in good faith
- [ ] Reservation: LASC Court Reservation System at lacourt.org
      BEFORE serving the notice

### Packet contents

- [ ] **Notice of Motion and Motion to Compel Further Responses**
      (CCP §§ 1010, 2031.310)
- [ ] **Memorandum of Points and Authorities** — max 15 pages
      (CRC 3.1113(d))
- [ ] **Separate Statement** — CRC 3.1345 — REQUIRED
- [ ] **Declaration of [movant]** — including meet-and-confer
      declaration; under CCP § 2015.5 with state-of-California
      + place of execution
- [ ] **Proposed Order** — separate document; "[PROPOSED]
      ORDER GRANTING DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO COMPEL FURTHER..."
- [ ] **Proof of Service** — POS-040 or POS-030

### Timing (CCP § 1005(b))

- [ ] 16 court days minimum notice
- [ ] Add 5 cal days for in-state mail (CCP § 1013)
- [ ] Add 2 court days for e-service (CCP § 1010.6)
- [ ] Opposition due 9 court days before hearing
- [ ] Reply due 5 court days before hearing

### Tentative-ruling regime

- [ ] CRC 3.1308 + LASC Local Rule 3.31
- [ ] Posted day before hearing
- [ ] Contest by 4:30 p.m. day before

### Sanctions

- [ ] CCP § 2023.030 — mandatory monetary sanctions
- [ ] Specify amount in proposed order

### Filing logistics

- [ ] LASC e-filing via Odyssey eFileCA
- [ ] PDF with bookmarks (CRC 3.1110(f)(4))

### Pro per conventions

- [ ] "In Pro Per" signature
- [ ] No bar number

### Substantive argument

- [ ] CCP § 2017.010 — broad discovery scope
- [ ] Each objection addressed in separate statement
- [ ] Chain-of-title relevance — Cal. Civ. Code § 1788.58
      FDBPA + UCC Art. 9 (Cal. Comm. Code § 9101+)
- [ ] Standing / authentication argument

## Common failure modes

- Missing the 45-day jurisdictional deadline
- Omitting the separate statement (CRC 3.1345)
- Combining Notice of Motion + Memo
- Citing FRCP 26 / 37 or ORCP 46 A or CR 37
- Using calendar days where CCP § 1005(b) requires court days
- Failing to reserve the date before serving notice
- Missing "[PROPOSED]" prefix on the order title
- Missing CCP § 2015.5 place-of-execution in the declaration
- Failing to invoke § 2023.030 sanctions
- Missing the tentative-ruling protocol reminder
