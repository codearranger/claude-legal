# ca-law-references — Civil rules lookup

## Prompt

What's the standard for a motion for summary judgment in
California civil cases?

## Expected triggers

- `ca-law-references`

## Acceptance criteria

### Operative rule

- [ ] CCP § 437c — California Summary Judgment / Summary
      Adjudication statute
- [ ] *Aguilar v. Atlantic Richfield Co.* (2001) 25 Cal.4th
      826 — leading case on standard

### Timing (CRITICAL CA distinction from FRCP)

- [ ] 75-day minimum notice (CCP § 437c(a)(2)) — significantly
      longer than FRCP 56's effective ~14-21 days
- [ ] Opposition due 14 days before hearing (CCP § 437c(b)(2))
- [ ] Reply due 5 days before hearing (CCP § 437c(b)(4))
- [ ] Hearing must be at least 30 days before trial (CCP
      § 437c(a)(3))

### Required documents

- [ ] **Separate Statement of Undisputed Material Facts** is
      MANDATORY (CCP § 437c(b)(1))
- [ ] Failure to comply with separate-statement requirement
      can be ground for denial
- [ ] Supporting evidence (declarations, depositions, RJN, etc.)

### Citation format

- [ ] California Style Manual: "Code Civ. Proc., § 437c"
- [ ] Case cite: "*Aguilar v. Atlantic Richfield Co.* (2001) 25
      Cal.4th 826"

## Common failure modes

- Citing FRCP 56 standard (federal) instead of CCP § 437c
- Missing the 75-day notice requirement (significantly longer
  than federal)
- Forgetting the separate-statement mandate
- Citing OR's ORCP 47 or WA's CR 56
- Importing the federal Celotex/Anderson standard verbatim
- Mis-citing the case (wrong reporter / wrong year / wrong cite)
