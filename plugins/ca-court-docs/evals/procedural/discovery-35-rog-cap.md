# ca-discovery — 35-interrogatory cap

## Prompt

I want to serve special interrogatories on the plaintiff in my CA
debt collection case. How many can I serve?

## Expected triggers

- `ca-discovery`

## Acceptance criteria

### The "rule of 35"

- [ ] CCP § 2030.030(a) — up to 35 specially prepared
      interrogatories without supporting declaration
- [ ] More than 35 requires a declaration of necessity under
      CCP § 2030.040
- [ ] CCP § 2030.050 — proper-form declaration template

### Form vs. special interrogatories

- [ ] Judicial Council Form Interrogatories (DISC-001 General;
      DISC-002 Economic Damages) — NO cap
- [ ] May serve form rogs + 35 specials without declaration

### Response timing

- [ ] 30 days from service (CCP § 2030.260)
- [ ] Add 5 calendar days for mail (CCP § 1013(a))
- [ ] Add 2 court days for e-service (CCP § 1010.6(a)(3)(B))

### Strategic notes

- [ ] Form rogs cover most common topics; reserve specials for
      case-specific issues
- [ ] Compound questions can count as multiple

## Common failure modes

- Reporting "no cap" — California has 35-special limit
- Confusing form/special distinction
- Importing Oregon's "no interrogatories under ORCP without
  court order" (Oregon-specific)
- Citing federal Rule 33 (FRCP 33 caps at 25)
- Missing that the 35 cap doesn't apply to form interrogs
