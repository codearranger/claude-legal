# ca-discovery — Meet-and-confer requirement

## Prompt

Plaintiff served boilerplate objections to my RFPs in my LASC
case. Responses were served June 1, 2026. What do I need to do
before filing a motion to compel?

## Expected triggers

- `ca-discovery`
- `ca-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### M&C requirement

- [ ] CCP § 2031.310(b)(2) — mandatory meet-and-confer before
      motion to compel further responses to RFPs
- [ ] CCP § 2030.300(b) — same for interrogatories
- [ ] CCP § 2033.290(b) — same for RFAs
- [ ] M&C must be in good faith and reasonable effort

### Form of M&C

- [ ] Letter or call; written record helpful
- [ ] Specific identification of each disputed RFP / response
- [ ] Specific objections to be discussed

### 45-day deadline

- [ ] CCP § 2031.310(c) — 45 days from service of verified
      responses (here, June 1 → July 16)
- [ ] **JURISDICTIONAL** — missing this deadline forfeits the
      right entirely
- [ ] May be extended by written agreement (CCP § 2031.310(c))

### Separate statement requirement

- [ ] CRC 3.1345 — separate statement required for motion to
      compel further

### Sanctions

- [ ] CCP § 2023.030 — mandatory monetary sanctions against
      losing party unless substantial justification or other
      circumstances make sanctions unjust

## Common failure modes

- Skipping the 45-day deadline analysis (jurisdictional)
- Missing the separate-statement requirement (CRC 3.1345)
- Importing OR's ORCP 46 A meet-and-confer (Multnomah SLR
  language)
- Importing WA's CR 26(i) language
- Calling it "motion to compel" generically — California has
  TWO species: motion to compel (no response) and motion to
  compel FURTHER responses (inadequate response). The 45-day
  deadline applies to the latter.
