## Arkansas — pleading standard (fact pleading)

Arkansas is a **fact-pleading** jurisdiction under Ark. R. Civ. P.
8(a): the complaint must plead **facts**, not conclusions, showing
entitlement to relief — a stricter bar than federal notice pleading.
A debt complaint that recites only that "defendant owes plaintiff $X"
without pleading the facts of ownership/assignment is vulnerable under
Ark. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6). **Confirm the current controlling
fact-pleading authority** in the Arkansas Supreme Court and Court of
Appeals (the principle is well settled; pull a current case stating
it before citing in a brief).

## Arkansas — summary judgment ("meet proof with proof")

On a Rule 56 motion, once the movant makes a **prima facie** showing
of entitlement, the burden shifts to the non-movant to **"meet proof
with proof"** and demonstrate a **genuine issue of material fact**.
- *Wallace v. Broyles* — the "meet proof with proof" formulation.
- *Flentje v. First National Bank of Wynne* — applying the standard.

**Debt-defense use, both directions:** force the **plaintiff** to put
**admissible** chain-of-title and account evidence in the record to
make its prima facie showing; where the consumer moves, the plaintiff
must then come forward with admissible proof of each link. Verify the
current formulation and cite a recent case.

## Arkansas — statute of limitations / contract characterization

The written-contract period (Ark. Code Ann. § 16-56-111) versus the
oral/open-account period (§ 16-56-105) turns on whether a sufficient
**writing** exists. **Pull current Arkansas authority** on when
credit-card / revolving-account debt is treated as a written contract
versus an open account before arguing the characterization — it is
outcome-determinative and fact-specific.

## Arkansas — § 16-22-308 prevailing-party fees

Ark. Code Ann. § 16-22-308 authorizes a **discretionary** reasonable
attorney's fee to the **prevailing party** on a contract claim — and
it runs to **whichever** party prevails. Confirm the current
prevailing-party / reasonableness authority (the *Chrisco v. Sun
Industries* factors govern fee reasonableness in Arkansas — verify the
current citation and factors).

## Arkansas — ADTPA after Act 986 of 2017

Act 986 of 2017 amended the ADTPA private right of action
(§ 4-88-113(f)) to require **actual financial loss** caused by
**reliance**, and restricted private class actions. **Pull current
post-2017 Arkansas (and Eighth Circuit / E.D./W.D. Ark.) decisions**
construing the reliance and actual-loss elements before pleading a
private ADTPA claim; the case law is developing.

## Federal — FDCPA (Eighth Circuit, covering Arkansas)

- **"Least sophisticated consumer"** standard governs whether a
  communication is false/misleading or unfair in most circuits,
  including the Eighth Circuit — confirm the current Eighth Circuit
  formulation.
- **Suing on time-barred debt** can violate §§ 1692e / 1692f — see the
  Supreme Court's treatment in *Midland Funding, LLC v. Johnson*, 581
  U.S. 224 (2017) (in the **bankruptcy proof-of-claim** context the
  Court held filing a proof of claim on a time-barred debt was not an
  FDCPA violation) and distinguish the **litigation** context (filing
  or threatening **suit** on time-barred debt). Read both carefully —
  the holding is context-specific — and pull current Eighth Circuit
  authority on suing on stale debt.
- **Statutory damages cap** and **mandatory fee-shifting** under
  § 1692k — confirm current figures in the federal text (`fdcpa.md`).

## Federal — debt-buyer standing / business records

Federal and state debt-buyer cases routinely turn on whether the
plaintiff can authenticate the **original creditor's** records under
the business-records exception (Ark. R. Evid. 803(6) / Fed. R. Evid.
803(6)) and tie **this** account to the chain. **Pull current Arkansas
and Eighth Circuit authority** on the affiant-without-knowledge and
"records of another business" problems before relying (see
`evidence-debt-buyer.md`).
