# SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE — Answer to a debt-buyer complaint (Arkansas)

> **SYNTHETIC / NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Every name, number, date, and
> dollar amount below is **fabricated** for illustration. This is a
> drafting **template**, not a filing and not advice. Do not file it.
> Adapt the caption, allegations, defenses, and dates to your actual
> case, confirm the **answer deadline** (Ark. R. Civ. P. 12; keep day
> counts in `ar-deadlines`), confirm the **forum** (District vs.
> Circuit Court), and verify every rule and statute against current
> law. A self-represented litigant signs for themselves and omits the

                              ANSWER

      Defendant Jane Q. Consumer, appearing pro se (Self-
Represented), for her Answer to the Complaint, states:

                       GENERAL DENIAL

      1.  Defendant denies each allegation of the Complaint not
expressly admitted below, and demands strict proof thereof.

                    RESPONSES TO ALLEGATIONS

      2.  Paragraph 1 (jurisdiction/venue): Admitted that Defendant
resides in Pulaski County. The remaining allegations are denied.

      3.  Paragraph 2 (Plaintiff's status as owner/assignee of the
account): DENIED. Defendant lacks knowledge or information sufficient
to form a belief as to whether Plaintiff owns the account on which it
sues and therefore denies the allegation. Plaintiff is not the
original creditor and has not pleaded facts establishing an unbroken
chain of assignment of this specific account to Plaintiff.

      4.  Paragraph 3 (existence and amount of the debt): DENIED.
Defendant denies that she owes the amount alleged, or any amount, to
this Plaintiff, and denies the existence of any agreement between
Defendant and this Plaintiff.

      5.  Paragraph 4 (account agreement attached as Exhibit A):
DENIED as to legal effect. The document attached as Exhibit A does
not bear Defendant's signature and does not establish that this
Plaintiff owns this account.

                     AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

      6.  FIRST DEFENSE (Failure to state facts — Ark. R. Civ. P.
12(b)(6)). The Complaint fails to state facts upon which relief can be
granted. Arkansas requires fact pleading (Ark. R. Civ. P. 8(a)); the
Complaint pleads conclusions of ownership and amount without pleading
the facts of the assignment chain or the account terms.

      7.  SECOND DEFENSE (Statute of limitations). Plaintiff's claim
is barred by the applicable statute of limitations. The account is an
open account governed by the three-year period of Ark. Code Ann.
§ 16-56-105 (no signed written agreement having been produced), and
more than three years elapsed between the date of last activity and
the filing of this suit. In the alternative, the claim is barred under
Ark. Code Ann. § 16-56-111.

      8.  THIRD DEFENSE (Lack of standing / failure to prove
assignment). Plaintiff cannot prove it owns this specific account
under Arkansas UCC Article 9 (Ark. Code Ann. § 4-9-101 et seq.).

      9.  FOURTH DEFENSE (Lack of capacity — collection-agency
licensing). On information and belief, Plaintiff is a collection
agency required to be licensed by the Arkansas State Board of
Collection Agencies (Ark. Code Ann. § 17-24-101 et seq.) and is not
licensed; Plaintiff therefore lacks capacity to maintain this action.
[Confirm covered-entity status and license status before asserting —
see ar-collection-agency-law.md.]

      10. FIFTH DEFENSE (Lack of privity / no account stated / failure
of consideration). There is no privity, no account stated, and no
consideration between Defendant and this Plaintiff.

      11. SIXTH DEFENSE (Payment / accord and satisfaction). To the
extent any obligation existed, it has been paid, settled, or otherwise
satisfied.

      12. Defendant reserves the right to assert additional defenses,
including discharge in bankruptcy and identity theft, as discovery
proceeds.

                        COUNTERCLAIM
              (Fair Debt Collection Practices Act)

      13. Plaintiff is a "debt collector" and the obligation is a
consumer "debt" within 15 U.S.C. § 1692a.

      14. By filing suit on a debt that is barred by the Arkansas
statute of limitations, and by representing that it is the owner of
the account without the documentation to prove it, Plaintiff made
false or misleading representations and used unfair means in violation
of 15 U.S.C. §§ 1692e and 1692f.

      15. Defendant is entitled to actual damages, statutory damages,
and costs and a reasonable attorney's fee under 15 U.S.C. § 1692k.
[Verify the 1-year FDCPA SOL under § 1692k(d) runs from the filing/
violation — see fdcpa.md.]

with prejudice, award Defendant her costs, grant judgment on the

**Authoring notes (delete before any real use):**

- Caption shape per Ark. R. Civ. P. 10(a); District Court caption
  differs — see `ar-statewide-format`.
- Plead the **SOL** characterization (open account → 3 yr; written
  contract → 5 yr) to your actual facts — see
  `ar-statutes-of-limitations.md`.
- Assert the **licensing** defense only after confirming covered-
  entity status and checking the Board roster — see
  `ar-collection-agency-law.md`.
- The **FDCPA counterclaim** is strict-liability with mandatory
  fee-shifting; weigh whether to plead it in state court or file
  separately in federal court — see `fdcpa.md` and `fees-consumer-debt.md`.
- Consider the **ADTPA** only if reliance-based actual loss is
  pleadable (Act 986) — see `ar-adtpa.md`.
