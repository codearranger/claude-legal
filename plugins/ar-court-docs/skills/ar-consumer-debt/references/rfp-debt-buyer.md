# Requests for Production bank — debt-buyer (Ark. R. Civ. P. 34)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** A drafting aid, not advice. Tailor every
> request to your facts; verify the current Rule 34 mechanics and
> response timing (keep day counts in `ar-deadlines`). Available as
> of right in **Circuit Court** (original filing or de novo appeal);
> District Court discovery is more limited — see the SKILL.md body.

**Rule:** Ark. R. Civ. P. 34 — requests for production of documents
and electronically stored information. Serve with or after the
complaint/answer per the rule; the responding party generally has the
Rule-34 response period (verify current day count) to respond or
object.

These requests target the **chain of title**, the **account
documentation**, and the **collector's license status**. The premise
is simple: the plaintiff must **own** this account, **prove the
amount**, and (if a covered collection agency) be **licensed**.

## Instructions / definitions (include in the set)

- **"YOU" / "PLAINTIFF"** means the plaintiff and its agents,
  servicers, attorneys, and predecessors in interest.
- **"THE ACCOUNT"** means the account identified in the complaint by
  account number ending [____].
- **"THE ORIGINAL CREDITOR"** means [____], the entity that opened the
  account.
- Produce documents as kept in the ordinary course or organized and
  labeled to correspond to the requests.

## Chain-of-title requests

1. The **complete cardholder/credit agreement** governing THE ACCOUNT,
   including all amendments in effect on the date of last activity.
2. Each **Bill of Sale** for every transfer of THE ACCOUNT from THE
   ORIGINAL CREDITOR to YOU, including every intermediate purchaser.
3. Each **Assignment** of THE ACCOUNT, including any that specifically
   identifies THE ACCOUNT by account number.
4. The **account-level data file** (electronic field-level record)
   transferred with each sale that includes THE ACCOUNT.
5. Each **purchase-and-sale agreement** governing each transfer in
   the chain (including exhibits and schedules referenced therein).
6. All documents evidencing that **THE ACCOUNT was within the pool**
   sold in each transaction (the schedule, data file, or load file
   tying THE ACCOUNT to the bill of sale).
7. All **affidavits, certifications, or declarations** YOU intend to
   offer to authenticate any of the foregoing, including any Ark. R.
   Evid. **902(11)** certification.

## Account / amount requests

8. All **periodic statements** for THE ACCOUNT.
9. Documents showing the **date and amount of the last payment** on
   THE ACCOUNT.
10. The **itemization** of the balance claimed, identifying principal,
    interest, fees, and the date from which each was calculated.
11. All documents showing the **terms** authorizing any interest or
    fees included in the amount claimed.
12. Any **charge-off statement** and the charge-off balance.

## Collector-status / license requests

13. YOUR current **Arkansas collection-agency license** (Arkansas
    State Board of Collection Agencies) and proof of the required
    **surety bond**, or all documents supporting any claim that YOU
    are **exempt** from licensure.
14. Documents showing the **license status** of any servicer or agent
    collecting THE ACCOUNT on YOUR behalf.

## Communications / FDCPA requests

15. All **communications** with the defendant concerning THE ACCOUNT
    (letters, the validation notice, call logs/recordings, emails,
    texts).
16. YOUR **policies and procedures** for verifying ownership and the
    amount of purchased accounts before filing suit.
