# Arkansas Commercial-Disputes Practice

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify every statute, SOL, and damages
> multiplier against the current Ark. Code Ann. before relying,
> and consult a licensed Arkansas attorney about your specific
> case.

Use this subject-matter bundle for Arkansas business and
commercial litigation — contract and sale-of-goods disputes,
secured-transaction and chain-of-title issues, trade-secret
misappropriation, business-to-business deceptive-practices claims,
LLC and corporate disputes (including derivative actions and
dissolution), fraudulent transfers, and arbitration.

## At a glance — the things that move every AR commercial case

1. **No dedicated business court.** Arkansas has **not** created a
   specialized commercial or business court. Commercial cases are
   heard in the **civil division of Circuit Court** (an
   administrative division of the unified trial court under
   Amendment 80, not a separate court). Do not look for a
   business-court program — there is none.
2. **UCC at Title 4** — Article 2 sales (§ 4-2), Article 3
   negotiable instruments (§ 4-3), Article 9 secured transactions
   (§ 4-9). See `references/ucc-and-secured.md`.
3. **Arkansas Trade Secrets Act** (§ 4-75-601 et seq.) — the UTSA
   enactment; injunction + damages + **exemplary up to 2x** for
   willful/malicious + attorney's fees. See
   `references/trade-secrets.md`.
4. **ADTPA — the Act 986 of 2017 narrowing.** A private plaintiff
   must now prove an **actual financial loss** caused by
   **reliance** on the deceptive practice; class actions are
   restricted. B2B claims face this reliance/actual-loss element.
   See `references/arbitration-and-fraudulent-transfer.md`.
5. **Arkansas Uniform LLC Act** (§ 4-38-101; **Act 1041 of 2021**)
   — replaced the prior Small Business Entity Tax Pass Through Act
   (§ 4-32-101). See `references/business-entities-and-derivative.md`.
6. **Business Corporation Act of 1987** (§ 4-27-101) — derivative
   actions, dissolution, shareholder remedies. See
   `references/business-entities-and-derivative.md`.
7. **Fraudulent transfer is still UFTA** (§ 4-59-201) — Arkansas
   has **not** adopted the UVTA rebrand. See
   `references/arbitration-and-fraudulent-transfer.md`.
8. **Arbitration: Revised UAA** (§ 16-108-201) with FAA-preemption
   practice. **Fraud must be pleaded with particularity** under
   Ark. R. Civ. P. 9(b).

## Forum — Circuit Court civil division

There is **no Arkansas business court**. Under **Amendment 80**
(effective July 1, 2001), Arkansas merged its former chancery
(equity), probate, and circuit (law) courts into a single
**Circuit Court** of general jurisdiction, administratively
organized into divisions (criminal, civil, probate, domestic
relations, juvenile). Commercial litigation is filed in the
**civil division** of the Circuit Court in the proper county. A
circuit judge may sit in any division; law and equity are
unified, so injunctive and legal relief are available in the same
forum.

## Contracts, sales, and secured transactions — UCC (Title 4)

Arkansas has enacted the **Uniform Commercial Code** at Title 4:

- **Article 1** (general provisions) — § 4-1.
- **Article 2 (sales of goods)** — § 4-2. Governs the formation,
  performance, breach, and remedies for contracts for the sale of
  **goods** (the **4-year** SOL on a sale-of-goods action lives in
  Article 2 — confirm in `references/ucc-and-secured.md`).
- **Article 3 (negotiable instruments)** — § 4-3. Promissory
  notes, checks, holder-in-due-course.
- **Article 9 (secured transactions)** — § 4-9. Attachment,
  perfection, priority, and the **assignment of accounts / chattel
  paper** that drives debt-buyer chain-of-title proof.

For a **common-law contract** (not goods), the SOL is **5 years**
(written, § 16-56-111) or **3 years** (oral/open account,
§ 16-56-105). For **goods**, Article 2's 4-year SOL controls. See
`references/ucc-and-secured.md`.

## Trade secrets — Arkansas Trade Secrets Act (§ 4-75-601 et seq.)

The **Arkansas Trade Secrets Act** (§ 4-75-601 et seq.) is the
Arkansas enactment of the Uniform Trade Secrets Act:

- **Definition** — information with independent economic value
  from not being generally known, subject to **reasonable secrecy
  efforts**.
- **Misappropriation** — acquisition by improper means, or use/
  disclosure without consent.
- **Remedies** — injunction; damages for actual loss + unjust
  enrichment (or a reasonable royalty); **exemplary damages up to
  2x** for willful and malicious misappropriation; and **attorney's
  fees** in defined circumstances.
- **Displacement** — the Act **displaces** conflicting tort
  remedies for trade-secret misappropriation (but not contract or
  other-property claims).

See `references/trade-secrets.md`.

## Business-to-business deceptive practices — ADTPA after Act 986

The **Arkansas Deceptive Trade Practices Act** (§ 4-88-101 et
seq.) reaches deceptive and unconscionable business acts. **Act
986 of 2017 narrowed the private right of action** (§ 4-88-113(f)):

- A private plaintiff must now prove an **actual financial loss**
  as a result of **reliance** on the deceptive practice — an
  actual-damages + reliance element that did not exist in the same
  form before.
- **Class actions** by private plaintiffs are **restricted**.
- The **Attorney General** retains broad enforcement authority
  unaffected by the private-action narrowing.

A **B2B** plaintiff bringing an ADTPA claim must satisfy the
reliance/actual-loss element. See
`references/arbitration-and-fraudulent-transfer.md`.

## Business entities — LLCs and corporations

- **Arkansas Uniform LLC Act** (§ 4-38-101 et seq.) — enacted by
  **Act 1041 of 2021**, replacing the prior **Small Business
  Entity Tax Pass Through Act** (§ 4-32-101). Governs formation,
  member/manager governance, fiduciary duties, derivative actions
  by members, dissociation, and dissolution.
- **Arkansas Business Corporation Act of 1987** (§ 4-27-101 et
  seq.) — governs corporate governance, **shareholder derivative
  actions**, judicial **dissolution**, and shareholder remedies
  (including the oppression / deadlock grounds for dissolution).

See `references/business-entities-and-derivative.md`.

## Fraudulent transfers — still UFTA (§ 4-59-201)

Arkansas's **Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act** (§ 4-59-201 et
seq.) lets a creditor avoid transfers made to hinder, delay, or
defraud creditors (actual intent) or made without reasonably
equivalent value while insolvent (constructive). **Arkansas has
not adopted the UVTA rebrand — it remains UFTA.** See
`references/arbitration-and-fraudulent-transfer.md`.

## Arbitration — Revised UAA (§ 16-108-201) + FAA preemption

The **Arkansas Uniform Arbitration Act** (§ 16-108-201 et seq.) is
Arkansas's enactment of the **Revised Uniform Arbitration Act**
(adopted 2011). It governs the enforceability of arbitration
agreements, the motion to compel, and confirmation/vacatur of
awards. Where interstate commerce is involved, the **Federal
Arbitration Act preempts** conflicting state limits — a frequent
issue. Note Arkansas's historical hostility to certain arbitration
clauses (e.g., mutuality-of-obligation challenges) and the FAA's
preemptive reach over them. See
`references/arbitration-and-fraudulent-transfer.md`.

## Fraud pleading — Ark. R. Civ. P. 9(b)

**Fraud and mistake must be pleaded with particularity** under
**Ark. R. Civ. P. 9(b)** — the who, what, when, where, and how of
the misrepresentation. Combined with Arkansas's **fact-pleading**
regime (Ark. R. Civ. P. 8(a) — the complaint must state facts, not
conclusions), a conclusory fraud allegation is vulnerable to a
Rule 12(b)(6) motion. Plead the elements and the specifics.

## High-yield Arkansas commercial checks

1. **Forum** — civil division of **Circuit Court** (no business
   court).
2. **Goods or services?** Sale of goods → UCC Article 2, **4-year**
   SOL; common-law contract → **5-year** written / **3-year**
   oral SOL.
3. **Trade secret?** Reasonable secrecy efforts? **Exemplary up to
   2x** + fees for willful misappropriation (§ 4-75-601).
4. **ADTPA (B2B)?** Plead **reliance + actual financial loss**
   (Act 986 of 2017); class actions restricted.
5. **Entity dispute?** LLC → Uniform LLC Act (§ 4-38-101, Act 1041
   of 2021); corporation → Business Corporation Act of 1987
   (§ 4-27-101) for derivative / dissolution.
6. **Fraudulent transfer?** **UFTA** (§ 4-59-201) — still UFTA.
7. **Arbitration clause?** Revised UAA (§ 16-108-201); check **FAA
   preemption**.
8. **Fraud count?** Plead with **particularity** (Rule 9(b)) on top
   of fact-pleading.

## Drafting checklist

- [ ] File in the **civil division of Circuit Court** in the
      proper county (no business court).
- [ ] Characterize the contract — **goods (UCC Art. 2, 4-yr SOL)**
      vs. common-law (5-yr written / 3-yr oral).
- [ ] For a trade-secret claim, plead **reasonable secrecy
      efforts**, the misappropriation, and — if willful —
      **exemplary up to 2x** + fees (§ 4-75-601).
- [ ] For a B2B ADTPA claim, plead **reliance** and **actual
      financial loss** (Act 986 of 2017); do not plead a private
      class action.
- [ ] For an entity dispute, confirm the governing act — **LLC Act
      (§ 4-38-101)** or **Business Corporation Act (§ 4-27-101)** —
      and the **derivative-action demand** requirements.
- [ ] For a fraudulent-transfer claim, plead the **UFTA**
      (§ 4-59-201) badges of fraud / constructive elements.
- [ ] If an **arbitration clause** is in play, brief **FAA
      preemption** alongside the Revised UAA (§ 16-108-201).
- [ ] Plead any **fraud** count with **particularity** (Rule 9(b)).
