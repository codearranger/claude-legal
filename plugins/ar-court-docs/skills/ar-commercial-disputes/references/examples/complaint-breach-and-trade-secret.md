# SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE — Business Complaint for breach of contract and trade-secret misappropriation (Arkansas Circuit Court, Civil Division)

> **SYNTHETIC / NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Every name, date, and dollar
> amount below is **fictional** and was invented for illustration.
> This shows document **structure** only — it is not a filing, not a
> form to submit as-is, and not legal advice. Arkansas has **no
> dedicated business court**; commercial cases are heard in the civil
> division of Circuit Court. Adapt the caption, allegations, and dates
> to your actual case; confirm the **statute of limitations** for each
> count (written contract vs. trade-secret misappropriation; keep the
> periods in `ar-statutes-of-limitations.md`); confirm **service**
> under Ark. R. Civ. P. 4; and verify every rule and statute against
> current law. Arkansas is a **fact-pleading** jurisdiction, and any
> fraud or mistake count must be pleaded with **particularity** under
> Ark. R. Civ. P. 9(b). A self-represented filer signs for themselves
> and omits the Arkansas Bar Number; a business plaintiff ordinarily
> must appear through counsel — confirm the entity-representation rule.

**Authoring notes (delete before any real use):**

- Arkansas has **no dedicated statewide business court**; commercial
  cases are filed in the **civil division of Circuit Court** — see
  `ar-commercial-disputes`. Do not caption to a nonexistent business
  court.
- A **business entity** ordinarily must appear through a licensed
  attorney; the attorney signs with the **Arkansas Bar Number** (Ark.
  R. Civ. P. 11). The self-represented signature block is shown only
  for a permitted individual filer — confirm the entity-representation
  rule before relying on it.
- Attach the contract as an **Exhibit** under Ark. R. Civ. P. 10(c);
  it becomes part of the pleading for all purposes — see
  `ar-statewide-format`.
- The **Arkansas Trade Secrets Act** (Ark. Code Ann. § 4-75-601 et
  seq.) allows injunctive relief, actual and unjust-enrichment damages,
  exemplary damages for willful and malicious misappropriation, and
  attorney's fees; keep the exemplary multiplier and fee specifics in
  the references corpus.
- Any **fraud** count must satisfy Ark. R. Civ. P. 9(b)
  particularity; plead the specific misrepresentation, speaker, date,
  reliance, and injury, or omit the count — see `ar-fraud-pleading.md`.
- Confirm the **SOL** for each count (written contract vs.
  trade-secret misappropriation) before filing — keep the periods in
  `ar-statutes-of-limitations.md`.
