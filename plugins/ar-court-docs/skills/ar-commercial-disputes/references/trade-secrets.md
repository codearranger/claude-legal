# Arkansas Trade Secrets Act — § 4-75-601 et seq.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the elements, the exemplary-damages
> multiplier, and the fee/displacement provisions against the
> current Ark. Code Ann.

The **Arkansas Trade Secrets Act** (Ark. Code Ann. § 4-75-601 et
seq.) is Arkansas's enactment of the **Uniform Trade Secrets Act
(UTSA)**.

## What is a trade secret (§ 4-75-601)

Information — a formula, pattern, compilation, program, device,
method, technique, or process — that:

1. Derives **independent economic value** from not being generally
   known to, and not readily ascertainable by, others who could
   obtain value from its disclosure or use; **and**
2. Is the subject of **reasonable efforts** under the circumstances
   to maintain its **secrecy**.

The **reasonable-secrecy-efforts** element is the most commonly
litigated — NDAs, access controls, marking, and need-to-know
practices establish it. Without reasonable efforts, the
information is not a protectable trade secret.

## Misappropriation (§ 4-75-601)

- **Acquisition** of a trade secret by a person who knows or has
  reason to know it was acquired by **improper means**; or
- **Disclosure or use** of a trade secret without consent by a
  person who used improper means, or knew/had reason to know the
  knowledge was derived from improper means or under a duty of
  secrecy.

## Remedies

- **Injunctive relief** (§ 4-75-604) — actual or threatened
  misappropriation may be enjoined; in exceptional cases a royalty
  for a limited period.
- **Damages** (§ 4-75-606) — actual loss **plus** unjust
  enrichment not captured by actual loss; or, in the alternative,
  a **reasonable royalty**.
- **Exemplary damages** — for **willful and malicious**
  misappropriation, up to **twice (2x)** the compensatory award.
- **Attorney's fees** (§ 4-75-607) — to the prevailing party in
  defined circumstances (bad-faith claim, bad-faith motion to
  terminate an injunction, or willful and malicious
  misappropriation).

## Displacement (§ 4-75-602)

The Act **displaces** conflicting tort, restitutionary, and other
law providing civil remedies for trade-secret misappropriation. It
does **not** displace contractual remedies (e.g., an NDA or
non-compete claim) or civil remedies not based on misappropriation
of a trade secret. Plead the statutory claim; preserve contract
counts separately.
