# Arkansas UCC and Secured Transactions — Title 4

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify every SOL, article citation, and
> perfection rule against the current Ark. Code Ann. and the model
> UCC reference.

Arkansas has enacted the **Uniform Commercial Code** at **Ark.
Code Ann. Title 4**. The commercially significant articles:

| Article | Subject | Citation |
|---|---|---|
| Article 1 | General provisions / definitions | § 4-1-101 et seq. |
| Article 2 | **Sales of goods** | § 4-2-101 et seq. |
| Article 3 | **Negotiable instruments** | § 4-3-101 et seq. |
| Article 9 | **Secured transactions** | § 4-9-101 et seq. |

## Article 2 — sale of goods

Governs contracts for the sale of **goods** (movable things).
Key features:

- **Statute of frauds** — a contract for the sale of goods for the
  price of $500 or more must generally be in writing (§ 4-2-201;
  confirm the threshold).
- **Statute of limitations** — a breach-of-sale-of-goods action
  carries a **4-year** SOL under § 4-2-725 (distinct from the
  common-law 5-year written / 3-year oral SOL).
- **Warranties** — express (§ 4-2-313), implied merchantability
  (§ 4-2-314), implied fitness (§ 4-2-315), and their disclaimers
  (§ 4-2-316).
- **Remedies** — cover, market-price, incidental/consequential
  damages, and the buyer's/seller's remedy catalogs.

**Goods vs. services / common-law contract**: characterize the
contract first. A **services** or other non-goods contract runs on
the **common-law** SOLs — **5 years** written (§ 16-56-111),
**3 years** oral / open account (§ 16-56-105) — not the Article 2
4-year clock.

## Article 3 — negotiable instruments

Governs promissory notes, checks, and drafts:

- **Holder in due course** (§ 4-3-302) takes free of many defenses.
- **Enforcement** of a note requires proving the instrument and
  the right to enforce; lost-instrument provisions at § 4-3-309.
- Relevant to debt-buyer cases where the underlying obligation is
  a note.

## Article 9 — secured transactions

Governs security interests in personal property and fixtures:

- **Attachment** (§ 4-9-203) — value + rights in collateral +
  authenticated security agreement.
- **Perfection** — filing a financing statement (§ 4-9-310 et
  seq.), possession, or control depending on collateral type.
- **Priority** (§ 4-9-322 et seq.) — first-to-file-or-perfect.
- **Assignment of accounts / chattel paper** — Article 9 governs
  the **sale and assignment** of accounts, which is the mechanism
  in **debt-buyer chain-of-title** proof: the original creditor →
  debt buyer assignment must be authenticated (Ark. R. Evid.
  803(6) / 902(11)). See `ar-consumer-debt`.

## SOL summary

| Claim | SOL | Citation |
|---|---|---|
| Sale of goods (UCC Art. 2) | **4 years** | § 4-2-725 |
| Written contract (common law) | **5 years** | § 16-56-111 |
| Oral contract / open account | **3 years** | § 16-56-105 |
