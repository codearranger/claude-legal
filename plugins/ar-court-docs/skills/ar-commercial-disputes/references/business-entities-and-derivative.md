## Arkansas Uniform LLC Act — § 4-38-101 et seq. (Act 1041 of 2021)

Arkansas adopted the **Arkansas Uniform Limited Liability Company
Act** via **Act 1041 of 2021** (effective September 2021),
codified at **Ark. Code Ann. § 4-38-101 et seq.** It **replaced**
the prior **Arkansas Small Business Entity Tax Pass Through Act**
(former § 4-32-101 et seq.).

- **Formation / operating agreement** — the operating agreement
  governs internal affairs; the Act supplies default rules where
  the agreement is silent.
- **Management** — member-managed by default; may be
  manager-managed.
- **Fiduciary duties** — duties of loyalty and care, subject to
  the operating-agreement modifications the Act permits.
- **Derivative actions** — a member may bring a **derivative
  action** to enforce a right of the LLC after making a **demand**
  on the company (or pleading why demand is excused / futile).
  Confirm the demand and standing requirements in the Act's
  derivative-action sections.
- **Dissociation and dissolution** — member dissociation;
  judicial dissolution on statutory grounds (e.g., not reasonably
  practicable to carry on, oppressive conduct). Confirm grounds.

**Transition note**: LLCs formed under the old § 4-32 act are
governed by the new § 4-38 act now in force; confirm any
transition provisions for pre-2021 entities.

## Arkansas Business Corporation Act of 1987 — § 4-27-101 et seq.

The **Arkansas Business Corporation Act of 1987** (Ark. Code Ann.
§ 4-27-101 et seq.) governs for-profit corporations:

- **Governance** — board, officers, shareholder meetings, voting.
- **Fiduciary duties** — directors' duty of care / loyalty;
  business-judgment-rule deference.
- **Shareholder derivative actions** (§ 4-27-740 et seq.) — a
  shareholder may sue on the corporation's behalf after a written
  **demand** on the board and the expiration of the statutory
  waiting period (Arkansas's MBCA-based universal-demand approach
  — confirm whether demand-futility is available or whether
  Arkansas requires demand in all cases).
- **Judicial dissolution** (§ 4-27-1430 et seq.) — on grounds
  including director **deadlock**, **illegal/oppressive/fraudulent**
  conduct by those in control, shareholder deadlock, or waste of
  assets. The oppression ground is the principal minority-
  shareholder remedy.
- **Dissenters' / appraisal rights** — for shareholders objecting
  to certain fundamental transactions.
