The Rules of the Supreme Court and Court of Appeals of Arkansas govern practice
in the two appellate courts (the Arkansas Supreme Court and the intermediate
Arkansas Court of Appeals), including the **citation form for Arkansas
appellate opinions** and the **format of appellate briefs**. Cite as
`Ark. Sup. Ct. R. [N-N]` (e.g., `Ark. Sup. Ct. R. 5-2`). E-filing is mandatory
in the appellate courts. The rules below are the ones a litigant most often
needs; verify each against the current official text.

---

## Rule 5-2 — Citation of Arkansas opinions (medium-neutral / public-domain)

Since **July 1, 2009**, Arkansas opinions carry a **medium-neutral /
public-domain citation** assigned by the court, and Rule 5-2 prescribes its
form. The citation is built from the **year + court abbreviation + sequential
opinion number**:

- **Supreme Court:** `YEAR Ark. NNN` — e.g., `Smith v. Jones, 2015 Ark. 100`.
- **Court of Appeals:** `YEAR Ark. App. NNN` — e.g., `Smith v. Jones, 2015 Ark.
  App. 200`.

The opinions also carry numbered paragraphs so that pinpoint cites reference the
**paragraph number** rather than a reporter page (e.g., `2015 Ark. 100, ¶ 7`).
A parallel `S.W.3d` cite may be appended where available. **Pre-July-2009**
opinions are cited to the Arkansas Reports (`Ark.` / `Ark. App.`) plus the
regional reporter (`S.W.2d` / `S.W.3d`). Arkansas has no separate formal style
manual; citation otherwise follows Bluebook conventions as modified by Rule 5-2.

## Appellate briefing — Rules 4-1 and 4-2

Unlike trial-court pleadings (which have no single statewide format rule),
appellate briefs are governed by a **strict format** under the Rules of the
Supreme Court and Court of Appeals:

- **Rule 4-1 — Briefs generally.** Governs the briefs the parties must file
  (appellant's brief, appellee's brief, reply brief), service, and the number of
  copies/electronic-filing requirements. Confirm current copy and e-filing
  requirements against the text.
- **Rule 4-2 — Contents and organization of briefs; the addendum and abstract.**
  Prescribes the required components and their order, including the
  **abstract** (a condensed, impartial summary of the material parts of the
  testimony and proceedings) and the **addendum** (the documents from the record
  essential for the court to understand the case and decide the issues — orders,
  the judgment, pleadings, exhibits, etc.). Rule 4-2 also sets formatting
  requirements (typography, length limits, organization) and provides for a
  **non-compliance / rebriefing** procedure when an abstract or addendum is
  deficient: the court may order the party to file a substituted, compliant brief
  rather than reach the merits. Verify page/word limits and the specific required
  components against the current text.

## Jurisdiction and the appellate courts (Rule 1-2)

Rule 1-2 allocates jurisdiction between the Supreme Court and the Court of
Appeals, identifying the categories of cases that the Supreme Court hears
directly (and the bases on which a case may be certified or transferred between
the two courts). Most civil appeals begin in the Court of Appeals unless they
fall within a category reserved to the Supreme Court.

## Records and the appellate record

The rules govern preparation and lodging of the record on appeal, the deadlines
for docketing the appeal, and the consequences of an untimely or deficient
record. The time for filing the notice of appeal and lodging the record is
governed in conjunction with the Arkansas Rules of Appellate Procedure–Civil
(verify the interaction and the controlling day counts against the current text).
