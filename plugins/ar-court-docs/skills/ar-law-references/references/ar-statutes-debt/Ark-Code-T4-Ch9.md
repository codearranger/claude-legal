## Scope

Title 4, Chapter 9 is Arkansas's enactment of **UCC Article 9 (Secured
Transactions)** — the law governing security interests in personal property
and fixtures, plus the **sale and assignment of accounts, chattel paper,
payment intangibles, and promissory notes.** For consumer-debt litigation,
Article 9 is the **chain-of-title backbone**: when an original creditor
sells a portfolio of receivables to a debt buyer, the transfer is governed
by Article 9 (which treats outright sales of accounts and chattel paper as
covered transactions), and the buyer's standing to sue rests on proving that
assignment chain. Section numbers run in the **§ 4-9-NNN** range, tracking
the official UCC § 9-NNN numbering.

## Scope and key definitions

- **§ 4-9-109 — Scope.** Article 9 applies to a transaction that creates a
  security interest in personal property/fixtures by contract **and to a
  sale of accounts, chattel paper, payment intangibles, and promissory
  notes.** This is the provision that pulls debt-buyer portfolio purchases
  into Article 9.
- **§ 4-9-102 — Definitions.** Defines "account," "chattel paper," "account
  debtor," "payment intangible," "security agreement," "collateral,"
  "financing statement," and similar terms. **"Account"** broadly covers a
  right to payment for goods/services; a charged-off credit-card receivable
  is typically handled as an account or payment intangible.

## Attachment — when a security interest becomes enforceable

- **§ 4-9-203 — Attachment and enforceability.** A security interest
  attaches and is enforceable against the debtor and third parties when
  (1) value is given, (2) the debtor has rights in the collateral, and
  (3) the debtor has authenticated a security agreement describing the
  collateral (or the secured party has possession/control). **Assignment
  relevance:** § 4-9-203 also addresses how attachment carries to a
  transferee.
- **§ 4-9-108 — Sufficiency of description of collateral.** A description is
  sufficient if it reasonably identifies the collateral; for accounts, a
  super-generic "all the debtor's assets" description is insufficient in the
  security agreement (though permitted in a financing statement under
  § 4-9-504).

## Perfection and priority

- **§ 4-9-308 / § 4-9-310 — Perfection generally; filing required.** A
  security interest is generally perfected by filing a financing statement,
  with exceptions for possession/control and automatic perfection.
- **§ 4-9-309 — Automatic perfection.** Lists interests perfected on
  attachment without filing — notably, **the sale of a payment intangible
  and the sale of a promissory note are automatically perfected.** This
  matters because a debt buyer's purchase of certain receivables may be
  perfected without any public filing, so the absence of a financing
  statement does not by itself defeat the transfer.
- **§ 4-9-310 through § 4-9-316 — Perfection by filing; continuity; lapse.**
- **§ 4-9-322 / § 4-9-324 — Priority among conflicting interests; PMSI
  priority.**
- **§ 4-9-501 through § 4-9-516 — The financing-statement filing system.**
  Where to file (the Secretary of State for most collateral), contents of a
  financing statement (§ 4-9-502), the debtor's name (§ 4-9-503), and the
  effect of errors.

## Assignment of accounts and chattel paper — the chain-of-title core

- **§ 4-9-406 — Discharge of account debtor; notification of assignment.**
  An account debtor may pay the assignor until it receives an authenticated
  notification of the assignment directing payment to the assignee. **Debt-
  buyer relevance:** the notification and account-debtor-protection rules
  bear on whether the debtor owes the assignee and on defenses the debtor
  may assert against the assignee.
- **§ 4-9-404 — Rights acquired by assignee; defenses against assignee.**
  Generally, the assignee takes **subject to the terms of the contract
  between the account debtor and assignor and any defense or claim in
  recoupment** arising from that transaction (subject to enforceable waiver-
  of-defenses clauses and consumer-protection limits). **This is the legal
  basis for asserting against a debt buyer every defense the debtor had
  against the original creditor.**
- **§ 4-9-405 — Modification of assigned contract.**

## Enforcement / default

- **§ 4-9-601 — Rights after default.** A secured party may reduce a claim
  to judgment, foreclose, or otherwise enforce by available judicial
  procedure.
- **§ 4-9-609 — Secured party's right to take possession after default**
  (self-help repossession without breach of the peace, or judicial process).
- **§ 4-9-610 / § 4-9-611 / § 4-9-613 / § 4-9-614 — Disposition of
  collateral; commercially reasonable sale; notification of disposition;
  contents of notification (consumer-goods specifics).**
- **§ 4-9-615 / § 4-9-616 — Application of proceeds; explanation of any
  deficiency** (consumer transactions have heightened explanation duties).
- **§ 4-9-625 / § 4-9-626 — Remedies for noncompliance; the rebuttable-
  presumption / absolute-bar rules on deficiency where the secured party
  failed to dispose commercially reasonably.** **Deficiency-defense hook:**
  a creditor seeking a deficiency after repossession must show a
  commercially reasonable disposition and proper notice.

## Drafting note for debt-buyer chain of title

In a debt-buyer collection case, build the chain-of-title defense on
Article 9: (1) require the plaintiff to prove the **assignment from original
creditor to each intermediate buyer to the plaintiff** (bill of sale +
specific account-identifying schedule), authenticated under Ark. R. Evid.
803(6) / 902(11); (2) use **§ 4-9-404** to assert against the assignee every
defense and recoupment claim the debtor had against the original creditor;
and (3) where the claim is repossession/deficiency-driven, test the
disposition against §§ 4-9-610 to 4-9-616 and the §§ 4-9-625 to 4-9-626
remedy rules. Note that automatic perfection under § 4-9-309 means the
absence of a public financing statement is **not**, standing alone, proof
that no valid assignment occurred — the defense is the missing or unproven
documentary chain, not the missing UCC-1.
