## Scope

Chapter 15 is the **Domestic Abuse Act of 1991** — Arkansas's civil
**order-of-protection** regime. It provides an expedited civil remedy
(separate from any criminal charge) for victims of domestic abuse. Petitions
are filed in the **Circuit Court** (Domestic Relations Division) of the county
where the petitioner or respondent resides or where the abuse occurred; no
filing fee is charged for an order-of-protection petition.

## Key sections

- **§ 9-15-101 — Title.** The "Domestic Abuse Act of 1991."
- **§ 9-15-102 — Purpose / legislative findings.** Declares the policy of
  protecting victims of domestic abuse through expedited civil orders.
- **§ 9-15-103 — Definitions.** Defines **"domestic abuse"** (physical harm,
  bodily injury, assault, or the infliction of fear of imminent physical harm
  between **family or household members**, plus sexual conduct against a minor
  family/household member) and **"family or household members"** — the
  **qualifying-relationship** universe that establishes standing (spouses,
  former spouses, persons related by blood or marriage, persons who have or had
  a child in common, cohabitants or former cohabitants, and persons in or
  formerly in a dating relationship — verify the exact relationship list against
  current text).
- **§ 9-15-201 — Petition for an order of protection.** Who may petition (the
  victim, or a parent/guardian/custodian on behalf of a minor or incapacitated
  person), contents, and venue; no cost/bond to file.
- **§ 9-15-205 / -206 — Ex parte temporary order of protection.** On a showing
  of **immediate and present danger of domestic abuse**, the court may issue an
  **ex parte temporary order** without notice, in force until the full hearing.
- **Hearing within a short statutory window.** A full hearing on the petition is
  set promptly after the ex parte order (commonly within roughly 30 days —
  verify the day count against current text), at which the respondent may appear
  and contest.
- **§ 9-15-205 — Relief available in a final order.** The court may, among other
  things: prohibit the respondent from contacting or abusing the petitioner;
  exclude the respondent from a shared residence; award temporary custody and
  visitation; order temporary support; and direct other relief necessary to
  protect the petitioner and household members.
- **Duration of a final order.** A final order of protection is entered for a
  fixed term (commonly stated as no less than 90 days and up to 10 years, with a
  permanent order available in defined circumstances — verify the minimum/maximum
  and the permanent-order grounds against current text), and is renewable.
- **Firearm surrender / federal firearm prohibition.** Entry of a qualifying
  order of protection triggers federal firearm-possession prohibitions (and any
  surrender mechanics the order imposes); flag this consequence for respondents.
- **Enforcement / violation.** Violating an order of protection is a **criminal
  offense** (and may be enforced by contempt); full faith and credit is given to
  out-of-state protective orders.

> **Distinctive features:** a **no-fee, expedited civil** order-of-protection
> track with **ex parte temporary relief** on a showing of immediate danger,
> a defined **qualifying-relationship** standing universe, and **firearm-surrender
> / federal-firearm** consequences on entry. Coordinate with the custody
> best-interest analysis in `Ark-Code-T9-Ch13.md` where abuse is alleged.
