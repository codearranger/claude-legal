## Scope

Chapter 9 confers **tort immunity on Arkansas political subdivisions** —
counties, municipalities, school districts, and special improvement districts.
It is the local-government counterpart to the State's constitutional sovereign
immunity (art. 5, § 20; State Claims Commission at § 19-10-201 et seq., see
`Ark-Code-T19-Ch10.md`). Unlike the State's near-absolute constitutional
immunity, the political-subdivision immunity here is **statutory** and is
**waived to the extent of liability-insurance coverage**.

## Political-subdivision tort immunity (§ 21-9-301)

- **§ 21-9-301 — Immunity from tort liability.** Declares that **counties,
  municipal corporations, school districts, and other political subdivisions** (and
  certain governmental volunteer/board contexts) are **immune from liability and
  from suit for damages** arising from **negligent** acts — **except to the extent
  they are covered by liability insurance**. Where the subdivision carries
  liability insurance, immunity is waived **only up to the policy limits / coverage
  amount**; uninsured negligence claims are barred. (Verify the exact entities
  listed, the insurance-waiver mechanics, and any intentional-tort treatment
  against current text.)
- **§ 21-9-302 et seq. — Related provisions.** Addresses the obtaining of
  liability insurance by political subdivisions and related matters (verify the
  precise sections and their reach against current text).

## Pleading and practice implications

A plaintiff suing a county, city, school district, or similar subdivision in tort
must reckon with § 21-9-301 immunity: the recovery is generally **limited to the
extent of available liability-insurance coverage**, and an uninsured negligence
claim faces dismissal. Plead and confirm the existence and limits of the
subdivision's liability coverage early, and distinguish (against current text and
case law) any conduct that falls outside the negligence-immunity bar.

> **Distinctive Arkansas posture:** Arkansas's immunity scheme is **two-tiered** —
> the **State** has near-absolute **constitutional** immunity (art. 5, § 20; *Bd.
> of Trustees v. Andrews*, 2018 Ark. 12) routed to the **State Claims Commission**
> (`Ark-Code-T19-Ch10.md`), while **political subdivisions** have **statutory**
> immunity under **§ 21-9-301** that is **waived only to the extent of
> liability-insurance coverage**. Identify which tier a defendant occupies before
> framing the claim.
