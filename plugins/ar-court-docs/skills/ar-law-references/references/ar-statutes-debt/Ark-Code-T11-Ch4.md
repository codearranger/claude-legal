## Scope

Chapter 4 contains the **Arkansas Minimum Wage Act** — the state's wage-floor,
overtime, recordkeeping, and wage-enforcement regime. It is the principal
state-law wage-and-hour vehicle and runs parallel to the federal Fair Labor
Standards Act, so a worker frequently has both state and federal theories.

## Arkansas Minimum Wage Act (§ 11-4-201 et seq.)

- **§ 11-4-202 — Declaration of policy.** States the legislative purpose of
  establishing a minimum-wage floor and fair labor standards.
- **§ 11-4-203 — Definitions.** Defines "employer," "employee," "employ," and
  "wage." **Coverage threshold:** the Act reaches employers with **four (4) or
  more employees** — a smaller-employer reach than the FLSA's enterprise test
  (verify the current numeric threshold and any exclusions against current
  text). Independent contractors and certain categories are excluded.
- **§ 11-4-210 — Minimum wage.** Sets the state minimum hourly wage. The rate
  was raised by **Issue 5 of 2018** (a ballot initiative) on a phased schedule
  fully implemented in 2021; the resulting state minimum wage is **$11.00/hour
  (verify against current text)**. Where the FLSA rate is lower, the higher
  Arkansas rate governs covered Arkansas employers.
- **§ 11-4-211 — Overtime.** Requires overtime at **one and one-half times** the
  regular rate for hours worked over **forty (40) in a workweek**, subject to the
  Act's exemptions (verify the exemption list and any tipped-employee /
  tip-credit mechanics against current text).
- **§ 11-4-217 — Records.** Requires employers to keep records of hours worked
  and wages paid and to make them available for inspection.
- **§ 11-4-218 — Enforcement / civil action; remedies.** Authorizes a civil
  action to recover unpaid minimum wages or overtime. Remedies include the unpaid
  wages plus an **additional equal amount as liquidated damages**, plus costs and
  a reasonable **attorney's fee** (verify the liquidated-damages multiplier, the
  limitations period, and the fee-shifting language against current text). The
  Department of Labor and Licensing also has enforcement authority.

> **Distinctive Arkansas provisions:** (1) the **4-or-more-employee** coverage
> threshold pulls in many small employers that fall outside FLSA enterprise
> coverage; (2) the minimum-wage rate is the product of **voter initiative
> (Issue 5 of 2018)**, so it sits above the federal floor — always confirm the
> current dollar figure, which is volatile, against the live statute. Workers'
> compensation is in **Chapter 9** (`Ark-Code-T11-Ch9.md`); right-to-work is in
> **Chapter 3** (`Ark-Code-T11-Ch3.md`).
