## Scope

Chapter 60 collects civil actions concerning real property and possession. For
landlord-tenant practice its key subchapter is **unlawful detainer** — Arkansas's
principal **civil** eviction track (distinct from the criminal failure-to-vacate
remedy in § 18-16-101). The chapter also houses related possessory actions
(ejectment, forcible entry and detainer, and partition appear among the Title 18
real-property chapters; confirm the precise subchapter for any non-eviction
possession claim).

## Unlawful detainer (§ 18-60-301 et seq.)

- **§ 18-60-301 — Definition of unlawful detainer.** Defines when a tenant (or
  other occupant) is unlawfully detaining the premises — typically a holdover
  after the tenancy is terminated or after a default that ends the right to
  possession.
- **§ 18-60-304 — Notice to vacate (3-day notice).** Before commencing the
  action the landlord must serve a written **notice to vacate**; the statutory
  period is commonly **three (3) days** after the demand (verify the exact day
  count, the events that trigger it, and the service method against current
  text).
- **§ 18-60-307 — Complaint; writ of possession; immediate-possession
  procedure.** Authorizes the landlord, on filing the complaint, to seek a
  **writ of possession**. The statute provides a notice-and-objection mechanism:
  the tenant is served and may file a **written objection** within the statutory
  window to contest immediate possession; if the tenant objects, a hearing is
  set, and if the tenant does not object, the writ issues. Verify the objection
  day count and hearing mechanics against current text.
- **§ 18-60-309 — Bond.** Addresses the **bond** requirements — the landlord's
  bond to obtain a writ of possession and the tenant's bond to retain possession
  pending the action (verify which party posts what, and the bond measure,
  against current text).
- **§ 18-60-310 et seq. — Trial / damages / judgment.** Provides for trial of the
  possession question and recovery of possession plus damages (back rent, holdover
  damages) where proven (verify any damages multiplier against current text).

## Forum and appeal

Unlawful-detainer matters are commonly filed in **District Court** (the
high-volume eviction forum) or in Circuit Court; a District Court possession
judgment is appealable to **Circuit Court for de novo review** (30-day appeal
window under the District Court Rules / Ark. R. Civ. P. — verify the current
day count). See `Ark-Code-T18-Ch16.md` for the criminal failure-to-vacate
alternative and the security-deposit rules, and `Ark-Code-T18-Ch17.md` for the
2007 RLTA + 2021 habitability standards.

> **Distinctive Arkansas posture:** Arkansas gives landlords **two parallel
> eviction tracks** — the **civil unlawful detainer** of Chapter 60 (with a writ
> of possession and a tenant objection-or-default mechanism) and the **criminal
> failure-to-vacate** statute (§ 18-16-101). Always confirm which track a filing
> invokes and the exact notice / objection day counts against current text.
