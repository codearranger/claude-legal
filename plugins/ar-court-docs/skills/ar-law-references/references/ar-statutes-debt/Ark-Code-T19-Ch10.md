## Scope

Chapter 10 creates and governs the **Arkansas State Claims Commission** — the
exclusive forum for monetary claims **against the State of Arkansas**. Because
**Ark. Const. art. 5, § 20** declares that "The State of Arkansas shall never be
made defendant in any of her courts," the State enjoys **near-absolute sovereign
immunity** from suit in Circuit Court. The Arkansas Supreme Court reaffirmed in
*Bd. of Trustees of Univ. of Ark. v. Andrews*, 2018 Ark. 12, that the
legislature cannot waive that constitutional immunity. The Claims Commission is
the legislatively created administrative channel through which a claimant may
seek redress that the courts cannot grant.

## Arkansas State Claims Commission (§ 19-10-201 et seq.)

- **§ 19-10-201 — Creation / purpose.** Establishes the **Arkansas State Claims
  Commission** as the body to hear and determine claims against the State that,
  because of sovereign immunity, cannot be brought in the ordinary courts.
- **§ 19-10-202 — Membership / organization.** Sets the composition of the
  Commission and its officers (verify the current membership and appointment
  mechanics against current text).
- **§ 19-10-204 — Jurisdiction.** Grants the Commission jurisdiction over claims
  against the State and state agencies — broadly including tort-type claims, breach
  and contract-type claims, and other demands for money from the State that the
  courts may not entertain (verify the precise jurisdictional grant and any
  carve-outs against current text).
- **§ 19-10-205 — Filing of claims; procedure.** Prescribes how a claim is filed,
  the contents of the claim, and the hearing procedure before the Commission;
  there is a limitations period for filing — confirm the exact period and any
  notice prerequisites against current text (do not assume a day or year count).
- **§ 19-10-209 — Awards; legislative appropriation.** Provides that the
  Commission **determines and certifies awards**, but payment of an award
  generally **requires an appropriation by the General Assembly** — the Commission
  recommends/awards, and the legislature must fund payment. This appropriation
  requirement is a structural feature distinguishing a Commission award from an
  enforceable court judgment.
- **§ 19-10-210 et seq. — Review / appeal.** Addresses review of Commission
  determinations (including legislative review of certain awards); verify the
  current review pathway against current text.

> **Distinctive Arkansas posture:** Arkansas's **constitutional** sovereign
> immunity (art. 5, § 20, per *Andrews*, 2018 Ark. 12) is **near-absolute** — a
> claimant cannot simply sue the State in Circuit Court. The **State Claims
> Commission** is the forum, its awards typically depend on a **legislative
> appropriation** to be paid, and the limitations and filing mechanics differ from
> ordinary civil practice. Note the contrast with **county/municipal** tort
> immunity, which is statutory (§ 21-9-301; see `Ark-Code-T21-Ch9.md`) and waived
> only to the extent of liability-insurance coverage, not routed to this
> Commission.
