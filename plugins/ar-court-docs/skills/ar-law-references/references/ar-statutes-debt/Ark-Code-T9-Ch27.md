## Scope

Chapter 27 is the **Arkansas Juvenile Code** — the omnibus statute governing
proceedings concerning children: **dependency-neglect, families in need of
services (FINS), delinquency, termination of parental rights (TPR),** and
related matters (paternity/legitimation issues touching juveniles, guardianship
in juvenile cases). All juvenile matters are heard in the **Juvenile Division of
Circuit Court**.

## Foundational provisions

- **§ 9-27-301 — Short title.** The "Arkansas Juvenile Code of 1989."
- **§ 9-27-303 — Definitions.** Defines the case types and key terms —
  **"dependent-juvenile" / "dependency-neglect,"** **"family in need of services
  (FINS),"** **"delinquent juvenile,"** **"abuse,"** **"neglect,"**
  **"abandonment,"** and **"juvenile"** (age boundaries; the upper age and the
  extended-foster-care window — verify the age cutoffs against current text).
- **§ 9-27-306 — Jurisdiction.** Vests the **Juvenile Division of Circuit Court**
  with exclusive original jurisdiction over the chapter's case types.
- **Juvenile-court judge / referee structure.** Cases are heard by a circuit
  judge sitting in the juvenile division; describe the referee/hearing-officer
  practice qualitatively where applicable.

## Dependency-neglect, FINS, and delinquency

- **Dependency-neglect.** Proceedings to protect a juvenile who is dependent or
  has been abused, neglected, or abandoned: emergency / **ex parte hold and
  probable-cause** hearing, **adjudication**, **disposition**, and **periodic
  review / permanency-planning** hearings, with the Department of Human Services
  (DHS) as the agency. Reasonable-efforts findings and the federal ASFA timelines
  apply (verify the review/permanency day and month counts against current text).
- **Families in Need of Services (FINS).** A non-delinquency status track for
  juveniles who are habitually truant, run away, or are beyond parental control,
  and their families.
- **Delinquency.** Proceedings charging a juvenile with conduct that would be a
  criminal offense if committed by an adult — petition, detention, adjudication,
  and disposition; describe the transfer/extended-juvenile-jurisdiction
  mechanics qualitatively and cite the controlling sections.

## Termination of parental rights (TPR)

- **§ 9-27-341 — Termination of parental rights.** Sets the **grounds** for TPR
  and the **standard of proof** — TPR requires proof by **clear and convincing
  evidence** of a statutory ground **and** that termination is in the child's
  best interest (considering likelihood of adoption and potential harm of
  returning the child). Describe the enumerated grounds qualitatively (e.g.,
  out-of-home placement for the statutory period despite reasonable efforts;
  abandonment; aggravated circumstances) and verify the period counts against
  current text.

## Paternity / legitimation and representation

- **Paternity / legitimation.** Establishment of paternity and legitimation
  issues affecting a juvenile are addressed within the juvenile framework (with
  the separate paternity statute governing parentage determinations generally);
  cite the specific provision invoked.
- **Attorney ad litem / CASA.** The court appoints an **attorney ad litem** to
  represent the **best interests of the child** in dependency-neglect and TPR
  proceedings, and may appoint a **Court Appointed Special Advocate (CASA)**;
  indigent parents are entitled to appointed counsel in dependency-neglect / TPR
  cases.

## Child support — Administrative Order No. 10 (income shares)

Although the **substantive child-support guideline** is not codified in Chapter
27, support obligations arising in juvenile and domestic-relations cases are
governed by the **Arkansas Supreme Court's Administrative Order No. 10**, which
since **July 1, 2020** adopts the **income-shares model** — combining **both
parents' gross incomes** against the **Family Support Chart** to set the
presumptive support obligation (with deviation findings on the record). (The
pre-2020 guideline used a percentage of the payor's income.) The Family Support
Chart and the deviation factors live in Administrative Order No. 10; pull the
order from the court-rules corpus and verify the current chart figures —
**(verify against current text)** — before computing support.

> **Distinctive features:** the **Juvenile Division of Circuit Court** as the
> exclusive forum; **clear-and-convincing** proof for TPR under § 9-27-341 with
> a best-interest overlay; mandatory **attorney ad litem** for the child and
> appointed counsel for indigent parents; and child support governed by the
> **income-shares Administrative Order No. 10** (eff. July 1, 2020).
