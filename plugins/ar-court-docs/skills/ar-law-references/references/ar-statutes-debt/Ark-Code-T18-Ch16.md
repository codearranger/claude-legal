## Scope

Chapter 16 carries Arkansas's **general landlord-tenant** provisions outside the
2007 Residential Landlord-Tenant Act (which lives in Chapter 17, see
`Ark-Code-T18-Ch17.md`). Two features make Arkansas distinctive nationally: the
**criminal failure-to-vacate** statute and the long-standing landlord-favorable
**security-deposit** regime with a small-landlord exemption.

## Criminal failure to vacate (§ 18-16-101)

- **§ 18-16-101 — Failure to pay rent / failure to vacate; penalty.** Arkansas is
  the only state that makes a tenant's failure to vacate after notice a **criminal
  misdemeanor**. The statute permits the landlord to pursue a criminal charge
  against a holdover tenant who, after **written notice** for nonpayment, does not
  surrender possession within the statutory period (commonly framed as a 10-day
  notice — verify the exact notice day count, the per-day continuing-offense
  structure, and the penalty against current text). The provision has been the
  subject of constitutional litigation, and **2021 amendments adjusted** its
  operation; confirm the current text and any procedural safeguards before
  advising on or responding to a criminal-eviction charge.

## Civil remedies / possession

Chapter 16 also contains general provisions touching landlord remedies and the
relationship of the parties. The principal **civil** eviction track — **unlawful
detainer** — lives in **Chapter 60** (§ 18-60-301 et seq.; see
`Ark-Code-T18-Ch60.md`), and the comprehensive residential statute is **Chapter
17** (the 2007 RLTA + 2021 implied-habitability standards). Cross-check which
chapter governs the remedy you are pleading.

## Security deposits (§ 18-16-301 et seq.)

- **§ 18-16-303 — Maximum / handling.** Governs the security deposit a landlord
  may collect and how it is held (verify any cap against current text).
- **§ 18-16-304 — Return and itemization.** On termination of the tenancy the
  landlord must **return the deposit, or provide an itemized written statement** of
  damages and the balance, within the statutory window — commonly **sixty (60)
  days** (verify the exact day count and the mailing/last-known-address mechanics
  against current text). Failure to comply can expose the landlord to damages.
- **§ 18-16-305 — Damages for noncompliance.** Provides the tenant's remedy where
  a covered landlord wrongfully withholds the deposit (verify the damages measure
  and any attorney-fee provision against current text).
- **§ 18-16-306 — Small-landlord exemption.** The security-deposit subchapter
  **does not apply** to a landlord who owns **five or fewer** (readings vary; some
  sources state "six or fewer") rental units, where the units are not managed by a
  third party. **Verify the exact unit threshold and management qualifier against
  current text** — this exemption is frequently dispositive of a deposit claim and
  should never be asserted from memory.

> **Distinctive Arkansas provisions:** (1) the **criminal failure-to-vacate**
> statute (§ 18-16-101) — unique in the nation; (2) the security-deposit
> **small-landlord exemption** (§ 18-16-306), which removes many individual
> owner-landlords from the return-and-itemization duty entirely. Arkansas remains
> among the most landlord-favorable states; pair this chapter with Chapters 17 and
> 60 to map the full eviction-and-deposit landscape.
