## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 62 contains Arkansas's **wrongful-death and survival**
provisions — the statutory causes of action that arise when a person is
killed by another's wrongful act, neglect, or default, together with the
**survival** of certain claims after death.

## Key sections

- **§ 16-62-102 — Wrongful Death Act.** The principal section. Creates a
  cause of action where death is caused by a **wrongful act, neglect, or
  default** that would have entitled the decedent to sue had death not
  resulted. Key features (verify against current text):
  - **Who sues:** the action is brought by the **personal representative** of
    the estate; if there is no personal representative, it may be brought by
    the **heirs at law**.
  - **Statutory beneficiaries:** recovery runs to the enumerated
    beneficiaries — **surviving spouse, children, parents, siblings, and
    persons standing in loco parentis or to whom the decedent stood in loco
    parentis** (confirm the exact beneficiary list against the statute).
  - **Recoverable damages:** include **pecuniary losses** (loss of support,
    services, contributions), **mental anguish** of the statutory
    beneficiaries, **loss of companionship/consortium**, and the decedent's
    **conscious pain and suffering** before death, plus funeral and medical
    expenses. **No damages cap applies** (Ark. Const. art. 5, § 32 bars caps
    on recovery for injuries resulting in death).
  - **Statute of limitations: 3 years** from the date of death (verify;
    different periods can apply where the underlying claim — e.g., medical
    malpractice — carries its own period, an area of recurring litigation).
- **§ 16-62-101 — Survival of actions.** Provides that certain causes of
  action **survive** the death of a party and may be prosecuted by or against
  the personal representative; distinguishes the **survival** action (the
  decedent's own claim, surviving to the estate) from the **wrongful-death**
  action (the beneficiaries' independent claim).

## Wrongful-death vs. survival

- **Survival action** (§ 16-62-101): the **decedent's** cause of action,
  surviving to the **estate** — measures the decedent's own losses (e.g.,
  pre-death pain and suffering, medical expenses).
- **Wrongful-death action** (§ 16-62-102): the **beneficiaries'** independent
  claim for *their* losses (loss of support, mental anguish, companionship).
  The two are typically pleaded together; allocate damages between estate and
  beneficiaries.

## Drafting note

Plead capacity carefully: confirm whether the plaintiff is the **personal
representative** (preferred) or proceeding as **heirs at law**, and join the
survival claim with the wrongful-death claim. Compute the **3-year** SOL from
death (verify), and check whether the underlying tort (e.g., medical
malpractice under § 16-114-203) imposes a different limitations rule.
