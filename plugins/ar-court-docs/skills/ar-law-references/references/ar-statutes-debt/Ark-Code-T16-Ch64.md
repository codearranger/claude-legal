## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 64 addresses **trial procedure** in civil actions. For
drafting, its centerpiece is Arkansas's **comparative-fault statute**, which
sets the rule for reducing or barring a plaintiff's recovery based on the
plaintiff's own share of fault.

## Key section — § 16-64-122 (modified comparative fault)

- **§ 16-64-122 — Comparative fault.** Arkansas follows **modified comparative
  fault** with a **"less than" threshold**:
  - If the **plaintiff's fault is of a lesser degree than** the combined fault
    of the parties from whom recovery is sought, the plaintiff **recovers**,
    but the award is **reduced in proportion to the plaintiff's percentage of
    fault**.
  - If the **plaintiff's fault is equal to or greater than** that of the
    defendants (i.e., **50% or more**), recovery is **barred entirely**.
  - Practically this is a **49% bar**: a plaintiff who is 49% at fault still
    recovers (reduced by 49%); a plaintiff who is 50% or more at fault
    recovers nothing. (Verify the exact statutory phrasing — "fault... of less
    degree than" — against current text.)
  - The statute directs the **fact-finder to compare fault** and the court to
    enter judgment on the **net** (reduced) amount. In a special verdict the
    jury assigns percentages to each party.

## Interaction with several liability

Comparative fault under § 16-64-122 works together with the
**several-liability** apportionment of the Civil Justice Reform Act
(§ 16-55-201, Title 16, ch. 55): the jury assigns each party a percentage of
fault, the plaintiff's recovery is reduced by the plaintiff's own
percentage, and each defendant is liable only for its several share. Recall
that the **nonparty-fault** allocation provision was struck
(*Johnson v. Rockwell Automation*, 2009 Ark. 241), so fault generally is
apportioned among the actual parties.

## Other Chapter 64 content

Chapter 64 also carries general trial-procedure provisions (order of trial,
verdicts, and related mechanics). For the controlling rules of civil
procedure governing trials, consult the Arkansas Rules of Civil Procedure
(see `references/court-rules/`).

## Drafting note

In a negligence complaint or answer, frame fault to keep the plaintiff
**below 50%** — at or above 50% the claim is barred under § 16-64-122.
Request a **special verdict / interrogatories** allocating fault percentages,
and compute the net recovery as the gross damages reduced by the plaintiff's
assigned percentage.
