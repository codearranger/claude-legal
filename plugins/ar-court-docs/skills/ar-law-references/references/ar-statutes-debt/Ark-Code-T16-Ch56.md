## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 56 sets the **statutes of limitations** for civil actions
in Arkansas — the deadlines for commencing suit, the accrual rules, and the
tolling/discovery doctrines. For consumer-debt defense the limitations
defense (an affirmative defense under Ark. R. Civ. P. 8(c)) is often
dispositive, so the written-vs-oral/open-account distinction is central.

## Key sections

- **§ 16-56-105 — Three-year limitations.** The general **3-year** period.
  Covers (verify enumerated categories against text): actions on **oral /
  unwritten contracts** and **open accounts / accounts stated**, **general
  torts and personal injury / negligence**, **property damage**, and many
  **statutory/common-law** claims not given a different period. Most
  **fraud** claims run 3 years, subject to the discovery rule for fraudulent
  concealment.
- **§ 16-56-111 — Five-year limitations on written contracts.** Actions to
  enforce a **written contract, promissory note, or other instrument in
  writing** run **5 years**. **DEBT-DEFENSE PIVOT:** whether a credit-card or
  installment debt is governed by the 5-year written-contract period or the
  3-year oral/open-account period turns on whether the collector can produce
  a **signed written agreement** (5-year) versus relying on an open-account
  theory (3-year). Make the collector prove the writing.
- **§ 16-56-114 — Limitations on actions on judgments.** A judgment is
  enforceable for **10 years**; the period may be extended by revival.
  Read together with **§ 16-65-501** (scire facias revival of dormant
  judgments).
- **§ 16-56-115 / -116 — Disability tolling and related accrual rules.**
  Toll the limitations period for minority/incompetency and address when a
  cause accrues; confirm exact section numbers and current text.
- **§ 16-56-126 / nonclaim and savings provisions.** Arkansas's **savings
  statute** lets a plaintiff whose timely action was nonsuited/dismissed
  other than on the merits **refile within one year** even if the original
  limitations period has run (verify the exact section and the one-year
  window against current text).

## Accrual and the discovery rule

The cause of action generally accrues when the **wrong occurs / the right to
sue arises** (e.g., the date of breach or last payment for a debt). Arkansas
applies a **discovery rule** in limited contexts (notably fraud / fraudulent
concealment and certain professional-negligence settings), delaying accrual
until the plaintiff knew or reasonably should have known of the injury —
confirm the governing case law for the specific claim.

## Credit-card / consumer-debt nuance

Arkansas courts generally treat credit-card debt as a **written contract
(5-year, § 16-56-111)** where a **signed cardholder agreement** exists; on an
**open-account / account-stated** theory without a signed writing, the
**3-year (§ 16-56-105)** period applies. The clock typically runs from the
**date of default / last activity** (verify the controlling accrual rule).
Partial payment or written acknowledgment may **restart or revive** the
period — confirm the current Arkansas rule before relying on revival.

## Drafting note

When pleading a limitations defense, identify the precise claim, match it to
§ 16-56-105 (3-year) or § 16-56-111 (5-year), state the accrual date, and
compute the bar date. For judgments use § 16-56-114 with § 16-65-501 revival.
