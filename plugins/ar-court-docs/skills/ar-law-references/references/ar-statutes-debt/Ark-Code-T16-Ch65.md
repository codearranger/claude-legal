## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 65 governs **judgments** in Arkansas — their entry, the
**judgment lien**, **execution**, the **life of a judgment**, dormancy, and
**scire facias revival**. This chapter is the enforcement-side counterpart to
the limitations chapter (Title 16, ch. 56) and is central to post-judgment
collection and to defending against revived/stale judgments.

## Key sections

- **§ 16-65-501 — Revival of judgments (scire facias) / judgment lien and
  dormancy.** A judgment is enforceable for **10 years** from entry. If not
  enforced within that period it becomes **dormant**; a dormant judgment may
  be **revived by scire facias** (a revival proceeding) to extend its life
  for an additional period (verify the exact day/year counts and the revival
  window against current text). **DEFENSE HOOK:** a judgment creditor who
  lets a judgment go dormant and then tries to execute without proper revival
  is vulnerable to challenge.
- **§ 16-65-117 — Judgment lien on real property.** A judgment, when properly
  entered/indexed, operates as a **lien on the judgment debtor's real
  property** in the county. Confirm the indexing/recording requirement and
  the duration of the lien against current text.
- **§ 16-65-114 — Interest on judgments.** Sets the **post-judgment interest
  rate** on Arkansas judgments (verify the current rate/formula — it tracks a
  statutory or contract rate). Note the Arkansas constitutional usury
  framework (Ark. Const. art. 19, § 13) bears on the contract-rate ceiling.
- **§ 16-65-118 — Satisfaction / release of judgment.** Procedure for
  entering **satisfaction** of a judgment of record once paid; a debtor who
  has paid can compel the creditor to acknowledge satisfaction.
- **Execution.** Chapter 65 (with related provisions) authorizes **writs of
  execution**, levy, and judicial sale to enforce a money judgment, and
  addresses the **exemptions** that shield certain property (read together
  with the **constitutional exemptions**, Ark. Const. art. 9, and the
  homestead/personal-property exemption framework).

## Life-cycle of a judgment (verify each figure)

1. **Entry** — judgment entered and indexed; becomes a **lien on real
   property** (§ 16-65-117).
2. **Enforcement window** — enforceable for **10 years**; **post-judgment
   interest** accrues (§ 16-65-114).
3. **Dormancy** — lapses if not enforced within the statutory period.
4. **Revival** — dormant judgment **revived by scire facias** (§ 16-65-501)
   to restore enforceability.
5. **Satisfaction** — entered of record on payment (§ 16-65-118).

## Drafting note

On the enforcement side, confirm the judgment is **within its 10-year life**
(or properly revived) before levying, and apply the correct **post-judgment
interest** rate. On the defense side, scrutinize whether a stale judgment was
**revived by scire facias** as required, and assert applicable **exemptions**
against execution. Label specific year/rate figures "(verify against current
text)."
