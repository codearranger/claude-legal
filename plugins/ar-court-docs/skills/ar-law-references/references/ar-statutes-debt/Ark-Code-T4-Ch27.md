## Scope

Title 4, Chapter 27 is the **Arkansas Business Corporation Act of 1987**
(§ 4-27-101 et seq.), the governing statute for for-profit corporations
organized in Arkansas. It is patterned on the Model Business Corporation
Act and supplies the rules for formation, governance, shareholder rights,
derivative litigation, fundamental transactions, and dissolution. For
commercial-dispute drafting it is the home of shareholder-remedy theories,
derivative-suit prerequisites, and dissolution petitions. Section numbers
run in the **§ 4-27-NNN** range.

## Formation and powers

- **§ 4-27-101 — Short title.** "Arkansas Business Corporation Act of 1987."
- **§ 4-27-120 / § 4-27-202 — Filing requirements; articles of
  incorporation.** Contents of the articles (name, authorized shares,
  registered agent/office, incorporator) and filing with the Secretary of
  State.
- **§ 4-27-203 — Incorporation; when corporate existence begins.**
- **§ 4-27-301 / § 4-27-302 — Purposes and general powers.** A corporation
  may engage in any lawful business and has the enumerated general powers.
- **§ 4-27-204 — Liability for preincorporation transactions.**
- **§ 4-27-501 through § 4-27-505 — Registered office and agent; service of
  process on the corporation.**

## Shares and shareholders

- **§ 4-27-601 / § 4-27-602 — Authorized shares; terms of classes/series.**
- **§ 4-27-620 through § 4-27-622 — Subscriptions; issuance; consideration
  for shares; liability of shareholders** (the general rule that a
  shareholder is not personally liable for corporate acts beyond the
  obligation to pay for shares — the principal limited-liability provision).
- **§ 4-27-640 — Distributions to shareholders** and the insolvency/balance-
  sheet limitations on distributions.
- **§ 4-27-701 through § 4-27-728 — Shareholder meetings, voting, voting
  trusts, voting agreements, and quorum/voting requirements.**

## Governance — directors and officers

- **§ 4-27-801 through § 4-27-809 — Board of directors.** Requirement for a
  board, number/election/terms, removal, and vacancies.
- **§ 4-27-830 — General standards of conduct for directors** (good faith,
  ordinary care, reasonable belief in the best interests of the
  corporation — the statutory duty-of-care/loyalty baseline).
- **§ 4-27-831 — Director conflict-of-interest transactions.**
- **§ 4-27-832 — Loans to directors.**
- **§ 4-27-840 through § 4-27-844 — Officers; standards of conduct.**
- **§ 4-27-850 through § 4-27-859 — Indemnification of directors and
  officers.**

## Derivative actions and shareholder remedies

- **§ 4-27-740 — Derivative proceedings.** Governs a shareholder's suit to
  enforce a right of the corporation: standing (contemporaneous-ownership
  and fair-representation requirements), the **written-demand requirement**
  on the board, the stay during board investigation, dismissal on a
  disinterested-body determination, court approval of discontinuance or
  settlement, and payment of expenses. **Pleading hook:** confirm the
  current demand-and-futility structure and the verified-complaint /
  demand-allegation requirements before filing a derivative complaint.
- **Inspection rights — § 4-27-1601 through § 4-27-1604.** A shareholder's
  right to inspect and copy corporate records on proper demand and for a
  proper purpose; remedy to compel inspection and recover costs.

## Fundamental transactions and appraisal

- **§ 4-27-1001 through § 4-27-1009 — Amendment of articles and bylaws.**
- **§ 4-27-1101 through § 4-27-1108 — Merger and share exchange.**
- **§ 4-27-1201 / § 4-27-1202 — Sale of assets** (sale of all or
  substantially all assets requires shareholder approval).
- **§ 4-27-1301 through § 4-27-1331 — Dissenters' / appraisal rights.** A
  shareholder who dissents from certain fundamental transactions may demand
  the **fair value** of shares through the statutory appraisal procedure.

## Dissolution

- **§ 4-27-1401 through § 4-27-1409 — Voluntary dissolution** (by
  incorporators/initial directors or by board-and-shareholder action),
  articles of dissolution, and winding up / known-and-unknown-claim
  procedures.
- **§ 4-27-1420 through § 4-27-1423 — Administrative dissolution** by the
  Secretary of State (e.g., failure to file/pay) and reinstatement.
- **§ 4-27-1430 through § 4-27-1440 — Judicial dissolution.** Grounds
  include action by the Attorney General, by a **shareholder** (director
  deadlock causing irreparable injury, illegal/oppressive/fraudulent
  conduct by those in control, shareholder deadlock failing to elect
  directors, or waste/misapplication of assets), or by a **creditor**;
  procedure, receivership, and the court's decree of dissolution.
  **Remedy hook:** shareholder-oppression and deadlock claims are pursued
  here; confirm the current grounds and any buyout/alternative-remedy
  mechanics in the statute.

## Foreign corporations

- **§ 4-27-1501 through § 4-27-1532 — Certificate of authority for foreign
  corporations; transacting business; the consequence of transacting
  without authority** (a bar on maintaining a proceeding in Arkansas courts
  until the corporation obtains authority — a potential capacity defense).

## Drafting note

For a closely-held-corporation dispute, the principal theories live in
§ 4-27-740 (derivative claims — mind the demand requirement and the
verified-complaint posture), the § 4-27-830 / § 4-27-831 conduct and
conflict-of-interest standards, the inspection-rights sections
(§ 4-27-1601 et seq.), and the judicial-dissolution grounds at § 4-27-1430
et seq. for oppression/deadlock. For a contract dispute against a foreign
corporation, check § 4-27-1502's transacting-without-authority bar as a
capacity issue. Plead fraud-based corporate claims with Ark. R. Civ. P. 9(b)
particularity.
