## Scope

Chapter 13 governs **child custody, visitation, and the best-interest standard**
in Arkansas. Custody is decided in the **Domestic Relations Division of Circuit
Court** as part of (or ancillary to) a divorce, paternity, or other family
proceeding.

## § 9-13-101 — Award of custody; the joint-custody presumption

- **Best-interest-of-the-child standard.** The controlling test for an award of
  custody is the **best interest and welfare of the child**, with the court
  considering all relevant factors (the parties' circumstances, the child's
  needs, each parent's capacity, and — where applicable — the child's
  preference).
- **Act 604 of 2021 — rebuttable joint-custody presumption (Arkansas-distinctive).**
  In an **original (initial) custody determination**, § 9-13-101 establishes a
  **rebuttable presumption that JOINT custody is in the best interest of the
  child.** The presumption is **rebutted only by clear and convincing evidence**
  that joint custody is not in the child's best interest (or by the parties'
  agreement, or where a party is not seeking custody). "Joint custody" is defined
  as the **approximate and reasonable equal division of time** with the child
  between the parents (verify the precise statutory phrasing and any percentage
  language against current text).
- **Domestic-violence / abuse considerations.** The presence of domestic abuse
  bears on the custody analysis and can rebut or qualify the joint-custody
  presumption; coordinate with the **Domestic Abuse Act** in
  `Ark-Code-T9-Ch15.md`.
- **Gender-neutrality.** Custody is awarded without preference based on the
  parent's sex.

## Visitation, modification, and related provisions

- **Visitation.** The court orders reasonable visitation/parenting time
  consistent with the child's best interest; supervised or restricted
  visitation where warranted.
- **Modification of custody.** A custody order may be modified on a showing of a
  **material change in circumstances** since the last order **and** that
  modification serves the child's best interest. (Note the joint-custody
  **presumption** applies most strongly to the **original** determination;
  describe the modification standard qualitatively and cite the controlling
  section/case law.)
- **Grandparent / third-party visitation.** Chapter 13 contains
  grandparent-visitation provisions (subject to the constitutional
  parental-rights overlay); cite the specific section and describe qualitatively.
- **Relocation.** A custodial parent's relocation with the child is governed by
  Arkansas case law (the relocation framework) overlaid on the best-interest
  standard; the analysis differs between a sole-custodian relocation and a
  joint-custody relocation. Describe qualitatively and verify the current
  controlling standard.

> **Distinctive Arkansas provision:** the **Act 604 of 2021** rebuttable
> presumption (§ 9-13-101) that **joint custody** is in the child's best
> interest in an original custody determination, **rebuttable only by clear and
> convincing evidence**. Child *support* is governed by **Administrative Order
> No. 10** (income shares), not this chapter — see `Ark-Code-T9-Ch27.md`.
