## Scope

Chapter 3 collects Arkansas's general labor-relations provisions. Its most
litigated component is the **right-to-work** subchapter, which codifies the
freedom-of-employment guarantee that originated in the Arkansas Constitution.
Wage-payment and final-wage mechanics also appear among the Title 11 labor
chapters; confirm which precise section governs final-pay timing against current
text before relying on it.

## Right-to-work (§ 11-3-301 et seq.)

Arkansas is a **right-to-work** state. The constitutional source is **Ark.
Const. Amendment 34** (adopted 1944), and the statutory subchapter implements
and supplements it. The core principle: a person's right to obtain or keep
employment may not be conditioned on membership or non-membership in, or the
payment of dues to, a labor union.

- **§ 11-3-301 — Freedom of employment / public policy.** Declares the public
  policy that no person shall be denied employment because of membership or
  non-membership in a labor union.
- **§ 11-3-302 — Union membership not a condition of employment.** No contract
  may require union membership (or non-membership) as a condition of getting or
  keeping a job.
- **§ 11-3-303 — Dues / fees not a condition of employment.** Compulsory payment
  of union dues, fees, or other charges as a condition of employment is
  prohibited (the agency-fee bar).
- **§ 11-3-304 — Agreements in violation are unlawful.** Contracts or
  arrangements that violate the subchapter are unlawful and unenforceable.
- **§ 11-3-305 et seq. — Remedies / enforcement.** Provides civil remedies and,
  where applicable, criminal penalties for violations, and authorizes injunctive
  relief and recovery of damages by an injured employee (verify the precise
  remedy section and any damages measure against current text).

> **Distinctive Arkansas provision:** right-to-work is **constitutionally
> entrenched** in Arkansas via **Amendment 34**, not merely statutory — the
> Chapter 3 sections implement the amendment. A union-security clause purporting
> to compel membership or dues is void as against Arkansas public policy.

## Wage payment / final wages

Final-wage and wage-payment timing provisions sit among the Title 11 labor
chapters. **Verify the exact section** that governs the deadline for paying a
discharged employee's final wages, any "double-wage" / continuing-wage penalty
for willful nonpayment, and the corporate-vs-individual coverage scope against
current text before pleading a final-wage claim — do not assume a day count or
penalty multiplier. (Minimum-wage and overtime rules live in **Chapter 4**; see
