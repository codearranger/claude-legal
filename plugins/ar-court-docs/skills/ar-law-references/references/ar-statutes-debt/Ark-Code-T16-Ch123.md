## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 123 is the **Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993 (ACRA)** —
the state's anti-discrimination statute, codified at **§ 16-123-101 et seq.**
It prohibits discrimination in **employment, housing, and public
accommodations**, and provides a **direct private right of action in Circuit
Court** without a state administrative-exhaustion prerequisite.

## Key sections

- **§ 16-123-101 — Title / purpose.** Names the Act and declares its remedial
  purpose; courts may look to analogous federal civil-rights law for guidance
  but the ACRA is independent state law.
- **§ 16-123-102 — Definitions, including the employer-coverage threshold.**
  Defines "employer" to reach employers with **nine (9) or more employees**
  (verify the exact count against current text). Employers below the threshold
  are outside ACRA employment coverage (though federal Title VII/ADA or other
  law may reach them).
- **§ 16-123-103 — Discriminatory practices prohibited.** Bars discrimination
  because of **race, religion, national origin/ancestry, gender, and the
  presence of any sensory, mental, or physical disability** (confirm the exact
  protected classes against current text). Note ACRA's protected-class list
  differs in scope from federal statutes.
- **§ 16-123-105 — Public accommodations / housing discrimination cause of
  action.** Creates a private action for discrimination in public
  accommodations (and addresses housing), with available remedies.
- **§ 16-123-107 — Employment-discrimination cause of action.** The core
  employment provision: a private right of action for unlawful employment
  discrimination.
  - **Remedies:** back pay, **compensatory and punitive damages**,
    injunctive/equitable relief, and **reasonable attorney's fees and costs**
    to a prevailing plaintiff (verify remedy scope and any caps — recall
    Ark. Const. art. 5, § 32 bars caps on personal-injury recovery).
  - **§ 16-123-107(c) — One-year statute of limitations.** A civil action
    under this section must be brought within **one (1) year** of the alleged
    discriminatory act (verify the exact trigger and tolling rules against
    current text). This is a **short** window — calendar it carefully.
  - **Individual liability:** Arkansas courts have, in some circumstances,
    permitted **individual liability** under ACRA (unlike Title VII, which
    generally does not reach individuals) — confirm the current case law for
    the specific defendant.
- **§ 16-123-108 — Interference / retaliation.** Prohibits retaliation and
  interference with the exercise of ACRA rights.

## ARKANSAS QUIRK — no state administrative exhaustion

Unlike a federal Title VII claim (EEOC charge prerequisite), an ACRA
employment claim generally has **no state administrative-exhaustion
prerequisite** — there is no Arkansas FEPA filing requirement, so a plaintiff
may **file directly in Circuit Court**. A parallel federal track (EEOC →
right-to-sue → federal court) often runs alongside; coordinate the two
limitations clocks (the ACRA **1-year** vs. the federal charge/suit
deadlines).

## Drafting note

Confirm **9+ employee** coverage (§ 16-123-102), match the conduct to a
protected class (§ 16-123-103), plead under § 16-123-107 (employment) or
§ 16-123-105 (public accommodations/housing), and **calendar the 1-year SOL**
(§ 16-123-107(c)). Pray for compensatory + punitive damages and **attorney's
fees**. Check whether **individual** defendants may be named under current
Arkansas case law.
