## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 17 governs the **District Courts** — Arkansas's
limited-jurisdiction trial courts (renamed from "Municipal Courts" under
Amendment 80, effective July 1, 2001). District Court is the **high-volume
consumer-debt and eviction forum**. The chapter establishes district-court
organization, the two kinds of district court (State vs. Local), civil and
small-claims jurisdiction, judges, clerks, and ancillary administration.

## District-court structure

- **State District Courts vs. Local District Courts.** Amendment 80 and
  implementing legislation created a transition toward **State District
  Courts** (state-funded, full-time judges) while many jurisdictions continue
  to operate **Local District Courts** (locally funded). Both are
  limited-jurisdiction courts hearing civil, small-claims, misdemeanor,
  traffic, and unlawful-detainer matters. Confirm which kind serves a given
  county/city via arcourts.gov.
- **City Courts** persist in some small cities that lack a district court
  (governed primarily elsewhere in Title 16); they exercise narrower
  jurisdiction.

## Key sections

- **§ 16-17-704 — Civil jurisdiction / amount-in-controversy cap.** Fixes the
  **monetary ceiling** on district-court civil jurisdiction. The general
  district-court civil cap is **$25,000** (verify against current text;
  exclusive of interest and costs). Claims above the cap belong in Circuit
  Court. This is the section to cite when a debt-buyer files (or should have
  filed) in district court.
- **Small-claims division.** Within district court, the **small-claims
  division** handles low-dollar claims up to a separate, lower cap —
  **$5,000** (verify against current text). Small-claims practice is informal:
  **attorneys are generally excluded**, and there are restrictions on
  assignees/collection entities proceeding through the small-claims division
  (a meaningful limit on debt buyers — confirm the current statutory
  language). Pleadings are simplified and the rules of evidence are relaxed.
- **District-court judges, terms, and qualifications.** Chapter 17 sets the
  election/qualification, terms, and compensation framework for district
  judges and addresses establishment, abolition, and consolidation of
  districts.
- **Clerks and records.** Provisions address the district-court clerk's
  duties, dockets, and record-keeping.

## Appeals — de novo to Circuit Court

An appeal from a district-court civil judgment goes to **Circuit Court for a
trial de novo** (not a record review). The appeal is perfected by filing a
certified record in circuit court within the time fixed by the **Arkansas
District Court Rules** / Ark. R. Civ. P. — a **30-day** window (verify the
exact mechanism and day count in the District Court Rules and references).
Because of the de novo posture, a defendant who wanted discovery unavailable
in the informal district-court forum can obtain it after appeal.

## Drafting note

For a consumer-debt or eviction matter, confirm (1) the amount in
controversy against the § 16-17-704 cap, (2) whether the small-claims
division's attorney-exclusion and assignee limits apply, and (3) the de novo
appeal deadline. The civil eviction track itself (unlawful detainer) is in
Title 18, ch. 60; the criminal failure-to-vacate statute is § 18-16-101.
