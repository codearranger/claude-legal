## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 22 governs attorneys at law — admission, licensing,
regulation, the attorney-client relationship, and, most important for civil
drafting, the **statutory fee-shifting provision** that lets a **prevailing
party recover attorney's fees** in specified categories of civil action.

## Key section — § 16-22-308 (attorney's fees)

- **§ 16-22-308 — Attorney's fees in contract and related actions.** In any
  civil action to recover on an **open account, statement of account,
  account stated, promissory note, bill, negotiable instrument, or contract
  relating to the purchase or sale of goods, wares, or merchandise, or for
  labor or services**, the **prevailing party may be allowed a reasonable
  attorney's fee** to be assessed by the court and collected as costs
  (verify the exact enumerated categories against current text).
  - The award is **discretionary** ("may be allowed"), not mandatory — the
    court determines whether and how much.
  - It is **two-way**: it runs to whichever side prevails, so a **consumer
    who defeats a collection suit on a contract/account/note** can move for a
    fee under § 16-22-308, and a debt buyer who wins can seek one too. Flag
    this exposure to a pro-se litigant on both sides.
  - The action must fall within an **enumerated category** (contract,
    account, note, etc.); tort actions and many statutory claims are outside
    § 16-22-308 and look to their own fee provisions (e.g., the ADTPA at
    § 4-88-113, the trade-secrets act at § 4-75-607, the Civil Rights Act at
    § 16-123-105/-107).
  - Factors courts weigh in setting a "reasonable" fee track the familiar
    lodestar/Chrisco factors (experience, time and labor, fee customarily
    charged, amount involved and results obtained, etc.) — confirm the
    governing case law and any updated statutory language.

## Other Chapter 22 content

- Admission to the bar, licensing, and the unauthorized-practice-of-law
  provisions sit in this chapter; for a pro-se filer the operative point is
  that a self-represented individual may sign and file for themselves
  (Ark. R. Civ. P. 11) without bar membership.
- Provisions on attorney liens and the attorney-client privilege/relationship
  also appear in or near this chapter — confirm exact section placement.

## Drafting note

When drafting a prayer for relief or a post-judgment fee motion in a
contract/account/note case, cite **§ 16-22-308** as the fee-shifting hook and
support the amount with a fee affidavit detailing the reasonableness factors.
Because the provision is bilateral, advise a pro-se litigant that losing such
a case can expose them to the other side's fees.
