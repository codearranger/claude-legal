## Scope

Title 16, Chapter 108 contains Arkansas's **arbitration** statutes. Arkansas
adopted the **Revised Uniform Arbitration Act (RUAA)**, codified at
**§ 16-108-201 et seq.**, which governs the validity, enforcement, and
judicial supervision of arbitration agreements and awards. Older
arbitration-act provisions appear earlier in the chapter; the **§ 16-108-201
et seq.** RUAA is the operative framework for modern agreements.

## Key sections (Revised UAA, § 16-108-201 et seq.)

- **§ 16-108-201 — Definitions.** Defines "arbitration organization,"
  "arbitrator," "court," "record," and related terms for the Revised UAA.
- **§ 16-108-206 — Validity of agreement to arbitrate.** A written agreement
  to submit existing or future controversies to arbitration is **valid,
  enforceable, and irrevocable** except on grounds that exist at law or
  equity for revoking a contract. **HISTORICAL NOTE:** Arkansas's older act
  carved out certain matters (e.g., some personal-injury / tort and
  insurance disputes) from arbitrability; confirm the current RUAA scope and
  any remaining state-law carve-outs against the text and case law.
- **§ 16-108-207 — Motion to compel or stay arbitration.** A party may move
  the court to **compel arbitration** (or to **stay** it); the court decides
  arbitrability, and litigation is stayed pending arbitration of arbitrable
  claims.
- **§ 16-108-209 — Appointment of arbitrator.** Court appoints an arbitrator
  if the agreed method fails.
- **§ 16-108-222 — Confirmation of an award.** After an award, a party may
  move the court to **confirm** it, leading to entry of judgment.
- **§ 16-108-223 — Vacating an award.** The **narrow** statutory grounds to
  **vacate** (corruption, fraud, evident partiality, arbitrator misconduct,
  arbitrator exceeding powers, no valid agreement, etc.).
- **§ 16-108-224 — Modifying / correcting an award.** Grounds to **modify or
  correct** (evident miscalculation, award on a matter not submitted, form
  imperfection).
- **§ 16-108-225 / -228 — Judgment on the award; attorney's fees and costs.**
  The court enters judgment on a confirmed/modified award and may award fees
  and costs of the confirmation/vacatur proceeding.

## FAA preemption

Where a contract **involves interstate commerce**, the **Federal Arbitration
Act (FAA)**, 9 U.S.C. § 1 et seq., applies and can **preempt** conflicting
state-law limits on arbitrability under *AT&T Mobility v. Concepcion* and
progeny. A state-law carve-out (e.g., a historical Arkansas exclusion of
certain claims) may be unenforceable as applied to an interstate-commerce
agreement. Always analyze whether the FAA or the Arkansas RUAA (or both)
governs, and brief the preemption question where the agreement touches
interstate commerce.

## Drafting note

To enforce an arbitration clause, move to **compel arbitration and stay
litigation** under § 16-108-207, identifying whether the **FAA** or the
**Arkansas RUAA (§ 16-108-201 et seq.)** governs. To resist, attack the
agreement on ordinary contract-formation/unconscionability grounds
(§ 16-108-206) or, post-award, on the **narrow vacatur grounds** of
§ 16-108-223. Confirm current scope and carve-outs against the text.
