## Scope

Chapter 17 is the **Arkansas Residential Landlord-Tenant Act of 2007** — the
state's comprehensive residential-tenancy statute — as supplemented by the
landmark **Act 1052 of 2021**, which for the first time imposed a baseline
**implied habitability / minimum-housing-standards** duty on residential
landlords. Arkansas was the **last state in the nation** to adopt any implied
warranty of habitability, and even the 2021 standards remain narrow and
landlord-favorable.

## Residential Landlord-Tenant Act of 2007 (§ 18-17-101 et seq.)

- **§ 18-17-101 — Title.** Names the Act.
- **§ 18-17-102 — Purposes.** States the legislative purposes (simplify and
  clarify the law governing residential rental relationships).
- **§ 18-17-201 et seq. — Definitions / scope.** Defines "landlord," "tenant,"
  "rental agreement," "dwelling unit," and the tenancies covered and excluded
  (verify exclusions — e.g., transient occupancy — against current text).
- **§ 18-17-301 et seq. — Rental agreements / terms; prohibited provisions.**
  Addresses permissible and prohibited lease terms and the parties' general
  obligations.
- **§ 18-17-401 et seq. — Tenant obligations.** Sets the tenant's duties
  (maintain the unit, comply with the agreement, etc.).
- **§ 18-17-601 et seq. — Termination / nonpayment; landlord remedies.** Provides
  the notice and termination mechanics for nonpayment and other breaches; the
  civil possession remedy ties to the unlawful-detainer track in Chapter 60
  (`Ark-Code-T18-Ch60.md`). Verify the nonpayment notice day count against
  current text.

## Implied habitability — minimum standards (Act 1052 of 2021; § 18-17-501 et seq.)

**Act 1052 of 2021** added the minimum-standards subchapter, effective for
residential leases entered into **on or after November 1, 2021**.

- **§ 18-17-501 — Application.** Establishes the subchapter and its effective-date
  reach (leases made on or after Nov. 1, 2021; verify the application qualifiers
  against current text).
- **§ 18-17-502 — Minimum habitability standards.** Requires the landlord to
  provide a dwelling unit that, at the inception of the tenancy, meets baseline
  standards — broadly: a **structurally sound roof, walls, and foundation;
  available potable running water; available electricity; an available heating /
  air-conditioning system; and working plumbing/sewer**. The duty is keyed to
  what is **available at the start of the lease**; verify the exact enumerated
  standards against current text.
- **§ 18-17-503 et seq. — Tenant remedy (notice → cure → terminate).** The
  tenant's remedy is deliberately **limited**: the tenant must give the landlord
  **written notice** of the deficiency; the landlord then has a **cure period
  (commonly 30 days — verify)**; and if the landlord fails to cure, the tenant's
  remedy is to **terminate the lease and recover the security deposit (and any
  prepaid rent)**. There is **no statutory rent-withholding right and no
  repair-and-deduct right** — distinctively narrow.

> **Distinctive Arkansas provisions:** (1) Arkansas was the **last U.S. state** to
> adopt any implied habitability, doing so only via **Act 1052 of 2021** for
> leases on/after **Nov. 1, 2021**; (2) the tenant remedy is **terminate-and-
> recover-deposit only** after written notice and a cure period — **no
> rent-withholding and no repair-and-deduct**, keeping Arkansas landlord-favorable
> even after the reform. Pair with Chapter 16 (criminal failure-to-vacate +
> security deposits) and Chapter 60 (civil unlawful detainer).
