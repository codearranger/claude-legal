## Scope

Chapter 11 governs the **formation of marriage** in Arkansas — capacity,
license, solemnization, prohibited and void marriages, and the distinctive
**covenant-marriage** regime. Arkansas recognizes **no common-law marriage**
contracted within the state, but honors a common-law marriage that was validly
formed in a jurisdiction that recognizes it. Marriage and its dissolution are
heard in the **Domestic Relations Division of Circuit Court**.

## Subchapter 1 — Marriage generally (license, capacity, solemnization)

- **§ 9-11-102 — Minimum age / capacity.** Sets the age and capacity rules to
  contract marriage; consent requirements where a party is a minor (verify the
  current age threshold and any judicial/parental-consent mechanics against
  current text).
- **§ 9-11-104 — Prohibited (consanguinity) marriages.** Marriages within the
  prohibited degrees of kinship are void.
- **§ 9-11-105 — Void / bigamous marriages.** A marriage contracted while a
  prior marriage subsists is void.
- **§ 9-11-201 et seq. — Marriage license.** A license issued by the county
  clerk is a prerequisite; describes application, issuance, and the limited
  waiting/expiration mechanics (verify any waiting-period or expiration day
  counts against current text).
- **§ 9-11-206 / -208 — Persons authorized to solemnize.** Lists the officiants
  (judges, certain ordained or licensed clergy, etc.) empowered to solemnize a
  marriage and the return-of-license duty.
- **§ 9-11-213 — No common-law marriage in Arkansas.** Arkansas does not
  recognize a marriage attempted to be contracted by common law within the
  state; a marriage must be licensed and solemnized. (A common-law marriage
  validly formed in a state that recognizes it is honored under comity.)

## Subchapter 8 — Covenant Marriage (§ 9-11-801 et seq.)

Arkansas is one of a small number of states offering an **optional covenant
marriage** — a heightened-commitment marital status that couples may elect at
the time of marriage or convert into during an existing marriage. The covenant
restricts the grounds on which the marriage may later be dissolved.

- **§ 9-11-802 — Election / declaration of intent.** Couples electing covenant
  marriage sign a recital and a notarized **Declaration of Intent** affirming
  the lifelong-commitment understanding and submit it with the license
  application; existing married couples may convert by filing the declaration.
- **§ 9-11-803 — Mandatory premarital counseling.** A covenant marriage
  requires **premarital counseling** (from clergy or a marriage counselor)
  covering the seriousness of marriage and the limited covenant grounds, plus
  an attestation/affidavit that the counseling occurred.
- **§ 9-11-804 — Informational statement / disclosure.** The Attorney General's
  prescribed informational statement on the nature of covenant marriage is
  provided to applicants.
- **§ 9-11-807 / -808 — Limited grounds for divorce or separation.** A covenant
  marriage may be dissolved (or a legal separation obtained) only on the
  **enumerated covenant grounds** — a narrower list than the ordinary § 9-12-301
  grounds (e.g., adultery; conviction of a felony / imprisonment; abandonment;
  physical or sexual abuse of a spouse or child; and living separate and apart
  for a specified statutory period). Verify the exact grounds list and the
  separation-period day/month counts against current text.
- **§ 9-11-809 — Counseling before covenant divorce.** Before a covenant
  divorce or separation, the parties are generally directed to obtain
  counseling.

> **Distinctive Arkansas provisions:** (1) the optional **covenant-marriage**
> election with mandatory premarital counseling and a **restricted divorce-grounds**
> menu under § 9-11-801 et seq.; (2) the statutory bar on **common-law marriage**
> formed in-state (§ 9-11-213) coupled with comity recognition of valid
> out-of-state common-law marriages. Ordinary (non-covenant) divorce grounds and
> the corroboration requirement live in **Chapter 12** (see `Ark-Code-T9-Ch12.md`).
