## Scope

Title 4, Chapter 38 is the **Arkansas Uniform Limited Liability Company Act**
(§ 4-38-101 et seq.), enacted by **Act 1041 of 2021** (effective in 2021)
and patterned on the Revised Uniform Limited Liability Company Act. It
**replaced the prior Small Business Entity Tax Pass Through Act** (the older
Arkansas LLC statute at Title 4, Chapter 32, § 4-32-101 et seq.) as the
governing law for Arkansas LLCs. **Transition note:** entities and disputes
arising before the new Act's effective date, and any grandfathered
provisions, may still implicate Chapter 32 — confirm which act governs by
the date of formation and the relevant transition provisions. Section
numbers run in the **§ 4-38-NNN** range.

## Formation and the operating agreement

- **§ 4-38-101 — Short title** ("Uniform Limited Liability Company Act").
- **§ 4-38-102 — Definitions** ("member," "manager," "operating agreement,"
  "transferable interest," "distributional interest," "person," etc.).
- **§ 4-38-105 / § 4-38-106 / § 4-38-107 — Nature, purpose, powers, and
  duration.** An LLC may be organized for any lawful purpose and has
  perpetual duration unless otherwise provided.
- **§ 4-38-201 — Formation; certificate of organization.** Filed with the
  Secretary of State; contents and effectiveness.
- **§ 4-38-110 / § 4-38-111 — Operating agreement; scope, function, and
  limitations.** The operating agreement governs relations among members
  and the LLC; the Act lists the **non-waivable provisions** the operating
  agreement may **not** alter (e.g., it may not eliminate the duty of
  loyalty entirely, may not unreasonably restrict access to records, and
  may not eliminate the good-faith-and-fair-dealing obligation). **Drafting
  hook:** disputes turn on the operating agreement first, then the Act's
  default and mandatory rules.

## Members, management, and fiduciary duties

- **§ 4-38-301 — No agency power of members as members** (under the RULLCA
  model, members are not statutory agents merely by status; agency authority
  is established by the operating agreement / other law). Verify the precise
  Arkansas formulation.
- **§ 4-38-401 through § 4-38-407 — Member relations; becoming a member;
  member-managed vs. manager-managed structure; sharing of and right to
  distributions; management rights.**
- **§ 4-38-409 — Standards of conduct for members and managers.** The
  fiduciary **duties of loyalty and care**, the obligation of **good faith
  and fair dealing**, and how those duties apply in member-managed versus
  manager-managed LLCs. **Remedy hook:** breach-of-fiduciary-duty claims
  against a managing member/manager are framed here.
- **§ 4-38-408 — Reimbursement, indemnification, advancement, and
  insurance.**

## Transfer of interests; member dissociation

- **§ 4-38-501 through § 4-38-504 — Transferable interest; transfer; rights
  of a transferee; charging order.** A member's economic interest is a
  **transferable interest**; a creditor of a member may reach it via a
  **charging order**, which is generally the **exclusive remedy** against a
  member's LLC interest. **Creditor hook:** the charging-order-exclusivity
  rule limits how a judgment creditor of an individual member may collect
  against the member's stake in the LLC.
- **§ 4-38-601 through § 4-38-603 — Member's dissociation; events causing
  dissociation; effect of dissociation** (loss of management rights;
  conversion to a transferable-interest holder).

## Dissolution and winding up

- **§ 4-38-701 — Events causing dissolution** (an event in the operating
  agreement, consent of the members, no members for a stated period, or a
  judicial decree).
- **§ 4-38-701(a)(4) / § 4-38-702 — Judicial dissolution.** A court may
  dissolve on application of a member where it is not reasonably practicable
  to carry on the business in conformity with the certificate and operating
  agreement, or because the managers/controlling members have acted (or are
  acting) **illegally, fraudulently, or in a manner oppressive** to the
  applicant. **Remedy hook:** member-oppression and deadlock claims are
  pursued here; verify the current grounds and whether the Act provides a
  buyout / alternative remedy.
- **§ 4-38-703 through § 4-38-708 — Winding up; distribution of assets;
  known and unknown claims against a dissolved LLC.**

## Derivative actions; records; foreign LLCs

- **§ 4-38-801 through § 4-38-806 — Direct and derivative actions.** A
  member may bring a **direct** action for an injury that is not solely to
  the LLC, or a **derivative** action to enforce a right of the LLC, subject
  to standing and (typically) a demand-or-futility prerequisite and special-
  litigation-committee mechanics. **Pleading hook:** confirm the demand and
  fair-representation requirements before filing.
- **§ 4-38-410 — Member/manager right to information and records.** Access
  to records on reasonable demand and for a purpose material to the
  member's interest; remedy to compel.
- **§ 4-38-901 through § 4-38-906 — Foreign LLCs; registration; transacting
  business without registration** (potential capacity bar).

## Drafting note

For an LLC governance dispute, start with the **operating agreement**, then
overlay the Act's mandatory (non-waivable) terms at § 4-38-110 / § 4-38-111
and the § 4-38-409 fiduciary standards. Oppression/deadlock relief runs
through judicial dissolution at § 4-38-701 / § 4-38-702; member claims run
through the direct/derivative provisions at § 4-38-801 et seq. For a
judgment creditor pursuing a member's interest, the **charging order**
(§ 4-38-503) is generally the exclusive remedy. Confirm whether a pre-2021
LLC is governed by this Act or by the legacy § 4-32-101 statute before
citing default rules.
