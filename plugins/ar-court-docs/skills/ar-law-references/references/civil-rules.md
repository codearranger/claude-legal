## Rule 4 — Summons and service of process

- Governs the **summons** and **initial service** of the complaint.
  Initial service is stricter than the Rule 5 service of later papers.
- Arkansas has historically imposed a **service window** within which
  the plaintiff must complete service after filing (historically
  **120 days** [VERIFY current period]); failure can result in
  dismissal without prejudice.
- Defective method, defective form of summons, or service on the wrong
  person is a **Rule 12(b)** defense (insufficiency of process /
  service of process) that is **waived** if not timely raised.

## Rule 5 — Service and filing of subsequent papers

- After initial Rule 4 service, **every later paper** is served on the
  parties under Rule 5. Serve a represented party's **attorney**.
- Methods: hand delivery, mail to the last known address, and
  **e-service** on registered e-filers under **Administrative Order
  No. 21** [VERIFY the current authorized-method list].
- A **certificate of service** is attached to each served paper.

## Rule 6 — Time computation

- **Rule 6(a)** — exclude the day of the event; include the last day
  unless it is a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday, in which case the
  period runs to the next day that is not. Arkansas legal holidays per
  **Ark. Code Ann. § 1-5-101**.
- **Rule 6(d)** — add **3 days** [VERIFY] to a period that runs from
  service when service is made **by mail**.

## Rule 8 — General rules of pleading

- **Rule 8(a)** — a claim for relief must contain "a statement in
  ordinary and concise language of **facts** showing that the pleader
  is entitled to relief." **ARKANSAS IS A FACT-PLEADING STATE** — facts,
  not conclusions; stricter than federal notice pleading. This drives
  Rule 12(b)(6) practice.
- **Rule 8(c)** — **affirmative defenses** must be set forth
  affirmatively (statute of limitations, payment, release, accord and
  satisfaction, fraud, illegality, estoppel, waiver, statute of frauds,
  etc.) or risk waiver.

## Rule 9 — Pleading special matters

- **Rule 9(b)** — circumstances constituting **fraud or mistake** must
  be stated with **particularity**.

## Rule 10 — Form of pleadings

- **10(a)** — caption: name of the court, the title (parties), the
  docket number ("Civil No." / "No. CV-__-____"), and a designation of
  the paper.
- **10(b)** — averments in **numbered paragraphs**, each limited as far
  as practicable to a single set of circumstances.
- **10(c)** — a copy of a **written instrument** that is an exhibit is
  a part of the pleading for all purposes.

## Rule 11 — Signing of pleadings and motions

- Every pleading / motion is **signed**; the signature **certifies** the
  Rule 11 representations (not for an improper purpose; warranted legal
  contentions; evidentiary support for factual contentions). A pro se
  party signs **for self** (omitting the bar number an attorney
  includes). Sanctions are available for violations.

## Rule 12 — Defenses and objections

- **Answer deadline**: a defendant generally answers within **30 days**
  [VERIFY] after service of the summons and complaint; a **nonresident
  / out-of-state** defendant has a **longer period** [VERIFY the exact
  count]; service on the State / governmental defendants has its own
  count [VERIFY].
- **Rule 12(b)** grounds (may be raised by motion): (1) lack of
  subject-matter jurisdiction; (2) lack of personal jurisdiction; (3)
  improper venue; (4)–(5) insufficiency of process / of service of
  process; **(6) failure to state facts upon which relief can be
  granted**; (7) failure to join a necessary party. Confirm the exact
  numbering [VERIFY].
- A timely Rule 12 motion **suspends** the time to answer until the
  court rules; verify the post-ruling answer window [VERIFY].
- **Waiver / consolidation**: the personal-jurisdiction, venue,
  process, and service-of-process defenses are **waived** if not raised
  in the first response; subject-matter jurisdiction and failure to
  state a claim are treated differently. Confirm current
  consolidation/waiver text [VERIFY].
- **12(b)(6) standard**: facts pleaded taken as true, mere conclusions
  disregarded; the court looks at the complaint + attached exhibits. If
  **matters outside the pleadings** are presented and not excluded, the
  motion is **converted to Rule 56** summary judgment.

## Rule 13 — Counterclaim and cross-claim

- **13(a) compulsory** — a claim arising out of the **same transaction
  or occurrence** must be pleaded or it is **barred**.
- **13(b) permissive** — unrelated claims may be brought.

## Rule 15 — Amended and supplemental pleadings

- Amendment is liberally allowed; **relation back** to the date of the
  original pleading where the claim arises out of the same conduct,
  transaction, or occurrence. Confirm the leave-of-court / timing
  conditions [VERIFY].

## Rules 26–37 — Discovery

- **Rule 26** — scope (any non-privileged matter **relevant** to the
  subject matter and reasonably calculated to lead to admissible
  evidence) + protective orders + the privilege log.
- **Rule 30 / 31** — depositions (oral / on written questions).
- **Rule 33** — **interrogatories**. Arkansas **allows** them, subject
  to a **presumptive cap of 30 interrogatories, including discrete
  subparts** [VERIFY the current number], absent leave of court or a
  stipulation. Answered separately and fully, in writing, **under
  oath**.
- **Rule 34** — requests for production of documents and things; ESI in
  a reasonably usable form.
- **Rule 36** — requests for admission; a matter is **deemed admitted**
  if not timely answered or objected to.
- **Rule 37** — failure to make discovery; **motion to compel** +
  sanctions (expenses/fees; for disobedience of an order, up to
  striking pleadings, dismissal, or default).
- **Response window**: the rule's response period after service
  [VERIFY the exact day count and any pre-answer / served-with-summons

## Rule 55 — Default judgment

- A defendant who fails to plead or otherwise defend within the time
  allowed may have a **default judgment** entered. The rule provides
  for **setting aside** a default / default judgment for the enumerated
  grounds (e.g., mistake, inadvertence, excusable neglect, a meritorious
  defense). Confirm the current grounds + timing [VERIFY].

## Rule 56 — Summary judgment

- The movant must make a **prima facie** showing of entitlement; the
  burden then shifts and the non-movant must **"meet proof with
  proof"** and demonstrate a **genuine issue of material fact**
  (*Wallace v. Broyles*; *Flentje v. First Nat'l Bank of Wynne* — see
  `key-cases.md`). The court views the evidence in the light most
  favorable to the non-movant.
- Response timing: confirm the current response period (commonly framed
  as a **21-day** response window) [VERIFY] in the verbatim rule.

## Rule 60 — Relief from judgment or order

- **Rule 60(a)** — **ARKANSAS QUIRK**: to correct error or mistake or
  to prevent the miscarriage of justice, a circuit court may **modify
  or vacate** a judgment, order, or decree **within 90 days** of its
  having been filed with the clerk.
- **Rule 60(c)** — after the **90-day** window, relief is available only
  on the **narrower enumerated grounds** (e.g., fraud, lack of
  jurisdiction, certain newly discovered evidence). Confirm the current
