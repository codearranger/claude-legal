## The American rule + the principal Arkansas fee statute

Arkansas follows the **American rule** — each party bears its own
attorney's fees unless a statute, rule, or contract shifts them.

- **Ark. Code Ann. § 16-22-308** — the principal Arkansas fee-shifting
  statute. In any **civil action to recover on an open account, statement
  of account, account stated, promissory note, bill, negotiable
  instrument, or contract** relating to the purchase or sale of goods,
  wares, or merchandise (and in certain other civil actions), the court
  **may** award the **prevailing party** a **reasonable attorney's
  fee**. Key features:
  - The award is **discretionary**, not mandatory.
  - It runs to the **prevailing party** — so a prevailing **defendant**
    (e.g., a consumer who defeats a debt-buyer's contract claim) may
    recover fees too. This is a real consideration on both sides of a
    consumer-debt case.
  - Reasonableness is assessed on the usual factors (time, skill, result,
    customary fee, etc.).

## Federal fee provisions commonly invoked alongside Arkansas claims

- **15 U.S.C. § 1692k(a)(3)** — **FDCPA**: a prevailing consumer
  recovers costs and a reasonable attorney's fee (and a court may award
  fees to a defendant for a bad-faith / harassment suit).
- **Ark. Code Ann. § 4-88-113** — **ADTPA** (Arkansas Deceptive Trade
  Practices Act): the remedies provisions; note the **Act 986 of 2017**
  narrowing of the private right of action (actual financial loss +
  reliance element). Confirm the current fee/remedy text [VERIFY].
- **Ark. Code Ann. § 4-75-607** — **Arkansas Trade Secrets Act**:
  attorney's fees available for bad-faith claims / willful and malicious
  misappropriation [VERIFY section].

## Contractual fee provisions

A contractual attorney-fee clause is enforceable per its terms, subject
to reasonableness review. In a contract / consumer-debt case, the
underlying agreement frequently contains a one-way or two-way fee
clause — read it, and note that Arkansas law and § 16-22-308 can affect
how it is applied.

## Costs

- The **prevailing party** is generally entitled to recover statutory
  **costs** (distinct from attorney's fees) — filing fees, service
  fees, and other taxable costs. See Ark. R. Civ. P. (costs) and the
  cost statutes; confirm the current taxable-cost categories [VERIFY].

## Filing fees

- Trial-court filing fees are set by **statute and by the clerk** of
  each court and differ between **Circuit Court** and **District Court**
  (and within District Court between the civil and small claims
  divisions). **Confirm the current filing fee with the clerk of the
  filing court** before filing — do not hard-code a dollar figure
  [VERIFY].

### Fee waiver / in forma pauperis

- An indigent litigant may seek leave to proceed **in forma pauperis**
  (without prepayment of fees) by affidavit of indigency. Confirm the
  current procedure and form with the clerk / arcourts.gov [VERIFY].
