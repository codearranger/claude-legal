# Arkansas Security Deposits — Ark. Code Ann. § 18-16-301 et seq.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the unit-count exemption, the cap,
> the return period, and the noncompliance remedy against the
> current Ark. Code Ann. before relying.

Arkansas's security-deposit statute (Ark. Code Ann. § 18-16-301
et seq.) sets a cap, a return-and-itemization period, and a
remedy for noncompliance — **but it carries a small-landlord
exemption that swallows a large share of cases.** The first
question in any deposit dispute is whether the landlord is even
**covered**.

## The small-landlord exemption — check this FIRST

The deposit rules **do not apply** to a landlord who owns at or
below the statutory unit count — commonly read as **five or
fewer** rental units (with a caveat where a management company is
involved). An exempt landlord is not bound by the cap, the
return period, or the itemization requirement.

- Confirm the exact unit-count threshold and the
  management-company caveat in the current § 18-16-303 / -305.
- Before pleading any deposit remedy, establish that the landlord
  is **covered** (above the exemption). If exempt, the statutory
  deposit claim fails.

## The rules (for covered landlords)

1. **Cap** on the amount of the deposit (a multiple of monthly
   rent — confirm the exact multiple).
2. **Return + itemization** within the statutory window after the
   tenant vacates and provides a forwarding address — a **60-day**
   period — with a **written itemized** statement of any
   deductions (unpaid rent, damages beyond ordinary wear).
3. **Noncompliance remedy.** A covered landlord who wrongfully
   withholds the deposit or fails to itemize is liable for the
   amount wrongfully withheld and (where the statute provides)
   additional damages. Confirm the remedy / any damages multiple.

## Ordinary wear and tear

Deductions are limited to unpaid rent and damage **beyond
ordinary wear and tear**. Routine cleaning, normal carpet wear,
and minor scuffs are not deductible.

## Volatile numbers (confirm against current statute)

| Item | Value | Citation |
|---|---|---|
| Small-landlord exemption | ≤ 5 units (confirm) | § 18-16-303 |
| Deposit cap | multiple of monthly rent | § 18-16-304 |
| Return + itemization period | 60 days | § 18-16-305 |
| Noncompliance remedy | amount withheld (+ damages) | § 18-16-305 |

## Practical posture

- **Tenant** asserting a deposit claim: confirm coverage first;
  send a written demand with a forwarding address; calendar the
  60-day return window; if the landlord is covered and fails to
  return/itemize, sue in District Court small claims.
- **Landlord**: if covered, itemize in writing within the window;
  if exempt, the statute does not govern (the lease and common
  law do).
