# Arkansas Implied Habitability — Act 1052 of 2021 (§ 18-17-501 et seq.)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the effective date, the minimum
> standards, the cure period, and the remedy against the current
> Ark. Code Ann. before relying.

For most of its history Arkansas recognized **no implied warranty
of habitability** — it was the **last state in the nation** with
none. **Act 1052 of 2021** changed that, adding a narrow set of
minimum standards at Ark. Code Ann. § 18-17-501 et seq. (the key
operative section is **§ 18-17-502**). The reform is real but
minimal, and the tenant's remedy is sharply limited.

## Coverage

- Applies to **residential** leases entered **after the Act's
  effective date** — leases made **after Nov. 1, 2021**. Leases
  predating the effective date are not covered. Confirm the
  trigger date against the codified effective-date language.

## Minimum standards (§ 18-17-502)

A short, enumerated floor. The landlord must provide, at the
**start of the lease**, a dwelling that meets minimum standards
such as:

- Sound structure (roof, walls, foundation in safe condition);
- Available **potable running water**;
- Available **electricity**;
- Working **plumbing**;
- **Heating and air conditioning** available at the
  commencement of the tenancy.

This is a **floor**, not the broad, ongoing implied warranty
recognized in other jurisdictions. Confirm the exact enumerated
list in the current § 18-17-502.

## The tenant's remedy — notice, cure, terminate

The remedy is **limited** and procedurally strict:

1. **Written notice.** The tenant must give the landlord
   **written notice** specifying the deficiency.
2. **Cure period.** The landlord has a **30-day** window to cure
   (confirm the exact period in § 18-17-502).
3. **Terminate + recover deposit.** If the landlord does **not**
   cure within the window, the tenant's remedy is to **terminate
   the lease and recover the security deposit**.

**There is no rent-withholding right and no repair-and-deduct
right.** A tenant who simply stops paying rent over a habitability
complaint is exposed to civil unlawful detainer (§ 18-60-301) and
criminal failure to vacate (§ 18-16-101). The only safe path is
the written-notice → cure-window → terminate-and-leave sequence.

## What it does NOT provide

- No rent escrow / rent withholding.
- No repair-and-deduct.
- No damages beyond return of the deposit on termination.
- No private cause of action for damages for breach of the
  standard beyond the termination remedy (confirm scope).
