# Arkansas Wage and Hour — Arkansas Minimum Wage Act (§ 11-4-201 et seq.)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** The minimum-wage figure is volatile —
> confirm the current rate and the coverage threshold against the
> live Ark. Code Ann. and the Department of Labor and Licensing
> before relying.

The **Arkansas Minimum Wage Act** (Ark. Code Ann. § 11-4-201 et
seq.) sets the state minimum wage, overtime, and recordkeeping
rules for covered Arkansas employers.

## Coverage

- **Four (4) or more** employees (§ 11-4-203 — confirm). Smaller
  employers fall outside the state Act (the federal FLSA may still
  apply based on enterprise/individual coverage).

## Minimum wage

- The Arkansas minimum wage was raised by **Issue 5 of 2018**, a
  voter ballot initiative, and **fully phased in by 2021**.
- **Current state minimum wage: $11.00/hour** (confirm against the
  current statute / agency rate — this figure is volatile and
  should be re-checked at every use).
- Where the federal FLSA minimum is higher, the higher rate
  controls for FLSA-covered employees.

## Overtime

- Overtime at **one-and-one-half** the regular rate for hours over
  40 in a workweek for covered, non-exempt employees (parallels
  the FLSA framework). Confirm exemptions.

## Remedies

- An employee may recover **unpaid wages** plus, where the Act
  provides, **additional/liquidated damages** and **attorney's
  fees**. Confirm the current remedy and any multiplier.
- The SOL for a wage claim should be confirmed (contract vs.
  statutory characterization).

## Sovereign-immunity trap

A wage claim against the **State** cannot be brought in circuit
court. *Bd. of Trustees of Univ. of Ark. v. Andrews*, 2018 Ark.
12, struck the Minimum Wage Act's purported waiver of State
sovereign immunity as unconstitutional — the legislature cannot
waive the Ark. Const. art. 5, § 20 bar. State-employer wage claims
route to the **Arkansas State Claims Commission**.
