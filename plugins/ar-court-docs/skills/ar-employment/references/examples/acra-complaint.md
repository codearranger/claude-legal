**Authoring notes (delete before any real use):**

- The Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993 (Ark. Code Ann. § 16-123-101
  et seq.) covers employers with **nine (9) or more employees** and
  permits a direct circuit-court action; confirm the coverage
  threshold and the **one-year** suit-filing period against current
  law — keep both in `ar-acra.md` / `ar-statutes-of-limitations.md`.
- The initiating Complaint is served with a **summons under Ark. R.
  Civ. P. 4**, not by a Rule 5 certificate of service.
- Arkansas is a **fact-pleading** jurisdiction (Ark. R. Civ. P. 8(a));
  plead concrete facts supporting the inference of discrimination, not
  bare conclusions — see `ar-first-30-days`.
- The ACRA may permit individual liability in some circumstances and
  allows compensatory and punitive damages plus attorney's fees;
  confirm the remedy menu (and note Arkansas's constitutional limit on
  damages caps, Ark. Const. art. 5, § 32) in the references corpus.
- Workers'-compensation, minimum-wage, and whistleblower theories sit
  in different statutes and forums (e.g., the Arkansas Workers'
  Compensation Commission for comp claims) — keep them separate; see
  `ar-employment`.
