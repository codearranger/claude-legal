# Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993 (ACRA) — § 16-123-101 et seq.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the coverage threshold, the SOL,
> and the protected-class list against the current Ark. Code Ann.

The **Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993** (Ark. Code Ann.
§ 16-123-101 et seq.) is the state employment-discrimination
statute. It runs in parallel with — but is distinct from — Title
VII, the ADA, and the ADEA.

## Coverage and protected classes

- **Employer coverage**: **nine (9) or more** employees
  (§ 16-123-102). (Note this is broader than Title VII's 15-
  employee floor for state-law purposes — but confirm the count
  rule and the look-back period.)
- **Protected classes** (§ 16-123-107): **race, religion, ancestry
  or national origin, gender, and disability**. ACRA does **not**
  enumerate age the way the ADEA does — confirm scope before
  relying.
- Disability claims cross-reference the federal ADA standards in
  practice; confirm the current statutory text.

## No administrative exhaustion

Arkansas has **no state fair-employment-practices agency** and
**no charge-filing prerequisite** for an ACRA claim. A plaintiff
may file **directly in circuit court**. (A plaintiff pursuing a
**federal** Title VII / ADA claim must still file an EEOC charge
within the federal deadline — keep the two tracks separate.)

## Statute of limitations — 1 year

ACRA carries a **one-year** limitations period to file suit. This
is short; calendar it at intake. The clock and any tolling for a
parallel EEOC charge should be confirmed against the current
statute and case law.

## Individual liability

ACRA can reach **individual** defendants (supervisors, managers)
in some circumstances — unlike Title VII, which limits liability
to the employer entity. Confirm the current scope; naming
individuals can affect removal and diversity.

## Remedies

- **Compensatory** damages.
- **Punitive** damages (no statutory cap — Ark. Const. art. 5,
  § 32).
- **Attorney's fees** (fee-shifting to the prevailing plaintiff).
- Equitable relief (reinstatement, back pay, front pay).

## Prima facie frameworks

ACRA claims generally follow the federal **_McDonnell Douglas_**
burden-shifting framework (prima facie case → legitimate
non-discriminatory reason → pretext) for circumstantial-evidence
cases, and a direct-evidence framework otherwise. Confirm the
current Arkansas construction.

## Sovereign-immunity trap

A claim against the **State** (a state agency, a state university)
**cannot be brought in circuit court** — *Bd. of Trustees of Univ.
of Ark. v. Andrews*, 2018 Ark. 12. State-employer discrimination
claims face the immunity bar; the federal track (Title VII / ADA)
may proceed against state employers in federal court under its own
abrogation analysis.
