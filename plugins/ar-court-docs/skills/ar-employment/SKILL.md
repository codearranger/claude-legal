# Arkansas Employment Practice

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Employment claims have short deadlines and
> coverage thresholds. Verify the SOL, the employee-count
> coverage, and every dollar figure against the current Ark. Code
> Ann. before relying, and consult a licensed Arkansas attorney
> about your specific case.

Use this subject-matter bundle for Arkansas employment disputes —
discrimination and harassment, wrongful termination, unpaid wages
and minimum wage, whistleblower retaliation, non-compete
enforcement, and workers' compensation overlap. Arkansas is an
**at-will**, **right-to-work** state with a state civil-rights act
that, unlike the federal scheme, has **no administrative-
exhaustion prerequisite** — a plaintiff may file directly in
circuit court.

## At a glance — the things that move every AR employment case

1. **Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993 (ACRA)** at § 16-123-101 et
   seq. — covers employers with **nine or more** employees; a
   **1-year SOL** to file suit; **individual liability** is
   available in some circumstances; recoverable compensatory +
   punitive + **attorney's fees**. **No state FEPA exhaustion** —
   file directly in circuit court. See
   `references/acra-discrimination.md`.
2. **Arkansas Minimum Wage Act** at § 11-4-201 et seq. — covers
   employers with **four or more** employees; state minimum wage
   set by **Issue 5 of 2018** ballot initiative (the dollar figure
   lives in `references/wage-and-hour.md`).
3. **At-will + public-policy exception** — *Sterling Drug, Inc. v.
   Oxford*, 294 Ark. 239 (1988). See
   `references/whistleblower-and-public-policy.md`.
4. **Arkansas Whistle-Blower Act** at § 21-1-601 et seq. — protects
   **public** employees who report waste/violations. See
   `references/whistleblower-and-public-policy.md`.
5. **Non-compete: Act 921 of 2015** at § 4-75-101 — codifies the
   enforceability standard and **authorizes judicial reformation
   ("blue-penciling")** of overbroad covenants. See
   `references/noncompete-and-workers-comp.md`.
6. **Workers' comp is the exclusive remedy** for workplace injury
   (§ 11-9-101/-105) — adjudicated by the **Arkansas Workers'
   Compensation Commission**, not circuit court.
7. **Right-to-work** — **Ark. Const. Amendment 34** (1944) +
   § 11-3-301 et seq.

## Discrimination — Arkansas Civil Rights Act of 1993 (ACRA)

The **ACRA** (Ark. Code Ann. § 16-123-101 et seq.) is the state
analog to the federal employment-discrimination statutes:

- **Protected classes**: race, religion, ancestry / national
  origin, gender, and disability (§ 16-123-107). (Confirm the
  precise list and any disability cross-reference.)
- **Coverage threshold**: employers with **nine (9) or more**
  employees (§ 16-123-102).
- **SOL**: **one (1) year** to file suit — short; calendar at
  intake.
- **No administrative exhaustion.** Unlike the federal scheme,
  Arkansas has **no state FEPA** requiring a charge before suit.
  A plaintiff may file **directly in circuit court** (a parallel
  EEOC/Title VII track has its own charge requirement).
- **Individual liability** is available in some circumstances —
  ACRA can reach individuals, unlike Title VII.
- **Remedies**: compensatory + punitive damages + **attorney's
  fees** (fee-shifting). No statutory cap (Ark. Const. art. 5,
  § 32).

See `references/acra-discrimination.md` for the prima-facie
frameworks, the disability provisions, and the remedies detail.

## Wage and hour — Arkansas Minimum Wage Act

The **Arkansas Minimum Wage Act** (§ 11-4-201 et seq.):

- **Coverage**: employers with **four (4) or more** employees.
- **Minimum wage**: set by **Issue 5 of 2018** (a voter
  initiative), fully phased in by 2021. The current dollar figure
  is in `references/wage-and-hour.md` (kept out of the body
  because it is volatile).
- **Overtime**, recordkeeping, and remedies parallel the federal
  FLSA framework; an unpaid-wage claim may carry liquidated
  damages and fees (confirm).
- Note the **sovereign-immunity** trap: a wage claim against the
  **State** cannot be brought in circuit court — *Bd. of Trustees
  of Univ. of Ark. v. Andrews*, 2018 Ark. 12, held the Act's
  waiver of State immunity unconstitutional. State-employer wage
  claims route to the State Claims Commission.

## At-will employment and the public-policy exception

Arkansas is a strong **at-will** state — absent a contract,
either party may end the employment for any reason or none. The
principal judicial exception is the **public-policy exception**
recognized in **_Sterling Drug, Inc. v. Oxford_, 294 Ark. 239
(1988)**: an at-will employee may sue for **wrongful discharge**
where the termination violates a well-established public policy of
the state (e.g., firing for refusing to commit an illegal act, for
performing a public duty, or for exercising a statutory right).
**Implied-contract / handbook** exceptions also exist where an
employer's manual creates enforceable promises. See
`references/whistleblower-and-public-policy.md`.

## Whistleblower — Arkansas Whistle-Blower Act (public employees)

The **Arkansas Whistle-Blower Act** (§ 21-1-601 et seq.) protects
**public** employees (of public employers) who report or refuse to
participate in waste, violations of law, or abuse of authority. It
does **not** cover private-sector employees — a private whistleblower
relies on the *Sterling Drug* public-policy tort instead. See
`references/whistleblower-and-public-policy.md` for the protected-
activity definition, the SOL, and remedies.

## Non-compete — Act 921 of 2015 (§ 4-75-101)

Before 2015, Arkansas courts would **void** an overbroad covenant
not to compete entirely (no blue-penciling). **Act 921 of 2015**
(codified at § 4-75-101) changed that:

- It **codifies the enforceability standard** — a covenant is
  enforceable if the employer has a **protectable business
  interest** and the covenant is **reasonable** in time and scope.
- It **expressly authorizes judicial reformation
  ("blue-penciling")** — a court may **modify** an overbroad
  covenant to make it reasonable rather than striking it whole.
  This is a significant change from prior common law.

See `references/noncompete-and-workers-comp.md` for the
protectable-interest factors and the reformation mechanics.

## Workers' compensation — exclusive remedy

Workplace injuries are governed by the **Arkansas Workers'
Compensation Act** (§ 11-9-101 et seq.), and § 11-9-105 makes the
Act the **exclusive remedy** against the employer — the injured
worker generally **cannot** sue the employer in tort. Claims are
adjudicated by the **Arkansas Workers' Compensation Commission**,
not circuit court. The exclusive-remedy bar is a frequent defense
to intentional-tort and negligence claims brought against an
employer; narrow exceptions exist (e.g., intentional injury).
See `references/noncompete-and-workers-comp.md`.

## Right-to-work — Amendment 34

**Ark. Const. Amendment 34** (1944) plus § 11-3-301 et seq. make
Arkansas a **right-to-work** state: union membership and the
payment of dues/fees cannot be a condition of employment.

## High-yield Arkansas employment checks

1. **Discrimination?** ACRA covers **9+** employees, **1-year
   SOL**, **no exhaustion**, individual liability, fees
   (§ 16-123-101). Parallel EEOC/Title VII track for federal
   claims.
2. **Wage claim?** Minimum Wage Act covers **4+** employees
   (§ 11-4-201); current minimum-wage figure in references. State
   employer? → Claims Commission (*Andrews*), not circuit court.
3. **Wrongful discharge?** At-will unless a **public-policy**
   (*Sterling Drug*) or implied-contract exception applies.
4. **Whistleblower?** Public employee → Whistle-Blower Act
   (§ 21-1-601). Private employee → *Sterling Drug* public-policy
   tort.
5. **Non-compete?** Act 921 of 2015 (§ 4-75-101) — protectable
   interest + reasonableness; courts **blue-pencil**.
6. **Workplace injury?** Workers' comp is the **exclusive remedy**
   (§ 11-9-105) before the Workers' Compensation Commission — not
   circuit court.

## Drafting checklist

- [ ] For an ACRA claim, confirm **9+ employees**, calendar the
      **1-year SOL**, and decide whether to name **individuals**
      (§ 16-123-101); plead **attorney's fees**.
- [ ] Do **not** assume a state-agency exhaustion step for ACRA —
      file directly in circuit court (preserve any parallel EEOC
      charge for the federal track).
- [ ] For a wage claim, confirm **4+ employees** and pull the
      **current minimum wage** from `references/wage-and-hour.md`;
      if the employer is the **State**, route to the Claims
      Commission (*Andrews*).
- [ ] For wrongful discharge, anchor to a **specific public
      policy** (*Sterling Drug*) or an enforceable handbook
      promise.
- [ ] For a non-compete dispute, brief the **protectable
      interest** and **reasonableness**, and address **blue-
      pencil reformation** (Act 921 of 2015).
- [ ] For any workplace injury, check the **workers'-comp
      exclusive-remedy bar** (§ 11-9-105) before pleading a tort.
