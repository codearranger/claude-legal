# Arkansas Divorce — Grounds, Corroboration, Residency, and the Waiting Period

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Reference notes for drafting. Verify every
> ground, day/month count, and citation against the current
> Ark. Code Ann. Title 9, ch. 12 before filing.

Statutory home: **Ark. Code Ann. Title 9, Chapter 12** (Divorce and
Annulment). Grounds at **§ 9-12-301**; residency at **§ 9-12-307**.

## Forum

Divorce is heard in the **Domestic Relations Division of Circuit
Court** (no separate family court; Amendment 80 (2001) unified the
trial courts). Parties are styled **Plaintiff / Defendant** (Arkansas
divorce uses Plaintiff/Defendant, not Petitioner/Respondent). See
`ar-family-court`.

## Grounds (§ 9-12-301) — Arkansas has NO pure no-fault divorce

Arkansas requires a statutory **ground**, pleaded and proved, in every
divorce. The grounds fall into two groups.

### Fault grounds (§ 9-12-301(b), enumerated)

Commonly relied upon:

- **General indignities** — the offering of such indignities to the
  person of the other as to render his or her condition intolerable
  (the workhorse fault ground in uncontested cases)
- **Adultery**
- **Cruel and barbarous treatment** as to endanger the life of the
  other
- **Habitual drunkenness** for one (1) year
- **Conviction of a felony or other infamous crime**
- **Willful failure** of the spouse to provide the common necessaries
  of life when of sufficient ability to do so
- **Impotence** (incurable)
- Other enumerated grounds (e.g., spouse insane and confined for the
  statutory period; certain incurable conditions)

> **Volatile — verify subsection lettering/numbering** at the current
> § 9-12-301 before pleading. The enumerated list and its sub-lettering
> are periodically amended.

### The "no-fault" ground — 18 months' continuous separation

The nearest thing to no-fault is **living separate and apart from each
other for eighteen (18) continuous months without cohabitation**
(**§ 9-12-301(b)**). The separation must be **continuous** and
**without cohabitation**. Even on this ground, **residence** and the
**fact and duration of separation** generally must be **corroborated**.

## The Arkansas corroboration requirement (distinctive)

Arkansas is **distinctive** in still requiring **independent
third-party corroboration**:

- **Corroboration of the ground** — testimony or affidavit of a
  witness other than the parties supporting the alleged ground (e.g.,
  that the parties have lived separate and apart, or to the conduct
  underlying general indignities). A bare admission by the defendant
  is **not sufficient**.
- **Corroboration of residence** — the residency allegation must also
  be corroborated.

> **Limited relaxation.** Where the parties have lived **separate and
> apart** and there is **no collusion**, Arkansas relaxes the
> corroboration-of-the-grounds requirement in some configurations (the
> corroboration may be minimal), but **residence still must be
> corroborated** and the relaxation does **not** dispense with proof.
> Verify the **current** statutory text and the controlling case law
> before assuming corroboration is excused.

Practical effect: plan for a **corroborating witness** (a friend,
relative, or neighbor) to testify or sign an affidavit, even in an
agreed/uncontested divorce.

## Residency (§ 9-12-307) — VOLATILE counts

**Ark. Code Ann. § 9-12-307** sets the residency requirement. As a
framework (confirm the exact figures against the current statute):

| Requirement | Period (VERIFY against current § 9-12-307) |
|---|---|
| Residence in Arkansas **before filing** | **60 days** |
| Residence in Arkansas **before the final decree** may be entered | **3 months** |

The residence must be **proved and corroborated**. Where the plaintiff
is a nonresident but the defendant is an Arkansas resident, venue and
residency analysis differ — verify.

## Waiting period before the decree — VOLATILE count

A divorce decree may **not** be entered until a statutory **waiting
period from filing** has run.

| Item | Period (VERIFY against current statute) |
|---|---|
| Waiting period from **filing the complaint** to entry of decree | **30 days** |

Compute with `ar-deadlines` (Ark. R. Civ. P. 6 time computation,
Ark. Code Ann. § 1-5-101 holidays). The clock runs from **filing**,
not service.

## Default vs. contested

- **Even on default or by agreement**, the plaintiff must still
  **prove and corroborate** grounds and residence to obtain the
  decree. Arkansas does **not** grant divorce on the pleadings alone;
  there is no "divorce by default judgment" in the ordinary sense —
  the court takes proof.
- **Contested** cases proceed to a hearing/trial on the ground(s),
  property, custody, support, and alimony.

## Pleadings

- **Complaint for Divorce** — Ark. R. Civ. P. 10 caption (Domestic
  Relations Division), numbered paragraphs (Rule 10(b)), residency and
  ground allegations, UCCJEA allegations if children, prayer for
  relief (divorce, property division, custody/support, alimony).
- **Answer** — due within the Ark. R. Civ. P. 12 period after service
  (longer for nonresident/out-of-state defendants) — see
  `ar-first-30-days`. A defendant may file a **waiver of service /
  entry of appearance** in an agreed case.
- **Redaction** — comply with **Administrative Order No. 19** (redact
  SSNs, account numbers, minors' identifiers) and attach the
  certificate of compliance; see `ar-statewide-format`.
