# Arkansas Alimony / Spousal Support — § 9-12-312

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Reference notes for drafting. Verify the
> factors and the modification/termination rules against the current
> Ark. Code Ann. § 9-12-312 and controlling case law before relying on
> anything here.

Statutory home: **Ark. Code Ann. § 9-12-312** (Allowances —
alimony / spousal support). Arkansas alimony is **discretionary** —
there is **no statutory formula**.

## Discretionary award — no formula

Whether to award alimony, in what amount, and for how long, is
committed to the **sound discretion of the circuit court**. There is
**no Arkansas alimony calculator or guideline**. The primary purpose
is to **rectify the economic imbalance** in earning power and standard
of living between the spouses, in light of the particular facts.

## Factors

The court weighs qualitative factors drawn from § 9-12-312 and
Arkansas case law (verify the current list):

- The **financial need** of one spouse and the **ability of the other
  to pay**
- The **earning capacity, education, and employability** of each
  spouse
- The **financial circumstances** of both parties, including the
  **property division** in the divorce
- The **length of the marriage**
- The **age and health** of the parties
- The **standard of living** established during the marriage
- The amount and nature of each party's income and resources
- (Where relevant) the **conduct** of the parties during the marriage

> Alimony interacts with the **property division** (see
> `property-distribution.md`): a court may consider the property each
> party receives — including **non-marital** property — when deciding
> alimony, and may adjust one to balance the other.

## Forms and timing

- **Temporary (pendente lite) alimony** — support during the pendency
  of the divorce.
- **Rehabilitative / fixed-term** or **permanent periodic** alimony —
  set by the court on the facts.
- Alimony may be awarded in **periodic payments**.

## Modification and termination

- **Modification.** Periodic alimony is generally **modifiable** on a
  **material change of circumstances**. File a petition to modify in
  the court that entered the decree.
- **Termination.** Alimony generally **terminates** on:
  - the **death** of either party,
  - the **remarriage** of the recipient, and
  - (commonly) **cohabitation** of the recipient in a marriage-like
    relationship — **verify the current statutory cohabitation rule**
    and the operative date / definition at § 9-12-312.

## Practical drafting notes

- **Plead the factual basis** (income disparity, contribution to the
  other's earning capacity, length of marriage) — not just a demand
  for alimony.
- **Coordinate with property and support.** Because alimony,
  property division, and child support interact, present them as a
  coherent financial picture.
- **Specify the type and duration** sought (temporary, fixed-term,
  permanent periodic) and the requested modification/termination
  terms.
