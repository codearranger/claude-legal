# Arkansas Covenant Marriage — § 9-11-801 et seq.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Reference notes for drafting. Verify the
> formation requirements, the limited divorce/separation grounds, and
> the counseling prerequisites against the current Ark. Code Ann.
> § 9-11-801 et seq. before relying on anything here.

Statutory home: **Ark. Code Ann. § 9-11-801 et seq.** (Covenant
Marriage Act). Arkansas is **one of only three states** that offer
covenant marriage. A covenant marriage is an **opt-in, more durable**
form of marriage.

## Formation

To contract a covenant marriage, the couple must (verify the current
statutory steps):

- Receive **authorized premarital counseling** emphasizing the
  seriousness and permanence of marriage and the agreement to seek
  counseling if marital difficulties arise; and
- Execute a **Declaration of Intent** to contract a covenant marriage,
  including a recital/affidavit in the statutorily prescribed form,
  signed by the parties (and the counselor), filed with the marriage
  license application.

**Existing married couples** may **convert** an ordinary marriage into
a covenant marriage by executing the prescribed declaration — verify
the current conversion procedure.

## Restricted divorce / separation grounds

The defining feature: a covenant-married spouse may obtain a
**divorce or legal separation only on a restricted, enumerated set of
grounds** — **narrower** than the ordinary § 9-12-301 grounds. The
covenant grounds typically include (verify the current list at
§ 9-11-808 / § 9-11-809):

- **Adultery**
- **Conviction of a felony or other infamous crime** with a sentence
  of imprisonment or death
- **Abandonment** for the statutory period
- **Physical or sexual abuse** of the spouse or a child of the parties
- **Living separate and apart** for the statutory covenant period
  (longer than / distinct from the ordinary 18-month ground — verify)

> Practical effect: for a covenant marriage, do **not** assume the
> full ordinary § 9-12-301 grounds menu (including general indignities
> as broadly applied) is available. Confirm which covenant grounds fit
> the facts.

## Mandatory counseling before dissolution

Before filing for divorce or separation, the covenant-married spouse
is generally required to have taken **reasonable steps to preserve the
marriage**, including **marital / authorized counseling** — verify the
current counseling prerequisite and any exceptions (e.g., for abuse).

## Practical drafting notes

- **Screen for covenant marriage at intake.** Ask whether the parties
  signed a covenant-marriage declaration; if so, the grounds and
  counseling analysis change materially.
- **Plead a covenant ground**, not an ordinary § 9-12-301 ground that
  is unavailable in a covenant marriage.
- **Document the counseling** prerequisite where required.
