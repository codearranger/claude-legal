# Arkansas Child Support — Income-Shares / Administrative Order No. 10

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Reference notes for drafting. The Family
> Support Chart figures, worksheet, and deviation criteria are set by
> **Administrative Order No. 10** and are amended periodically — always
> run the **current** official worksheet rather than relying on stale
> numbers.

Governing authority: the Arkansas Supreme Court's **Administrative
Order No. 10** (child-support guidelines and the Family Support
Chart). Statutory support backdrop in **Ark. Code Ann. Title 9,
ch. 12 / ch. 14**.

## The income-shares model (since July 1, 2020)

Effective **July 1, 2020**, Arkansas switched from the old
**percentage-of-payor-income** model to an **income-shares** model.
Under income shares:

1. **Combine both parents' gross incomes.**
2. Look up the **basic child-support obligation** on the **Family
   Support Chart** for the **combined income** and the **number of
   children**.
3. **Apportion** the obligation between the parents in **proportion to
   their respective incomes**.
4. **Adjust** for parenting time / overnights, child-care, and
   health-insurance / medical costs.

The result is the **presumptively correct** support amount.

## Worksheet inputs (VERIFY against the current Order No. 10 worksheet)

- **Gross income** of each parent, with statutory adjustments (e.g.,
  for self-employment, pre-existing support orders, support of other
  children) — verify the current definition of income and the
  adjustments
- **Number of overnights / parenting time** with each parent (the
  parenting-time adjustment)
- **Work-related child-care** costs
- **Health-insurance premium** attributable to the child
- **Recurring uninsured / extraordinary medical** expenses

## Deviation

The chart amount is a **rebuttable presumption**. A court may
**deviate** up or down only with **written findings** stating:

- the **presumptive** chart amount,
- the **deviation factors** relied upon, and
- why the deviation is in the **best interest of the child**.

Common deviation factors (verify the current list in Order No. 10):
extraordinary medical/educational/dental needs of the child, the
income of a custodial parent that materially affects the analysis, the
needs of additional dependents, extraordinary travel costs for
visitation, and the overall best interest of the child.

## Modification

- Support may be **modified** on a **material change of
  circumstances**.
- A change in gross income or in the chart that produces a difference
  of a statutory percentage / dollar threshold from the existing order
  is often treated as a material change — **verify the current
  threshold** in Order No. 10 / Title 9 before relying on it.
- File a **motion / petition to modify** in the court that entered the
  order. Modification is generally **prospective** from the date of
  filing.

## Enforcement

Enforcement runs through the Arkansas child-support program (Office of
Child Support Enforcement / DHS): **income withholding / wage
assignment**, license suspension, tax intercept, liens, and
**contempt**. Interstate establishment, modification, and enforcement
go through **UIFSA, Ark. Code Ann. § 9-17-101 et seq.** — see
`uccjea-uifsa.md`.
