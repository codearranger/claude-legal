# Arkansas UCCJEA and UIFSA — § 9-19-101 and § 9-17-101

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Reference notes for drafting. Verify the
> jurisdictional definitions, the home-state analysis, and the
> registration mechanics against the current Ark. Code Ann.
> § 9-19-101 et seq. and § 9-17-101 et seq. before relying on anything
> here.

Two separate interstate frameworks:

- **UCCJEA** — Uniform Child Custody Jurisdiction and Enforcement Act,
  **Ark. Code Ann. § 9-19-101 et seq.** — governs which state has
  jurisdiction over **custody / parenting** determinations.
- **UIFSA** — Uniform Interstate Family Support Act, **Ark. Code Ann.
  § 9-17-101 et seq.** — governs interstate **child-support**
  establishment, modification, and enforcement.

## UCCJEA — custody jurisdiction (§ 9-19-101 et seq.)

### Home-state analysis

The threshold for an **initial** custody determination is the child's
**home state**:

- **Home state** = the state where the child lived with a parent (or a
  person acting as a parent) for **at least 6 consecutive months**
  immediately before the proceeding (or since birth, for a child under
  6 months). A temporary absence counts as part of the period.
- Arkansas has **initial jurisdiction** if Arkansas **is** the child's
  home state, or **was** the home state within **6 months** before the
  proceeding and the child is absent but a parent still lives in
  Arkansas.
- If no state qualifies as home state, the **significant-connection /
  substantial-evidence** basis may apply — verify § 9-19-201.

### Other UCCJEA pieces

- **Exclusive, continuing jurisdiction** (§ 9-19-202) — the state that
  made the initial determination generally keeps jurisdiction to modify
  it until the statutory connection is lost.
- **Modification jurisdiction** (§ 9-19-203) — limits when Arkansas may
  modify another state's order.
- **Temporary emergency jurisdiction** (§ 9-19-204) — where the child
  is present in Arkansas and abandoned, or emergency protection is
  needed from abuse.
- **Registration and enforcement** of out-of-state custody orders
  (§ 9-19-301 et seq.).
- **UCCJEA affidavit.** Pleadings must include the child's residence
  history / the statutorily required information about where the child
  has lived and any other custody proceedings — include this affidavit
  with the petition.

## UIFSA — interstate support (§ 9-17-101 et seq.)

UIFSA controls interstate **child-support** (and spousal-support
enforcement) matters:

- **One controlling order.** UIFSA's central rule is that there is
  **one controlling support order** at a time; UIFSA resolves which
  state's order controls when more than one exists.
- **Continuing, exclusive jurisdiction (CEJ).** The state that issued
  the controlling order generally retains **CEJ** to modify it as long
  as the obligor, obligee, or child resides there (or the parties
  consent).
- **Establishment.** A support order may be established against a
  nonresident obligor through **long-arm** bases or through a
  **two-state** proceeding.
- **Registration for enforcement / modification.** Register an
  out-of-state order in Arkansas to **enforce** it; **modification** of
  a foreign order requires the stricter UIFSA modification conditions
  (none of the parties resides in the issuing state, or consent).
- Administered with the **Office of Child Support Enforcement** (DHS) —
  see `child-support.md`.

## Practical drafting notes

- **Plead UCCJEA jurisdiction** in any petition involving children:
  allege Arkansas is the home state (with the residence dates) and
  attach the UCCJEA affidavit of the child's residences.
- **Check for a prior order** in another state before filing — a prior
  custody or support order can deprive Arkansas of jurisdiction to make
  an initial determination or limit it to enforcement.
- **Match the framework to the relief**: custody → UCCJEA; support →
  UIFSA. They are distinct and can put jurisdiction in different states.
