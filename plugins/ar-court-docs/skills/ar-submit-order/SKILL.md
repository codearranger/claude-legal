# Post-Hearing Order Submission (Arkansas)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This skill is a procedural and drafting aid,
> not legal advice. Verify current rules, deadlines, and venue-specific
> order-submission practices before relying on anything here. Pair with
> substantive review by counsel where stakes warrant.

Use this skill **after the court has ruled** and a written order must be
prepared and submitted. In Arkansas, a ruling from the bench is **not
final until a written order is signed and entered of record** — and the
**prevailing party usually prepares the proposed order** at the court's
direction. Getting this step right is what makes the ruling actually
take effect (and start any appeal clock).

## Why the written order matters

- An oral ruling does not dispose of the matter; the **entry of a
  written, signed order** does. Deadlines (including the time to appeal
  and the Rule 60(a) 90-day window) generally run from **entry**, not
  from the bench ruling — see `ar-deadlines` and `ar-post-judgment`.
- The order's wording controls. Draft it to capture **exactly** what the
  court ruled — no more (do not slip in relief the court did not grant)
  and no less. See `ar-fact-check` to confirm the order matches the
  ruling and the motion.

## The standard sequence

1. **Prepare the proposed order** — use the Ark. R. Civ. P. 10(a)
   caption (see `ar-statewide-format`), recite the matter heard and the
   date, state the court's findings/rulings, and provide a signature
   line for the judge and a date/entry line. See `ar-draft-order`.
2. **Circulate to opposing counsel / the other party for "approved as
   to form."** The customary Arkansas practice is to send the proposed
   order to the other side and ask them to sign or initial an
   **"Approved as to form"** line before it goes to the judge.
   - "Approved as to form" means the other side agrees the order
     **accurately reflects the ruling** — it is **not** a concession on
     the merits.
   - If the other side **disagrees** with the form, they may propose
     **redlines**, submit a **competing/precedent order**, or note their
     objection so the court can resolve the wording.
   - If the other side **does not respond** within the time the local
     rule / the court allows, follow the venue's procedure for
     submitting over non-response (often a cover letter to chambers
     noting the circulation and lack of objection) — confirm the local
     practice.
3. **Transmit to chambers for signature** — submit the proposed order
   (commonly an editable copy plus a PDF) to the judge's office per the
   division's preferred method (e-filing the proposed order under
   Administrative Order No. 21, a chambers email, or however that judge
   directs). Include a brief **transmittal letter** identifying the case,
   the hearing date, the motion ruled on, and noting the approval-as-to-
   form status.
4. **Confirm entry of record.** After the judge signs, confirm the
   clerk **enters** the order. Entry is what starts the relevant clocks.

## Chambers transmittal — template

```
   Re: [Case caption], Case No. ____, [County] County Circuit Court

   Dear Judge [Name] / [Chambers]:

   Enclosed is a proposed [title of order] reflecting the Court's
   ruling at the hearing on [date] on [moving party]'s [motion].
   [Opposing party] has approved the proposed order as to form / has
   been circulated the proposed order and has not stated an objection
   as of [date] / objects and has submitted a competing order.

   An editable version and a PDF are submitted [via eFlex / by email]
   for the Court's consideration.

   [Signature block — Ark. Bar No. ##### for an attorney; "Pro Se,"
   no bar number, for a self-represented filer]
```

Adapt the approval clause to the actual status, and the transmittal
method to the judge's directed practice.

## Checklist

- [ ] Proposed order tracks **exactly** what the court ruled (verified
      against the motion and the ruling — see `ar-fact-check`).
- [ ] Correct **caption** and a clean **signature/entry line** for the
      judge.
- [ ] **Circulated** for "Approved as to form" and the status reflected
      in the transmittal.
- [ ] Submitted per the **division's preferred method** (eFlex /
      chambers email) with a transmittal letter.
- [ ] **Redaction** check if the order contains sensitive data
      (Administrative Order No. 19) — see `ar-file-packet`.
- [ ] **Entry** confirmed with the clerk after signature; deadlines
      calendared from entry — see `ar-deadlines`.
