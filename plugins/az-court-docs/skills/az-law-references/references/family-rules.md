# Arizona Rules of Family Law Procedure (ARFLP) — map

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Navigational map only. Read the verbatim rule in
> `court-rules/` and confirm it is current before relying on it.

> **A SEPARATE RULE SET.** The ARFLP — **not** the Arizona Rules of Civil
> Procedure — govern **Title 25 family-law cases** in the Superior Court:
> dissolution of marriage (divorce), legal separation, annulment,
> paternity, legal decision-making (custody) and parenting time, child
> support, and spousal maintenance. The ARFLP have their **own numbering**
> and their **own disclosure rule (ARFLP Rule 49)** distinct from civil
> Rule 26.1. **Do not cite an Ariz. R. Civ. P. number in a family matter
> or an ARFLP number in a general civil matter.** Where the ARFLP are
> silent, they sometimes incorporate a civil rule by reference — confirm
> in the specific ARFLP provision.

## Why this matters for the plugin

Arizona is one of the states that maintains an entirely **separate
procedural code for family cases**. A pro se filer drafting a dissolution
petition, a response, a parenting plan, or a child-support worksheet is in
ARFLP territory, not Ariz. R. Civ. P. territory. The pleading mechanics,
disclosure obligations, conference structure, and ADR steps differ. Flag
this distinction prominently whenever a matter touches Title 25.

## Commencing and responding

- **The petition** — a family action is commenced by a **petition**
  (e.g., Petition for Dissolution of Marriage), not a "complaint." The
  ARFLP set the petition's required contents and the accompanying
  documents (summons, preliminary injunction, family cover sheet, and —
  where children are involved — child-support and parenting-related
  filings). Confirm the current required-document list.
- **The response** — the opposing party files a **response** within the
  time set by the ARFLP after service (confirm the current day count).
- **The preliminary injunction** — Arizona issues an automatic
  **preliminary injunction** at filing that restrains both parties from
  dissipating assets, removing children from the state, and altering
  insurance, among other things. Confirm scope and timing.

## Disclosure — ARFLP Rule 49

ARFLP **Rule 49** is the family-law disclosure rule (the family analog to
civil Rule 26.1). It requires the parties to disclose financial
information and documents — typically including the **Affidavit of
Financial Information (AFI)**, income and asset documentation, and the
factual and legal bases relevant to the contested issues — on the ARFLP
timetable. Confirm the current categories and deadlines in the verbatim
rule; disclosure obligations in family cases are heavily document-driven.

## Temporary orders

Either party may seek **temporary orders** (temporary parenting time,
legal decision-making, child support, spousal maintenance, use of
property, exclusive use of the residence) while the case is pending,
typically on a noticed motion or expedited procedure under the ARFLP.
Confirm the current temporary-orders procedure and any required financial
filings.

## Conferences and case management

- **Resolution Management Conference (RMC)** — an early conference at
  which the court identifies the contested issues and sets a case-
  management path. Confirm the current trigger and required pre-conference
  statements.
- **Parenting conferences / parenting evaluations** — where legal
  decision-making and parenting time are contested, the court may order a
  **parenting conference**, a **comprehensive family evaluation**, or
  appointment of a professional to assist the court. Confirm the current
  options and which county programs apply.

## ADR — mediation and alternative processes

The ARFLP and county programs provide for **mediation and other ADR** of
parenting and (sometimes) financial disputes. Many counties run a
court-connected mediation program for legal decision-making and parenting
time. Confirm what ADR is mandatory vs. available in the filing county.

## Substantive Title 25 anchors (statutory, not procedural)

The **substantive** family law lives in **A.R.S. Title 25**, not in the
ARFLP. The ARFLP is the *procedure*; Title 25 is the *substance*. Anchors
to confirm in `az-statutes-debt/`:

- **A.R.S. § 25-211** — property acquired during marriage is **community
  property** (Arizona is a community-property state), subject to the
  statutory exceptions.
- **A.R.S. § 25-213** — separate property.
- **A.R.S. § 25-215** — liability of community and separate property for
  community and separate debts.
- **A.R.S. § 25-318** — **equitable division** of community property and
  debt on dissolution.
- Legal decision-making and parenting time, child support guidelines, and
  spousal maintenance provisions elsewhere in Title 25 — confirm current
  sections and the current Child Support Guidelines.

precedents (and verify each citation before relying on it).
