# Arizona Rules of Civil Procedure (Ariz. R. Civ. P.) — civil-practice map

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a navigational map of the Arizona Rules of
> Civil Procedure. Read the verbatim rule in `court-rules/` and confirm it
> is current before relying on any number, day count, or threshold.

The Ariz. R. Civ. P. govern civil practice in the **Superior Court** and,
by adoption / parallel rules, much practice in the limited-jurisdiction
courts. **Title 25 family-law cases are NOT governed by these rules** —
they are governed by the Arizona Rules of Family Law Procedure (ARFLP);
see `family-rules.md`.

## Pleadings and motions — Rules 7-15

- **Rule 7** — pleadings allowed and the form of motions.
- **Rule 7.1** — motion practice mechanics (where local rules and this
  rule set briefing and response/reply timing — confirm current day
  counts).
- **Rule 8** — general rules of pleading; claims for relief, the demand
  for judgment, and pleading the **amount-in-controversy tier** (Rule 8
  ties into the Rule 26.2 tier-certification requirement).
- **Rule 9** — pleading special matters (fraud and mistake with
  particularity, etc.).
- **Rule 10** — form of pleadings: caption, numbered paragraphs, exhibits.
- **Rule 11** — signing of pleadings; the certification and sanctions
  provision.
- **Rule 12** — defenses and objections. **Rule 12(b)(6)** is the motion
  to **dismiss for failure to state a claim upon which relief can be
  granted**; the other 12(b) grounds cover jurisdiction, venue, process,
  and service. Note the rule on converting a 12(b)(6) motion to a Rule 56
  motion when matters outside the pleadings are presented.
- **Rule 13** — counterclaims and cross-claims (compulsory vs.
  permissive).
- **Rule 14** — third-party practice.
- **Rule 15** — amended and supplemental pleadings; amendment as of right,
  by leave (freely given when justice requires), and relation back.

## Disclosure and discovery

### Rule 26.1 — mandatory initial disclosure (distinctive)

Arizona requires **broad, automatic disclosure** early in the case —
**without waiting for a discovery request**. The disclosure obligation
typically reaches:

- the factual basis of each claim and defense;
- the legal theory on which each claim or defense rests;
- the names and information of witnesses (fact and expert);
- the documents and tangible things a party may use; and
- a computation of damages.

Rule 26.1 imposes a **continuing duty to supplement**. Failure to
disclose can trigger preclusion of the undisclosed witness, document, or
theory. This front-loaded disclosure regime is a defining feature of
Arizona civil practice — confirm the current scope and deadlines in the
verbatim rule.

### Rule 26.2 — the tiered-case discovery system (distinctive)

Civil cases are assigned to one of **three tiers**, driven primarily by
the **amount in controversy**, with each tier setting its own discovery
limits and discovery period. The tiers (verify current thresholds and
limits in `court-rules/`):

- **Tier 1** — lowest amount-in-controversy band; least discovery
  (shortest discovery period; lowest caps on deposition hours,
  interrogatories, requests for production, and requests for admission).
- **Tier 2** — middle band; intermediate discovery allowance.
- **Tier 3** — highest band and factually/legally complex cases; the most
  discovery (longest period and highest caps).

Tier assignment can be **adjusted by stipulation or court order** for
proportionality — the amount in controversy is the default sorter, not a
hard cap on what the case actually needs. A party generally must
**certify the tier** in an early filing. **The tier's discovery caps
control over the general per-device limits** in Rules 30/33/34/36; when
the numbers differ, the tier limit governs — confirm.

### Discovery devices

- **Rule 30** — depositions upon oral examination (deposition hours are
  capped by the Rule 26.2 tier).
- **Rule 31** — depositions upon written questions.
- **Rule 33** — **interrogatories.** General ceiling of **40
  interrogatories** (including discrete subparts) absent stipulation or
  court order — but the **Rule 26.2 tier limit is the operative cap** in a
  tiered case; confirm which controls.
- **Rule 34** — requests for production of documents and things; entry on
  land.
- **Rule 35** — physical and mental examinations.
- **Rule 36** — requests for admission; matters admitted are conclusively
  established unless withdrawn or amended.
- **Rule 37** — sanctions and motions to compel for discovery failures
  (including failure to make Rule 26.1 disclosure).

## Dispositive motions

- **Rule 56 — summary judgment.** Arizona's analog to FRCP 56: judgment is
  proper when there is **no genuine dispute as to any material fact** and
  the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law. The governing
  standard was articulated in *Orme School v. Reeves*, 166 Ariz. 301, 802
  P.2d 1000 (1990) — see `key-cases.md`. Confirm the current
  response/reply timing and the statement-of-facts requirement in the
  verbatim rule and any local rule.

## Default and judgments

- **Rule 55 — default.** Two steps: **entry of default** (Rule 55(a),
  with the grace period that runs before default becomes effective) and
  **default judgment** (Rule 55(b), by the clerk or by the court).
  Confirm the current waiting period and any application/hearing
  requirement.
- **Rule 54 — judgments; costs.** Includes the **Rule 54(b)**
  certification for final judgment on fewer than all claims/parties and
  the **Rule 54(c)** finality language.
- **Rule 58 — entry of judgment.** Form and entry; a judgment is generally
  effective on entry.

## Post-judgment / relief

- **Rule 59 — new trial; altering or amending a judgment.** Grounds and
  the timing for the motion (confirm the current day count).
- **Rule 60 — relief from a judgment or order.** Mistake, inadvertence,
  excusable neglect, newly discovered evidence, fraud, void judgment, or
  any other reason justifying relief; confirm the time limits, which
  differ by ground.

## Compulsory arbitration — Rules 72-77

Civil cases at or under the **county's arbitration jurisdictional limit**
are routed to **mandatory, non-binding arbitration**:

- **Rule 72** — scope and the certification of whether the case is subject
  to compulsory arbitration; the **dollar limit is set by each county by
  local rule** — confirm the current figure for the filing county.
- **Rules 73-76** — appointment of the arbitrator, hearing procedure, and
  the arbitrator's award/decision.
- **Rule 77** — **appeal from the arbitration award for a trial de novo**
  in the Superior Court, with the cost/fee consequences that attach if the
  appealing party fails to improve its position. Confirm the current
  appeal deadline and the sanction provision.
