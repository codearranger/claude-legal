# Idaho Debtor Exemptions (I.C. §§ 11-605, 55-1003)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Exemption dollar amounts are set and periodically
> adjusted by statute — **do not state a figure from memory.** Look up the
> current amount in the verbatim corpus and verify before asserting it.

A judgment debtor can shield property from execution and garnishment
through statutory **exemptions**. This file maps the categories; the
**dollar amounts live in the corpus**, not here.

---

## Where the dollar amounts live — read these

- **Personal property — Idaho Code § 11-605.** Household furnishings and
  appliances, wearing apparel, **tools of the trade**, a **motor-vehicle**
  value, jewelry, a "wildcard" amount, and prescribed benefits — each with
  a **statutory dollar cap**. Read the current caps in
  `../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/Title11-exemptions-garnishment.md`.
  **Verify the current figure** — these are amended over time.
- **Homestead — Idaho Code § 55-1003.** Protects equity in the debtor's
  **residence** up to a statutory amount, often automatic on the
  homeowner's dwelling. Read the current amount in
  `../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/homestead-Title55.md`.
  **Verify the current figure** before relying on it.
- **Earnings (wage exemption) — Idaho Code § 11-207.** The lesser of 25%
  of disposable weekly earnings or the excess over 30× the federal minimum
  wage; see `garnishment.md` and the same Title 11 corpus file.

> ⚠ **Drift warning.** The § 55-1003 homestead figure and the § 11-605
> personal-property caps change. Always pull the current number from the
> corpus above; never embed a remembered dollar amount in a filing.

---

## Categorically protected funds (federal + Idaho)

- **Social Security** benefits — **42 U.S.C. § 407** (protected even when
  deposited, subject to tracing rules).
- Certain **public assistance**, unemployment, and workers' compensation
  benefits.
- Many **retirement accounts** and pension benefits (verify the specific
  account type and any cap in the corpus).

---

## Claiming an exemption

To protect property from a levy or garnishment, the debtor files a written
**claim of exemption** with the **issuing court** within the period the
garnishment or levy papers specify; the court then resolves the claim
(often on a short hearing). **Act promptly** — the window is short. Compute
it with `id-deadlines`.

A claim of exemption typically pairs with a supporting **affidavit or
declaration** identifying the property and the exemption claimed — draft
with `id-draft-declaration`.
