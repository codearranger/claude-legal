# Idaho Wage and Continuing Garnishment (I.C. § 11-207; Title 11, ch. 7)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Garnishment caps and objection windows are
> date- and dollar-critical; verify against current Idaho law before
> relying on them.

Idaho garnishment reaches a debtor's **earnings** (wages) and, via a
**continuing** garnishment, successive pay periods. Pull verbatim statute
text from
`../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/Title11-exemptions-garnishment.md`;
pull rule text from
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## Earnings exemption — I.C. § 11-207

> **The garnishable amount is capped.** Under **Idaho Code § 11-207**, the
> amount of a debtor's aggregate **disposable** weekly earnings subject to
> garnishment is limited to the **lesser of**:
>
> - **25% of disposable weekly earnings**, **or**
> - the amount by which disposable weekly earnings **exceed 30 times the
>   federal minimum hourly wage**.

This tracks the federal Consumer Credit Protection Act floor
(15 U.S.C. §§ 1671–1677). **Higher caps apply to support orders** (child
support / spousal support garnishments reach a larger share). "Disposable
earnings" are gross earnings less amounts required by law to be withheld.
Confirm the current computation, the support-order caps, and the
definition of disposable earnings in the corpus.

---

## Continuing wage garnishment — I.C. Title 11, ch. 7

Idaho provides a **continuing** garnishment that captures earnings over
**multiple successive pay periods** from a single garnishment, rather than
requiring a fresh writ each pay period:

- **Continuing cap — § 11-712.** The continuing garnishment operates
  subject to the statutory cap of **§ 11-712**; verify the current cap and
  duration in the corpus.
- **Sheriff's returns / accounting — § 11-705.** Service mechanics, the
  garnishee's obligations, and the sheriff's **returns** are governed by
  **§ 11-705**.
- The per-period withholding still cannot exceed the **§ 11-207** earnings
  exemption (above).

---

## What a debtor can do

- **Verify the withholding** is within the § 11-207 cap (lesser of 25% of
  disposable earnings or the excess over 30× the federal minimum wage). An
  over-withholding is a basis to object.
- **Assert exemptions** on garnished **bank accounts** — e.g., **Social
  Security** (42 U.S.C. § 407), certain public benefits, and the
  personal-property exemptions of **§ 11-605** (see `exemptions.md`).
- **File a claim of exemption** with the issuing court within the period
  the garnishment papers specify — act promptly; see `id-deadlines`.
