# Idaho Post-Judgment Motions (I.R.C.P. 59, 11.2, 60(b))

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** The 14-day windows are short and some are not
> extendable; verify every interval against current Idaho law and compute
> with `id-deadlines` before relying on a date.

Four routes attack an Idaho judgment after entry. Pull verbatim rule text
from
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

---

## New trial / alter or amend — I.R.C.P. 59

- **New trial — Rule 59(a).** Grounds include irregularity in the
  proceedings, jury or adverse-party misconduct, accident or surprise,
  newly discovered material evidence, **excessive or inadequate damages**,
  insufficiency of the evidence / verdict against the weight of the
  evidence, and error in law.
- **Alter or amend the judgment — Rule 59(e).**

> ⚠ **14 days — not extendable.** A Rule 59 motion for a new trial
> (**Rule 59(b)**) or to alter or amend (**Rule 59(e)**) must be filed
> **no later than 14 days after entry of judgment**, and that window is
> **not extendable**. Calendar it the moment judgment enters.

---

## Reconsideration — I.R.C.P. 11.2

Idaho provides a distinct **motion for reconsideration**:

- **Of a final judgment** — must be filed **within 14 days after entry of
  the final judgment** (**Rule 11.2(b)**).
- **Of an interlocutory order** — may be made at any time before entry of
  final judgment.

Confirm the current intervals and scope in the corpus.

---

## Relief from judgment — I.R.C.P. 60(b)

Grounds:

1. mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or **excusable neglect**;
2. newly discovered evidence;
3. fraud, misrepresentation, or misconduct of an opposing party;
4. the judgment is **void** (e.g., defective service);
5. the judgment is satisfied/released or no longer equitable to apply
   prospectively;
6. any other reason justifying relief.

> **Timing.** A Rule 60(b) motion must be made within a **reasonable
> time**, and for grounds **(1)–(3)** no later than the **outer limit**
> the rule sets (a fixed number of months after entry — confirm under
> **I.R.C.P. 60(c)** in the corpus). A **void-judgment** attack under
> (b)(4) is not bound by that same fixed window.

---

## Setting aside a default — I.R.C.P. 55

- **Rule 55(c)** — set aside an **entry of default** for **good cause**.
- A **default *judgment*** is set aside under **Rule 60(b)**; the movant
  generally must show a ground plus a **meritorious defense**, except a
  void-for-defective-service attack (Rule 60(b)(4)), which does not need
  the meritorious-defense showing. See `id-first-30-days`
  (`default-and-set-aside.md`) and `id-consumer-debt`.

---

## Costs and fees after judgment

- **Memorandum of costs — 14 days.** The prevailing party files a
  memorandum of costs **within 14 days** after entry of judgment
  (**I.R.C.P. 54(d)**).
- **Attorney fees** — claimed under **Idaho Code § 12-120** (certain
  contract / commercial claims) or **§ 12-121** (frivolous actions), per
  the rule's procedure
  (`../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/attorney-fees-costs-Title12.md`).
