# Idaho Execution and Judgment Liens (I.C. §§ 10-1110, 10-1111, 5-215)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Lien terms, renewal mechanics, and the judgment
> SOL are date-critical; verify against current Idaho law and compute
> with `id-deadlines` before relying on a date.

A money judgment is enforced by a **writ of execution** and protected by a
**judgment lien** on the debtor's real property. Both operate only while
the judgment remains enforceable — and a judgment has a finite life. Pull
verbatim statute text from
`../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/Title5-limitations.md`
(SOL) and the Idaho Code lien sections; pull rule text from
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

---

## Writ of execution

A **writ of execution** directs the sheriff to **levy on and sell the
debtor's non-exempt property** to satisfy the judgment. The writ must
issue while the judgment is still enforceable (see the SOL below), and the
levy reaches only property not protected by an exemption — see
`exemptions.md` and `garnishment.md`. Confirm the current writ procedure
and the sheriff's duties in the corpus.

---

## Judgment liens on real property — I.C. §§ 10-1110, 10-1111

- **Creating the lien — § 10-1110.** Recording the judgment (or a
  transcript / abstract) with the **county recorder** creates a
  **judgment lien on the debtor's real property** in that county. The lien
  has a statutory life — verify the current term in the corpus.
- **Renewal — § 10-1111.** The lien must be **renewed** to remain
  effective. On a timely renewal, a renewed lien runs for a fresh
  statutory period (the lien runs **10 years from the renewal order** —
  confirm the current figure). **The renewal order also restarts the
  § 5-215 action-on-a-judgment clock.** Verify the renewal requirements,
  timing, and effect in the corpus before relying on them.

---

## The judgment's own statute of limitations — I.C. § 5-215

> ⚠ **11 years.** An **action upon a judgment** must be brought within
> **11 years** under **Idaho Code § 5-215**.

A creditor must keep the lien renewed (§ 10-1111) and bring any action on
the judgment within the **11-year** period. A debtor facing collection on
an **old** judgment should check whether the lien lapsed (no timely
renewal) or whether the 11-year period has run. A § 10-1111 renewal order
restarts the § 5-215 clock, so confirm the renewal history. Compute with
`id-deadlines`.

---

## Debtor's examination / supplemental proceedings

To locate assets, the creditor may compel the judgment debtor (or a third
party) to appear and answer under oath about income, property, and
accounts. **Failure to appear after proper service can expose the debtor
to contempt.** A debtor who appears can still assert exemptions
(`exemptions.md`). Confirm the current procedure and notice requirement in
the corpus.
