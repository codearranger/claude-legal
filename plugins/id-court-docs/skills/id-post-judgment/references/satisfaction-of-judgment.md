# Idaho Satisfaction of Judgment

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the satisfaction procedure and any
> statutory duty or penalty against current Idaho law before relying on
> it.

When a judgment is **paid in full** (or settled), the **satisfaction**
should be entered of record so the judgment no longer encumbers the
debtor's property or credit. Pull verbatim statute text from
`../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/Title5-limitations.md`
(judgment SOL context) and the Idaho Code judgment sections; pull rule
text from
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

---

## How a judgment is satisfied of record

- **Creditor files a satisfaction.** After payment, the **judgment
  creditor** ordinarily files a **Satisfaction of Judgment** with the
  court and, **where a judgment lien was recorded** with the county
  recorder (see `execution-and-liens.md`, § 10-1110), **records a
  satisfaction** to release the lien on the debtor's real property.
- **Partial satisfaction.** A payment of less than the full amount may be
  acknowledged as a **partial satisfaction**, reducing the balance.

---

## When the creditor will not acknowledge payment

If the creditor **refuses or fails** to enter satisfaction after the
judgment is paid, the **debtor may move the court** to enter satisfaction,
supported by **proof of payment** (receipts, canceled checks, payoff
confirmation) via an **affidavit or declaration**. Verify any **statutory
duty** of the creditor to acknowledge satisfaction after demand — and any
**penalty** for failure to do so — in the corpus before invoking it.

---

## Why entering satisfaction matters to the debtor

- **Releases the judgment lien** so it stops clouding title to real
  property (otherwise the lien persists until its term lapses or is
  renewed under § 10-1111).
- **Stops further execution / garnishment** on a paid judgment.
- **Closes the case** of record and supports correcting the debtor's
  credit reporting (cross-reference the shared federal FCRA self-help
  layer for dispute steps).
