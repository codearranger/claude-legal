# Transmittal Cover + Service Plan

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** A transmittal aid. The proposed form must reflect
> the court's actual ruling, and **only a judge** signs it into effect.
> Confirm the assigned judge's submission practice before transmitting.

When the court directs a party to prepare a proposed order or judgment,
transmit the proposed form to the court with a short cover and serve a
copy on every party under **I.R.C.P. 5**. Pull the controlling rule text
from
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## Transmittal cover

```
[Date]

Clerk of the District Court / Hon. [Judge]
[Nth Judicial District], [County] County
[Courthouse address]
[Or: submitted via iCourt as a proposed order per local practice]

RE:  [Case Short Title], Case No. [Number]
     Proposed [Order / Judgment] re: [matter heard]

To the Court:

Submitted herewith is a proposed [Order / Judgment] reflecting the
Court's ruling on [date] re: [matter]. A copy was served on all
parties on [date] under I.R.C.P. 5.

[If a separate-document Judgment:] The proposed Judgment is submitted
as a separate document under I.R.C.P. 54(a).

[If a partial judgment:] The form requests an I.R.C.P. 54(b)
certificate so the judgment may be final and appealable.

Respectfully submitted,

[Name]  [ISB No. ___ if counsel / "Self-Represented" if pro se]
[Address / Phone / Email]

cc: [Other parties / counsel]
```

## Submission channel

Confirm the deciding judge's submission practice. Some judges want the
proposed form in an **editable format** or transmitted **through iCourt
as a proposed order** rather than a paper cover. See `id-file-packet`
for the iCourt / Odyssey File & Serve channel.

## Service plan (the deliverable)

```
PROPOSED ORDER / JUDGMENT — SERVICE PLAN

Case:           [Short title], Case No. [Number]
Court:          [Nth Judicial District], [County] County
                [District Court / Magistrate Division], [judge]
Proposed form:  [Title — Order re: ___ / Judgment]

1. Date proposed form SUBMITTED to the court:    [DATE]
2. Date proposed form SERVED on all parties:     [DATE]
3. Method of service (I.R.C.P. 5):               [iCourt e-service /
                                                  mail / ...]
4. Service add-on, if mailed (+3 days):          [+3 days]
5. Parties served (name + address / e-service):
   - [Party] — [how served]
   - [Party] — [how served]
6. On entry: confirm the DATE OF ENTRY with the clerk —
   deadlines run from entry, not from the hearing.
```

Compute any objection or deadline window with `id-deadlines` (I.R.C.P.
2.2 time computation + the +3-day mail add-on).

## "Approved as to form" signatures

A party (or counsel) may sign below the judge's line **"approved as to
form"** — meaning the form **accurately states** the ruling, **not** that
the party agrees with the result. "Approved as to form and content" /
"stipulated" means the parties also agree to the substance. Only the
**judge** signs the order or judgment into effect.
