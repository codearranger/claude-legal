## The separate-document rule — I.R.C.P. 54(a)

A **Judgment** that finally resolves the matter must be a **separate
document** that states **only the relief** granted — no recitals, no
reasoning, no findings. Keep the two document types distinct:

- An **Order** disposes of a motion and may state the court's findings
  and reasoning.
- A **Judgment** under Rule 54(a) is a separate document stating only
  relief. The appeal and post-judgment clocks run from the entry of that
  separate-document judgment.

A judgment whose decretal language is buried in an order's reasoning does
not satisfy Rule 54(a) and can leave the appeal period unstarted.

## Entry — I.R.C.P. 58

Under **I.R.C.P. 58**, a judgment is **entered** when the court signs it
and the clerk files it in the record. The ordinary path:

1. **Prepare** the proposed order/judgment reflecting the ruling
   (`id-draft-order`).
2. **Submit** it to the court and **serve** a copy on every party under
   **I.R.C.P. 5** with a certificate of service.
3. **The court signs** (resolving any wording dispute first), and **the
   clerk enters** it.

A judgment is generally **effective when entered**. Track the **entry
date**, not the hearing date — post-judgment motions under **I.R.C.P. 59
/ 60** and the appeal period under the **Idaho Appellate Rules (I.A.R.)**
run from **entry**. Confirm the current entry mechanics and any local
objection window in `id-law-references`.

## Rule 54(b) certificate — partial finality

When the ruling disposes of **fewer than all claims, or the rights of
fewer than all parties**, the judgment is **not final or appealable**
unless the court enters an **I.R.C.P. 54(b) certificate** — an express
determination that there is no just reason for delay and an express
direction for entry of a final judgment.

- If the ruling **fully** resolves the case, no 54(b) certificate is
  needed — the separate-document Judgment under Rule 54(a) is final.
- If you intend a 54(b) judgment, the proposed form must **request and
  recite** the certificate; the **court** — not the drafter — decides
  whether to make it.

Confirm the exact 54(b) certificate language against `id-law-references`
before submitting.

## After entry

1. **Obtain the entered order/judgment** and confirm the **date of
   entry** with the clerk — deadlines run from entry.
2. **Verify the entered form matches** what was submitted (the court
   sometimes interlineates changes).
3. **Calendar deadlines that run from entry** (I.R.C.P. 59 / 60 motions;
   the appeal period) — see `id-deadlines`.
4. **Confirm service** of the entered order/judgment on all parties.

## Who signs

Only the **judge** signs the order or judgment into effect (District
Judge / Magistrate Judge). Parties and counsel sign only the **approval
/ stipulation** block; a party's signature does not enter the judgment.
Self-represented parties **omit the bar number** and identify as
"Self-Represented [Party]"; Idaho-licensed counsel use the **Idaho State
Bar number**.
