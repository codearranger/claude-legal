# Idaho Affirmative-Defense Catalog (I.R.C.P. 8(c))

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** A checklist for issue-spotting; confirm each
> defense and its elements against current Idaho law before pleading.

**I.R.C.P. 8(c)** requires a party to **affirmatively state** any
avoidance or affirmative defense in the responsive pleading. A defense
that is not pleaded in the answer can be **waived**. Plead **every**
defense that may apply; a defense can always be abandoned later, but one
omitted is hard to revive. Verify Rule 8(c) and the waiver consequences
in `../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

---

## Frequently load-bearing in collection and contract cases

- **Statute of limitations.** Often dispositive on stale debt. Written
  contract **5 years** (Idaho Code § 5-216); oral contract / open account
  **4 years** (Idaho Code § 5-217). Confirm the period and accrual date
  against `../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/Title5-limitations.md`
  and compute with `id-deadlines`. (An **action on a judgment** runs
  **11 years**, § 5-215 — relevant when the suit is on a prior judgment.)

- **Lack of standing / chain of title.** Common in debt-buyer suits:
  plaintiff must prove it owns the debt through an unbroken assignment
  chain. Put the plaintiff to its proof; see `id-consumer-debt`.

- **Payment.** The obligation was paid, in whole or part.

- **Accord and satisfaction / release.** The parties settled or
  discharged the claim by agreement.

- **Discharge in bankruptcy.** The debt was discharged; pleading it
  preserves the bar (see the shared federal bankruptcy corpus).

- **Statute of frauds.** A contract within the statute is unenforceable
  if not in a signed writing.

- **Failure of consideration / want of consideration.**

---

## Equitable and preclusion defenses

- **Estoppel** (equitable / promissory).
- **Waiver** — plaintiff relinquished a known right.
- **Laches** — unreasonable delay causing prejudice (equitable claims).
- **Res judicata (claim preclusion) / collateral estoppel (issue
  preclusion).** A prior judgment bars the claim or issue.

---

## Conduct- and capacity-based defenses

- **Fraud** in the inducement or execution.
- **Illegality / unconscionability** of the underlying transaction
  (e.g., usurious or unlicensed-lender claims — see
  `../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/consumer-protection-collection.md`).
- **Duress / undue influence.**
- **Failure to mitigate damages.**
- **Comparative negligence** (tort matters).
- **Accord, satisfaction, and novation.**

---

## Rule-12 grounds preserved in the answer

- **Failure to state a claim** (I.R.C.P. 12(b)(6)) — may be raised in the
  answer as a defense.
- **Lack of personal jurisdiction, improper venue, insufficient process,
  insufficient service of process** (12(b)(2)–(5)) — these are
  **waivable** if not raised in the first Rule 12 motion or the answer.
  See `motion-to-dismiss-grounds.md`.

---

## Drafting note

State each defense as a separately numbered/labeled paragraph in the
**AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES** section of the answer (see `answer-template.md`).
A bare label generally suffices to preserve the defense, but supporting
facts strengthen it. Reserve the right to assert further defenses as
discovery develops. Claim costs and fees under **Idaho Code § 12-120 /
§ 12-121** where applicable
(`../../id-law-references/references/id-statutes-debt/attorney-fees-costs-Title12.md`).
