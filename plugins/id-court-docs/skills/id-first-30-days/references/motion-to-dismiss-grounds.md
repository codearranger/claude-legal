# Idaho Pre-Answer Motion to Dismiss — I.R.C.P. 12(b)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Confirm every ground, the waiver rule, and the
> Rule 56 conversion mechanics against current Idaho law before filing.

A defendant may move to dismiss under **I.R.C.P. 12(b)** *before* or
*with* the answer, in lieu of answering. A timely pre-answer Rule 12(b)
motion **alters the time to file a responsive pleading** (the answer is
not due on the original 21-day clock while the motion is pending — verify
the new period under Rule 12(a) and compute with `id-deadlines`). Pull
verbatim text from
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

---

## The seven grounds — I.R.C.P. 12(b)

| Subrule | Ground | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **(b)(1)** | Lack of **subject-matter jurisdiction** | Never waived; can be raised any time. |
| **(b)(2)** | Lack of **personal jurisdiction** | **Waivable** — raise first. |
| **(b)(3)** | **Improper venue** | **Waivable** — raise first. |
| **(b)(4)** | **Insufficient process** | **Waivable** — raise first. |
| **(b)(5)** | **Insufficient service of process** | **Waivable** — raise first; check the I.R.C.P. 4 service rules and the 182-day filing/service window (Rule 4(b)(2)). |
| **(b)(6)** | **Failure to state a claim** upon which relief can be granted | Tested on the pleadings; convertible to Rule 56 (below). |
| **(b)(7)** | **Failure to join a party** under Rule 19 | Required/indispensable parties. |

---

## Waiver — raise the threatened defenses first

The defenses in **(b)(2)–(5)** — personal jurisdiction, venue, process,
and service — are **waived** if not raised in the first Rule 12 motion or
in the answer. Do not file a Rule 12(b) motion on some grounds while
silently omitting an available (b)(2)–(5) defense; consolidate them.
Subject-matter jurisdiction (b)(1) is never waived. Confirm the
consolidation and waiver provisions (the Idaho analogue to Rule 12(g)/(h))
in the corpus.

---

## Rule 12(b)(6) — tested on the pleadings; conversion to Rule 56

A **12(b)(6)** motion challenges the **legal sufficiency** of the
complaint on the **pleadings alone**, accepting the well-pleaded factual
allegations as true and drawing reasonable inferences for the
non-movant.

> ⚠ **Conversion.** If matters **outside the pleadings** are presented to
> and not excluded by the court, a 12(b)(6) motion is **treated as one
> for summary judgment under I.R.C.P. 56**, and all parties must be given
> a reasonable opportunity to present Rule 56 material. **Do not attach
> evidence (affidavits/declarations, account records) to a 12(b)(6)
> motion unless you intend conversion.** When a fact dispute is the real
> issue, move under **Rule 56** instead and follow its briefing timeline
> (developed in `id-draft-motion` and `id-deadlines`).

---

## Related Rule 12 motions

- **Rule 12(e) — more definite statement.** For a pleading so vague the
  defendant cannot reasonably respond; disfavored and narrow.
- **Rule 12(f) — motion to strike** redundant, immaterial, impertinent,
  or scandalous matter.

## Drafting and noticing

Caption and format per `id-statewide-format`; draft the motion and any
supporting memorandum with `id-draft-motion`; notice it for hearing with
`id-schedule-hearing` and `id-hearings`. Sign under **I.R.C.P. 11**. If
the motion is **denied**, the answer comes due on the period Rule 12(a)
sets after the ruling — calendar it immediately with `id-deadlines` to
avoid default (`default-and-set-aside.md`).
