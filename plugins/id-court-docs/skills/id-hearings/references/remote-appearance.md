# Remote / Telephonic Appearance

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Remote-appearance practice is set by the court
> and the assigned judge and changes frequently. Confirm the current
> instructions for your hearing before relying on anything here.

Idaho courts conduct many proceedings by **video (Zoom) or telephone**
at the court's discretion — status conferences, oral argument, and some
evidentiary hearings. Whether a given hearing is in person, by video, or
by telephone is **set by the court's current practice and the assigned
judge** (District Court or Magistrate Division).

## Confirm — do not assume

> Do **not** assume a platform or a meeting ID. Pull the link / dial-in
> from the court's website or the most recent notice for **your**
> hearing. Meeting IDs change — never cache or guess them. If the notice
> does not state the format, contact the assigned judge's clerk.

## Remote etiquette

- **Join early** (10-15 minutes) using the court's published link or
  number.
- Use your **full legal name** as the display name.
- **Mute** on entry; unmute only when addressed by the court.
- **Camera on** for video hearings unless the court directs otherwise.
- **Quiet, professional background**; test audio and video beforehand.
- **Stable connection** — wired or strong Wi-Fi; have the dial-in number
  as a backup if video drops.
- Address the court as **"Your Honor"**; identify yourself before
  speaking ("[Name] for the [Plaintiff / Defendant]").
- **Do not record** the proceeding unless the court expressly permits.

## Remote evidentiary hearings

For a remote evidentiary hearing, additional logistics matter:

- **Exhibits** must reach the court and the witnesses in advance —
  confirm how the court wants pre-marked exhibits delivered.
- **Witness administration** — confirm how the oath is administered
  remotely and how witnesses are kept separated (I.R.E. 615 exclusion).
- **The record** — confirm with the clerk how the remote proceeding is
  recorded and how the record is preserved.
