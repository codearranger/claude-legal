# Hearing-Day Checklist

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** A preparation aid. Confirm courthouse logistics,
> the assigned judge's procedures, and any courtesy-copy requirement
> with the court before the hearing.

## Before you leave

| Check | Done |
|-------|------|
| Hearing format confirmed (in person / Zoom / telephone) and link or dial-in saved | ☐ |
| Courthouse address, courtroom, and assigned judge confirmed | ☐ |
| Arrival time set — **30 min early** in person (security), **10-15 min** early remote | ☐ |
| `id-fact-check` / `id-quality-check` complete on every paper to be argued | ☐ |

## What to bring (in person)

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| 2 paper copies of each filing (court + opposing party) | Hard copies at the podium even in e-filed (iCourt / Odyssey) cases |
| Judge's courtesy copy, if local rule / procedures require one | Some courts want a courtesy copy in advance |
| Printed, tabbed I.R.C.P. / I.R.E. excerpts | Governing rule text at hand — pull from `id-law-references` |
| Key cases printed (note Court of Appeals "(Ct. App. YEAR)" cites) | Flag the dispositive authorities |
| Exhibit list + a copy of each exhibit (original + court + opposing party) | Pre-marked per the court's numbering convention |
| Witness outlines (evidentiary hearing) | One sequential exam outline per witness |
| Proposed order / form of judgment (1 copy + 1 spare) | The court may sign at the hearing; see `id-submit-order` |
| Notepad + 2 pens | Notes during the hearing |

## On arrival

- **Check in** with the clerk / bailiff; confirm the case is on the
  calendar and where to sit.
- **Silence** the phone and put it away.
- **Dress** in business attire; self-represented filers wear
  business-casual at minimum (no shorts, hats, or athletic wear).
- **Stand** when the court takes the bench and when addressing the
  court ("May it please the Court, Your Honor").

## During the hearing

- Address the judge as **"Your Honor"**; the opposing party as
  **"Mr./Ms. [surname]."**
- Follow the argument structure in
  `references/oral-argument-outline.md`.
- **Take notes** on the ruling — capture the exact relief granted; you
  may have to draft the order (`id-submit-order`).

## After the hearing

- If directed to prepare the order/judgment, note the wording precisely
  and proceed to `id-submit-order`.
- Calendar any deadline that runs from **entry** of the order/judgment
  (not the hearing date) with `id-deadlines`.
