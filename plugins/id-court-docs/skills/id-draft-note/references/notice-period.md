# Idaho Notice Period for a Motion Hearing

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This summarizes the notice-period rules. Confirm the
> current day counts in the corpus and compute the actual dates with
> `id-deadlines` before filing.

The notice period for an Idaho motion hearing is set by **I.R.C.P.
7(b)(3)**, with a longer track for summary judgment under **I.R.C.P. 56**.
Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## The ordinary 14-day rule — I.R.C.P. 7(b)(3)

The moving party serves and files the Notice of Hearing together with the
motion, and the **notice and motion must be served and filed at least 14
days before the hearing**. This is the default for most motions.

## The summary-judgment exception — I.R.C.P. 56

A motion for summary judgment runs on a longer track that displaces the
14-day rule:

| Step | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Motion + supporting brief and affidavits served | **at least 28 days before the hearing** |
| Answering brief + opposing affidavits served | **at least 14 days before the hearing** |
| Motion filed | **at least 90 days before the date set for trial** (unless the court orders otherwise) |

The Notice of Hearing for a Rule 56 motion must set the hearing date so the
28-day service window is satisfied.

## Computing the count — I.R.C.P. 2.2 + mail add-on

Do not count days by hand. Compute the actual hearing-date floor with
`id-deadlines`, which applies:

- **I.R.C.P. 2.2** time computation — **Rule 6 is reserved** and carries no
  time-computation text in Idaho. Exclude the triggering day, count every
  intervening day, and roll a deadline that lands on a Saturday, Sunday, or
  Idaho Code § 73-108 legal holiday forward to the next available day.
- the **+3 days for service by mail**, where service is by mail.

Because the 14-day (or 28-day) period runs **backward** from the hearing,
the practical effect is that the service/filing date must be far enough
ahead of the hearing that the full period — plus any mail add-on — fits.
Confirm the current figures in the corpus before relying on a date.
