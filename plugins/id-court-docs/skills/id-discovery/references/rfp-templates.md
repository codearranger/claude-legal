# Request-for-Production Templates — I.R.C.P. 34

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Drafting scaffolds only. Verify the current rule
> text in the corpus before serving.

Requests for production of documents, electronically stored information
(ESI), and tangible things — and requests for entry onto land for
inspection — are governed by **I.R.C.P. 34**.

- **Response window — 30 days** (I.R.C.P. 34(b)(2)).
- The responding party produces documents **as kept in the usual course of
  business** or organized to correspond to the categories of the request.
- **ESI** is produced in a reasonably usable form; address
  form-of-production and metadata by stipulation, consistent with the
  proportionality limits of **I.R.C.P. 26(b)(1)**.

Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.
Use the I.R.C.P. 2 caption (defer to `id-statewide-format`) and a
certificate of service.

## Preamble

```
[CAPTION — per id-statewide-format]

PLAINTIFF'S FIRST REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to I.R.C.P. 34, [Plaintiff] requests that [Defendant] produce the
documents and things described below for inspection and copying within 30
days of service, at [location / electronically]. These requests are
continuing; supplement as required by I.R.C.P. 26(e).
```

## Sample requests

```
REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION NO. 1: All documents identified in, or used to
prepare, your answers to [Plaintiff's] First Set of Interrogatories.

REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION NO. 2: All documents that support, refer, or relate
to [the claim / defense / transaction at issue].

REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION NO. 3: All communications between you and [person /
entity] concerning [subject].

REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION NO. 4: All [contracts / account statements /
invoices / records] relating to [the account or transaction at issue],
including any electronically stored information in native format with
metadata.

REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION NO. 5: All documents you may offer as exhibits at
trial or any hearing in this action.
```

## Response block (when responding)

```
RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 1: Responsive documents are produced as Bates
[range], kept as maintained in the usual course of business.

RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 2: OBJECTION. [State the specific ground — e.g.,
overbroad and not proportional to the needs of the case under I.R.C.P.
26(b)(1) because ___.] Subject to and without waiving the objection,
[party] will produce [scope].

RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 3: After a reasonable search, no responsive,
non-privileged documents exist in [party]'s possession, custody, or
control.
```

Log any document withheld on a claim of privilege (I.R.C.P. 26(b)(5)). End
with a certificate of service.
