# Deposition Notice — I.R.C.P. 30

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Drafting scaffold only. Verify the current rule
> text and any local-rule notice requirement in the corpus before serving.

Oral depositions are governed by **I.R.C.P. 30**.

- Serve **reasonable** written notice of the time, place, and identity of
  the deponent on every party.
- A party may take up to **30 depositions** without leave of court; to
  exceed that number, obtain a stipulation or move for leave. Confirm the
  current limit in the corpus.
- For an **entity deposition**, describe the matters for examination with
  reasonable particularity so the entity can designate one or more
  witnesses to testify on its behalf.
- State the **method of recording** (stenographic, audio, or video).
  Remote / video depositions are permitted by stipulation or order.

Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.
Use the I.R.C.P. 2 caption (defer to `id-statewide-format`) and a
certificate of service.

## Notice of deposition — individual deponent

```
[CAPTION — per id-statewide-format]

NOTICE OF DEPOSITION OF [DEPONENT NAME]

PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that, pursuant to I.R.C.P. 30, [Plaintiff] will take
the deposition upon oral examination of [Deponent Name] as follows:

  DATE:     [date]
  TIME:     [time]
  PLACE:    [address / videoconference platform and access details]
  BEFORE:   a notary public or other officer authorized to administer oaths

The deposition will be recorded by [stenographic / audio / video] means
and will continue from day to day until completed. [If a subpoena duces
tecum accompanies this notice for a non-party, so state and attach.]

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.
                                            /s/ [Name]
                                            [Party / Attorney for Party]
```

## Notice of deposition — entity (I.R.C.P. 30(b)(6))

```
NOTICE OF DEPOSITION OF [ENTITY] PURSUANT TO I.R.C.P. 30(b)(6)

PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that [Plaintiff] will take the deposition of [Entity],
which must designate one or more officers, directors, managing agents, or
other persons to testify on its behalf about the following matters,
described with reasonable particularity:

  1. [Matter for examination]
  2. [Matter for examination]
  ...

[Date, time, place, recording method as above.]
```

End with a certificate of service. Calendar the deposition consistent with
any scheduling-order discovery cutoff — see `id-deadlines`.
