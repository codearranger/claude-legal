# Motion to Compel + Sanctions — I.R.C.P. 37

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Drafting scaffold only. Verify the current subpart
> lettering and the sanctions provisions against the verbatim Rule 37 text
> in the corpus before filing.

If good-faith conferral fails, move to compel under **I.R.C.P. 37**.
Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.
A motion to compel is noticed for hearing under **I.R.C.P. 7(b)** —
see `id-hearings` and `id-schedule-hearing`. Use the I.R.C.P. 2 caption
(defer to `id-statewide-format`).

## What the motion must do

1. **Identify the specific deficiencies** — which interrogatory, RFP, or
   RFA, and why each response is inadequate.
2. **Attach** the requests and the responses as exhibits.
3. **Certify the good-faith conferral** (and attach the correspondence) —
   see `meet-and-confer-templates.md`.
4. **Address each objection** on the merits.
5. **Request specific relief** — an order compelling responses by a date
   certain, plus an award of the reasonable expenses caused by the failure.

## Motion scaffold

```
[CAPTION — per id-statewide-format]

MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY RESPONSES AND FOR EXPENSES

[Plaintiff] moves the Court under I.R.C.P. 37 for an order compelling
[Defendant] to serve complete responses to [the discovery at issue] and
awarding the reasonable expenses caused by the failure, and states:

  1. On [DATE], [Plaintiff] served [First Set of Interrogatories / RFPs /
     RFAs] (Exhibit A). Responses were due [DATE] under I.R.C.P.
     [33(b)(2) / 34(b)(2) / 36(a)(3)].
  2. [Defendant] [served deficient responses on ___ / failed to respond]
     (Exhibit B).
  3. The responses are deficient because [specific grounds, item by item].
  4. [Plaintiff] conferred in good faith on [DATE] (Exhibit C); the
     dispute was not resolved.

WHEREFORE, [Plaintiff] requests an order:
  (a) compelling complete, non-evasive responses within [14] days;
  (b) overruling [Defendant's] objections to [items]; and
  (c) awarding [Plaintiff] the reasonable expenses, including attorney
      fees, caused by the failure under I.R.C.P. 37.

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.
                                            /s/ [Name]
```

Support the motion with a **Memorandum** and an **Affidavit / Declaration**
authenticating the exhibits and the conferral. Accompany it with a Notice
of Hearing (the 14-day clock — I.R.C.P. 7(b)(3); see `id-deadlines`).

## Sanctions ladder

- **On a granted motion to compel.** I.R.C.P. 37 authorizes an award of
  the reasonable **expenses, including attorney fees**, caused by the
  failure, unless the opposing position was substantially justified or
  other circumstances make an award unjust.
- **For disobedience of a discovery order.** Rule 37 escalates to stronger
  sanctions — up to designating facts as established, striking pleadings,
  prohibiting claims or defenses, dismissal, or default judgment.
- **Failure to admit (RFA).** Rule 37 addresses the consequences of an
  unjustified failure to admit under I.R.C.P. 36 — the requesting party
  may recover the expenses of proving the matter.
