# Request-for-Admission Templates — I.R.C.P. 36

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Drafting scaffolds only. Verify the current rule
> text in the corpus before serving or responding.

Requests for admission are governed by **I.R.C.P. 36**.

- **Critical — deemed admitted.** Under **I.R.C.P. 36(a)(3)** a matter is
  **deemed admitted** unless the responding party serves a written answer
  or objection within **30 days** of service. A missed RFA deadline can
  concede dispositive facts. Calendar these dates carefully.
- A party may move to **amend or withdraw** an admission on the rule's
  conditions (the merits are subserved and the requesting party is not
  prejudiced).

Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.
Use the I.R.C.P. 2 caption (defer to `id-statewide-format`) and a
certificate of service.

## Preamble

```
[CAPTION — per id-statewide-format]

PLAINTIFF'S FIRST REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to I.R.C.P. 36, [Plaintiff] requests that [Defendant] admit the
truth of each matter below within 30 days of service. A matter is admitted
unless, within 30 days, you serve a written answer or objection
(I.R.C.P. 36(a)(3)).
```

## Sample requests

```
REQUEST FOR ADMISSION NO. 1: Admit that you entered into the [contract /
agreement] attached as Exhibit A.

REQUEST FOR ADMISSION NO. 2: Admit that Exhibit A is a true and accurate
copy of the [document].

REQUEST FOR ADMISSION NO. 3: Admit that you [did / did not] [specific act
or fact at issue].

REQUEST FOR ADMISSION NO. 4: Admit that you have no documents supporting
[your contention that ___].

REQUEST FOR ADMISSION NO. 5: Admit the genuineness of each document
identified on the attached list.
```

## Response block (when responding)

```
RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 1: Admitted.

RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 2: Denied.

RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 3: Admitted in part and denied in part. [Party]
admits ___; [Party] denies the remainder because ___.

RESPONSE TO REQUEST NO. 4: [Party] has made reasonable inquiry and the
information known or readily obtainable is insufficient to admit or deny,
and on that basis the request is neither admitted nor denied
(I.R.C.P. 36(a)(4)).

OBJECTION TO REQUEST NO. 5: [State the specific ground.]
```

> A response must fairly meet the substance of the request; an evasive or
> incomplete answer can be treated as a failure to respond. The
> consequences of an unjustified failure to admit are addressed in
> **I.R.C.P. 37** — see `motion-to-compel.md`.

End with a certificate of service.
