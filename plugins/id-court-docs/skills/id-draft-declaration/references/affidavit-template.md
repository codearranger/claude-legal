# Idaho Notarized Affidavit Template

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a fill-in-the-blank drafting aid. You, the
> affiant, are personally responsible for the truth of every statement; a
> knowingly false sworn statement can constitute perjury. Verify the
> current rules before filing.

An **affidavit** is a written statement of facts the affiant swears (or
affirms) to be true, signed in the presence of a notary who administers the
oath and completes a **jurat**. It is acceptable supporting evidence under
**I.R.C.P. 56(c)**, equally with an Idaho Code § 9-1406 declaration (see
`declaration-template.md`). Use the affidavit form where a rule, statute,
or local practice expects a notarized oath. The full caption recipe and
line numbering come from `id-statewide-format` (I.R.C.P. 2). Verbatim Rule
56 text: `../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## Completing the jurat

The Idaho notary watches the affiant sign (or acknowledge a prior
signature), administers the oath or affirmation, then signs and seals with
the commission-expiration date. Bring valid photo identification and do
**not** sign until you are in front of the notary.

## Certificate of Service (I.R.C.P. 5)

When the Affidavit is filed and served, include a certificate of service.
See `id-statewide-format` for the certificate block. For the no-notary
alternative, use `declaration-template.md` (Idaho Code § 9-1406). For the
personal-knowledge / admissibility rules, see `personal-knowledge.md`.
