# Idaho Unsworn Declaration Template (Idaho Code § 9-1406)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a fill-in-the-blank drafting aid. You, the
> declarant, are personally responsible for the truth of every statement;
> a knowingly false declaration is punishable as perjury. Verify the
> current statute before filing.

Idaho permits an **unsworn declaration** in lieu of a notarized affidavit
wherever a matter may be supported by a sworn affidavit, declaration,
verification, or oath. If the statement is **dated and signed** by the
person and subscribed as true **under penalty of perjury**, it carries the
**same force and effect** as a notarized affidavit (**Idaho Code
§ 9-1406**) and needs **no notary**. It is acceptable supporting evidence
under **I.R.C.P. 56(c)**. The full caption recipe and line numbering come
from `id-statewide-format` (I.R.C.P. 2). Verbatim Rule 56 text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## Certificate of Service (I.R.C.P. 5)

When the Declaration is filed and served, include a certificate of service
(papers served after the complaint require one). See `id-statewide-format`
for the certificate block, and `affidavit-template.md` for the notarized
alternative under the same caption.

## Notes

- **No notary is required** — the § 9-1406 subscription substitutes for the
  jurat.
- The subscription language is fixed: *"I declare under penalty of perjury
  under the laws of the State of Idaho that the foregoing is true and
  correct."*
- Keep each averment to facts the declarant could competently testify to at
  trial; see `personal-knowledge.md`.
- For the notarized-affidavit form, use `affidavit-template.md`. Both forms
  support a motion under I.R.C.P. 56(c).
