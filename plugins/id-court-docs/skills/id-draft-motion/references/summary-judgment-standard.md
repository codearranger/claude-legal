# I.R.C.P. 56 — Summary-Judgment Standard and Timing

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This summarizes the standard and the timing track.
> Confirm the current Rule 56 text and every day count in the corpus before
> relying on a computed date.

Summary judgment in Idaho is governed by **I.R.C.P. 56**. Verbatim rule
text: `../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## The substantive standard

The court grants summary judgment when there is **no genuine dispute as to
any material fact** and the moving party is **entitled to judgment as a
matter of law**. The court construes the record in the **light most
favorable to the party opposing the motion** and draws all reasonable
inferences in that party's favor. A "material" fact is one that, under the
governing law, could affect the outcome; a "genuine" dispute is one a
reasonable fact-finder could resolve either way.

The moving party bears the initial burden of showing the absence of a
genuine dispute. The burden then shifts to the non-movant, who may not rest
on the allegations of the pleadings but must come forward with admissible
evidence establishing a genuine issue for trial.

## Supporting evidence — I.R.C.P. 56(c)

A Rule 56 motion is **supported by Affidavits or Declarations** and
documentary exhibits. Under **I.R.C.P. 56(c)**, a supporting or opposing
Affidavit/Declaration must be **made on personal knowledge**, **set out
facts that would be admissible in evidence**, and **show that the affiant
or declarant is competent to testify** on the matters stated. An Idaho Code
§ 9-1406 declaration under penalty of perjury serves equally with a
notarized affidavit. See `id-draft-declaration`.

## The timing track (confirm current figures in the corpus)

Rule 56 sets a **distinct timing track** that overrides the ordinary 14-day
motion-notice rule of I.R.C.P. 7(b)(3):

| Step | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Motion + supporting brief and affidavits served | **at least 28 days before the hearing** |
| Answering brief + opposing affidavits served | **at least 14 days before the hearing** |
| Reply brief served | within the period the rule sets |
| Motion filed | **at least 90 days before the date set for trial** (unless the court orders otherwise) |

Compute the actual dates with `id-deadlines`, which applies **I.R.C.P. 2.2**
time computation (Rule 6 is reserved) and the **+3 days for service by
mail**.

## Drafting checklist

- [ ] Memorandum states the no-genuine-dispute standard and applies it
- [ ] Each material fact carries a record cite to an Affidavit/Declaration
      or exhibit (I.R.C.P. 56(c))
- [ ] Supporting affidavits/declarations are on personal knowledge and set
      out admissible facts
- [ ] 28-day service / 14-day answering-brief / 90-day-before-trial track
      confirmed against the corpus and computed with id-deadlines
- [ ] Notice of Hearing respects the 28-day track (id-draft-note)
- [ ] Proposed Order / Judgment drafted separately (id-draft-order)
