# Structuring the Legal Argument in an Idaho Memorandum

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is drafting guidance, not a substitute for
> independent legal analysis. Verify the controlling standard and every
> citation before filing.

The Memorandum is the brief that supports a motion under **I.R.C.P. 7(b)**.
Its job is to **argue the law**; the supporting Affidavit/Declaration's job
is to **state the facts** (see `id-draft-declaration`). Keep them separate.
Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## The pro-se drafting framework

Self-represented filers (and counsel) should follow five steps:

1. **State the relief clearly** in the opening sentence of the motion.
2. **Cite the rule** (I.R.C.P. ____) that empowers the court to grant that
   relief, stating the grounds with particularity per I.R.C.P. 7(b)(1).
3. **State the facts** that satisfy each element of the controlling
   standard, each with a record citation to a supporting Affidavit or
   Declaration.
4. **Apply controlling Idaho authority** to those facts.
5. **Conclude** with the specific order sought, and lodge a proposed Order
   (`id-draft-order`).

## Section-by-section structure

| Section | Purpose | Keep out |
|---|---|---|
| **I. Introduction** | The case, the relief, and the headline reasons in 1–2 paragraphs | Block citations; long fact recitals |
| **II. Statement of Facts** | Material facts, each with a record citation | Argument; characterization |
| **III. Argument** | Rule → standard → application → authority, under lettered headings | Unsupported facts |
| **IV. Conclusion** | The precise order sought | New arguments |

## Writing each argument heading (CRAC)

Within each lettered argument heading, lead with the legal framework and
close with the application:

- **Conclusion** — the point this heading proves, stated as a heading.
- **Rule** — the controlling standard, with its citation. Lead with the
  rule, not the facts.
- **Application** — apply the rule to the facts, citing the record by
  paragraph: ([Name] Aff. ¶ 3), (Ex. B).
- **Conclusion** — restate the relief this heading supports.

## Citing Idaho authority

Cite Idaho cases to **Idaho Reports and the Pacific Reporter**, e.g.,
*Smith v. Jones*, ___ Idaho ___, ___ P.3d ___ (YEAR). For a Court of
Appeals decision, add the court parenthetical: *(Ct. App. YEAR)*. Cite
rules as **I.R.C.P. [number]** and statutes as **Idaho Code § [number]**.
Run `id-fact-check` to verify every citation, and check that no fact in the
Memorandum lacks a record cite to a sworn statement.

## Record citations, not bare assertion

Every material fact in the Statement of Facts and the Application must trace
to record support — an Affidavit/Declaration paragraph, a verified pleading,
an admission, or an exhibit. A fact with no record cite is argument, and at
summary judgment an unsupported assertion cannot create or defeat a genuine
dispute. See `summary-judgment-standard.md`.
