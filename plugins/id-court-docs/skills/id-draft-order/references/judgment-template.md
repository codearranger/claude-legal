# Idaho Separate-Document Judgment Template (I.R.C.P. 54(a) / 58)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a fill-in-the-blank drafting aid. **Only a
> judge signs a judgment** — the filer prepares the proposed form and
> submits it but never signs. Verify the current rules and local practice
> before submitting.

Under **I.R.C.P. 54(a)**, a **judgment must be set out in a separate
document** — distinct from any memorandum decision, opinion, order, or
minute entry. The judgment **states only the relief** the court grants; it
does **not** recite reasoning or procedural history. It is **entered** under
**I.R.C.P. 58** when the court signs it and the Clerk files it, and the
**date of entry** starts the post-judgment (I.R.C.P. 59 / 60) and appeal
(Idaho Appellate Rules) clocks. The full caption recipe comes from
`id-statewide-format` (I.R.C.P. 2). Verbatim rule text:
`../../id-law-references/references/court-rules/IRCP-civil-procedure.md`.

## No recitals in a Judgment

Omit recitals entirely. Rule 54(a) requires the separate document to state
only the relief — keep the findings and reasoning in the Order or
memorandum decision (`order-template.md`), and keep the Judgment short and
operative.

## Rule 54(b) certificate — fewer than all claims/parties

When the Judgment resolves **fewer than all claims or fewer than all
parties**, it is not final or appealable unless the court enters an
**I.R.C.P. 54(b) certificate** — an express determination that there is **no
just reason for delay** and an express direction for entry of a final
judgment. Include the certificate language only if finality is intended; if
the form is interlocutory, omit it. **The court — not the drafter — decides
whether to certify.** Confirm the exact certificate language in the corpus.

## Entry and effective date — I.R.C.P. 58

A judgment is generally effective on the **date of entry** — the date the
Clerk files the signed judgment — not the date the judge signed it or the
date of the hearing. The date of entry is the reference point `id-deadlines`
uses to compute I.R.C.P. 59 / 60 and appeal deadlines. For a default
judgment, keep the Judgment itself a separate Rule 54(a) document and recite
the predicate findings in the supporting application/order. See
`id-submit-order` for transmittal.
