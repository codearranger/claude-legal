# Idaho filing fees and cost / fee-shifting framework

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Navigational map only. **Dollar amounts change.**
> Confirm every fee and ceiling with the clerk of the filing court and against
> the statutes in `id-statutes-debt/` before relying on any figure here.

Idaho follows the **American rule**: each party bears its own attorney fees
unless a **statute, court rule, or contract** shifts them. Costs (as distinct
from attorney fees) are more routinely awarded to the prevailing party.

## Costs to the prevailing party — I.R.C.P. 54(d)

**I.R.C.P. 54(d)** governs **costs to the prevailing party.** A prevailing
party who seeks costs must serve and file a **memorandum of costs within 14
days** after entry of the judgment (confirm the current day count and the
distinction between costs **as a matter of right** and **discretionary**
costs). An opposing party may move to disallow or contest the memorandum
within the time set by the rule. The same rule framework is the vehicle for
claiming attorney fees that a statute or contract authorizes — the fee request
travels with the cost memorandum.

## Attorney fees — Idaho Code Title 12

- **I.C. § 12-120 — attorney fees to the prevailing party in certain
  actions.** Key subsections:
  - **§ 12-120(1)** — attorney fees to the prevailing party in actions where
    the amount pleaded is at or below a **stated small-claims dollar cap**
    (verify the current cap figure against legislature.idaho.gov before
    relying), subject to the statute's pre-suit demand condition.
  - **§ 12-120(3)** — attorney fees to the prevailing party in any civil
    action to recover on a **commercial transaction** (and on certain
    contract and account claims). This is the central fee-shifting hook in
    **consumer-debt and contract litigation** — and it reaches **both
    sides**, so a consumer who **successfully defends** a collection suit on
    a commercial transaction may recover fees, while a losing defense can
    expose the consumer to the plaintiff's fees. Confirm the current text and
    the case law on what qualifies as a "commercial transaction."
- **I.C. § 12-121 — frivolous-litigation fees.** The court may award
  reasonable attorney fees to the prevailing party where the case was
  **brought, pursued, or defended frivolously, unreasonably, or without
  foundation.** Discretionary; confirm the current standard and any
  rule-based findings requirement.
- **I.C. § 12-123 — sanctions for frivolous conduct.** Authorizes the court
  to award costs and attorney fees as a **sanction for frivolous conduct** in
  a civil action (conduct that serves merely to harass, is not warranted by
  existing law, etc.). Confirm the current procedure and standard.

## Filing fees — I.C. § 31-3201

**Idaho Code § 31-3201** sets **District Court / Magistrate Division filing
fees.** **Do not embed amounts here** — they are revised periodically.
Confirm the current figures with the clerk of the filing court and against
`id-statutes-debt/`. Small-claims, magistrate-civil, and District-Court civil
filing fees differ; verify the correct fee for the case type and amount in
controversy.

## Fee waiver / deferral

A party unable to pay court fees may apply for a **waiver or deferral** of
fees and costs. Idaho courts provide an application form through the
self-help / iCourt portal — see `online-sources.md`. Confirm the current
income/eligibility standard and the form name with the clerk.
