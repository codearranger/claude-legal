# Homestead Exemption — Idaho Code Title 55

> **Scope:** The Idaho homestead exemption — the protection of a debtor's
> residence from forced sale to satisfy a judgment — under Idaho Code Title 55,
> chapter 10.
> **Source:** Idaho Legislature — https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/Title55/
> **Format:** curated digest (citation + scope summary per section; not verbatim).

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** **The homestead dollar amount must be verified against
> the current Idaho Code** — it is revised periodically. Confirm before

## I.C. § 55-1001 to § 55-1011 — homestead exemption (the chapter)

Title 55, chapter 10 creates and governs the **homestead exemption**:
definitions of the homestead, how the exemption attaches (automatically to an
owner-occupied dwelling, with a declaration available for certain property),
the value of the exemption, and how it interacts with execution sales,
voluntary conveyances, and certain liens. Read the whole chapter (§§ 55-1001
through 55-1011) when analyzing a homestead claim.

## I.C. § 55-1003 — amount of the homestead exemption

Sets the **dollar value of the homestead exemption** — protecting up to
**$175,000** (**verify current figure against legislature.idaho.gov before
relying**) of equity in the debtor's residence from forced sale on an ordinary
money judgment. The exemption generally attaches automatically to a
qualifying owner-occupied home (no recorded declaration required for the
automatic exemption), subject to the chapter's conditions and exceptions
(e.g., consensual mortgages, mechanic's liens, and certain other claims that
the homestead does not defeat).

## Key points to confirm

- The **automatic** vs. **declared** homestead and any recording requirement.
- Whether the exemption covers the specific property type (house, mobile home,
  condominium, etc.).
- The liens / claims **excepted** from the homestead (e.g., purchase-money and
  consensual security interests, tax liens, mechanic's liens).
- Proceeds protection after a sale and any reinvestment period.
