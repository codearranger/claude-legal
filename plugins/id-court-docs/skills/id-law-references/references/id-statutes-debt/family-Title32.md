# Domestic Relations / Community Property — Idaho Code Title 32

> **Scope:** The substantive family law in Idaho Code Title 32 — marriage,
> divorce, spousal maintenance, child support, custody, and the
> community-property system. The *substance* that the I.R.F.L.P. (Magistrate
> Division) supplies the *procedure* for.
> **Source:** Idaho Legislature — https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/Title32/
> **Format:** curated digest (citation + scope summary per section; not verbatim).

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Confirm each section against the current Idaho Code
> before relying. Family-law outcomes turn heavily on the facts and the court's
> best-interests findings.

---

## I.C. § 32-201 — common-law marriage abolished after January 1, 1996

A valid marriage requires consent plus issuance of a license and
solemnization. **Common-law marriage in Idaho was abolished for marriages
entered after January 1, 1996**; a common-law marriage validly established
**before** that date may still be recognized. Confirm the date rule for any
purported common-law marriage.

## I.C. § 32-603 — grounds for divorce

Enumerates the **grounds for divorce** (e.g., adultery, extreme cruelty,
desertion, neglect, habitual intemperance, conviction of a felony, permanent
insanity, and **irreconcilable differences**). Confirm the current list and the
proof required for each ground.

## I.C. § 32-610 — irreconcilable differences

Provides for divorce on the ground of **irreconcilable differences** — the
no-fault path. Confirm the current text and any required findings.

## I.C. § 32-705 — spousal maintenance

Authorizes **spousal maintenance** where a spouse lacks sufficient property and
is unable to support themselves through employment; lists the factors the court
weighs (financial resources, time to acquire training, duration of the
marriage, age and health, earning capacity, etc.). Confirm the current factor
list.

## I.C. § 32-706 — child-support factors

Sets the **factors for a child-support award** (financial resources of the
child and parents, standard of living, physical/emotional condition and
educational needs, available medical coverage). The numeric computation runs
through the **Idaho Child Support Guidelines (I.R.F.L.P. 120)** — see
`../family-rules.md`.

## I.C. § 32-716 / § 32-717 — custody and the best-interests standard

- **§ 32-716** — custody / parenting-arrangement provisions.
- **§ 32-717** — custody is decided under the **best interests of the child**
  standard, weighing the statutory factors (wishes of the parents and child,
  the child's relationships and adjustment, the character and circumstances of
  the parties, domestic-violence considerations, etc.). Confirm the current
  factor list.

## I.C. § 32-903 / § 32-906 — separate vs. community property

- **§ 32-903 — separate property.** Property owned by a spouse before
  marriage, and that acquired afterward by **gift, bequest, devise, or
  descent** (with its rents/issues/profits), is the **separate property** of
  that spouse.
- **§ 32-906 — community property.** All other property acquired by either
  spouse **during marriage** is **community property.** Idaho is a
  **community-property** state; income and acquisitions during marriage are
  presumptively community, subject to the separate-property exceptions and
  any valid agreement. Confirm the current text and the management/liability
  provisions.

## I.C. § 32-11-101 et seq. — UCCJEA

Idaho's enactment of the **Uniform Child Custody Jurisdiction and Enforcement
Act**, governing which state has jurisdiction to make and modify child-custody
determinations and the enforcement of out-of-state custody orders. Screen
UCCJEA jurisdiction at the outset of any multi-state custody matter.
