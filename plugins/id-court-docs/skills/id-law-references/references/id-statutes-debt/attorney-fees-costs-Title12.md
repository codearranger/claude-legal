# Attorney Fees and Costs — Idaho Code Title 12

> **Scope:** The statutory attorney-fee and cost provisions in Idaho Code Title
> 12 — the fee-shifting hooks in contract, commercial-transaction, and
> consumer-debt litigation, plus cost-recovery.
> **Source:** Idaho Legislature — https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/Title12/
> **Format:** curated digest (citation + scope summary per section; not verbatim).

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Confirm each section and the case law construing it
> against the current Idaho Code before relying. Fee awards turn on the cause
> of action, prevailing-party status, and the court's findings.

---

## I.C. § 12-120 — attorney fees to the prevailing party (certain actions)

The central state fee-shifting statute. Key subsections:

- **§ 12-120(1)** — attorney fees to the **prevailing party** in actions where
  the amount pleaded is at or below a **stated small-claims dollar cap**
  (**verify the current cap figure** against legislature.idaho.gov), subject to
  the statute's **pre-suit written demand** condition. Confirm the demand
  prerequisite and the cap.
- **§ 12-120(3)** — attorney fees to the **prevailing party** in any civil
  action to recover on a **commercial transaction** (and on certain notes,
  accounts, and contract claims). This is the **central fee hook in
  consumer-debt and contract litigation**: it reaches **both sides**, so a
  consumer who **successfully defends** a commercial-transaction collection
  suit may recover fees, and a losing defense can expose the consumer to the
  plaintiff's fees. Confirm the "commercial transaction" / "integral to the
  claim" case-law gloss.

## I.C. § 12-121 — frivolous-litigation attorney fees

Authorizes the court, in any civil action, to award **reasonable attorney fees
to the prevailing party** where the action was **brought, pursued, or defended
frivolously, unreasonably, or without foundation.** Discretionary; confirm the
current standard and any required findings (and the implementing rule).

## I.C. § 12-123 — sanctions for frivolous conduct

Authorizes the court to award **costs and reasonable attorney fees as a
sanction for frivolous conduct** in a civil action — conduct that serves merely
to harass or maliciously injure, or that is not warranted under existing law
and lacks a good-faith argument for extension. Confirm the current procedure
(motion timing, hearing) and standard.

## I.C. § 12-301 — costs (general)

General **costs** provision in Title 12 governing the recovery of taxable costs
in civil actions. The **procedural vehicle** for claiming costs (and
statutory/contractual fees) is the **memorandum of costs under I.R.C.P.
54(d)**, filed within **14 days** of judgment — see `../fees-and-costs.md`.

## How to use this file

1. Identify the **cause of action** — a commercial transaction routes to
   § 12-120(3); a small-dollar claim with a pre-suit demand may route to
   § 12-120(1).
2. For **frivolous** conduct, consider § 12-121 (fees) and § 12-123
   (sanctions).
3. Claim fees/costs through the **I.R.C.P. 54(d) memorandum of costs** within
   14 days of judgment.
