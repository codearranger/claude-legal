## I.C. Title 28, chapter 9 — Secured Transactions (UCC Article 9)

Idaho has adopted **UCC Article 9** as **Title 28, chapter 9** of the Idaho
Code. It governs **security interests in personal property and fixtures**:

- **Attachment** — when a security interest becomes enforceable against the
  debtor (value, rights in the collateral, and a security agreement /
  possession-or-control).
- **Perfection** — how a secured party makes its interest effective against
  third parties (usually by filing a UCC-1 financing statement, or by
  possession/control for certain collateral).
- **Priority** — the rules ranking competing claims to the same collateral
  (first-to-file-or-perfect, purchase-money priority, etc.).
- **Default and enforcement** — the secured party's remedies on default
  (repossession, disposition of collateral with commercial reasonableness and
  notice, deficiency / surplus). These default-and-enforcement provisions are
  the ones most relevant to **consumer secured-debt** disputes (e.g., a
  repossessed vehicle and a deficiency claim).
