# Exemptions and Wage Garnishment — Idaho Code Title 11

> **Scope:** Personal-property exemptions, the wage-garnishment exemption, and
> continuing wage garnishment under Idaho Code Title 11 (Enforcement of
> Judgments). The shield a judgment debtor raises against execution and
> garnishment.
> **Source:** Idaho Legislature — https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/Title11/
> **Format:** curated digest (citation + scope summary per section; not verbatim).

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** **Every dollar amount below must be verified against
> the current Idaho Code** — exemption figures are revised periodically and
> some are inflation-adjusted. Confirm before claiming an exemption.

---

## I.C. § 11-201 et seq. — exemptions generally

Opening provisions of the exemptions article: which property of a judgment
debtor is exempt from execution and attachment, and the framework for claiming
exemptions. Read with the specific-property list in § 11-605.

## I.C. § 11-207 — wage-garnishment exemption

Limits the portion of a debtor's earnings subject to garnishment: the
garnishable amount is generally the **lesser of (a) 25% of disposable weekly
earnings or (b) the amount by which disposable weekly earnings exceed 30 times
the federal minimum hourly wage.** Tracks the federal Consumer Credit
Protection Act garnishment cap. Confirm the current text and any
support-order or higher-priority exception.

## I.C. § 11-605 — personal-property exemptions

Enumerates personal property exempt from execution, each with a value cap.
**All figures below are flagged "verify current figure against
legislature.idaho.gov before relying":**

- **Motor vehicle** — up to **$10,000** (verify current figure).
- **Tools / implements / professional books of the debtor's trade** — up to
  **$10,000** (verify current figure).
- **Household furnishings, goods, and appliances** — up to **$1,000 per item**,
  **$7,500 aggregate** (verify current figures).
- **Jewelry** — up to **$1,000** (verify current figure).
- **One firearm** — up to **$1,500** (verify current figure).
- **Other tangible personal property** — up to **$1,500** (verify current
  figure).
- **Wages / earnings** — an exemption figure (e.g., **$2,500 per year**) also
  appears in this section (verify current figure and read with § 11-207's
  garnishment cap).

Confirm the complete current list, each cap, and any additional exempt
categories (benefits, certain proceeds, etc.) against the live statute.

## I.C. § 11-705 — continuing wage garnishment (Title 11, ch. 7)

Idaho's **continuing garnishment** mechanism for earnings: a single wage
garnishment continues to attach successive pay periods (rather than requiring a
fresh writ each period), subject to the § 11-207 exemption cap. Confirm the
duration, priority, and the employer's answer obligations.

## I.C. § 11-712 — continuing garnishment procedure / employer duties

Procedural provisions within the continuing-garnishment chapter (e.g.,
employer withholding, remittance, and answer requirements). Confirm the current
text before serving or answering a continuing wage garnishment.
