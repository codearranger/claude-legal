# Statutes of Limitation — Idaho Code Title 5

> **Scope:** Periods of limitation for civil actions under Idaho Code Title 5,
> chapter 2 — the core deadlines for consumer-debt, contract, tort, fraud, and
> judgment-enforcement matters.
> **Source:** Idaho Legislature — https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/Title5/
> **Format:** curated digest (citation + scope summary per section; not verbatim).

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Confirm each section and its accrual rule against the
> current Idaho Code before relying. Limitation periods turn on accrual,
> tolling, and the nature of the obligation — verify before calendaring.

---

## I.C. § 5-201 — actions can only be commenced within the prescribed period

General provision: civil actions may be commenced only within the periods
prescribed by Title 5 after the cause of action accrues, except where a
different limitation is prescribed by statute.

## I.C. § 5-214A — action on a written contract for sale of goods / account stated (verify scope)

Limitation provision touching contracts for the sale of goods / account
matters; confirm the precise scope and the period against the current text
(the UCC sale-of-goods limitation also appears in Title 28). Read alongside
§ 5-216 and the UCC.

## I.C. § 5-215 — judgment: 11 years

Action **upon a judgment or decree** of a court of the United States or of any
state/territory must be commenced within **11 years.** (Judgment-lien renewal
is separately governed — see I.C. § 10-1111.) This is the controlling period
for reviving / suing on an Idaho judgment.

## I.C. § 5-216 — written contract: 5 years

Action upon any **contract, obligation, or liability founded upon a written
instrument** must be commenced within **5 years.** This is the core
written-contract limitation for consumer-debt defense (e.g., a signed credit
agreement or promissory note).

## I.C. § 5-217 — oral contract / open account: 4 years

Action upon a **contract, obligation, or liability not founded upon a written
instrument** (oral contract and open account) must be commenced within **4
years.** Pair with the accrual rule in § 5-222 for open accounts.

## I.C. § 5-218 — statutory liability / fraud / certain torts: 3 years

Three-year limitation covering, among others, an action **upon a liability
created by statute** (other than a penalty/forfeiture), certain property torts,
and an action for **relief on the ground of fraud or mistake** (with the cause
of action deemed not to accrue until discovery of the facts constituting the
fraud or mistake). Confirm which subdivision governs the specific claim.

## I.C. § 5-219 — personal injury / professional malpractice: 2 years

Two-year limitation for actions for **personal injury** (and certain
professional-malpractice and wrongful-death actions). Confirm the accrual rule
for the specific injury claim.

## I.C. § 5-222 — accrual of open / mutual account

Sets the **accrual rule for an open or mutual current account**: the cause of
action is deemed to accrue from the time of the **last item** in the account
(read with the 4-year period in § 5-217). Important for dating the limitation
clock on revolving / open-account debt.
