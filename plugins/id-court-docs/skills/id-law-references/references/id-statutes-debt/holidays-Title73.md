## I.C. § 73-108 — holidays enumerated

Idaho's enumerated **legal holidays** (the days the courts treat as holidays
for the last-day rule under I.R.C.P. 2.2):

1. **New Year's Day** — January 1.
2. **Martin Luther King, Jr.–Idaho Human Rights Day** — third Monday in
   January.
3. **Washington's Birthday** (Presidents' Day) — third Monday in February.
4. **Memorial Day** — last Monday in May.
5. **Independence Day** — July 4.
6. **Labor Day** — first Monday in September.
7. **Columbus Day** — second Monday in October.
8. **Veterans Day** — November 11.
9. **Thanksgiving Day** — fourth Thursday in November.
10. **Christmas Day** — December 25.
11. **Every Sunday.**
12. Any day **appointed by the President** or by the **Governor** as a holiday
    (e.g., a special day of mourning or observance).

> **Idaho-specific notes:**
> - Idaho **observes Columbus Day** (second Monday in October).
> - Idaho does **not** include **Juneteenth** as a § 73-108 state legal
>   holiday (verify the current statute on each refresh — the enumerated list
>   is the controlling source).

## Observance rule (weekend shift)

When a holiday falls on a **Saturday**, the **preceding Friday** is observed as
the holiday; when a holiday falls on a **Sunday**, the **following Monday** is
observed. Apply this observance rule together with I.R.C.P. 2.2 when the last
day of a period lands on a weekend or holiday.

## I.C. § 73-109 — transaction of business on holidays

Governs the legal effect of acts done, and business transacted, on a holiday
(and the treatment of an act required to be performed on a day that is a
holiday). Confirm the current text when an act's due date falls on a holiday.
