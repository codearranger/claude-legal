# Consumer Protection, Collection Agencies, and the Idaho Credit Code

> **Scope:** Idaho's consumer-protection and debt-collection regulatory
> statutes — the Idaho Consumer Protection Act (Title 48), the Idaho Collection
> Agency Act (Title 26), and the Idaho Credit Code / UCCC (Title 28). The core
> state-law hooks for consumer-debt defense and unfair-practice claims.
> **Source:** Idaho Legislature — https://legislature.idaho.gov/statutesrules/idstat/
> **Format:** curated digest (citation + scope summary per section; not verbatim).

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Confirm each section, the private-action prerequisites,
> and any licensing question against the current Idaho Code and the Idaho
> Department of Finance before relying.

---

## Idaho Consumer Protection Act (ICPA) — I.C. § 48-601 et seq.

- **I.C. § 48-601 et seq.** — the **Idaho Consumer Protection Act**, declaring
  unfair or deceptive acts or practices in trade or commerce unlawful and
  authorizing Attorney General enforcement. The act lists prohibited methods
  of competition and unfair/deceptive practices and is construed in light of
  FTC Act interpretations. Relevant to abusive collection and
  account-servicing conduct in trade or commerce.
- **I.C. § 48-608 — private right of action.** A person who suffers an
  ascertainable loss of money or property as a result of an ICPA violation may
  bring a **private action** for actual damages (or a statutory amount, the
  greater), with potential for additional relief and attorney fees. Confirm
  the current statutory-damages figure (verify current figure), the
  ascertainable-loss requirement, and any pre-suit condition.

## Idaho Collection Agency Act — I.C. § 26-2222 et seq.

- **I.C. § 26-2222 et seq.** — the **Idaho Collection Agency Act**, governing
  the licensing and conduct of collection agencies (and related credit/finance
  actors) and administered by the **Idaho Department of Finance**
  (finance.idaho.gov).
- **I.C. § 26-2223 — license required.** No person may engage in the
  collection-agency business in Idaho without a **license** from the
  Department of Finance. The licensing requirement reaches a range of
  collection actors; **debt buyers are covered via subsection (6)** (confirm
  the current subsection lettering and the precise definition that sweeps in
  debt buyers / purchasers of delinquent accounts). A collector's or debt
  buyer's **failure to be licensed** is a common defense / counterclaim hook
  in a collection suit — verify licensure through the Department of Finance.

## Idaho Credit Code (Uniform Consumer Credit Code) — I.C. Title 28, ch. 41-46

- **I.C. § 28-41-101 et seq.** — the **Idaho Credit Code** (Idaho's enactment
  of the Uniform Consumer Credit Code), spanning **Title 28, chapters 41
  through 46.** It governs consumer credit transactions: disclosures, finance
  charges, permitted terms, and remedies/limitations on creditors and
  assignees in consumer-credit and consumer-loan transactions. Confirm the
  applicable chapter for the specific transaction type (consumer credit sale,
  consumer loan, etc.) and the remedy/penalty provisions.

## How to use this file

1. For a **collection-practices** problem, screen against the FDCPA / FCRA
   (federal, in `../federal-debt-laws/`) **and** the ICPA (§ 48-601 et seq.)
   and the Collection Agency Act (§ 26-2222 et seq.).
2. For a **licensing** defense, verify the collector / debt buyer through the
   Idaho Department of Finance and cite § 26-2223 (debt buyers via subsection
   (6)).
3. For a **credit-terms** problem, identify the controlling Idaho Credit Code
   chapter (Title 28, ch. 41-46) and the transaction type.
