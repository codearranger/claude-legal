# Idaho citation format (Bluebook + Idaho State Bar conventions)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Style guidance only. When in doubt, follow the
> current edition of *The Bluebook*, the Idaho State Bar conventions, and any
> local-rule citation requirement of the filing court.

Idaho follows **Bluebook** citation as supplemented by **Idaho State Bar**
conventions, with **parallel citation** to the official **Idaho Reports** and
the regional **Pacific Reporter**. **Idaho does not use a neutral /
public-domain citation** — there is no `20XX IDSC NN`-style vendor-neutral
cite, so always give the reporter parallel.

## Cases

Cite to the **official Idaho Reports** (`Idaho`) **and** the regional
**Pacific Reporter** (`P.`, `P.2d`, `P.3d`) in parallel:

```
Smith v. Jones, 113 Idaho 730, 747 P.2d 752 (1987)
```

- **Case name** — italicized (or underlined in typed documents).
- **Idaho Supreme Court** — reporter is **`Idaho`**; no court designation in
  the parenthetical (just the year): `(1987)`.
- **Idaho Court of Appeals** — add **`Ct. App.`** in the parenthetical:
  `(Ct. App. 2005)`. The parallel cite is to **Idaho Reports** and `P.2d` /
  `P.3d`.
- **Parallel cite order** — **Idaho Reports first**, then the Pacific
  Reporter: `113 Idaho 730, 747 P.2d 752`.
- **Pinpoint** — `113 Idaho 730, 733, 747 P.2d 752, 755 (1987)`.

Worked example (form only — verify any specific cite before relying):

```
Smith v. Jones, 138 Idaho 123, 58 P.3d 728 (Ct. App. 2002)
```

> **No neutral citation.** Do not append a vendor-neutral / public-domain
> cite for Idaho; the reporter parallel is the citation.

## Statutes

Cite the **Idaho Code** as **`I.C. § ...`** with the section symbol:

```
I.C. § 5-216
I.C. § 12-120(3)
I.C. § 32-906
```

- Use `§` for a single section and `§§` for a range or multiple sections.
- Subsections in parentheses: `I.C. § 12-120(3)`.

## Rules

Cite the relevant Idaho rule set by its standard abbreviation and rule number:

```
I.R.C.P. 56            (Idaho Rules of Civil Procedure)
I.R.C.P. 12(b)         (defenses)
I.R.E. 803(6)          (Idaho Rules of Evidence)
I.R.E. 902(11)
I.R.F.L.P. 120         (Idaho Rules of Family Law Procedure)
I.A.R. 14              (Idaho Appellate Rules)
I.R.E.F.S. 4(a)        (Idaho Rules for Electronic Filing and Service)
```

- The **Idaho Rules of Family Law Procedure** are abbreviated **`I.R.F.L.P.`**
  Do not interchange them with the civil rules.

## Constitutional and other authority

- **Idaho Constitution**: `Idaho Const. art. I, § 13`.
- **Limited-jurisdiction / electronic-filing rules** follow their own
  abbreviations (`I.R.E.F.S.`); confirm the correct rule set for the forum.

## Practical notes

- **Always verify a citation** against `online-sources.md` /
  `legal-data-apis.md` (CourtListener Idaho courts, the isc.idaho.gov opinions
  pages) before relying on it.
- When an opinion is not yet in the bound Idaho Reports, cite the regional
  reporter with the court and year in the parenthetical, and update to the
  full parallel cite when available.
