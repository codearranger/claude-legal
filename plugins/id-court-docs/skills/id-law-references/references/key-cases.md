## Summary judgment (I.R.C.P. 56)

- **Standard.** Summary judgment is proper when there is **no genuine issue
  of material fact** and the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter
  of law; the record and all reasonable inferences are construed in favor of
  the non-moving party. Where the case will be tried to the court (no jury),
  Idaho permits the trial judge, on summary judgment, to draw the most
  probable inferences from undisputed evidentiary facts. *(Doctrine

## Pleading-stage dismissal / notice pleading

- Idaho applies a **notice-pleading** standard; on a motion to dismiss the
  court assumes the truth of the well-pleaded factual allegations and grants
  dismissal only where the plaintiff could prove no set of facts entitling it
  to relief. *(Doctrine described. **TODO:** identify and verify the

## Attorney fees — commercial transaction (I.C. § 12-120(3))

- A prevailing party recovers attorney fees under **I.C. § 12-120(3)** where
  the **gravamen of the claim** is a commercial transaction integral to the
  cause of action; the commercial transaction must be the basis on which the
  party is attempting to recover, not merely incidental. *(Doctrine

## Frivolous-litigation fees (I.C. § 12-121)

- Fees under **I.C. § 12-121** are awarded only when the court is left with
  the abiding belief that the action was **pursued, defended, or brought
  frivolously, unreasonably, or without foundation.** *(Doctrine described.

## Community property and family law (Idaho Code Title 32)

Idaho is a **community-property** state. The substantive framework is
statutory (I.C. §§ 32-903, 32-906; see `family-rules.md` and
`id-statutes-debt/family-Title32.md`), and the case law construes it:

- **Community-property presumption.** Property acquired by either spouse
  during marriage is presumed **community property** under I.C. § 32-906; the
  spouse claiming an asset is **separate** bears the burden of overcoming the
  presumption (by the standard the Idaho courts apply). *(Doctrine described.

- **Best interests of the child.** Custody is decided under the **best
  interests of the child** standard (I.C. §§ 32-717), weighing the statutory

## How to use this file

1. Start from the **doctrine** stated here.
2. Pull the **current published opinion** via CourtListener (Idaho Supreme
   Court and Idaho Court of Appeals) or the bundled CourtListener + Legal Data
   Hunter MCP servers — see `legal-data-apis.md`.
3. Confirm the **parallel Idaho Reports + P.2d/P.3d cite**, the court and
   year, and that the holding has not been overruled, limited, or superseded.
4. Only then cite it, using the format in `citation-format.md`.
