# Idaho Rules of Family Law Procedure (I.R.F.L.P.) — map

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Navigational map only. Read the verbatim rule (the
> canonical isc.idaho.gov page, or `court-rules/` once populated) and confirm
> it is current before relying on it.

> **A SEPARATE RULE SET.** The I.R.F.L.P. — **not** the Idaho Rules of Civil
> Procedure — govern **family-law cases** in the **Magistrate Division** of
> the District Court. The I.R.F.L.P. have their **own numbering** and their
> own form-of-documents rule (**I.R.F.L.P. 208**). **Do not cite an I.R.C.P.
> number in a family matter or an I.R.F.L.P. number in a general civil
> matter.** Where the I.R.F.L.P. are silent, they may incorporate a civil
> rule by reference — confirm in the specific I.R.F.L.P. provision.

## Forum — the Magistrate Division

Family-law cases are heard in the **Magistrate Division** of the District
Court. A pro se filer drafting a divorce petition, a response, a parenting
plan, or a child-support worksheet is in **I.R.F.L.P. territory**, not
I.R.C.P. territory. Flag this distinction prominently whenever a matter
touches a family-law subject.

## Scope — I.R.F.L.P. 101

**I.R.F.L.P. 101** sets the scope of the family-law rules. They govern:

- annulment;
- divorce (dissolution of marriage);
- legal separation;
- separate maintenance;
- child support;
- child custody;
- grandparent visitation; and
- paternity.

Confirm the current scope list and any excluded proceeding in the rule text.

## Form of documents — I.R.F.L.P. 208

**I.R.F.L.P. 208** sets the **form of documents** in family cases (the family
analog to the civil format rule). Confirm paper, font, spacing, margin,
caption, and signature-block requirements against the current rule before
formatting a family filing.

## Child support — I.R.F.L.P. 120 (Idaho Child Support Guidelines)

**I.R.F.L.P. 120** adopts and incorporates the **Idaho Child Support
Guidelines**, an **income-shares** model: the guideline computes each
parent's support obligation from the parents' combined income, the number of
children, and statutory adjustments (e.g., health-insurance and child-care
costs, parenting-time credits). The court applies the guideline amount unless
a written finding supports a deviation. Confirm the current guideline figures,
the worksheet, and the deviation standard before relying on a number.

## Commencing and responding

- A family action is commenced by a **petition** (e.g., Petition for Divorce),
  not a "complaint." Confirm the required accompanying documents (summons,
  and — where children are involved — child-support and parenting-related
  filings) in the current I.R.F.L.P.
- The opposing party files a **response** within the time set by the
  I.R.F.L.P. after service (confirm the current day count).
- Either party may seek **temporary orders** (temporary custody, parenting
  time, child support, spousal maintenance, use of property) while the case is
  pending; confirm the current temporary-orders procedure and any required
  financial disclosures.

## Substantive family law (statutory, not procedural) — Idaho Code Title 32

The **substantive** family law lives in **Idaho Code Title 32**, not in the
I.R.F.L.P. The I.R.F.L.P. is the *procedure*; Title 32 is the *substance*.
Anchors to confirm in `id-statutes-debt/family-Title32.md`:

- **I.C. § 32-906 — community property.** Idaho is a **community-property**
  state; property acquired during marriage is presumptively community
  property, with separate-property exceptions (gift, devise, descent, and
  property owned before marriage).
- **I.C. § 32-903 / § 32-906** — separate vs. community property.
- **I.C. § 32-201 — common-law marriage abolished after January 1, 1996.**
  A common-law marriage entered before that date may still be recognized.
- **I.C. § 32-603 — grounds for divorce; § 32-610 — irreconcilable
  differences.**
- **I.C. § 32-705 — spousal maintenance; § 32-706 — child-support factors;
  §§ 32-716 / 32-717 — custody and the best-interests standard.**
- **UCCJEA — I.C. § 32-11-101 et seq.** (interstate custody jurisdiction).

See `key-cases.md` for landmark Idaho community-property and family
precedents (and verify each citation before relying on it).
