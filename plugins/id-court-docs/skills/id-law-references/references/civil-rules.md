# Idaho Rules of Civil Procedure (I.R.C.P.) — civil-practice map

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a navigational map of the Idaho Rules of
> Civil Procedure. Read the verbatim rule (the canonical isc.idaho.gov page,
> or `court-rules/` once populated) and confirm it is current before relying
> on any number, day count, or threshold.

The I.R.C.P. govern civil practice in the **District Court** and the
**Magistrate Division** for general civil matters. **Family-law cases are
NOT governed by these rules** — they are governed by the Idaho Rules of
Family Law Procedure (I.R.F.L.P.); see `family-rules.md`.

## Form, caption, and signing

- **Rule 2 — form and caption (the format rule).** Documents are on 8½×11
  white paper; font **≥ 11 point**; **double or 1.5 line spacing**; margins
  **≥ 1.2" at the top and sides** and **≥ 1" at the bottom**; the **title of
  court ≥ 3" from the top of page one**; and the **document title appears at
  the bottom of each page**. This is the Idaho document-format anchor —
  confirm the current text before formatting a filing.
- **Rule 10 — form of pleadings.** Caption, **numbered paragraphs**, claims
  founded on separate transactions stated in separate counts, exhibits
  attached and incorporated.
- **Rule 11 — signing of pleadings, motions, and other papers.** Signature
  certification and sanctions; the attorney's **Idaho State Bar (ISB)
  number** is included in the attorney signature block.
- **Rule 7(b) — motions.** Motions state with particularity the grounds and
  the relief sought. A **Notice of Hearing** must be served **at least 14
  days before the hearing** (confirm current day count and any local-rule
  variation).

## Time computation — Rule 2.2 (Rule 6 is RESERVED)

> **Idaho computes time under I.R.C.P. 2.2 — Rule 6 is RESERVED and does
> NOT carry the time-computation provisions.** Do not cite "Rule 6" for
> Idaho deadline arithmetic.

- **Rule 2.2** governs computing and extending time: how to count days,
  exclude/include the triggering day, and handle the last day falling on a
  weekend or legal holiday.
- **+3 days for service by mail** — when a period runs from service of a
  paper and service was by mail, add **3 days** (confirm current text and
  the electronic-service treatment).
- Legal holidays for the last-day rule are enumerated in **I.C. § 73-108**
  (see `id-statutes-debt/holidays-Title73.md`).

## Service

- **Rule 4 — summons and service of the complaint.** A summons must be
  served, and proof of service made, **within 182 days of filing the
  complaint** (or the action is subject to dismissal as to the unserved
  defendant) — confirm the current text and any good-cause extension.
- **Rule 5 — service of pleadings and other papers** after the original
  complaint (on parties; method, including mail and electronic service).

## Pleadings and defenses

- **Rule 8 — general rules of pleading;** claim for relief and demand for
  judgment.
- **Rule 12 — defenses and objections.** The **answer is due 21 days** after
  service of the summons and complaint (**Rule 12(a)(1)(A)**). Rule 12(b)
  defenses include failure to state a claim; confirm the conversion-to-Rule
  56 treatment when matters outside the pleadings are presented.
- **Rule 13 — counterclaims and cross-claims** (compulsory vs. permissive).
- **Rule 15 — amended and supplemental pleadings** (amendment as of right,
  by leave freely given when justice requires, and relation back).

## Discovery

- **Rule 26 — general provisions** governing the scope of discovery and
  protective orders.
- **Rule 30 — depositions upon oral examination.**
- **Rule 33 — interrogatories.** A party may serve **no more than 40
  interrogatories, including all discrete subparts**, without leave of court
  or stipulation. **Responses are due within 30 days.**
- **Rule 34 — requests for production** of documents and things.
- **Rule 36 — requests for admission.** A matter is **deemed admitted** if
  not answered or objected to within the response period (confirm the
  current window).
- **Rule 37 — motions to compel and sanctions** for discovery failures.

## Summary judgment — Rule 56

- The motion must be **filed at least 90 days before the date set for
  trial** (confirm current text).
- The **motion and supporting brief** must be served **at least 28 days
  before the hearing**.
- The **answering brief** (and opposing affidavits) must be served **at
  least 14 days before the hearing**.
- The standard mirrors the federal no-genuine-dispute-of-material-fact test;
  confirm the moving-party / non-moving-party burdens and the
  statement-of-facts requirement in the current rule.

## Default and judgments

- **Rule 55 — default.** Entry of default and default judgment; confirm the
  current notice/application requirements and any hearing on damages.
- **Rule 54 — judgments and costs.** Includes the **memorandum of costs**
  procedure — a prevailing party files a **memorandum of costs within 14
  days** of the judgment (**Rule 54(d)**); see `fees-and-costs.md`.

## Post-judgment / relief

- **Rule 59 — new trial; motion to alter or amend a judgment.** The motion
  must be filed within **14 days** after entry of judgment.
- **Rule 11.2 — motion for reconsideration.** A motion to reconsider an
  interlocutory or final order is filed within **14 days** (confirm the
  current rule designation and scope — Idaho's reconsideration rule has its
  own number, distinct from Rule 59).
- **Rule 60(b) — relief from a judgment or order.** Mistake, inadvertence,
  excusable neglect, newly discovered evidence, fraud, void judgment, or
  any other reason justifying relief; confirm the time limits, which differ
  by ground.

## Appeal (I.A.R.)

Appeals are governed by the **Idaho Appellate Rules (I.A.R.)**, a separate
rule set:

- **I.A.R. 14 — time for appeal.** A notice of appeal is generally filed
  within **42 days** of the judgment or order appealed from (confirm the
  current text and tolling motions).
- **I.A.R. 15 — cross-appeal.**
