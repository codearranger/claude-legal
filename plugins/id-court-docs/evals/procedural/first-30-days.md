# id-first-30-days — response window after service

## Prompt

I just got served with a civil complaint in Idaho. What are my
options in the first month, and what happens if I do nothing?

## Expected triggers

- `id-first-30-days`
- `id-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] States the **21-day** answer deadline (I.R.C.P. 12(a)(1)(A)) and
      cross-references `id-deadlines` for the I.R.C.P. 2.2 day-count
- [ ] Lays out the triage: answer, **Rule 12(b)** pre-answer motions
      to dismiss, affirmative defenses (**I.R.C.P. 8(c)** — flag the
      waiver risk of omitting them), and counterclaims (**I.R.C.P. 13**)
- [ ] Warns that failing to respond risks **entry of default** and
      default judgment (I.R.C.P. 55)
- [ ] Routes by amount in controversy between the Magistrate Division
      and the District Court, reading the current jurisdictional line
      from the corpus

## Common failure modes

- Wrong answer window
- Omits the affirmative-defense waiver risk
- Forgets the default-judgment consequence
