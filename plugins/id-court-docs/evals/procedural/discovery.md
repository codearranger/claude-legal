# id-discovery — interrogatories, RFPs, RFAs, motion to compel

## Prompt

I'm the defendant in an Idaho District Court case. What written
discovery can I send the plaintiff, and how many interrogatories am I
allowed?

## Expected triggers

- `id-discovery`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Confirms Idaho **allows written interrogatories** and states the
      cap of **40 interrogatories, including all discrete subparts**
      (I.R.C.P. 33(a)(1)), absent stipulation or court order
- [ ] Covers requests for production (I.R.C.P. 34) and requests for
      admission (I.R.C.P. 36), including that unanswered RFAs are
      **deemed admitted**
- [ ] States the **30-day** response window for written discovery
- [ ] Describes the meet-and-confer obligation and the **motion to
      compel** under I.R.C.P. 37, including the availability of
      sanctions/fees
- [ ] States the rule numbers and defers verbatim rule text to the
      court-rules corpus

## Common failure modes

- Says Idaho does not allow interrogatories
- Gives the wrong interrogatory cap or omits "including subparts"
- Omits the deemed-admitted consequence for RFAs
