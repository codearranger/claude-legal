# id-family-law — community-property division in divorce

## Prompt

My spouse and I are divorcing in Idaho. How will the court divide what
we own and owe? We bought a house during the marriage but I had a
retirement account before we married.

## Expected triggers

- `id-family-law`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Identifies Idaho as a **community-property** state and cites
      **I.C. § 32-906** (community property) and the separate-property
      framework (**I.C. § 32-903**)
- [ ] Distinguishes **community property** (generally acquired during
      marriage) from **separate property** (owned before marriage, or
      by gift/inheritance), and explains tracing/commingling for an
      account that pre-dates the marriage
- [ ] Notes the proceeding is heard in the **Magistrate Division**
      under the **Idaho Rules of Family Law Procedure (I.R.F.L.P.)**,
      not the I.R.C.P.
- [ ] Addresses divorce grounds (irreconcilable differences,
      **I.C. § 32-610**) and routes child support / custody to the
      guideline framework (I.R.F.L.P. 120) where relevant
- [ ] Flags fact-specificity and does not assert a fixed percentage
      split or hard-coded figures

## Common failure modes

- Treats Idaho as an equitable-distribution state
- Applies the I.R.C.P. instead of the I.R.F.L.P. to the divorce
- Ignores the separate-property / tracing issue for the premarital account
