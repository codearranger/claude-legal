Chief Justice Directive 85-25
                                                                     Amended April 2017
                                                                    Amended August 2018
                                                                Amended September 2018


                   SUPREME COURT OF COLORADO
                    OFFICE OF THE CHIEF JUSTICE

       OATHS OF OFFICE FOR JUDGES AND MAGISTRATES

I.     TIMING OF TAKING OATH

       Newly appointed judges must take an oath of office before entering office or
       conducting judicial business. Judges who have been retained in office for
       additional terms should take an oath of office on or before the first day of the
       new term. Magistrates must take an oath of office on or before their first day of
       employment with a district.


II.    TIMING OF TAKING OFFICE

       Regardless of when an oath of office is administered, judges cannot take
       office until the effective date of their appointments. The reason for this policy
       is to make certain that new judges do not enter office or attempt to conduct
       judicial business while an incumbent judge is still in office or prior to the
       time when a new judgeship actually comes into existence.


III.   FILING OF OATHS

       Oaths of justices, court of appeals judges, and district court judges must be filed
       with the Secretary of State. County court judges must file their oaths with the
       county clerk and recorder of their respective counties as well as with the
       Secretary of State. Magistrates must file their oaths with the court executives(s)
       for the district(s) in which they are employed.


IV.    PLACEMENT ON JUDICIAL PAYROLL

       New judges and magistrates will not be placed on the Judicial Department
       payroll until the effective date of their appointment or employment.

   V.     AUTHORITIES

          Colorado Constitution, Article XII, Section 9
          Section 24-12-101(3), C.R.S.
