Chief Justice Directive 86-01


                            SUPREME COURT OF COLORADO
                             OFFICE OF THE CHIEF JUSTICE

  DIRECTIVE CONCERNING CONDUCTING OFFICIAL BUSINESS OF
                THE JUDICIAL DEPARTMENT

When official judicial business is conducted at the facilities of organizations which limit
membership or admission, in policy or practice, on the basis of race, religion, gender, or national
origin, the public perception of the fairness of the judicial system can be undermined. Therefore,
judges and judicial department employees shall not conduct official business, including
seminars, conferences and meetings, at nonpublic facilities which limit membership or
admission, in policy or practice, on the basis of race, religion, gender, or national origin. No
judicial department funds shall be expended in connection with any official business, including
seminars, conferences and meetings, held at a nonpublic facility which limits membership or
admission on the basis of race, religion, gender, or national origin.


Reissued this __16th___ day of April 2025.


                                              __________/s/_______________
                                              Monica M. Márquez, Chief Justice
