# Colorado C.R.S. — Debt-Relevant and Civil-Practice Articles Corpus

This directory holds verbatim text of Colorado Revised Statutes
(C.R.S.) articles most relevant to consumer-debt defense, civil
collection, civil-practice procedure, and family-law practice in
Colorado. The contents are pulled from the official 2024 C.R.S. PDFs
published by the Colorado General Assembly's Office of Legislative
Legal Services at
**https://content.leg.colorado.gov/sites/default/files/images/olls/**
via `scripts/pull_co_statutes.py`.

## Key sections by article

### C.R.S. Title 4, Article 9 — UCC Article 9 (Secured Transactions)

- **§ 4-9-203** — Attachment of a security interest; the
  three-pronged enforceability test (value, rights in collateral,
  authenticated security agreement / possession / control)
- **§ 4-9-308 through 4-9-316** — Perfection rules and choice of law
- **§ 4-9-406** — Account-debtor protections; assignability of
  accounts and chattel paper
- **§ 4-9-601 through 4-9-628** — Default and enforcement (notice
  of disposition, commercial reasonableness, deficiency)

### C.R.S. Title 5, Article 1 — UCCC general

- **§ 5-1-201** — UCCC scope, "consumer credit transaction"
- **§ 5-1-301** — Definitions
- **§ 5-1-302** — Inflation indexing (the dollar thresholds that
  define "consumer" credit transactions are adjusted annually)

### C.R.S. Title 5, Article 16 — CFDCPA

- **§ 5-16-102** — Definitions ("debt", "debt collector", "consumer")
- **§ 5-16-103 through 5-16-110** — Prohibited practices catalog,
  parallel to and somewhat broader than 15 U.S.C. § 1692 et seq.
- **§ 5-16-115** — Licensure requirement for collection agencies
  doing business in Colorado
- **§ 5-16-124** — Validation notice contents
- **§ 5-16-135** — Civil liability and damages (mirrors FDCPA's
  $1,000 statutory damages + actual + fees)

### C.R.S. Title 6, Article 1 — CCPA

- **§ 6-1-105** — Catalog of deceptive trade practices (the
  long-form enumeration that supplies the "deceptive trade
  practice" predicate for any CCPA claim)
- **§ 6-1-113** — Civil remedies: actual damages or $500 statutory,
  **trebling** for bad faith, **mandatory** reasonable attorney
  fees and costs to a prevailing plaintiff
- **§ 6-1-115** — Limitations: 3 years from discovery
- **§ 6-1-101 through 6-1-105.5** — Definitions and general provisions

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 22 — Mediation / ADR

- **§ 13-22-301 through 13-22-310** — Dispute Resolution Act;
  confidentiality of mediation; admissibility of statements
- **§ 13-22-401 through 13-22-414** — Uniform Arbitration Act
- **§ 13-22-501 through 13-22-509** — International commercial
  arbitration

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 50 — Joint rights and obligations

- **§ 13-50-101** — Joint obligations construed as joint and several
- **§ 13-50-102 through 13-50-104** — Effect of release of one
  joint debtor; remaining-debtor proportionate-share liability;
  surety rights
- **§ 13-50-105** — Partnership / unincorporated-association suit
  procedure

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 52 — Judgment enforcement

- **§ 13-52-102** — Execution; how issued and returned
- **§ 13-52-111** — Judgment lien on real property (recording
  transcript of judgment in county clerk's office)
- **§ 13-52-112 through 13-52-114** — Duration and revival of the
  judgment lien (6-year initial lien; revival rules)

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 54 — Exemptions

- **§ 13-54-101 through 13-54-108** — Property exempt from levy
  and sale on execution (personal-property exemptions catalog)
- **§ 13-54-104** — **Wage garnishment cap (75% / 30 × federal
  minimum wage rule)**, harmonized with federal CCPA
- **Homestead exemption** — see C.R.S. § 38-41-201 et seq. (in
  Title 38, not republished here; the homestead cap was raised to
  **$250,000** (general) / **$350,000** (60+ or disabled) by
  SB22-086 effective 2022)

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 54.5 — Garnishment

- **§ 13-54.5-101 through 13-54.5-112** — Continuing writ of
  garnishment on earnings; writ of continuing garnishment;
  writ of garnishment with notice of exemption
- **§ 13-54.5-103** — Exempt earnings calculation
- **§ 13-54.5-108** — Notice-of-exemption-and-pending-levy procedure

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 80 — Limitations of actions

- **§ 13-80-101** — General 3-year period; contract actions; fraud
- **§ 13-80-102** — 2-year period; tort actions; many specific
  catalogued claims
- **§ 13-80-103** — 1-year period; selected claims (libel, slander,
  assault, etc.)
- **§ 13-80-103.5** — **6-year period for liquidated debt** (the
  provision controlling consumer-credit-card and installment-loan
  defense, as construed by *Hassler v. Account Brokers of Larimer
  Cnty.*, 2012 CO 24)
- **§ 13-80-104** — 2-year period; property damage and improvements
- **§ 13-80-108** — Accrual rules
- **§ 13-80-109** — Tolling

### C.R.S. Title 13, Article 90 — Witnesses

- **§ 13-90-101 through 13-90-104** — Competency of witnesses
- **§ 13-90-107** — **Privileged communications catalog** (attorney-
  client, physician-patient, clergy-penitent, accountant-client,
  spousal, and others)
- **§ 13-90-108** — Subpoena authority
- **§ 13-90-110** — Out-of-state subpoena enforcement

### C.R.S. Title 14, Article 10 — UDMA

- **§ 14-10-106** — Dissolution of marriage; legal separation;
  declaration of invalidity; jurisdictional and residency rules
  (**91-day residency**, **91-day waiting period**)
- **§ 14-10-111** — Declaration of invalidity (annulment) — narrow
  enumerated grounds
- **§ 14-10-114** — Maintenance (formerly "spousal maintenance");
  the 2014 advisory-formula reform and 2024 amendments
- **§ 14-10-115** — **Child support guidelines** (income-shares
  model with **93-overnight rule**, JDF 1820 E worksheet, imputation
  of income); the central child-support statute in Colorado
- **§ 14-10-122** — Modification of decree; **10% threshold**
  presumption for child-support modification
- **§ 14-10-124** — **Allocation of parental responsibilities**
  (decision-making and parenting time); best-interests factors
- **§ 14-10-129** — Relocation; the *In re Marriage of Ciesluk*
  factors

### C.R.S. Title 14, Article 13 — UCCJEA

- **§ 14-13-101 through 14-13-110** — General provisions, definitions
- **§ 14-13-201 through 14-13-210** — Jurisdiction (initial,
  exclusive continuing, modification, temporary emergency, declining)
- **§ 14-13-301 through 14-13-317** — Enforcement (registration,
  warrants, hearings)

### C.R.S. Title 24, Article 4 — State APA

- **§ 24-4-101 through 24-4-108** — General provisions, definitions
- **§ 24-4-103** — Rulemaking procedure
- **§ 24-4-105** — Adjudicatory proceedings; ALJ practice; final
  agency action
- **§ 24-4-106** — Judicial review of final agency action

## How to (re-)pull

```bash
python3 scripts/pull_co_statutes.py \
  --out plugins/co-court-docs/skills/co-law-references/references/co-statutes-debt \
  --workers 4
```

Requires `poppler` (for `pdftotext`):

```bash
brew install poppler
```

The puller downloads six title PDFs (Titles 4, 5, 6, 13, 14, 24)
from `content.leg.colorado.gov`, converts each with
`pdftotext -layout`, and slices out the configured articles
using the `ARTICLE N` headers that delimit each article in the
official text.

When the General Assembly publishes a new annual edition
(`crs<YYYY>-title-<NN>.pdf`), update the `PDF_URL` constant in
`scripts/pull_co_statutes.py` and re-run.
