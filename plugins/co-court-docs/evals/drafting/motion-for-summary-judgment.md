# co-draft-motion — Summary judgment under C.R.C.P. 56 + *Warne v. Hall* standard

## Prompt

I'm defending against a debt-collection case in Colorado
District Court and I think the plaintiff can't prove they
own the debt because their evidence is just a generic
affidavit. I want to file a motion for summary judgment.
How do I structure it, what's the legal standard, and what
do I attach?

## Expected triggers

- `co-draft-motion`
- `co-fact-check`
- `co-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### Legal standard

- [ ] States the **C.R.C.P. 56** summary-judgment
      standard: no genuine issue of material fact +
      moving party entitled to judgment as a matter of
      law — reads the current rule text from the corpus
      rather than paraphrasing from memory
- [ ] Cites the Colorado plausibility-era case that
      governs how courts evaluate evidence on summary
      judgment — notes that the controlling Colorado
      authority should be **verified against `key-cases.md`**
      rather than asserted from memory
- [ ] Correctly notes that the **non-moving party**
      bears the burden of producing evidence once the
      movant makes a prima facie showing (distinguishing
      Colorado's framework from the federal
      *Celotex* framework)

### Motion structure (C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15)

- [ ] Organizes the motion: introduction (relief
      sought + standard), statement of undisputed
      material facts (numbered, with record cites),
      argument, conclusion
- [ ] Stays within the **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15** page
      limit — reads the current limit from the corpus
      rather than asserting a number from memory
- [ ] No Certificate of Conferral (C.R.C.P. 56 is
      a dispositive motion — conferral not required
      under C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15(8))

### Supporting declaration + exhibits

- [ ] Attaches a **C.R.S. § 13-27-104 unsworn
      declaration** (or notarized affidavit) with
      exhibits establishing the factual record; flags
      that evidence must be admissible in form

### Proposed order

- [ ] Includes a **separate proposed order** granting
      summary judgment — not embedded in the motion
      (cross-reference `co-draft-order`)

## Common failure modes

- Includes a Certificate of Conferral on a dispositive
  motion (conferral not required)
- Buries the proposed order inside the motion
- Asserts the summary-judgment standard from memory
  without citing or verifying the controlling Colorado
  authority
- Confuses C.R.C.P. 56 with C.R.C.P. 12(b)(5)
  (failure to state a claim)
