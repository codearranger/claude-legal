# co-draft-motion — C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15 motion to compel with conferral certificate

## Prompt

The debt buyer suing me in Colorado district court blew off my
interrogatories and requests for production — boilerplate objections
and no documents. I want to file a motion to compel. Draft it for me
the right way — I've heard Colorado makes you talk to the other side
first and there are page limits.

## Expected triggers

- `co-draft-motion`
- `co-discovery`
- `co-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### Conferral (C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15(8))

- [ ] Requires a real **conferral** attempt before filing and
      includes a **Certificate of Conferral** under
      **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15(8)** stating when and how the movant
      conferred and the opposing party's position
- [ ] Notes the discovery-dispute conferral expectation under
      **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-12** and that the court will not entertain
      a motion to compel without a credible conferral effort

### Motion structure and page limits

- [ ] Builds the motion under **C.R.C.P. 37(a)** with the specific
      deficiencies identified per request, the requests and
      responses attached as exhibits, and the **C.R.C.P. 26(b)(5)**
      particularity requirement quoted against boilerplate
      objections
- [ ] Stays within the **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15(1)** page limits
      (commonly framed as 15 pages for a motion or response, 10 for
      a reply) — read the current limits from the skill references
      rather than asserting them from memory
- [ ] Notes the briefing clock (response and reply windows under
      C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15) by reading the day counts from the
      corpus via `co-deadlines`
- [ ] Requests **C.R.C.P. 37(a)(5)** expenses/fees relief

### Composition

- [ ] Caption per the Colorado **flexible caption** with the COURT
      USE ONLY box (`co-statewide-format`); proposed order drafted
      as a **separate** document (`co-draft-order`)
- [ ] Self-represented signature block with no Atty. Reg. #

## Common failure modes

- Files the motion with no Certificate of Conferral (or treats
  conferral as optional)
- Asserts the page limits or the response/reply day counts from
  memory instead of the corpus
- Buries the proposed order inside the motion instead of a separate
  document
- Cites FRCP 37 instead of C.R.C.P. 37
