# co-draft-note — Colorado scheduling notices (the court sets; parties do not self-schedule)

## Prompt

My motion in Colorado district court has been fully briefed for
weeks and nothing is happening. In Washington I would just note it
for the motion docket. What's the Colorado equivalent — do I file
some kind of notice to get a hearing date, and what does it look
like?

## Expected triggers

- `co-draft-note`
- `co-schedule-hearing`
- `co-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### The Colorado scheduling model

- [ ] Explains that in Colorado civil practice **the court issues a
      Notice of Setting** after a motion is fully briefed — parties
      do **not self-schedule** the way Washington's "Note for Motion
      Docket" works
- [ ] Routes the user to the correct party-side action: a
      **chambers / judicial-assistant email** (or division-clerk
      call) requesting a setting, per the assigned judge's practice
      standards (cross-reference `co-schedule-hearing`)

### The notices a party actually files

- [ ] Identifies the party-filed variants this skill drafts —
      Notice of Hearing (echoing a court-set hearing to the
      parties), Notice of Filing, Notice of Withdrawal /
      Substitution of Counsel — and drafts the one that fits
- [ ] The drafted notice carries the case caption, the motion being
      set, the date/time/division/courtroom as set by the court,
      and a certificate of service

### Composition

- [ ] Caption per the Colorado flexible caption with the COURT USE
      ONLY box (`co-statewide-format`)
- [ ] Reads per-judicial-district scheduling contact practice from
      the skill references rather than inventing chambers emails or
      local rule numbers

## Common failure modes

- Imports another state's self-scheduling model (e.g., a Washington
  "Note for Motion Docket" or a noting date) into Colorado
- Drafts a "Notice of Setting" as if the party issues it rather
  than the court
- Invents a chambers email address or a local-rule citation instead
  of reading the per-JD practice from the references
