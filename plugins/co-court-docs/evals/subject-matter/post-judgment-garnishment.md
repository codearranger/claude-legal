# co-post-judgment — C.R.S. § 13-54.5 garnishment + expanded SB22-086 exemptions

## Prompt

A Colorado court entered a judgment against me two months
ago. Now the creditor is trying to garnish my wages and
bank account. I own a house and have some personal
property. What can they actually take, and how do I claim
my exemptions?

## Expected triggers

- `co-post-judgment`
- `co-first-30-days`

## Acceptance criteria

### Garnishment procedure

- [ ] Explains the **C.R.S. § 13-54.5 garnishment
      regime**: the creditor obtains a writ of
      garnishment from the court clerk; the writ is
      served on the employer (wages) or bank (account);
      the debtor receives a notice of exemption rights
      — reads the current procedural steps from the
      references corpus rather than asserting them from
      memory
- [ ] States the applicable **federal and state wage-
      garnishment caps** — reads the current limits
      from the corpus (Colorado generally cannot exceed
      the CCPA-garnishment federal cap at 15 U.S.C.
      §§ 1671-1677 from the shared `federal-debt-laws/`
      corpus)

### Colorado exemptions (SB22-086 / C.R.S. § 13-54)

- [ ] Identifies the key Colorado exemptions updated
      by **SB22-086**, reading the current dollar
      amounts from the **C.R.S. § 13-54** corpus
      rather than asserting fixed figures from memory —
      must flag:
      - **Homestead exemption** (the substantially
        increased amount after SB22-086, up to $250,000
        — read the current figure from the corpus)
      - **Vehicle exemption** (read current limit from
        the corpus)
      - **Personal-property exemptions** (household
        goods, tools of trade — read current limits)
- [ ] Notes that exemptions must be **claimed** by the
      debtor — they are not automatic; explains how
      to file the exemption claim with the court

### Supplemental proceedings / C.R.C.P. 69

- [ ] Notes the creditor's right to conduct
      **C.R.C.P. 69 supplemental proceedings**
      (debtor exam) to identify non-exempt assets

## Common failure modes

- Asserts exemption dollar amounts from memory
  instead of reading post-SB22-086 corpus figures
- Tells the debtor exemptions apply automatically
  without a claim filing
- Ignores the federal wage-garnishment caps in the
  shared `federal-debt-laws/` corpus
- Describes the older, lower pre-SB22-086 homestead
  exemption amount
