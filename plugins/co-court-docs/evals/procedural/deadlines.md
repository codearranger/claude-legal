# co-deadlines — C.R.C.P. 6 time computation + C.R.S. § 24-11-101 Colorado holidays

## Prompt

I have a deadline in a Colorado civil case and I'm trying
to count it correctly. My response to a motion is due 21
days after service by mail. The 21st day falls on a Monday
that I think might be a Colorado state holiday — Cabrini
Day or something. How do I count this properly?

## Expected triggers

- `co-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### C.R.C.P. 6(a) computation

- [ ] Explains **C.R.C.P. 6(a)**: exclude day zero,
      count forward, roll to the next court day if the
      last day is a Saturday, Sunday, or legal holiday —
      reads the current rule text from the references
      corpus rather than summarizing from memory
- [ ] Applies the **mail add-on** (C.R.C.P. 6(e) /
      applicable rule) for service by mail — reads the
      current additional-day count from the corpus rather
      than asserting a number from memory

### Colorado legal holidays (C.R.S. § 24-11-101)

- [ ] Recites the Colorado holiday list by reading from
      the **C.R.S. § 24-11-101** corpus entry — must
      include:
      - **Frances Xavier Cabrini Day** (1st Monday in
        October; replaced Columbus Day in 2020 by H.B.
        20-1031)
      - **Juneteenth** (June 19; added 2022 by SB22-228)
- [ ] Correctly identifies which Monday in October is
      Cabrini Day and whether the user's deadline falls
      on that day — does NOT assert a specific calendar
      date as definitively Cabrini Day without checking

### Named-rule catalog

- [ ] Demonstrates awareness of the **named-rule
      deadline catalog** (answer due, motion-response
      window, summary-judgment service, SOL chart) and
      reads the current day counts from the corpus for
      any rule it invokes
- [ ] Notes the **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15** briefing clock
      (response/reply windows) by reading the corpus

## Common failure modes

- Asserts day counts for response windows from memory
  instead of the corpus
- Misidentifies Cabrini Day as Columbus Day
- Forgets Juneteenth as a Colorado state holiday
- Fails to apply the mail add-on when service was by mail
- States a specific calendar date as definitively a
  holiday without reading the corpus
