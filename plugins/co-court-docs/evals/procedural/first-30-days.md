# co-first-30-days — 21-day answer + C.R.C.P. 12(b) triage + *Warne v. Hall* plausibility

## Prompt

I just got served with a civil complaint in Colorado District
Court. I think the complaint barely says anything and doesn't
really state a claim. What are my options in the first few
weeks — can I move to dismiss, and if I answer instead, how
do I preserve my defenses?

## Expected triggers

- `co-first-30-days`
- `co-deadlines`
- `co-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### Answer deadline

- [ ] States the Colorado answer window: **21 days** for
      in-state service; reads the out-of-state and
      publication-service variants from the references
      corpus via `co-deadlines` rather than asserting day
      counts from memory
- [ ] Applies **C.R.C.P. 6(a)** weekend/holiday roll-forward
      and notes the C.R.S. § 24-11-101 holidays (including
      Frances Xavier Cabrini Day and Juneteenth)

### Pre-answer motion (C.R.C.P. 12(b))

- [ ] Identifies the **C.R.C.P. 12(b)(5) motion to dismiss**
      for failure to state a claim as the pre-answer vehicle
      and explains its effect on the answer deadline (read
      current tolling mechanics from the corpus)
- [ ] States the **Colorado plausibility pleading standard**
      under ***Warne v. Hall*, 2016 CO 50** — the Colorado
      Supreme Court adopted Twombly/Iqbal-style plausibility
      pleading — noting the holding should be
      **read/verified** from `key-cases.md` rather than
      asserted from memory

### Answer and affirmative defenses

- [ ] States affirmative defenses must be pleaded in the
      answer or they are **waived** (C.R.C.P. 8(c)) —
      including statute of limitations, payment, accord and
      satisfaction, and lack of standing
- [ ] Notes **compulsory counterclaims** must be raised
      (read the rule from the corpus)

### County-court distinction

- [ ] Notes that if the claim is ≤ $25,000 it may be in
      **county court** rather than district court, with the
      simplified **C.R.C.P. 311** procedure — reads the
      current jurisdictional threshold from the references
      corpus rather than asserting a dollar figure from
      memory

## Common failure modes

- Asserts a 30-day answer window (Colorado is 21 days)
- States the plausibility standard from memory without
  citing or verifying *Warne v. Hall*
- Omits affirmative-defense waiver warning
- Confuses C.R.C.P. 12(b)(5) (failure to state a claim)
  with C.R.C.P. 12(b)(1) (subject-matter jurisdiction)
