# co-discovery — C.R.C.P. 26–37 + 25-interrogatory cap + conferral requirement

## Prompt

I'm in a Colorado civil case and I want to send discovery
to the other side. How many interrogatories can I send?
What are my options for other discovery tools, and what
happens if they don't respond?

## Expected triggers

- `co-discovery`
- `co-draft-motion`

## Acceptance criteria

### Interrogatory cap

- [ ] States the **C.R.C.P. 33** presumptive limit of
      **25 interrogatories** (including subparts) without
      leave of court — reads the current number from the
      references corpus rather than asserting from memory
- [ ] Notes that exceeding the presumptive cap requires
      either **leave of court** or a stipulation, and
      identifies the criteria the court applies

### Full discovery toolkit

- [ ] Identifies all available discovery tools and their
      governing rules: interrogatories (C.R.C.P. 33),
      requests for production (C.R.C.P. 34), requests for
      admission (C.R.C.P. 36 — reads the RFA presumptive
      limit from the corpus), depositions (C.R.C.P. 30/31),
      and initial disclosures under C.R.C.P. 26(a)(1)
- [ ] Notes the **C.R.C.P. 26(b)** proportionality
      standard for scope

### Meet-and-confer + motion to compel

- [ ] States that a motion to compel requires a real
      **conferral attempt** under **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-12**
      and a **Certificate of Conferral** under
      **C.R.C.P. 121 § 1-15(8)** — the court will not
      entertain the motion without it
- [ ] Notes the court may award **fees and expenses**
      under C.R.C.P. 37(a)(5) on a granted motion to
      compel

### County-court distinction

- [ ] Notes that county-court cases (≤ $25,000) proceed
      under the **simplified C.R.C.P. 311** discovery
      regime — no C.R.C.P. 26(a)(1) initial disclosures;
      reads the scope from the corpus

## Common failure modes

- Asserts the interrogatory limit as 30 or 40 (FRCP
  number) instead of the Colorado 25
- Fails to mention the conferral + Certificate of
  Conferral requirement for a motion to compel
- Ignores the county-court simplified discovery
  distinction
- Cites FRCP rules instead of C.R.C.P. counterparts
