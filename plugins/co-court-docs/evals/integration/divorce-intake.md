# Integration — Colorado divorce intake: UDMA + parenting plan + financial disclosures

## Prompt

My spouse and I have decided to divorce. We've lived in
Colorado for two years. We have two kids (ages 6 and 9),
a house we both own, two cars, and retirement accounts.
I've heard Colorado requires financial disclosure forms.
Walk me through the full process from filing to
decree — what do I file, what forms are mandatory, how
are the kids handled, and what are the key deadlines?

## Expected triggers

- `co-family-law`
- `co-deadlines`
- `co-file-packet`
- `co-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### Residency + filing (co-family-law)

- [ ] Confirms Colorado's **91-day residency
      requirement** under C.R.S. § 14-10-106 is
      satisfied (two years is well above 91 days)
      and notes the **91-day waiting period** before
      a decree can enter — reads both thresholds from
      the corpus rather than asserting days from memory
- [ ] Identifies the petition as the opening filing
      under the **UDMA (C.R.S. art. 10 of title 14)**
      and notes both parties may file a Joint Petition
      for an uncontested dissolution

### C.R.C.P. 16.2 financial disclosures (mandatory)

- [ ] Flags the mandatory **C.R.C.P. 16.2 Sworn
      Financial Statement (JDF 1111)** — exchange is
      required within **42 days** of initial filing
      (reads the current deadline from the corpus);
      both parties must exchange it
- [ ] Notes that failing to exchange the JDF 1111 can
      result in sanctions; the court relies on it for
      property division and maintenance analysis

### Property division

- [ ] States Colorado is an **equitable-distribution**
      state — reads the C.R.S. § 14-10-113 factors
      from the corpus; notes the house and retirement
      accounts are likely marital property subject
      to division
- [ ] Flags the **QDRO requirement** for retirement
      accounts (specialized document to divide without
      tax penalty)

### Parental responsibilities + child support

- [ ] Requires a **permanent parenting plan or agreed
      allocation order** with decision-making and
      parenting-time components; routes to C.R.S.
      § 14-10-124 best-interest factors (read from
      the corpus)
- [ ] Directs computation of child support via the
      **JDF 1820 E worksheet** under the C.R.S.
      § 14-10-115 income-shares guideline; notes the
      **93-overnight adjustment** if parenting time
      is substantial

### Formatting + CCEFS filing

- [ ] All filings carry the two-block Colorado
      caption (C.R.C.P. 10 + CJD 11-01) —
      "District Court, [County] County, State of
      Colorado" for a district court dissolution
      (Family Division if the county has one)
- [ ] Petitioner e-files through **CCEFS** with the
      correct document codes for petition, financial
      disclosure, and proposed parenting plan —
      reads per-JD CCEFS filing practice from the
      skill references

## Common failure modes

- States the waiting period as 60 days instead of 91
- Omits the mandatory C.R.C.P. 16.2 / JDF 1111
  financial disclosure requirement
- Describes Colorado as a community-property state
- Uses "custody" / "visitation" terminology instead
  of UDMA's "parental responsibilities" /
  "parenting time"
- Fails to flag the QDRO for retirement accounts
- Asserts financial-disclosure deadlines from memory
  instead of the corpus
