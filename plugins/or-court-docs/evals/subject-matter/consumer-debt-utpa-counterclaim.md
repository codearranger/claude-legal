# or-consumer-debt — Oregon UTPA counterclaim

## Prompt

The debt buyer made false statements about the amount I
owed and sued me past the SOL. Can I counterclaim under
Oregon law?

## Expected triggers

- `or-consumer-debt`

## Acceptance criteria

### Identifies UTPA as the Oregon vehicle

- [ ] Cites **ORS 646.605 et seq.** (UTPA)
- [ ] Explains UTPA covers **any person** in trade or
      commerce — broader than FDCPA which covers only
      "debt collectors"
- [ ] Identifies specific UTPA subsections:
  - **ORS 646.607(3)** — demanding payment not legally owed
  - **ORS 646.608(1)(s)** — false representations
  - **ORS 646.608(1)(u)** — unfair or deceptive conduct

### Damages and remedies

- [ ] Cites **ORS 646.638**:
  - Actual damages OR $200 statutory minimum
  - Punitive damages available
  - Equitable relief (declaration, injunction)
  - **Mandatory attorney fees** under ORS 646.638(3)
- [ ] Notes **no $1,000 cap** like FDCPA
- [ ] References **1-year SOL** from discovery (ORS
      646.638(6))

### Pair with FDCPA

- [ ] Suggests pleading FDCPA AND UTPA in parallel
      counterclaims
- [ ] Notes that the same conduct often violates both
- [ ] Identifies advantages of UTPA over FDCPA in Oregon
      practice (no cap; broader equitable relief)

### Ascertainable loss

- [ ] References **ORS 646.638(1)** ascertainable-loss
      requirement
- [ ] Notes the broad interpretation under *Denson* and
      related cases
- [ ] Suggests damages categories (time, out-of-pocket
      expenses, etc.)

## Common failure modes

- Suggesting only FDCPA without UTPA
- Treating UTPA as having the same $1,000 cap as FDCPA
- Forgetting the mandatory ORS 646.638(3) fees
- Missing the 1-year SOL trap
- Generic UTPA advice without specific subsection cites
