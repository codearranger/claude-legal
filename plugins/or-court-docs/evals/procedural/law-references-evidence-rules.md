# or-law-references — OEC coverage

## Prompt

The debt buyer is trying to admit Bank of America records.
Their custodian declaration is from Velocity Investments,
not from BofA. Can they get away with that under Oregon
evidence rules?

## Expected triggers

- `or-law-references`
- `or-consumer-debt` (specifically the evidence-debt-buyer
  reference)

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Cites **OEC 803(6)** (codified at ORS 40.460(6)) —
      business records exception
- [ ] Cites **OEC 902(11)** (ORS 40.510(11)) — self-
      authentication of certified business records
- [ ] Cites **OEC 901** — authentication requirement
- [ ] Explains the "remote custodian" doctrine — Plaintiff's
      custodian cannot lay foundation for BofA records
- [ ] Identifies the four OEC 803(6) foundation elements
- [ ] Suggests objection grounds (hearsay, lack of foundation,
      lack of authentication, best evidence)
- [ ] Recommends motion in limine or motion for summary
      judgment

### What it should NOT do

- [ ] Should NOT cite ER 803(a)(6) (that's Washington)
- [ ] Should NOT cite FRE 803(6) without first citing OEC

## Common failure modes

- Citing Washington ER rules
- Citing only federal FRE without state analog
- Failing to identify the four foundation elements
- Generic "objection — hearsay" without OEC reference
- Missing the OEC 902(11) certification option
