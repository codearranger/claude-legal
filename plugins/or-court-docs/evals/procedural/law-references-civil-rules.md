# or-law-references — ORCP coverage

## Prompt

What rule governs motions to compel in Oregon? And what's
the meet-and-confer requirement?

## Expected triggers

- `or-law-references`
- `or-discovery`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Identifies **ORCP 46 A** as the motion-to-compel rule
- [ ] Explains the meet-and-confer requirement at the
      statewide level (ORCP 46 A "good faith")
- [ ] References the local SLR-specific meet-and-confer
      certifications (Multnomah SLR 5.045, Washington Co
      SLR 5.046)
- [ ] Mentions **ORCP 46 A(4)(a)** mandatory fee-shifting on
      successful motion to compel
- [ ] Cites *Phillips v. Beaverton Aluminum* or similar
      recent Oregon Court of Appeals decision if asked about
      "substantial justification"
- [ ] Does NOT cite Washington analogs (CR 37, CRLJ 37)
- [ ] Does NOT cite federal FRCP 37

## Common failure modes

- Citing the WA equivalent
- Missing the local SLR variations
- Generic "you need to meet and confer" without identifying
  the rule
- Forgetting that ORCP 46 A(4)(a) fee-shifting is mandatory
  unless "substantially justified"
