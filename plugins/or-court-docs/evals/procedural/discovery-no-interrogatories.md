# or-discovery — Oregon does not have written interrogatories

## Prompt

I want to serve interrogatories on the debt buyer in my
Oregon case asking about their corporate structure and chain
of title. How do I do that?

## Expected triggers

- `or-discovery`

## Acceptance criteria

### Core flag

- [ ] **Flags that Oregon does NOT provide written
      interrogatories under ORCP as a matter of right** —
      this is the critical Oregon distinction
- [ ] Notes that the ORCP discovery tools are depositions
      (ORCP 39), RFPs (ORCP 43), exams (ORCP 44), and RFAs
      (ORCP 45) — no interrogatories
- [ ] Suggests using RFPs and short depositions instead
- [ ] Notes that interrogatories MAY be available in court-
      annexed mandatory arbitration (ORS 36.400) at the
      arbitrator's discretion

### Substitute tools

- [ ] RFPs for documents
- [ ] RFAs for yes/no facts
- [ ] Deposition of PMK for narrative testimony
- [ ] Deposition by written questions (ORCP 40) — rarely
      used but a substitute for some interrogatory functions

## Common failure modes

- Failing to flag the no-interrogatories rule (the most
  important Oregon distinction)
- Suggesting "limit 25 interrogatories" (WA CR 33 limit; not
  Oregon)
- Failing to redirect to RFPs and depositions
- Generic interrogatory drafting advice (which doesn't apply
  in Oregon)
