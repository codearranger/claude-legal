# or-first-30-days — Initial response

## Prompt

I was just served with a summons and complaint by a debt
buyer in Oregon. The complaint is in Multnomah Circuit Court.
The debt is allegedly from a Bank of America credit card. I
don't recognize the account number. What do I do?

## Expected triggers

- `or-first-30-days`
- Likely `or-consumer-debt` (debt-buyer fact pattern)
- `or-pro-se`
- `or-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### Timing

- [ ] Identifies the 30-day ORCP 7 C(2) answer deadline
- [ ] Encourages immediate calendaring
- [ ] References ORCP 10 A end-of-period rule

### Triage

- [ ] Recommends preserving evidence (photographing
      summons, complaint, envelope)
- [ ] Identifies the three response options: ORCP 21 motion,
      Answer with defenses + counterclaims, or stipulated
      extension

### Defense inventory

- [ ] References affirmative defenses (SOL ORS 12.080,
      lack of standing, ORS 697.105 lack of capacity)
- [ ] Specifically flags ORS 697.015 registration
      requirement and the DCBS lookup
- [ ] Mentions OEC 803(6) authentication issues for
      debt-buyer cases

### Counterclaim inventory

- [ ] FDCPA (15 USC § 1692 et seq.)
- [ ] Oregon UTPA (ORS 646.605 et seq.)
- [ ] ORS 697.085 (if Plaintiff is unregistered)

### Process

- [ ] Recommends drafting both the Answer + Counterclaims AND
      first RFPs targeting chain of title
- [ ] Recommends QC and fact-check before filing
- [ ] Recommends eFile via File and Serve

### What NOT to do

- [ ] Does NOT recommend missing the 30-day deadline
- [ ] Does NOT cite WA-specific rules
- [ ] Does NOT recommend "interrogatories" as a discovery tool

## Common failure modes

- Generic "answer the complaint" advice without defense
  inventory
- Missing the 30-day deadline computation
- Forgetting to flag the ORS 697.105 capacity defense
- Suggesting interrogatories (not available in Oregon ORCP)
- Conflating with WA-style debt-buyer defense (e.g., CR 12
  instead of ORCP 21)
