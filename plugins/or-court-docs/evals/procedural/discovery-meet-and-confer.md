# or-discovery — Meet-and-confer protocol

## Prompt

The opposing party objected to all my RFPs without producing
anything. I want to file a motion to compel in Multnomah. What
do I need to do before filing?

## Expected triggers

- `or-discovery`
- `or-multcc`

## Acceptance criteria

### Meet-and-confer requirement

- [ ] Identifies ORCP 46 A "good faith" meet-and-confer
      requirement
- [ ] Identifies Multnomah SLR 5.045 — specific certification
      requirement
- [ ] Lists the required elements of the certification:
  - Specific identification of disputed RFPs
  - Date and method of conference
  - Opposing party's position
  - Inability to resolve

### Process recommendation

- [ ] Recommends a written meet-and-confer letter FIRST
      identifying each disputed item
- [ ] Recommends a phone call after the letter
- [ ] Recommends a follow-up email memorializing the call
- [ ] Suggests 14-day deadline for opposing party to
      supplement
- [ ] Cautions against filing motion too quickly (give
      opposing 7–14 days)

### What NOT to do

- [ ] Does NOT recommend immediate motion without M&C
- [ ] Does NOT cite WA CR 26(i) or KCDC procedure
- [ ] Does NOT skip the SLR-specific certification

## Common failure modes

- Generic "meet and confer" advice without ORCP 46 A and
  Multnomah SLR 5.045 specifics
- Missing the certification format
- Allowing the motion without prior written record
- Conflating with WA's CR 26(i) framework
