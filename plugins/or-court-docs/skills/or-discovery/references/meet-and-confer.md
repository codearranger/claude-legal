# Meet and Confer — Oregon Discovery

## Why this matters

ORCP 46 A requires that a moving party "in good faith have
conferred or attempted to confer" with the opposing party
before filing a motion to compel. Local SLRs (Multnomah 5.045,
Washington Co 5.046) add detailed certification requirements.

A defective or missing meet-and-confer is the most common reason
Oregon judges deny motions to compel without prejudice. The
remedy is to confer (or confer again) and re-file — pure delay.

## Required elements of a good meet-and-confer

1. **Specific identification of each disputed item**
   ("RFP No. 3" not "your discovery responses")
2. **Statement of the moving party's position** — why the
   request is appropriate
3. **Inquiry into the opposing party's position** — what would
   resolve their concern
4. **Documented attempt to resolve** — proposed alternatives,
   timeline for supplementation
5. **A response from the opposing party** (or a documented
   non-response)
6. **A statement of impasse**

If any element is missing, the certification is defective.

## Communication channels

In order of preference for Oregon practice:

1. **Phone call followed by email summary** — strongest record
2. **Email exchange** — clear written record but slower
3. **Letter (USPS or via File and Serve)** — formal but slow
4. **In-person conference** — most thorough but logistically
   hard

The pattern: phone call to discuss substance, immediate
follow-up email to memorialize. This combines the efficiency of
real-time discussion with the documentation of writing.

## Sample sequence

### Day 1 — Initial email

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Meet and Confer — Discovery, Case No. 25CV12345

Counsel:

I have reviewed your responses to my First Requests for
Production served [date]. I believe we have disagreements
about RFP Nos. 3, 5, and 6 that we should attempt to resolve
under ORCP 46 A and Multnomah SLR 5.045.

Specifically:

1.  RFP No. 3 (cardholder agreement). You objected on grounds
    of relevance, burden, and "documents not in Plaintiff's
    possession." I disagree because [reasons]. Plaintiff
    alleges in its Complaint that it owns this account; the
    cardholder agreement is the foundational document for that
    allegation.

2.  RFP No. 5 (assignment schedule). You objected on grounds
    of "trade secret." A protective order under ORCP 36 C is
    available to protect any genuine trade-secret concerns.
    Please identify the specific trade-secret concern and the
    protective-order scope you would accept.

3.  RFP No. 6 (account history). You objected on grounds of
    "burden" — please quantify (number of documents,
    estimated hours).

I am available for a phone call this week to discuss. Please
let me know a time that works for you. If we cannot resolve
these within 14 days, by [date], I will be required to move
to compel under ORCP 46 A.

Best,
[Name][, pro se]
[Phone]
```

### Day 3 — Phone call

Schedule a 30-minute call. During the call:

- Walk through each disputed item one at a time
- Ask the opposing party to clarify their position
- Propose alternatives (narrower time scope, sample subset,
  protective order)
- Take notes — date, time, who said what, agreed-upon next
  steps
- Set a deadline for supplemental response

### Day 3 — Follow-up email

Immediately after the call:

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Re: Meet and Confer — Discovery, Case No. 25CV12345

Counsel:

Thank you for the call today, [day, time].

To confirm what we discussed:

1.  RFP No. 3 (cardholder agreement). You agreed to supplement
    the response within 21 days, by [date], producing the
    cardholder agreement and incorporated terms and conditions.
    Thank you.

2.  RFP No. 5 (assignment schedule). We agreed to negotiate a
    protective order. I will send a draft within 5 days. The
    documents will be produced within 7 days after the
    protective order is entered.

3.  RFP No. 6 (account history). You declined to supplement.
    You stated [opposing party's reasons]. We remain at
    impasse on this request.

If I have mis-recorded any aspect of our call, please let me
know within 3 business days. Otherwise, I will rely on this
summary in any subsequent motion practice.

Best,
[Name]
```

The opposing party's silence in response to this email is a
**tacit confirmation** of the summary. If they later dispute
the contents at a hearing, you have a contemporaneous record.

### Day 14 — Verification

If the agreed supplementation has occurred, the dispute is
resolved. If not, send a final email:

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Re: Meet and Confer — Final Notice

Counsel:

The deadline we agreed to for supplemental response on RFP
No. 3 (cardholder agreement) was [date]. I have not received
supplemental documents.

Per our [date] meet-and-confer email exchange (attached), I
will move to compel under ORCP 46 A within the next 7 days
unless I receive supplementation by [date + 7].

Best,
[Name]
```

### Day 21 — File the motion if no resolution

By this point you have:

- Initial email identifying the dispute
- Phone call notes
- Follow-up email memorializing the call
- A final-notice email
- Opposing party's response (or non-response) at each stage

The meet-and-confer certification in the motion to compel
recites this sequence.

## Pro se modifications

Pro se filers without opposing counsel as a phone-call partner
should:

- Use email exclusively if the opposing party will not accept
  calls
- Send letters by USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt as a
  belt-and-suspenders backup
- Document every attempt — even if the opposing party never
  responds, "attempted to confer" satisfies ORCP 46 A

The standard for "good faith" attempt is **objective**, not
subjective. A reasonable judge should be able to look at the
written record and say "yes, this party tried to resolve this
without court intervention."

## Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Single email without follow-up | Send phone-call summary; create written record |
| Vague "we have discovery disputes" | Identify each request by number |
| No proposed timeline | Set 14-day default; show specific dates |
| Letter only, no phone | Phone tends to resolve faster; email + phone is the bar |
| Skipping the meet-and-confer because "it'll be denied" | The court can deny the underlying motion for the missed step alone |
| Going to motion the same day as the meet-and-confer | Looks bad-faith; wait at least 7 days for response |
| Compound meet-and-confer (multiple discovery battles bundled) | Split into separate emails for cleaner record |

## Certification template

Drop into the motion to compel:

```
                CERTIFICATION OF MEET AND CONFER
                  (Multnomah SLR 5.045 / ORCP 46 A)

Pursuant to Multnomah SLR 5.045 and ORCP 46 A, counsel for
Defendant certifies that:

1.  On [date 1], Defendant served First Requests for Production
    Nos. 1–6 on Plaintiff. (Decl. ¶ 2; Ex. 1.)

2.  On [date 2], Plaintiff served Responses, objecting on
    grounds of [grounds] without producing responsive
    documents as to RFP Nos. 3, 5, and 6. (Decl. ¶ 3; Ex. 2.)

3.  On [date 3], Defendant sent Plaintiff a meet-and-confer
    letter (Decl. ¶ 4; Ex. 3) identifying the specific
    objections, stating Defendant's position, and requesting
    supplementation within 14 days.

4.  On [date 4], Defendant and Plaintiff's counsel spoke by
    telephone for approximately 30 minutes. (Decl. ¶ 5.)
    Defendant memorialized the call by email the same day.
    (Decl. ¶ 5; Ex. 4.)

5.  On [date 5], Plaintiff partially supplemented (producing
    documents responsive to RFP Nos. 1, 2, and 4) but
    maintained objections to Nos. 3, 5, and 6. (Decl. ¶ 6;
    Ex. 5.)

6.  On [date 6], Defendant sent a final-notice email
    identifying the remaining disputes. (Decl. ¶ 7; Ex. 6.)

7.  As of the filing of this Motion, Plaintiff has not
    supplemented as to RFP Nos. 3, 5, and 6. The parties are
    at impasse.

Defendant respectfully requests an order compelling production
under ORCP 46 A and an award of reasonable expenses under
ORCP 46 A(4)(a).
```

This certification, with its supporting declaration and
exhibits, satisfies SLR 5.045 / ORCP 46 A on its face.
