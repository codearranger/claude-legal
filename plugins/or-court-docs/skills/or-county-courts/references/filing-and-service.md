# Filing and Service — Oregon County Quirks

## Statewide baseline

Every Oregon circuit court uses:

- **OJD File and Serve** (Tyler Tech) for eFiling — mandatory
  for represented parties; pro se parties may register and use
  it or file at the counter
- **UTCR 21** — eFiling rules statewide
- **ORCP 9** — service of papers and pleadings (Oregon civil)
- **ORCP 7** — service of summons and complaint
- **Statewide forms** at courts.oregon.gov/forms

The mechanics are the same statewide. What differs county-by-
county is the **local execution**: which clerk receives walk-in
filings when, where working copies go, which judges' chambers
take orders directly vs. via the Civil Division.

## eFiling

### File and Serve portal

- URL: https://oregon.tylertech.cloud/
- Login: Each filer registers an account (free)
- File size: 25 MB per document; split larger filings
- Document codes: Pick from a dropdown matching the UTCR 2.110
  title; if no match, use "Motion (Other)" or "Notice (Other)"
- Filing fees: Paid at submission via credit card, ACH, or
  pre-funded escrow account
- Confirmation: Email confirmation of filing typically within
  a few minutes; clerk acceptance within 1–2 business days

### When eFiling is excused

- Fee waiver pending under ORS 21.682 (file at counter, fee
  deferred)
- Filer lacks technology access — apply for paper-filing
  exemption under UTCR 21.080
- Confidential matters that cannot be eFiled — UTCR 2.140
  (motion to file under seal)

### eService

Per ORCP 9 G(1)(d) and UTCR 21.100, eService through File and
Serve counts as service for registered parties. The receiving
party gets:

- An email notification of the filing
- A link to download the served document
- A timestamp counting as the moment of service

Unregistered parties (most pro se litigants who have not opted
in) must be served by another method under ORCP 9 D (mail, hand
delivery, fax with prior consent).

## Service of initial process (ORCP 7)

The original summons and complaint must be served per ORCP 7,
NOT through File and Serve. Acceptable methods:

- **Personal service** (ORCP 7 D(2)) — hand delivery to the
  defendant
- **Substituted service** (ORCP 7 D(2)(b)) — left at dwelling
  with a competent adult resident, then mailed
- **Office service** (ORCP 7 D(2)(c)) — for businesses, served
  on a registered agent or specified person
- **Service by mail** (ORCP 7 D(2)(d)) — only when the
  defendant consents in writing OR another method has been
  attempted unsuccessfully
- **Service by publication** (ORCP 7 D(6)) — last resort,
  requires court order

A **Return of Service** (proof of service) must be filed within
**63 days** of service per ORCP 7 D(3). If not, the case can be
dismissed for failure to prosecute under ORCP 54.

## Filing fees (statewide schedule)

Per ORS 21.135 et seq. Approximate amounts (verify against
https://www.courts.oregon.gov/services/fees/Pages/default.aspx
— legislature can update annually):

| Filing | Fee (approximate) |
|--------|-------------------|
| Civil complaint > $10,000 | ~$281 |
| Civil complaint $10,000 or less | ~$144 |
| Small claims complaint | ~$57 |
| Civil answer or first appearance | ~$252 |
| Motion (most types) | ~$11 |
| Jury fee (within 30 days of trial) | ~$305 |
| Subpoena issuance fee | varies |

Fee waiver: File the **Application for Deferral or Waiver of
Fees and Court Costs** simultaneously with the underlying
pleading. The court decides on the basis of inability to pay
(ORS 21.682). Pending the decision, the filing is accepted
and the fee deferred.

## Working-copy practice

Working copies are paper copies delivered to chambers for the
judge's review before hearing. Not every county requires them;
not every motion requires them.

Common thresholds (verify against the county's SLR):

| County | Working-copy threshold |
|--------|------------------------|
| Multnomah | Total packet > 25 pages OR judge requests OR summary judgment |
| Washington Co (OR) | Total packet > 15 pages |
| Clackamas | Encouraged for any motion; required for SJ |
| Lane | Per assigned judge's standing order |
| Marion | Required for SJ; otherwise judge-discretion |
| Most rural counties | Typically not required, but courteous |

Working-copy format (general):

- Tabbed (each enclosed document on its own tab — Motion,
  Memo, Decl, Exhibits, Proposed Order)
- Bound (binder clip, staple, or perfect bind depending on
  size)
- Cover with case caption, hearing date, judge name, short
  index
- Reference the case number and hearing date prominently

Delivery: Civil Division clerk's counter OR direct to
courtroom (depends on the county). For mailing, address to:

```
Hon. [Judge Name]
c/o [JA Name or Civil Division]
[County] Circuit Court
[Courthouse Address]
[City], OR [ZIP]

WORKING COPIES — Case No. [N]
Hearing Date: [Date]
```

Use a flat envelope so the packet is not creased. Most pro se
filers send via USPS Priority Mail with tracking.

## County-specific quirks

### Multnomah

- Working copies → Mail room (loading dock) OR courtroom
- Orders to chambers via the JA's email (not the clerk's
  office)
- Civil Self-Help on Floor 3 is unusually robust

### Washington Co (Oregon)

- Working copies → Civil Division counter on 2nd floor
- Lower working-copy threshold (15 pages)
- Notice of Hearing must be filed with the motion (not after)

### Clackamas

- Working copies → Civil counter, 807 Main Street
- Standing orders on individual judges' pages

### Lane

- Multiple courthouses (Eugene main; Florence branch)
- Strong JA practice; check the assigned judge's standing
  order
- Court Administrator's office at 125 E 8th Ave handles
  scheduling that doesn't route through a JA

### Marion

- State-government civil filings concentrated here (Salem)
- Civil Department on Floor 2; Probate / Family on Floor 1
- Marion SLR Chapter 5 is well-developed; pull from
  courts.oregon.gov/courts/marion

### Jackson, Deschutes, Linn, Benton, Yamhill, Polk, Douglas

- Lightly centralized scheduling
- Working-copy thresholds vary
- SLR exists but is lighter than Multnomah/Washington
- Local pro bono and legal-aid organizations often have
  courthouse hours

### Rural counties (Wallowa, Wheeler, Sherman, etc.)

- Centralized motion docket; one judge handles all civil
- Court coordinator schedules
- SLR minimal; UTCR governs almost everything
- Pro se filers welcomed; clerks often informally help with
  procedural questions

## Confirming current addresses

Phones, addresses, and courthouse hours change. Before:

- Mailing working copies
- Driving to the courthouse for a hearing
- Calling Civil Division

… verify against the county's page at
https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/Pages/find-a-court.aspx.
This guide cannot guarantee currency.
