# Oregon Circuit Court Directory — all 36 counties

Oregon has **27 judicial districts** covering its **36 counties**.
Some districts include multiple counties (e.g., the 6th District
is Morrow + Umatilla). Each county has at least one courthouse.

The canonical source for every court's address, phone, and judges
is the OJD "Find a Court" page:

**https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/Pages/find-a-court.aspx**

Pull the current page when you need a definitive contact —
addresses and phones change.

## Judicial districts that cover multiple counties

Some Oregon judicial districts cover more than one county. In a
multi-county district, judges may rotate between courthouses and
civil filings may consolidate at the district seat:

- 1st: Jackson + Josephine
- 6th: Morrow + Umatilla
- 7th: Gilliam, Hood River, Sherman, Wasco, Wheeler (five
  counties; multiple courthouses)
- 10th: Union + Wallowa
- 13th: Klamath + Lake
- 15th: Coos + Curry
- 21st: Benton + Lincoln
- 22nd: Crook + Jefferson
- 24th: Grant + Harney

If your case is filed in a county that shares a district, file
in the venue county under ORS 14.080. Motion practice still
flows through that county's courthouse, even if the assigned
judge sits primarily in another county within the district.

## Small claims and FED in rural counties

Smaller rural counties often combine small claims (≤ $10,000)
and FED dockets with the general civil docket. The same judge
hears all civil matters. Check the local SLR — small claims
procedure in ORS Ch. 46 is the same statewide, but scheduling
differs.

## Languages and access

Court interpreters are available statewide through the OJD
Language Access Services. Request through the assigned court at
least 5 days before hearing. ASL interpreters are also
available; request through Civil Division.

For self-represented parties:

- Every Oregon courthouse posts the **Statewide Self-Help
  Resources** page: courts.oregon.gov/help
- Most county bar associations have a Modest Means Panel or
  Lawyer Referral Service for reduced-fee representation
- Legal Aid Services of Oregon has regional offices (Portland,
  Salem, Eugene, Medford, Klamath Falls, Pendleton, etc.) for
  income-eligible litigants

## Quick reference by county type

- **High volume (Multnomah, Washington, Clackamas, Lane,
  Marion)**: individual-JA scheduling, working copies common,
  more developed SLR
- **Mid volume (Jackson, Deschutes, Linn, Yamhill, Benton,
  Douglas)**: lightly centralized scheduling, working-copy
  thresholds lower, SLR exists but lighter
- **Low volume (Wallowa, Wheeler, Sherman, etc.)**: centralized
  motion docket, no JA-specific scheduling, working copies may
  be encouraged but not required, SLR minimal
