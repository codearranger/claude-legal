# Fees and Costs — Oregon (ORS Ch. 20 + ORCP 68 + ORCP 46)

Oregon, like most American jurisdictions, follows the **American
Rule**: each side bears its own attorney fees unless authorized
by statute, contract, or court rule. *Domingo v. Anderson*, 325
Or 385, 938 P2d 206 (1997).

This reference catalogs the most common bases for fee recovery
in Oregon civil practice and the procedure for claiming them.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Always verify the current ORS — the
> legislature has updated several fee statutes recently.

## Procedural framework

### ORCP 68 — Allowance and recovery of fees

Once the underlying entitlement to fees is established (by
statute, contract, or rule), ORCP 68 governs the **procedure**
for claiming them:

- **ORCP 68 A**: Definitions ("attorney fees" includes
  paralegal fees in many circumstances)
- **ORCP 68 B**: When fees recoverable — by statute, by
  contract, or by rule
- **ORCP 68 C(2)**: **Statement of Attorney Fees** filed within
  **14 days** of judgment (or post-judgment hearing notice)
- **ORCP 68 C(3)**: Objections to the statement within 14 days
- **ORCP 68 C(4)**: Court resolves on the papers or by hearing;
  must enter findings on the requested amount
- **ORCP 68 C(5)**: Factors and reasonableness criteria (see ORS
  20.075)

### Skipping the procedure = waiver

Failure to comply with ORCP 68 C(2)–(4) results in **waiver** of
the fee claim. The 14-day window is short and unforgiving;
calendar it immediately upon judgment.

## Bases for fee recovery

### 1. Statutory fee-shifting

A statute authorizes fees in the action. Examples:

- **ORS 646.638(3)** (UTPA) — prevailing-party fees mandatory
- **ORS 90.255** (landlord-tenant) — prevailing-party fees
- **15 USC § 1692k(a)(3)** (FDCPA) — prevailing-consumer fees
  mandatory (Oregon courts apply federal statutory fees in
  state-court FDCPA actions)
- **ORS 20.190** — statutory prevailing-party fees (mandatory
  $200–$590 depending on case type; this is a flat fee, not a
  full attorney-fee shift)
- **ORS 20.082** — small claims fees ($60–$160 to prevailing
  party)
- **ORS 31.250** — torts arising from frivolous use of legal
  process

### 2. Contractual fee-shifting

The contract contains a fee clause. Two important rules:

- **ORS 20.096** — **Reciprocal fee-shifting**: when a contract
  provides fees to one side only (typically the lender or
  landlord), Oregon law makes the clause reciprocal — the
  prevailing party on either side recovers under the clause
- **Strict construction**: contract fee clauses are construed
  against the drafter; ambiguous clauses don't trigger fees

Many credit-card contracts contain one-sided fee clauses. When
the consumer prevails against the debt buyer (whether by
dismissal, judgment, or favorable settlement), ORS 20.096
sweeps the consumer in as a fee-eligible prevailing party.

### 3. Rule-based fee-shifting

- **ORCP 46 A(4)(a)** — Mandatory fees to prevailing party on
  motion to compel, unless opposing party's failure was
  "substantially justified"
- **ORCP 17 C–E** — Sanctions for unfounded pleadings
- **ORCP 32** — Class action attorney fees
- **ORCP 54 E** — Fees on dismissed claims (limited)
- **ORCP 68 C(5)** — Procedural — once entitled, this is the
  process

### 4. Objectively unreasonable position (ORS 20.105)

The rough analog to CR 11 / FRCP 11 — but on a different theory.
ORS 20.105 authorizes a fee award against any party (or attorney)
who:

- Files a claim, defense, or other position that the court
  finds was made **without an objectively reasonable basis**

The standard is the **objective** reasonableness of the legal
position — not the subjective state of mind. *Mattiza v. Foster*,
311 Or 1, 803 P2d 723 (1990).

ORS 20.105 fees are awardable **against pro se parties** as well
as represented ones — pro se status is not a shield.

## Calculation: the lodestar method

When fees are awarded, Oregon applies the **lodestar** method:

- **Hours reasonably expended** × **reasonable hourly rate** =
  lodestar
- Lodestar may be adjusted up or down for the ORS 20.075 factors

### ORS 20.075 factors (statutory criteria)

When fees are authorized by statute, the court must consider:

- The conduct of the parties in the transaction
- The objective reasonableness of the claims and defenses
- The extent to which an award would deter the conduct
- The objective reasonableness of the parties' settlement
  conduct
- The amount the prevailing party recovered relative to the
  amount sought
- The novelty and difficulty of the questions involved
- The skill required to render the legal services
- Whether acceptance of the case precluded other employment
- The customary fee charged in the locality for similar work
- The fixed or contingent nature of the fee
- The time limitations imposed by the client or circumstances
- The amount involved and the results obtained
- The experience, reputation, and ability of the attorneys
- The undesirability of the case
- The nature and length of the professional relationship
- Awards in similar cases

*Strawn v. Farmers Ins. Co. of Oregon*, 353 Or 210 (2013), is
the leading recent decision on lodestar methodology and the ORS
20.075 factors.

## Pro se fee recovery

A pro se litigant **cannot** recover attorney fees for their own
time spent on the case. The lodestar method requires actual
attorney services. However:

- A pro se litigant CAN recover **costs** under ORS 20.180
- A pro se litigant CAN recover the **statutory prevailing-
  party fee** under ORS 20.190 (the flat $200–$590)
- A pro se litigant who later retains counsel can recover fees
  for the counsel's time

This rule (no fees for self-represented time) parallels federal
law (*Kay v. Ehrler*, 499 US 432 (1991)) and Washington law.

## Cost recovery (ORS 20.020, ORCP 68)

Beyond attorney fees, the prevailing party recovers **costs**:

- Filing fees and reporter fees
- Service of process fees
- Witness fees and mileage
- Deposition transcript costs (necessarily obtained)
- Copying / exhibit production
- Statutory prevailing-party fee (ORS 20.190)

Costs are claimed by **Cost Bill** filed within 14 days of
judgment (ORCP 68 C).

## Sample fee-claim language in a motion

```
VII. RELIEF REQUESTED

Defendant respectfully asks the Court to enter an order:

A.  Granting Defendant's Motion to Compel;

B.  Compelling Plaintiff to produce documents responsive to
    Requests Nos. 1–6 by [date];

C.  Awarding Defendant reasonable expenses, including attorney
    fees, incurred in making this Motion pursuant to ORCP 46
    A(4)(a). Defendant will submit a detailed statement under
    ORCP 68 C(2) within 14 days of the Order on this Motion.

D.  Such other and further relief as the Court deems just and
    proper.
```

## Sample Statement of Attorney Fees (ORCP 68 C(2))

```
            IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF THE STATE OF OREGON
                  FOR THE COUNTY OF MULTNOMAH

[Caption]                       │   Case No. 25CV12345
                                │
                                │   STATEMENT OF ATTORNEY FEES
                                │   AND COSTS UNDER ORCP 68
                                │   C(2)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Defendant submits this Statement of Attorney Fees and Costs
under ORCP 68 C(2), in support of the Court's Order dated
__________, 20__, granting Defendant's Motion to Compel and
awarding fees and costs under ORCP 46 A(4)(a).

I.  SUMMARY

    Defendant claims:
        Attorney fees:  $X,XXX.XX  (XX hours @ $XXX/hour)
        Costs:          $XXX.XX
        Total:          $X,XXX.XX

II. HOURS, TASKS, AND RATES

    [Itemize by date, task, attorney, time, rate, amount —
     usually 1/10 hour increments]

III. ORS 20.075 FACTORS — SHORT NARRATIVE

    A.  Objective reasonableness ...
    B.  Skill required ...
    [etc.]

IV. SUPPORTING DECLARATION

    The Declaration of [Lead Attorney] is filed herewith,
    establishing the credentials, hourly rate, hours, and
    tasks.

V.  COSTS DETAIL

    [Itemize each cost]

DATED this ____ day of __________, 20__.

[Signature, OSB#, etc.]
```

## Common fee-claim pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Missing the 14-day ORCP 68 C(2) deadline | Waiver |
| Failing to address ORS 20.075 factors in the statement | Reduced award |
| Block-billing without task detail | Court may slash hours |
| No supporting declaration | No basis to award |
| Pro se claiming attorney-rate fees | Disallowed |
| Cost claim without itemization | Cost bill may be denied |

## Comparison to Washington

| Concept | Oregon | Washington |
|---------|--------|------------|
| Fees procedure rule | ORCP 68 C(2)–(4) | None — KCLCR 7(b)(11), motion practice |
| Substantive criteria | ORS 20.075 | *Bowers* factors |
| Lodestar method | Yes (Strawn) | Yes (Bowers) |
| Reciprocal contract fees | ORS 20.096 | RCW 4.84.330 |
| Objectively unreasonable | ORS 20.105 | None direct (CR 11 for sanctions only) |
| Sanctions rule | ORCP 17 | CR 11 |
| Motion-to-compel mandatory | ORCP 46 A(4)(a) | CR 37(a)(5) / CRLJ 37(a)(4) |
| Statutory prevailing-party fee | ORS 20.190 ($200–$590) | RCW 4.84.080 ($200) |
