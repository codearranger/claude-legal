# Oregon Evidence Code (OEC) — Reference

The Oregon Evidence Code is codified in **ORS 40.010–40.585**.
The OEC parallels the Federal Rules of Evidence at the section
level (OEC 401 = FRE 401, OEC 803 = FRE 803, etc.), but
substantive rules sometimes diverge — always check the OEC text
rather than relying on FRE knowledge.

Authoritative source:
https://www.oregonlegislature.gov/bills_laws/ors/ors040.html

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify each rule against the current ORS
> before citing.

## Rules at a glance — civil-practice focus

### Article IV — Relevance

- **OEC 401** (ORS 40.150): Definition of relevant evidence
- **OEC 402** (ORS 40.155): Relevant evidence generally
  admissible
- **OEC 403** (ORS 40.160): Exclusion for danger of unfair
  prejudice, confusion, or delay
- **OEC 404** (ORS 40.170): Character evidence; civil cases —
  generally not admissible to prove conduct on a particular
  occasion
- **OEC 406** (ORS 40.180): Habit; routine practice

### Article V — Privileges

- **OEC 501–514** (ORS 40.225–40.295): Various privileges
  (attorney-client, doctor-patient, psychotherapist-patient,
  spousal, clergy-penitent, etc.)
- **OEC 503** (ORS 40.225): Lawyer-client privilege

### Article VI — Witnesses

- **OEC 601** (ORS 40.305): Competency
- **OEC 602** (ORS 40.310): Personal knowledge — **critical** for
  declarations: the declarant must have personal knowledge of
  every fact stated, OR the declaration must specify the basis
  of belief
- **OEC 605** (ORS 40.330): Judge as witness — generally
  prohibited
- **OEC 606** (ORS 40.335): Juror as witness — generally
  prohibited
- **OEC 607–610** (ORS 40.340–40.355): Impeachment

### Article VII — Opinion

- **OEC 701** (ORS 40.405): Lay opinion — rationally based on
  perception, helpful to fact finder
- **OEC 702** (ORS 40.410): Expert testimony — scientific,
  technical, or specialized knowledge that will assist the
  trier of fact
- **OEC 703** (ORS 40.415): Basis of expert opinion — may rely
  on inadmissible facts if of a type reasonably relied upon by
  experts in the field
- **OEC 704** (ORS 40.420): Ultimate issue

### Article VIII — Hearsay (the workhorse article for debt cases)

- **OEC 801** (ORS 40.450): Definitions — hearsay = out-of-court
  statement offered for the truth of the matter asserted
  - **OEC 801(4)** (ORS 40.450(4)): Statements that are **not**
    hearsay (party admission, prior consistent/inconsistent
    statements, etc.)
- **OEC 802** (ORS 40.455): General hearsay rule — inadmissible
  unless an exception applies
- **OEC 803** (ORS 40.460): Hearsay exceptions — declarant's
  availability immaterial; key civil exceptions:
  - **(1)** Present sense impression
  - **(2)** Excited utterance
  - **(3)** State of mind
  - **(4)** Statement for medical diagnosis
  - **(5)** Recorded recollection
  - **(6)** **Business records of regularly conducted activity**
    — THE critical exception in debt cases. Requires:
    - Made at or near the time of the event
    - By, or from information transmitted by, a person with
      knowledge
    - Kept in the course of a regularly conducted business
      activity
    - Regular practice of that business to make the record
    - Foundation laid by the custodian or other qualified
      witness, or by certification under OEC 902(11)
  - **(7)** Absence of entry in business records
  - **(8)** Public records and reports
  - **(15)** Statements in documents affecting interest in
    property
  - **(18)** Learned treatises
  - **(24)** Residual exception (rare; requires guarantees of
    trustworthiness)
- **OEC 804** (ORS 40.465): Hearsay exceptions — declarant
  unavailable (former testimony, dying declaration,
  statement against interest, statement of personal or family
  history)

### Article IX — Authentication

- **OEC 901** (ORS 40.505): Requirement of authentication —
  evidence sufficient to support a finding that the matter is
  what its proponent claims; common methods:
  - (1) Testimony of a witness with knowledge
  - (2) Non-expert opinion on handwriting
  - (3) Comparison by trier or expert witness
  - (4) Distinctive characteristics
  - (5) Voice identification
  - (6) Telephone conversations
  - (7) Public records or reports
  - (8) Ancient documents (20 years old; condition unsuspicious)
  - (9) Process or system
  - (10) Methods provided by statute or rule
- **OEC 902** (ORS 40.510): Self-authentication — extrinsic
  evidence not required:
  - (1) Domestic public documents under seal
  - (4) Certified copies of public records
  - (6) Newspapers and periodicals
  - (7) Trade inscriptions
  - (8) Acknowledged documents
  - (9) Commercial paper and related documents
  - (10) Presumptions under Acts of Congress
  - (11) **Certified domestic records of regularly conducted
    activity** — business records certified by the custodian's
    declaration in lieu of live testimony (the OEC 803(6)
    foundation route)

### Article X — Best Evidence

- **OEC 1001** (ORS 40.550): Definitions
- **OEC 1002** (ORS 40.555): **Requirement of original** — to
  prove the content of a writing, recording, or photograph, the
  original is required, except as otherwise provided
- **OEC 1003** (ORS 40.560): Admissibility of duplicates —
  duplicate admissible to the same extent as original unless
  a genuine question is raised about authenticity, or
  circumstances make it unfair to admit the duplicate
- **OEC 1004** (ORS 40.565): Other evidence of contents — when
  the original is lost, destroyed, in possession of opponent,
  or related to a collateral matter

### Article XI — Miscellaneous

- **OEC 1101** (ORS 40.580): Applicability of OEC — applies in
  all proceedings in Oregon courts except for certain
  preliminary determinations

## Foundation for business records (OEC 803(6) + 902(11))

The critical evidentiary pattern in debt-collection cases — and
most civil cases involving business documents. Two routes:

### Route 1 — Live custodian testimony

Witness testifies (live or by declaration):

1. I am the custodian (or "other qualified witness") of the
   business records of [Company]
2. The records were made at or near the time of the events
   described
3. The records were made by, or from information transmitted by,
   a person with knowledge of the events
4. The records were kept in the course of regularly conducted
   business activity
5. It was the regular practice of [Company] to make such records

### Route 2 — Certified business records (OEC 902(11))

Records are accompanied by a certification by the custodian
satisfying the same four foundation elements above. The party
intending to offer the records under this rule must give
written notice to all parties of the intent and make the records
and certification available for inspection sufficiently in
advance to provide a fair opportunity to challenge.

### Foundation challenges in debt cases

Debt-buyer cases often present foundation challenges where the
plaintiff (debt buyer) offers business records from the **original
creditor** (e.g., Bank of America). The plaintiff's custodian
typically lacks personal knowledge of the original creditor's
record-keeping practices, and the records were not made in the
debt buyer's regular course of business.

Common defense objections:

- **Lack of personal knowledge** (OEC 602) — declarant did not
  observe the events
- **Insufficient foundation** (OEC 803(6) / 901) — declarant
  cannot attest to the original creditor's record-keeping
  practices
- **Hearsay within hearsay** (OEC 805) — original-creditor record
  embeds statements from cardholder or other parties
- **Best evidence** (OEC 1002) — claim of an original cardholder
  agreement requires the original or a duplicate of the original

See `or-consumer-debt/references/evidence-debt-buyer.md` for the
debt-defense-specific patterns.

## Comparison to Washington Evidence Rules (ER)

| Concept | Oregon (OEC) | Washington (ER) |
|---------|--------------|-----------------|
| Relevance | OEC 401 | ER 401 |
| Hearsay definition | OEC 801 | ER 801 |
| Business records | OEC 803(6) | ER 803(a)(6) |
| Authentication | OEC 901 | ER 901 |
| Self-authentication | OEC 902 | ER 902 |
| Certified business records | OEC 902(11) | ER 902(11) |
| Best evidence | OEC 1002 | ER 1002 |
| Codification | ORS 40.010–40.585 | Court Rules (not statutory) |

The structure is nearly identical. The main difference: OEC is
**statutory** (ORS Ch. 40), so changes require legislative
action; WA's ER are court rules subject to amendment by the
state Supreme Court.
