# Oregon Citation Format

Oregon uses the **Oregon Appellate Courts Style Manual** (OACSM,
the "Oregon Style Manual") published by the Oregon Judicial
Department. The Style Manual diverges from Bluebook in several
specifics — match the OACSM, not Bluebook.

Authoritative source:
https://www.courts.oregon.gov/programs/oja/Pages/style-manual.aspx

## Case citations

### Oregon Supreme Court

```
Buchler v. Oregon Corrections Div., 316 Or 499, 853 P2d 798
(1993)
```

- Reporter abbreviation: `Or` (no period)
- Regional reporter: `P2d` or `P3d` (no periods)
- Parties: italicized; `v.` (lowercase, with period)
- Year: in parentheses at end

### Oregon Court of Appeals

```
State v. Smith, 250 Or App 1, 280 P3d 1 (2012)
```

- Reporter: `Or App` (no periods)
- Otherwise identical to Supreme Court form

### Pin cite (page reference within case)

```
Buchler v. Oregon Corrections Div., 316 Or 499, 504, 853 P2d
798, 802 (1993)
```

- Pin to specific page in both reporters

### Short form (after first cite)

```
Buchler, 316 Or at 504
```

Use the lead party's name plus the volume and page; no parallel
cite required for short form.

### Slip opinion (no reporter cite yet)

```
State v. Doe, ___ Or ___, ___ P3d ___ (June 4, 2025)
                        (CA No. A123456)
```

When the case has been decided but is not yet in the bound
reporter, use a slip-opinion citation with the underlines for
volume and page (or omit them entirely) and add the appellate
case number.

## Federal case citations

```
Mansor v. Or. Inv. Co., 819 F3d 1119 (9th Cir 2016)
```

- Reporter: `F`, `F2d`, `F3d`, `F Supp`, `F Supp 2d`, `F Supp
  3d` — **no periods**
- Court: `9th Cir`, `D Or`, `1st Cir` — **no periods**
- Year in parentheses

### U.S. Supreme Court

```
Henson v. Santander Consumer USA, Inc., 582 US 79, 137 S Ct
1718, 198 L Ed 2d 177 (2017)
```

- Reporter: `US` (no periods)
- Parallel cites: `S Ct` and `L Ed 2d` (no periods)
- All three reporters cited at first instance; thereafter
  short form

## Statutes

### Oregon Revised Statutes (ORS)

```
ORS 12.080(1)
ORS 646.605 et seq.
```

- **No section symbol** (ORS style differs from Bluebook,
  which uses § for state statutes)
- Use parentheses for subsections; lowercase letters with
  parentheses for sub-subsections
- "et seq." is acceptable for chapter ranges (italicized in
  Oregon style)

### U.S. Code

```
15 USC § 1692k(a)(1)
12 USC § 1601 et seq.
```

- "USC" no periods
- Use section symbol for U.S. Code (different from ORS)
- Subsections in parentheses

### Code of Federal Regulations

```
12 CFR § 1006.30
12 CFR pt 1006 (Regulation F)
```

- "CFR" no periods
- Section symbol present
- For an entire part, "pt" with no period

### Oregon Administrative Rules (OAR)

```
OAR 137-005-0010
```

- Hyphenated three-part number; no periods or section symbol

## Court rules

### Oregon Rules of Civil Procedure (ORCP)

```
ORCP 21 A(8)
ORCP 46 A(4)(a)
```

- Top-level subsection: capital letter, separated by SPACE (not
  parentheses)
- Lower subsections: parenthesized
- This is the Style Manual convention; deviating to "ORCP
  21(A)(8)" is a strong porter-from-Washington tell

### Oregon Evidence Code (OEC)

```
OEC 803(6)
OEC 902(11)
```

- Use OEC numbering, NOT the parallel ORS Ch. 40 numbering
- Subsections in parentheses with numerals

### Uniform Trial Court Rules (UTCR)

```
UTCR 2.010(1)
UTCR 5.100(2)
```

- Decimal numbering (`2.010` not `2.01`)
- Subsections in parentheses

### Supplemental Local Rules (SLR)

```
Multnomah SLR 5.100
Washington Co SLR 5.045
Marion SLR 7.010
```

- County name + "SLR" + decimal number
- Cite by county since SLR numbering varies

### Oregon Rules of Appellate Procedure (ORAP)

```
ORAP 5.45
```

## Italicization

- **Case names** including the `v.`: italicize
- **"et seq."**: italicize
- **"id."** and **"supra"**: italicize
- **Statutes and rules**: no italics

## Short forms

### Case short form

```
First cite:  Buchler v. Oregon Corrections Div., 316 Or 499,
             504, 853 P2d 798, 802 (1993)

Short form:  Buchler, 316 Or at 504

"Id."        Used for the *immediately preceding* cite only;
             italicize, no period at end if mid-sentence
```

### Statute short form

```
First cite:  ORS 12.080(1)
Subsequent:  ORS 12.080(1)   (no abbreviation; ORS is short
                              enough)
```

There is no Bluebook-style "§" short form in Oregon style.

## Quotations

- Short quotations (≤ 50 words): incorporate into the text in
  quotation marks
- Long quotations (>50 words): block quote, single-spaced,
  indented left and right ½ inch, no quotation marks

Cite immediately after the quotation:

> "Plaintiffs seeking summary judgment have the burden of
> demonstrating there is no genuine issue of material fact and
> that the moving party is entitled to judgment as a matter of
> law." *Two Two v. Fujitec America, Inc.*, 355 Or 319, 324, 325
> P3d 707 (2014).

## Parentheticals

```
ORS 20.105 (authorizing fees against party with objectively
unreasonable position); Mattiza v. Foster, 311 Or 1, 803 P2d
723 (1990) (interpreting "objectively unreasonable").
```

Short, explanatory parentheticals after a citation are encouraged
in Oregon practice.

## Numbers

- Spell out one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above
- Exception: in tables, statistics, and dates, always use
  numerals
- Dollar amounts: numerals with comma separators for thousands;
  no cents unless the amount is under $1.00

## Dates

- "January 14, 2025" (preferred in motions and declarations)
- "Jan 14, 2025" or "14 January 2025" — not Oregon convention
- "the 14th day of January, 2025" — formal style for signature
  blocks and orders

## Comparison to Bluebook and Washington

| Element | Oregon Style | Bluebook | Washington |
|---------|--------------|----------|------------|
| Oregon SC | `Or` (no period) | `Or.` | n/a |
| Oregon CoA | `Or App` | `Or. App.` | n/a |
| WA SC | n/a | `Wash. 2d` | `Wn.2d` |
| WA CoA | n/a | `Wash. App.` | `Wn. App.` |
| Reg reporter | `P3d` | `P.3d` | `P.3d` |
| USC | `15 USC § 1692k` | `15 U.S.C. § 1692k` | `15 U.S.C. § 1692k` |
| CFR | `12 CFR § 1006.30` | `12 C.F.R. § 1006.30` | `12 C.F.R. § 1006.30` |
| ORCP top sub | `ORCP 21 A(8)` | varies | n/a |
| Section symbol | None for ORS | § for state statutes | None for RCW |

The most common porting mistake from Washington or federal
practice: adding periods where Oregon style omits them
(`Or. App.` instead of `Or App`). When in doubt, consult the
OACSM directly.

## How to verify a citation

For Oregon cases:

- **CourtListener**: https://www.courtlistener.com/?q=&type=o
  (Oregon court filter)
- **OJD opinions**: https://www.publications.ojd.state.or.us/

For ORS:

- **Oregon Legislative Search**:
  https://www.oregonlegislature.gov/bills_laws/Pages/ORS.aspx

For court rules:

- **ORCP**: https://counciloncourtprocedures.org/
- **UTCR / ORAP / etc.**:
  https://www.courts.oregon.gov/rules/Pages/default.aspx

For federal:

- **USC**: https://uscode.house.gov/
- **CFR**: https://www.ecfr.gov/

See `online-sources.md` for the full catalog and
`legal-data-apis.md` for programmatic access.
