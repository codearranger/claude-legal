# Oregon Hearing Day — Single-Page Checklist

A pro-se-friendly printable checklist. Use the morning of the
hearing.

## 24 hours before

- [ ] Confirm date, time, courtroom or WebEx link
- [ ] Check the assigned judge's standing order (any last-
      minute notes?)
- [ ] Print or download:
  - [ ] Motion + memorandum
  - [ ] Declaration + exhibits
  - [ ] Opposing party's response (if any)
  - [ ] Your reply (if filed)
  - [ ] Proposed order
  - [ ] Notice of Hearing
  - [ ] Your outline (2–3 main points)
- [ ] Anticipate 2–3 likely judge questions and how you would
      answer
- [ ] Confirm working copies were delivered (if SLR required)
- [ ] Prepare your opening (15–30 seconds)
- [ ] Prepare your closing (15–30 seconds)

## Day of hearing

### Logistics

- [ ] **For in-person**:
  - Allow 30 minutes for travel
  - Allow 15–20 minutes for security at major courthouses
  - Address:
    - Multnomah Central Courthouse: 1200 SW 1st Ave, Portland
    - Washington Co Hillsboro: 145 NE 2nd Ave, Hillsboro
    - [Other county address]
- [ ] **For WebEx**:
  - Log in 10–15 minutes early
  - Test camera and microphone using a test call
  - Have the call-in number as backup
  - Quiet location; pets and roommates managed
  - Camera at eye level; plain background; good front
    lighting

### Materials at the lectern (or beside the laptop)

- [ ] Your outline (one page, key points)
- [ ] Motion + memo, ready to reference
- [ ] Exhibits, tabbed or indexed
- [ ] Pen and paper for notes
- [ ] Water (if available)

### Dress

- [ ] Professional dress — collared shirt or equivalent
- [ ] No hats indoors (except religious head coverings)
- [ ] For WebEx: same standard, top half on camera

### Devices

- [ ] Cell phone silenced or off
- [ ] Laptop charged (for WebEx)
- [ ] Working copy of any digital exhibits accessible (if
      screen-sharing is needed for an evidentiary hearing)

## During the hearing

### When called

- [ ] **Stand and approach the lectern** (or activate the
      WebEx microphone)
- [ ] **Open** with your 15-second introduction
- [ ] **Identify yourself, the case, and the relief**

### Argument

- [ ] **Strongest point first** — 2–4 minutes
- [ ] **Anticipated counter** — 1–2 minutes
- [ ] **Answer judge questions directly** (yes/no first, then
      explain)
- [ ] **Concede appropriate points** — preserves credibility
- [ ] **Refer to the record** by exhibit and page

### Stay aware of

- [ ] **Time** — most matters are 10–20 minutes per side
- [ ] **The judge's body language** — leaning forward = engaged;
      leaning back / looking down = ready to move on
- [ ] **Opposing counsel's points** — take notes; respond if
      given time

### Closing

- [ ] **Restate the relief** — what specifically you want
- [ ] **Reference the proposed order** in the file
- [ ] **Thank the court**

## During opposing argument

- [ ] **Sit and listen**
- [ ] **Take notes** on mis-statements of the record or
      missed points
- [ ] **Do NOT interrupt** — wait for your reply
- [ ] **Stand when given reply time**, address the most
      important mis-statement

## When the court rules

### From the bench

- [ ] **Write down the specific words** of the ruling
- [ ] **Note any bench modifications** to your proposed
      order
- [ ] **Ask clarifying questions if needed** (not arguing —
      clarifying)
- [ ] **Thank the court**

### Under advisement

- [ ] **Ask when to expect ruling** — "When might we expect
      the Court's ruling?"
- [ ] **Note any actions to take in the interim**
- [ ] **Thank the court**

## After the hearing

- [ ] **Apply any bench modifications** to the proposed order
- [ ] **Re-serve the modified order** on opposing counsel
      (3 days under UTCR 5.100)
- [ ] **Submit the signed order** to chambers after the 3-day
      window
- [ ] **Send a hearing recap email to your records**
- [ ] **Update your case calendar** with any new deadlines the
      court set

## Common pro se hearing mistakes to avoid

- [ ] **Reading from the brief** — judge already read it
- [ ] **Dodging judge questions** — answer first, explain
      second
- [ ] **Arguing with opposing counsel** — speak to the judge
- [ ] **Failing to concede obvious points** — loses
      credibility
- [ ] **Being late** — adverse inference
- [ ] **Talking over the judge** — always lose
- [ ] **Bringing too many materials** — clutters the lectern
- [ ] **Forgetting to close with the relief request**

## Quick reference for forms of address

| Person | Address |
|--------|---------|
| Judge | "Your Honor" or "the Court" |
| Opposing counsel | "Counsel" or "Mr./Ms. [Name]" |
| Opposing pro se | "Mr./Ms. [Name]" or "Plaintiff/Defendant" |
| Court clerk | "Madam/Mr. Clerk" |
| Witness | "Mr./Ms. [Name]" or "Sir/Ma'am" |
