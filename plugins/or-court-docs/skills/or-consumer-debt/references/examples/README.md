# Example Filings — Oregon Consumer-Debt Defense

These are **synthetic** example filings for a fictional
Oregon debt-buyer-defense fact pattern. They illustrate the
Parker framework applied to debt-defense practice in
Multnomah Circuit Court.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** The parties and facts are fictional.
> Do not use these documents in actual litigation without
> substantive review by counsel.

## The fact pattern

- **Plaintiff**: VELOCITY INVESTMENTS, LLC — a Delaware debt
  buyer
- **Defendant**: JANE DOE — Oregon resident, pro se
- **Original Creditor**: Bank of America, N.A.
- **Alleged Account**: BofA credit card ending 1234
- **Allegations**: Plaintiff purchased the account through
  a chain of debt-buyer transfers; $7,432.18 alleged
  balance; suit filed January 14, 2025
- **Defendant's position**: doesn't recognize the account;
  even if hers, last payment was more than 6 years ago
  (SOL); Plaintiff is unregistered with Oregon DCBS

## How to use these examples

For a real case:

1. **Read each example** to see how the Parker framework
   applies in debt-defense practice
2. **Adapt the structure** to your fact pattern — keep the
   sections (I–VII for motions; numbered paragraphs for
   declarations); change the substance
3. **Verify the citations** with `or-fact-check`
4. **Run** `or-quality-check` for UTCR 2.010 format
5. **Substitute** your facts, parties, exhibits, dates

## What these examples illustrate

- **Caption format** per UTCR 2.100 (use "v.", numbered
  exhibits, Multnomah County header)
- **Parker framework sections** (Relief, Facts, Issues,
  Evidence, Authorities, Argument, Conclusion)
- **Oregon-specific citations** (ORCP, OEC, ORS, UTCR — no
  Bluebook periods)
- **Counterclaim drafting** (FDCPA + UTPA + ORS 697
  alternative pleading)
- **Meet-and-confer documentation** (email summary; phone-
  call memorialization)
- **Pro se signature block** (no OSB#; "pro se" designation)
- **Certificate of Service** under UTCR 1.090

## What these examples are NOT

- **Not** generic Oregon debt-defense templates — the facts

- **Not** substitutes for substantive legal review
- **Not** complete — every real case has unique facts that
