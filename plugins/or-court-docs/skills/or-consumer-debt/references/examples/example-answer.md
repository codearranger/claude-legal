# Example — Answer with Affirmative Defenses and Counterclaims

Synthetic example. Fictional parties. Not legal advice.

---

```
            IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF THE STATE OF OREGON
                  FOR THE COUNTY OF MULTNOMAH

VELOCITY INVESTMENTS, LLC,      │   Case No. 25CV01234
                                │
     Plaintiff,                 │   DEFENDANT JANE DOE'S
                                │   ANSWER, AFFIRMATIVE
     v.                         │   DEFENSES, AND
                                │   COUNTERCLAIMS
JANE DOE,                       │
                                │
     Defendant.                 │
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Defendant Jane Doe, pro se, answers Plaintiff's Complaint
as follows:

                           ANSWER

1.  PARAGRAPH 1 OF COMPLAINT (jurisdiction and venue):
    ADMITTED.

2.  PARAGRAPH 2 OF COMPLAINT (parties): ADMITTED that
    Defendant Jane Doe resides in Multnomah County, Oregon.
    DENIED that Plaintiff is a "Delaware limited liability
    company duly authorized to do business in Oregon," for
    lack of information sufficient to admit or deny.

3.  PARAGRAPH 3 OF COMPLAINT (allegation regarding the
    account): DENIED. Defendant does not recognize the
    alleged account.

4.  PARAGRAPH 4 OF COMPLAINT (allegation that Plaintiff
    acquired the account from Bank of America by
    assignment): DENIED for lack of information sufficient
    to admit or deny.

5.  PARAGRAPH 5 OF COMPLAINT (alleged balance): DENIED.

6.  PARAGRAPHS 6–10 OF COMPLAINT (allegations of breach):
    DENIED.

                      AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

FIRST AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Statute of Limitations — ORS 12.080)

7.  The alleged account, if it exists at all, was charged
    off by Bank of America more than 6 years before the
    filing of this action. Under ORS 12.080(1) and (2), the
    statute of limitations on a written contract or open
    account is 6 years from the date of last activity. The
    action is therefore time-barred.

SECOND AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Lack of Capacity to Sue — ORS 697.105)

8.  Plaintiff is a "collection agency" as defined in ORS
    697.005 because Plaintiff purchases debts for the
    purpose of collection. Under ORS 697.015, every
    collection agency must register with the Oregon
    Department of Consumer and Business Services before
    collecting debts in Oregon. Under ORS 697.105, an
    unregistered collection agency cannot maintain an
    action in Oregon courts. Plaintiff is not registered or
    has lapsed in its registration.

THIRD AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Lack of Standing — Defective Chain of Title)

9.  Plaintiff alleges it "acquired" the account through an
    assignment from Bank of America. Plaintiff cannot prove
    the chain of title from the original creditor to itself.
    Plaintiff lacks the foundational documents (cardholder
    agreement, bill of sale, assignment schedule) necessary
    to establish ownership and the right to sue.

FOURTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Failure to State Ultimate Facts — ORCP 21 A(8))

10. The Complaint, accepting all allegations as true,
    fails to state ultimate facts entitling Plaintiff to
    relief. The Complaint does not allege facts evidencing
    Plaintiff's chain of title; the bare allegation of
    "acquired by assignment" is conclusory.

FIFTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Failure to Provide Validation — 15 USC § 1692g; 12 CFR
§ 1006.34)

11. Plaintiff failed to provide a validation notice
    complying with 15 USC § 1692g(a) and 12 CFR § 1006.34
    before initiating collection or this action.

SIXTH AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSE
(Unauthorized Charges)

12. To the extent the alleged account is not Defendant's,
    or includes charges Defendant did not authorize,
    Plaintiff cannot recover.

                          COUNTERCLAIMS

13. Defendant/Counter-Claimant Jane Doe, pro se, files the
    following counterclaims against Plaintiff/Counter-
    Defendant Velocity Investments, LLC.

COUNTERCLAIM I — Fair Debt Collection Practices Act
                 and Regulation F
                 (15 USC § 1692 et seq.; 12 CFR pt 1006)

14. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant Velocity Investments, LLC
    is a "debt collector" within the meaning of 15 USC
    § 1692a(6).

15. The alleged debt is a "consumer debt" within the meaning
    of 15 USC § 1692a(5) — an obligation arising from a
    personal credit-card account.

16. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant violated the FDCPA and
    Regulation F by:

    (a)  Bringing this action on a debt past the 6-year
         statute of limitations under ORS 12.080, in
         violation of 15 USC § 1692e(5) and 12 CFR
         § 1006.26(b);

    (b)  Falsely representing the character, amount, and
         legal status of the alleged debt, in violation of
         15 USC § 1692e(2);

    (c)  Threatening to take action that cannot legally be
         taken — suing on debt Plaintiff cannot prove it
         owns — in violation of 15 USC § 1692e(5);

    (d)  Using deceptive means to collect, in violation of
         15 USC § 1692e(10); and

    (e)  Failing to provide the validation notice required
         by 15 USC § 1692g(a) and 12 CFR § 1006.34.

17. As a direct and proximate result, Counter-Claimant
    suffered actual damages including time spent on this
    litigation, lost wages from court appearances, attorney
    consultation expenses, and emotional distress, in
    addition to $1,000 statutory damages under 15 USC
    § 1692k(a)(2)(A).

COUNTERCLAIM II — Oregon Unlawful Trade Practices Act
                  (ORS 646.605 et seq.)

18. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant is a "person" engaged in
    "trade or commerce" within the meaning of ORS 646.605.

19. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant violated the UTPA by:

    (a)  Demanding payment of an amount Plaintiff knew was
         not legally owed (because the SOL had run and
         because Plaintiff cannot prove ownership), in
         violation of ORS 646.607(3);

    (b)  Making false representations regarding the
         character, amount, and legal status of the alleged
         debt, in violation of ORS 646.608(1)(s) and (u);

    (c)  Engaging in unconscionable conduct in the
         collection of consumer debt, in violation of ORS
         646.607.

20. Counter-Claimant suffered ascertainable loss of money
    and property, as alleged in paragraph 17.

21. Counter-Claimant is entitled to actual damages, $200
    statutory minimum, punitive damages, equitable relief
    (declaration and injunction against further collection),
    and attorney fees and costs under ORS 646.638.

COUNTERCLAIM III — Oregon Collection Agency Registration
                   Act Violations (ORS 697)

22. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant is a "collection agency"
    as defined in ORS 697.005.

23. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant was not, and is not,
    registered with the Oregon DCBS under ORS 697.015 at
    the time of the filing of this action.

24. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant's unregistered collection
    activity, including filing this action, violated ORS
    697.015 and ORS 697.105.

25. Plaintiff/Counter-Defendant also engaged in prohibited
    practices under ORS 697.058 by [enumerate as
    applicable].

26. Counter-Claimant suffered actual damages as alleged in
    paragraph 17 and is entitled to $200 statutory damages,
    attorney fees, and costs under ORS 697.085.

                       PRAYER FOR RELIEF

WHEREFORE, Defendant/Counter-Claimant Jane Doe prays for:

A.  Dismissal of Plaintiff's Complaint with prejudice;

B.  Judgment on Counterclaim I (FDCPA / Regulation F) for
    actual damages to be proven at trial; $1,000 statutory
    damages under 15 USC § 1692k(a)(2)(A); reasonable
    attorney fees and costs under 15 USC § 1692k(a)(3);

C.  Judgment on Counterclaim II (Oregon UTPA) for actual
    damages to be proven at trial; $200 statutory minimum;
    punitive damages; equitable relief; reasonable
    attorney fees and costs under ORS 646.638(3);

D.  Judgment on Counterclaim III (ORS 697) for actual
    damages; $200 statutory damages; attorney fees and
    costs under ORS 697.085;

E.  A declaration that the alleged debt is unenforceable
    and is not owed by Defendant;

F.  An injunction against Plaintiff's further collection
    of the alleged debt;

G.  Costs and disbursements;

H.  Statutory prevailing-party fee under ORS 20.190;

I.  Such other relief as the Court deems just and proper.

DATED this ____ day of __________, 20__.
```

## Key features illustrated

1. **Caption** uses Multnomah County and "v." (not "vs.")
2. **Affirmative defenses** are numbered and titled with the
   legal basis; SOL is first (strongest)
3. **Counterclaims** are alternative-pleaded: FDCPA + UTPA
   + ORS 697 cover the same conduct with different remedies
4. **Prayer for relief** itemizes each ground with its
   specific damages and fee statute
5. **Signature block** is pro se: no OSB#; clear "pro se"
   designation
