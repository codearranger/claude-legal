## Key features illustrated

### Title

- `[PROPOSED]` bracket present (will be removed when judge
  signs)
- Specific rule cited (ORCP 46 A)
- Indicates what is being granted

### Findings

- Numbered, each one supporting an element of the ruling
- Tie back to the motion's Statement of Facts and Argument
- Provide a record for appellate review (if any)

### Order text

- Operative phrase: "IT IS HEREBY ORDERED"
- Each relief paragraph (A, B, C, D) is **one sentence**
- Specific deadline with "or [date], whichever is sooner"
  (the blank is filled in when the judge signs)
- Specific fee award with mechanism (ORCP 68 C(2) or ORS
  20.190 for pro se)

### Notice of Presentation

- Indicates the order tracks the briefed motion
- States the 3-court-day window has been served per UTCR
  5.100

### Pro se considerations

- No OSB# on Defendant's signature block
- Approval-as-to-form line provided for opposing counsel
- Alternative fee award noted for the pro se situation
  ($200 ORS 20.190 + costs)

## Post-hearing modifications

If the judge grants the motion but modifies the relief
(e.g., 21-day production rather than 14-day):

1. **Remove the `[PROPOSED]` bracket** from the title
2. **Apply the modification** to paragraph B ("within 21
   calendar days")
3. **Re-serve** under UTCR 5.100 (3 court days)
4. **Submit** to chambers (see `or-submit-order`)

If the judge denies the motion:

1. Re-draft as "ORDER DENYING DEFENDANT'S MOTION..."
2. Adjust the Findings to reflect the basis for denial
3. Order paragraph A: "Defendant's Motion to Compel is
   DENIED"
