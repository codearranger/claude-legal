## Letter 1 — Initial meet-and-confer letter (Exhibit 3 to
Motion to Compel)

```
                                    Jane Doe
                                    123 Main Street
                                    Portland, OR 97201
                                    (503) 555-0100
                                    jdoe@example.com

                                    May 10, 2025

Mr. Robert Smith, Esq.
Smith & Associates
500 SW Stark Street, Suite 200
Portland, OR 97204
rsmith@smithlaw.com

Re:  Velocity Investments, LLC v. Jane Doe
     Multnomah Co. Circuit Court, Case No. 25CV01234
     Meet and Confer — Discovery Disputes

Dear Mr. Smith:

I am writing to meet and confer under ORCP 46 A and
Multnomah SLR 5.045 regarding Plaintiff's Responses to my
First Requests for Production, served May 1, 2025.

Specifically, I disagree with Plaintiff's objections to the
following RFPs:

1.  RFP No. 1 (Cardholder Agreement).

    Plaintiff objected on grounds of "not in possession,
    custody, or control." I disagree because ORCP 36 B(1)
    defines "control" to include the legal right to obtain.
    Plaintiff's Complaint, at paragraph 4, alleges that
    Plaintiff "acquired" the account through "an assignment
    from Bank of America." Plaintiff cannot simultaneously
    claim ownership and disclaim control over the
    foundational document evidencing it.

    I request that Plaintiff supplement by May 24, 2025
    and produce the original cardholder agreement,
    including all signature pages, incorporated terms and
    conditions, and electronic acceptance records.

2.  RFP No. 3 (Bill of Sale) and RFP No. 5 (Bulk Sale
    Agreement).

    Plaintiff objected on grounds of "trade secret." A
    protective order under ORCP 36 C is available to
    address any genuine trade-secret concerns. I propose
    that we negotiate a stipulated protective order
    limiting disclosure. Please identify the specific
    trade-secret concern and the scope of protection
    Plaintiff would accept.

3.  RFP No. 4 (Assignment Schedule).

    Plaintiff produced an assignment schedule with every
    row other than mine redacted. The redaction is
    overbroad. ORCP 36 B(1) requires production with the
    document's structure intact — the redaction here makes
    it impossible to verify the schedule's authenticity or
    whether the schedule existed at the time of the
    alleged transfer.

    I request the unredacted Assignment Schedule, with
    row-level redaction of other accounts' identifying
    information (account number, party name, balance) but
    leaving the schedule's structural elements (header,
    footer, dates, signatures, sequence numbers) visible.

    Alternatively, Plaintiff may produce the full schedule
    subject to a protective order.

4.  RFP No. 12 (Plaintiff's DCBS registration).

    Plaintiff objected on grounds of "irrelevance." This
    document is directly relevant to Defendant's Second
    Affirmative Defense (lack of capacity under ORS
    697.105 because Plaintiff is allegedly unregistered
    with Oregon DCBS).

    I request that Plaintiff supplement by May 24, 2025 by
    either (a) producing all DCBS registration documents
    covering the period from the date Plaintiff acquired
    the alleged account to the present, or (b) stating
    affirmatively that Plaintiff is not registered with
    DCBS.

I am available for a phone call this week to discuss. I
propose Wednesday, May 14, 2025 at 2:00 PM or Thursday,
May 15, 2025 at 11:00 AM. Please let me know which time
works.

If we cannot resolve these by May 24, 2025, I will be
required to move to compel under ORCP 46 A and seek the
reasonable expenses of that motion under ORCP 46 A(4)(a).

Sincerely,

Jane Doe
Defendant, pro se
```

## Letter 2 — Phone-call follow-up email (Exhibit 4 to
Motion to Compel)

```
From: Jane Doe <jdoe@example.com>
To:   Robert Smith <rsmith@smithlaw.com>
Date: May 14, 2025 — 3:15 PM
Subject: Re: Meet and Confer — May 14, 2025 Phone Call,
         Velocity Investments v. Doe (25CV01234)

Mr. Smith,

Thank you for the phone call today, May 14, 2025, beginning
at approximately 2:00 PM and lasting approximately 30
minutes.

To confirm what we discussed:

1.  RFP No. 1 (Cardholder Agreement).

    You stated that Plaintiff does not have the cardholder
    agreement in its possession and does not intend to
    obtain it from Bank of America. You stated that the
    bulk sale agreement between Plaintiff's predecessor in
    interest and Bank of America does not require Bank of
    America to provide account-specific documents on
    request.

    I disagree, but understand Plaintiff's position. We
    remain at impasse on RFP No. 1.

2.  RFP No. 3 (Bill of Sale).

    You stated that Plaintiff would produce a copy of the
    Bill of Sale from the first intermediate purchaser to
    Plaintiff but would not produce the Bill of Sale from
    Bank of America to the first intermediate purchaser,
    on grounds of "not in possession."

    I disagree. The Bill of Sale from Bank of America is
    necessary to establish the chain of title. We remain
    at impasse.

3.  RFP No. 4 (Assignment Schedule).

    You stated that Plaintiff would not produce an
    unredacted assignment schedule due to "privacy" of
    other accounts in the pool. We discussed alternatives,
    including producing the schedule with row-level
    redaction of identifying information but leaving
    structural elements visible. You declined.

    We remain at impasse.

4.  RFP No. 5 (Bulk Sale Agreement).

    Same impasse as Nos. 3 and 4.

5.  RFP No. 12 (DCBS Registration).

    You stated that Plaintiff is currently registered with
    Oregon DCBS but that the registration "may not have
    been in effect" at the time of the original Complaint
    filing. You declined to produce the registration
    documents until further investigation.

    I requested that Plaintiff supplement within 14 days
    by either producing the registration documents or
    confirming the registration status. You stated that
    Plaintiff would consider but did not commit.

    We remain at potential impasse.

If I have mis-recorded any aspect of our call, please let
me know within 3 business days. Otherwise, I will rely on
this summary in any subsequent motion practice. If you do
not supplement on RFP No. 12 within 14 days, by May 28,
2025, I will move to compel on all five RFPs (Nos. 1, 3,
4, 5, and 12).

Sincerely,

Jane Doe
Defendant, pro se
```

## What this correspondence illustrates

### Initial letter

- **Specific identification** of each disputed RFP by number
- **Plaintiff's objection** quoted or paraphrased
- **Defendant's position** with legal basis (ORCP 36 B(1),
  ORS 697.105, etc.)
- **Concrete proposal** with deadline
- **Phone-call options** offered for further discussion
- **Threat of motion** with specific authority (ORCP 46 A(4)(a))

### Follow-up email

- **Date and time of call** with duration
- **Each disputed item** recapped with each party's
  position
- **Resolution status** for each (resolved / impasse)
- **Tacit-confirmation clause** ("If I have mis-recorded
  any aspect...")
- **Final deadline and consequence** stated

### What makes this good meet-and-confer

1. **Specificity** — each RFP discussed individually
2. **Legal basis** — Defendant cites the rule supporting
   the position
3. **Reasonable timeline** — 14 days to supplement
4. **Concrete alternatives** — protective order, redaction
   compromise
5. **Documentation** — both letter and follow-up email
   create a clean record
6. **Clear escalation** — the path to motion is explicit

### What makes this insufficient (for plaintiff)

If plaintiff's counsel had:
- Refused to take the call
- Failed to respond to the letter
- Provided only "I'll consider" without commitment

…the defendant would have a stronger record for the
motion-to-compel certification.

## Use in the motion-to-compel certification

The certification recites the dates and substance:

> 3. On May 10, 2025, Defendant sent Plaintiff's counsel a
>    meet-and-confer letter... (Ex. 3.)
>
> 4. On May 14, 2025, Defendant and Plaintiff's counsel
>    spoke by telephone for approximately 30 minutes...
>    (Ex. 4.)
>
> 5. Defendant memorialized the call by email the same day.
>    (Ex. 4.)

Both letters become exhibits to the supporting declaration.
