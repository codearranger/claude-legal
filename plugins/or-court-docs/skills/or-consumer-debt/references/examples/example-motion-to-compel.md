                          MEMORANDUM

I.  RELIEF REQUESTED

    Defendant asks the Court to enter an order:
    (a) compelling Plaintiff to produce, within 14 calendar
        days of the date of the Order, the following:
          - The original cardholder agreement (RFP 1)
          - The bills of sale in the chain of title (RFP 3)
          - Unredacted assignment schedules (RFP 4)
          - The bulk sale agreements in the chain of title
            (RFP 5)
          - Plaintiff's DCBS registration documentation
            under ORS 697.015 (RFP 12);
    (b) overruling Plaintiff's objections to RFPs 1, 3, 4,
        5, and 12; and
    (c) awarding Defendant reasonable expenses incurred in
        making this Motion under ORCP 46 A(4)(a).

II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

    1.  Plaintiff filed the Complaint on January 14, 2025,
        alleging that Defendant owes $7,432.18 on an
        account "acquired" from Bank of America. (Compl.
        ¶¶ 3–4.)

    2.  Defendant answered on February 11, 2025, denying
        ownership of the alleged account and raising
        affirmative defenses of (a) statute of limitations
        under ORS 12.080, (b) lack of capacity to sue
        under ORS 697.105, and (c) lack of standing for
        defective chain of title. (Answer ¶¶ 7–10.)

    3.  Defendant served First Requests for Production
        Nos. 1–13 on April 1, 2025, targeting the chain
        of title and Plaintiff's DCBS registration. (Decl.
        ¶ 2; Ex. 1.)

    4.  Plaintiff served Responses on May 1, 2025,
        objecting on grounds of "relevance, burden,
        possession, and privilege" without producing any
        of the responsive documents identified in this
        Motion. (Decl. ¶ 3; Ex. 2.)

    5.  Defendant sent Plaintiff's counsel a meet-and-
        confer letter on May 10, 2025, identifying the
        specific objections and requesting supplementation
        within 14 days. (Decl. ¶ 4; Ex. 3.)

    6.  Defendant and Plaintiff's counsel spoke by phone
        on May 14, 2025. Defendant memorialized the call
        by email the same day. (Decl. ¶ 5; Ex. 4.)

    7.  As of the date of this Motion, Plaintiff has not
        supplemented its Responses as to RFPs 1, 3, 4, 5,
        and 12. Plaintiff maintains its objections. (Decl.
        ¶ 6.)

III. STATEMENT OF ISSUES

     1. Whether documents proving the chain of title from
        the original creditor to Plaintiff are within ORCP
        36 B(1) scope when Defendant denies the existence
        of the alleged account.

     2. Whether Plaintiff's "not in possession, custody,
        or control" objection is valid when Plaintiff's
        Complaint alleges Plaintiff owns the account.

     3. Whether Plaintiff's DCBS registration status under
        ORS 697.015 is within ORCP 36 B(1) scope given
        Defendant's lack-of-capacity defense.

     4. Whether Plaintiff's failure to supplement is
        "without substantial justification" under ORCP 46
        A(4)(a) such that mandatory fee-shifting applies.

IV. EVIDENCE RELIED UPON

    - Declaration of Jane Doe, filed herewith
    - Exhibit 1: First Requests for Production (April 1,
      2025)
    - Exhibit 2: Plaintiff's Responses (May 1, 2025)
    - Exhibit 3: Meet-and-confer letter (May 10, 2025)
    - Exhibit 4: Phone-call follow-up email (May 14, 2025)

V.  AUTHORITIES

    - ORCP 36 B(1) — scope of discovery
    - ORCP 43 — requests for production
    - ORCP 46 A — motion to compel
    - ORCP 46 A(4)(a) — mandatory fee-shifting on
      successful motion to compel
    - Multnomah SLR 5.045 — meet-and-confer certification
    - OEC 803(6) / 902(11) — business records foundation
    - ORS 697.015, 697.105 — collection agency registration
      requirement and capacity to sue
    - ORS 20.105 — objectively unreasonable position
    - Phillips v. Beaverton Aluminum Co., 314 Or App 257,
      498 P3d 814 (2021)
    - Mattiza v. Foster, 311 Or 1, 803 P2d 723 (1990)

VI. ARGUMENT

    A.  The cardholder agreement and chain-of-title
        documents are within ORCP 36 B(1) scope.

        ORCP 36 B(1) defines the scope of discovery as
        relevant to a claim or defense and proportional
        to the needs of the case. Plaintiff alleges it
        "acquired" the account through assignment.
        (Compl. ¶ 4.) Each link in the chain of title is
        an element of Plaintiff's claim. Plaintiff cannot
        evade producing the foundational documents while
        claiming the account is its own.

    B.  Plaintiff's "not in possession" objection
        contradicts Plaintiff's Complaint.

        Plaintiff's Complaint alleges ownership.
        Plaintiff's Response then disclaims "possession,
        custody, or control" of the documents proving
        that ownership. The two cannot both be true.

        Moreover, ORCP 36 B(1) "control" includes the
        legal right to obtain. Plaintiff's bulk sale
        agreement with the original creditor gives
        Plaintiff the contractual right to obtain account-
        specific documentation. Plaintiff therefore has
        "control" of the documents.

    C.  The DCBS registration request is plainly relevant.

        Defendant's Second Affirmative Defense is that
        Plaintiff is an unregistered collection agency
        under ORS 697.015 and lacks capacity to sue
        under ORS 697.105. Plaintiff's DCBS registration
        status is the central fact in this defense.
        Whatever else Plaintiff may object to, registration
        is not a "privilege" or "trade secret" — it is a
        public-record matter.

    D.  Fee-shifting under ORCP 46 A(4)(a) is mandatory.

        Phillips v. Beaverton Aluminum Co. confirms that
        fee-shifting on a granted motion to compel
        requires "substantial justification" — more than
        non-frivolousness. Plaintiff's objections to RFPs
        targeting its own allegations cannot be
        substantially justified.

VII. CONCLUSION

     For the foregoing reasons, Defendant respectfully
     requests entry of the Proposed Order filed herewith.

## Key features illustrated

1. **Cite the rule first**: "Pursuant to ORCP 46 A..."
2. **Sections I–VII** in strict Parker framework
3. **Detailed statement of facts** with citation to
   declaration paragraphs and exhibits
4. **Statement of Issues** as yes/no questions
5. **Specific authorities** including Oregon case (*Phillips*),
   ORS, ORCP, Multnomah SLR
6. **Argument** organized by issue with bold lead-ins
7. **Conclusion** restating the relief
8. **Meet-and-confer certification** at the end (per Multnomah
   SLR 5.045)
9. **Pro se signature block** — no OSB#
