# Meet-and-Confer Correspondence — Debt-Buyer Cases (Oregon)

Specific meet-and-confer templates for the most common
debt-buyer discovery disputes. Layer onto the general meet-
and-confer framework in `or-discovery/references/meet-and-
confer.md`.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Adapt to your specific case.

## Why these matter

ORCP 46 A requires good-faith meet-and-confer before a motion
to compel. Local SLRs (Multnomah 5.045, Washington Co 5.046)
require specific certification. In debt-buyer cases, the
common discovery disputes are:

1. Plaintiff's "not in possession" objection to chain-of-
   title documents
2. Plaintiff's redacted assignment schedule
3. Plaintiff's "burden" objection to monthly statements
4. Plaintiff's "privilege" assertion without log
5. Plaintiff's failure to produce validation-notice records

## Template 1: Chain of title — "not in possession" objection

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Meet and Confer — Discovery Disputes,
         Case No. 25CV12345

Counsel:

I am writing to meet and confer under ORCP 46 A and
Multnomah SLR 5.045 regarding Plaintiff's responses to
Defendant's First Requests for Production, served [date].

Specifically:

1.  RFP No. 1 (cardholder agreement). Plaintiff objected on
    grounds of "not in possession, custody, or control."

    I disagree. ORCP 36 B(1) defines "possession, custody, or
    control" to include the legal right to obtain. The
    cardholder agreement is a document that Plaintiff's
    predecessor in interest (Bank of America, by way of the
    chain of debt-buyer transfers) had the right to obtain
    under the bulk sale agreement that Plaintiff has, in
    turn, acquired. Plaintiff therefore has "control" of the
    cardholder agreement.

    Moreover, Plaintiff's Complaint, at paragraph 4, alleges
    that Plaintiff "acquired" the account from Bank of
    America. That allegation places the cardholder agreement
    within Plaintiff's contemplated scope of ownership.
    Plaintiff cannot simultaneously claim ownership of the
    account and disclaim control over the foundational
    document evidencing it.

    I request that Plaintiff supplement its response by
    [date 14 days hence] and produce the cardholder
    agreement, including all signature pages, incorporated
    terms and conditions, and electronic acceptance records.

2.  RFP No. 3 (Bill of Sale). Plaintiff objected on grounds
    of "trade secret."

    A protective order under ORCP 36 C is available to
    address any genuine trade-secret concerns. I propose
    that we negotiate a stipulated protective order limiting
    the scope of disclosure. Please identify the specific
    trade-secret concern and the protective-order scope
    Plaintiff would accept.

3.  RFP No. 4 (Assignment Schedule). Plaintiff produced a
    schedule with every row other than the Defendant's row
    redacted.

    The redaction is overbroad. ORCP 36 B(1) requires
    production with the document's structure intact — the
    redaction here makes it impossible to verify the
    schedule's authenticity or whether the schedule existed
    at the time of the alleged transfer. Plaintiff should
    produce the unredacted Assignment Schedule, with row-
    level redaction of OTHER accounts' identifying
    information (account number, party name, balance), but
    leaving the schedule's structural elements (header,
    footer, dates, signatures, sequence numbers) visible.

    Alternatively, Plaintiff may produce the schedule in
    full subject to a protective order.

I am available for a phone call on [proposed dates and
times]. Please let me know a time that works for you.

If we cannot resolve these by [date 14 days hence], I will
be required to move to compel under ORCP 46 A and seek the
fees and costs of that motion under ORCP 46 A(4)(a).

Best,
[Name][, pro se]
[Phone]
```

## Template 2: Monthly statements — "burden" objection

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Meet and Confer — Discovery, Case No. 25CV12345

Counsel:

Regarding RFP No. 2 (Monthly statements for the 60-month
period preceding charge-off), Plaintiff objected on grounds
of "undue burden."

The objection is not specific. Under ORCP 36 B(1),
proportionality is a balancing test. To evaluate burden, I
need:

- The number of monthly statements potentially responsive
  (typically 60 for a 5-year statement period)
- The format in which they are maintained (paper, scanned PDF,
  digital data export)
- The estimated time and cost of production

Please provide this information so we can evaluate the
proportionality. If the burden is genuine and
disproportionate, I am willing to negotiate a sampled or
time-limited production (e.g., the 12 months immediately
preceding charge-off, or every fourth statement). But a
flat "burden" assertion without quantification is
insufficient.

I request a response within 14 days, by [date]. If you do
not supplement, I will move to compel.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Template 3: Privilege log

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Meet and Confer — Privilege Log,
         Case No. 25CV12345

Counsel:

Regarding RFP Nos. 7, 8, and 11, Plaintiff asserted
"attorney-client privilege" and "work product" without
providing a privilege log.

ORCP 36 B(2) requires the producing party to "describe the
nature of the documents, communications, or things not
produced or disclosed — and do so in a manner that, without
revealing information itself privileged or protected, will
enable other parties to assess the claim."

Please provide a privilege log within 14 days, by [date],
identifying for each withheld document:

  - Document author and recipient(s)
  - Date
  - General subject matter
  - Specific privilege asserted (attorney-client / work
    product / other)
  - Basis for the privilege (whether and how the document
    falls within the privilege)

A bare assertion of "privilege" without a log is
insufficient and supports motion to compel.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Template 4: ORS 697 registration — failure to produce

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Meet and Confer — ORS 697 Discovery,
         Case No. 25CV12345

Counsel:

Regarding RFP No. 12 (Plaintiff's registration with the
Oregon DCBS under ORS 697.015), Plaintiff [objected on
grounds of irrelevance / failed to respond].

This document is directly relevant to Defendant's
First Affirmative Defense (Plaintiff is an unregistered
collection agency and lacks capacity to maintain this
action under ORS 697.105). Plaintiff's registration status
is a threshold legal issue.

I request that Plaintiff supplement within 14 days by:

  (a) Producing all DCBS registration documents covering
      the period from [date Plaintiff acquired the Account]
      to the present, or

  (b) Stating affirmatively that Plaintiff is not
      registered with DCBS.

If Plaintiff fails to supplement, I will move to compel and
seek summary judgment on the lack-of-capacity defense
under ORCP 47.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Template 5: Telephone call follow-up

After any meet-and-confer phone call, immediately send a
written summary:

```
To:      [Opposing counsel]
Subject: Re: Meet and Confer — [date] Phone Call

Counsel:

Thank you for the call today, [date] at [time], lasting
approximately [duration] minutes.

To confirm what we discussed:

1.  RFP No. 1 (cardholder agreement). You stated [opposing
    party's position]. I disagree because [your reasons].
    [Resolution status: agreed to supplement / remain at
    impasse / agreed to defer pending court order].

2.  RFP No. 3 (Bill of Sale). [Same format.]

3.  RFP No. 4 (Assignment Schedule). [Same format.]

If I have mis-recorded any aspect of our call, please let
me know within 3 business days. Otherwise, I will rely on
this summary in any subsequent motion practice.

Best,
[Name]
```

The opposing party's silence is tacit confirmation. The
record now supports a clean meet-and-confer certification.

## What to do when the opposing party doesn't respond

If your meet-and-confer email gets no response within 7 days:

1. Send a follow-up email referencing the original date and
   substance
2. If no response within another 7 days, the meet-and-confer
   is effectively complete (attempt + no response =
   inability to resolve)
3. File the motion to compel; the certification will recite
   the dates and the lack of response

A non-responsive opposing party makes the motion stronger.

## Pattern: opposing party's "we're producing soon" stall

Some opposing parties offer "we'll supplement in [time]" and
then don't. Counter:

1. Confirm the timeline in writing: "Thank you. Please
   confirm that you will produce by [date]."
2. Calendar the date
3. If the date passes without supplementation, the meet-
   and-confer is complete; file the motion

Don't agree to open-ended supplementation timelines.

## What "good faith" requires

The "good faith" requirement of ORCP 46 A means:

- Reasonable effort to understand opposing's position
- Reasonable effort to propose alternative resolutions
- Reasonable patience (allow 7–14 days for written response;
  follow-up calls are encouraged)

It does NOT require:

- Indefinite delay
- Repeated requests when opposing has clearly indicated
  refusal
- Agreement to unreasonable conditions
