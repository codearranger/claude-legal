# UCC Article 9 (ORS Ch. 79) — Secured Transactions and
Debt-Buyer Cases

Oregon's enactment of UCC Article 9 is at **ORS Chapter 79**.
It governs **secured transactions** — debts that are secured
by collateral. Most credit-card debt is **unsecured**, so
Article 9 doesn't apply directly. But it applies to:

- Auto loans
- Equipment finance
- Secured personal loans
- Some commercial-line transactions

And the chain-of-title analysis from Article 9 (perfection,
priority, assignment) informs debt-buyer analysis even for
unsecured debt.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify current ORS.

## When Article 9 applies in debt-defense

### Secured-debt cases

If the alleged debt is secured (auto loan, secured personal
loan, equipment finance), Article 9 governs:

- Perfection of the security interest (ORS 79.0203)
- Priority among secured creditors (ORS 79.0322)
- Default and repossession (ORS 79.0601 et seq.)
- Disposition of collateral after default (ORS 79.0610)

### Unsecured-debt cases

Article 9 doesn't directly apply, but the **assignment**
concepts in Article 9 inform debt-buyer chain of title:

- ORS 79.0203 — attachment requires authenticated agreement
- ORS 79.0406 — assignment rules
- ORS 79.0102 — definitions

Debt buyers often invoke Article 9 concepts (authenticated
records, assignment to subsequent purchasers) even when the
underlying debt is unsecured.

## Key ORS 79 sections for debt analysis

### ORS 79.0102 — Definitions

- **"Account"** — includes a right to payment for goods or
  services
- **"Account debtor"** — the person obligated on an account
  (the consumer in debt cases)
- **"Authenticate"** — to sign or to execute an electronic
  record with the present intent to adopt

### ORS 79.0203 — Attachment and enforceability

For a security interest to attach:

- Value has been given
- Debtor has rights in the collateral
- One of:
  - Debtor has authenticated a security agreement describing
    the collateral, OR
  - Collateral is in the possession of secured party, OR
  - Collateral is non-tangible electronic chattel paper

If the security agreement is missing or unauthenticated, the
security interest never attached.

### ORS 79.0309 — Filing

Perfection of a security interest in most types of collateral
requires filing a UCC-1 financing statement in the
appropriate office (Secretary of State for most general
collateral; county recorder for fixtures).

### ORS 79.0406 — Discharge of account debtor; notification of
assignment

Critical for debt-buyer cases:

- The account debtor (consumer) may discharge their
  obligation by paying the assignor (original creditor) UNTIL
  they receive notification of the assignment from the
  assignee
- After notification, the consumer must pay the assignee
- Notification must be authenticated and identify the
  assignment

In debt-buyer practice, the question is whether the consumer
received valid notification of each assignment in the chain.
Most consumers did not — the "notification" is often a
collection letter from the latest buyer, not from the
intermediate buyer.

### ORS 79.0610 — Disposition of collateral after default

For secured cases (auto, equipment, etc.):

- Secured party may repossess and sell after default
- Must give notice to debtor before disposition
- Commercial reasonableness required for the sale (ORS
  79.0610(2))
- Failure to give notice or to act commercially reasonably
  triggers ORS 79.0625 remedies for the debtor

In secured-auto debt-buyer cases, the new secured party (debt
buyer) must establish:

- Valid security agreement (ORS 79.0203)
- Proper notification of assignment under ORS 79.0406
- Compliance with ORS 79.0610 notice and disposition
  requirements

A debt buyer that purchased an auto loan deficiency must
prove the original repossession was compliant — otherwise
the deficiency is unenforceable.

## Application in Oregon debt-buyer practice

### Pattern A: Auto loan deficiency

Defendant defaults on an auto loan. Original creditor
repossesses, sells the car at auction, and assigns the
deficiency balance to a debt buyer. Debt buyer sues.

**Defenses**:

- **ORS 79.0610 noncompliance** — original creditor's notice
  of disposition was defective
- **Commercial unreasonableness** — sale at auction was below
  commercially reasonable price
- **Lack of UCC-1 perfection** — if security interest wasn't
  perfected, transaction may be undermined
- **Authentication** — chain-of-title documents must be
  authenticated under OEC 803(6) / 902(11)
- **Notification under ORS 79.0406** — was the consumer
  validly notified of the assignment?

**Counterclaims**:

- **ORS 79.0625** — remedies for noncompliance: actual damages,
  failure to give notice supports class actions
- **Oregon UTPA** — unconscionable conduct in the
  repossession / sale
- **Federal Trade Commission Act** if applicable

### Pattern B: Equipment finance default

Similar analysis to auto loans. Watch for:

- Whether collateral was properly identified in the security
  agreement
- Whether perfection was through UCC-1 filing or other means

### Pattern C: Unsecured debt with attempted ORS 79 framing

Sometimes debt buyers attempt to invoke Article 9 concepts
even for unsecured debt — usually to argue assignment
mechanics. Defense:

- The underlying debt is not "secured" within ORS 79.0102
- The analogous concepts apply by analogy, but the
  authentication / notice requirements of Article 9 are
  not satisfied
- The debt buyer's chain-of-title must be proved under
  general contract assignment law, not Article 9

## Key cases on UCC chain of title

### *In re Veal*, 450 BR 897 (9th Cir BAP 2011)

Bankruptcy court analysis of mortgage chain-of-title under
UCC Article 3 (negotiable instruments). Holds:

- Holder of a note must prove possession of the original
- Mere assignment of the chain isn't enforcement absent
  possession

Applied by analogy to debt-buyer cases. The debt buyer must
either:

- Possess the original note / cardholder agreement, OR
- Prove a continuous chain of authenticated transfers

### Oregon decisions on UCC Article 9 perfection

Standard Article 9 analysis from Oregon courts is well-
developed; check recent Oregon Court of Appeals decisions
for:

- Repossession notice requirements (ORS 79.0610)
- Commercial reasonableness of disposition (ORS 79.0627)
- Assignment notification (ORS 79.0406)

## Discovery targeting Article 9 issues

For secured-debt cases:

- **Security agreement** (RFP)
- **UCC-1 financing statements** filed with Oregon Secretary
  of State
- **Notice of default** sent to debtor before disposition
- **Notice of disposition** sent before sale
- **Sale records** — date, price, buyer, advertising
- **Calculation of deficiency** — sale price, costs, balance

For all cases (secured or unsecured):

- **Notification of assignment under ORS 79.0406** or
  comparable letter to the consumer

## Oregon Secretary of State UCC database

UCC-1 financing statements are filed with the Oregon
Secretary of State and searchable at:

https://sos.oregon.gov/business/Pages/uccs-search.aspx

Search by:

- Debtor name
- Secured party name
- Filing number

If the alleged secured creditor cannot show a current UCC-1
filing, perfection is questionable.
