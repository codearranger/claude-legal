# Declaration Template (Oregon)

A declaration with numbered paragraphs, verification clause,
signature block, and optional exhibit list + cover pages. Compliant
with UTCR 2.010, UTCR 2.100, UTCR 2.110, and ORCP 1 E.

## Structure

1. Court header (centered, bold)
2. Two-column caption (parties left, case number + title right)
3. Horizontal rule
4. Salutation: "I, [Name], declare under penalty of perjury..."
5. Numbered paragraphs
6. Verification clause (per ORCP 1 E)
7. Date and place of execution
8. Signature block
9. Exhibit list (new page)
10. Exhibit cover pages + content

## Notes

- Use "v." between parties, not "vs."
- Number exhibits (not letter them — Oregon convention)
- The verification clause language tracks ORCP 1 E exactly; do
  not paraphrase
- "DATED" line uses fill-in blanks for the litigant to handwrite
  the date if filing in person; if filing electronically, fill it
  in before saving as PDF
- "Executed at [City], Oregon" satisfies 28 USC § 1746 form
  requirements (which ORCP 1 E parallels) — if the declarant is
  outside Oregon, use "[City], [State]" but also state the
  declaration is being made under Oregon law

## What goes in a declaration vs. a motion

- **Declarations** are sworn statements of fact: who, what,
  where, when. They attach exhibits.
- **Motions** are arguments asking for relief; they cite to the
  attached declarations and exhibits.
- Never argue in a declaration ("Plaintiff is clearly trying to
  bully me"). Argument goes in the motion. State facts in the
  declaration. Let the motion characterize them.

## Pro se declaration tone

- First person ("I", "me")
- Past tense for events ("I called the company"); present tense
  for ongoing states ("I do not owe this debt")
- Concrete details: dates, dollar amounts, addresses, names
- One fact per sentence where possible
- Each paragraph = one topical fact or short series of related
  facts
