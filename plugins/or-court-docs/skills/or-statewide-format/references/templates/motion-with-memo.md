# Motion + Memorandum Template (Oregon)

A motion (the "ask") with a companion memorandum (the
"argument"), formatted under UTCR 2.010 and following the Parker
framework adapted to Oregon procedural conventions.

## Structure

1. Caption (court header + parties + case number + title)
2. Motion (short, declaratory — "Defendant moves the Court for an
   order...")
3. Memorandum in support, sections I–VII (see below)
4. Signature block
5. Certificate of Service
6. (Filed separately: Declaration in Support + Exhibits)

## Length and structure rules

- **Length cap**: 6 pages for the memorandum (Parker framework).
  Anything longer should split fact-narrative into the
  declaration and keep argument tight in the memo.
- **Sections I–VII are required** in the Parker framework. Skip
  none. Empty placeholders teach the judge what the structure is
  on the first page.
- **Bold lead-ins** in Argument (Section VI) let the judge skim
  the structure and find the strong points.

## Oregon-specific motion conventions

- **State the rule first**: "Pursuant to ORCP 46 A..." — Oregon
  judges expect the procedural authority on the first line of
  the motion.
- **Oral argument**: state whether requested in the document
  title (e.g., "ORAL ARGUMENT REQUESTED — 20 MINUTES") per UTCR
  5.050. Some local SLRs require this in a separate Notice of
  Hearing rather than the title — see `or-multcc` and `or-wccc`.
- **Mandatory arbitration carve-out**: if the case is in
  court-annexed mandatory arbitration under ORS 36.400, certain
  motions go to the arbitrator rather than the judge. The title
  should reflect that: "MOTION TO COMPEL... (IN ARBITRATION
  PROCEEDING)".
- **Discovery motions and meet-and-confer**: ORCP 46 A and many
  local SLRs (e.g., Multnomah SLR 5.045) require a meet-and-
  confer certification BEFORE the motion is filed. Include the
  certification in the memorandum (Section II) or as a separate
  certificate attached to the motion.
- **Fee request**: when fee-shifting is available (ORCP 46 A(4),
  ORCP 17, ORS 20.075, ORS 20.105), state the amount and method
  of calculation in the conclusion or by separate motion. ORS
  20.190 controls statutory prevailing-party fees; **ORS 20.105**
  controls mandatory fee-shifting for objectively unreasonable
  positions (the rough analog of CR 11 sanctions in Washington).

## ORCP 21 / motion-to-dismiss specific notes

If the motion is under ORCP 21 (motion to dismiss for lack of
jurisdiction, failure to state a claim, insufficiency of service,
etc.), the structure is the same but the title differs:

```
DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO DISMISS UNDER ORCP 21 A(8) FOR FAILURE
TO STATE ULTIMATE FACTS CONSTITUTING A CLAIM
```

Use the precise sub-letter: ORCP 21 A(1) for lack of jurisdiction
over the subject matter, A(2) for personal jurisdiction, A(3) for
proper service, ... A(8) for failure to state a claim, B for
defenses asserted as motions. **Oregon does not use "12(b)(6)"
labels** — those are Federal Rule designations. Use ORCP 21 A(8).
