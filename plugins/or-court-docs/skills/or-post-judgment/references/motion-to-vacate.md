# Motion to Vacate — ORCP 71 (Oregon)

ORCP 71 is the Oregon analog to FRCP 60(b) / WA CR 60. It
governs relief from a final judgment or order.

## Six grounds (ORCP 71 B)

The motion must invoke one of:

1. **Mistake, inadvertence, surprise, or excusable neglect**
2. **Newly discovered evidence** (with reasonable diligence
   not previously discoverable)
3. **Fraud, misrepresentation, or other misconduct of an
   adverse party**
4. **Judgment is void** (no jurisdiction; defective service)
5. **Judgment satisfied, released, or discharged**; or no
   longer equitable to enforce
6. **Any other reason justifying relief** (catch-all)

## Time limits

| Ground | Time limit |
|--------|------------|
| (1), (2), (3) | "Reasonable time", not exceeding **1 year** after entry |
| (4) | "Reasonable time" — no fixed cap, but diligence matters |
| (5) | "Reasonable time" — no fixed cap |
| (6) | "Reasonable time" — judicial discretion; "extraordinary circumstances" standard |

The 1-year cap is **strict** for grounds (1)–(3). Beyond 1
year, the defendant must fit into ground (4)–(6), which is
narrower.

## What the motion must prove

Under *Wagar v. Prudential Ins. Co. of America*, 276 Or 827,
556 P2d 658 (1976), and the recent ORCP 71 line of decisions:

1. **A specific ground** — invoke one of (1)–(6) and show
   facts establishing it
2. **Diligence** — the defendant moved promptly upon learning
   of the judgment ("reasonable time")
3. **Meritorious defense** — if the judgment is vacated, the
   defendant has a real defense to advance on the merits, not
   just a desire to re-litigate
4. **Lack of prejudice to the plaintiff** — vacating won't
   unfairly harm plaintiff (lost evidence, faded memory of
   witnesses, etc.)

## Supporting declaration — required content

The declaration must:

1. **Identify the declarant** — "I am the Defendant in this
   action"
2. **State the facts of service** — what the process server
   says happened, contrasted with what actually happened
3. **State when the defendant learned of the judgment** —
   the diligence narrative
4. **State the meritorious defense** — at least summary level
5. **Attach exhibits** — return of service, default judgment,
   the proposed answer

The declaration must be under penalty of perjury (ORCP 1 E).
The defendant's own personal-knowledge declaration is the
strongest evidence; supplement with declarations from family
members, employers, medical providers, etc., as the facts
support.

## Stay of execution pending the motion

Filing the motion does NOT automatically stay execution.
Concurrent options:

### Option A: Stipulated stay

If plaintiff agrees, file a stipulated motion for stay
pending the vacate motion. Plaintiffs sometimes agree because
contested vacation litigation is more expensive than agreed
stays.

### Option B: Motion for stay

File a Motion for Stay of Execution Pending Motion to Vacate.
Show:

- Likely success on the merits of the vacate motion
- Irreparable harm without stay (garnishment will continue;
  bank account will be drained)
- Balance of equities favors stay
- Public interest

The court typically rules on the stay motion before the vacate
motion. If denied, garnishment continues — file the exemption
claim under ORS 18.700+ in parallel.

## Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Missing the 1-year cap for grounds (1)–(3) | Must proceed under (4)–(6); harder |
| No supporting declaration | Motion fails — must have personal-knowledge facts |
| No proposed answer attached | Court can't see the meritorious defense |
| Filing without serving plaintiff | Procedural failure |
| Not asserting a specific ORCP 71 ground | Court doesn't know which standard to apply |
| Insufficient diligence (long delay between learning and filing) | Court denies for non-diligence |
| Conclusory "I have a defense" without specifics | Insufficient meritorious-defense showing |

## After the motion is filed

- File a Notice of Hearing (see `or-multcc` / local SLR)
- Prepare for the hearing (see `or-hearings`)
- Continue claiming exemptions in any ongoing garnishment
  (don't let the levy continue without challenge)
- If the motion is granted, immediately file the answer that
  was attached as Exhibit 4
- If denied, consider an appeal under ORS 19.255 (30 days)
