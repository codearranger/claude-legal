# Supplemental Proceedings — Oregon Debtor Examination

After judgment, the creditor may serve a **Notice of Debtor's
Examination** (often called a "supplemental proceeding" or
"OEX" — Order for Examination) under **ORS 18.265 et seq.**
requiring the debtor to appear and answer questions about
assets, income, and ability to satisfy the judgment.

## Mechanics

- **Service**: under ORS 18.265 — typically personal service of
  the Notice
- **Location**: courthouse, before the judge or a court
  reporter
- **Duration**: typically 30–90 minutes; complex cases can run
  longer
- **Recording**: official court recording or court reporter
- **Subpoena duces tecum**: creditor may attach a subpoena
  requiring production of specified financial records
  (bank statements, pay stubs, tax returns, vehicle titles,
  retirement statements)

## What's asked

The creditor's lawyer (or pro per creditor) asks about:

- **Employment**: employer, position, pay rate, pay schedule
- **Bank accounts**: institution, type, balance, signers
- **Real property**: ownership, addresses, mortgages, value
- **Vehicles**: make/model, year, lien, value
- **Other assets**: investment accounts, retirement, jewelry,
  business interests, accounts receivable owed to the debtor,
  tax refunds expected, lawsuits as plaintiff
- **Income sources**: wages, benefits, gifts, side work
- **Transfers in the last 4 years**: any property transferred
  to family or others (relevant under fraudulent-transfer
  analysis, ORS 95.230)

## Mandatory truthful answers

Under oath. **Perjury is a felony** (ORS 162.065). The debtor
must answer truthfully even when the answer is unhelpful to
their position.

## Claiming exemptions on the record

If the creditor asks about exempt property, the debtor:

1. **Answers the question truthfully** — identifies the
   property
2. **Asserts the exemption** — names the property as exempt
   and cites the statute

Example:

> Q: Do you have any bank accounts?
>
> A: Yes, I have a checking account at Wells Fargo, account
> ending 4567. The balance currently is approximately $850.
> These funds are protected as Social Security direct
> deposits under 42 USC § 407, and I am claiming the exemption.

The debtor must affirmatively claim — silence is treated as
evasion.

Example list of common exempt-property assertions:

| Property | Statute |
|----------|---------|
| Direct-deposit federal benefits | 42 USC § 407; 31 CFR 212 |
| 75% of wages | ORS 18.385 |
| Tools of trade | ORS 18.345 |
| One motor vehicle (up to $3,000) | ORS 18.345 |
| Retirement (401k, IRA, PERS) | ORS 18.358 |
| Homestead | ORS 18.395 |

## Document production

If the Notice includes a subpoena duces tecum, bring the
specified documents. Failure to produce can support
contempt (ORS 18.275).

Common subpoenaed documents:

- 6 months of bank statements
- 3–4 recent pay stubs
- Tax returns (last 2 years)
- Vehicle titles
- Retirement statements
- Mortgage statements

If a document is privileged or doesn't exist, state so on the
record. Don't simply withhold without explanation.

## Procedural protections for the debtor

- **No fishing expedition into pre-judgment matters** — the
  inquiry is limited to assets and ability to pay (see ORS
  18.265). Past disputes about the underlying debt are not
  within scope.
- **No questions about confidential third parties** — bank
  accounts of unrelated family members aren't discoverable
- **No questions about spousal income** — except as relates
  to the debtor's standard of living
- **Reasonable accommodations** for medical or disability
  needs

## Failure to appear

Failing to appear at a scheduled supplemental proceeding can
result in:

- **Bench warrant** for the debtor's arrest (ORS 18.275)
- **Contempt order**
- **Money judgment for the creditor's costs**

If unable to appear for legitimate reasons (medical, work),
contact the creditor's counsel and the court **in advance**
to request a continuance.

## What the creditor does with the information

The information from the exam supports:

- **Writs of garnishment** on identified accounts
- **Writs of execution** on non-exempt property
- **Fraudulent-transfer actions** if the debtor disclosed
  transfers within 4 years (ORS 95.230)
- **Further exams** every 6 months (subject to court approval)

## Pro se preparation

### Before the exam

- **Read the Notice carefully** — date, time, location, what
  documents are demanded
- **Gather subpoenaed documents** in advance
- **Make a list of exempt property** with statutory grounds
- **Practice asserting exemptions** ("These funds are Social
  Security direct deposits, exempt under 42 USC § 407")
- **Bring a notebook** to take notes during the exam

### At the exam

- **Answer truthfully** — under oath
- **Speak clearly** — for the record
- **Listen carefully** to each question; ask for clarification
  if needed
- **Take a moment** before answering complex questions
- **Don't volunteer** — answer what's asked, no more
- **Claim exemptions affirmatively** when relevant property
  comes up
- **Don't sign anything** the creditor's lawyer hands you
  without reading

### After the exam

- **Get a copy of any recording or transcript** for your
  records
- **Update your case calendar** — the creditor may now issue
  new writs; the 30-day Challenge window resets for each
  writ
- **Don't make new transfers** of disclosed property — that
  would be a fraudulent transfer subject to ORS 95.230

## Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Failing to claim an exemption on the record | Property treated as non-exempt and seized |
| Lying about assets | Perjury (felony) |
| Failing to produce subpoenaed documents | Contempt |
| Missing the exam date | Bench warrant |
| Volunteering information about non-asked topics | Hands the creditor leverage |
| Signing settlement documents without reading | May waive rights |

## Multiple exams

The creditor can request a follow-up exam every 6 months
under ORS 18.265. The debtor must appear unless they have a
specific objection (e.g., the creditor is harassing without
new asset basis).
