# Washington County Courthouse (Hillsboro, Oregon)

## Address and contact

- Street: **145 NE 2nd Avenue, Hillsboro OR 97124**
- Civil Division: (503) 846-2950
- Court Administrator: (503) 846-2924
- Court website:
  https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/washington/Pages/default.aspx

## Hours

- Court business hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM, Monday–Friday
- Civil Division counter: 8:30 AM – 4:00 PM
- Closed on state legal holidays (ORS 187.010)

## Getting there

- **MAX Light Rail Blue Line**: Hatfield Government Center
  station, two blocks from the courthouse
- **Bus**: Multiple TriMet bus lines serve downtown Hillsboro
- **Parking**: Public parking garage at SE 2nd & Walnut, two
  blocks south of the courthouse
- **Bike**: Bike racks at the main NE 2nd entrance

## Building layout

The Washington County Courthouse is a multi-floor building with:

- **Lobby and security**: Main entrance on NE 2nd Avenue
- **Civil Division (clerk's office)**: 2nd floor
- **Civil courtrooms**: 4th–7th floors (assignments rotate)
- **Family law**: 3rd floor
- **Probate / small claims**: 2nd floor
- **Juvenile**: Juvenile Services Center, 222 SW 1st Avenue
  (separate building)

Security screening at the main entrance — allow 15 minutes on
hearing days.

## Civil Division

The Civil Division clerk's office handles:

- Counter filings (when eFiling is excused — fee waiver pending,
  etc.)
- Hearing date reservations (per WCCC SLR 5.025)
- Document pickup
- Public file inspection (per UTCR 11)
- Civil Self-Help (limited hours; verify on website)

The Civil Division is the primary contact point for scheduling
motions. Email: WCCCCivilCT@ojd.state.or.us. Phone: (503) 846-
2950.

## Working-copy delivery

Working copies (WCCC SLR 5.100) are delivered to the **Civil
Division clerk's counter** on the 2nd floor. The clerk routes to
the assigned judge's chambers internally. For very large
packets (over 200 pages), call ahead to arrange delivery to
chambers directly.

Working copies should:

- Be tabbed (each enclosed document on its own tab)
- Be bound (binder clip or staple acceptable for smaller
  packets; perfect binding for large packets)
- Have a cover with the case caption, hearing date, and a short
  index of contents
- Reference the case number and hearing date prominently

## Judge roster

Washington County has approximately 15 circuit court judges.
Civil docket judges rotate every two years (typical January 1
cycle). Current judges should be looked up from the OJD website
each session.

## Civil Self-Help

Washington County has a limited Civil Self-Help program (less
robust than Multnomah's). For self-represented parties:

- The Civil Division clerk's counter answers procedural
  questions ("when is my answer due?")
- Free legal-aid referrals available through Legal Aid Services
  of Oregon, Tualatin Valley regional office
- Forms are available on the OJD statewide forms page
  (https://www.courts.oregon.gov/forms)

The Washington County Bar Association also runs a Modest Means
Panel for reduced-fee legal representation.

## After-hours filings

For ex parte emergencies after normal hours (TRO, immediate
protective orders), call the Court Administrator's after-hours
number, available from the main court line.

## Disclaimer

Court layouts, hours, contact addresses, and rosters change.
Verify against the Washington County Circuit Court website
before any in-person proceeding. Do not rely on cached values.
