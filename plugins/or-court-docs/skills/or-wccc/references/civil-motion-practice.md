# Washington County Civil Motion Practice

## The scheduling model — lightly centralized

Washington County sits between Multnomah's individual-calendar
model and the older centralized-motion-docket model used by
many smaller Oregon counties.

- **Date reservations**: through the Civil Division clerk (not
  the assigned judge's JA directly)
- **Hearings**: on the assigned judge's calendar, not a
  centralized motion docket
- **Practical effect**: faster scheduling than Multnomah; less
  judge-specific customization

## Step-by-step

### 1. Identify the assigned judge

From the OJD Case Information page or File and Serve.

### 2. Email Civil Division to reserve

Send an email to **WCCCCivilCT@ojd.state.or.us** (or call (503)
846-2950) with case number, motion type, estimated time, and
proposed dates. Include the assigned judge's name.

### 3. Receive confirmation

Civil Division replies (usually within 1–2 business days) with
the confirmed date, courtroom, and mode. If the assigned judge's
JA needs to weigh in, the Civil Division loops them.

### 4. File the motion AND Notice of Hearing simultaneously

WCCC SLR 5.045 requires the Notice to be filed **with** the
motion, not later. This is different from Multnomah (where the
Notice follows the JA's confirmation by a few days).

### 5. Serve under ORCP 9 / UTCR 21.100

eService for registered parties; mail or hand delivery for
others.

### 6. Working copies if over 15 pages

Deliver to Civil Division counter on the 2nd floor.

### 7. Confirmations and continuances

- **Confirm**: Some judges require confirmation 3 court days
  before hearing. Check the assigned judge's standing order.
- **Continue**: File an agreed motion or motion for continuance
  via File and Serve, copy Civil Division, copy the JA.
- **Strike**: Email the Civil Division at least 24 hours in
  advance.

## Discovery motions — meet and confer (WCCC SLR 5.046)

Like Multnomah SLR 5.045, Washington County SLR 5.046 requires
a meet-and-confer certification on every discovery motion. The
certification must state:

- The specific discovery issue
- Date(s) and method(s) of conference
- The opposing party's position
- That the parties have been unable to resolve

If the certification is defective or missing, the motion can be
denied without prejudice and the parties ordered to confer.

## Hearing modes

Washington County courts vary by judge:

- Some default to WebEx for routine civil motions
- Others default to in-person
- Mode is identified on the Civil Division's confirmation email
  and on the Notice of Hearing
- Telephone conferences are available for short matters and
  status conferences (per WCCC SLR 7.010)

For self-represented parties:

- WebEx is generally accessible — the connection URL is on the
  confirmation email and the Notice of Hearing
- If technology access is a barrier, contact the Civil Division
  to arrange a telephone appearance or in-person attendance

## Mandatory arbitration (ORS 36.400)

Cases between $10,000 and $50,000 go to arbitration. WCCC SLR
8.010 implements:

- Arbitrators selected from the OJD panel (the Washington
  County Bar maintains a regional sub-roster)
- Arbitration hearing typically within 90 days of case
  assignment
- Award filed with the court; trial de novo available under
  ORS 36.425 within 20 days
- Trial de novo fee-shifting: if the appealing party does not
  improve on the award by more than 10%, ORS 36.425(5) shifts
  fees against them

## Pretrial conference

WCCC SLR 12.010 sets a pretrial conference 30–60 days before
trial. Counsel and pro se parties must appear (in person or
WebEx per the judge's standing order). The PTC covers:

- Status of discovery
- Witness lists
- Exhibit lists and stipulations as to admissibility
- Jury selection logistics (for jury trials)
- Settlement prospects

A trial-readiness form may be required — check the assigned
judge's standing order.

## Order submission (WCCC SLR 5.101)

Proposed orders are submitted to chambers via the assigned
judge's email (look up on the OJD website) or via File and
Serve "Order — Proposed" document code. The submitting party
should:

- Serve the proposed order on opposing parties 3 days in advance
  (UTCR 5.100)
- Include a Notice of Presentation (or "Approval as to Form"
  line for opposing counsel if they have approved)
- Attach both `.docx` (chambers needs an editable copy for bench
  modifications) and PDF

## Small claims and FED

Washington County's small claims (≤ $10,000) and forcible-
entry-and-detainer (FED, landlord-tenant) cases run on
separate dockets in the Civil Department:

- Small claims: ORS Ch. 46; informal trial without lawyers (for
  the plaintiff; defendant may have counsel)
- FED: ORS Ch. 105; expedited landlord-tenant eviction
  procedure with the **First Appearance** at 7 days from
  service

Neither uses the standard motion practice — see ORS 46.405 for
small claims procedure and ORS 105.135 for FED.

## After-hours and emergency

For TROs, immediate protective orders, and other after-hours
emergencies, call the Court Administrator at the main court
line; the after-hours line routes to the duty judge.
