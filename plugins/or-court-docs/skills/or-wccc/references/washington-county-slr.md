# Washington County (Oregon) Supplemental Local Rules

## Authoritative source

**Pull the current PDF from**:
https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/washington/rules

Washington County updates its SLR on an irregular cadence —
verify against the current PDF before citing in a brief.

## SLR sections most relevant to civil practice

### Chapter 1 — General

- **SLR 1.001** — Effective date and scope. Applies to all
  civil cases filed in Washington County Circuit Court.

### Chapter 5 — Motions

- **SLR 5.025** — Setting motions through the Civil Division.
  Date reservations go to (503) 846-2950 or
  WCCCCivilCT@ojd.state.or.us.
- **SLR 5.045** — Notice of Hearing filed simultaneously with
  motion. (Different from Multnomah, which allows up to 3
  business days after JA confirmation.)
- **SLR 5.046** — Meet-and-confer certification on discovery
  motions. Required content: specific issue, date/method of
  conference, opposing party's position, inability to resolve.
- **SLR 5.060** — Ex parte motions. Notice required unless
  excused; civil ex parte at Civil Division counter.
- **SLR 5.100** — Working copies. Required for motions where
  total packet exceeds 15 pages (lower threshold than
  Multnomah's 25-page floor).
- **SLR 5.101** — Submission of orders. Submitted to assigned
  judge's chambers via email or File and Serve.

### Chapter 7 — Conferences and remote hearings

- **SLR 7.010** — Telephonic and remote hearings. Mode set by
  the assigned judge's standing order; identified on Civil
  Division confirmation and Notice of Hearing.

### Chapter 8 — Mandatory arbitration (ORS 36.400)

- **SLR 8.010** — Mandatory arbitration program. Cases
  $10,000–$50,000. Arbitrator panel maintained by the OJD;
  Washington County Bar regional sub-panel. Trial de novo
  under ORS 36.425.

### Chapter 12 — Pretrial and trial

- **SLR 12.010** — Pretrial conference 30–60 days before trial.
  Counsel and pro se must appear.

### Chapter 14 — ADR

- **SLR 14.010** — Mediation referrals available by stipulation
  or court order.

### Chapter 21 — eFiling (parallels UTCR 21)

- **SLR 21.010** — eFiling required for civil filings.

## Frequent error patterns

1. **Filing the Notice of Hearing later than the motion**.
   WCCC SLR 5.045 requires simultaneous filing. Sequential
   filing risks the motion being treated as un-noted.
2. **Working-copy threshold confusion**. WCCC's 15-page
   threshold is lower than Multnomah's. A 16-page motion (with
   exhibits) in Washington County requires working copies; the
   same motion in Multnomah does not.
3. **Going to the JA directly for date reservation**. Civil
   Division is the gatekeeper in Washington County. The JA may
   redirect you back to Civil Division.
4. **Missing the meet-and-confer certification (SLR 5.046)**.
   Like Multnomah's SLR 5.045, defective or missing
   certification supports denial of the discovery motion.

## Cross-references

- **Standing orders** — Each civil judge has a standing order
  on motion practice. Pull from the judge's page on the OJD
  website.
- **OJD UTCR** — see `or-law-references/references/court-rules/
  UTCR.md` once pulled.
- **Civil Self-Help** — limited program; the Civil Division
  counter answers procedural questions but cannot give legal
  advice. Free legal-aid referrals via Legal Aid Services of
  Oregon (Tualatin Valley).
