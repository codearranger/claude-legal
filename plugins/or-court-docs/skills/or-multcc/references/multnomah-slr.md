# Multnomah Supplemental Local Rules (SLR) — Summary

The Multnomah SLR sit on top of the statewide UTCR. When the
two conflict, the UTCR controls (UTCR 1.040). But when the UTCR
is silent, the SLR fills in.

**Authoritative source**: pull the current PDF from
https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/multnomah/rules

Multnomah updates its SLR more frequently than other Oregon
counties (typically annual revisions effective February 1 or
August 1). Do not cite a specific SLR section from memory —
always verify against the current PDF.

## SLR sections most relevant to civil practice

### Chapter 1 — General

- **SLR 1.001** — Effective date and scope. The SLR applies to
  all civil cases filed in Multnomah Circuit Court, including
  small claims and mandatory arbitration matters.

### Chapter 5 — Motions

- **SLR 5.015** — Notice of Hearing on Motion. The Notice must
  be filed within 3 business days of the JA confirming the date.
  Format is per the statewide template (see `or-statewide-
  format/references/templates/notice-of-hearing.md`).
- **SLR 5.025** — Setting motions. Motions are set by request to
  the assigned judge's JA. See `civil-motion-scheduling.md`.
- **SLR 5.045** — Meet-and-confer certification (discovery
  motions). Every discovery motion under ORCP 36, 43, 44, 45,
  or 46 must include a certification.
- **SLR 5.060** — Ex parte motions. Generally require notice to
  opposing counsel unless excused; civil ex parte hours are
  Monday–Friday 8:30 AM – 12:00 PM in the Ex Parte Department.
- **SLR 5.100** — Working copies. Required for motions over 25
  pages total (including exhibits and proposed order), summary
  judgment motions, and any motion where the assigned judge so
  requests. Working copies are tabbed paper copies delivered to
  chambers.
- **SLR 5.101** — Submission of orders. Proposed orders are
  submitted to chambers (not the clerk) via email to the JA,
  attaching both `.docx` and PDF.

### Chapter 7 — Conferences and remote hearings

- **SLR 7.010** — Telephonic and remote hearings. WebEx is the
  default for most civil motions; in-person is reserved for
  evidentiary hearings, summary judgment, and complex matters.
  The hearing mode goes on the Notice of Hearing.

### Chapter 8 — Mandatory arbitration (ORS 36.400)

- **SLR 8.010** — Mandatory arbitration program. Cases with
  amounts in controversy between $10,000 and $50,000 go to
  arbitration. Arbitrators are selected from the OJD arbitrator
  panel. Trial de novo is available under ORS 36.425, subject
  to fee-shifting if the appealing party does not improve on
  the arbitration award by more than 10%.

### Chapter 12 — Pretrial and trial

- **SLR 12.010** — Pretrial conferences. Mandatory in non-
  arbitration cases set for trial. Held 30–60 days before trial.
  Counsel and pro se parties must appear in person unless
  excused.

### Chapter 14 — ADR and mediation

- **SLR 14.010** — Mediation referrals. Multnomah has an active
  court-annexed mediation program. Cases may be referred to
  mediation at any time by stipulation of the parties or by
  court order. Most mediations are conducted via private
  mediators on the OJD roster.

### Chapter 21 — eFiling (parallels UTCR 21)

- **SLR 21.010** — eFiling required for all civil filings.
  Document codes must match the UTCR 2.110 title.

## Frequent error patterns

When porting practice from another county or from Washington:

1. **Forgetting the meet-and-confer certification on discovery
   motions** — Multnomah SLR 5.045 is stricter than UTCR; many
   counties allow a less formal showing. In Multnomah, the
   certification's absence is grounds for denial.
2. **Trying to set motions on a centralized calendar** — there
   is none. Always email the assigned judge's JA.
3. **Submitting orders through the clerk's office** — Multnomah
   SLR 5.101 routes orders directly to chambers. Submitting via
   File and Serve "Order — Proposed" is the eFile equivalent;
   the JA pulls from there or from chambers email.
4. **Missing the working-copy threshold** — anything over 25
   pages requires working copies under SLR 5.100. Pro se filers
   sometimes assume eFiling supersedes; it does not.
5. **Mode mismatch on Notice of Hearing** — if the JA confirms
   WebEx and the Notice says "in person", the hearing may not
   proceed. Confirm the mode in writing.

## Cross-references

- **Standing orders** — Each Multnomah civil judge has a
  standing order on motion practice, working copies, and pretrial
  procedure. These supplement the SLR and are linked from the
  judge's page on the OJD website. Pull the assigned judge's
  standing order at the start of any new case.
- **OJD UTCR** — see `or-law-references/references/court-rules/
  UTCR.md` (re-pull from courts.oregon.gov on a rule-change
  cycle).
- **Multnomah Civil Self-Help** — Floor 3, Central Courthouse;
  pro se filer assistance.
