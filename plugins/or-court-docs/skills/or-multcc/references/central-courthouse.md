# Multnomah Central Courthouse — Venue Information

The **Multnomah County Central Courthouse** opened in 2020 at
1200 SW 1st Avenue, Portland OR 97204. It replaced the historic
Multnomah County Courthouse (1914), which closed in 2020. The
Central Courthouse houses all civil, criminal-felony, family, and
probate proceedings for Multnomah County.

## Address and contact

- Street: 1200 SW 1st Avenue, Portland OR 97204
- Civil Division phone: (503) 988-3022
- Court Administrator phone: (503) 988-3022
- Court website:
  https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/multnomah/Pages/default.aspx

## Hours

- Court business hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM, Monday–Friday
- Filing window (clerk's office): 8:30 AM – 4:00 PM, Monday–Friday
- Closed on state legal holidays (ORS 187.010)

## Getting there

- **TriMet**: Multiple bus lines stop at SW 1st & Jefferson. The
  MAX light rail Yellow/Green/Red lines stop at SW 5th & Pine,
  five blocks east.
- **Bike**: Bike racks at the SW 1st Avenue entrance.
- **Parking**: Limited metered street parking. The Smart Park
  garages at SW 1st & Jefferson and 4th & Yamhill are the
  closest public lots.

## Building layout

The Central Courthouse is 17 stories. Civil courtrooms are on
floors 4–7; criminal felony on floors 8–11; family law on
floors 12–13; probate on floor 14. Specific courtroom
assignments rotate; check the assigned judge's page on the OJD
website for the current courtroom.

Public entrance is through the SW 1st Avenue lobby; security
screening (metal detector + bag X-ray) is required. Allow 15–20
minutes for security on hearing days.

## Civil Department

The Civil Department clerk's office is on Floor 3. They handle:

- Counter filings (paper filings, when a fee waiver is in
  place or the filing is exempt from eFiling)
- Document pickup (signed orders, certified copies)
- Information for self-represented parties

The Civil Department also runs the Multnomah **Family Law
Facilitator** and **Civil Self-Help** programs (free help with
form completion; staff cannot give legal advice). Hours and
schedule: https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/multnomah/help

## Working-copy delivery

Working copies for motions in pending civil cases (Multnomah SLR
5.100) are delivered to:

- The **mail room** on the loading-dock side of the building
  (accessible from the back entrance), addressed to the assigned
  judge's chambers
- The **courtroom directly** during business hours, given to the
  courtroom clerk or judicial assistant

The mail room receives chambers mail Monday–Friday 8:00 AM –
4:00 PM. After 4:00 PM, working copies must be hand-delivered to
the courtroom the next business morning before the hearing.

## Judge roster

Multnomah has approximately 35 active circuit court judges
(rotating; check the OJD website for the current roster). Civil
judges rotate dockets every two years (typical assignment cycle:
January 1 – December 31, two-year terms).

Current civil docket judges should be looked up from the OJD
website each session — assignments rotate.

## Where pro se filers go for help

The Multnomah Civil Self-Help office is on Floor 3 of the Central
Courthouse. Staff cannot give legal advice but can:

- Help with form completion
- Explain the filing process
- Direct to free legal-aid organizations (Legal Aid Services of
  Oregon, Multnomah Bar Modest Means Panel)
- Provide a workstation for File and Serve access

Hours and scheduling: see https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/
multnomah/help/Pages/civil-self-help.aspx

## Disclaimer

Court layouts, hours, and rosters change. Before any in-person
proceeding, verify courtroom assignment, judge, and hours via the
Multnomah Circuit Court website. Do not rely on cached values.
