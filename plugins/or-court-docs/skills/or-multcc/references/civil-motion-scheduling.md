# Multnomah Civil Motion Scheduling

## Why Multnomah is different

Most Oregon circuit courts use a centralized motion docket (one
or two judges handle all civil motions on rotating weeks).
Multnomah is **individual-calendar**: every motion is heard by
the assigned trial judge. This means setting a motion in
Multnomah is a one-on-one negotiation with the assigned judge's
**judicial assistant (JA)** rather than a calendar clerk.

## Two-track scheduling

Multnomah civil cases have two trial tracks set by Multnomah SLR
1.001 (and the OJD case-management standards under UTCR 7):

- **Expedited / Mandatory Arbitration Track**: ≤ $50,000 cases
  go to mandatory arbitration under ORS 36.400; motions during
  arbitration go to the arbitrator
- **Standard Civil Track**: > $50,000 cases; motions go to the
  assigned circuit judge

If the case is in arbitration, see SLR 8.010 and the **Arbitration
Commissioner's** office for motion scheduling (the Commissioner
handles motions about the arbitration process, not the
substantive motions before the arbitrator).

## Step-by-step: setting a motion before a circuit judge

### 1. Identify the assigned judge

The assigned judge is listed on the OJD Case Information page
(https://publicaccess.courts.oregon.gov/ — case lookup) and in
File and Serve. For a newly filed case, the assignment usually
appears within 24–48 hours of filing.

### 2. Look up the JA

The JA roster is at
https://www.courts.oregon.gov/courts/multnomah/judges/Pages/default.aspx

Each judge's page lists their JA name and email. **Pull the
current page each time** — assignments rotate.

### 3. Email the JA with a date request

Format:

```
To:      [JA email]
Subject: Hearing Date Request — [Case Short Title], [Case No.]

Dear [JA Name]:

I represent [self] in the above-captioned matter, assigned to
the Hon. [Judge].

I am requesting a hearing date for [Motion Title], filed [date]
(or to be filed [date]). I estimate [X minutes] for argument.

Proposed dates:
   [Date 1] — [Time preference, e.g., morning]
   [Date 2]
   [Date 3]

[I have / I have not] conferred with opposing counsel about
these dates. Opposing counsel [agrees / objects / has not
responded].

If the Court prefers WebEx, please confirm and I will include
the connection details on the Notice of Hearing.

Thank you,
[Name][, pro se / OSB #]
[Contact info]
```

### 4. Receive the JA's confirmation

The JA replies typically within 1–2 business days. The reply
identifies:

- Confirmed date and time
- Courtroom number (or "WebEx — see attached link")
- Whether opposing counsel must be looped in
- Whether working copies are required (judge-specific)

### 5. File the Notice of Hearing

File a Notice of Hearing on File and Serve (use document code
"Notice of Hearing") within 3 business days of the JA's
confirmation. Serve all parties under ORCP 9 / UTCR 21.100.

### 6. Working copies (Multnomah SLR 5.100)

A working copy is a tabbed paper copy of the motion packet for
the judge's chambers. Multnomah SLR 5.100 requires working copies
for:

- Motions where the total page count (motion + memo + decl +
  exhibits + proposed order) exceeds 25 pages
- Summary judgment motions (always)
- Trial briefs
- Any motion where the assigned judge has so requested

Working copies are delivered to the mail room (loading dock,
1st Avenue side) or directly to the courtroom. The cover should
include:

- Case caption and number
- Title of the motion
- Hearing date and time
- A short index of what's enclosed (e.g., "Tabs 1–6: Motion,
  Memorandum, Declaration of [Name] with Exhibits 1–4, Proposed
  Order")

Pro se filers: mailing the working copy via USPS is acceptable
(addressed to: Hon. [Judge], c/o [JA Name], Multnomah County
Central Courthouse, 1200 SW 1st Ave, Portland OR 97204 —
**reference the case number** prominently on the envelope).

### 7. Confirmations and strikes

- **Confirm**: Multnomah does not have a uniform confirm-by-X-
  date rule. Some judges have a standing order requiring
  confirmation 3 court days out; check the JA's email signature
  block or the judge's standing-order page.
- **Strike**: To strike a hearing (cancel), email the JA at
  least 24 hours in advance and copy opposing counsel.
- **Continue**: A continuance requires an agreed motion or a
  motion for continuance — emailing the JA is not enough.

## Hearing modes

| Mode | When used | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **In person** | Default for evidentiary hearings, summary judgment, complex motions | Central Courthouse only; security 15–20 min |
| **WebEx** | Default for routine motions in most civil departments | JA emails connection details; record on Notice |
| **Telephone** | Some judges allow for short matters | Confirm with JA |
| **Hybrid** | Rare; party in court + opposing on WebEx | Confirm with JA |

The mode goes on the Notice of Hearing (UTCR 7.010 / Multnomah
SLR 7.010).

## Discovery motions — meet and confer

Multnomah SLR 5.045 requires a **meet-and-confer certification**
on every discovery motion. The certification must state:

- The specific issue presented to the opposing party
- The date(s) and method(s) of conference
- The opposing party's position
- That the parties have been unable to resolve the dispute

A defective certification (missing date, missing position) is
grounds for the court to **deny the motion without prejudice**
and require the parties to confer again. See `or-discovery` for
the full meet-and-confer protocol.

## Ex parte and emergency motions

Multnomah handles ex parte (without notice to opposing) and
emergency motions through the **Ex Parte Department**, which
sits in a dedicated courtroom on Floor 4. Schedule:

- Civil ex parte hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 12:00 PM (varies
  — check the Ex Parte schedule on the website)
- Filing: walk in with the motion, declaration, and proposed
  order; or eFile and contact the Ex Parte clerk
- Notice: ex parte motions require advance notice to opposing
  counsel unless excused (ORCP 7 H, UTCR 5.060)

Emergency motions (TRO, immediate protective order) — call the
Civil Division at (503) 988-3022 to get routed to the on-duty
judge.

## Setting trial dates and case schedules

Trial dates in Multnomah are set at the **First Appearance**
hearing or by an Order Setting Civil Case Schedule (Multnomah
SLR 12.010 / UTCR 7.010). The case schedule sets:

- Discovery cutoff (typically 30 days before trial)
- Motion cutoff (typically 60 days before trial)
- Pretrial conference date
- Trial date

A motion to extend the case schedule is required if the parties
need additional time. The motion must be set on a date before
the affected deadline.
