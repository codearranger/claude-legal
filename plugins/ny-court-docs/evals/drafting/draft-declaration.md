# ny-draft-declaration — CPLR 2106 Affirmation in Support

## Prompt

Draft an affirmation supporting my motion in NY County
Supreme Court. I need to explain that the plaintiff debt
buyer hasn't produced the assignment paperwork from the
original creditor (Citibank) and refused to confirm the
sale price. I am pro se.

## Expected triggers

- `ny-draft-declaration`
- `ny-statewide-format`
- `ny-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria

### Form

- [ ] Uses the **post-2023 universal CPLR 2106 affirmation**
      ("I [name] affirm this {NN} day of {month} {year}
       under the penalties of perjury under the laws of New
       York that the following is true...") — NOT the pre-
       2023 attorney-only form, NOT a CPLR 2309 notarized
       affidavit
- [ ] Caption with `-against-` separator and Index No.
- [ ] Numbered paragraphs starting from "1."
- [ ] Each substantive paragraph one assertion
- [ ] Signature block compatible with pro se (no OCA bar
      number; no NY Attorney Registration #)
- [ ] Date and signature line

### Content

- [ ] States affiant's competence (over 18, personal
      knowledge of the facts)
- [ ] Attaches exhibits by letter (A, B, C, ...) and
      references them in the body
- [ ] Avoids legal conclusions in the affidavit/affirmation
      itself (those belong in the affirmation in support of
      the motion or the brief)
- [ ] Final paragraph affirms statements under the penalties
      of perjury under the laws of New York

## Common failure modes

- Uses CPLR 2309 affidavit form requiring a notary even
  though the post-2023 CPLR 2106 form is available to all
- Pro se forgets to omit attorney bar number / firm name
- Mixes affidavit and brief argument inside the affirmation
  body
- Forgets the "under the penalties of perjury under the laws
  of New York" closing language
