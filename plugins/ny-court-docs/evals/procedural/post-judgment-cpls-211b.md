# ny-post-judgment — 20-year money-judgment SOL (CPLR 211(b))

## Prompt

A creditor obtained a NY money judgment against me in 2010.
Can they still enforce it?

## Expected triggers

- `ny-post-judgment`
- `ny-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Cites **CPLR 211(b)**: 20-year SOL on actions to
      enforce money judgments
- [ ] Notes that 2010 + 20 = 2030, so YES, still enforceable
      (in the current 2026 timeframe)
- [ ] Cites **CPLR 5014** procedure for renewing a judgment
      before the 20 years runs
- [ ] Identifies enforcement mechanics: CPLR Article 52
      (income execution at § 5231, restraining notice at
      § 5222, judgment debtor's exam at § 5224)
- [ ] References **exemptions** under CPLR 5205 / 5206 +
      Personal Property Law / Real Property Law overlays
- [ ] Notes the **§ 5222-a/b/c** consumer-protection
      notification requirements before bank-account
      restraint (2008 EIPA reforms)

## Common failure modes

- Treats federal money-judgment SOL (often 20 years federal
  too, but cites the wrong source)
- Cites the 6-year contract SOL (CPLR 213(2)) instead of
  the 20-year judgment-enforcement SOL
- Misses the 2008 Exempt Income Protection Act
  notifications before income/bank restraint
