# Bronx County Supreme Court

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Verify the assigned Justice's Part
> Rules before every filing.

## At a glance

- **Court**: Supreme Court of the State of New York, Bronx
  County (12th Judicial District)
- **Courthouse**: **851 Grand Concourse, Bronx NY 10451**
  (Mario Merola Building)
- **Commercial Division threshold**: **$150,000**
- **E-filing**: NYSCEF mandatory for most civil case types
- **Hearings**: Microsoft Teams for routine; in-person for
  trials; remote Mandatory Settlement Conferences

## Distinctives

### Personal-injury volume

Bronx County has historically been New York's highest-volume
personal-injury trial venue. The county has a dedicated
**Personal Injury Part** (IAS) calendar with its own
calendaring practices. Pro se litigants in non-PI matters
should expect substantial scheduling pressure as a
consequence.

### CPLR 3408 foreclosure conferences

Bronx County, like Kings and Queens, runs a Foreclosure
Settlement Conference Part for home-loan foreclosures under
CPLR 3408.

### Trial-readiness practice

Bronx Justices typically enforce strict Note-of-Issue
deadlines and aggressive trial calendars. The county has
been a Civil Differentiated Case Management (DCM) pilot
jurisdiction.

## Filing checklist

1. **NYSCEF**: Summons + Complaint via NYSCEF; $210 fee
2. **RJI**: UCS-840 filed before motion practice
3. **Service**: CPLR 306-b — within 120 days
4. **Preliminary Conference**: scheduled by IAS Part under
   22 NYCRR § 202.12
