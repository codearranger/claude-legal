# in-draft-note — Indiana Notice of Hearing

## Prompt

I filed a motion to compel discovery in Lake Superior Court
and the court issued a setting. I need to give the other side
formal notice of the hearing date. What document do I file
and serve, and what does it need to say?

## Expected triggers

- `in-draft-note`
- `in-lake`
- `in-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### Correct document name — Notice of Hearing

- [ ] Identifies the correct Indiana document as a
      **Notice of Hearing** (or Notice of Setting) —
      **not** a "Note for Motion Calendar" (that is
      Washington State practice) and not a "Notice of
      Motion" (that is federal / New York practice)
- [ ] Notes that in Indiana the court issues the setting
      and parties then notify each other — Indiana courts
      generally do not self-schedule motions into an
      open motion calendar the way some states do

### Caption + document title

- [ ] Caption follows T.R. 10(A): Lake Superior Court
      heading, parties, cause number — reads the Lake
      cause-number format (`45Dxx-YYMM-CT-NNNNNN`) from
      `in-lake`
- [ ] Document title clearly states the nature of the
      hearing: "NOTICE OF HEARING ON DEFENDANT'S MOTION
      TO COMPEL DISCOVERY"

### Body content

- [ ] States the date, time, and courtroom / division of
      the scheduled hearing (populated from the court's
      setting notice)
- [ ] Identifies the motion being heard by its full title
      and the date it was filed
- [ ] Includes a **Certificate of Service** at the end
      certifying service on all parties under T.R. 5(B);
      for Odyssey-registered opposing counsel, notes that
      Odyssey auto-serves on e-filing

### Format

- [ ] T.R. 5(E) page format; numbered paragraphs per
      T.R. 10(B) for the body; pro se signature block
      under T.R. 11(A) if self-represented

## Common failure modes

- Calls the document a "Note for Motion Calendar" (WA
  term) or "Notice of Motion" (NY/federal term)
- Invents a self-scheduling mechanism; Indiana parties
  do not reserve calendar slots unilaterally — the
  court issues the setting
- Omits the Certificate of Service
- Omits the case number or division designation
