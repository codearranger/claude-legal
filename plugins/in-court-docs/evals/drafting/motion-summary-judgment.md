# in-draft-motion — T.R. 56 motion for summary judgment with the *Jarboe* burden

## Prompt

I'm representing myself in Marion Superior Court. A debt buyer
sued me on an old credit-card account for about $9,000. Through
discovery they couldn't produce any assignment paperwork or
account statements — just a one-page "affidavit of debt." I want
to file a motion to get the case thrown out because there's no
admissible evidence they actually own this debt. How do I draft
it?

## Expected triggers

- `in-draft-motion`
- `in-statewide-format`
- `in-consumer-debt`
- `in-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria

### Caption and title

- [ ] Caption follows the Indiana format per **T.R. 10(A)**
      (STATE OF INDIANA / court / county heading; parties stacked
      with the `)` chain; "v." not "vs."; cause number) — read the
      current layout from `in-statewide-format`
- [ ] Title identifies the filing as a **Motion for Summary
      Judgment under Trial Rule 56**
- [ ] Distinguishes T.R. 56 (no genuine issue of material fact on
      the designated evidentiary record) from a T.R. 12(B)(6)
      motion (failure to state a claim on the pleadings alone) and
      explains why T.R. 56 is the right vehicle when the litigant
      relies on the discovery record

### Standard — the Indiana-distinctive *Jarboe* burden

- [ ] States Indiana's summary-judgment burden: the **movant must
      affirmatively negate an element of the opponent's claim with
      admissible evidence** — merely pointing to an absence of
      evidence in the record is NOT enough (*Jarboe v. Landmark
      Cmty. Newspapers*, 644 N.E.2d 118 (Ind. 1994); reaffirmed in
      *Hughley v. State*, 15 N.E.3d 1000 (Ind. 2014))
- [ ] Explicitly flags that Indiana **rejected** the federal
      *Celotex* "show the absence of evidence" approach — a brief
      written on the FRCP 56 model understates the movant's burden
- [ ] Includes a **statement of material facts not in genuine
      dispute** with paragraph-numbered facts, each citing a sworn
      declaration or designated exhibit, and notes the response
      timing under **T.R. 56(C)** (cite the rule; read the current
      day counts from the references corpus rather than asserting
      them as fixed facts)

### Substantive theory (in-consumer-debt)

- [ ] Frames the standing / chain-of-title defect — the debt buyer
      must prove ownership through an admissible assignment chain
      under **T.R. 17(A)** (real party in interest)
- [ ] Notes that a bare "affidavit of debt" may fail the
      business-records foundation under **Ind. Evid. R. 803(6)**
      and does not by itself establish ownership of the specific
      account; points to `in-consumer-debt` for the *Klotz* line
      rather than asserting holdings from memory

### Composition

- [ ] Packet includes motion + memorandum in support + supporting
      declaration + **proposed order** (Marion practice requires a
      proposed order with every motion — see `in-marion`)
- [ ] Pro se signature block per T.R. 11(A) — no Attorney Number
      (see `in-pro-se`)

## Common failure modes

- Drafts to the federal *Celotex* standard instead of the
  Indiana *Jarboe* affirmative-negation burden
- Confuses T.R. 56 with a T.R. 12(B)(6) motion
- Omits the proposed order from the packet
- Treats the affidavit of debt as conclusive proof of ownership
- Invents an Attorney Number for a pro-se filer
