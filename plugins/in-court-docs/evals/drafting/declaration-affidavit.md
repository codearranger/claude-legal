# in-draft-declaration — T.R. 11(B) verified declaration / affidavit

## Prompt

I'm self-represented and need to attach a sworn statement to
my motion in Marion Superior Court describing the debt
collector's conduct — the calls, the threats, the amount they
claim I owe. What's the proper form for a sworn document in
Indiana, and what language do I put at the end to make it
legally effective?

## Expected triggers

- `in-draft-declaration`
- `in-statewide-format`
- `in-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria

### Document form — declaration vs. affidavit

- [ ] Distinguishes the two sworn-document forms in Indiana:
      (1) a **notarized affidavit** (signed before a notary
      public under Indiana notary statutes) and (2) a
      **verified declaration under T.R. 11(B)** — signed
      under the penalties of perjury per IC 35-44.1-2-1
      without a notary
- [ ] States that a T.R. 11(B) declaration is valid for most
      motion-support purposes; notes when a notarized
      affidavit is specifically required (e.g., some IC
      34-26-5 protective-order petitions)

### Verification language — T.R. 11(B) / IC 35-44.1-2-1

- [ ] Produces the correct Indiana verification phrase:

      > "I affirm, under the penalties for perjury, that the
      > foregoing representations are true."

- [ ] Warns **not** to import California, Oregon, or federal
      verification language ("I declare under penalty of
      perjury under the laws of the State of California …")
      — Indiana courts have declined non-conforming
      verifications
- [ ] Notes the signature line format: declarant's name and
      date below the affirmation

### Body — numbered paragraphs

- [ ] Body is organized in **numbered paragraphs** under
      T.R. 10(B), each paragraph limited to a single set
      of circumstances
- [ ] First-person statements of personal knowledge frame
      each factual assertion: "I personally received …",
      "On [DATE], the caller stated …"

### Caption + format

- [ ] Caption follows T.R. 10(A): court identification,
      parties with the `)` chain, cause number — read the
      caption layout from `in-statewide-format` rather than
      asserting details from memory
- [ ] T.R. 5(E) format: 12-pt serif font, double spacing,
      2-inch top margin page 1, 1-inch all-other margins —
      read current requirements from the corpus

### Pro se signature block

- [ ] Uses the pro se signature block (no Attorney Number)
      per T.R. 11(A) + `in-pro-se`

## Common failure modes

- Imports California or federal perjury-penalty language
- Omits the verification phrase or places it in the wrong
  location (must be at the end, before the signature)
- Writes unnumbered narrative paragraphs instead of T.R.
  10(B)-compliant numbered paragraphs
- Adds a bar number to a pro se signature block
- States that a notary is always required for Indiana
  declarations (T.R. 11(B) declarations do not require one)
