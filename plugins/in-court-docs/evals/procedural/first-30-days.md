# in-first-30-days — 20-day answer + T.R. 12(B)(6) + affirmative defenses

## Prompt

I just got served in Indiana. A debt buyer is suing me for
$12,000 in Allen Superior Court. I think the complaint
doesn't state a valid claim because they haven't shown they
actually own the debt. What are my options in the first
few weeks — can I move to dismiss, how do I answer, and
what defenses do I raise?

## Expected triggers

- `in-first-30-days`
- `in-deadlines`
- `in-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### 20-day answer clock — T.R. 6(C)

- [ ] States the **20-day answer deadline** under
      T.R. 6(C), counting from the date of service
      (not the date of mailing)
- [ ] Notes that T.R. 6(E) was abolished in 2009 — **no
      3-day mail extension** exists under Indiana practice
      (a distinctive Indiana trap that differs from FRCP
      6(d) and many state rules)
- [ ] Applies the T.R. 6(A) weekend/holiday roll-forward:
      if Day 20 falls on a weekend or IC 1-1-9-1 legal
      holiday, extend to the next court day
- [ ] Warns that default judgment under T.R. 55 follows
      a missed answer — and that T.R. 60(B) relief is
      harder to obtain than filing on time

### Pre-answer motion — T.R. 12(B)(6)

- [ ] Identifies **T.R. 12(B)(6)** (failure to state a
      claim) as a permissible pre-answer motion; notes
      it tolls the answer deadline
- [ ] Notes the Indiana pleading standard (T.R. 8(A))
      and the requirement to state a claim entitling the
      pleader to relief — reads/cross-references the
      applicable Indiana standard from the references
      corpus rather than asserting a specific case-law
      formulation from memory
- [ ] Distinguishes T.R. 12(B)(6) (legal sufficiency of
      the pleading) from T.R. 56 summary judgment
      (designated evidentiary record); notes that
      ownership / chain-of-title arguments typically
      require discovery and a T.R. 56 motion, not a
      12(B)(6) motion

### Affirmative defenses — T.R. 8(C)

- [ ] States that affirmative defenses must be **pleaded
      in the Answer under T.R. 8(C)** (warning of waiver
      if omitted)
- [ ] Identifies the key defenses for a debt-buyer case:
      statute of limitations (IC 34-11-2-7, 6-year open
      account), lack of standing / chain of title
      (T.R. 17(A) real party in interest), and FDCPA
      defenses as counterclaims

### T.R. 3.1 Appearance

- [ ] Notes the Appearance form that must be filed under
      T.R. 3.1 to enter the case

## Common failure modes

- States a 30-day or 21-day answer period (Indiana's
  is 20 days — different from many other states)
- Claims a 3-day mail extension exists (it was abolished
  in Indiana in 2009)
- Advises filing a 12(B)(6) motion based on missing
  chain-of-title evidence (that is a T.R. 56 argument,
  not a pleading-sufficiency argument)
- Omits affirmative defenses from the answer (waiver
  risk under T.R. 8(C))
