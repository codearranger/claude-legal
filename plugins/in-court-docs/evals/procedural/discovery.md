# in-discovery — T.R. 26-37 discovery + 25-interrogatory cap + motion to compel

## Prompt

I'm defending a debt-collection case in Lake Superior Court.
The debt buyer hasn't produced the Bills of Sale showing
they own my account. I want to send them interrogatories,
a request for production, and maybe force them to answer
through the court if they don't respond. Walk me through
Indiana discovery rules.

## Expected triggers

- `in-discovery`
- `in-draft-motion`
- `in-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### Discovery scope — T.R. 26(B)

- [ ] States Indiana's T.R. 26(B)(1) discovery scope:
      "reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of
      admissible evidence" — the **pre-2015 federal
      standard** (Indiana did NOT adopt FRCP 26(b)(1)'s
      2015 proportionality amendment)
- [ ] Notes this makes Indiana discovery **broader** than
      current federal discovery on the proportionality
      dimension — a distinctive Indiana feature

### Interrogatories — T.R. 33 + 25-cap

- [ ] States the **25-interrogatory limit** under
      T.R. 33(A) — including subparts that are not
      logically subsumed within the parent question
- [ ] States the **30-day response period** (reads the
      current day count from the corpus)
- [ ] Identifies the chain-of-title interrogatory targets:
      identify every Bill of Sale with dates and parties,
      identify the business-records witness, identify
      the verification process for purchased accounts

### Requests for Production — T.R. 34

- [ ] States the **30-day response period** for T.R.
      34(B) RFPs (reads from corpus)
- [ ] Identifies the chain-of-title RFP targets: all
      Bills of Sale, Loan Schedules, original-creditor
      account records, validation notice, call logs,
      policies

### Motion to compel — T.R. 37(A) + meet-and-confer

- [ ] States the **meet-and-confer requirement** under
      T.R. 37(E) before filing a motion to compel — the
      movant must certify the good-faith attempt
- [ ] States the T.R. 37(A) motion-to-compel framework
      and the sanction options for continued non-
      compliance (T.R. 37(B)) — reads current detail
      from the corpus rather than asserting sanctions
      from memory

### Indiana-specific distinctions

- [ ] Notes that Indiana does NOT require initial
      disclosures (unlike FRCP 26(a)) — discovery begins
      with served requests, not automatic disclosure
- [ ] Identifies the 25-interrogatory cap as a key
      Indiana distinction (federal practice allows more
      under FRCP 33(a)(1))

## Common failure modes

- Asserts FRCP proportionality limits on Indiana
  discovery (T.R. 26(B) does not incorporate them)
- States Indiana requires initial disclosures (it does
  not — that is FRCP 26(a))
- Omits the 25-interrogatory cap
- Omits the T.R. 37(E) meet-and-confer prerequisite
- Asserts the 30-day response period as a number from
  memory instead of reading from the corpus
