# in-post-judgment — T.R. 59 / T.R. 60(B) / T.R. 69 supplemental + garnishment exemptions

## Prompt

The debt buyer got a default judgment against me in Marion
Superior Court because I didn't know I had to respond so
fast. The judgment was entered three weeks ago. Can I still
get it thrown out? And separately, they've started garnishing
my wages — can I protect any of my income?

## Expected triggers

- `in-post-judgment`
- `in-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### T.R. 59 — Motion to Correct Error (within 30 days)

- [ ] States the **30-day filing deadline** from entry of
      the default judgment under T.R. 59(C) and correctly
      characterizes it as **jurisdictional** — missing
      the deadline strips the trial court of authority
      to consider the motion
- [ ] Notes the motion is still timely on the facts (3
      weeks = 21 days) and explains the relief available
      for a default judgment
- [ ] Notes T.R. 59(B): if the court does not rule within
      45 days, the motion is **deemed denied**

### T.R. 60(B) — Relief from Judgment

- [ ] Identifies T.R. 60(B)(1) (mistake, surprise,
      excusable neglect) as the most likely ground for
      a default-judgment defendant who did not receive
      adequate notice
- [ ] States the **1-year filing deadline** for T.R.
      60(B)(1)-(3) grounds (reads the current period
      from the corpus)
- [ ] Notes Indiana's relaxed "any reasonable basis"
      standard for pro se defendants on default judgments
      — reads/verifies from the corpus rather than
      asserting a case citation from memory

### T.R. 69 — Proceedings Supplemental + debtor exam

- [ ] States that after judgment, a creditor may initiate
      **proceedings supplemental under T.R. 69** to
      discover assets and pursue garnishment
- [ ] Notes the creditor may compel the judgment debtor
      to appear for examination

### Wage garnishment — exemptions

- [ ] States the **federal CCPA / IC 24-4.5-5-105
      wage-garnishment cap**: the lesser of 25% of
      disposable earnings or the amount by which
      disposable earnings exceed 30 times the federal
      minimum wage per week — reads the current threshold
      from the corpus
- [ ] Notes the **IC 34-55-10 exemptions** (personal
      property exemption amounts, deposit-account
      protections) — reads current dollar amounts from
      the corpus rather than asserting them from memory

## Common failure modes

- States the T.R. 59 deadline is extendable (it is
  jurisdictional and cannot be extended)
- Asserts specific garnishment exemption dollar amounts
  from memory instead of the corpus
- Conflates T.R. 59 (motion to correct error) with T.R.
  60(B) (relief from judgment) — they serve different
  functions and have different deadlines
- Omits T.R. 69 supplemental proceedings as the
  enforcement vehicle
