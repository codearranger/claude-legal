# Michigan Court Rules (MCR) — Civil-Practice Map

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This is a navigational map of the Michigan Court
> Rules, not the rule text. Read the cited rule in full and confirm it
> is current against the verbatim corpus in `court-rules/` and the
> official text at courts.michigan.gov before relying on it. Subrule
> lettering and day counts change.

The Michigan Court Rules govern practice and procedure in all Michigan
courts. The chapters relevant to civil practice are 1 through 4. The
MCR are adopted and amended by the Michigan Supreme Court.

### Summary disposition — MCR 2.116 (the dispositive motion)

Michigan's pretrial dispositive motion is the **motion for summary
disposition under MCR 2.116**. The grounds are lettered subrules in
**MCR 2.116(C)**; cite the precise subrule. The most-used grounds:

| Ground | What it tests | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| **MCR 2.116(C)(8)** | **Failure to state a claim** on which relief can be granted | Tested on the **pleadings alone**; all well-pleaded factual allegations accepted as true; granted only if the claim is so clearly unenforceable that no factual development could justify recovery |
| **MCR 2.116(C)(10)** | **No genuine issue of material fact** | Tested on the **documentary evidence** (affidavits, depositions, admissions, documents) viewed in the light most favorable to the non-moving party; the non-movant may not rest on the pleadings but must set forth specific facts showing a genuine issue for trial |
| **MCR 2.116(C)(7)** | **Claim barred** — release, payment, prior judgment, statute of limitations, immunity, agreement to arbitrate, etc. | The court considers pleadings plus any affidavits/documents; well-pleaded allegations accepted unless contradicted by the documentary evidence |

Other **MCR 2.116(C)** grounds include lack of subject-matter or
personal jurisdiction (C)(1)-(4), insufficiency of process/service
(C)(3), and incapacity. **MCR 2.116(G)** governs the supporting and
opposing papers and the form requirements; **MCR 2.116(I)** authorizes
the court to grant judgment to the opposing party if appropriate.

> Use **(C)(8)** when attacking the legal sufficiency of the complaint
> on its face (no evidence considered) and **(C)(10)** when relying on
> evidence outside the pleadings. Mislabeling the subrule changes the
> standard the court applies. See `key-cases.md` for *Maiden v Rozwood*
> and *Quinto v Cross & Peters Co* on the (C)(10) standard.

### Default and judgments

| Rule | Subject |

## Chapter 3 — Special Civil Proceedings

Chapter 3 carries specialized civil tracks. For civil practitioners the
key cross-reference is to **eviction / summary proceedings**, which are
heard in district court under Chapter 4 and the Summary Proceedings
Act. Chapter 3 also houses domestic-relations procedure (the Family
Division of Circuit Court handles divorce, custody, and support),
declaratory judgments, and other special actions. Confirm the precise
subchapter for a given proceeding in `court-rules/`.

---

## Chapter 4 — District Court / Limited Jurisdiction

| Rule | Subject |
|---|---|
| **MCR 4.001-4.003** | Applicability in district court |
| **MCR 4.201** | **Summary proceedings to recover possession of premises (landlord-tenant / eviction)** — the complaint, the summons and its short return period, the demand-for-possession / notice-to-quit predicate, the answer, and judgment for possession. The Summary Proceedings Act (MCL Chapter 600, the 5700-series) supplies the substantive framework. |
| **MCR 4.202** | Summary proceedings to recover possession of land (land contract forfeiture) |
| **MCR 4.301-4.306** | **Small claims** — the Small Claims Division of district court; informal, lawyers and formal rules of evidence excluded, no jury, no appeal of right (a party may instead remove to the general civil docket); confirm the current dollar ceiling in `mi-statutes-debt/` (the small-claims jurisdictional cap is set by statute and adjusted over time) |

> The **district-court civil money-claim ceiling** and the
> **small-claims ceiling** are set by statute (Revised Judicature
> Act). Do not embed the current figures here — confirm them in
> `mi-statutes-debt/` before pleading the jurisdictional amount.
