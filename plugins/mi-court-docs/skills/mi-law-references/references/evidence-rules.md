# Michigan Rules of Evidence (MRE) — Map

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Navigational map only. Read the cited rule in
> full and confirm it is current against `court-rules/` and the
> official text at courts.michigan.gov before relying on it.

The Michigan Rules of Evidence govern the admission of evidence in
Michigan courts. They track the Federal Rules of Evidence closely but
are independently adopted by the Michigan Supreme Court and diverge in
places.

> **2024 restyling.** The MRE were **comprehensively restyled effective
> January 1, 2024**, modernizing the wording to match the federal
> restyling project. Rule numbers were held stable; the substance was
> intended to remain largely the same, but the phrasing changed
> throughout. **Always confirm current rule text** — older quotations
> may no longer match the rule verbatim.

## Authentication — MRE 901-902

| Rule | Subject |
|---|---|
| **MRE 901** | **Authentication / identification** — the proponent must produce evidence sufficient to support a finding that the item is what it is claimed to be |
| **MRE 902** | **Self-authenticating** evidence — items that require no extrinsic authenticating evidence |
| **MRE 902(11)** | **Certified domestic records of a regularly conducted activity** — a business record accompanied by a qualifying written certification of the custodian (with advance notice to the adverse party) is self-authenticating, dispensing with a live foundation witness. The companion to the MRE 803(6) hearsay exception in documentary civil practice. |

### MRE 803(6) — business records (load-bearing)

The single most important evidentiary rule in documentary civil
practice (especially debt-buyer / assigned-account matters). A record
of an act, event, condition, opinion, or diagnosis is admissible as a
business record when:

1. it was **made at or near the time** by — or from information
   transmitted by — someone **with knowledge**;
2. it was **kept in the course of a regularly conducted business
   activity**;
3. **making the record was a regular practice** of that activity; and
4. these conditions are shown by the testimony of the **custodian or
   another qualified witness** — **or by a self-authenticating
   certification under MRE 902(11)**;

unless the **source of information or the method or circumstances of
preparation indicate a lack of trustworthiness**. The
trustworthiness/foundation attack — particularly where a debt buyer
seeks to introduce records of a prior creditor it acquired — is the
recurring battleground; pair with the chain-of-title analysis in
`mi-consumer-debt`.

## Opinion and expert testimony — MRE 701-706

|---|---|

## Privileges

The MRE recognize evidentiary privileges, but many Michigan privileges
are **statutory** (located in the Michigan Compiled Laws rather than
the MRE themselves). Commonly invoked: attorney-client,
physician-patient / psychologist-patient, spousal, and clergy
privileges. The privilege analysis usually starts with the relevant
MCL section rather than an MRE number — confirm the controlling statute
in `mi-statutes-debt/` and the privilege provisions referenced in
`court-rules/`.
