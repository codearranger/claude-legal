# Michigan Citation Format — Michigan Appellate Opinion Manual

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Style guidance only. Confirm conventions against
> the current **Michigan Appellate Opinion Manual** (published by the
> State Court Administrative Office / the Reporter of Decisions) before
> relying on a form.

Michigan's appellate courts cite authority using the **Michigan
Appellate Opinion Manual (MAOM)** — the in-house style of the Court of
Appeals and Supreme Court. It departs from the Bluebook in several
visible ways, most notably **no italics** and **lowercased party
names** in case citations. Briefs filed in Michigan courts conventionally
follow the MAOM.

---

## Cases

**Form**: `party v party, volume reporter page; parallel-volume parallel-reporter parallel-page (court year)`

- **Party names are lowercased and not italicized.** The connector is a
  lowercase `v` with **no period**.
- A **semicolon** separates parallel citations (official Michigan
  reporter; regional reporter).

Examples (verified):

- `Maiden v Rozwood, 461 Mich 109; 597 NW2d 817 (1999)`
- `Quinto v Cross & Peters Co, 451 Mich 358; 547 NW2d 314 (1996)`
- `Sparks v Sparks, 440 Mich 141; 485 NW2d 893 (1992)`
- `Berger v Berger, 277 Mich App 700; 747 NW2d 336 (2008)`

**Reporters**:

| Reporter | Court | Abbreviation |
|---|---|---|
| Michigan Reports | Michigan Supreme Court (official) | `Mich` |
| Michigan Appeals Reports | Michigan Court of Appeals (official) | `Mich App` |
| North Western Reporter (2d/3d) | regional, both courts | `NW2d`, `NW3d` |

- The court parenthetical for the Supreme Court is the **year alone**
  (the `Mich` reporter already identifies the court): `(1999)`. For the
  Court of Appeals, the `Mich App` reporter identifies the court, so
  the parenthetical is again the year: `(2008)`.
- **Pinpoint pages** follow the first page after a comma:
  `Maiden, 461 Mich at 119`.
- **Unpublished** Court of Appeals opinions are cited with the
  unpublished designation, the docket number, and the decision date,
  and are **not binding precedent** (persuasive only) — flag an
  opinion's published/unpublished status when citing it.

## Statutes — Michigan Compiled Laws

- **Form**: `MCL 600.5807` — the letters **MCL** (no periods), a space,
  then the section number. **No section symbol (§)** in the standard
  Michigan form, and no comma in the number.
- Subsections: `MCL 600.2959(1)`; `MCL 600.5807(8)`.
- The chapter/section numbering encodes the act: `600.xxxx` sections
  are in the **Revised Judicature Act of 1961** (Chapter 600).

---

## Court rules and evidence rules

- **Court rules**: `MCR 2.116(C)(10)` — letters **MCR**, the rule
  number, then lettered/numbered subrules in parentheses with no
  spaces.
- **Evidence rules**: `MRE 803(6)`; `MRE 902(11)`.
- **Administrative orders**: `Administrative Order No. YYYY-N` (or the
  `AO` short form per the MAOM).

---

## Federal authority

Federal citations in Michigan briefs generally follow standard
practice: `15 USC 1692g(b)` (Michigan style omits the section symbol
and periods, mirroring the MCL convention) or the Bluebook
`15 U.S.C. § 1692g(b)` — confirm the venue's preference. Federal cases:
`Twombly v Bell Atlantic Corp` style with `US`, `S Ct`, `F3d`, `F Supp
3d` reporters per the MAOM's federal-citation guidance.

---

## Quick checklist

- [ ] Party names lowercased, not italicized; connector `v` (no period)
- [ ] Official Michigan reporter first, then regional, separated by `;`
- [ ] Year-only court parenthetical for `Mich` / `Mich App` cites
- [ ] `MCL 600.xxxx` — no §, no periods in "MCL"
- [ ] `MCR` / `MRE` with parenthesized subrules
- [ ] Published vs. unpublished status flagged for COA opinions
