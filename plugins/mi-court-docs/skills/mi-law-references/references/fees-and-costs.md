# Michigan Fees and Costs — Framework

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Navigational map only. Filing-fee **amounts**
> drift and are set by statute and by the clerk of the filing court —
> confirm the current figure with the clerk and against the verbatim
> statutory text in `mi-statutes-debt/` before relying on any number.

Michigan follows the **American rule**: each party bears its own
attorney's fees unless a **statute, court rule, or contract** shifts
them. "Costs" (a narrower category — filing fees, statutory witness
fees, certain enumerated charges) are separately taxable to the
prevailing party.

---

## Filing fees

Civil filing fees are fixed by the **Revised Judicature Act** (Chapter
600 of the Michigan Compiled Laws — the Chapter 24 "Costs" and Chapter
25 "Fees" provisions) and collected by the clerk of each court. The
fee schedule varies by court level (circuit vs. district) and by the
amount in controversy.

- **MCL 600.2401** — directs that the **taxation of costs** be regulated
  by Supreme Court rule, and that the items and amounts of allowed costs
  in the Supreme Court, Circuit Court, and District Court be governed by
  the Chapter 24 cost provisions except as otherwise provided.
- The specific dollar amounts (civil action filing fee, motion fee,
  jury demand fee, garnishment fee, small-claims fee, etc.) are in the
  Chapter 25 fee sections and are periodically amended. **Do not embed
  current amounts here** — confirm them in `mi-statutes-debt/` or with
  the clerk.
- **Fee waiver** — an indigent party may move to **waive or suspend
  fees and costs** under **MCR 2.002**; confirm the current eligibility
  standard and the form/affidavit required.

## Taxation of costs to the prevailing party — MCR 2.625

**MCR 2.625** governs the **taxation of costs**. Costs are generally
allowed to the **prevailing party** as of course, on a timely **bill of
costs** filed and verified after judgment. Taxable costs are limited to
the categories the statutes and rules enumerate (they do **not**
ordinarily include attorney's fees absent a separate fee-shifting
authority). MCR 2.625 also addresses costs where there are multiple
parties or claims, and where a judgment is less favorable than an
offer.

---

## Offer of judgment — MCR 2.405 (the principal cost-shifting device)

**MCR 2.405** lets a party serve a written **offer of judgment** /
counteroffer. If the offer is rejected and the **adjusted verdict** is
not more favorable to the rejecting party than the average offer, the
rejecting party must pay the offeror's **actual costs** (including
reasonable attorney fees incurred after rejection). The court may
**decline** to award actual costs **in the interest of justice** —
MCR 2.405 enumerates circumstances bearing on that exception (for
example, where the offer was de minimis/trivial in context, or the case
presents an issue of first impression or public interest). With the end
of case-evaluation sanctions (below), MCR 2.405 is now the dominant
mechanism for shifting post-offer costs in Michigan civil litigation.

---

## Case evaluation — MCR 2.403 (sanctions ended 2022)

**Historically**, MCR **2.403(O)** imposed **case-evaluation sanctions**:
a party that rejected a unanimous case-evaluation award and then failed
to improve its position at trial by more than 10% had to pay the
opposing party's **actual costs**, including attorney fees. This was a
defining feature of Michigan civil practice for decades.

> **Change to note.** Effective **January 1, 2022**, the Michigan
> Supreme Court **amended MCR 2.403 to eliminate MCR 2.403(O)
> case-evaluation sanctions** and to make case evaluation **no longer
> mandatory**. Pre-amendment cases may still carry sanction exposure
> under the prior rule depending on timing — confirm which version
> controls for any matter that straddles the amendment, and confirm the
> current text of MCR 2.403.

## Frivolous filings — MCL 600.2591 / MCR 1.109(E)

Two overlapping authorities sanction frivolous litigation:

- **MCL 600.2591** — on a party's motion, if the court finds a **civil
  action or defense was frivolous**, it **shall** award the prevailing
  party the **costs and reasonable attorney fees** incurred because of
  the frivolous claim/defense, against the non-prevailing party and/or
  its attorney. The statute defines "frivolous" (primary purpose to
  harass; no reasonable basis to believe the facts true; legal position
  devoid of arguable merit).
- **MCR 1.109(E)** — the **signature certification** rule: by signing a
  document, the signer certifies it is well-grounded in fact, warranted
  by existing law or a good-faith argument for change, and not for an
  improper purpose. A violation **requires** the court to impose an
  appropriate sanction (the Michigan analog to a Rule 11 sanction).

## Fee-shifting by statute or contract

Specific statutes create their own fee-shifting hooks — for example,
consumer-protection and debt-collection statutes and the FDCPA (**15
U.S.C. § 1692k(a)(3)**, prevailing-consumer fees). Contractual
attorney-fee provisions are enforceable per their terms subject to a
reasonableness review. Identify the controlling statute (in
`mi-statutes-debt/` or `federal-debt-laws/`) before asserting a
fee-shifting right; the subject-matter bundles (`mi-consumer-debt`)
carry the matter-specific hooks.
