# mi-pro-se — Self-represented signature block and SCAO form usage

## Prompt

I can't afford a lawyer and I'm handling my Michigan case
myself. How do I sign my court papers — do I need some kind of
bar number? And someone mentioned I should be using "SCAO
forms." What are those and where do they fit in?

## Expected triggers

- `mi-pro-se`
- `mi-statewide-format`

## Acceptance criteria

### Self-represented signature block (MCR 1.109(E))

- [ ] States that a self-represented party signs under
      **MCR 1.109(E)** with name, address, and telephone number
      but **no P-number** (the P-number is the State Bar of
      Michigan attorney number, which a non-lawyer does not have)
- [ ] Notes the signer may designate themselves "In Pro Per" /
      "Self-Represented" and that the signature still carries the
      MCR 1.109(E) certification obligations
- [ ] Does **not** fabricate a P-number or hold the litigant to
      attorney-only formatting

### SCAO forms

- [ ] Explains that the **State Court Administrative Office
      (SCAO)** publishes standardized statewide court forms and
      that many filings (especially in family, landlord-tenant,
      and small-claims matters) have an approved SCAO form that
      Michigan courts expect or require
- [ ] Notes that SCAO-approved forms are identified by a form
      number and that the litigant should check whether an SCAO
      form exists for the document before drafting from scratch —
      read the current catalog/pointers from the references
      corpus rather than asserting specific form numbers from
      memory
- [ ] Distinguishes SCAO mandatory forms from documents that may
      be drafted free-form under MCR 1.109 / 2.113

### Composition

- [ ] Formatting otherwise tracks `mi-statewide-format`

## Common failure modes

- Invents or requires a P-number for a non-lawyer
- Treats SCAO forms as optional everywhere when some are
  mandatory in their context
- Asserts specific SCAO form numbers from memory rather than
  pointing to the corpus/SCAO catalog
- Holds the pro-se litigant to attorney-only signature rules
