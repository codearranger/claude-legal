# mi-draft-note — Michigan Notice of Hearing under MCR 2.119(C)

## Prompt

I filed a motion in my Michigan circuit court case and the
clerk told me I have to "notice it for hearing." I have no idea
what that means or what document to file. How do I draft the
notice of hearing and how much advance notice does the other
side get?

## Expected triggers

- `mi-draft-note`
- `mi-statewide-format`
- `mi-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria

### Document identification

- [ ] Identifies the document as a **Notice of Hearing** that
      accompanies the motion and tells the parties when and
      where the motion will be heard
- [ ] Explains that under **MCR 2.119(C)** written motions and
      notices of hearing must be served on the other parties a
      minimum number of days before the hearing — cite
      MCR 2.119(C) and **read the current minimum notice period
      (and the mail-service add-on) from the references corpus**
      rather than asserting a day count from memory
- [ ] Notes the interaction with **MCR 1.108** time computation
      when counting backward from the hearing date

### Contents of the notice

- [ ] Caption per **MCR 1.109 / MCR 2.113**
- [ ] States the title of the motion being noticed, the date,
      time, and location/judge of the hearing, and that it will
      be heard pursuant to MCR 2.119
- [ ] Includes a proof/certificate of service consistent with
      service rules (cite the controlling rule; read current
      service mechanics from the corpus)

### Composition

- [ ] Self-represented signature block (no P-number)
- [ ] Reminds the litigant to confirm the motion date with the
      court's practice (some judges set motion days; defer to
      `mi-schedule-hearing` for date-getting mechanics)

## Common failure modes

- Confuses the Notice of Hearing with a praecipe or with the
  motion itself
- Asserts a fixed minimum-notice day count rather than reading
  it from the corpus
- Omits the certificate of service
- Computes the notice period without applying MCR 1.108
