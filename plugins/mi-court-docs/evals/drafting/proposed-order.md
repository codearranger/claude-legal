# mi-draft-order — Proposed order and the MCR 2.602(B)(3) 7-day rule

## Prompt

The judge granted my motion in Michigan and told me to "submit
an order." The other side's lawyer says I have to send them my
proposed order first before the judge will sign it. Is that
right? How do I draft the proposed order and what's this
"7-day" thing they keep mentioning?

## Expected triggers

- `mi-draft-order`
- `mi-statewide-format`
- `mi-submit-order`
- `mi-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria

### Form of the proposed order

- [ ] Caption per **MCR 1.109 / MCR 2.113**; titled as a
      proposed order matching the relief the court granted
- [ ] Decretal language tracks the court's ruling (orders only
      what was granted; no argument or recitals beyond what is
      needed)
- [ ] Leaves a signature line for the judge and a date; notes
      that the order is effective when signed/entered (cite the
      controlling rule; read current entry mechanics from the
      corpus)

### The MCR 2.602(B)(3) "7-day rule"

- [ ] Explains that under **MCR 2.602(B)(3)** the party
      submitting the order may serve it on the other parties
      with a notice that it will be submitted to the court for
      entry **unless written objections are filed within the
      rule's window** — cite MCR 2.602(B)(3) and **read the
      current objection window (the "7 days") from the references
      corpus** rather than asserting the number from memory
- [ ] Distinguishes the 7-day-rule path from the other
      MCR 2.602(B) methods (e.g., order approved as to form by
      all parties, or settled at a hearing) and explains when
      each applies
- [ ] Notes that if timely objections are filed, the court
      resolves the form of the order

### Composition

- [ ] Self-represented signature block (no P-number)
- [ ] Hands off transmittal mechanics to `mi-submit-order`

## Common failure modes

- Drafts an order that grants more than the court actually ruled
- Misses the MCR 2.602(B)(3) 7-day-rule procedure entirely
- Asserts the objection window as a hard-coded number instead of
  reading it from the corpus
- Treats the proposed order as self-executing without judicial
  signature/entry
