# Citation Format — Washington State Courts

Washington courts follow the Washington Reporter of Decisions
conventions (with Bluebook for federal citations). Proper citation
form is expected from pro se litigants; clerks and judges apply the
rules strictly.

## Washington state cases

### Basic form

> *Party v. Party*, Volume Reporter Page, Parallel-Volume Reporter
> Page (Court Year).

Examples:

- *Hangman Ridge Training Stables, Inc. v. Safeco Title Ins. Co.*,
  105 Wn.2d 778, 719 P.2d 531 (1986).
- *Discover Bank v. Bridges*, 154 Wn. App. 722, 226 P.3d 191 (2010).
- *State v. Ziegler*, 114 Wn.2d 533, 789 P.2d 79 (1990).

### Reporters

- **Wn.2d** — Washington Supreme Court, 2d series (1939–)
- **Wn. App.** — Washington Court of Appeals (1969–)
- **Wash.** — Washington Supreme Court, 1st series (pre-1939)
- **P.2d / P.3d** — Pacific Reporter (parallel citation)

Always include the parallel Pacific Reporter citation when available.

### Short form (after first citation)

- *Hangman Ridge*, 105 Wn.2d at 784.
- *Id.* at 785. (only if the immediately preceding citation is to
  the same case)

### Court designation

- No court designation needed for Washington Supreme Court cases in
  Wn.2d
- Court of Appeals cases in Wn. App. do not need "(Ct. App.)" — the
  reporter name indicates the court

### Pin cites

Use "at [page]" for the specific page reference within the opinion.

> *Bridges*, 154 Wn. App. at 726, 226 P.3d at 193.

## Washington statutes

### RCW (Revised Code of Washington)

> RCW 19.86.090.
> RCW 4.16.040(1).
> RCW 19.16.250(21).

- No year required in basic citation
- Use dots, not hyphens, in section numbers
- Subsections in parentheses

### WAC (Washington Administrative Code)

> WAC 308-29-060.

## Washington court rules

> CR 12(b)(6).
> CRLJ 37(a)(4).
> GR 14(a).
> ER 803(a)(6).

Short form of "Washington Court Rules." No date.

## Federal citations

### U.S. Code

> 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(2)(A).
> 15 U.S.C. §§ 1692e-1692f (range).
> 28 U.S.C. § 1331 (jurisdiction).

Always use "§" (section symbol). The "§§" indicates multiple sections.

### Code of Federal Regulations

> 12 C.F.R. § 1006.34 (Regulation F validation notice).

### Federal cases

Follow Bluebook:

> *Rotkiske v. Klemm*, 589 U.S. 8, 140 S. Ct. 355, 205 L. Ed. 2d 291
> (2019).
> *Henson v. Santander Consumer USA Inc.*, 582 U.S. 79, 137 S. Ct.
> 1718, 198 L. Ed. 2d 177 (2017).
> *Tourgeman v. Collins Fin. Servs., Inc.*, 755 F.3d 1109 (9th Cir.
> 2014).
> *Midland Funding LLC v. Brent*, 644 F. Supp. 2d 961 (N.D. Ohio
> 2009).

### Ninth Circuit

For persuasive authority from the Ninth Circuit:

> *Donohue v. Quick Collect, Inc.*, 592 F.3d 1027 (9th Cir. 2010).

## Constitutional provisions

> U.S. Const. amend. XIV, § 1.
> Wash. Const. art. I, § 3.

## Session laws

> Laws of 1973, ch. 104, § 3.

## Common citation pitfalls

### Italicization

- **Italicize** party names and signals ("See," "Cf.," "Accord," "But
  see")
- Do **not** italicize the reporter designation (Wn.2d, P.2d)
- In markdown, wrap in `*` or `_` for italics

### "Citation omitted"

When quoting from a case that itself cites another source:

> *Hangman Ridge*, 105 Wn.2d at 785 (citations omitted).

### Parenthetical explanations

Add after the citation, in parentheses, after the year:

> *Panag v. Farmers Ins. Co. of Wash.*, 166 Wn.2d 27, 42, 204 P.3d
> 885 (2009) (holding that CPA applies to communications from
> third-party debt collectors).

### Signal words (Bluebook)

- `See` — authority supports the proposition (indirect)
- `See also` — additional authority
- `Cf.` — authority supports by analogy
- `Accord` — another case reaching the same result
- `But see` — authority contradicts
- `Contra` — authority directly opposed

## Full citation checklist

For every citation, confirm:

- [ ] Case name italicized
- [ ] Volume number, reporter, first page
- [ ] Parallel Pacific Reporter cite (for WA cases)
- [ ] Pin cite (if quoting or referencing specific page)
- [ ] Year in parentheses
- [ ] Court designation if not obvious from reporter
- [ ] Subsequent history if any ("aff'd", "rev'd", "cert. denied")

## Notes

- **Always verify** that a case has not been overturned or
  distinguished — use Shepard's, KeyCite, or Casetext's SmartCite
- **Pin cites** are mandatory when you quote or rely on a specific
  holding
- A brief with citation errors loses credibility quickly — budget
  time to check every citation against the original source
