# Washington Civil Rules (CR / CRLJ)

Two parallel rule sets:

- **CR** — Civil Rules for Superior Court (RCW Title 4 cases, higher
  jurisdictional amounts)
- **CRLJ** — Civil Rules for Courts of Limited Jurisdiction (District
  and Municipal court, ≤ $100,000 and specified small claims)

Rule numbering is parallel wherever feasible. CRLJ 6 is the district-
court version of CR 6, etc. Where district practice differs (e.g., no
CR 56 summary judgment in district court), note below.

## Rule 11 — Sanctions

Both CR 11 and CRLJ 11 authorize sanctions against an attorney or
party who signs a pleading that is not well grounded in fact, not
warranted by existing law or a good-faith argument for extension, or
is filed for an improper purpose.

- **Safe harbor (21 days)** — serve the motion first, wait 21 days;
  if the offending paper is withdrawn during that window, the motion
  cannot be filed
- Must be a **separate motion**, not combined with other relief
- Sanctions may include fees, dismissal, or other appropriate
  sanctions

Applied carefully — rule 11 motions that fail often boomerang. Hold
them in reserve for clear patterns of misconduct.

## Rule 12 — Defenses and Objections

CR/CRLJ 12(b) enumerates pre-answer defenses:

1. Lack of subject-matter jurisdiction
2. Lack of personal jurisdiction
3. Improper venue
4. Insufficient process
5. Insufficient service of process
6. **Failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted**
7. Failure to join a party under CR 19

### CR 12(b)(6) — Failure to state a claim

The most common motion-to-dismiss ground. Standard: taking all
factual allegations as true and drawing all reasonable inferences,
can the plaintiff prove any set of facts justifying relief?

The plaintiff must plead every element of the claim with enough
specificity that the court can see a plausible entitlement to
relief. Conclusory or bare-bones pleadings (e.g., "defendant owes
an amount") are vulnerable to CR 12(b)(6). Subject-matter skills
supply the element lists for specific claim types.

### CR 12(f) — Motion to Strike

Court may strike from a pleading any insufficient defense or any
redundant, immaterial, impertinent, or scandalous matter. Used
against improper affidavits or declarations containing argument.

### CR 12(c) — Judgment on the Pleadings

After pleadings closed — same standard as 12(b)(6) but converts to
summary judgment if matters outside the pleadings are considered.

## Rule 26 — Discovery

### CR 26(b)(1) — Scope

Any matter not privileged that is relevant to the claim or defense
and proportional to the needs of the case.

### CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f) — Conference of Counsel

**Required before filing any discovery motion.** Must:

1. Confer in good faith
2. Attempt to resolve the dispute
3. Document the attempt

Certification of CR 26(i) compliance must be included in any motion
to compel. Failure to comply is grounds for denial without reaching
the merits.

Template certification:

```
CERTIFICATION OF CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f) COMPLIANCE

Pursuant to CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f), [Movant] certifies that [they]
conferred with opposing counsel on [date] via [phone / email /
letter], attempting in good faith to resolve the discovery dispute
without court intervention. [Description of efforts.] The parties
were unable to reach resolution, and this motion is therefore
necessary.
```

## Rule 37 — Motion to Compel and Sanctions

The primary discovery-enforcement tool.

### CR 37(a) — Motion to Compel

Available when a party:

- Fails to answer a question on deposition
- Fails to answer an interrogatory (CR 33)
- Fails to respond or produce under CR 34 (requests for production)
- Fails to respond to requests for admission (CR 36)

### CR 37(a)(4) — Expenses

**If the motion is granted**, the court **shall** require the losing
party to pay the moving party's reasonable expenses, including
attorney fees, unless the court finds the opposition substantially
justified or other circumstances make award unjust. This is
mandatory fee-shifting language.

Include an express request for fees in the motion.

### CR 37(b) — Failure to Comply with Order

Escalating sanctions: designating facts as established, striking
pleadings, dismissal, contempt.

### CR 37(d) — Failure to Attend Deposition / Respond

Sanctions without first obtaining an order compelling.

## Rule 55 — Default

### CR 55(a) — Entry of Default

When a party fails to appear, plead, or otherwise defend, the court
enters default. Requires:

- Proof of service of the summons
- Declaration that no answer has been filed and time to answer has
  expired
- **Military affidavit** (SCRA compliance)

### CR 55(b) — Default Judgment

After entry of default, judgment may be entered. If damages are
unliquidated, a hearing or declaration is required to establish the
amount.

CRLJ 55 tracks CR 55 closely in district court.

## Rule 56 — Summary Judgment (Superior Court only)

No equivalent in CRLJ. District court does not have summary judgment
procedure under the civil rules.

### Standard

No genuine issue of material fact and the movant is entitled to
judgment as a matter of law. Evidence viewed in the light most
favorable to the non-movant.

### Timing (CR 56(c))

- Motion must be filed and served at least **28 days** before the
  hearing
- Response and opposing affidavits at least **11 days** before the
  hearing
- Reply and rebuttal affidavits at least **5 days** before

### Supporting materials

Affidavits / declarations must be based on personal knowledge, show
admissibility of facts at trial, and be made by competent witnesses
(CR 56(e)). Conclusory statements or statements based on "information
and belief" do not suffice.

## Rule 6 — Time Computation

### CR 6(a) — Computation

- Exclude the day of the act
- Include the last day unless it is a weekend / holiday
- Intermediate weekends and holidays **count** if the period is more
  than 11 days

### CR 6(d) — Enlargement / Reduction

- Motion to extend time must be made before the period expires,
  unless good cause and excusable neglect for late filing

### CRLJ 6(d) — Motion deadlines in district court

- Motion must be filed and served at least **5 court days** before
  the hearing
- Response at least **3 court days** before
- Reply no later than **noon the day before** the hearing

Different from superior court — in CR, motions are noted with
different lead times (typically 6+ calendar days for civil motions).

## Service Rules

### CR / CRLJ 4 — Service of Summons

Personal service on the defendant or substituted service per statute.
Defects in service (e.g., process-server affidavit lacking personal
knowledge of the person served) are grounds for a CR 12(b)(5)
motion.

### CR / CRLJ 5 — Service of Pleadings After Summons

- Mail, email (with consent), or delivery
- Service by email is permitted only if parties agree or local rule
  permits
- **3 days added** for service by mail (CR 6(e))

## Typical pro se motion stack

General civil defense cadence:

1. **Answer + Counterclaims** (CR/CRLJ 8, 12, 13) — within 20
   days of service (20-day window applies to most civil cases;
   verify against the governing statute and see
   `wa-first-30-days`)
2. **First discovery** — requests for production, interrogatories
   (CR 33, 34)
3. **CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f) conference** if responses deficient
4. **Motion to compel** with fee request (CR 37(a))
5. **Second discovery round** targeting remaining gaps
6. **Motion to dismiss / for SJ** (CR 12(b)(6) / CR 56) — in
   superior court (CRLJ has no direct SJ analogue)
7. **CR 11 motion** if pattern of misconduct documented (district
   court: CRLJ 11)

Subject-matter skills supply the claim-specific variations on
this stack (e.g., a typical debt-buyer defense adds targeted
motion-to-compel rounds focused on chain of title — see
`wa-consumer-debt/SKILL.md`).

## Notes

- Always read the current rule text before relying on these summaries
  — the Washington Court Rules are amended regularly
- Local county rules supplement state rules — check KCDC's local
  rules (`wa-kcdc/references/`) before filing
- The rule numbers are parallel but not identical — some CR rules have
  no CRLJ counterpart (like CR 56)
