# Key Cases — General Civil Procedure (Washington)

A condensed citation reference for cases that bear on Washington
civil motion practice, summary judgment, fee-shifting, and default-
vacation — the general-purpose procedural doctrines. Subject-matter
case catalogs live alongside their bundles (e.g., debt-specific
cases are in `wa-consumer-debt/references/key-cases.md`).

Each entry: full citation, one-sentence holding, what it's cited
for, and a fetch pointer for verification.

> **Always shepardize before citing.** The holdings below are
> summaries, not substitutes for reading the opinion. Washington
> doctrine on fee-shifting, SJ burden, and CR 60 has evolved;
> treat these as starting points.

---

## Civil Rules — Motion Practice and Summary Judgment

### *Folsom v. Burger King*

- **Cite**: 135 Wn.2d 658, 958 P.2d 301 (1998)
- **Holding**: On summary judgment, the court must construe
  evidence and inferences in favor of the non-moving party.
- **Cite for**: summary judgment standard (non-movant)
- **Fetch**: CourtListener / Google Scholar (search "Folsom Burger
  King Washington")

### *Young v. Key Pharmaceuticals, Inc.*

- **Cite**: 112 Wn.2d 216, 770 P.2d 182 (1989)
- **Holding**: A defendant moving for summary judgment may meet
  its initial burden by pointing out the absence of evidence on
  an element on which the plaintiff bears the burden; the
  plaintiff must then produce admissible evidence on that
  element.
- **Cite for**: SJ burden-shift; defendant moving for SJ when
  plaintiff cannot produce required proof
- **Fetch**: CourtListener ("Young v. Key Pharmaceuticals")

## Attorney Fees and Costs

### *Mellor v. Chamberlin*

- **Cite**: 100 Wn.2d 643, 673 P.2d 610 (1983)
- **Holding**: RCW 4.84.330 makes one-sided attorney-fee clauses
  in contracts reciprocal; the prevailing party recovers
  reasonable fees.
- **Cite for**: reciprocal fee-shifting under RCW 4.84.330

### *Bowers v. Transamerica Title Ins. Co.*

- **Cite**: 100 Wn.2d 581, 675 P.2d 193 (1983)
- **Holding**: Washington calculates reasonable attorney fees
  using the lodestar method (reasonable hours × reasonable
  rate, adjusted).
- **Cite for**: fee calculation methodology

### *City of Seattle v. McCready*

- **Cite**: 131 Wn.2d 266, 931 P.2d 156 (1997)
- **Holding**: Washington follows the American rule — each party
  bears its own fees absent contract, statute, or recognized
  equitable exception.
- **Cite for**: baseline fee rule; used to frame why a specific
  statute or contract provision controls

## CR 60 — Motions to Vacate

### *Morin v. Burris*

- **Cite**: 160 Wn.2d 745, 161 P.3d 956 (2007)
- **Holding**: On a CR 60(b) motion to vacate a default
  judgment, the court considers (1) whether the defaulting
  party has a meritorious defense, (2) whether the failure to
  appear was due to mistake or excusable neglect, (3) due
  diligence in moving to vacate, and (4) prejudice to the
  opposing party.
- **Cite for**: standard for vacating default judgments
- **Fetch**: CourtListener ("Morin v. Burris Washington")

### *White v. Holm*

- **Cite**: 73 Wn.2d 348, 438 P.2d 581 (1968)
- **Holding**: The four-part test for setting aside a default.
- **Cite for**: historical foundation of the *Morin* framework

## Citation format

Washington opinions: *Case Name*, Volume Wn.2d (or Wn. App.)
Page, Parallel P.2d (or P.3d) Page (Year)

```
   Folsom v. Burger King,
   135 Wn.2d 658, 958 P.2d 301 (1998).
```

Federal opinions: *Case Name*, Volume F.3d Page (Circuit Year),
*cert. granted/denied*, Year.

Supreme Court: *Case Name*, Volume U.S. Page, Volume S. Ct. Page
(Year).

See `citation-format.md` for long-form and short-form
conventions.

## Fetch strategy

For any case in this catalog:

1. Search CourtListener:
   `https://www.courtlistener.com/?q=[case name]`
2. Open the opinion page — URL is stable.
3. Cite-check against the official reporter (Wn.2d / Wn. App. /
   P.2d / P.3d).
4. If the case is not on CourtListener, try Google Scholar
   case-law search.
