## Scope note

This plugin is **civil-focused**; WPIC is indexed here as a companion pointer
for completeness, not as primary coverage. The criminal procedural rules the
plugin carries verbatim are `../court-rules/CrR.md` (Superior Court) and
`../court-rules/CrRLJ.md` (Courts of Limited Jurisdiction).

## How WPIC is organized

WPIC uses a **topic-number** scheme (`WPIC <chapter>.<n>`) and is organized
into **Parts I–XVI**:

| Parts | Function |
|---|---|
| **I–III** | Issues of general applicability: stages of a criminal trial; common definitions; burden of proof; lesser-included offenses; elements of the crime; frequently used oral instructions; evidentiary issues; principles of liability (accomplice, causation, etc.). |
| **IV–XIII** | Instructions specific to particular criminal **charges and defenses** (the offense-specific chapters — homicide, assault, sex offenses, property crimes, drug offenses, etc., and affirmative defenses). |
| **XIV–XV** | Concluding pattern instructions and **verdict forms**. |
| **XVI** | Instructions for the jury's findings as to **aggravating circumstances** for exceptional sentences under the Sentencing Reform Act (RCW 9.94A). |

Browse the live table of contents for the exact chapter and instruction
numbers within each Part; the Committee revises and renumbers between editions.

## Verification

underlying RCW offense definition and controlling case law are current
(`wa-fact-check`), and tailor the elements instruction to the charge as
actually charged. Cite the controlling RCW / case as authority — the pattern
instruction is a drafting aid, not law.
