# Washington Evidence Rules (ER)

The ER apply in both superior and district court. The most
frequently-litigated rules across civil practice are hearsay
(ER 801–803), authentication (ER 901–902), best evidence
(ER 1001–1004), and personal knowledge (ER 602).

Subject-matter skills apply these rules to their own facts (e.g.,
`wa-consumer-debt/references/evidence-debt-buyer.md` for business-
records foundation in a debt-buyer case).

## ER 801 — Definitions

**Hearsay** (ER 801(c)): a statement, other than one made by the
declarant while testifying, offered to prove the truth of the matter
asserted.

**Statement** includes oral, written, or non-verbal conduct intended
as an assertion.

## ER 802 — Hearsay Rule

Hearsay is not admissible unless a rule, statute, or constitutional
provision provides otherwise.

## ER 803 — Exceptions (availability immaterial)

### ER 803(a)(6) — Business Records

A record of an act, event, or condition is not excluded if:

1. Made at or near the time of the event
2. By, or from information transmitted by, a person with knowledge
3. Kept in the course of a regularly conducted activity
4. It was the regular practice of that activity to make the record
5. Shown by the testimony of the **custodian** or other qualified
   witness

### Leading Washington cases on business-records foundation

- *State v. Iverson*, 126 Wn. App. 329, 108 P.3d 799 (2005) —
  business-records foundation; records received from a third party
  need authentication by the recipient or a witness with knowledge
  of the third-party's practices
- *State v. Ziegler*, 114 Wn.2d 533, 789 P.2d 79 (1990) — standards
  for custodian testimony
- *State v. Quincy*, 122 Wn. App. 395, 95 P.3d 353 (2004) —
  printouts from computer systems
- *Cashmere Valley Bank v. Dep't of Revenue*, 181 Wn.2d 622, 334
  P.3d 1100 (2014) — general business-records foundation

### Other ER 803 exceptions

- **803(a)(1)** — Present sense impression
- **803(a)(2)** — Excited utterance
- **803(a)(3)** — Then-existing mental, emotional, or physical
  condition
- **803(a)(4)** — Statements for medical diagnosis or treatment
- **803(a)(8)** — Public records (records of public offices or
  agencies)

## ER 804 — Exceptions (declarant unavailable)

Former testimony, dying declarations, statements against interest,
statement of personal or family history.

## ER 901 — Authentication

Requirement satisfied by evidence sufficient to support a finding
that the matter is what its proponent claims.

### Common methods (ER 901(b))

- (1) Testimony of witness with knowledge
- (2) Nonexpert opinion on handwriting
- (4) Distinctive characteristics
- (6) Telephone conversations
- (9) Process or system showing reliability (for computer-
  generated records)

### Authentication of electronic records

Computer-generated records — whether account printouts, database
exports, or log files — typically require:

1. Testimony from someone with knowledge of how the system creates
   and stores records, and
2. A showing that the system is reliable

## ER 902 — Self-Authentication

Certain documents authenticate themselves:

- (1) Domestic public documents under seal
- (2) Domestic public documents not under seal
- (4) Certified copies of public records
- (5) Official publications
- (8) Acknowledged documents (notarized)
- (11) **Certified domestic records of regularly conducted
  activity** — business records with a written declaration of
  custodian, provided written notice was given and an opportunity
  to inspect was provided

ER 902(11) bypasses the need for a live custodian — but the
declaration must still meet ER 803(a)(6) substance.

## ER 1001–1004 — Best Evidence Rule

### ER 1002 — Requirement of Original

To prove the content of a writing, recording, or photograph, the
original is required, except as provided in these rules.

### ER 1003 — Duplicates

Duplicates admissible to the same extent as originals unless a
genuine question is raised as to authenticity of the original or
it would be unfair to admit the duplicate.

### ER 1004 — Admissibility of Other Evidence

Original not required if:

1. Original lost or destroyed (not through proponent's bad faith)
2. Original not obtainable
3. Original in possession of opponent
4. Not closely related to a controlling issue

### ER 1006 — Summaries

Contents of voluminous writings, recordings, or photographs which
cannot conveniently be examined in court may be presented in the
form of a chart, summary, or calculation — **provided the
underlying originals or duplicates are made available to the other
party at a reasonable time and place**.

## ER 602 — Personal Knowledge

A witness may not testify unless evidence is introduced sufficient
to support a finding that the witness has personal knowledge of the
matter.

## Evidentiary objections at hearing

Short-form objections for the record:

- "Objection — hearsay, ER 802. No exception laid."
- "Objection — lack of foundation, ER 901 and ER 803(a)(6)."
- "Objection — best evidence, ER 1002. No showing the original is
  unavailable."
- "Objection — lack of personal knowledge, ER 602."

## Pleading evidentiary challenges

### In opposition to summary judgment

File a declaration identifying each supporting document and stating:

1. The document is hearsay (ER 801/802)
2. No exception has been laid (ER 803(a)(6) foundation)
3. Authentication is not shown (ER 901)
4. Best-evidence unavailability not established (ER 1002/1004)

A court must disregard unsworn or inadmissible evidence on SJ.

### In motion in limine (before trial)

Ask the court to exclude inadmissible evidence pre-trial.

## Notes

- Hearsay and foundation arguments are often case-dispositive —
  especially at summary judgment — when a party relies on records
  it did not itself create and cannot lay foundation for.
- Always preserve the objection on the record; failure to object
  forfeits the issue on appeal.
- Judges vary in how strictly they enforce ER 803(a)(6) foundation
  requirements — research the assigned judge's prior rulings.
- Subject-matter skills supply the fact-pattern application of
  these rules (e.g., debt-buyer custodian affidavits, landlord
  ledgers, medical records).
