# KCSC — case assignment area, facilities, and the CICS

> Verify everything here against the **current King County LCR** and the
> King County Superior Court Clerk's forms page before relying on it.
> Boundaries, form versions, and assignment codes change.

## The two facilities

| Facility | Address | Assignment area |
|---|---|---|
| **King County Courthouse** | 516 Third Ave, Seattle, WA 98104 | **Seattle** |
| **Maleng Regional Justice Center (MRJC)** | 401 Fourth Ave N, Kent, WA 98032 | **Kent** |

The Clerk's main office and Ex Parte operate at both locations; some
calendars/departments sit at only one. Check the Superior Court
"calendars and locations" page before an in-person appearance:
`https://kingcounty.gov/en/court/superior-court`.

## Case assignment area — LCR 82

A civil case is assigned to either the **Seattle** or the **Kent** area
under **LCR 82(e)**. The rough split (confirm the exact text in the
current LCR 82):

- **Seattle area** — King County **north of I-90** and the I-90
  right-of-way, plus all of the cities of **Seattle, Mercer Island,
  Bellevue, Issaquah, and North Bend**, and all of **Vashon and Maury
  Islands**.
- **Kent area** — all of King County **south of I-90** except the areas
  in the Seattle area above.

Notes:

- **Cause-number code.** Every pleading and document must show the
  assignment-area code after the cause number (commonly rendered `SEA`
  or `KNT`). Under LCR 82 the **Clerk may reject** a filing that omits
  it. Take the exact code from the caption you were served or from the
  Clerk's filing confirmation — don't invent it.
- **Older cases.** Civil cases filed before Sept. 1, 1995 default to the
  Seattle area unless the court orders otherwise.
- **Wrong area / transfer.** If a case is filed in the wrong area, the
  remedy is a motion to change the case assignment area under LCR 82 —
  not a refiling.

## The Case Information Cover Sheet / Case Assignment Designation (CICS)

Filed with the **initial pleading**. It (a) classifies the case type for
the Clerk and (b) certifies, under LCR 82(e), which assignment area the
case belongs in. Separate **civil** and **family-law** versions exist —
use the civil CICS for a debt or general civil matter. Pull the current
form from the King County Superior Court Clerk (DJA) forms page:
`https://kingcounty.gov/en/dept/dja/courts-jails-legal-system/court-forms-document-filing/forms`.

If you're the **defendant** and not filing an initial pleading, you
don't file a CICS — but you do need to put the existing assignment code
on everything you file, and you should calendar matters at the correct
facility.
