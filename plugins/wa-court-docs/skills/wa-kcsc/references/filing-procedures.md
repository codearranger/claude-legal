# Filing Procedures — KCSC

## E-filing through the Clerk (DJA)

KCSC accepts e-filing through the **King County Superior Court Clerk's
E-Filing application**, reached from the Department of Judicial
Administration (DJA) filing page:
`https://kingcounty.gov/en/dept/dja/courts-jails-legal-system/court-forms-document-filing/filing`.

- **Civil filings** are e-filed; most documents are accepted as PDF, one
  document per upload (motion, memorandum, declaration, exhibits-by-
  declaration, proposed order, note, certificate of service — each as a
  separate PDF).
- **Initial pleading** e-filings receive an electronic **conformed copy**
  back from the Clerk. View later filings through **eRecords**.
- **CICS / Case Assignment Designation** travels with the initial
  pleading (see `seattle-and-mrjc.md`). Without it, the case is not
  fully set up.
- **Cause-number area code** (`SEA` / `KNT`) must appear on the cause
  number on every document; under LCR 82 the Clerk may reject a filing
  that omits it.
- **Original signed orders**: in some scenarios the Clerk requests an
  original paper signature — confirm with the assigned department or
  Ex Parte before relying on an e-signed proposed order for a
  consequential order.
- **Exhibits**: travel with the declaration that authenticates them; do
  not file exhibits separately. Each exhibit gets a cover page labeled
  **EXHIBIT A/B/C**.
- **File size**: typical portal caps (commonly ≈ 25 MB / document);
  split oversized PDFs (re-OCR voluminous exhibits at a lower DPI if
  needed).
- **Fees**: see the Clerk's current fee schedule on the DJA site. The
  statewide filing-fee baseline is in `wa-law-references`; King County
  adds local fees.

## Service

Under **CR 5**, service on opposing counsel can be by:

- **Email** (if the parties have agreed, or after initial appearance —
  CR 5(b)(7) framework)
- **Personal delivery**
- **U.S. Mail** or commercial delivery (adds 3 days to certain deadlines
  under CR 6(e))
- **Fax** (rare; confirm opposing counsel still accepts)

Serve the same day you file. **For pro se filers with opposing counsel
who has a documented history of disputing service**, use both email
**and** certified mail with return receipt, and include a Certificate of
Service showing both methods.

## Working copies (LCR 7) — required

KCSC **requires** working copies for noted motions (this is the key
operational difference from KCDC). Mechanics:

- Deliver through the Clerk's electronic **Submit Working Copies** tool
  (linked from the DJA filing page), or as the department directs;
  some departments accept or prefer **paper** working copies.
- Due by the LCR 7 deadline (a set number of court days before the
  hearing — confirm the current LCR 7).
- Carry the **working-copy coversheet** identifying the judge/department,
  hearing date, and moving party.
- Working copies are **not** filed in the Clerk's record — they are
  delivered to chambers separately from the e-filed documents.
- Include the motion, memorandum, declarations, exhibits, and proposed
  order — i.e., everything the judge needs to rule.

For longer/voluminous motions, also offer a **paper** working copy by
courtesy even if the department normally accepts electronic — confirm
with chambers.

## Certificate of Service

Every filing should include a Certificate (or Declaration) of Service:

```
                    CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I certify under penalty of perjury under the laws of the State of
Washington that on [date] I caused a true and correct copy of the
foregoing [Motion/Declaration/Order] to be served on the following:

   [Opposing counsel name, firm]
   [Address]
   [Email]

   [X]  By email to: [email addresses]
   [X]  By U.S. Mail, Certified Mail No. [tracking number]

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.

                                 ______________________________
                                 [Name], pro se
```

## Preserving service evidence

- **Photograph postmarked envelopes** before opening incoming mail
- **Keep USPS certified mail receipts and green return cards** — scan
  both sides
- **Save email metadata**: full headers, timestamps, attachment lists
- **For certified mail**: record the Article Number on your Certificate
  of Service and attach the scanned receipt

## Ex Parte — when to use it

Many KCSC matters do not go on a department motion calendar at all:

- **Agreed orders** (continuances, dismissals, stipulated orders)
- **Defaults and default judgments** in eligible cases
- **Supplemental-proceeding orders** (orders compelling a debtor exam,
  etc.) and other post-judgment procedural orders
- **Sealing, name-change, and certain probate orders**

Present these through **Ex Parte via the Clerk** (the Clerk-administered
review process) or in person at the **Ex Parte and Probate Department**
at either facility. The DJA site posts the current Ex Parte procedures,
forms, and submission queue.

## Common filing errors to avoid

| Error | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Missing case caption | Copy caption exactly from a prior filing |
| Wrong cause-number area code | Use **`SEA`** or **`KNT`** as on the served caption; under LCR 82 the Clerk may reject a filing that omits it |
| Missing CICS on initial pleading | File the civil **Case Information Cover Sheet / Case Assignment Designation** with the complaint |
| Missing Certificate of Service | Attach one to every filing |
| Missing working copies | Deliver through the **Submit Working Copies** tool (or as the department directs) by the LCR 7 deadline — KCSC requires them |
| Unconfirmed motion | Confirm by the LCR 7 / department deadline; unconfirmed motions are struck |
| Missing Note for Civil Motion Calendar | File the King County Note (pull current version from DJA forms) with the packet |
| Exhibits not labeled | Each exhibit gets a cover page labeled EXHIBIT A/B/C |
| Colored text in body | Violates GR 14 — use bold/italic for emphasis |
| PDF too large | Split or re-OCR at lower resolution |
| Wrong rule cited (CRLJ instead of CR) | KCSC follows **CR** (not CRLJ — that is the district-court set) and the **King County LCR** |
