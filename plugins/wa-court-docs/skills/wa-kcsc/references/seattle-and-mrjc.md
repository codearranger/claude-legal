# King County Superior Court — facilities and case assignment area

KCSC sits at **two** facilities. The **Case Assignment Area** under
LCR 82 fixes which facility a case belongs to, and the area code travels
with the cause number for the life of the case.

## Locations

**King County Courthouse — Seattle (`SEA` case assignment area)**
516 Third Ave
Seattle, WA 98104

**Maleng Regional Justice Center (MRJC) — Kent (`KNT` case assignment area)**
401 Fourth Ave N
Kent, WA 98032

Each facility hosts a share of the judge departments, the Clerk's filing
counters, and the Ex Parte and Probate Department. The Clerk operates a
single Ex Parte queue across both facilities for many matters
(Ex Parte via the Clerk).

## Case Assignment Area — LCR 82

A civil case is assigned to either the **Seattle** or the **Kent** area
under **LCR 82(e)**. The rough split (verify the current LCR 82 text):

- **Seattle area** — King County **north of I-90** and the I-90
  right-of-way, plus all of the cities of **Seattle, Mercer Island,
  Bellevue, Issaquah, and North Bend**, and all of **Vashon and Maury
  Islands**.
- **Kent area** — all of King County **south of I-90** except the areas
  in the Seattle area above.

Consequences for every filing:

- **Cause-number code**: every pleading and document must show the
  assignment-area code after the cause number — commonly `SEA` for
  Seattle or `KNT` for Kent. Per LCR 82, the Clerk **may reject** a
  filing that omits the code. Take the code from the caption you were
  served or from the Clerk's filing confirmation — don't invent one.
- **Older cases**: civil cases filed before September 1, 1995 default to
  the Seattle area unless the court orders otherwise.
- **Wrong area / transfer**: the remedy for a case filed in the wrong
  area is a **motion to change the case assignment area** under LCR 82,
  not a refiling.

## Case Information Cover Sheet / Case Assignment Designation ("CICS")

Filed with the **initial pleading**, the CICS does two things:

1. **Classifies the case type** for the Clerk's case-management system.
2. **Certifies the LCR 82(e) assignment area** (Seattle vs. Kent) under
   the rule's criteria.

Separate **civil** and **family-law** versions exist; use the civil CICS
for a debt-collection, consumer-protection, or general civil matter. Pull
the **current** version from the DJA forms page above (the form is
revised periodically).

If you are the **defendant** and not filing an initial pleading, you do
not file a CICS — but every document you file must carry the existing
assignment-area code, and you should calendar matters at the correct
facility.

## Judge departments and courtrooms

KCSC operates by **department**, not by a single county-wide motion
docket. Department assignments rotate; the assigned department appears on
the Case Schedule and any orders. Confirm:

- **Current department assignment** for your case: from the Case
  Schedule, the Clerk's eRecords, or by contacting the assigned
  department.
- **Department procedures** (noting, confirmation, working-copy delivery
  preference, page limits, oral argument practice): from the
  department's page on the Superior Court site.

Courtrooms and Ex Parte rooms are identified on each facility's posted
calendar; do not pre-fill a courtroom number on a Note until the
department has confirmed it.

## Civil jurisdiction

KCSC is a court of general jurisdiction. Civil matters at KCSC include:

- Contract and tort claims (no upper monetary limit; the **lower** limit
  is set by district-court concurrent jurisdiction under RCW 3.66.020)
- Real-property actions (UD over the district-court limit, quiet title,
  foreclosure)
- Family law (separate calendar; not the focus of this plugin)
- Probate, guardianship, and trust matters (Ex Parte and Probate
  Department)
- Civil-protection orders within the superior-court rubric
- Appeals from KCDC under the **RALJ**
