# Satisfaction of Judgment — RCW 4.56.100

When a judgment is paid — in full or in part — the creditor **must
file a satisfaction** with the court. This clears the docket,
releases any liens, and ends collection activity.

## Authority

- **RCW 4.56.100** — creditor's duty to file satisfaction
- **RCW 4.56.110** — penalties for refusal to file
- **RCW 4.56.190** — entry of satisfaction by the court

## Creditor's duty

Within **60 days** of payment, the creditor must:

1. File a **Satisfaction of Judgment** with the court clerk
2. Provide a copy to the debtor

Failure to file within 60 days exposes the creditor to:

- **$500 damages** payable to the debtor
- **Attorney fees and costs** of a motion to compel
- **Credit-reporting consequences** (failure to report as paid
  may violate the FCRA)

## If the creditor files promptly

- Request a file-stamped copy from the court
- Check credit reports — the judgment should be updated to
  "paid / satisfied" within 30-45 days
- Check with any recording offices where judgment liens were
  recorded — file the satisfaction there too if needed

## If the creditor does not file

### Step 1 — Demand letter

Send a written demand, certified mail, to the creditor (or its
counsel of record).

### Template

```
[Date]

[Creditor / Counsel of Record]
[Address]

Via Certified Mail — Return Receipt Requested

    Re:  Demand for Satisfaction of Judgment
         [Case name], Cause No. [number]
         [Court name]

Dear [Name]:

On [date], I paid the full amount of the judgment entered
against me in the above matter, totaling $[amount]. A copy of
[the cleared check / the wire confirmation / the payment
receipt] is attached as Exhibit A.

Under RCW 4.56.100, you are required to file a Satisfaction of
Judgment with the Court within 60 days of receipt of payment.
More than 60 days have now passed. No satisfaction has been
filed.

I hereby demand that you file a Satisfaction of Judgment with the
[Court name] Court within 14 days of this letter. If you fail to
do so, I will move the Court for an order declaring the judgment
satisfied and for sanctions under RCW 4.56.110, including $500
statutory damages and attorney fees and costs.

Sincerely,

[Name]
[Address, phone, email]
```

Keep the **certified mail return receipt** as proof.

### Step 2 — Motion to compel satisfaction

If the creditor does not respond within 14 days (or within a
reasonable time), file a motion with the court.

### Motion template

```
[COURT HEADER AND CAPTION]

            [PARTY]'S MOTION FOR ORDER OF SATISFACTION
                    OF JUDGMENT AND SANCTIONS

                      I. RELIEF REQUESTED

[Party] respectfully requests that the Court:

    SATISFIED in full;

    this motion;

                    II. STATEMENT OF FACTS

    of $[amount] by [check / wire / other].

    as Exhibit A.

4.  Under RCW 4.56.100, [Plaintiff / Creditor] was required to
    file a Satisfaction of Judgment within 60 days of receiving
    payment.

5.  More than 60 days have passed. No Satisfaction has been
    filed.

6.  On [date], [Party] sent a written demand, attached as
    Exhibit B, with certified-mail return receipt attached as
    Exhibit C. [Plaintiff / Creditor] has not responded and has
    not filed.

                III. STATEMENT OF ISSUES

A.  Whether the Court should enter an Order declaring the
    Judgment satisfied.

                 IV. EVIDENCE RELIED UPON

                    VI. ARGUMENT

[Email]

5.  [Plaintiff / Creditor] shall pay [Party] $[amount] in
    attorney fees and costs.

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.

                              ____________________________________
                              Judge / Court Commissioner
```

## Partial satisfaction

If the debtor has paid less than the full amount:

- Creditor files a **Partial Satisfaction** showing the amount
  paid and the remaining balance
- Remaining balance continues to accrue **post-judgment
  interest** at 12% simple per RCW 4.56.110
- Liens remain for the unpaid balance

## Entry of satisfaction by the court

Under RCW 4.56.190, the court may enter a satisfaction on its own
if:

- The judgment has been paid, AND
- The creditor cannot be found, OR
- The creditor refuses to file, OR
- The judgment has been satisfied by operation of law (e.g.,
  the debt was discharged in bankruptcy)

Process: motion, declaration with evidence of payment or
discharge, proposed order. The court can enter satisfaction
without creditor involvement.

## Satisfaction after bankruptcy

If the judgment debt was **discharged in bankruptcy**:

- The judgment is **no longer enforceable**
- The debtor is entitled to have the judgment cleared from the
  court docket and any recording offices
- Process: motion with attached discharge order; proposed
  satisfaction order

## Credit reporting after satisfaction

Once the satisfaction is filed:

- Credit bureaus should update within 30-45 days
- Dispute any bureau that still reports "unsatisfied" — FCRA
  rights apply
- Creditors who fail to update the reporting can be liable under
  the FCRA for furnishing inaccurate information (15 U.S.C. §
  1681s-2)

## If the creditor threatens further collection

If the judgment is satisfied but the creditor (or a successor who
bought the paper) continues to collect:

- **Send cease-and-desist** under FDCPA § 1692c(c)
- **File an FDCPA claim** in federal court for continued
  collection on a paid debt — this is a textbook § 1692e
  violation
- **File a CPA claim** under RCW 19.86 in state court
- **File a motion for contempt** or for sanctions in the
  original case

## Best practices

### Pay by traceable method

- Cashier's check, wire transfer, or business check — not cash
- Keep all receipts, wire confirmations, cleared-check copies

### Get a payoff letter before paying

- Ask the creditor for a **written payoff amount** good through a
  specific date
- Pay exactly the payoff amount
- Reference the cause number and payoff letter on the payment

### Get satisfaction in writing same day

- At the time of final payment, demand a written satisfaction
  ready to be filed
- Better yet: negotiate a settlement that includes filing of
  satisfaction as a condition

### Record-keeping

- Keep payment records for at least **7 years** — credit-
  reporting disputes may arise years later
- Keep the file-stamped satisfaction indefinitely

## Notes

- **Post-judgment interest** is 12% simple per RCW 4.56.110 — it
  accrues daily from the date of judgment. When paying off a
  judgment, compute through the day of expected payment (or a
  few days after to account for mail / processing time)
- **Creditor-side costs** added to the judgment (attorney fees,
  post-judgment costs) must be authorized by statute or contract
  — review before paying
- **Unclaimed judgments** — some old judgments have been sold or
  assigned multiple times; verify current owner before paying
- **Pro bono** support is available from the King County Bar and
  other clinics for satisfaction motions, especially where the
  creditor is unresponsive
