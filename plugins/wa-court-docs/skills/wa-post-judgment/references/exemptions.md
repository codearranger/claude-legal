# Washington Exemptions — Reference Charts

Detailed exemption amounts and sources. Use when completing an
Exemption Claim Form or planning collection defense.

## Wages

### Consumer debt (RCW 6.27.150(1)(a)(ii))

**80% of disposable earnings exempt.** "Disposable earnings" means
gross earnings less mandatory deductions (taxes, Social Security,
Medicare). Voluntary deductions (401k, health insurance beyond
mandatory) typically do not reduce disposable earnings for this
calculation — verify current formulation in the statute.

### Non-consumer debt (RCW 6.27.150(1)(a)(i))

Federal minimum applies: **75% of disposable earnings** or the
amount above **35 × federal minimum wage per week**, whichever
gives the debtor more.

### Child support / spousal support

**Higher garnishment allowed** under federal CCPA (15 U.S.C. §
1673(b)):

- 50% if debtor supports another spouse or child
- 60% if not
- Additional 5% if payments are more than 12 weeks in arrears

### Current calculation example (consumer debt)

Assume weekly gross $1,000, disposable $800 after mandatory
deductions.

- 80% exempt = $640 exempt
- 20% available for garnishment = $160/week

## Bank accounts and cash

### Statutory allowance (RCW 6.15.010)

- Individual debtor: **$2,500** total bank/cash exemption
- Applies across accounts, not per account

### Household goods (RCW 6.15.010)

- Clothing: unlimited reasonable value
- Household goods, furniture, appliances: up to **$6,500**
  combined
- Private library: up to **$3,500**

### Motor vehicles (RCW 6.15.010)

- Up to **$10,000** aggregate value in two vehicles
- Applies to equity, not market value — loan balance reduces

### Tools of trade (RCW 6.15.010)

- Up to **$15,000** in farm implements, tools, professional
  equipment necessary for trade or business

### Personal property catch-all

- Up to **$3,000** of any personal property (except wages,
  bank, homestead, federal-law-protected)

## Homestead (RCW 6.13)

Amended 2021 to tie to county median sale price. Current amount:

- **Equal to the county median sale price of a single-family
  home** in the preceding year, published annually by Washington
  Center for Real Estate Research
- Ranges by county from ~$125,000 (rural counties) to ~$1.2M+
  (King, San Juan)

### Protection

- Protects **equity** (value minus encumbrances), not market
  value
- Automatic on the debtor's principal residence
- **Does not** protect against purchase-money mortgages or tax
  liens
- **Does** protect against most other creditors

### How to claim

- Homestead is **automatic** on the principal residence
- If property is sold or transferred, the exemption proceeds
  generally transfer for 1 year
- For disputes, a declaration of homestead can be recorded
  (RCW 6.13.040)

## Retirement and insurance

### Employer retirement plans (ERISA)

**100% exempt** under 29 U.S.C. § 1056(d) (ERISA anti-alienation)
— 401(k), 403(b), pension plans.

### IRAs (RCW 6.15.020)

**Exempt to the extent reasonably necessary for support** — in
practice, usually fully exempt unless extraordinary balance.

### Social Security (42 U.S.C. § 407)

**100% exempt**. Federal preemption. Applies to benefits on
deposit in a bank account identifiable as Social Security under
31 C.F.R. § 212.

### SSI (42 U.S.C. § 1383(d))

**100% exempt**.

### VA benefits (38 U.S.C. § 5301)

**100% exempt**.

### Unemployment (RCW 50.40.020)

**100% exempt** while in the hands of the Department or in the
payee's account.

### Workers' compensation (RCW 51.32.040)

**100% exempt**.

### Life insurance cash value (RCW 48.18.410)

Exempt to beneficiary; for debtor's own cash value, see statute
for limits.

### Disability insurance proceeds (RCW 48.18.420)

Exempt.

## Public benefits

### TANF / cash public assistance (RCW 74.04.280)

**100% exempt**.

### SNAP (food stamps)

**100% exempt** under 7 U.S.C. § 2017(b).

### Child support received (RCW 6.15.020(3)(b))

**100% exempt**.

### Earned Income Tax Credit refund (RCW 6.15.010)

Exempt to the extent reasonably necessary for family support
(courts have generally protected EITC refunds).

## Prisoner of war / military service

Federal Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (50 U.S.C. § 3901 et
seq.) provides additional protections against default
judgments, interest rate caps, and suspension of certain
collection actions.

## Personal injury awards

### Washington (RCW 6.15.010)

Proceeds from personal injury or wrongful death claims:
**generally exempt** to the extent reasonably necessary for
support of the debtor and dependents.

### Federal

Certain settlement proceeds protected by federal statute (e.g.,
VA disability payments).

## Professional licenses, trade tools

Beyond the $15,000 tools-of-trade exemption:

- Professional licenses themselves are not "property" subject to
  execution in most cases
- Earnings from professional practice are wages and subject to
  the wage exemption formula

## Prepaid tuition accounts

### GET / Washington 529 accounts (RCW 28B.95.070)

Exempt from claims of the account owner's creditors (with some
exceptions for the student beneficiary's creditors).

## Exempt funds mixed with non-exempt

**Tracing rule**: If exempt funds (e.g., Social Security) are
deposited into an account that also holds non-exempt funds, the
debtor bears the burden of tracing — but courts apply a presumption
favoring exempt funds where mixed in good faith.

**Best practice**: Keep exempt income in a separate account. A
"Social Security only" account makes exemption claims nearly
automatic.

## Sources to cite on the Exemption Claim Form

When claiming, cite the specific authority. Judges and garnishee
attorneys move faster when the legal basis is clear:

- "Social Security Act, 42 U.S.C. § 407; 31 C.F.R. § 212"
- "VA benefits, 38 U.S.C. § 5301"
- "RCW 6.27.150 — 80% of disposable earnings"
- "RCW 6.15.010 — $2,500 bank account exemption"
- "ERISA, 29 U.S.C. § 1056(d)"

## Practical tips

- **Separate accounts** — keep Social Security / VA / pension
  deposits in accounts that receive only protected funds
- **Keep statements** — 2 months back to show the source of
  deposits under 31 C.F.R. § 212 lookback
- **Update addresses** — if a notice of garnishment is sent to
  the wrong address, you may miss the 21-day window; keep your
  address current with the court and creditors
- **Re-file** if a new writ issues — the exemption claim is
  specific to a particular writ; a second writ needs a second
  exemption claim
- **Aggregate** — if multiple accounts are caught, be sure to
  claim the bank exemption across accounts, not just one

## Notes

- **Dollar amounts** in RCW 6.15.010 were updated by the 2021
  legislature; verify current amounts against the statute at the
  time of filing — older guides may cite superseded numbers
- **Federal floor**: state exemptions cannot be less than federal
  floors for ERISA and federal benefit programs
- **Bankruptcy**: Chapter 7 and 13 debtors in Washington may elect
  either state or federal exemptions (check current election
  rules) — exemption amounts differ
- **Pro bono review** of an exemption claim is strongly advised
  where significant funds are at stake
