# Washington Landlord-Tenant — Statutory + Reform Framework

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** RLTA was extensively rewritten by
> SB 5160 (2021) and supplemental legislation through 2024.
> Verify every cite against the current RCW text — pre-2021
> precedent on no-cause terminations and 3-day notices is
> NO LONGER ACCURATE for residential tenancies.

## At a glance — Washington's L&T architecture

- **Substantive code**: **RCW 59.18 — Residential
  Landlord-Tenant Act (RLTA)** as comprehensively rewritten
  by **SB 5160 (2021)**. RCW 59.20 covers manufactured /
  mobile-home L&T. RCW 59.12 sets out unlawful-detainer
  summary proceedings.
- **Court rules**: General **CR** + **CRLJ** (residential
  evictions can be Superior or District depending on
  rent); **GR 14** formatting; **ELLLTC** (Eviction
  Limited Landlord-Tenant Court — district-court track).
- **Tenant Right to Counsel**: Washington became the
  **first state in the U.S.** to enact a state-level
  tenant Right to Counsel under **HB 1815 (2021)**,
  funding appointment of counsel for low-income tenants
  facing eviction.
- **ERP** — Eviction Resolution Program required pre-
  filing dispute resolution in many counties post-COVID.

## Just cause eviction — RCW 59.18.650 (the 2021 reform)

**SB 5160 (2021)** created statewide just-cause eviction
for residential tenancies. As of July 23, 2021, a landlord
**MAY NOT** terminate a periodic tenancy or refuse to
renew a fixed-term lease at expiration **without one of
the enumerated grounds** at RCW 59.18.650(2):

(a) Nonpayment of rent (14-day pay-or-vacate)
(b) Substantial breach of lease (10-day cure-or-vacate)
(c) Waste / nuisance / unlawful activity (3-day no-cure)
(d) Owner-occupied move-in (90-day notice)
(e) Sale of unit to buyer who will occupy (90-day notice)
(f) Substantial rehabilitation / demolition (90-day
    notice + relocation assistance for low-income tenants)
(g) Change of use (e.g., conversion to commercial) (120-
    day notice + relocation)
(h) Owner refuses to renew because tenant refused to
    sign rent increase (very narrow)
(i)-(p) Additional narrow grounds — see statute

**Pre-2021 "no cause" 20-day terminations are gone for
covered residential tenancies.**

### Coverage carve-outs

The just-cause requirement does NOT apply to:

- Shared-housing arrangements where tenant shares the
  landlord's primary residence (sublet from owner)
- Tenancies in single-family rentals if landlord owns
  ≤ 4 such units (qualified small-landlord exception in
  RCW 59.18.650(3))
- Some seasonal / transitional housing

## Notices — the critical changes

### Pay-or-vacate

- **14 days** (RCW 59.18.057, since 2019 — was previously
  3 days)
- Must use the **statutory mandatory form** at RCW
  59.18.057(1) — includes mandatory information about
  legal aid + tenant resources
- Insufficient-form notice = defective; landlord cannot
  proceed to unlawful detainer

### Comply-or-vacate (lease violation)

- **10 days** (RCW 59.18.180) — tenant has 10 days to
  cure curable breaches

### No-cure / immediate

- **3 days** for waste / nuisance / criminal activity
  (RCW 59.12.030(4))
- **20 days** for end of fixed-term lease without renewal
  IF a just-cause ground applies (RCW 59.18.650(2)(d)-(g))

### Owner move-in / sale

- **90 days** under RCW 59.18.650(2)(d)-(e)
- Owner must actually occupy / buyer must actually move in
- Tenant displaced for owner move-in has **right to first
  refusal** if owner re-rents within 12 months (RCW
  59.18.650(7))

## Unlawful detainer — RCW 59.12 + RCW 59.18.365-415

Washington's eviction summary proceeding:

### Step 1 — Service of notice

Landlord serves one of the notices above (14-day,
10-day, etc.) per RCW 59.12.040 service methods.

### Step 2 — Eviction Resolution Program (ERP) referral

Many counties (King, Pierce, Snohomish, Spokane,
Thurston, others) require landlord to **submit case to
ERP** before filing eviction. The Dispute Resolution
Center attempts mediation + rental-assistance matching.

### Step 3 — Summons and complaint

If ERP unsuccessful or not required, landlord files
unlawful-detainer complaint + Summons per RCW 59.18.365.
The summons is the **abbreviated 7-day** unlawful-
detainer summons, not the standard CR 4 20-day summons.

### Step 4 — Tenant response

Tenant has **7 days** to respond (RCW 59.18.365). A
written notice of appearance (not a full answer) within
7 days is enough to avoid default.

### Step 5 — Show cause hearing

If tenant appears, court sets **show cause hearing**
within **14-30 days** (RCW 59.18.370). At hearing:

- Tenant raises affirmative defenses
- Court determines whether to issue writ of restitution
  immediately OR set the matter for trial
- Common defense: notice-form defect; warranty of
  habitability; retaliation; rental assistance pending

### Step 6 — Writ of restitution

If landlord prevails at show cause, court issues writ of
restitution. Sheriff serves the writ + posts notice;
tenant typically has **3 calendar days** to vacate after
posting. Then sheriff returns to physically remove + lock
out (RCW 59.18.395).

## Right to Counsel — HB 1815 (2021)

Washington became the **first state in the U.S.** to
fund tenant counsel statewide for evictions:

- Tenants at or below 200% of federal poverty level
  qualify
- Counsel appointed by the Office of Civil Legal Aid
- Tenants must affirmatively request counsel; not
  automatic
- ~85% of WA eviction respondents now appear with counsel
  (post-2021 measurement)

This radically changed eviction landscape — landlords now
face represented tenants who can raise the full RLTA
defense playbook.

## Security deposits — RCW 59.18.260-285

- **No statutory cap** on deposit amount (but RCW
  59.18.610 caps additional fees)
- **Move-in checklist** required at start of tenancy
  (RCW 59.18.260)
- **14 days** to return deposit + itemized statement
  after tenancy ends (RCW 59.18.280) — formerly 21 days
- Failure to return + itemize within 14 days = **tenant
  recovers full deposit + may recover up to 2x as
  damages** (RCW 59.18.280(1))

## Warranty of habitability — RCW 59.18.060

Landlord must:

- Maintain premises fit for human habitation
- Maintain common areas
- Provide reasonable security
- Heat / hot water / electricity functioning
- Comply with applicable building codes

**Tenant remedies for breach** under RCW 59.18.100:

- Notice + reasonable time to repair
- After reasonable time: repair-and-deduct (up to 2
  months' rent annually); diminution-of-rent action;
  rent into court registry; rescission

## Retaliation — RCW 59.18.240

Landlord may not terminate tenancy / increase rent /
decrease services in retaliation for tenant's:

- Complaint to landlord or government about habitability
- Demand for repairs
- Tenant union activity
- Filing or threatening to file an enforcement action

**Presumption of retaliation** if landlord acts within
**90 days** of protected activity (RCW 59.18.240(1)).

## Manufactured / mobile-home L&T — RCW 59.20

Distinct rule set governs landlord-tenant relationships
in mobile-home parks. Key features:

- **20-day notice** to vacate for cause
- **Statutory grounds** at RCW 59.20.080 (limited)
- **Park rule changes** require notice + reasonableness
- **First right of refusal** if park sold (RCW 59.21)
