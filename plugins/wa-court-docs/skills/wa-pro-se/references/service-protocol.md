# Service Protocol for Pro Se Filers

## Why service discipline matters for pro se litigants

Opposing counsel sometimes disputes that a filing was served, or serves
you late and denies it. A disciplined service protocol protects the
record.

## The default method: email + certified mail

For every filing:

1. **Email** the PDF to opposing counsel (all attorneys and the
   paralegal of record)
2. **Mail** a paper copy by **USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt**
   or **Priority Mail Express** (both produce a tracking number)
3. **Scan and save** the certified mail receipt and the return card as
   PDFs
4. **Photograph the envelope and postmark** before the letter leaves your
   hands
5. **Record the tracking number** on your Certificate of Service

When opposing counsel has a documented history of false Certificates of
Service, this belt-and-suspenders approach is essential.

## Certificate of Service template

Attach at the end of every filing (or as a separate document):

```
                 CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I, [Name], certify under penalty of perjury under the laws of the
State of Washington that on [date] I caused a true and correct copy
of the foregoing [document name] to be served on:

   [Opposing Counsel, Firm]
   [Address]
   [Email]

   [X] By email to: [email addresses]
   [X] By USPS Certified Mail, Article No. [number]

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__, at [CITY], Washington.

                                 ______________________________
                                 [Name], pro se
```

## Memorializing oral interactions

Every phone call, meet-and-confer, or in-person interaction with
opposing counsel should be **memorialized in writing within 24 hours**:

```
Subject: Confirming our call today — [Case short title], [Case number]

[Counsel name],

Confirming our conversation earlier today at [time] regarding [subject].
My understanding of what we discussed:

1. [Point one]
2. [Point two]
3. [Next step with date]

Please correct me in writing if any of the above is incorrect.

[Name]
```

Send as soon as practical. If they don't correct the summary, it
becomes the record.

## Preserving incoming service

For every letter or filing received:

- **Photograph the envelope** with the postmark visible before opening
- **Save the envelope** in case the postmark date becomes contested
- **Note date of receipt** on a case log with method (mail / email)
- **For email** — save the full headers, not just the body

## Common service pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Email only, no receipt | Opposing counsel denies receipt | Also mail certified |
| Generic "serving via email" note | Insufficient under CRLJ 5 | List addresses, date, time |
| Serving wrong attorneys | Deemed unserved | Serve all attorneys of record |
| Missing the Certificate of Service | Filing may be stricken | Attach one to every filing |
| Tracking number not in filing | Hard to prove service later | Cite the tracking number |
| Forgetting to update service list | Miss service on new counsel | Re-confirm after any appearance |

## Escalation indicators (who to copy)

When opposing counsel has been evasive or missed deadlines, add their
supervising attorneys to the service list. Courts notice — it reads as
a documented escalation rather than an accusation.

- **Primary contact**: the attorney who has been corresponding
- **Attorney of record**: the senior/supervising attorney listed on the
  court's docket
- **Paralegal**: the paralegal whose name appears on Certificates of
  Service (for cross-checking dates)

## When service is disputed

If opposing counsel claims they didn't receive a filing:

1. **Respond with evidence**: USPS tracking printout, email with full
   headers, certified mail article number
2. **File a supplemental Declaration of Service** with the evidence
   attached as exhibits
3. **Do not rely on your word alone** — always have the paper trail
4. **If the court orders a hearing on service**: bring the original
   USPS receipts and certified mail return card to the hearing
