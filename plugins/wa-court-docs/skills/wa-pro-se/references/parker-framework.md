# Brian Parker / KillDebt.com Drafting Framework

A concise drafting framework for pro se litigants in consumer debt and
general civil matters. Named for attorney Brian Parker, whose free pro se
materials (killdebt.com) promote a disciplined, fact-forward style.

## The four principles

### 1. Front-load the strongest fact

The judge should know the single best fact in the case within the first
paragraph of the motion — ideally the first sentence. Do not lead with
procedural history.

**Bad:**
> This matter comes before the Court on Defendant's motion to compel
> discovery responses served on March 27, 2026, following a course of
> meet-and-confer conferences beginning on March 31, 2026 and continuing
> through April 15, 2026...

**Good:**
> Plaintiff has failed to produce the chain-of-title documents required
> by RCW 19.16.260. RFA No. 18 has been deemed admitted under CRLJ 36(a).
> On that basis alone, Plaintiff cannot establish standing.

### 2. Keep it concise

Target 4–6 pages for a motion + memorandum. Every sentence should earn
its place. If a sentence could be cut without harming the argument, cut
it.

- **Bold lead-ins** at the start of paragraphs help judges skim
- **Short paragraphs** (3–5 sentences) scan better than walls of text
- **One idea per paragraph**

If the factual record is long, move narrative to a supporting
declaration and leave the motion lean.

### 3. Write to the judge

Not to opposing counsel. Not to the internet. The only audience is the
judge who has 10 minutes to read the motion before the hearing.

- **Avoid ad hominem.** Even when opposing counsel has misbehaved, state
  the facts and let the judge characterize.
- **Avoid sarcasm.** It reads as unseriousness.
- **Avoid legal jargon you don't fully control.** Plain English, cited
  to rule and statute, is stronger than elevated vocabulary.
- **Use active voice.** "Plaintiff produced no documents" is stronger
  than "No documents were produced by Plaintiff."

### 4. Let the record speak

The record is your best advocate. If the point can be made by attaching
an exhibit and citing to it, do that.

- **Attach everything you cite.** Judges will not take your word.
- **Index exhibits** — exhibit letters, short captions, page references
- **Quote selectively** — short, devastating quotes from the other side
  are more powerful than paraphrases

## Structure of a Parker-style motion

1. **Introduction** (1 paragraph) — the strongest fact and the relief
   requested
2. **Statement of facts** (1 page max) — chronological, cited to
   exhibits
3. **Argument** (2–3 pages) — numbered points, each with a bold
   lead-in, each tied to a rule or statute
4. **Conclusion / Relief requested** (1 paragraph) — exactly what the
   motion asks the court to do

## Drafting checklist

- [ ] Does the first paragraph state the single best fact?
- [ ] Is every factual assertion tied to an exhibit or sworn statement?
- [ ] Is every legal assertion tied to a rule or case citation?
- [ ] Is the tone neutral and professional throughout?
- [ ] Is the relief requested spelled out precisely?
- [ ] Is the motion ≤ 6 pages?
- [ ] Are bold lead-ins used to help skimming?

## Common mistakes in pro se motions

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Emotional language | Rewrite as neutral facts; let judge characterize |
| Over-explaining rules | Cite the rule; trust the judge to know it |
| Hiding the ball | Lead with the best fact, not the oldest fact |
| Lists of grievances | Limit to the 2–3 best; drop the rest |
| Attacking counsel personally | Describe conduct with dates; let the judge draw conclusions |
| "As the Court is well aware..." | Never assume what the judge remembers; restate briefly |

## Example opening paragraph (for a motion to compel)

> Plaintiff served verified discovery responses on March 27, 2026 that
> omit the single document this case turns on: any assignment connecting
> Cross River Bank to Upgrade, Inc. or to Plaintiff. The promissory note
> is marked "NON-NEGOTIABLE" and signed only by Upgrade as attorney-in-
> fact. Without the chain-of-title documents required by RCW 19.16.260,
> Plaintiff cannot establish standing. Defendant moves under CR 37(a)
> for an order compelling production and for expense-shifting.

Every element is there: the strongest fact (missing assignment), why it
matters (standing), the legal basis for relief (CR 37(a)), and the
relief (compel + fees). Five sentences.
