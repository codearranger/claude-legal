## Part One — Before you file

- Consider resolving the dispute outside court: ask the other side to fix the problem (many
  agencies have mandatory pre-suit procedures), seek help from local/state agencies, contact
  an attorney. Filing pro se means the court treats you the same as an attorney: clear and
  concise issues, every deadline met, every rule followed.
- **Wrong-court screen:** divorce, child custody, adoption, name change, landlord-tenant,
  wills → state court (`www.courts.wa.gov`). Federal law / Social Security or federal-benefit
  denials / federal-agency decisions → likely correct here.
- **Jurisdiction types:** (1) United States is a party (SSA, VA, IRS, USPS); (2) federal
  question (Constitution + federal statutes); (3) diversity — parties in different states AND
  more than $75,000 claimed.
- **Statute of limitations:** the clock usually starts at injury/violation; untimely claims
  get dismissed. Filing in the wrong court can forfeit the deadline.
- **FRCP 11(b):** frivolous/harassing suits risk sanctions including the other side's fees.
  Losing parties commonly pay the winner's costs (deposition transcripts, witness fees,
  copies) — these can reach thousands of dollars.

## Part Two — Filing the case

**Required:** (1) Complaint, (2) Civil Cover Sheet (JS-44, required by LCR 3(a)),
(3) $405.00 filing fee **or** IFP application.

**Complaint mechanics:** pick the correct pro se template (catalog in
`wawd-forms-and-resources.md`); typed or neatly written in blue/black ink, white 8½×11,
one side only; name, address, phone on each document; dated + original signature
(FRCP 11(a)); state in the body what happened, where, when, how, who; exhibits allowed.

**LCR 5.2(a) redaction (mandatory before filing):** dates of birth → year only; names of
minors → initials; Social Security numbers → remove entirely; financial account numbers →
last four digits; taxpayer IDs / passport numbers / driver-license numbers → remove
entirely.

**Civil Cover Sheet walk-through:** § I parties (pro se filer lists self under "Attorneys");
§ II basis of jurisdiction (U.S. defendant / federal question / diversity); § III citizenship
(diversity only); § IV nature of suit (pick the single closest box); § V origin (1 = new
case; 2 = removal); § VI cause of action (brief statement); § VII jury demand (LCR 38(b));
§ VIII related cases (also file a Notice of Related Cases — no general court form; create
your own); sign under "Signature of Attorney of Record."

**Summons:** court's form; issued immediately if the fee is paid, held until IFP is granted
otherwise; clerk can only issue for defendants named in the complaint; proof of service is
page 2 of the form. A **praecipe** asks the clerk to issue summons later.

**IFP:** complete the application fully — the judge decides ability to pay on it; the order
is mailed to the address on the complaint; summons wait for the grant.

**Court-appointed counsel:** no right to counsel in civil cases. Application forms exist for
civil-rights and employment-discrimination cases (create your own for other claims). Factors:
ability to pay, efforts to hire counsel, ability to self-present, complexity.

**Where to file a new case:** clerk's window 9am–4pm M–F; lobby drop box after hours; mail
to the proper courthouse; or email — **new cases only** —
`newcases.seattle@wawd.uscourts.gov` / `newcases.tacoma@wawd.uscourts.gov`. After the judge
is assigned: in person, mail, or CM/ECF only.

**Payment** (per the current Paying Fees page): credit card (miscellaneous fees), credit
card/ACH through CM/ECF for registered users, cashier's check, money order, personal check
($53 NSF fee). **No cash.** Per the Rev. Jul-24 guide the clerk's window takes
Visa/MasterCard/AmEx/Discover, personal/cashier's checks, and money orders.

**Clerk's office can:** answer general how-the-court-works questions, supply forms, explain
policies. **Cannot:** give legal advice, say whether/what to file, relay messages to the
judge, predict rulings on IFP or motions, interpret orders or rules.

## Part Three — CM/ECF (e-filing)

- Optional; paper always works. Prerequisites: case open, fee paid or IFP granted, party to
  the case.
- Technical requirements per the guide: computer + internet, supported browser (the guide
  lists Internet Explorer/Firefox as supported — check current guidance), PDF conversion
  software or 200-dpi scanner, Adobe Reader, an email account.
- Two registration tiers: **E-Service only** (receive by email, still file on paper) or
  **E-Filer + E-Service** (file and receive electronically; waives paper service entirely —
  no paper copies mailed).
- Register: per-case **ECF Pro Se Registration Form** (one per case; case number required) +
  PACER "Case Search Only" account + "Non-Attorney Filers" access at pacer.gov.
- **NEF "free look":** one free view per document via the emailed link; expires 14 days;
  after that PACER charges (~$0.10/page). Only PDFs can be e-filed.
- ECF support: (206) 370-8440 option 2 · `cmecf@wawd.uscourts.gov` · M–F 8–5.

## Part Four — Service (FRCP 4)

- Serve each defendant within **90 days** of filing (FRCP 4(m)) or risk dismissal; the court
  does not serve for you absent an order (U.S. Marshal service possible under FRCP 4(c)(3)).
- Server: any person 18+ who is not a party. **You may not serve your own documents.**
- **Waiver** (FRCP 4(d)): send complaint + Notice of Lawsuit and Request for Waiver (AO 398)
  + Waiver of Summons (AO 399); waiving defendant gets 60 days to respond; the United States
  cannot waive.
- **Serving the United States (FRCP 4(i)):** registered/certified mail to the civil-process
  clerk, U.S. Attorney's office for the district (or deliver to the U.S. Attorney), AND
  registered/certified mail to the U.S. Attorney General in Washington, D.C.; if challenging
  a nonparty officer/agency order, also serve that officer/agency. Federal
  officer/agency/corporation defendants: both steps above plus registered/certified mail to
  the named officer/agency.
- File the original **proof of service** for each defendant (FRCP 4(l)).

## Part Five — After filing

- Random case-number + judge assignment by county of the events/defendants; no judge
  requests. Possible magistrate-judge referral; magistrates can preside including trial
  under FRCP 73(a). Case number goes on every filing.
- No answer → default + default judgment under FRCP 55(a)–(b). Answer/motion → pretrial.
- **Discovery:** opens after the FRCP 26(f) planning conference (FRCP 26(d)); litigation-hold
  / preservation letters may go earlier. Tools: depositions (FRCP 30 — requester pays the
  court reporter), interrogatories (FRCP 33), requests for production (FRCP 34), requests
  for admission (FRCP 36). Discovery is exchanged, not filed.
- **Dispositive motions:** FRCP 12 and FRCP 56.
- **Trial:** bench vs. jury; courtroom rules (stand when the judge enters, "Your Honor," no
  recording devices, phones off); sequence — opening statements, plaintiff's evidence, rest,
  defense + rebuttal, closing argument, jury instructions, verdict/finding.
- **Judgment:** clerk prepares per the court's order; a losing plaintiff can be ordered to
  pay defense costs and fees.
- **Appeal:** to the **Ninth Circuit** (`www.ca9.uscourts.gov` · (415) 355-8000); deadlines
  in **FRAP 4**; file a Notice of Civil Appeal in this court. Fee per the current Fee
  Schedule: **$605 (includes filing + docketing)** — the Rev. Jul-24 guide's $505 figure is
  outdated. District-court IFP status carries forward subject to Ninth Circuit review;
  otherwise an IFP may be filed with the appeal.

## Part Six — Filing documents in an open case

- **Format:** 8½×11 white paper, **25 line numbers in the left margin**, caption + case
  number, typed or neatly written (blue/black ink, one side), dated + original signature,
  name/address/phone on every document. Serve every filing on all parties (FRCP 5, LCR 5).
  Check the assigned judge's chambers procedures (`wawd.uscourts.gov/judges`).
- **Noting dates (LCR 7):** every motion states on its face, under the title, the date it is
  ready for decision.

| Category | Earliest noting date | Response due | Reply due |
|---|---|---|---|
| Same-day: stipulations/agreed motions (LCR 10(g)); over-length briefs (LCR 7(f)); reconsideration (LCR 7(h)); joint LCR 37 submissions (LCR 37(a)(2)); appoint mediator (LCR 39.1(c)(3)); default (LCR 55(a)); default judgment vs. non-appearing party (LCR 55(b)(1)); ex parte (LCR 55(b)(2)); TRO (LCR 65) | day of filing | none unless ordered | none unless ordered |
| 14-day: relief from a deadline (LCR 7(d)); protective orders (LCR 7(d)); motions to seal (LCR 5(g)) | 14 calendar days after filing | 9 days after filing | by the noting date |
| 21-day: other non-dispositive motions (amend, remand, compel discovery) | 21 calendar days after filing | 15 days after filing | by the noting date |
| 28-day: dispositive — dismiss, preliminary injunction, class certification, remand/transfer/compel arbitration (LCR 7(d)(3)) | 28 calendar days after filing | 21 days after filing | by the noting date |
| Motions in limine (LCR 7(d)(4)) | ≥21 days after filing AND ≤6 days before the pretrial conference | Monday before the noting date | none unless ordered |

- **Sealed documents (LCR 5(g)):** motion to seal + proposed order + supporting
  declaration/affidavit; sealed material **cannot be e-filed** — sealed envelope marked
  "FILED UNDER SEAL" with caption + case number, delivered in person (LCR 5(g)(3), (9));
  remains sealed until further order if granted (LCR 5(g)(7)); unsealed if denied, with a
  withdraw-instead option (LCR 5(g)(6)); once sealed, even the filer needs a granted motion
  to unseal to access it (LCR 5(g)(8)).
- **Oral argument:** write "Oral Argument Requested" under the case number and title (LCR 7).
- **Contact-information changes:** written notice within **10 days** (LCR 10(f)).
