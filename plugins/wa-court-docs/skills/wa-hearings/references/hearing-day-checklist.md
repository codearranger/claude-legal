# Hearing Day Checklist

A structured checklist for the day of the hearing. Customize for
your own case.

## Two days before

- [ ] **Re-read the briefs** — yours and opposing's
- [ ] **Update your one-page outline** with any last points
- [ ] **Prepare the Q&A sheet** — list likely judge questions
      with one-sentence answers
- [ ] **Review the proposed order** — make sure the relief is
      precisely what you are asking for
- [ ] **Confirm the hearing time and location** — check the
      court's online docket for any changes
- [ ] **For Zoom**: test the link, camera, microphone
- [ ] **For in-person**: plan the route, parking, security line
      time

## One day before

- [ ] **Print** (or finalize digital copies of):
  - [ ] Motion and supporting brief
  - [ ] Response / reply
  - [ ] Supporting declarations and exhibits
  - [ ] Proposed order
  - [ ] Your one-page outline
  - [ ] Your Q&A sheet
  - [ ] Meet-and-confer correspondence (if relevant)
- [ ] **Organize the binder** (in-person) with tabs:
  - Tab 1: Outline
  - Tab 2: Motion
  - Tab 3: Brief
  - Tab 4: Opposition
  - Tab 5: Reply
  - Tab 6: Exhibits (one sub-tab each)
  - Tab 7: Cases (printed key authorities with key passages
    highlighted)
  - Tab 8: Proposed Order
- [ ] **Organize the desktop folder** (Zoom):
  - Subfolder "briefs" with all pleadings
  - Subfolder "exhibits" with each exhibit as a separate file
  - Subfolder "cases" with PDFs of the 3 to 5 key authorities
  - One "OUTLINE.pdf" at the top level, pre-opened
- [ ] **Confirm witnesses** (if any are to appear)
- [ ] **Lay out clothes** — suit, shirt, tie, shoes polished
- [ ] **Charge all devices** — laptop, phone, backup device
- [ ] **Set two alarms** for the morning

## Morning of

### 2 hours before

- [ ] **Eat a light breakfast** — not heavy, not caffeinated past
      your usual
- [ ] **Shower and dress**
- [ ] **Final review**:
  - [ ] Read the outline aloud once, timed
  - [ ] Review the Q&A sheet
  - [ ] Review the top 2 or 3 authorities
- [ ] **Restroom, water, clean mouth**

### 1 hour before

**In-person:**

- [ ] **Depart** — build in 30 minutes buffer for traffic,
      parking, security
- [ ] **Bring:**
  - [ ] Binder
  - [ ] Extra copies of the proposed order (3+) for the court
        and opposing counsel
  - [ ] Pen and notepad
  - [ ] Reading glasses (if needed)
  - [ ] Quiet watch (not one that beeps)
  - [ ] Phone (off or silent)
  - [ ] Water bottle
  - [ ] Photo ID
  - [ ] Parking money / pass

**Zoom:**

- [ ] **Final tech check**: camera, mic, internet, Zoom version
- [ ] **Close every other app** on the computer
- [ ] **Set a Do Not Disturb** on the computer and phone
- [ ] **Brief your household** — do not enter, do not disturb,
      for the next 2 hours
- [ ] **Open the hearing link** in a fresh browser window
- [ ] **Open your outline** on a second device or printed
- [ ] **Have the backup call-in number ready** on phone

### 30 minutes before

**In-person:**

- [ ] **Arrive at the courthouse**
- [ ] **Clear security** — bring only the binder, pen, ID
- [ ] **Locate the courtroom**
- [ ] **Check in with the clerk** — identify yourself and your
      case
- [ ] **Sit quietly in the gallery** until your case is called
- [ ] **Observe the courtroom's rhythm** — how the judge is
      ruling, time allowed per side

**Zoom:**

- [ ] **Log in to the Zoom waiting room**
- [ ] **Display name** set correctly: "[Last], [First] — Pro Se
      Defendant"
- [ ] **Camera on, mic muted**
- [ ] **Silent household confirmed**

### 15 minutes before

- [ ] **Review your outline one last time**
- [ ] **Take 10 slow breaths**
- [ ] **Stand up and stretch** (at home or in the gallery)
- [ ] **Visualize the opening** — the exact first sentence

### Case called

**In-person:**

- [ ] Walk briskly to counsel table
- [ ] Set binder flat on table
- [ ] Stand to identify yourself when the judge addresses you

**Zoom:**

- [ ] Unmute when recognized
- [ ] State appearance
- [ ] Re-mute

## During the hearing

- [ ] **Listen actively** to opposing counsel — note factual
      errors to correct in rebuttal
- [ ] **Answer judge's questions first, explain after**
- [ ] **Watch the clock** — do not exceed your allotted time
      unless invited
- [ ] **Take notes on rulings from the bench** — every specific
      word the judge says
- [ ] **Confirm the ruling** before leaving — "Your Honor, so
      the Court's order is [X]?"

## After the hearing

### Immediately

- [ ] **Thank the judge**
- [ ] **Hand up the proposed order** if requested
- [ ] **Collect all materials**
- [ ] **Exit quietly**

### Within 24 hours

- [ ] **Write down everything the judge said** — the ruling,
      the reasoning, any directives for next steps
- [ ] **Update the case calendar** with new deadlines
- [ ] **Prepare the final order** if the judge asked for a
      revised version — submit it to the court per the judge's
      preferred method

### Within 10 days (if you lost)

- [ ] **Consider reconsideration** under CR 59 — new evidence,
      manifest error, or clear abuse of discretion; strict
      10-day deadline
- [ ] **Consider appeal** — applicable timelines (30 days for
      superior court appeal to Court of Appeals)
- [ ] **Decide whether to accept and move on** — most orders
      are not worth the cost of reconsideration

### Within 5 days (if the judge wants findings / order)

- [ ] **Draft findings of fact and conclusions of law** (if
      requested) following the judge's oral ruling
- [ ] **Circulate to opposing counsel** — some courts require
      circulation before submission
- [ ] **Submit via the court's preferred method** — in-person,
      electronic upload, or email to the judge's assistant

## What to bring — checklist summary

**In-person hearing:**

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Binder with all filings | Primary reference |
| Outline — one page | Speaking notes |
| Q&A sheet | Anticipated questions |
| Proposed Order — 3 copies | For judge, opposing, and your file |
| Pen and notepad | Notes on rulings |
| Photo ID | Courthouse security |
| Phone (off) | Emergency only |
| Water bottle | Quiet sips only |
| Watch | Time management |

**Zoom hearing:**

| Item | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Laptop (charged, wired) | Zoom |
| Backup phone with call-in | Audio backup |
| Second screen or printout | Outline + briefs |
| Headset mic | Audio quality |
| Notepad + pen | Notes |
| Glass of water | Out of camera view |
| Closed-door, quiet room | No interruption |

## Do not bring

- Emotional support objects that draw attention (teddy bears,
  lucky charms — visible items only)
- Large beverages — spill risk, distraction
- Food
- Reading material unrelated to the case
- Friends or family in the courtroom well (they can sit in the
  gallery but cannot speak or assist during argument)
- Open laptop with anything but case materials (in-person)
- A phone that might ring

## Mental preparation

The morning of:

- **You know the facts** — you lived them
- **You know the law** — you wrote the brief
- **The judge has read the papers** — you are there to answer
  questions, not recite
- **Be calm, be short, be specific** — the three rules

One last reminder: **the goal is not to be brilliant, the goal
is to be clear.** Clarity wins motions. Save brilliance for
later.
