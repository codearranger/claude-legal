# Civil Motion Docket — KCDC

Applies to all three divisions: **East (Redmond)**, **South (Burien)**,
and **West (Seattle)**. Each division publishes its own civil motions
calendar and uses its own Note for Motion Docket form, but the
scheduling protocol below is common across divisions.

## Scheduling a motion (3-step protocol)

1. **Email `KCDC.CivilMGT@kingcounty.gov` to request a date.** After
   reviewing the target division's published civil motions calendar,
   email the calendar clerks with:

   - Case number(s)
   - Type of hearing requested
   - Requested hearing date and time

   Offering 1–2 alternative dates in the same email reduces back-and-
   forth. The CivilMGT inbox is centralized (Seattle Court) even for
   Burien or Redmond matters. Do **not** attach the Note to this
   email — the Note is filed after the date is issued.

2. **Wait for the clerk's confirmation.** The clerks respond by email,
   either confirming the requested date and time or proposing an
   alternative. The confirmation identifies the reserved courtroom
   and sets a deadline — normally **2 business days** from the
   confirmation — by which the requesting party must e-file the
   required documents. Do not pre-fill a courtroom code on the Note
   for Motion Docket before the clerk has issued it.

3. **E-file the packet through the portal by the clerk's deadline.**
   Upload the Note for Motion Docket, Motion, supporting
   declaration(s), exhibits, and Proposed Order through the King
   County District Court e-Filing / Case Access portal. Serve
   opposing counsel the same day per CRLJ 5 (email, certified mail,
   or personal service) and attach a Certificate of Service to the
   filing. **Failure to timely e-file results in the hearing being
   cancelled and the date released.**

> ⚠ **Motions noted for a date that was NOT issued by the clerks will
> be rejected.** Do not self-select a date and e-file without a
> clerk-issued confirmation.

## Timing rules (CRLJ 6)

- Motions must be filed and served at least **7 days** before the hearing
  (CRLJ 6(d)(1)). For some motions, 14 days.
- Opposition must be filed and served at least **3 days** before the
  hearing (CRLJ 6(d)(2)).
- Reply must be filed and served no later than **noon the day before**
  the hearing.

Check the current rule text — these are commonly misremembered. The
safer course is to give more notice than the minimum.

## Note for Motion Docket — standard form

KCDC uses the standard WA note form. Required fields:

- Court and case caption
- Motion being noted (exact title)
- Requested hearing date
- Time estimate
- Whether oral argument is requested
- Movant's signature, address, email
- Certificate of service (included or separate)

See `../../skills/wa-statewide-format/references/templates/note-for-motion-docket.md`
for a complete template.

## Date-request email template

This email goes to the clerks **before** any filing. It does not need
the Note attached — the Note is filed through the portal after the
clerk issues the date.

```
To:      KCDC.CivilMGT@kingcounty.gov
Subject: Hearing Date Request — [Case Short Title],
         Case No. [Cause Number]

Dear Clerks:

I am the [pro se defendant / plaintiff / counsel] in [case short
title], Case No. [cause number], assigned to Judge [name] in the
[Division, e.g., South Division, Burien Courthouse].

I am requesting to reserve [preferred date] at [preferred time] for
a hearing on [exact motion title, e.g., Defendant's Motion to Compel
Discovery under CR 37(a) (applicable through CRLJ 26(f))]. If that
date is not available, I would alternatively request
[alt 1, alt 2, alt 3].

Thank you,
[Name][, Pro Se Defendant]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

CC'ing opposing counsel at the date-request stage is **optional**.
Some practitioners do it for transparency; others wait until the date
is issued and include counsel on the e-filing service record.

## What happens after the clerk issues the date

1. Clerk emails a confirmation identifying the reserved date, time,
   courtroom, and e-filing deadline (normally 2 business days from
   the confirmation)
2. File the Note and packet through the portal by the clerk's deadline
3. Serve opposing counsel the same day per CRLJ 5; include a
   Certificate of Service
4. The Zoom meeting ID, dial-in numbers, and phone controls for the
   courtroom are printed on the division's current Note for Motion
   Docket form (pulled from the KCDC civil filings page). Chambers
   does not issue a separate Zoom link; use the form's connection
   details.
5. Opposing counsel may file a response; the moving party may file a
   reply (CRLJ does not fix a reply deadline — practice varies by
   judge)
6. Appear in person or by Zoom; the judge rules from the bench or
   takes the matter under advisement
7. The prevailing party submits the Proposed Order for signature
8. The signed Order is filed by the clerk and served on all parties

## Confirmations, continuances, and strikes

- **Confirmations**: the moving party is **not** required to confirm
  that a scheduled matter is proceeding to hearing (KCDC does not
  use a pre-hearing check-in).
- **Continuances**: file an **Agreed Motion to Continue and Proposed
  Order ex parte** through the e-Filing portal before the scheduled
  hearing date. KCDC cannot continue cases administratively. If
  opposing counsel won't stipulate, file a noted Motion to Continue
  instead.
- **Strikes**: the moving party must advise the court of a strike at
  least **1 business day** before the scheduled hearing, and must
  properly serve and notify the other party or parties of the strike.
