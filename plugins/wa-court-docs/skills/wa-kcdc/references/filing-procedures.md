# Filing Procedures — KCDC

## E-filing

KCDC accepts e-filing through the King County Clerk's Office portal. In
general:

- **Civil filings** are e-filed; most documents are accepted as PDF
- **Signed orders** may require an original paper filing in some cases —
  confirm with chambers for longer or complex orders
- **Exhibits** travel with the declaration they support; do not file
  exhibits separately
- **File size**: Most portals cap at 25MB per document; split oversized
  PDFs (OCR'd exhibits especially)

## Service

Under CRLJ 5, service on opposing counsel can be by:

- **Email** (if the parties have agreed, or after initial appearance)
- **Personal delivery**
- **U.S. Mail** or commercial delivery service (adds 3 days to deadlines)
- **Fax** (rare; confirm opposing counsel still accepts)

**For pro se filers with opposing counsel who has a documented history
of disputing service**, use both email and certified mail with return
receipt, and include a Certificate of Service showing both methods.

## Working copies

Unlike King County Superior Court, KCDC civil motions generally do not
require paper working copies to be delivered to chambers. But:

- Confirm with the specific judge's chambers for longer or complex
  motions
- Pro se filers are sometimes asked to bring extra copies to the hearing
- If the motion relies on voluminous exhibits, offer a paper working
  copy out of courtesy

## Certificate of service

Every filing should include a Certificate (or Declaration) of Service:

```
                    CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I certify under penalty of perjury under the laws of the State of
Washington that on [date] I caused a true and correct copy of the
foregoing [Motion/Declaration/Order] to be served on the following:

   [Opposing counsel name, firm]
   [Address]
   [Email]

   [X]  By email to: [email addresses]
   [X]  By U.S. Mail, Certified Mail No. [tracking number]

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.

                                 ______________________________
                                 [Name], pro se
```

## Preserving service evidence

- **Photograph postmarked envelopes** before opening incoming mail
- **Keep USPS certified mail receipts and green return cards** — scan
  both sides
- **Save email metadata**: full headers, timestamps, attachment lists
- **For certified mail**: record the Article Number on your Certificate
  of Service and attach the scanned receipt

## Common filing errors to avoid

| Error | Fix |
|-------|-----|
| Missing case caption | Copy caption exactly from a prior filing |
| Wrong case number | Double-check — WA format is YY-CIV-####-KCX |
| Missing Certificate of Service | Attach one to every filing |
| Noting a motion for a self-picked date | Email `KCDC.CivilMGT@kingcounty.gov` first to get a date issued; motions noted for a date NOT issued by the clerks will be rejected |
| Missing Note for Motion Docket | File the division-specific Note (Burien / Redmond / Seattle form) with the packet — it is the scheduling document |
| Exhibits not labeled | Each exhibit gets a cover page labeled EXHIBIT A/B/C |
| Colored text in body | Violates GR 14 — use bold/italic for emphasis |
| PDF too large | Split or re-OCR at lower resolution |
