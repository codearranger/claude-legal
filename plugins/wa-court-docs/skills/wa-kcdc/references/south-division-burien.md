## Courtrooms

The clerk's scheduling confirmation email identifies the specific
courtroom reserved for the hearing. Room assignments vary — do not
pre-fill a courtroom code into the Note for Motion Docket before
the clerk has issued the date.

## Civil motion docket

- Heard on the South Division's civil motion calendar; hearing days
  and connection details are published on the division's rolling
  motions calendar and current Note for Motion Docket form — pull
  both from the civil filings page linked above every time
- **Scheduling**: a date is **not** self-selected from the calendar.
  The moving party must email `KCDC.CivilMGT@kingcounty.gov` with
  case number, type of hearing, and the requested date/time (with 1–2
  alternatives). The clerk replies with a reserved date, courtroom,
  and e-filing deadline (normally 2 business days)
- Time estimate: note an estimate on the Note for Motion Docket; most
  civil motions are allotted 10–15 minutes
- Remote appearance: Zoom videoconference is accepted for most civil
  motions; the Zoom link, meeting ID, and phone controls are printed
  on the current Note for Motion Docket form, not issued separately
  by chambers
- Oral argument: expect to argue briefly; the judge will often ask
  clarifying questions and rule from the bench

## Civil jurisdiction

KCDC is a court of limited jurisdiction. Civil cases include:
- Contract and tort claims up to $100,000
- Small claims (separate track)
- Landlord/tenant (unlawful detainer)
- Anti-harassment protection orders
- Name changes
