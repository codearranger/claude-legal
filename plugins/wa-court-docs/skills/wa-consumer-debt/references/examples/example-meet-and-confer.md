# Example: CRLJ 26(f) Meet-and-Confer Letter

> **SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE.** Illustrates a professional, specific
> meet-and-confer letter that creates a paper trail for a later
> motion to compel. Keep this letter-tone, not motion-tone.

---

```
February 25, 2026


Attorney for Plaintiff
Law Firm Name
Address
City, State ZIP
email@example.com

    Re:  Acme Portfolio Recovery, LLC v. Jane Q. Public
         King County District Court, South Division
         Cause No. 25CIV200000KCX
         CRLJ 26(f) Meet-and-Confer — Deficient Discovery
         Responses


Dear Counsel:

    I write to confer under CRLJ 26(f) regarding Plaintiff's
February 17, 2026 responses to my First Requests for Production
and First Interrogatories. I identify the following deficiencies
and request supplementation within 10 business days.

1.  **RFP 1 — Bill of sale schedule identifying Defendant's
    account.**
    Response: "Overly broad; unduly burdensome; confidential."
    Deficiency: the bill of sale schedule goes to the threshold
    question of Plaintiff's standing. It is not overly broad — it
    asks for a specific document. A confidentiality objection is
    unsupported by any privilege log. I request the unredacted
    schedule showing Defendant's account.

2.  **RFP 3 — Intermediate assignment documents.**
    Response: "Plaintiff has produced the Certificate of
    Indebtedness; no further response required."
    Deficiency: the Certificate of Indebtedness does not identify
    any intermediate assignments. The chain of title from the
    Originator Bank to Plaintiff requires each intermediate
    assignment. I request production of all such documents.

3.  **RFP 5 — Original cardholder agreement.**
    Response: "Plaintiff will produce a representative specimen."
    Deficiency: a specimen agreement is not the contract
    Defendant allegedly signed. Under ER 1002, the original is
    required; under RCW 4.16.040, the written-contract theory
    requires the actual written contract. I request the specific
    cardholder agreement in force at the time Defendant's account
    was opened.

4.  **RFP 7 — Monthly account statements.**
    Response: "Not in Plaintiff's possession."
    Deficiency: if Plaintiff does not possess the statements, it
    cannot prove the amount owed. Please confirm in writing that
    no monthly statements exist in Plaintiff's or its agents'
    possession, custody, or control, and identify the efforts
    Plaintiff has made to obtain them from the Originator Bank.

5.  **RFP 9 — Documents showing the basis for the claimed balance.**
    Response: (none)
    Deficiency: no response was provided. I request a complete
    response identifying every document on which Plaintiff bases
    the principal, interest, and fee components of its claim.

6.  **IROG 1 — Each entity that held ownership of Defendant's
    account.**
    Response: "Plaintiff is the current owner of the account."
    Deficiency: the request asks for the complete chain of
    ownership, not the current ownership. I request a complete
    response identifying each entity, the date of transfer to
    that entity, and the document evidencing the transfer.

[Continue by response number as warranted.]

    The requested supplementation is necessary to evaluate
Plaintiff's claim. If I do not receive adequate supplementation
by March 11, 2026, I intend to file a motion to compel under
CRLJ 37(a), including a request for reasonable expenses under
CRLJ 37(a)(4). I remain available to meet by phone or Zoom to
discuss any of these items.

                                Sincerely,


                                Jane Q. Public, pro se
                                1234 Main Street
                                Seattle, WA 98101
                                (206) 555-0000
                                jpublic@example.com
```
