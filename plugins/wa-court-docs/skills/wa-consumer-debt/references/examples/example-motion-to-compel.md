# Example: Motion to Compel Discovery

> **SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE.** Names and facts fictional. Illustrates
> the Parker framework, the CR 26(i) certification, and the
> mandatory fee-shifting request under CR 37.

---

```
                                  IN THE DISTRICT COURT OF THE
                                  STATE OF WASHINGTON FOR KING
                                  COUNTY, SOUTH DIVISION

                                  )
    ACME PORTFOLIO RECOVERY, LLC,  )    NO.  25CIV200000KCX
                                  )
                Plaintiff,        )    DEFENDANT'S MOTION TO
                                  )    COMPEL DISCOVERY AND FOR
          v.                      )    AN AWARD OF EXPENSES
                                  )
    JANE Q. PUBLIC,               )    Hearing:  [date] at [time]
                                  )    Judge:    [name]
                Defendant.        )    Via:      [Zoom / in person]
                                  )
    ____________________________  )

                             I.  RELIEF REQUESTED

    Defendant Jane Q. Public, pro se, moves this Court for an
    Order compelling Plaintiff to produce documents responsive to
    Defendant's First Requests for Production, Nos. 1–10, and to
    provide complete, verified answers to Defendant's First
    Interrogatories, Nos. 1–5. Defendant further moves for an
    award of the reasonable expenses incurred in bringing this
    motion under CRLJ 37(a)(4).

                              II.  FACTS

    1.  Plaintiff, a debt buyer, sued Defendant on a purchased
        credit-card account originating from [Originator Bank].

    2.  On January 15, 2026, Defendant served her First Requests
        for Production and First Interrogatories, seeking the
        documents that would prove Plaintiff's chain of title to
        Defendant's specific account. The requests are attached
        as Exhibit A to the accompanying Declaration of Jane Q.
        Public.

    3.  On February 17, 2026, Plaintiff served responses. See
        Exhibit B. The responses consist almost entirely of
        boilerplate objections — "overly broad," "unduly
        burdensome," "not proportional," "attorney-client
        privilege" — without the specificity required under CRLJ
        26(b) or any privilege log.

    4.  Plaintiff produced only a single one-page document: an
        unsigned "Certificate of Indebtedness" executed by one of
        Plaintiff's own employees, who did not work for the
        Originator Bank.

    5.  Plaintiff did not produce: the unredacted bill-of-sale
        schedule identifying Defendant's account (RFP 1); any
        intermediate assignment documents (RFP 3); the original
        cardholder agreement (RFP 5); any monthly statements (RFP
        7); or any document showing the basis for the claimed
        balance (RFP 9).

    6.  On February 25, 2026, Defendant sent a CRLJ 26(f) meet-
        and-confer letter identifying each deficiency and asking
        for supplementation. See Exhibit C.

    7.  On March 5, 2026, Plaintiff's counsel responded by letter
        stating only that "Plaintiff stands on its objections" and
        declined to supplement. See Exhibit D.

                      III.  AUTHORITY AND ARGUMENT

    A.  The CRLJ 26(f) conference requirement has been satisfied.

    A motion to compel requires a prior conference-of-counsel
    effort under CRLJ 26(f). Defendant sent a detailed
    meet-and-confer letter on February 25, 2026 (Exhibit C). On
    March 5, 2026, Plaintiff declined to supplement (Exhibit D).
    No further conference would have been fruitful.

    B.  Plaintiff's boilerplate objections are insufficient.

    Under CRLJ 26(b)(1), discovery extends to any matter
    relevant to a claim or defense and proportional to the needs
    of the case. Objections must be stated with specificity (CRLJ
    34(b)); generic "overly broad" or "burdensome" objections,
    unsupported by a particular showing, do not preserve
    non-production. Plaintiff's objections are boilerplate; they
    identify no specific burden, no privileged document, and no
    disproportionality.

    C.  The requested documents go to the elements Plaintiff must
        prove.

    Plaintiff's claim depends on proving: (1) that it owns
    Defendant's specific account; (2) the written contract
    underlying the account; and (3) the amount owed. Each
    requested document targets one of these elements:

        •  RFP 1 (bill-of-sale schedule) goes to ownership. Under
           RCW 62A.9A-108, the assignment description must
           "reasonably identify" the specific account; without
           the schedule, the assignment fails.

        •  RFP 3 (intermediate assignments) completes the chain.

        •  RFP 5 (original cardholder agreement) proves the
           contract claim under RCW 4.16.040; the "sample"
           agreement Plaintiff has proffered cannot be
           authenticated as Defendant's agreement without
           satisfying ER 901 and 1002.

        •  RFP 7 (monthly statements) proves the account history
           and the amount claimed; without them, no foundation
           exists for the balance.

    D.  Expenses are mandatory under CRLJ 37(a)(4).

    If a motion to compel is granted, the court "shall" award the
    moving party reasonable expenses, including attorney fees,
    unless one of the enumerated exceptions applies. None applies
    here: the meet-and-confer certification (Exhibit C) shows good-
    faith effort; Plaintiff's refusal to supplement was not
    substantially justified; and no circumstances make an award
    unjust. Defendant itemizes her actual expenses — filing fees,
    postage, and copy costs — in the accompanying Declaration.

                            IV.  CONCLUSION

    Plaintiff cannot plead a debt on which it has produced no
    chain of ownership, no underlying contract, and no account
    history. The Court should compel Plaintiff to produce the
    requested documents within 21 days and to answer the
    interrogatories under verification within the same period.
    The Court should also award Defendant the reasonable expenses
    of this motion under CRLJ 37(a)(4).
```
