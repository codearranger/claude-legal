# Example: Answer, Affirmative Defenses, and Counterclaim

> **SYNTHETIC EXAMPLE.** Names and facts are fictional. Format and
> structure illustrate how this plugin assembles an answer packet
> for a debt-buyer case in KCDC.

                              I.  ANSWER

    Defendant Jane Q. Public ("Defendant"), appearing pro se,
    answers the Complaint of Acme Portfolio Recovery, LLC
    ("Plaintiff") as follows:

    1.  Paragraph 1 of the Complaint states legal conclusions to
        which no response is required; to the extent a response is
        required, the allegations are denied.

    2.  Defendant admits only that she resides in King County,
        Washington. The remaining allegations of paragraph 2 are
        denied.

    3.  Defendant lacks knowledge or information sufficient to
        form a belief as to the truth of the allegations of
        paragraph 3 and on that basis denies them.

    4.  Denied.

    5.  Denied. Defendant specifically denies that Plaintiff is the
        owner of any account belonging to Defendant, and denies that
        any chain of title has been established from any original
        creditor to Plaintiff.

    6.  Denied. Defendant specifically denies owing any sum to
        Plaintiff.

    7.  Denied.

    [Continue paragraph-by-paragraph, admitting only what is
    documentarily true and denying the rest.]

                         II.  AFFIRMATIVE DEFENSES

    Defendant asserts the following affirmative defenses, each
    pleaded separately and without waiver of any other:

    A.  **Statute of limitations.** Plaintiff's claim is barred in
        whole or in part by RCW 4.16.040 (6 years, written
        contracts) or, in the alternative, RCW 4.16.080(3) (3
        years, oral / account stated).

    B.  **Lack of standing.** Plaintiff is not the real party in
        interest under CR 17(a) / CRLJ 17(a). Plaintiff has not
        established an unbroken chain of assignment from the
        original creditor to Plaintiff with respect to Defendant's
        specific account.

    C.  **Lack of capacity to sue.** Upon information and belief,
        Plaintiff is not licensed as a collection agency in
        Washington under RCW 19.16.110 and lacks capacity to
        bring this action.

    D.  **Failure to state a claim.** The Complaint fails to state
        a claim upon which relief can be granted under CRLJ
        12(b)(6).

    E.  **Failure of consideration.** The asserted transfer of
        Defendant's account fails the attachment requirements of
        RCW 62A.9A-203 and the description sufficiency requirement
        of RCW 62A.9A-108.

    F.  **Unclean hands.** Plaintiff's violations of the Fair Debt
        Collection Practices Act and RCW 19.16 bar equitable
        relief.

    G.  **Set-off.** Any sums owed by Defendant to Plaintiff are
        offset by Plaintiff's liability to Defendant under the
        counterclaims pleaded below.

    H.  **Waiver, estoppel, and laches.**

    I.  **Payment.** To the extent any sums are shown to have been
        owed, Defendant has made payments that have not been
        credited.

    J.  **Reservation.** Defendant reserves the right to assert
        additional affirmative defenses as discovery reveals facts
        supporting them.

                           III.  COUNTERCLAIMS

    Defendant counterclaims against Plaintiff as follows:

    **Counterclaim I — Violation of the Fair Debt Collection
    Practices Act, 15 U.S.C. § 1692 et seq.**

    1.  Plaintiff is a "debt collector" within the meaning of 15
        U.S.C. § 1692a(6), in that Plaintiff regularly collects
        defaulted consumer debts purchased from originators.

    2.  The obligation Plaintiff asserts against Defendant is a
        "debt" within the meaning of 15 U.S.C. § 1692a(5).

    3.  Plaintiff violated 15 U.S.C. § 1692e(2)(A) by falsely
        representing the character, amount, or legal status of
        Defendant's alleged debt, including by claiming ownership
        of an account without proof of assignment.

    4.  Plaintiff violated 15 U.S.C. § 1692f(1) by attempting to
        collect an amount (including interest, fees, and other
        charges) not expressly authorized by the agreement
        creating the debt or permitted by law, and by attempting
        to collect on an account Plaintiff does not own.

    5.  Plaintiff violated 15 U.S.C. § 1692g by filing suit
        without providing a valid validation notice.

    6.  Defendant is entitled to actual damages, statutory
        damages up to $1,000, and reasonable attorney fees and
        costs under 15 U.S.C. § 1692k.

    **Counterclaim II — Violation of RCW 19.16.**

    7.  Plaintiff is a "collection agency" within the meaning of
        RCW 19.16.100.

    8.  Plaintiff violated RCW 19.16.250 by attempting to collect
        an amount not owing, by making false and deceptive
        representations concerning the character and ownership of
        the alleged debt, and by filing a lawsuit on a debt for
        which Plaintiff lacks documentary proof of ownership.

    9.  Upon information and belief, Plaintiff violated RCW
        19.16.110 by conducting collection activity in Washington
        without a license.

    **Counterclaim III — Violation of the Washington Consumer
    Protection Act, RCW 19.86.**

    10. Plaintiff's conduct constitutes unfair and deceptive acts
        or practices in the conduct of trade or commerce.

    11. Under RCW 19.16.440, each violation of RCW 19.16 is a per
        se violation of RCW 19.86.

    12. Plaintiff's conduct has caused Defendant injury in her
        business or property, including time spent defending this
        action, filing fees, postage and copy costs, and
        transportation expenses.

    13. Defendant is entitled to actual damages, treble damages
        up to $25,000, and reasonable attorney fees and costs
        under RCW 19.86.090.
