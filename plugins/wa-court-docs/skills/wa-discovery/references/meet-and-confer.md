# Meet and Confer — CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f)

Required before any discovery motion. The conference must be a good-
faith attempt to resolve the dispute — not a formality.

## The rule

**CR 26(i)**: "The court will not entertain any motion or objection
with respect to rules 26 through 37 unless counsel have conferred
with respect to the motion or objection."

**CRLJ 26(f)**: parallel language for district court.

Failure to confer = denial of the motion without reaching the merits.
Judges enforce this rigorously.

## What counts as a conference

Not a conference:

- A single email demanding compliance
- Voicemail left with no follow-up
- A letter titled "meet and confer" with no actual discussion

Is a conference:

- **Two-way written exchange** that addresses the specific
  deficiencies and responses, OR
- A **phone or Zoom call** where both sides discuss the issues,
  followed by documentation

Best practice: **both** — a written letter identifying the
deficiencies, followed by a phone or Zoom call (or documented
declined call).

## Step 1 — Deficiency letter

### Template

```
[Date]

Via email and U.S. Mail
[Opposing counsel name]
[Firm]
[Email]
[Address]

    Re:  Meet-and-Confer under CR 26(i) [or CRLJ 26(f)]
         [Case name], Cause No. [number]

Dear [Name]:

I write under CR 26(i) [CRLJ 26(f)] regarding Plaintiff's responses
to [Defendant]'s First [Requests for Production / Interrogatories /
Requests for Admission] served [date]. The responses dated [date]
are deficient in the following specific respects:

1.  RFP No. [N] — Request for [document]. Plaintiff responded
    "none in Plaintiff's possession." No privilege log was
    provided. Please confirm in writing that no such document is
    in the possession, custody, or control of Plaintiff or any
    agent, or produce.

2.  RFP No. [N] — Request for [document identifying the subject
    matter]. Plaintiff produced a document that does not on its
    face identify the subject matter at issue. Please produce the
    complete document including all schedules, exhibits, and
    attachments that identify it.

3.  IROG No. [N] — Identification of [fact]. Plaintiff's answer
    is non-responsive: it does not identify the dates, amounts,
    or persons requested. Please serve a full, verified response.

4.  IROG No. [N] — Calculation. Plaintiff's answer states only a
    conclusory figure. Please provide the full breakdown
    requested (underlying numbers, dates, and arithmetic).

5.  RFA No. [N] — Plaintiff neither admitted nor denied and
    stated inability to admit or deny without describing the
    inquiry undertaken. The response is insufficient under
    CR 36(a); please serve an amended response.

I propose we confer by telephone or Zoom to discuss these issues.
I am available [dates and times over next 7 business days]. Please
respond by [date — typically 5–7 business days out] with your
availability and your position on each deficiency.

If we are unable to resolve these issues through conference, I
will seek relief from the Court under CR 37 / CR 36 and will cite
this letter as part of the record of my efforts to confer.

Sincerely,

[Name], pro se
[Address]
[Email]
[Phone]
```

### Content requirements

- Identify each deficient response **by number and reason**
- Attach or quote the relevant response
- Propose specific resolution
- Propose dates for a phone / Zoom conference
- Set a response deadline

## Step 2 — Follow-up phone or Zoom

If opposing counsel responds by agreeing to confer, schedule it. If
they decline or ignore, send a second letter noting the decline — that
becomes part of the good-faith record.

### What to cover on the call

- Walk through each deficiency
- Explain why the response is insufficient
- Listen to plaintiff's position (record your notes)
- Propose a resolution for each
- Agree to timelines or next steps
- Send a **confirmation letter** after the call memorializing what
  was discussed

### Confirmation letter

```
[Date]

Re:  Follow-up to [Date] CR 26(i) Conference
     [Case name], Cause No. [number]

Dear [Name]:

This confirms our telephone conversation of [date] at [time]
regarding Plaintiff's discovery responses served [date].

1.  RFP No. 1: You stated [Plaintiff's position]. My position is
    [Defendant's position]. We [agreed / did not agree] on
    resolution.

2.  RFP No. 4: [summary].

[...]

The issues on which we did not reach resolution are: [list].

Unless we reach a further agreement by [date], I will move to
compel under CR 37(a) on the unresolved items.

Sincerely,
[Name], pro se
```

## Step 3 — Certification for the motion

If the motion becomes necessary, include this certification in the
motion itself:

```
            CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f) CERTIFICATION

Pursuant to CR 26(i) [CRLJ 26(f)], [Defendant] certifies that
[they] conferred in good faith with [Plaintiff's counsel] regarding
the discovery deficiencies that are the subject of this motion.

1.  On [date], [Defendant] served a written deficiency letter
    identifying the specific deficiencies in Plaintiff's responses.
    A true and correct copy of the letter is attached as Exhibit
    [A].

2.  On [date], [Defendant] and [Plaintiff's counsel] conferred by
    [telephone / Zoom] for approximately [X] minutes. The parties
    discussed each deficient response. The parties [reached
    agreement on items X and Y / were unable to reach agreement].

3.  [Defendant] sent a confirmation letter on [date], attached as
    Exhibit [B], summarizing the conference.

4.  Despite these efforts, the following deficiencies remain
    unresolved and are the subject of this motion: [list].

The parties have genuinely conferred and been unable to resolve
the dispute. This motion is therefore necessary.
```

## Common pitfalls

- **Going straight to motion** after a single email — denied
- **Failing to attach the letter / confirmation** as an exhibit —
  undermines the certification
- **Vague certification language** ("I tried to confer") — judges
  want specifics: dates, times, participants, what was discussed
- **Confusing the timing** — the conference must happen **before**
  filing the motion, not after

## Notes

- Keep the tone of the deficiency letter **professional** — not
  accusatory. Judges read these exhibits
- Save all emails and letters — they become exhibits to the motion
- If opposing counsel refuses to confer or is nonresponsive, the
  documented nonresponse itself satisfies good-faith effort; attach
  the sent letter and note the nonresponse
