# Motion + Supporting Memorandum Template

A motion with a companion memorandum. Follows the pro-se drafting framework:
concise, fact-forward, ≤ 6 pages total.

## Option A — Self-contained motion (no separate memo)

For simple motions, combine the motion and argument in one document.

```
          [COURT NAME]
    IN AND FOR THE STATE OF WASHINGTON

[PARTIES]                      │   No. [case number]
                               │
                               │   [MOTION TITLE, ALL CAPS, BOLD]
                               │
                               │   NOTE ON MOTION CALENDAR:
                               │   [Date, time, courtroom]

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                    I.  RELIEF REQUESTED

[One paragraph — state exactly what the motion asks the court to do.]

                II.  STATEMENT OF FACTS

[Chronological, ≤ 1 page. Cite exhibits by letter. Cite supporting
declaration paragraphs.]

                    III.  ARGUMENT

**A. [Bold lead-in — first argument heading].**

[Body of argument. Cite rule, statute, case. Tie back to facts.]

**B. [Second argument heading].**

[Body.]

**C. [Third argument heading, if needed].**

[Body.]

                    IV.  CONCLUSION

[One paragraph — restate relief and why the court should grant it.]

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.

____________________________________
[Name, role]
[Address]
[Email]
```

## Option B — Motion with separate memorandum

For more complex motions, file a short motion with the relief requested
and a separate memorandum with the argument.

### Motion (1–2 pages)

```
                       I.  MOTION

[Name] respectfully moves the Court for an order [specific relief].
This motion is based on CR/CRLJ [rule], the accompanying Memorandum in
Support, the Declaration of [Name] filed herewith, and the pleadings
on file.

                II.  GROUNDS FOR MOTION

1.  [First ground, one sentence]
2.  [Second ground]
3.  [Third ground]

                 III.  RELIEF REQUESTED

[Name] requests that the Court:

   a.  [First request]
   b.  [Second request]
   c.  Grant such other relief as the Court deems just.
```

### Memorandum (3–5 pages)

```
          I.  INTRODUCTION

[One paragraph — the single best fact and the relief requested.]

        II.  STATEMENT OF FACTS

[Chronological, cited to exhibits.]

           III.  ARGUMENT

A.  [First point, bold lead-in]

[Legal standard. Authority. Application to facts.]

B.  [Second point]

[Body.]

C.  [Third point, if needed]

[Body.]

           IV.  CONCLUSION

[Restate relief requested.]
```

## Notes

- **Length**: motion ≤ 2 pages, memo ≤ 5 pages. Combined ≤ 6 pages.
- **Argument headings** are bold, lettered (A, B, C), and state the
  conclusion of the argument ("A. The chain of title is missing."),
  not the topic ("A. Chain of title.")
- **Every factual assertion** is tied to an exhibit or declaration
  paragraph
- **Every legal assertion** is tied to a rule, statute, or case
- The companion **declaration** carries the detailed factual narrative
  so the motion itself stays lean
