# Note for Motion Docket Template

A scheduling form that places a motion on the court's calendar. Required
by most WA courts before a motion can be heard.

## Structure

```
          [COURT NAME, CENTERED, BOLD]
      IN AND FOR THE STATE OF WASHINGTON

[PARTIES]                       │   No. [case number]
                                │
                                │   NOTE FOR [CIVIL MOTION /
                                │   MOTION] DOCKET

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TO:    The Clerk of the above-entitled Court;
AND TO: All parties and their counsel of record.

PLEASE TAKE NOTICE that the [Motion Name] filed by [Name] in the
above-captioned matter will be heard on the [Civil Motion] Docket at
the following date, time, and location:

     Date:                 [Day, Month DD, 20YY]
     Time:                 [HH:MM AM/PM]
     Courtroom/Judge:      [Judge Name or courtroom number]
     Location:             [Courthouse address]
     Appearance:           [☐ In-person    ☐ By Zoom]
     Time estimate:        [XX minutes]
     Oral argument:        [☒ Requested]

The following documents are filed and served in support:

     1.  [Motion Name]
     2.  Memorandum in Support of [Motion Name]
     3.  Declaration of [Name] in Support, with Exhibits [A–D]
     4.  Proposed Order
     5.  Certificate of Service

DATED this ___ day of __________, 20__.


____________________________________
[Full Name]
[Role designation, e.g., "Defendant, pro se"]
[Address]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

## KCDC variant (Burien / South Division)

Add to the top of the note (above the "TO:" block):

```
DOCKET:          Civil Motion Docket
SCHEDULING:      KCDC.CivilMGT@kingcounty.gov
DIVISION:        South Division, Burien
```

And include in the email to `KCDC.CivilMGT@kingcounty.gov` when noting:

- The signed Note for Motion Docket (this document)
- The motion packet
- A request for the specific date

The court will confirm the date (or propose an alternative) before the
motion is considered on calendar.

## Notes

- The note is a **scheduling** document, not an argument. Keep it one
  page.
- Include an accurate **time estimate**. Civil motions usually run
  10–20 minutes.
- List **every document** traveling with the motion so the court sees
  the complete packet
- Always include a **Certificate of Service** or serve and note it
  separately
