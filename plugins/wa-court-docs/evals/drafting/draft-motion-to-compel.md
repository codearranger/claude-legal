# wa-draft-motion — Motion to Compel

## Prompt
Draft a motion to compel further responses to my First Requests for
Production, Nos. 1, 4, 5, and 9, and First Interrogatories, Nos. 2 and
7, in my KCDC case.

## Expected triggers
- `wa-draft-motion`
- `wa-discovery`
- `wa-law-references` (civil-rules.md)
- `wa-statewide-format` (GR 14)
- `wa-kcdc` (local-rules.md)

## Acceptance criteria
- **Caption** with KCDC, Burien Division (if applicable), correct
  cause number with clerk date, parties correctly listed
- **Introduction / Relief Sought** block
- **Background** — date each discovery request was served, date
  responses were received, specific deficiencies
- **Legal Standard** — cites CRLJ 26(b)(1) proportionality, CRLJ 37(a)
  motion to compel
- **Argument** — request-by-request analysis (RFP 1, RFP 4, RFP 5,
  RFP 9, IROG 2, IROG 7) — identifies what was asked and why response
  was deficient
- **CRLJ 26(f) Certification** — documents the meet-and-confer effort
  with specific dates, format (letter/call), and outcome
- **Prayer for Relief** — compel full responses, CRLJ 37(a)(4)
  attorney fees and expenses (note pro se limits on fees, but costs
  and any sanctions are recoverable)
- **Signature block** with pro se name, address, email, phone, date
- GR 14 format: 1" margins (3" first-page top for court stamp), 12pt
  font, double-spaced, line numbers in left margin
- **Certificate of Service** as a separate final section

## Common failure modes
- Using CR 37 / CR 26(i) instead of CRLJ 37 / CRLJ 26(f)
- Vague certification language (fails CR 26(i) specificity)
- Omitting the prayer for fees
- Wrong caption format (wrong cause number format, missing clerk date)
- Missing Certificate of Service
- Paragraph numbering that breaks across pages awkwardly
