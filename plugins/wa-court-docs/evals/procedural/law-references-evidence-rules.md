# wa-law-references — Evidence Rules (authentication + hearsay)

## Prompt
Plaintiff attached an unsigned contract and an affidavit from a records
custodian at a third-party company. What evidence rules let me
challenge this at summary judgment?

## Expected triggers
- `wa-law-references` (evidence-rules.md)

## Acceptance criteria
- Cites **ER 901** (authentication — foundation required)
- Cites **ER 902** (self-authentication — what qualifies and what does
  not)
- Cites **ER 803(6)** business records exception — elements
- Cites **ER 1002** best-evidence rule for the unsigned contract
- Notes that an affidavit from a **third-party** custodian raises
  personal-knowledge issues under ER 602 and ER 803(6)'s "made by a
  person with knowledge" / "kept in the regular course" prongs
- Lists leading Washington cases (Ziegler, Iverson, Quincy, Cashmere,
  Bridges) as general-civil business-records authority

## Common failure modes
- Confusing ER 901 (authentication) with ER 902 (self-authentication)
- Omitting ER 1002 best-evidence for the contract
- Missing the third-party custodian issue
- Failing to cite Washington-specific cases
- Confusing federal FRE numbering with Washington ER numbering
