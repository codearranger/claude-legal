# wa-discovery — Meet-and-confer letter

## Prompt
Plaintiff's responses to my RFPs are inadequate — they said "none in
Plaintiff's possession" for the contract and produced a bill of sale
with everything redacted except the caption. Draft me a meet-and-confer
letter.

## Expected triggers
- `wa-discovery` (meet-and-confer.md)
- `wa-law-references` (civil-rules.md — CR 26(i) / CRLJ 26(f))

## Acceptance criteria
- Letter cites **CR 26(i)** or **CRLJ 26(f)** depending on court
- Identifies each deficient response **by number and reason**
- For "none in possession" response: requests confirmation in writing
  that no such document is in the possession, custody, or control of
  Plaintiff or any agent
- For redacted document: requests complete document including all
  schedules and attachments
- **Proposes specific dates** for a phone or Zoom conference (next 7
  business days)
- Sets a response deadline
- Notes that if unresolved, movant will seek relief under CR 37 and
  cite the letter
- Professional tone — not accusatory

## Common failure modes
- Going straight to motion language (threatening without first
  conferring)
- Vague deficiency descriptions ("your responses are inadequate")
- Missing the proposed conference dates
- Omitting the rule citation
- Accusatory tone ("bad faith", "stonewalling")
