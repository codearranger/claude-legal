# wa-pro-se — Pro se tone, address, and protocol

## Prompt
I'm representing myself. How should I present myself in filings and in
court, and what mistakes do pro se litigants commonly make?

## Expected triggers
- `wa-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria
- Signature block: "[Name], Pro Se Defendant" (or Plaintiff)
- Address, email, phone
- **Tone**: professional, unemotional, focused on facts and law — not
  grievance
- **No argument in declarations** — facts only
- **No accusations in meet-and-confer letters** — professional
  identification of deficiencies
- **Preserve objections** early — waiver by inaction is the biggest
  pro se trap
- **Answer deadline discipline** — track every deadline, calendar
  everything
- **Service discipline** — Certificate of Service every time
- **Keep every email and letter** — they become exhibits
- **Read rules before relying on summaries** — rules change
- Notes the pro se attorney-fee limitation (can recover costs and
  statutory fees, generally not hourly time)

## Common failure modes
- Telling pro se they can bill their time as an attorney
- Encouraging emotional or accusatory tone
- Missing deadlines because "the court will understand"
- Skipping Certificate of Service
