# wa-consumer-debt — Chain of title / evidence-debt-buyer

## Prompt
The debt buyer plaintiff attached (1) an affidavit of sale with
nothing but conclusory language, (2) a bill of sale that redacts my
name and account number, and (3) what they call a "computer record"
from the original creditor with no custodian affidavit. How do I
attack this at SJ?

## Expected triggers
- `wa-consumer-debt` (evidence-debt-buyer.md, chain-of-title.md)
- `wa-law-references` (evidence-rules.md)

## Acceptance criteria
- Cites **ER 901** — authentication required
- Cites **ER 902** — self-authentication; notes what does not qualify
  (a conclusory affidavit does not self-authenticate a business
  record)
- Cites **ER 803(6)** — four prongs:
  1. Made at or near time of event
  2. By person with knowledge (or from information transmitted by
     one)
  3. Kept in course of regularly conducted business activity
  4. Regular practice to make the record
- Cites **ER 1002** best-evidence rule — the **bill of sale that does
  not identify plaintiff's account** fails to prove assignment
- Identifies the **chain-of-title problem**: debt buyer must establish
  each link in the chain (original creditor → intermediate assignees →
  plaintiff) with admissible evidence
- Cites leading Washington cases: **Ziegler**, **Iverson**, **Quincy**,
  **Cashmere**, **Bridges** — a remote custodian who has no personal
  knowledge of the original creditor's record-keeping cannot
  authenticate those records
- Cites **UCC Article 9** / **RCW 62A.9A** — assignments of accounts
  and rules for perfection / transfer (relevant to whether the
  assignment is valid)
- Suggests a combined strategy: motion in limine to exclude the
  business records, motion to strike the affidavit, opposition to SJ
  pointing out unmet prima facie

## Common failure modes
- Conflating ER 901 with ER 902
- Missing the redacted-bill-of-sale ER 1002 point
- Missing the chain-of-title doctrine
- Missing the remote-custodian authentication issue
- Missing UCC Article 9 coverage
