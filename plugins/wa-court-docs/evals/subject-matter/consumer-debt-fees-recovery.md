# wa-consumer-debt — Fee recovery in debt-buyer cases

## Prompt
If I win my CPA / FDCPA counterclaim against the debt buyer, what
fees can I recover and under what statute?

## Expected triggers
- `wa-consumer-debt` (fees-consumer-debt.md)

## Acceptance criteria
- Cites **RCW 19.86.090** — Washington CPA: actual damages + treble
  damages up to $25,000 + **reasonable attorney fees and costs**
- Cites **15 U.S.C. § 1692k** — FDCPA: actual damages + $1,000
  statutory damages + **reasonable attorney fees and costs**
- Cites **RCW 19.16.450** — Collection Agency Act: recovery for
  violations
- Cites **RCW 4.84.330** — reciprocal fee-shifting for one-sided
  contract clauses (applies to the underlying account agreement, not
  the counterclaim)
- Notes the **pro se fee limitation** — pro se parties generally
  cannot recover attorney fees for their own time, but can recover
  statutory costs and statutory per-diem fees under RCW 4.84.080
- Notes **lodestar method** from *Bowers v. Transamerica* for
  represented parties
- Provides a four-ground prayer-for-relief template (CPA, FDCPA,
  Collection Agency Act, reciprocal contract clause)

## Common failure modes
- Telling pro se they can bill their time
- Missing one of the four statutory grounds
- Confusing the contract reciprocal with statutory fee-shifting
- Missing the treble-damages cap
