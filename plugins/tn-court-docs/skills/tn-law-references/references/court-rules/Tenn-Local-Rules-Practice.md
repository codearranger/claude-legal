# Tennessee Local Rules of Practice (per-county index)

> **AOC index:** https://www.tncourts.gov/courts/court-rules/local-rules
> **Authority:** Tenn. Sup. Ct. R. 18 (uniform Rules of Court for trial courts)
> **Fetched:** 2026-05-28
> **Format:** canonical-URL directory (per-county entry points)

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** This file is an index of canonical URLs only.
> Local rules change; verify against the live source before filing.
> Tennessee has **no statewide page/margin/font rule**; typography
> defers to the local rules of the filing court.

---

## Scope

Per-county / per-district local rules controlling typography, page
limits, chambers copies, motion-docket scheduling, e-filing, proposed-
order format, and the local intake mechanics that the four statewide
rule sets (Tenn. R. Civ. P., Tenn. R. Evid., Tenn. R. App. P., Tenn.
Sup. Ct. Rules) explicitly leave to each court.

Tennessee local rules are published one set per Judicial District
(some by individual court) and are **not aggregated** into a single
walkable index. The AOC index page above frequently shows only a
subset of districts due to a long-running "temporarily unavailable"
notice; the most reliable path is each county's own clerk site, with
the AOC mirror as a fallback.

This file lists the **canonical entry points** for the flagship
counties covered by the `tn-davidson`, `tn-shelby`, `tn-knox`, and
`tn-hamilton` venue skills, plus the General Sessions and Juvenile
divisions. Agent behavior: when drafting for one of these counties,
fetch the linked source before setting page limits, motion dockets,
chambers copies, or e-filing requirements.

## Davidson County — 20th Judicial District (Nashville)

Metropolitan Nashville and Davidson County operates a **consolidated
city-county government**. The Trial Courts of Metropolitan Nashville
& Davidson County encompass Circuit, Chancery, Criminal, Probate, and
General Sessions, each with its own published rules.

| Court | Canonical source |
|---|---|
| Circuit / Chancery / Criminal / Probate (Courts of Record) | https://circuitclerk.nashville.gov/local-rules-davidson-county-courts-of-record/ |
| Courts of Record — full PDF | https://circuitclerk.nashville.gov/wp-content/uploads/Local-Rules_Davidson-County_post_copy_043019.pdf |
| General Sessions Court (Civil) — landing | https://gscourt.nashville.gov/coming-to-court/local-rules-of-court/ |
| General Sessions Court (Civil) — current PDF (effective 6-1-24) | https://gscourt.nashville.gov/wp-content/uploads/Local-Rules-Civil-Updated-6-1-24-3.pdf |
| Probate Court — local rules | https://circuitclerk.nashville.gov/local-rules-probate-court/ |
| Juvenile Court — AOC mirror | https://www.tncourts.gov/sites/default/files/juv20.pdf |
| Trial Courts portal (judges, divisions) | https://trialcourts.nashville.gov/ |

**Note:** the Davidson **Courts of Record** rules above carry a 2019
revision date on the PDF filename. The Davidson clerk has flagged
**new local rules effective June 1, 2026**; before final filing,
check the landing page above for the updated PDF.

## Knox County — 6th Judicial District (Knoxville)

Knox County's local rules are organized as a **Uniform Local Rules of
Practice for the Sixth Judicial District** (adopted under Tenn. Sup.

| Court | Canonical source |

4:30 p.m. with noon recess 12:00–1:30 p.m.; ex parte matters in
chambers 9:00–9:30 a.m. Confirm before scheduling.

## Hamilton County — 11th Judicial District (Chattanooga)

Hamilton County publishes a single **11th Judicial District Local
Rules of Civil Practice** covering Chancery and Circuit (Rules 1-14
apply to both; Rules 15-18 are Chancery-only). The current published
edition is dated **January 12, 2022**; a revised edition effective
**February 1, 2024** is mirrored at the AOC.

| Court | Canonical source |
|---|---|
| 11th JD Local Rules — Hamilton clerk PDF (Jan. 12, 2022) | https://www.hamiltontn.gov/pdf/courts/ClerkMaster/Local%20Rules%20of%20Civil%20Practice%20January%2012%202022.pdf |
| 11th JD Revised Local Rules — AOC mirror (eff. 2-1-2024)¹ | https://www.tncourts.gov/sites/default/files/docs/11th%20District%20-%20Revised%20Local%20Rules%20%28Effective%202-1-2024%29.pdf |
| Chancery Court rules & fees page | https://www.hamiltontn.gov/Chancery_RulesFees.aspx |
| Chancery Court dockets | https://www.hamiltontn.gov/ChanceryCourt_Dockets.aspx |
| Courts portal (entry point) | https://www.hamiltontn.gov/Courts.aspx |
| AOC index node for Hamilton | https://www.tncourts.gov/node/7416 |

¹ The AOC URL contains parentheses in the path; URL-encode `(` as
`%28` and `)` as `%29` when fetching. The bare-paren form 404s in
some HTTP clients.

**Motion-docket convention (Chancery):** Part 1 motions are heard
1:30 p.m. on the **first and third** Mondays; Part 2 motions are
heard 1:30 p.m. on the **second and fourth** Mondays. Confirm
against the current rules before noticing.

## Other populous counties (carried in `tn-county-courts`)

Per-county local rules for Rutherford / Williamson / Montgomery /
Sumner / Wilson / Sevier / Washington / Madison / Bradley / et al.
are published one set per Judicial District at each clerk's site,
with the AOC index above as the canonical entry point. The AOC's
**Judicial District map** at
https://www.tncourts.gov/jdmap resolves a county to its JD; from
the JD page the AOC links the published local rules where available.
When AOC shows "temporarily unavailable" for a district, contact the
local clerk via the Tennessee **Clerks List** at
https://tncourts.gov/courts/court-clerks/clerks-list.

## How the agent should use this file

1. Identify the filing court's JD (via `tn-davidson` / `tn-shelby` /
   `tn-knox` / `tn-hamilton` / `tn-general-sessions` / `tn-county-courts`
   or the AOC JD map).
2. Fetch the linked source for that court / JD **before** setting:
   - page limits and brief format
   - chambers-copy / working-copy requirements
   - motion-docket day and time
   - e-filing platform and document-type selection
   - proposed-order transmittal format
3. Cross-check the local rule against Tenn. R. Civ. P. 10 / 11 and
   the relevant Tenn. Sup. Ct. Rule (esp. Rule 18) — the local rule
   prevails where there is conflict and a statewide rule does not
   pre-empt.
4. If the local rule cannot be reached on the day of filing, fall
   back to the marketplace common-practice defaults checked by
   `scripts/format-check.py` (US Letter, 1" margins, 12-point body,
   double spacing, footer pagination) and confirm typography with
   the assigned judge's chambers before the responsive deadline.
