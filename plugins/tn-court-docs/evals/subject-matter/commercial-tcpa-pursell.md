# tn-commercial-disputes — TCPA + Pursell B2B exclusion

## Prompt

My company is a small Tennessee LLC. We were defrauded by a
larger supplier that knowingly shipped defective inventory and
misrepresented the goods in writing. Counsel suggested pleading
the Tennessee Consumer Protection Act for treble damages and
mandatory attorney's fees. Should we?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-commercial-disputes`
- `tn-consumer-debt` (for TCPA general framework)

## Acceptance criteria

### Pursell B2B exclusion

- [ ] Identifies ***Pursell v. First American National Bank*,
      937 S.W.2d 838 (Tenn. 1996)** as the controlling
      authority narrowing the TCPA's reach
- [ ] States the rule: the TCPA was intended to protect
      **consumers**, not businesses; pure **business-to-
      business commercial disputes are generally outside the
      TCPA's scope**
- [ ] Frames the *Pursell* default as **strong** — most B2B
      cases will lose the TCPA count on a Rule 12.02(6) motion

### Statutory framework

- [ ] Cites the TCPA at **Tenn. Code Ann. § 47-18-101 et
      seq.** for the general statutory text
- [ ] Notes the TCPA's broad statutory language ("any trade or
      commerce") on its face could read to include B2B, but
      *Pursell* narrowly construes it

### When TCPA may still reach B2B conduct

- [ ] Identifies the **narrow refinements** post-*Pursell*:
      TCPA may apply where one side functions as a **consumer**
      (small-business buyer of consumer-grade product); where
      the conduct affects the **broader consumer marketplace**;
      or where the dispute involves **deceptive advertising**
      flowing to consumers
- [ ] Notes these are fact-specific exceptions, not categorical
      escapes from the *Pursell* default

### What to plead instead

- [ ] Recommends **breach of contract** under the 6-year SOL
      at Tenn. Code Ann. § 28-3-109 (or 4-year UCC § 47-2-725
      for sale of goods)
- [ ] Recommends **common-law fraud** with **Rule 9.02
      particularity** — time, place, contents, speaker, what
      was obtained
- [ ] Recommends **breach of UCC warranty** (express,
      merchantability, fitness for particular purpose) under
      § 47-2-313 / -314 / -315 for the sale-of-goods overlay
- [ ] (Bonus) Recommends **tortious interference** or **civil
      conspiracy** if facts support; recognizes these are
      tort claims with 3-year SOL

### Damages comparison

- [ ] Notes the **TCPA's draws** (treble damages + mandatory
      fees) are what makes plaintiffs want to plead it, but
      *Pursell* removes them in B2B
- [ ] Notes that **breach + fraud** does not offer the same
      multiplier — compensatory damages + punitives only on
      fraud (subject to **§ 29-39-104** punitive cap of greater
      of $500k or 2x compensatory)

### Pleading defense angle

- [ ] (Bonus) Notes that if the defendant has been served with
      a TCPA-counted complaint, defense counsel should
      **move to dismiss the TCPA count under Rule 12.02(6)
      with *Pursell* citation** rather than answering it on
      the merits

## Common failure modes

- Pleads the TCPA without acknowledging *Pursell*
- Treats *Pursell* as overruled or weakened (it isn't)
- Misses the narrow-refinement exceptions (consumer-grade
  product; broader-marketplace effect)
- Ignores common-law fraud + Rule 9.02 as the alternative
- Misses the **4-year UCC** SOL where the dispute is over sale
  of goods
- Misses the punitive cap at § 29-39-104
- Treats § 47-18-109 (the TCPA's private-action provision) as
  unilaterally available for any deceptive conduct
- Doesn't pivot to the UCC warranty framework for sale-of-
  goods cases
