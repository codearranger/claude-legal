# tn-consumer-debt — § 20-6-104 debt-buyer documentation requirement (2024)

## Prompt

A debt buyer sued me in Tennessee General Sessions. I didn't
respond and didn't show up. Will they automatically get a
default judgment? What does Tennessee require them to prove
even on default?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-consumer-debt`
- `tn-general-sessions`

## Acceptance criteria

### The 2024 statute

- [ ] Identifies **Tenn. Code Ann. § 20-6-104** as the
      controlling 2024 statute imposing documentation
      requirements on debt-buyer plaintiffs **before any
      default judgment**
- [ ] Notes the statute took effect in 2024

### Required documentation

- [ ] States that the plaintiff must present to the court
      **before any default judgment** is entered:
      (1) **documentation showing the plaintiff's authority
          to collect** the debt (assignment / bill of sale /
          chain of title from the original creditor through
          each intermediate assignee); AND
      (2) **at least one document showing the debt's
          existence** (e.g., signed account agreement, billing
          statement, or other contemporaneous evidence)
- [ ] States these requirements apply **irrespective of any
      affidavit** submitted with the complaint — a sworn
      statement of debt is not a substitute

### Coverage limitation

- [ ] Notes the rule applies to **subsequent creditors / debt
      buyers**, **not** to original creditors / lienholders
- [ ] Frames the rule as a **debt-buyer-specific protection**
      reflecting concerns about default-judgment abuse where
      assignment chains and source documents are missing

### Strategic implications

- [ ] Identifies the rule as **dispositive** in many debt-
      buyer cases: even where the defendant doesn't appear,
      a debt-buyer plaintiff lacking source documents and
      chain of title **cannot get a default judgment**
- [ ] Notes the rule effectively **flips the no-appearance
      strategy** — non-appearance can still defeat a
      documentation-deficient debt buyer at the default-
      judgment stage
- [ ] Distinguishes this from a **judgment on the merits**
      after appearance, where the burdens shift differently

### Procedural overlay

- [ ] Notes the General Sessions clerk's role in flagging
      documentation deficiency at the default-judgment
      docket; some clerks are more attentive than others
- [ ] Suggests filing a **motion to vacate** under Tenn. R.
      Civ. P. 60.02 if a default judgment is granted in
      violation of § 20-6-104 (where the case appealed to
      Circuit) or via the General Sessions equivalent

## Common failure modes

- Treats default judgment as automatic on non-appearance
- Says the plaintiff's complaint affidavit alone satisfies
  the documentation requirement
- Applies § 20-6-104 to original creditors
- Misses the statute entirely (cites only chain-of-title +
  FDCPA, missing the Tennessee statutory protection)
- States the effective date as something other than 2024
- Suggests the rule is mere best practice rather than a
  jurisdictional gate on default judgments
