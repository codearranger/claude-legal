# tn-family-law — Uncontested irreconcilable-differences divorce

## Prompt

My spouse and I both want a divorce and we agree on
everything. We have one child who is 7. We're in Tennessee.
What's the process for an irreconcilable-differences divorce,
how long does it take, and how are property and child support
handled?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-family-law`
- `tn-family-court`

## Acceptance criteria

### Grounds and procedure

- [ ] Identifies **irreconcilable differences** as a ground
      under **T.C.A. § 36-4-101(a)(14)**, with the ID procedure
      at **T.C.A. § 36-4-103**
- [ ] Notes a marital dissolution agreement (and, because
      there is a minor child, a permanent parenting plan) must
      be in place for an uncontested ID divorce

### Waiting period

- [ ] States the **statutory waiting period under T.C.A.
      § 36-4-103(a)** and correctly applies the **90-day**
      period because there is an **unmarried minor child**
      (the 60-day period applies only when there is no minor
      child); confirm the current figures against the
      references corpus
- [ ] Counts the waiting period from the date the complaint is
      on file

### Property division

- [ ] States Tennessee is an **equitable-distribution** state
      under **T.C.A. § 36-4-121** (NOT community property):
      classify marital vs. separate property, then divide the
      marital estate equitably (not necessarily equally) per
      the § 36-4-121(c) factors

### Parenting plan

- [ ] Requires a **permanent parenting plan** (T.C.A.
      § 36-6-401 et seq.) with the **best-interest factors**
      (§ 36-6-106(a))
- [ ] Routes married-parent custody incident to divorce to
      **Circuit/Chancery** (not Juvenile Court)

### Child support

- [ ] States child support uses the **income-shares model**
      (statutory basis **T.C.A. § 36-5-101**; Guidelines at
      **Tenn. Comp. R. & Regs. ch. 1240-02-04**); read current
      figures/worksheet from the references corpus rather than
      asserting fixed amounts

## Common failure modes

- Applies the 60-day waiting period despite the minor child
- Describes Tennessee as a community-property state
- Sends a married-parent / incident-to-divorce custody matter
  to Juvenile Court
- Asserts a fixed child-support figure instead of pointing to
  the income-shares Guidelines
- Cites § 36-4-101(b) for the waiting period (the procedure is
  § 36-4-103)
