# tn-employment — THRA discrimination + retaliation intake

## Prompt

I work at a small Tennessee company (about 10 employees).
After I complained to HR about my supervisor making racist
comments, I was put on a "performance improvement plan" and
then terminated 2 months later. I want to sue. What are my
options under Tennessee and federal law?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-employment`
- `tn-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### Statutory framework — federal vs. state coverage

- [ ] Identifies that **Title VII** requires **15+ employees**
      (42 U.S.C. § 2000e(b)) and **may NOT apply** to a 10-
      employee employer
- [ ] Identifies the **Tennessee Human Rights Act (THRA)** at
      **Tenn. Code Ann. § 4-21-101 et seq.** with its **8+
      employee** threshold under § 4-21-102(5) — **does apply**
      to a 10-employee employer
- [ ] Frames the THRA as **the primary statutory vehicle** in
      this fact pattern, with Title VII potentially unavailable

### THRA causes of action

- [ ] Identifies the **THRA race-discrimination** claim under
      § 4-21-401 (or the parallel provision)
- [ ] Identifies the **THRA retaliation** claim under § 4-21-
      301 — protected activity (complaint to HR) + adverse
      action (PIP + termination) + causal connection
- [ ] Notes that retaliation claims often succeed where the
      underlying discrimination claim does not, because the
      protected-activity element only requires good-faith
      belief that conduct violated the THRA

### SOLs

- [ ] States the **THRA judicial SOL** as **1 year** from the
      last discriminatory act under **§ 4-21-311(d)** — short
      compared to most peer states
- [ ] States the **THRA administrative SOL** as **180 days** to
      THRC under § 4-21-302(a)
- [ ] Cites the **federal 300-day** EEOC deadline (Tennessee is
      a **deferral state** via the THRC work-share agreement)
- [ ] Notes the **THRA judicial track and administrative track
      run independently** — filing in court does not require
      exhaustion at THRC; filing at THRC may bar later judicial
      action via election of remedies

### Forum strategy

- [ ] Identifies the available forums as **Chancery or Circuit**
      (concurrent subject-matter jurisdiction under THRA)
- [ ] Notes Chancery is often preferred where **equitable
      remedies** (reinstatement, injunction) are sought
- [ ] States that **General Sessions does NOT have THRA
      jurisdiction**
- [ ] (Bonus) Notes the **federal-court option** via
      supplemental jurisdiction if a Title VII or § 1981 claim
      is also pleaded

### Damages and remedies

- [ ] Identifies that the THRA has **no statutory damages cap**
      — compensatory, punitive, back pay, front pay, and
      emotional-distress damages are uncapped under state law
- [ ] Notes **mandatory attorney's fees** to the prevailing
      plaintiff under § 4-21-311(b)
- [ ] (Bonus) Contrasts with Title VII's **sliding statutory
      caps** based on employer size

### § 1981 federal overlay

- [ ] (Bonus) Identifies **42 U.S.C. § 1981** as a federal
      race-discrimination statute with **no employer-size
      threshold** and a **4-year SOL** for post-1990
      contract-modification claims — provides a federal vehicle
      when Title VII coverage is unavailable

## Common failure modes

- States Title VII applies to a 10-employee employer (it
  doesn't)
- Misses the THRA's lower 8+ employee threshold
- States the THRA SOL as 180 days (that's administrative; the
  judicial SOL is 1 year)
- Treats the THRA judicial track as requiring administrative
  exhaustion (it doesn't, but election of remedies applies)
- Tries to file in General Sessions
- Imposes a Title VII-style damages cap on the THRA claim
- Misses § 1981 as a federal vehicle for small-employer race
  cases
- Forgets the mandatory attorney's-fees provision
