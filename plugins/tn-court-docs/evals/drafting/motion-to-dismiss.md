# tn-draft-motion — Rule 12.02(6) motion to dismiss a debt complaint

## Prompt

I'm representing myself. A debt buyer sued me in Circuit
Court for about $4,000 on an old credit-card account, but the
complaint just says I "owe" the money with no documents and
no explanation of how they got the account. I want to draft a
motion to dismiss for failure to state a claim. I'm in
Tennessee.

## Expected triggers

- `tn-draft-motion`
- `tn-statewide-format`
- `tn-first-30-days`
- `tn-pro-se`
- `tn-consumer-debt`

## Acceptance criteria

### Caption

- [ ] Tenn. R. Civ. P. **10.01** caption: court name, parties,
      file/docket number, and the designation of the pleading
      (per Rule 7.01)
- [ ] Title identifies the motion: "MOTION TO DISMISS" citing
      **Tenn. R. Civ. P. 12.02(6)** (failure to state a claim
      upon which relief can be granted)

### Standard

- [ ] States the Rule 12.02(6) standard: the court tests the
      legal sufficiency of the complaint, accepting well-
      pleaded factual allegations as true
- [ ] Notes that if matters outside the pleadings are
      presented and not excluded, a 12.02(6) motion is
      **converted to a Tenn. R. Civ. P. 56 summary-judgment**
      motion (cite the controlling rule; read current
      conversion language from the references corpus)

### Fee-shifting flag (Tennessee-specific)

- [ ] **Flags T.C.A. § 20-12-119(c) fee-shifting exposure** —
      a party that prevails on a 12.02(6) dismissal may recover
      costs and reasonable attorney's fees against the non-
      prevailing party; cite the statute and read the current
      cap/limits from the references corpus rather than
      asserting a fixed dollar figure
- [ ] Frames this as a risk the self-represented litigant
      should weigh before moving (i.e., losing the motion can
      cut both ways)

### Composition

- [ ] References self-represented status without lawyer-speak
- [ ] Signature block per Tenn. R. Civ. P. 11

## Common failure modes

- Defaults to FRCP 12(b)(6) instead of Tenn. R. Civ. P.
  12.02(6)
- Omits the § 20-12-119(c) fee-shifting warning entirely
- Asserts a specific § 20-12-119(c) dollar cap as a hard fact
  rather than pointing to the statute / current law
- Misses that outside-the-pleadings matter converts the
  motion to Rule 56
