# tn-draft-declaration — Supporting affidavit (jurat / Rule 10.02)

## Prompt

Draft an affidavit to support my motion in my Tennessee
Circuit Court case. I need to swear to the facts about how
the plaintiff debt buyer never sent me the account agreement
or any proof they bought my account. I am representing
myself.

## Expected triggers

- `tn-draft-declaration`
- `tn-statewide-format`
- `tn-pro-se`

## Acceptance criteria

### Form

- [ ] Produces a **notarized affidavit** with a proper
      **jurat** ("Sworn to and subscribed before me this ___
      day of ___, 20__" + notary signature/seal/commission
      expiration) — Tennessee uses the sworn-and-notarized
      affidavit form (NOT an unsworn declaration in lieu of
      affidavit)
- [ ] Caption per Tenn. R. Civ. P. **10.01** (court, parties,
      docket number, pleading designation)
- [ ] **Numbered paragraphs** per Tenn. R. Civ. P. **10.02**,
      each limited so far as practicable to a single set of
      circumstances
- [ ] Signature block compatible with a self-represented
      affiant (no bar number)

### Content

- [ ] Opens with the affiant's competence and **personal
      knowledge** ("I am over 18, of sound mind, and have
      personal knowledge of the facts stated below")
- [ ] Each substantive paragraph states one factual assertion
- [ ] Confines the affidavit to facts within personal
      knowledge; reserves legal argument for the motion/brief
- [ ] Attaches/labels exhibits and references them in the body
- [ ] Closing language that the statements are true and
      correct, sworn under oath

## Common failure modes

- Drafts an unsworn "declaration under penalty of perjury"
  with no notary jurat
- Omits or garbles the jurat / notary block
- Runs multiple unrelated facts into a single paragraph
  (violates Rule 10.02)
- Mixes legal conclusions and argument into the sworn body
- Fails to assert personal knowledge / affiant competence
