# tn-general-sessions — Civil warrant practice and limits

## Prompt

I'm being sued in General Sessions Court in Tennessee. What
does a case look like there compared to a "real" Circuit Court
lawsuit? Is there a dollar limit, and can I get discovery?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-general-sessions`

## Acceptance criteria

### The civil-warrant model

- [ ] Recognizes that General Sessions civil cases are
      commenced by a **civil warrant** (not a formal
      summons-and-complaint), and that practice is **informal**
- [ ] Notes the **Tenn. R. Civ. P. generally do NOT apply in
      General Sessions** except as specifically made applicable

### Jurisdictional cap

- [ ] States the **$25,000 General Sessions civil
      jurisdictional cap** and cites the controlling statute
      (T.C.A. § 16-15-501); read the current figure from the
      references corpus rather than asserting it as fixed
- [ ] Notes the **forcible entry & detainer (eviction)
      exception** — unlimited jurisdiction in FED matters

### No formal discovery

- [ ] States that there is **no formal discovery as of right**
      in General Sessions

### De novo appeal

- [ ] States the **10-day de novo appeal to Circuit Court**
      from entry of the General Sessions judgment, citing
      T.C.A. § 27-5-108 (trial de novo under full civil rules)

## Common failure modes

- Treats General Sessions like a court of record with full
  TRCP discovery
- Applies the Circuit Court summons-and-complaint model
- Asserts the cap as a hard-coded dollar figure rather than
  citing the statute / current law
- States the wrong appeal period or the wrong appeal mechanism
  (it is a 10-day de novo appeal, not a 30-day record appeal)
