# tn-first-30-days — Summary-judgment standard and Rule 56.04 timing

## Prompt

The plaintiff in my Tennessee Circuit Court case says they're
going to file for summary judgment. What standard does the
court apply, and how much notice do I get before the hearing?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-first-30-days`
- `tn-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### The governing standard

- [ ] Identifies the controlling summary-judgment standard as
      stated in ***Rye v. Women's Care Center of Memphis,
      MPLLC*, 477 S.W.3d 235 (Tenn. 2015)**
- [ ] Describes the Rye/Celotex-style standard: the moving
      party may either **affirmatively negate an essential
      element** of the nonmovant's claim **or** demonstrate
      that the nonmovant's evidence is **insufficient to
      establish an essential element** as a matter of law
- [ ] Correctly treats *Rye* as having **overruled the prior
      *Hannan* standard**

### Timing (Rule 56.04)

- [ ] Cites **Tenn. R. Civ. P. 56.04**: the motion must be
      **served at least 30 days before** the hearing
- [ ] Notes the adverse party's response deadline relative to
      the hearing (read the current figure from the references
      corpus rather than asserting a fixed number)

## Common failure modes

- Recites the old *Hannan v. Alltel* standard as current law
- Applies the federal Rule 56 standard without anchoring to
  *Rye* / Tenn. R. Civ. P. 56
- Misstates the 30-day-before-hearing service requirement
- Confuses the summary-judgment hearing-notice period with
  the answer deadline
