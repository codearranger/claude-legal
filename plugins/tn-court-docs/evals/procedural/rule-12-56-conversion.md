# tn-first-30-days — Tenn. R. Civ. P. 12.02(6) → Rule 56 conversion + § 20-12-119(c) fee-shifting

## Prompt

A defendant in my Tennessee Circuit Court case filed a "motion
to dismiss for failure to state a claim." They attached two
affidavits and a contract — material that's outside my
complaint. What does the court do with the affidavits, and is
there any fee risk if the dismissal is granted?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-first-30-days`
- `tn-deadlines`

## Acceptance criteria

### Conversion to Rule 56

- [ ] Identifies that under **Tenn. R. Civ. P. 12.02**, when
      matters **outside the pleading** are presented to and
      not excluded by the court on a Rule 12.02(6) motion, the
      motion is **converted to a motion for summary judgment**
      under Rule 56
- [ ] States that on conversion, **all parties shall be given
      reasonable opportunity to present** all material made
      pertinent to a Rule 56 motion (the right to take
      discovery and submit opposing evidence)
- [ ] Notes the court may, in its discretion, **exclude the
      outside material** and decide the motion strictly under
      Rule 12.02(6) — the conversion is not automatic

### The § 20-12-119(c) fee-shifting trap

- [ ] Cites **Tenn. Code Ann. § 20-12-119(c)** — the
      mandatory fee-shifting provision for Rule 12.02(6)
      dismissals
- [ ] States that where a Rule 12.02(6) motion is granted in
      whole or in part, the **non-prevailing party shall pay
      the prevailing party's reasonable and necessary**
      attorney's fees and costs **up to $10,000**
- [ ] Notes the fee-shift applies on **dismissals on the
      face of the pleadings only** — a conversion to Rule 56
      typically takes the case out of the fee-shift scope
- [ ] Notes the fee-shift does NOT apply to claims by or
      against state/local government, pro-se parties (unless
      specifically pleaded), or certain enumerated case types

### Strategy implications

- [ ] Frames the central strategic decision: a defendant
      attaching outside material **risks losing the
      § 20-12-119(c) fee-shift** even if the dismissal motion
      ultimately succeeds, because the motion has been
      converted to Rule 56
- [ ] Notes that the plaintiff in this scenario should
      consider **whether to ask the court to convert** the
      motion (taking the case out of the fee-shift) or to
      **move to strike** the outside material so the motion
      is decided on the pleadings alone

## Common failure modes

- Treats the outside material as automatically rejected without
  discussing Rule 12.02 conversion
- Misses the § 20-12-119(c) fee-shifting trap entirely
- Asserts the fee-shifting cap is something other than $10,000
- Applies a federal Rule 12(b)(6) / Twombly-Iqbal framework
  without anchoring to the Tennessee notice-pleading baseline
- Claims § 20-12-119(c) applies to summary-judgment dismissals
- Misses that the fee-shifting trap can drive a plaintiff to
  weigh non-meritorious counts carefully
