# tn-general-sessions — 10-day de novo appeal under § 27-5-108

## Prompt

I just lost a General Sessions civil case in Davidson County
(Nashville). The judgment was entered yesterday. I want to
appeal because I never had a chance to do any discovery and
the judge wouldn't hear my evidence. What are my options and
what's the deadline?

## Expected triggers

- `tn-general-sessions`
- `tn-deadlines`
- `tn-first-30-days`

## Acceptance criteria

### The 10-day appeal clock

- [ ] Cites **Tenn. Code Ann. § 27-5-108** — General
      Sessions civil judgments may be appealed **de novo to
      Circuit Court within 10 days** of the judgment
- [ ] Notes the 10-day period is **calendar days** (with the
      Rule 6.01 weekend / holiday extension); the clock is
      **jurisdictional** and cannot be enlarged after expiration
- [ ] States that filing the notice of appeal **plus the
      appeal cost bond** (or pauper's oath under § 20-12-127)
      is what perfects the appeal

### The de novo nature of the appeal

- [ ] States that **de novo means tried afresh** — Circuit
      hears the case from scratch under the **full Tenn. R.
      Civ. P.**, with formal discovery, summary judgment, and
      jury demand all available
- [ ] Frames the de novo appeal as the **principal vehicle**
      for accessing formal discovery in cases that started in
      General Sessions
- [ ] Notes the General Sessions judgment is **vacated**, not
      reviewed — Circuit does not give it deference

### Possession-case overlay (where relevant)

- [ ] If the case is a detainer warrant, identifies the
      **possession bond under Tenn. Code Ann. § 29-18-130(b)**
      to stay execution pending appeal
- [ ] Notes case law has held appeal **not automatically
      dismissed** for bond default (e.g., *Johnson v.
      Hopkins*, 432 S.W.3d 840 (Tenn. 2013)), but bond is
      required to stay the writ of possession
- [ ] Notes the **pauper's oath under § 20-12-127** as a
      substitute for bond on showing of indigency

### Strategy implications

- [ ] Identifies the **de novo appeal as a discovery
      strategy** — start the case in General Sessions to test
      the plaintiff's evidence, lose without formal discovery,
      appeal to Circuit, then move to compel
- [ ] Notes the practical effect: the General Sessions hearing
      becomes a free "preview" of the plaintiff's case;
      defendant can elect to settle or fight in Circuit

## Common failure modes

- Misstates the 10-day clock (cites 30 days; treats as
  business days only; treats as merely directory)
- Confuses de novo with an appellate-record review
- Forgets the bond / pauper's-oath requirement in possession
  cases
- Asserts the General Sessions judgment carries any preclusive
  weight in Circuit
- Misses the discovery-strategy framing of de novo appeal
