# Cross-State Procedural Quirks

A reference catalog of procedural quirks across the most-
populous U.S. states that should be **prominently flagged**
in the relevant skill descriptions when adding a new state
plugin. A pro se filer porting practice from one state to
another typically gets tripped up by exactly these
differences.

> **NOT LEGAL ADVICE.** Each entry is a starting point.
> Verify the current rule before relying.

## Discovery

### Written interrogatories

| State | Available by default? | Limit |
|-------|----------------------|-------|
| Federal | Yes (FRCP 33) | 25 |
| Washington | Yes (CR 33) | 25 |
| **Oregon** | **NO** — court order required | n/a |
| California | Yes (CCP § 2030) | 35 (form) + 35 (special) |
| Texas | Yes (TRCP 197) | Varies by discovery level |
| Florida | Yes (Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340) | 30 |
| New York | Yes (CPLR 3130) | 25 |

Oregon's no-interrogatories rule is the most distinctive
discovery quirk in the U.S.

### Discovery control plans (Texas)

Texas TRCP 190 imposes mandatory discovery levels (1, 2, 3)
based on case characteristics — affects discovery scope,
deposition limits, time limits. Unique to Texas.

### Mandatory disclosures

| State | Required? | Authority |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Federal | Yes | FRCP 26(a) |
| California | NO | n/a (no broad mandatory disclosure) |
| Texas | Yes — TRCP 194 | "initial disclosures" within 30 days |
| Florida | NO | n/a |

### Discovery cutoffs

State practice on discovery cutoffs varies:

- Federal: 30 days before trial typically
- California: 30 days before trial (CCP § 2024.020)
- Texas: 30 days before trial typically
- Florida: pretrial conference sets

## Pleading standards

### Notice vs. fact pleading

| State | Standard |
|-------|----------|
| Federal | Notice pleading (Twombly/Iqbal plausibility) |
| Washington | Notice pleading |
| **Oregon** | **Fact pleading** — must allege ultimate facts (Granewich) |
| California | "Ultimate facts" / fact pleading — but liberalized |
| Texas | Fact pleading |
| Florida | Notice pleading |
| New York | Fact pleading (CPLR 3013) |

When porting a complaint from a notice-pleading state to a
fact-pleading state, expect ORCP 21 A(8)-style failure-to-
state attacks.

### Verified pleadings (New York)

New York has the concept of verified vs. unverified pleadings
with distinct treatment under CPLR 3020. Unique to New York
in modern practice.

## Summary judgment

| State | Rule | Quirks |
|-------|------|--------|
| Federal | FRCP 56 | Standard |
| Washington | CR 56 | 28-day filing window |
| Oregon | ORCP 47 C | 60-day filing window |
| California | **CCP § 437c** | **75-day filing window**; 14-day reply; very procedurally strict |
| Texas | TRCP 166a | "Traditional" + "no-evidence" types |
| Florida | Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510 | **2021 amendment** aligned standard with federal *Anderson v. Liberty Lobby* |
| New York | CPLR 3212 | No partial summary judgment in some contexts |

California's CCP § 437c timing is the most-aggressive of any
state — the 75-day notice period is a hard floor.

Florida's 2021 SJ standard change is a recent quirk; pre-2021
Florida case law on SJ should be flagged as superseded.

## Default and vacation

| State | Default rule | Vacation rule |
|-------|--------------|---------------|
| Federal | FRCP 55 | FRCP 60(b) |
| Washington | CR 55 | CR 60 |
| Oregon | ORCP 69 | ORCP 71 |
| California | CCP § 585 | CCP § 473 |
| Texas | TRCP 239 | TRCP 320 (motion for new trial) |
| Florida | Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.500 | Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.540 |
| New York | CPLR 3215 | CPLR 5015 |

### Time limits on default vacation

| State | Outer cap |
|-------|-----------|
| Federal | 1 year (FRCP 60(c)(1)) |
| Oregon | 1 year (ORCP 71 B(1)-(3)) |
| California | 6 months (CCP § 473(b)) |
| Texas | 30 days post-judgment for motion for new trial |
| Florida | 1 year (Rule 1.540) |

California's 6-month cap is shorter than most.

## Service

### ORCP 7 / CCP § 415 / FRCP 4 analogs

Each state has its own service rules. Notable quirks:

- **California (CCP § 415)**: substituted service requires a
  10-day mailing; service complete on 10th day
- **Texas (TRCP 106)**: motion for substituted service
  required; cannot use without court order
- **New York (CPLR 308)**: "nail and mail" service requires
  affidavit detailing prior attempts

### Pre-trial deadlines

The Return of Service (or analog) deadline varies:

| State | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| Oregon | 63 days (ORCP 7 D(3)) |
| Washington | 90 days (CR 4(d)) |
| California | 60 days (CCP § 583.210) |
| Federal | 90 days (FRCP 4(m)) |

## Mandatory arbitration / ADR

| State | Mandatory program? | Amount in controversy |
|-------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Washington | Yes (RCW 7.06) | Up to $100,000 |
| Oregon | Yes (ORS 36.400) | $10,000–$50,000 |
| California | No (counties may have local ADR) | n/a |
| Texas | Limited (TRCP 169 for expedited; TRCP 167 for offers of settlement) | n/a |
| Florida | County-by-county varies | Varies |

## Statutes of limitations on contract debt

| State | SOL | Authority |
|-------|-----|-----------|
| Washington | 6 yrs (written), 3 yrs (oral) | RCW 4.16.040, .080 |
| Oregon | 6 yrs | ORS 12.080 |
| **California** | **4 yrs (written), 2 yrs (oral)** | **CCP § 337, § 339** |
| Texas | 4 yrs | Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.004 |
| Florida | 5 yrs | Fla. Stat. § 95.11 |
| New York | 6 yrs general; **3 yrs for revolving credit (2022)** | NY CPLR § 213; § 214-i |
| Illinois | 10 yrs (written), 5 yrs (oral) | 735 ILCS 5/13-205 |

Notable shorter SOLs:

- California 4-year on contract
- New York's 2022 reform shortening credit-card SOL to 3
  years (CPLR § 214-i)

## Revival of SOL by partial payment

| State | Partial payment revives? | Authority |
|-------|--------------------------|-----------|
| Washington | Yes | RCW 4.16.270 |
| **Oregon** | **NO — written promise only** | **ORS 12.230** |
| California | Yes | CCP § 360 |
| Texas | NO — written promise only | Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.065 |
| Florida | Acknowledgment must be written | Fla. Stat. § 95.04 |
| New York | NO — written acknowledgment only (CPLR § 17) | CPLR § 17 |

Many states require a written promise. The Oregon flag in
`or-consumer-debt/references/or-statutes-of-limitations.md` is
applicable to several other states; adapt language when adding
those states' plugins.

## Consumer-protection / unfair-practices statutes

| State | Statute | Key features |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| Washington | RCW 19.86 (CPA) | Hangman Ridge factors; treble damages capped at $25,000 |
| Oregon | ORS 646.605 (UTPA) | $200 statutory minimum; no cap |
| California | Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200 (UCL) + Civ. Code § 1750 (CLRA) + Civ. Code § 1788 (Rosenthal) | Multiple overlapping statutes; private right of action available |
| Texas | Tex. Bus. & Com. Code § 17 (DTPA) | Treble damages on "knowing" violations |
| Florida | Fla. Stat. § 501.201 (FDUTPA) | Actual damages + fees |
| New York | GBL § 349, § 350 | $50 minimum, $1,000 cap on willful; very narrow |

Compared to FDCPA:

- All state statutes typically cover original creditors
  (FDCPA does not)
- State statutes often have no $1,000 cap
- State fee provisions vary; California Rosenthal has
  mandatory fees similar to FDCPA

## Debt-collection licensing

| State | License/Registration? | Effect of non-compliance |
|-------|-----------------------|--------------------------|
| Washington | RCW 19.16 — license | Cannot collect; private right (RCW 19.16.440) |
| Oregon | ORS 697 — registration | **Cannot maintain action (ORS 697.105)** |
| California | Cal. Fin. Code § 100001+ (CDCLA, 2022) — license | License required as of Sept 2022 |
| Texas | Tex. Fin. Code § 392 — bond | Voids communications (§ 392.401) |
| Florida | Fla. Stat. § 559.55+ — license | Various penalties |
| New York | NY Gen. Bus. Law § 600 — license | Affidavit requirements |

The Oregon lack-of-capacity defense (ORS 697.105) is unusually
strong. California's CDCLA (post-2022) has growing analogs.

## Time computation

| State | Rule | Mail rule |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Federal | FRCP 6 | 3 days (Rule 6(d)) |
| Washington | CR 6 | 3 days (CR 6(e)) |
| Oregon | ORCP 10 | 3 days (ORCP 10 C) |
| California | CCP § 1013, § 1010.6 | 5 days (CCP § 1013) for mailed papers |
| Texas | TRCP 4 | 3 days (TRCP 21a) |
| Florida | Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.090 | 5 days (Rule 1.090(e)) |
| New York | CPLR 2103 | 5 days (CPLR 2103(b)(2)) for mailed papers |

California's and New York's 5-day mail rule (vs. 3 days in
WA/OR/federal) is a frequent error source.

## Exhibit conventions

| State | Convention |
|-------|------------|
| Washington | Lettered (A, B, C) |
| Oregon | Numbered (1, 2, 3) |
| California | Numbered (1, 2, 3) |
| Texas | Numbered or lettered (varies by court) |
| Florida | Numbered |
| Federal | Lettered (Bluebook); but local rules vary |

When porting templates, the exhibit-numbering swap is the
most common cosmetic miss.

## Citation style

The state's style manual diverges from Bluebook in nearly
every state:

| State | Periods in reporter? |
|-------|---------------------|
| Washington | Yes (Wn.2d, Wn. App., P.3d) |
| **Oregon** | **No (Or, Or App, P3d, USC, CFR)** |
| California | Yes, but specific format (Cal. 3d, Cal. App. 4th) |
| Texas | Yes (Tex., Tex. App.) |
| Florida | Yes (Fla., Fla. App.) |
| New York | Yes (N.Y., N.Y.2d) |

Oregon's no-period convention is the most distinctive among
state style manuals.

## Party-name conventions

| State | Separator | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Washington | "vs." | All caps in caption |
| **Oregon** | **"v."** | Lowercase with period |
| California | "v." | Per CRC 2.111 |
| Texas | "v." | |
| Florida | "v." | |
| New York | "v." | |

Washington's "vs." is the outlier. Most other states use "v."
with a period.

## Hearings and remote appearances

| State | Default civil hearing mode (typical for routine motions) |
|-------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Washington | In-person (Zoom available); KCDC mixed |
| Oregon | WebEx (per assigned judge's standing order) |
| California | Mixed; varies by court (LA tentative-ruling system) |
| Texas | In-person; some Zoom |
| Florida | Mostly in-person post-COVID |
| New York | In-person; virtual conferencing growing |

## Ethical / pro se rules

| Quirk | State |
|-------|-------|
| OSB# on signature block | Oregon (counsel only) |
| WSBA# on signature block | Washington (counsel only) |
| Cal. Bar # on signature | California (CCP § 396a) |
| "Pro se" vs. "in pro per" | Most states "pro se"; California "in pro per" |

California's "in pro per" terminology is unique among major
states.

## How to use this catalog

When adding a new state plugin:

1. Identify the quirks from this catalog that apply to the
   new state
2. Author them prominently in the relevant skill's
   `description:` (so the skill triggers when the user
   stumbles into the quirk)
3. Author them prominently in the skill body (so when the
   skill fires, the agent walks the user through correctly)
4. Add them to the new state's `fact-patterns.md` (the
   common-failure-modes list)

When adding a state not yet on this list (Arizona, Colorado,
Michigan, etc.), research the analogous quirks from primary
sources and update this catalog as well.
